Entropy as a mirror map

Definition. On the probability simplex, the negative entropy \(\psi(q)=\sum_iq_i\log q_i\) generates the KL Bregman divergence \[ D_\psi(q,r)=\sum_iq_i\log(q_i/r_i). \]

Theorem. Mirror descent with negative entropy gives the exponentiated-gradient update \[ q_i^{+}\propto q_i\exp\{-\eta g_i\}. \]

Proof. The mirror step minimizes \(\langle g,q\rangle+\eta^{-1}D_\psi(q,q^{old})\) over the simplex. The Lagrange equation is \(g_i+\eta^{-1}(\log q_i-\log q_i^{old}+1)+\lambda=0\), which yields the displayed proportionality after normalization.

VI interpretation

Remark. Coordinate-ascent VI, natural-gradient VI, and entropic regularization all use KL geometry. The difference is which object is optimized: a factor, a parameterized distribution, or a regularized transport of mass.

Bregman proximal form

Theorem. Mirror descent with mirror map \(\psi\) solves \[ x^{+}=\arg\min_x\{\eta\langle g,x\rangle+D_\psi(x,x^{old})\}. \] When \(\psi\) is negative entropy on the simplex, this is exponentiated gradient.

Proof. The first-order condition is \(\nabla\psi(x^+)=\nabla\psi(x^{old})-\eta g\) plus the simplex normalization multiplier. For negative entropy, \(\nabla\psi(x)=1+\log x\), so the update is multiplicative followed by normalization.
