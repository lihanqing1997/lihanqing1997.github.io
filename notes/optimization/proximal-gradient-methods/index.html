This page records the definitions, propositions, and proof mechanisms for Proximal gradient methods.

Composite objective

Definition. For \(F(x)=f(x)+g(x)\), with \(f\) smooth and \(g\) convex possibly nonsmooth, proximal gradient uses \(x_{k+1}=\operatorname{prox}_{\eta g}(x_k-\eta\nabla f(x_k))\).

Descent theorem

Theorem. If \(f\) is \(L\)-smooth and \(0<\eta\le1/L\), proximal gradient decreases the standard quadratic upper model and converges to a minimizer for closed convex composite objectives.

Accelerated proximal gradient

Proposition. FISTA combines Nesterov extrapolation with a proximal step and obtains \(O(1/k^2)\) objective convergence for convex composite objectives.

Statistical penalties

Remark. Lasso, group lasso, nuclear-norm regularization, and constrained empirical risk are naturally composite: smooth loss plus nonsmooth structure penalty.
