16. Newton's method

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimization/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimization/mirror-descent-bregman-geometry/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimization/quasi-newton-secant-methods/" %}

This page records the definitions, propositions, and proof mechanisms for Newton's method.

Newton step

Definition. For twice differentiable \(f\), Newton's method solves the local quadratic model and updates \(x_{k+1}=x_k-[\nabla^2f(x_k)]^{-1}\nabla f(x_k)\), when the Hessian is invertible.

Quadratic convergence

Theorem. If \(x^\star\) satisfies \(\nabla f(x^\star)=0\), \(\nabla^2f(x^\star)\) is nonsingular, and the Hessian is locally Lipschitz, then Newton iterates started sufficiently close to \(x^\star\) converge quadratically.

Affine invariance

Proposition. The exact Newton direction is invariant under nonsingular affine reparametrizations. This is why Newton steps adapt to local curvature, unlike ordinary Euclidean gradient steps.

Globalization

Remark. Far from the optimum, the pure Newton step can be unstable or increase the objective. Line search, trust regions, and damping turn the local method into a global algorithm.
