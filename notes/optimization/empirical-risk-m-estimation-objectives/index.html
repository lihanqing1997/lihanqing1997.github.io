27. Empirical risk and M-estimation objectives

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimization/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimization/em-mm-algorithms/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimization/natural-gradient-information-geometric-optimization/" %}

This page records the definitions, propositions, and proof mechanisms for Empirical risk and M-estimation objectives.

Empirical risk

Definition. Given losses \(\ell_\theta(X_i)\), empirical risk minimization chooses \(\hat\theta_n\in\arg\min_\theta P_n\ell_\theta\). M-estimation is this principle in statistical form.

Population target

Definition. The deterministic target is \(\theta_0\in\arg\min_\theta P\ell_\theta\). Consistency compares the random objective \(P_n\ell_\theta\) to \(P\ell_\theta\) uniformly over relevant parameter sets.

Basic consistency theorem

Theorem. If \(\sup_{\theta\in\Theta}|P_n\ell_\theta-P\ell_\theta|\to0\) in probability and the population risk is separated away from its unique minimizer \(\theta_0\), then every approximate empirical minimizer converges to \(\theta_0\).

Optimization-statistics split

Remark. In computation one often returns an approximate minimizer. The final error decomposes into approximation error, optimization error, and statistical error.
