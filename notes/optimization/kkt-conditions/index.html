8. KKT conditions

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimization/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimization/fenchel-conjugates-duality/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimization/normal-cones-projected-optimality/" %}

This page records the definitions, propositions, and proof mechanisms for KKT conditions.

Inequality-constrained problem

Definition. For \(\min f(x)\) subject to \(g_i(x)\le0\) and \(h_j(x)=0\), the Lagrangian is \(\mathcal L=f+\sum_i\lambda_i g_i+\sum_j\nu_j h_j\), with \(\lambda_i\ge0\).

KKT theorem

Theorem. Under a constraint qualification, every local optimum satisfies stationarity, primal feasibility, dual feasibility \(\lambda_i\ge0\), and complementary slackness \(\lambda_i g_i(x^\star)=0\). For convex problems, these conditions are sufficient for global optimality.

Complementary slackness

Remark. A multiplier can be positive only for an active constraint. Inactive constraints have zero price because locally relaxing them changes nothing.

Convex proof

Proof. If KKT holds and the problem is convex, then the Lagrangian gives a global affine lower bound: \(f(x)\ge\mathcal L(x,\lambda,\nu)\ge\mathcal L(x^\star,\lambda,\nu)=f(x^\star)\) for feasible \(x\).
