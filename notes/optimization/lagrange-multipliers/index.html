6. Lagrange multipliers

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimization/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimization/existence-coercivity-error-bounds/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimization/fenchel-conjugates-duality/" %}

This page records the definitions, propositions, and proof mechanisms for Lagrange multipliers.

Equality-constrained problem

Definition. For \(\min f(x)\) subject to \(h(x)=0\), the Lagrangian is \(\mathcal L(x,\lambda)=f(x)+\lambda^\top h(x)\). Multipliers price the constraints.

Multiplier rule

Theorem. If \(x^\star\) locally solves the equality-constrained problem and \(Dh(x^\star)\) has full row rank, then there exists \(\lambda^\star\) such that \(\nabla_x\mathcal L(x^\star,\lambda^\star)=0\) and \(h(x^\star)=0\).

Tangent-space proof

Proof. At a constrained local minimum, \(\nabla f(x^\star)\) annihilates every tangent vector \(v\) with \(Dh(x^\star)v=0\). Linear algebra says the annihilator of this kernel is the row span of \(Dh(x^\star)\), giving \(\nabla f+Dh^\top\lambda=0\).

Interpretation

Remark. Multipliers are not algorithmic artifacts. They are coordinates for normals to the feasible manifold and become dual variables in convex optimization.
