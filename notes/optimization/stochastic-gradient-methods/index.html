14. Stochastic gradient methods

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimization/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimization/coordinate-descent-block-methods/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimization/mirror-descent-bregman-geometry/" %}

This page records the definitions, propositions, and proof mechanisms for Stochastic gradient methods.

Unbiased stochastic gradient

Definition. A random vector \(G(x,\xi)\) is an unbiased stochastic gradient if \(\mathbb E[G(x,\xi)\mid x]=\nabla f(x)\). Stochastic gradient descent uses \(x_{k+1}=x_k-\eta_kG(x_k,\xi_k)\).

Robbins-Monro step sizes

Theorem. For strongly convex objectives with bounded gradient noise, step sizes satisfying \(\sum_k\eta_k=\infty\) and \(\sum_k\eta_k^2<\infty\) can yield almost sure convergence to the minimizer.

Bias-variance tradeoff

Proposition. Constant step sizes converge quickly to a noise neighborhood; decreasing step sizes remove asymptotic bias more slowly. Averaging iterates often improves asymptotic variance.

Statistical interpretation

Remark. For empirical risk, stochastic gradients replace full sample averages by mini-batches. The algorithmic noise is then deliberately introduced sampling noise, separate from statistical sampling noise.
