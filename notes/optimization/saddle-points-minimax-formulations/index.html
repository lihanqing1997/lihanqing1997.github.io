10. Saddle points and minimax formulations

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimization/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimization/normal-cones-projected-optimality/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimization/gradient-descent/" %}

This page records the definitions, propositions, and proof mechanisms for Saddle points and minimax formulations.

Saddle point

Definition. A pair \((x^\star,y^\star)\) is a saddle point of \(L(x,y)\) if \(L(x^\star,y)\le L(x^\star,y^\star)\le L(x,y^\star)\) for all feasible \(x,y\).

Weak minimax inequality

Proposition. Always \(\sup_y\inf_x L(x,y)\le\inf_x\sup_y L(x,y)\). Equality is strong minimax duality and requires convexity, compactness, continuity, or related regularity.

Sion-type principle

Theorem. When \(X\) is compact convex, \(Y\) is convex, and \(L\) is convex lower semicontinuous in \(x\) and concave upper semicontinuous in \(y\), the minimax equality holds.

Decision-theoretic link

Remark. Least favorable priors and minimax rules are saddle points: a procedure minimizes maximum risk, while a prior maximizes Bayes risk.
