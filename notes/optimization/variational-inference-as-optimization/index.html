29. Variational inference as optimization

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimization/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimization/natural-gradient-information-geometric-optimization/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimization/optimization-error-statistical-error/" %}

This page records the definitions, propositions, and proof mechanisms for Variational inference as optimization.

Variational family

Definition. Variational inference approximates a posterior \(\Pi(\cdot|X)\) by choosing \(q\in\mathcal Q\) that optimizes a divergence or lower bound over a tractable family \(\mathcal Q\).

ELBO

Proposition. Minimizing reverse KL \(D(q\|\Pi(\cdot|X))\) is equivalent to maximizing the evidence lower bound \(\mathbb E_q\log p(X,Z)-\mathbb E_q\log q(Z)\), up to the fixed log evidence.

Coordinate ascent

Theorem. For mean-field families \(q(z)=\prod_jq_j(z_j)\), the coordinate optimum satisfies \(\log q_j(z_j)=\mathbb E_{-j}\log p(X,Z)+\text{constant}\), whenever the update is inside the family.

Geometry of approximation

Remark. Reverse KL often prefers concentrated approximations, while forward KL tends to cover posterior mass. The distinction is geometric and statistical, not only computational.
