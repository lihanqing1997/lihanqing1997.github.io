2. Convex sets and separation

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimization/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimization/optimization-problems-local-minima/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimization/convex-functions-subdifferentials/" %}

This page records the definitions, propositions, and proof mechanisms for Convex sets and separation.

Convex set

Definition. A set \(C\subset\mathbb R^d\) is convex if \(tx+(1-t)y\in C\) for all \(x,y\in C\) and \(t\in[0,1]\). Convexity means that line segments do not leave the feasible region.

Projection theorem

Theorem. If \(C\) is nonempty, closed, and convex, then for every \(x\) there is a unique projection \(P_Cx\in C\) minimizing \(\|x-y\|\). It is characterized by \(\langle x-P_Cx,y-P_Cx\rangle\le0\) for all \(y\in C\).

Separating hyperplane

Theorem. If \(C\) is closed and convex and \(x\notin C\), then there exists a nonzero vector \(a\) and a scalar \(b\) such that \(\langle a,x\rangle>b\ge\langle a,y\rangle\) for all \(y\in C\).

Role in optimality

Remark. Separation is the geometric source of multipliers and dual variables. Optimality often says that a descent direction cannot be separated from the feasible cone in the wrong direction.
