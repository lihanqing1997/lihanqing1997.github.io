This page records the definitions, propositions, and proof mechanisms for Line search and trust regions.

Line search

Definition. Given a descent direction \(p_k\), line search chooses \(\alpha_k>0\) and sets \(x_{k+1}=x_k+\alpha_kp_k\). Armijo and Wolfe conditions balance sufficient decrease with curvature information.

Trust region

Definition. Trust-region methods minimize a local model \(m_k(p)\) subject to \(\|p\|\le\Delta_k\), then expand or shrink \(\Delta_k\) according to agreement between actual and predicted decrease.

Global convergence principle

Theorem. For smooth bounded-below objectives, standard line-search or trust-region globalization ensures that every accumulation point is first-order stationary, under mild regularity on descent directions or models.

Second-order methods

Remark. Newton directions become reliable only when the local quadratic approximation is trusted. These mechanisms decide how much of that local model to believe.
