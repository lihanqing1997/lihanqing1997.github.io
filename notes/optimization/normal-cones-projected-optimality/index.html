This page records the definitions, propositions, and proof mechanisms for Normal cones and projected optimality.

Normal cone

Definition. For a convex set \(C\), the normal cone at \(x\in C\) is \(N_C(x)=\{v:\langle v,y-x\rangle\le0\text{ for all }y\in C\}\).

Projected optimality condition

Theorem. A point \(x^\star\in C\) minimizes differentiable convex \(f\) over \(C\) if and only if \(0\in\nabla f(x^\star)+N_C(x^\star)\). Equivalently, \(x^\star=P_C(x^\star-\eta\nabla f(x^\star))\) for any \(\eta>0\).

Indicator functions

Proposition. The normal cone is the subdifferential of the indicator function \(\iota_C\). Thus constrained optimization is unconstrained nonsmooth optimization of \(f+\iota_C\).

Algorithmic meaning

Remark. Projected gradient methods alternate a gradient move with projection. The fixed-point equation is exactly the normal-cone optimality condition.
