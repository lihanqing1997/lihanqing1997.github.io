This page records the definitions, propositions, and proof mechanisms for EM and MM algorithms.

Majorization-minimization

Definition. An MM algorithm minimizes a surrogate \(Q(x|x_k)\) satisfying \(Q(x_k|x_k)=f(x_k)\) and \(Q(x|x_k)\ge f(x)\). The next iterate minimizes or decreases \(Q\).

Monotonicity

Proposition. If the surrogate majorizes the objective and is tight at \(x_k\), then exact surrogate minimization gives \(f(x_{k+1})\le f(x_k)\).

EM algorithm

Definition. For latent-variable likelihoods, EM alternates an E-step computing conditional expectations under the current parameter and an M-step maximizing the expected complete-data log likelihood.

Variational identity

Remark. EM is an MM algorithm because the observed log likelihood equals an evidence lower bound plus a KL divergence. Tightness occurs when the auxiliary distribution is the current conditional law of the latent variables.
