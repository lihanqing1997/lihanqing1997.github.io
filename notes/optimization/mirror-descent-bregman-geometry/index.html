This page records the definitions, propositions, and proof mechanisms for Mirror descent and Bregman geometry.

Bregman divergence

Definition. For a differentiable strictly convex potential \(\psi\), the Bregman divergence is \(D_\psi(x:y)=\psi(x)-\psi(y)-\langle\nabla\psi(y),x-y\rangle\).

Mirror descent step

Definition. Mirror descent updates by \(x_{k+1}=\arg\min_{x\in C}\{\eta_k\langle g_k,x\rangle+D_\psi(x:x_k)\}\). In dual coordinates this is a gradient step: \(\nabla\psi(x_{k+1})=\nabla\psi(x_k)-\eta_kg_k\), before projection.

Regret bound

Theorem. For convex Lipschitz losses and strongly convex \(\psi\), mirror descent satisfies the usual \(O(\sqrt n)\) online regret bound with constants measured by the Bregman geometry rather than Euclidean diameter.

Information geometry

Remark. On the simplex, entropy mirror descent gives multiplicative weights. In exponential families, mirror descent is naturally expressed through dual affine coordinates.
