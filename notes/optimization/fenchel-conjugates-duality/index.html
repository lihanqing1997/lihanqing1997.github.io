7. Fenchel conjugates and Fenchel duality

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimization/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimization/lagrange-multipliers/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimization/kkt-conditions/" %}

This page records the definitions, propositions, and proof mechanisms for Fenchel conjugates and Fenchel duality.

Fenchel conjugate

Definition. The Fenchel conjugate of \(f\) is \(f^*(y)=\sup_x\{\langle y,x\rangle-f(x)\}\). It records all affine minorants of \(f\).

Fenchel inequality

Theorem. For every \(x,y\), \(f(x)+f^*(y)\ge\langle x,y\rangle\). Equality holds exactly when \(y\in\partial f(x)\).

Fenchel dual problem

Proposition. For \(\inf_x f(Ax)+g(x)\), the Fenchel dual is \(\sup_y -f^*(y)-g^*(-A^\top y)\), under the usual regularity conditions. Strong duality follows when the primal and dual problems have no separating gap.

Information connection

Remark. Legendre duality in information geometry and log-partition/entropy duality in exponential families are Fenchel duality in smooth coordinates.
