This page records the definitions, propositions, and proof mechanisms for Splitting methods and ADMM.

Splitting principle

Definition. Splitting methods rewrite one hard problem as coupled simpler subproblems, often by introducing variables so that smooth, nonsmooth, and constraint parts are handled separately.

ADMM iteration

Definition. For \(\min f(x)+g(z)\) subject to \(Ax+Bz=c\), ADMM alternates minimization of the augmented Lagrangian in \(x\) and \(z\), followed by a dual ascent step.

Convergence theorem

Theorem. For closed proper convex \(f,g\) and a saddle point, standard ADMM converges in objective and feasibility residuals under mild rank and solvability conditions.

Why it is useful

Remark. ADMM is valuable when different pieces of the objective have cheap proximal maps or live on different machines, variables, or model components.
