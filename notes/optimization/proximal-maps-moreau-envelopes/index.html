21. Proximal maps and Moreau envelopes

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimization/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimization/self-concordance-interior-point-newton/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimization/proximal-gradient-methods/" %}

This page records the definitions, propositions, and proof mechanisms for Proximal maps and Moreau envelopes.

Proximal map

Definition. For proper closed convex \(g\), \(\operatorname{prox}_{\eta g}(x)=\arg\min_z\{g(z)+\frac{1}{2\eta}\|z-x\|^2\}\). It is an implicit gradient step for nonsmooth functions.

Optimality condition

Proposition. The point \(z=\operatorname{prox}_{\eta g}(x)\) satisfies \(0\in\partial g(z)+\eta^{-1}(z-x)\), or equivalently \(x-z\in\eta\partial g(z)\).

Moreau envelope

Definition. The Moreau envelope \(g_\eta(x)=\min_z\{g(z)+\frac{1}{2\eta}\|z-x\|^2\}\) is a smooth approximation of \(g\), with gradient \(\nabla g_\eta(x)=\eta^{-1}(x-\operatorname{prox}_{\eta g}(x))\).

Examples

Remark. Projection is the prox of an indicator. Soft-thresholding is the prox of the \(\ell_1\) norm. These two examples drive much of constrained and sparse optimization.
