18. Gauss-Newton, Fisher scoring, and iteratively reweighted least squares

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimization/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimization/quasi-newton-secant-methods/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimization/line-search-trust-regions/" %}

This page records the definitions, propositions, and proof mechanisms for Gauss-Newton, Fisher scoring, and iteratively reweighted least squares.

Gauss-Newton approximation

Definition. For nonlinear least squares \(\frac12\|r(\theta)\|^2\), Gauss-Newton replaces the Hessian by \(J(\theta)^\top J(\theta)\), where \(J\) is the Jacobian of residuals.

Fisher scoring

Definition. For likelihood maximization, Fisher scoring replaces the observed negative Hessian by expected information: \(\theta_{k+1}=\theta_k+I(\theta_k)^{-1}S(\theta_k)\).

IRWLS for generalized linear models

Proposition. In regular generalized linear models, Fisher scoring can be written as iteratively reweighted least squares, with working responses and weights determined by the mean-variance relation and link derivative.

Statistical meaning

Remark. These methods are Newton-like but replace unstable curvature by a positive semidefinite approximation tied to local prediction or information geometry.
