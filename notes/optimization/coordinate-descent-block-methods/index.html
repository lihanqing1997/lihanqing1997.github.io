This page records the definitions, propositions, and proof mechanisms for Coordinate descent and block methods.

Coordinate update

Definition. Coordinate descent updates one coordinate or block at a time: \(x_{k+1}=x_k+u_k\), where \(u_k\) is supported on the selected block and reduces the objective over that block.

Exact block minimization

Proposition. If \(f\) is convex and each block subproblem is minimized exactly, every limit point of a cyclic coordinate descent sequence is globally optimal, provided standard regularity rules exclude pathological flat directions.

Random coordinate rate

Theorem. For smooth convex functions with coordinate Lipschitz constants \(L_i\), randomized coordinate descent decreases expected objective at a rate controlled by \(\sum_iL_i\), often much smaller than using the full Lipschitz constant in high dimension.

Sparse models

Remark. Coordinate methods are natural for lasso, generalized linear models, and separable penalties because each update can exploit sparsity and cheap one-dimensional minimization.
