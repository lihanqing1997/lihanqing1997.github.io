28. Natural gradient and information-geometric optimization

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimization/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimization/empirical-risk-m-estimation-objectives/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimization/variational-inference-as-optimization/" %}

This page records the definitions, propositions, and proof mechanisms for Natural gradient and information-geometric optimization.

Riemannian gradient

Definition. Given a metric \(g_\theta\), the natural gradient \(\widetilde\nabla L\) is defined by \(g_\theta(\widetilde\nabla L,v)=\mathrm{d}L_\theta(v)\) for every tangent vector \(v\).

Fisher metric

Proposition. On a statistical model, using Fisher information as \(g\) makes the gradient direction invariant under smooth reparametrization and measures step size by local statistical distinguishability.

Constrained KL step

Theorem. The infinitesimal solution of maximizing linearized improvement subject to a small KL or Fisher-distance constraint is the natural-gradient direction.

Connection to mirror descent

Remark. In dually flat models, natural-gradient and mirror-descent updates are two coordinate views of the same geometry, linked by Legendre dual potentials.
