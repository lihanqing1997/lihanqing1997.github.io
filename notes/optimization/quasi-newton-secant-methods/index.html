This page records the definitions, propositions, and proof mechanisms for Quasi-Newton and secant methods.

Secant equation

Definition. Quasi-Newton methods maintain an approximation \(B_k\) to the Hessian satisfying the secant equation \(B_{k+1}s_k=y_k\), where \(s_k=x_{k+1}-x_k\) and \(y_k=\nabla f(x_{k+1})-\nabla f(x_k)\).

BFGS update

Proposition. The BFGS update is the minimal symmetric rank-two correction preserving positive definiteness when \(s_k^\top y_k>0\). Its inverse form gives a practical approximate Newton direction without solving a full linear system.

Superlinear convergence

Theorem. Under smooth strongly convex conditions and suitable line search, BFGS converges superlinearly: the error ratio improves beyond every fixed linear factor near the solution.

Large-scale version

Remark. Limited-memory BFGS stores only recent \((s_k,y_k)\) pairs. It is often the default deterministic method when full Hessians are too expensive but curvature information matters.
