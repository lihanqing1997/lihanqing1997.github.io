This page records the definitions, propositions, and proof mechanisms for Sparse optimization and coordinate-proximal methods.

Sparse objective

Definition. A typical sparse objective has the form \(\ell(\beta)+\lambda\|\beta\|_1\), where \(\ell\) is a smooth empirical loss. The nonsmooth term induces exact zeros.

Soft-thresholding update

Proposition. For quadratic coordinate models, the lasso coordinate update is soft-thresholding: \(\beta_j\leftarrow S_\lambda(z_j)/a_j\), where \(S_\lambda(z)=\operatorname{sign}(z)(|z|-\lambda)_+\).

KKT screening

Theorem. At a lasso optimum, active coordinates satisfy gradient balance, while inactive coordinates satisfy \(|\nabla_j\ell(\beta)|\le\lambda\). These KKT inequalities justify screening and active-set strategies.

High-dimensional link

Remark. Sparse optimization is not merely computational. Its KKT conditions connect directly to variable selection, debiasing, and high-dimensional inference.
