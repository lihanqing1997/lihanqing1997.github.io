25. Monotone operators and variational inequalities

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimization/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimization/sparse-optimization-coordinate-proximal-methods/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimization/em-mm-algorithms/" %}

This page records the definitions, propositions, and proof mechanisms for Monotone operators and variational inequalities.

Monotone operator

Definition. An operator \(T\) is monotone if \(\langle u-v,x-y\rangle\ge0\) whenever \(u\in T(x)\) and \(v\in T(y)\). Subdifferentials of convex functions are monotone.

Variational inequality

Definition. The variational inequality \(\operatorname{VI}(C,F)\) asks for \(x^\star\in C\) such that \(\langle F(x^\star),x-x^\star\rangle\ge0\) for all \(x\in C\).

Resolvent

Proposition. For maximal monotone \(T\), the resolvent \((I+\eta T)^{-1}\) is single-valued and firmly nonexpansive. Proximal maps are resolvents of subdifferentials.

Unifying role

Remark. Gradient descent, proximal point, projected methods, saddle-point algorithms, and equilibrium problems can all be written as finding zeros or variational inequalities of monotone operators.
