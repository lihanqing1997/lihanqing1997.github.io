This page records the definitions, propositions, and proof mechanisms for Accelerated gradient methods.

Momentum form

Definition. Accelerated methods combine a gradient step with an extrapolated point, for example \(y_k=x_k+\beta_k(x_k-x_{k-1})\), \(x_{k+1}=y_k-L^{-1}\nabla f(y_k)\).

Nesterov rate

Theorem. For convex \(L\)-smooth \(f\), Nesterov acceleration achieves \(f(x_k)-f^\star=O(L\|x_0-x^\star\|^2/k^2)\), improving the \(O(1/k)\) rate of ordinary gradient descent.

Strongly convex rate

Proposition. For \(\mu\)-strongly convex and \(L\)-smooth objectives, accelerated schemes reach a linear rate governed by \(1-1/\sqrt\kappa\), where \(\kappa=L/\mu\).

Caution

Remark. Acceleration is sensitive to curvature estimates and noise. In statistical optimization it may reduce deterministic optimization error while amplifying stochastic variation if used without damping.
