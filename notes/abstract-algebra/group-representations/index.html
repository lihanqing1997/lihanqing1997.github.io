Group representations records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the abstract algebra note.

Definition

Definition. A representation of a group \(G\) over a field \(F\) is a homomorphism \(G\to GL(V)\). It realizes abstract group elements as linear transformations.

Maschke theorem

Theorem. For a finite group over a characteristic-zero algebraically closed field, every finite-dimensional representation decomposes as a direct sum of irreducible representations.

Proof

Proof. Average any inner product over the group to obtain a \(G\)-invariant inner product. Orthogonal complements of invariant subspaces are invariant, giving complete reducibility by induction.

Remark

Remark. Representation theory studies groups through their linear shadows. Characters then compress representations into class functions.
