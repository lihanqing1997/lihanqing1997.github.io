13. Separable extensions

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/abstract-algebra/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/abstract-algebra/splitting-fields-and-normality/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/abstract-algebra/galois-groups-and-the-fundamental-theorem/" %}

Separable extensions records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the abstract algebra note.

Definition

Definition. An algebraic element is separable over \(K\) if its minimal polynomial has distinct roots in an algebraic closure. An extension is separable if all its elements are separable.

Proposition

Proposition. Over characteristic zero, every algebraic extension is separable. In positive characteristic, inseparability appears when derivatives of minimal polynomials vanish.

Proof

Proof. A polynomial has repeated roots exactly when it shares a factor with its derivative. In characteristic zero irreducible polynomials cannot have zero derivative.

Remark

Remark. Separability is the condition that enough embeddings exist. Without it, the Galois group is too small to see the extension degree.
