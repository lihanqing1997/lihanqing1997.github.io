14. Galois groups and the fundamental theorem

Galois groups and the fundamental theorem records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the abstract algebra note.

Definition

Definition. For a Galois extension \(L/K\), the Galois group \(\operatorname{Gal}(L/K)\) consists of field automorphisms of \(L\) fixing \(K\). Fixed fields reverse inclusions of subgroups.

Fundamental theorem of Galois theory

Theorem. There is an inclusion-reversing bijection between intermediate fields \(K\subset E\subset L\) and subgroups of \(\operatorname{Gal}(L/K)\). Normal subgroups correspond to Galois intermediate extensions.

Proof

Proof. Show that elements fixed by a subgroup form a field, and automorphisms fixing an intermediate field form a subgroup. Separability and normality make the two constructions inverse.

Remark

Remark. The theorem converts questions about polynomial roots into group theory. Solvability by radicals is the classical example.
