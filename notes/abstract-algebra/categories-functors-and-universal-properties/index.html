19. Categories, functors, and universal properties

Categories, functors, and universal properties records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the abstract algebra note.

Definition

Definition. A category has objects and morphisms with associative composition and identities. A functor preserves objects, morphisms, identities, and composition.

Proposition

Proposition. Universal properties characterize objects by their maps to or from all other objects. Products, quotients, tensor products, free objects, and localizations are standard examples.

Proof

Proof. To prove two universal objects are isomorphic, use each universal property to construct maps in both directions; uniqueness forces the composites to be identities.

Remark

Remark. Category language is useful when the same construction recurs across groups, rings, modules, spaces, and vector spaces.
