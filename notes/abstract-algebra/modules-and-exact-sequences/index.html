9. Modules and exact sequences

Modules and exact sequences records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the abstract algebra note.

Definition

Definition. A module over a ring is an abelian group with scalar multiplication by ring elements. Exact sequences record kernels and images of module homomorphisms.

Proposition

Proposition. Over a field, modules are vector spaces and short exact sequences split. Over general rings, short exact sequences need not split, and this failure is structural information.

Proof

Proof. A splitting requires a compatible choice of representatives for quotient classes. Fields allow basis extension; general rings may lack bases or complements.

Remark

Remark. Modules are linear algebra over rings. The price of greater generality is that dimension is replaced by subtler invariants.
