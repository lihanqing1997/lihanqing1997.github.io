4. Group actions and orbit-stabilizer

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/abstract-algebra/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/abstract-algebra/isomorphism-theorems/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/abstract-algebra/sylow-theory/" %}

Group actions and orbit-stabilizer records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the abstract algebra note.

Definition

Definition. A group action of \(G\) on \(X\) is a homomorphism from \(G\) to permutations of \(X\). Each point has an orbit \(Gx\) and stabilizer \(G_x\).

Proposition

Proposition. For a finite group action, \(|Gx|=[G:G_x]\). Thus orbit size times stabilizer size equals \(|G|\).

Proof

Proof. The map \(G/G_x\to Gx\), \(gG_x\mapsto gx\), is well-defined and bijective. Counting cosets gives the formula.

Remark

Remark. Actions let a group be studied through what it does. Counting orbits is the path to class equations, Sylow theory, and representation theory.
