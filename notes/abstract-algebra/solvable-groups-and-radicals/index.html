16. Solvable groups and radicals

Solvable groups and radicals records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the abstract algebra note.

Definition

Definition. A group is solvable if its derived series eventually reaches the trivial group. Radical field extensions are built by repeatedly adjoining roots.

Proposition

Proposition. A polynomial equation is solvable by radicals only when the corresponding Galois group has an appropriate solvable structure. In characteristic zero, this criterion explains the Abel-Ruffini theorem.

Proof

Proof. Radical extensions produce towers whose Galois groups have abelian successive quotients. Conversely, solvable Galois groups can be analyzed by such towers after adding needed roots of unity.

Remark

Remark. The statement is not merely about formulas; it identifies symmetry as the obstruction to certain algebraic solution procedures.
