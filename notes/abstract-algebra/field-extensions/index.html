11. Field extensions

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/abstract-algebra/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/abstract-algebra/structure-theorem-over-a-pid/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/abstract-algebra/splitting-fields-and-normality/" %}

Field extensions records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the abstract algebra note.

Definition

Definition. A field extension \(L/K\) is an inclusion of fields. Elements of \(L\) may be algebraic over \(K\), satisfying a nonzero polynomial, or transcendental.

Proposition

Proposition. If \(\alpha\) is algebraic over \(K\), then \(K(\alpha)\cong K[x]/(m_\alpha)\), where \(m_\alpha\) is the minimal polynomial. The degree of the extension is \(\deg m_\alpha\).

Proof

Proof. Evaluation \(K[x]\to K(\alpha)\) has kernel generated by the irreducible minimal polynomial. The first isomorphism theorem gives the quotient description.

Remark

Remark. Field extensions turn polynomial equations into algebraic objects. The minimal polynomial records exactly the algebraic information of one element.
