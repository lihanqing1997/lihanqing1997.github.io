5. Sylow theory

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/abstract-algebra/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/abstract-algebra/group-actions-and-orbit-stabilizer/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/abstract-algebra/rings-ideals-and-quotient-rings/" %}

Sylow theory records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the abstract algebra note.

Definition

Definition. For a finite group \(G\) with \(|G|=p^a m\), \(p\nmid m\), a Sylow \(p\)-subgroup is a subgroup of order \(p^a\).

Sylow theorems

Theorem. Sylow subgroups exist, are conjugate, and their number is congruent to \(1\) modulo \(p\) while dividing \(m\).

Proof

Proof. Let \(G\) act on suitable collections of subsets or subgroups by translation or conjugation. Orbit-stabilizer and fixed-point counting force the divisibility and congruence constraints.

Remark

Remark. Sylow theory is a precise bridge between arithmetic of \(|G|\) and internal group structure. It often proves nonexistence or uniqueness of groups of a given order.
