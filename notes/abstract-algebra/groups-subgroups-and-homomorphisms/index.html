1. Groups, subgroups, and homomorphisms

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/abstract-algebra/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/abstract-algebra/cosets-and-quotient-groups/" %}

Groups, subgroups, and homomorphisms records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the abstract algebra note.

Definition

Definition. A group is a set with associative multiplication, identity, and inverses. A subgroup is closed under the same operations, and a homomorphism preserves multiplication.

Proposition

Proposition. The kernel of a group homomorphism is a normal subgroup, and the image is a subgroup. A homomorphism is injective exactly when its kernel is trivial.

Proof

Proof. Closure of the kernel follows from preservation of products and inverses. If two elements have the same image, their quotient lies in the kernel, giving the injectivity criterion.

Remark

Remark. Groups formalize symmetry. Homomorphisms are the maps that preserve symmetry, and kernels measure exactly which symmetry has been collapsed.
