18. Characters and orthogonality

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/abstract-algebra/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/abstract-algebra/group-representations/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/abstract-algebra/categories-functors-and-universal-properties/" %}

Characters and orthogonality records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the abstract algebra note.

Definition

Definition. The character of a representation is \(\chi(g)=\operatorname{tr}\rho(g)\). Characters are constant on conjugacy classes and often determine representations.

Proposition

Proposition. Irreducible characters of a finite group over \(\mathbb C\) are orthonormal under the inner product \(\langle \chi,\psi\rangle=|G|^{-1}\sum_{g\in G}\chi(g)\overline{\psi(g)}\).

Proof

Proof. Average projection operators over the group and apply Schur's lemma. Taking traces of these averaged maps gives the orthogonality relations.

Remark

Remark. Character tables turn representation theory into computable linear algebra while retaining deep group structure.
