3. Isomorphism theorems

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/abstract-algebra/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/abstract-algebra/cosets-and-quotient-groups/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/abstract-algebra/group-actions-and-orbit-stabilizer/" %}

Isomorphism theorems records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the abstract algebra note.

Definition

Definition. The isomorphism theorems compare homomorphisms, kernels, subgroups, and quotients. They are algebraic bookkeeping principles for passing through equivalence relations.

First isomorphism theorem

Theorem. If \(\varphi:G\to H\) is a homomorphism, then \(G/\ker\varphi\cong\operatorname{im}\varphi\). Analogous second and third isomorphism theorems describe nested quotients.

Proof

Proof. Define the quotient map by \(g\ker\varphi\mapsto\varphi(g)\). The kernel condition makes the map well-defined, and the remaining properties follow directly from the definition.

Remark

Remark. The first isomorphism theorem is rank-nullity for groups: quotient by what disappears, and what remains is exactly the image.
