10. Structure theorem over a PID

Structure theorem over a PID records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the abstract algebra note.

Definition

Definition. A finitely generated module over a PID is controlled by free rank and torsion elementary divisors or invariant factors.

Structure theorem

Theorem. Every finitely generated module over a PID decomposes as \(R^r\oplus\bigoplus_i R/(d_i)\), with divisibility relations among the invariant factors.

Proof

Proof. Present the module by generators and relations, then diagonalize the relation matrix using row and column operations allowed over a PID. The diagonal entries give the cyclic factors.

Remark

Remark. Jordan form and rational canonical form are module-structure theorems for \(F[x]\)-modules in disguise.
