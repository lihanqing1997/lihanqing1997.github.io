12. Splitting fields and normality

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/abstract-algebra/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/abstract-algebra/field-extensions/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/abstract-algebra/separable-extensions/" %}

Splitting fields and normality records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the abstract algebra note.

Definition

Definition. A splitting field of a polynomial over \(K\) is the smallest extension in which the polynomial factors completely into linear factors. An algebraic extension is normal if irreducible polynomials with one root in the extension split there.

Proposition

Proposition. Every polynomial has a splitting field, unique up to \(K\)-isomorphism. Finite normal extensions are exactly the fields stable under all embeddings into an algebraic closure.

Proof

Proof. Adjoin roots one at a time for existence. For uniqueness, extend isomorphisms inductively along minimal polynomials and roots.

Remark

Remark. Normality is the field-theoretic form of containing all conjugates. It is one of the two ingredients of being Galois.
