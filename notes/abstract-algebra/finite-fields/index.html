15. Finite fields

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/abstract-algebra/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/abstract-algebra/galois-groups-and-the-fundamental-theorem/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/abstract-algebra/solvable-groups-and-radicals/" %}

Finite fields are rigid: their size determines them up to isomorphism. Their structure combines field theory, cyclic groups, and Frobenius automorphisms.

Existence and uniqueness

Theorem. A finite field has \(p^n\) elements for some prime \(p\) and integer \(n\ge1\). Conversely, for every \(p^n\) there is a finite field with \(p^n\) elements, unique up to isomorphism.

Proof. The prime subfield is \(\mathbb F_p\), and the field is a finite-dimensional vector space over it, so its size is \(p^n\). Existence comes from the splitting field of \(x^{p^n}-x\), whose roots form a field. Uniqueness follows because any field of size \(p^n\) is exactly the set of roots of this polynomial.

Construction by irreducible polynomials

Proposition. If \(f\in\mathbb F_p[x]\) is irreducible of degree \(n\), then \(\mathbb F_p[x]/(f)\) is a field with \(p^n\) elements.

Example. Since \(x^2+x+1\) is irreducible over \(\mathbb F_2\), \[ \mathbb F_4\cong\mathbb F_2[x]/(x^2+x+1). \] If \(\alpha=x+(x^2+x+1)\), then \(\alpha^2=\alpha+1\).

Multiplicative group

Theorem. The multiplicative group \(\mathbb F_q^\times\) of a finite field is cyclic of order \(q-1\).

Remark. A generator of \(\mathbb F_q^\times\) is called a primitive element. This theorem is one of the most useful places where finite field theory uses finite abelian group structure.

Frobenius automorphism

Theorem. The Frobenius map \(\operatorname{Frob}_p:x\mapsto x^p\) is an automorphism of \(\mathbb F_{p^n}\). The Galois group \(\operatorname{Gal}(\mathbb F_{p^n}/\mathbb F_p)\) is cyclic of order \(n\), generated by Frobenius.

Proof. In characteristic \(p\), \((x+y)^p=x^p+y^p\), so Frobenius is a homomorphism. It is injective on a field, hence bijective because the field is finite. Its fixed field inside \(\mathbb F_{p^n}\) is \(\mathbb F_p\), and \(\operatorname{Frob}_p^n=1\).

Subfields

Proposition. The subfields of \(\mathbb F_{p^n}\) are exactly \(\mathbb F_{p^d}\) for \(d\mid n\).

Calculation. The field \(\mathbb F_{2^6}\) has subfields \(\mathbb F_2\), \(\mathbb F_4\), \(\mathbb F_8\), and \(\mathbb F_{64}\), corresponding to divisors \(1,2,3,6\) of \(6\).

Irreducible polynomials and roots

Proposition. An irreducible polynomial \(f\in\mathbb F_q[x]\) of degree \(d\) divides \(x^{q^n}-x\) iff \(d\mid n\). Equivalently, its roots lie in \(\mathbb F_{q^n}\) exactly when \(d\mid n\).
