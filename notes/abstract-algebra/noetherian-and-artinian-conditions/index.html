20. Noetherian and Artinian conditions

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/abstract-algebra/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/abstract-algebra/categories-functors-and-universal-properties/" %}

Noetherian and Artinian conditions records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the abstract algebra note.

Definition

Definition. A module is Noetherian if ascending chains of submodules stabilize, and Artinian if descending chains stabilize. Rings inherit these names through their module structure over themselves.

Proposition

Proposition. For modules, Noetherian is equivalent to every submodule being finitely generated. Many finiteness theorems in commutative algebra begin with this equivalence.

Proof

Proof. If ascending chains stabilize, build a submodule from an arbitrary generating set and show finitely many generators suffice. Conversely, a union of an ascending chain is finitely generated, so the chain stabilizes.

Remark

Remark. Chain conditions are compactness principles for algebra. They prevent infinite algebraic complexity from escaping every finite stage.
