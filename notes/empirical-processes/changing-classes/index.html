Many applications use function classes \(\mathcal F_n\) that change with \(n\): shrinking neighborhoods, sieve spaces, local alternatives, or estimated nuisance classes.

Definition. A changing class result controls \(\sup_{f\in\mathcal F_n}|(P_n-P)f|\) or \(\sup_{f\in\mathcal F_n}|\mathbb G_nf|\) with entropy and envelope bounds that may depend on \(n\).

Triangular-array complexity

  • Shrinking classes often make stochastic terms smaller than the global bound.
  • Growing sieve dimensions trade approximation bias against stochastic complexity.
  • Triangular-array empirical processes require checking both entropy and Lindeberg-type envelope conditions.

Local neighborhoods

Changing classes are the local language behind rates, nonstandard limits, sieve estimators, and bootstrap processes under drifting models.

Changing-class maximal bound

Proposition. For \(\mathcal F_n\), entropy, envelope, and variance bounds may all depend on \(n\). A useful check is whether the maximal inequality still gives \(E^*\|\mathbb G_n\|_{\mathcal F_n}=o(r_n)\) or \(O(r_n)\) at the scale required by the application.

Shrinking neighborhoods

Local asymptotic arguments often use \(\mathcal F_n=\{f_\theta-f_{\theta_0}:d(\theta,\theta_0)\le Rr_n^{-1}\}\). The shrinking radius reduces stochastic variation and reveals the correct rate or limiting process.
