Empirical Measures and Glivenko-Cantelli

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/empirical-processes/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/empirical-processes/donsker-and-gaussian-limits/" %}

Let \(X_1,\ldots,X_n\) be i.i.d. from \(P\). The empirical measure is \(P_n=n^{-1}\sum_{i=1}^n\delta_{X_i}\). For a measurable \(f\), \(P_nf=n^{-1}\sum_if(X_i)\) and \(Pf=Ef(X)\).

Uniform laws of large numbers

A class \(\mathcal F\) is \(P\)-Glivenko-Cantelli if

\[ \|P_n-P\|_{\mathcal F}:=\sup_{f\in\mathcal F}|P_nf-Pf|\to0 \]

in probability or almost surely. This strengthens the ordinary law of large numbers from each fixed \(f\) to all \(f\in\mathcal F\) at once.

Distribution functions

For \(\mathcal F=\{1_{(-\infty,t]}:t\in\mathbb R\}\), \(P_nf=F_n(t)\) and \(Pf=F(t)\). The classical Glivenko-Cantelli theorem says

\[ \sup_t|F_n(t)-F(t)|\to0 \]

almost surely. It is the prototype of all uniform laws.

Finite classes

If \(\mathcal F=\{f_1,\ldots,f_N\}\) is finite and \(|f|\le M\), Hoeffding's inequality and a union bound give

\[ P\left(\sup_{f\in\mathcal F}|P_nf-Pf|>t\right) \le 2N\exp\left(-\frac{nt^2}{2M^2}\right). \]

This is the simplest uniform law: complexity enters through \(\log N\). Entropy methods replace \(N\) by the number of balls needed to approximate an infinite class.

Why size matters

If \(\mathcal F\) is too rich, \(\sup_{f\in\mathcal F}|P_nf-Pf|\) may not vanish. For example, if \(\mathcal F\) contains indicators of all measurable sets, then \(P_nA\) can be \(1\) for a set containing all observations while \(PA\) is small. Uniform convergence requires structural restrictions: monotonicity, smoothness, finite VC dimension, finite entropy, or compactness.

Measurability and outer probability

For very large classes, the supremum over \(\mathcal F\) may not be measurable. Formal empirical-process theory often uses outer probability and outer expectation. In ordinary statistical applications, separability or countable approximating subclasses usually remove this technical issue.

Use in consistency

If an estimator maximizes \(P_nm_\theta\), and \(\mathcal M=\{m_\theta:\theta\in\Theta\}\) is Glivenko-Cantelli, then \(P_nm_\theta\) is uniformly close to \(Pm_\theta\). If \(Pm_\theta\) has a unique maximizer \(\theta_0\), the maximizer of \(P_nm_\theta\) must be close to \(\theta_0\). This is the empirical-process version of the argmax theorem.

Mesures Empiriques et Glivenko-Cantelli

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Retour" back_url="/notes/empirical-processes/" next_label="Page suivante" next_url="/notes/empirical-processes/donsker-and-gaussian-limits/" %}

Soient \(X_1,\ldots,X_n\) i.i.d. de loi \(P\). La mesure empirique est \(P_n=n^{-1}\sum_{i=1}^n\delta_{X_i}\). Pour une fonction mesurable \(f\), \(P_nf=n^{-1}\sum_if(X_i)\) et \(Pf=Ef(X)\).

Lois uniformes des grands nombres

Une classe \(\mathcal F\) est \(P\)-Glivenko-Cantelli si

\[ \|P_n-P\|_{\mathcal F}:=\sup_{f\in\mathcal F}|P_nf-Pf|\to0 \]

en probabilité ou presque sûrement. Cela renforce la loi des grands nombres ordinaire en une convergence uniforme sur \(\mathcal F\).

Fonctions de répartition

Pour \(\mathcal F=\{1_{(-\infty,t]}:t\in\mathbb R\}\), \(P_nf=F_n(t)\) et \(Pf=F(t)\). Le théorème classique de Glivenko-Cantelli affirme

\[ \sup_t|F_n(t)-F(t)|\to0 \]

presque sûrement.

Classes finies

Si \(\mathcal F=\{f_1,\ldots,f_N\}\) est finie et \(|f|\le M\), l'inégalité de Hoeffding et une borne d'union donnent

\[ P\left(\sup_{f\in\mathcal F}|P_nf-Pf|>t\right) \le 2N\exp\left(-\frac{nt^2}{2M^2}\right). \]

La complexité apparaît donc par \(\log N\). L'entropie remplace \(N\) pour les classes infinies.

Pourquoi la taille compte

Si \(\mathcal F\) est trop riche, la convergence uniforme peut échouer. Il faut des restrictions structurelles : monotonie, régularité, dimension VC finie, entropie finie ou compacité.

Mesurabilité

Pour des classes très grandes, le supremum peut ne pas être mesurable. La théorie formelle utilise parfois probabilité et espérance extérieures; dans les applications, des sous-classes séparantes dénombrables suffisent souvent.

Consistance

Si un estimateur maximise \(P_nm_\theta\), et si \(\{m_\theta:\theta\in\Theta\}\) est Glivenko-Cantelli, alors \(P_nm_\theta\) est uniformément proche de \(Pm_\theta\). Si \(Pm_\theta\) a un unique maximiseur \(\theta_0\), le maximiseur empirique converge vers \(\theta_0\).

经验测度与 Glivenko-Cantelli

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="返回" back_url="/notes/empirical-processes/" next_label="下一页" next_url="/notes/empirical-processes/donsker-and-gaussian-limits/" %}

设 \(X_1,\ldots,X_n\) 独立同分布于 \(P\)。经验测度为 \(P_n=n^{-1}\sum_{i=1}^n\delta_{X_i}\)。对可测函数 \(f\)，\(P_nf=n^{-1}\sum_if(X_i)\)，而 \(Pf=Ef(X)\)。

统一大数定律

若

\[ \|P_n-P\|_{\mathcal F}:=\sup_{f\in\mathcal F}|P_nf-Pf|\to0, \]

则称 \(\mathcal F\) 是 \(P\)-Glivenko-Cantelli 类。它把固定函数的大数定律加强为对整个函数类的一致收敛。

分布函数

取 \(\mathcal F=\{1_{(-\infty,t]}:t\in\mathbb R\}\)，则 \(P_nf=F_n(t)\)，\(Pf=F(t)\)。经典 Glivenko-Cantelli 定理说明

\[ \sup_t|F_n(t)-F(t)|\to0 \]

几乎必然成立。

有限函数类

若 \(\mathcal F=\{f_1,\ldots,f_N\}\) 有限且 \(|f|\le M\)，Hoeffding 不等式和 union bound 给出

\[ P\left(\sup_{f\in\mathcal F}|P_nf-Pf|>t\right) \le 2N\exp\left(-\frac{nt^2}{2M^2}\right). \]

这是最简单的统一大数定律：复杂度通过 \(\log N\) 进入。熵方法则用覆盖数替代无限类中的 \(N\)。

函数类大小为什么重要

若 \(\mathcal F\) 太大，一致收敛可能失败。例如包含所有可测集合指标函数的类太丰富。为了得到统一收敛，需要单调性、光滑性、有限 VC 维、有限熵或紧性等结构。

可测性问题

对很大的函数类，\(\sup_{f\in\mathcal F}\) 可能不可测。严格的经验过程理论常使用外概率和外期望。在常见统计应用中，可分性或可数逼近子类通常可以避免这个技术问题。

在一致性中的作用

若估计量最大化 \(P_nm_\theta\)，且 \(\{m_\theta:\theta\in\Theta\}\) 是 Glivenko-Cantelli 类，则 \(P_nm_\theta\) 与 \(Pm_\theta\) 一致接近。若 \(Pm_\theta\) 有唯一最大点 \(\theta_0\)，则经验最大点必须接近 \(\theta_0\)。
