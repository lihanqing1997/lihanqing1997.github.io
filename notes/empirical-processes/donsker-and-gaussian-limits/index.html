Donsker Classes and Gaussian Limits

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/empirical-processes/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/empirical-processes/empirical-measures-and-gc/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/empirical-processes/entropy-and-maximal-inequalities/" %}

The empirical process indexed by \(\mathcal F\) is \(\mathbb G_nf=\sqrt n(P_n-P)f\). A class \(\mathcal F\) is \(P\)-Donsker if \(\mathbb G_n\) converges weakly in \(\ell^\infty(\mathcal F)\) to a tight Gaussian process \(\mathbb G\).

Definition. \(\mathcal F\) is \(P\)-Donsker if \(\mathbb G_n\rightsquigarrow\mathbb G\) in \(\ell^\infty(\mathcal F)\), where \(\mathbb G\) is a tight mean-zero Gaussian process with covariance \(P(fg)-Pf\,Pg\).

Covariance structure

The limiting Gaussian process has mean zero and covariance

\[ \operatorname{Cov}\{\mathbb Gf,\mathbb Gg\}=P(fg)-Pf\,Pg. \]

For a finite class \(\mathcal F=\{f_1,\ldots,f_k\}\), this is just the multivariate CLT. Donsker theory asks when a whole infinite class behaves like a finite-dimensional vector.

Brownian bridge example

For \(\mathcal F=\{1_{(-\infty,t]}:t\in\mathbb R\}\), \(\mathbb G_n(t)=\sqrt n(F_n(t)-F(t))\). If \(F\) is continuous, then \(\mathbb G_n(t)\) converges to \(B(F(t))\), where \(B\) is a Brownian bridge with covariance \(\operatorname{Cov}(B(u),B(v))=\min(u,v)-uv\).

Tightness intuition

Finite-dimensional convergence is not enough. Tightness prevents the process from oscillating too violently over nearby functions. One usually proves tightness by showing that \(d(f,g)=\{P(f-g)^2\}^{1/2}\)-nearby functions have small empirical-process increments.

Asymptotic equicontinuity

Theorem. If the finite-dimensional distributions of \(\mathbb G_n\) converge to those of \(\mathbb G\), and \(\mathbb G_n\) is asymptotically equicontinuous for \(d(f,g)=\{P(f-g)^2\}^{1/2}\), then \(\mathbb G_n\rightsquigarrow\mathbb G\) in \(\ell^\infty(\mathcal F)\).

A useful equivalent form of tightness is asymptotic equicontinuity: for every \(\varepsilon,\eta>0\), there is \(\delta>0\) such that

\[ \limsup_nP\left\{ \sup_{d(f,g)<\delta}|\mathbb G_n(f-g)|>\varepsilon \right\}<\eta. \]

Together with finite-dimensional convergence, this yields weak convergence in \(\ell^\infty(\mathcal F)\). Intuitively, the process is determined by a finite net plus small local oscillations.

Permanence properties

Donsker classes are stable under many operations. If \(\mathcal F\) is Donsker and \(\phi:\mathbb R\to\mathbb R\) is Lipschitz, then \(\phi\circ\mathcal F\) is often Donsker under boundedness conditions. Finite unions and sums of Donsker classes are Donsker. These closure rules make the theory usable in semiparametric models, where influence functions are built from simpler components.

Consequences

Donsker convergence gives distributional limits for suprema, such as \(\sup_{f\in\mathcal F}|\mathbb G_nf|\). It underlies Kolmogorov-Smirnov tests, confidence bands, and the asymptotic distribution of estimators whose linearizations involve a process rather than one fixed function.

Classes de Donsker et Limites Gaussiennes

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Retour" back_url="/notes/empirical-processes/" prev_label="Page précédente" prev_url="/notes/empirical-processes/empirical-measures-and-gc/" next_label="Page suivante" next_url="/notes/empirical-processes/entropy-and-maximal-inequalities/" %}

Le processus empirique indexé par \(\mathcal F\) est \(\mathbb G_nf=\sqrt n(P_n-P)f\). Une classe \(\mathcal F\) est \(P\)-Donsker si \(\mathbb G_n\) converge faiblement dans \(\ell^\infty(\mathcal F)\) vers un processus gaussien tendu \(\mathbb G\).

Définition. \(\mathcal F\) est \(P\)-Donsker si \(\mathbb G_n\rightsquigarrow\mathbb G\) dans \(\ell^\infty(\mathcal F)\), où \(\mathbb G\) est un processus gaussien centré et tendu de covariance \(P(fg)-Pf\,Pg\).

Covariance

La limite gaussienne est centrée, de covariance

\[ \operatorname{Cov}\{\mathbb Gf,\mathbb Gg\}=P(fg)-Pf\,Pg. \]

Pour une classe finie, c'est simplement le TCL multivarié. La théorie de Donsker étend cette idée aux classes infinies.

Pont brownien

Pour \(\mathcal F=\{1_{(-\infty,t]}:t\in\mathbb R\}\), \(\mathbb G_n(t)=\sqrt n(F_n(t)-F(t))\). Si \(F\) est continue, la limite est \(B(F(t))\), où \(B\) est un pont brownien.

Intuition de la tension

La convergence des lois finies ne suffit pas. La tension empêche le processus d'osciller trop fortement entre fonctions proches. On contrôle donc les incréments pour la distance \(d(f,g)=\{P(f-g)^2\}^{1/2}\).

Équicontinuité asymptotique

Théorème. La convergence des lois finies, ajoutée à l'équicontinuité asymptotique pour \(d(f,g)=\{P(f-g)^2\}^{1/2}\), donne la convergence faible dans \(\ell^\infty(\mathcal F)\).

Une forme utile de tension est l'équicontinuité asymptotique : pour tout \(\varepsilon,\eta>0\), il existe \(\delta>0\) tel que

\[ \limsup_nP\left\{ \sup_{d(f,g)<\delta}|\mathbb G_n(f-g)|>\varepsilon \right\}<\eta. \]

Avec la convergence des lois finies, elle donne la convergence faible dans \(\ell^\infty(\mathcal F)\).

Propriétés de stabilité

Les classes de Donsker sont stables sous plusieurs opérations : unions finies, sommes, et transformations lipschitziennes sous des conditions de bornitude. Ces règles permettent de construire des limites gaussiennes à partir de blocs simples.

Conséquences

La convergence de Donsker donne des limites pour \(\sup_{f\in\mathcal F}|\mathbb G_nf|\), donc pour les tests de Kolmogorov-Smirnov, les bandes de confiance et de nombreux estimateurs fonctionnels.

Donsker 类与高斯极限

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="返回" back_url="/notes/empirical-processes/" prev_label="上一页" prev_url="/notes/empirical-processes/empirical-measures-and-gc/" next_label="下一页" next_url="/notes/empirical-processes/entropy-and-maximal-inequalities/" %}

由 \(\mathcal F\) 索引的经验过程为 \(\mathbb G_nf=\sqrt n(P_n-P)f\)。若 \(\mathbb G_n\) 在 \(\ell^\infty(\mathcal F)\) 中弱收敛到紧的高斯过程 \(\mathbb G\)，则称 \(\mathcal F\) 是 \(P\)-Donsker 类。

定义. 若 \(\mathbb G_n\rightsquigarrow\mathbb G\) 于 \(\ell^\infty(\mathcal F)\)，其中 \(\mathbb G\) 是均值零、紧的高斯过程，协方差为 \(P(fg)-Pf\,Pg\)，则称 \(\mathcal F\) 是 \(P\)-Donsker 类。

协方差结构

极限高斯过程均值为零，协方差为

\[ \operatorname{Cov}\{\mathbb Gf,\mathbb Gg\}=P(fg)-Pf\,Pg. \]

若 \(\mathcal F\) 有限，这只是多元 CLT。Donsker 理论研究无限函数类何时仍然像有限维向量一样收敛。

Brownian bridge 例子

对 \(\mathcal F=\{1_{(-\infty,t]}:t\in\mathbb R\}\)，有 \(\mathbb G_n(t)=\sqrt n(F_n(t)-F(t))\)。若 \(F\) 连续，则极限为 \(B(F(t))\)，其中 \(B\) 是 Brownian bridge，协方差为 \(\min(u,v)-uv\)。

紧性的直觉

有限维分布收敛还不够。紧性阻止过程在相近函数之间剧烈振荡。通常需要证明在 \(d(f,g)=\{P(f-g)^2\}^{1/2}\) 下相近的函数，其经验过程增量也小。

渐近等连续性

定理. 若 \(\mathbb G_n\) 的有限维分布收敛到 \(\mathbb G\) 的有限维分布，并且 \(\mathbb G_n\) 对半度量 \(d(f,g)=\{P(f-g)^2\}^{1/2}\) 渐近等连续，则 \(\mathbb G_n\rightsquigarrow\mathbb G\) 于 \(\ell^\infty(\mathcal F)\)。

紧性的一个常用等价形式是渐近等连续性：对任意 \(\varepsilon,\eta>0\)，存在 \(\delta>0\)，使得

\[ \limsup_nP\left\{ \sup_{d(f,g)<\delta}|\mathbb G_n(f-g)|>\varepsilon \right\}<\eta. \]

它与有限维分布收敛合在一起，可推出 \(\ell^\infty(\mathcal F)\) 中的弱收敛。直觉上，过程由有限网格和小的局部振荡共同控制。

稳定性质

Donsker 类在许多操作下保持稳定。有限并、和，以及有界条件下的 Lipschitz 变换，通常仍给出 Donsker 类。这些闭包性质使理论可以用于半参数模型，因为复杂影响函数常由简单成分组合而成。

后果

Donsker 收敛给出 \(\sup_{f\in\mathcal F}|\mathbb G_nf|\) 等泛函的分布极限，支撑 Kolmogorov-Smirnov 检验、置信带以及许多泛函估计量的渐近分布。
