10. Weak and weak-star topologies

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/functional-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/functional-analysis/dual-spaces-and-annihilators/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/functional-analysis/banach-alaoglu-theorem/" %}

Weak and weak-star topologies records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the functional analysis note.

Definition

Definition. The weak topology on \(X\) is the coarsest topology making every \(f\in X^*\) continuous. The weak-star topology on \(X^*\) is pointwise convergence on \(X\).

Proposition

Proposition. Weak convergence \(x_n\rightharpoonup x\) means \(f(x_n)\to f(x)\) for every \(f\in X^*\). Weak-star convergence of \(f_n\) means \(f_n(x)\to f(x)\) for every \(x\in X\).

Proof

Proof. These are initial topologies generated by families of scalar maps, so convergence is exactly pointwise convergence through those maps.

Remark

Remark. Weak topologies trade metric strength for compactness. This is crucial in PDE, optimization, probability, and statistics.
