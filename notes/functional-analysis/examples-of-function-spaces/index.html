3. Examples of function spaces

Continuous functions

Example. For compact \(K\), \(C(K)\) with \(\|f\|_\infty=\sup_K|f|\) is Banach. Completeness follows because a uniform limit of continuous functions is continuous.

Lebesgue spaces

Example. For a measure space \((X,\mathcal A,\mu)\), \(L^p(\mu)\) is Banach for \(1\le p\le\infty\). The case \(p=2\) is Hilbert with inner product \(\langle f,g\rangle=\int f\overline g\,\mathrm{d}\mu\).

Hölder and Minkowski inequalities

Theorem. Hölder's inequality controls products in dual exponents, and Minkowski's inequality gives the triangle inequality in \(L^p\). These inequalities are the analytic foundation of the \(L^p\) norm.

Spaces of differentiable functions

Example. The space \(C^k([a,b])\) becomes Banach under \(\|f\|=\sum_{j=0}^k\|f^{(j)}\|_\infty\). Such norms remember finitely many derivatives and prepare for Sobolev spaces.

Choice of norm matters

Remark. The same algebraic vector space can have different useful completions under different norms. Functional analysis tracks which topology matches the analytic problem.
