14. Riesz representation theorem

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/functional-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/functional-analysis/hilbert-spaces-and-orthogonal-projection/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/functional-analysis/compact-operators/" %}

Riesz representation theorem records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the functional analysis note.

Definition

Definition. In a Hilbert space, every continuous linear functional is inner product with a unique vector.

Riesz representation theorem

Theorem. For every \(f\in H^*\), there exists a unique \(y\in H\) such that \(f(x)=\langle x,y\rangle\) for all \(x\in H\), and \(\|f\|=\|y\|\).

Proof

Proof. If \(f\ne0\), project onto the orthogonal complement of \(\ker f\) to find the representing direction. Uniqueness follows from nondegeneracy of the inner product.

Remark

Remark. Riesz representation makes Hilbert spaces self-dual, but the identification depends on the inner product.
