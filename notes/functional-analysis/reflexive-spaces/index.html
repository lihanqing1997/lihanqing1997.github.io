13. Reflexive spaces

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/functional-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/functional-analysis/banach-alaoglu-theorem/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/functional-analysis/hilbert-spaces-and-orthogonal-projection/" %}

Canonical embedding

Definition. The canonical map \(J:X\to X^{**}\) is defined by \(Jx(f)=f(x)\). It is always an isometry. The space \(X\) is reflexive if \(J\) is onto.

Weak compactness criterion

Theorem. A Banach space is reflexive if and only if its closed unit ball is weakly compact. One direction uses Banach-Alaoglu in \(X^{**}\); the other uses separation to show the canonical image fills \(X^{**}\).

Closed subspaces and quotients

Proposition. Closed subspaces and quotients of reflexive Banach spaces are reflexive. This stability makes reflexivity a robust compactness property.

Existence of minimizers

Corollary. In reflexive spaces, coercive weakly lower semicontinuous convex functionals often attain minima. Weak compactness replaces finite-dimensional compactness in direct methods.
