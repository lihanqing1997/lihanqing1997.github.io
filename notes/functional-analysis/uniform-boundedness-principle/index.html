5. Uniform boundedness principle

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/functional-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/functional-analysis/baire-category-theorem/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/functional-analysis/open-mapping-and-inverse-theorems/" %}

Uniform boundedness principle records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the functional analysis note.

Definition

Definition. The uniform boundedness principle turns pointwise boundedness of a family of continuous linear operators into uniform operator-norm boundedness.

Uniform boundedness principle

Theorem. If \(X\) is Banach and \(\mathcal F\subset\mathcal L(X,Y)\) satisfies \(\sup_{T\in\mathcal F}\|Tx\|<\infty\) for every \(x\in X\), then \(\sup_{T\in\mathcal F}\|T\|<\infty\).

Proof

Proof. Apply Baire category to the closed sets \(E_n=\{x:\sup_T\|Tx\|\le n\}\). One has nonempty interior, and scaling turns local boundedness into global operator-norm boundedness.

Remark

Remark. The theorem is a warning: in Banach spaces, pointwise estimates are often secretly uniform if they hold everywhere.
