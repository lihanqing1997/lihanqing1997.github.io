11. Banach-Alaoglu theorem

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/functional-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/functional-analysis/weak-and-weak-star-topologies/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/functional-analysis/reflexive-spaces/" %}

Banach-Alaoglu theorem records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the functional analysis note.

Definition

Definition. Banach-Alaoglu states that the closed unit ball of the dual space is compact in the weak-star topology.

Banach-Alaoglu theorem

Theorem. For any normed space \(X\), the set \(\{f\in X^*:\|f\|\le1\}\) is weak-star compact.

Proof

Proof. Embed the dual unit ball into the product \(\prod_{x\in X}\{z:|z|\le\|x\|\}\) by evaluation. Tychonoff gives product compactness, and the dual ball is closed by linearity conditions.

Remark

Remark. This is compactness without norm compactness. It is one reason topological methods are unavoidable in infinite-dimensional analysis.
