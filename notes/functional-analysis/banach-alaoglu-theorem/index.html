12. Banach-Alaoglu theorem

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/functional-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/functional-analysis/weak-and-weak-star-topologies/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/functional-analysis/reflexive-spaces/" %}

Compact dual ball

Theorem. The closed unit ball \(B_{X^*}\) is compact in the weak-star topology \(\sigma(X^*,X)\). This is Banach-Alaoglu, one of the main compactness theorems in functional analysis.

Product compactness proof

Proof. Embed \(B_{X^*}\) into \(\prod_{x\in X}\{\lambda:|\lambda|\le\|x\|\}\) by evaluation. Tychonoff's theorem makes the product compact, and the conditions of linearity are closed, so the image is compact.

Metrizability on separable spaces

Proposition. If \(X\) is separable, the weak-star topology on bounded subsets of \(X^*\) is metrizable. A countable dense subset of \(X\) supplies countably many evaluation seminorms.

Weak compactness in reflexive spaces

Corollary. If \(X\) is reflexive, then the closed unit ball of \(X\) is weakly compact. Identify \(X\) with \(X^{**}\) and apply Banach-Alaoglu.

Measure compactness

Example. For \(X=C(K)\), Banach-Alaoglu gives weak-star compactness of bounded sets of finite measures. This is the functional-analytic seed of compactness results for probability measures.

Compactness without sequences

Remark. Banach-Alaoglu is often a net compactness theorem. Sequential compactness requires additional separability or metrizability hypotheses.
