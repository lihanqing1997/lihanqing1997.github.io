9. Dual spaces and annihilators

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/functional-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/functional-analysis/hahn-banach-theorem/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/functional-analysis/weak-and-weak-star-topologies/" %}

Dual spaces and annihilators records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the functional analysis note.

Definition

Definition. The dual \(X^*\) is the Banach space of continuous linear functionals on \(X\). The annihilator of \(M\subset X\) is \(M^\perp=\{f\in X^*:f(m)=0\text{ for all }m\in M\}\).

Proposition

Proposition. For a closed subspace \(M\), the dual of \(X/M\) is isometrically identifiable with \(M^\perp\).

Proof

Proof. A functional on \(X/M\) pulls back to a functional on \(X\) vanishing on \(M\). Conversely, any functional vanishing on \(M\) is constant on cosets and descends to the quotient.

Remark

Remark. Annihilators are the exact-sequence language of functional analysis. They make quotient spaces and constraints dual to each other.
