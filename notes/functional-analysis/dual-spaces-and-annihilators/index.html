10. Dual spaces and annihilators

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/functional-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/functional-analysis/separation-of-convex-sets/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/functional-analysis/weak-and-weak-star-topologies/" %}

Continuous dual

Definition. The dual space \(X^*\) is the Banach space of continuous linear functionals \(f:X\to\mathbb F\), with norm \(\|f\|=\sup_{\|x\|\le1}|f(x)|\).

Annihilators

Definition. For \(M\subset X\), define \(M^\perp=\{f\in X^*:f(x)=0\text{ for all }x\in M\}\). For \(N\subset X^*\), define \({}^\perp N=\{x\in X:f(x)=0\text{ for all }f\in N\}\).

Closed subspaces from annihilators

Proposition. The annihilator \(M^\perp\) is always weak-star closed in \(X^*\), and \({}^\perp(M^\perp)=\overline{\operatorname{span}M}\). Hahn-Banach proves the reverse inclusion by separating points outside the closure.

Dual of a quotient

Theorem. If \(M\) is a closed subspace of \(X\), then \((X/M)^*\) is isometrically isomorphic to \(M^\perp\). A functional on the quotient corresponds to a functional on \(X\) vanishing on \(M\).

Dual of a subspace

Theorem. The dual \(M^*\) is isometrically isomorphic to \(X^*/M^\perp\) in the natural restriction sense. Hahn-Banach ensures every functional on \(M\) extends to \(X\).
