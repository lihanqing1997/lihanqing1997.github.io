13. Hilbert spaces and orthogonal projection

Hilbert spaces and orthogonal projection records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the functional analysis note.

Definition

Definition. A Hilbert space is a complete inner-product space. Orthogonality and projection make Hilbert spaces the infinite-dimensional continuation of Euclidean geometry.

Projection theorem

Theorem. If \(C\) is a nonempty closed convex subset of a Hilbert space, every \(x\) has a unique nearest point in \(C\). For closed subspaces this gives orthogonal decomposition \(H=M\oplus M^\perp\).

Proof

Proof. Take a minimizing sequence, use the parallelogram identity to prove it is Cauchy, and use closedness for existence. The variational inequality gives uniqueness.

Remark

Remark. Projection is the geometric source of least squares, conditional expectation, Galerkin methods, and Hilbert-space decompositions.
