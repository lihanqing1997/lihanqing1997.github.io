19. Sobolev spaces

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/functional-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/functional-analysis/distributions-and-weak-derivatives/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/functional-analysis/semigroups-of-operators/" %}

Sobolev spaces records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the functional analysis note.

Definition

Definition. Sobolev spaces \(W^{k,p}\) consist of \(L^p\) functions whose weak derivatives up to order \(k\) are also in \(L^p\).

Proposition

Proposition. Sobolev embedding theorems convert integrability of weak derivatives into improved integrability, continuity, or compactness, depending on dimension and smoothness.

Proof

Proof. The proof combines approximation by smooth functions, integral representation estimates, interpolation, and covering arguments. Compact embeddings require localization plus equicontinuity or tightness.

Remark

Remark. Sobolev spaces are the natural energy spaces of PDE. They also clarify what boundary values and weak solutions mean.
