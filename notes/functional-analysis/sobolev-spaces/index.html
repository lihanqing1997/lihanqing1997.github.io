24. Sobolev spaces

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/functional-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/functional-analysis/distributions-and-weak-derivatives/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/functional-analysis/semigroups-of-operators/" %}

Sobolev spaces

Definition. For an open set \(U\subset\mathbb R^d\), \(W^{k,p}(U)\) consists of functions \(f\in L^p(U)\) whose weak derivatives \(\partial^\alpha f\) lie in \(L^p(U)\) for \(|\alpha|\le k\).

Norm and completeness

Theorem. With \(\|f\|_{W^{k,p}}=\sum_{|\alpha|\le k}\|\partial^\alpha f\|_{L^p}\), the space \(W^{k,p}(U)\) is Banach. For \(p=2\), \(H^k(U)=W^{k,2}(U)\) is Hilbert.

Density of smooth functions

Theorem. Under standard domain hypotheses, smooth functions are dense in Sobolev spaces, often with compact support after imposing zero boundary conditions. Mollifiers provide the local approximation mechanism.

Sobolev embedding

Theorem. Sobolev embedding theorems convert weak differentiability and integrability into improved integrability or continuity. The relation between \(k\), \(p\), and dimension \(d\) determines the target regularity.

Poincaré inequality

Proposition. On suitable bounded domains, functions with zero boundary trace satisfy \(\|u\|_{L^p}\le C\|\nabla u\|_{L^p}\). This makes derivative norms control the whole Sobolev norm.

Variational equations

Example. Weak formulations of elliptic PDE ask for \(u\in H_0^1(U)\) such that \(\int \nabla u\cdot\nabla v\,\mathrm{d}x=\int fv\,\mathrm{d}x\) for all test functions \(v\). Functional analysis then supplies existence and uniqueness.
