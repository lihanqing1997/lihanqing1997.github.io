24. Sobolev spaces

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/functional-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/functional-analysis/distributions-and-weak-derivatives/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/functional-analysis/semigroups-of-operators/" %}

Weak differentiability

Definition. For open \(U\subset\mathbb R^d\), \(W^{k,p}(U)\) consists of \(f\in L^p(U)\) whose weak derivatives \(\partial^\alpha f\in L^p(U)\) for all \(|\alpha|\le k\). The norm is \(\|f\|_{W^{k,p}}=\sum_{|\alpha|\le k}\|\partial^\alpha f\|_{L^p}\). The space \(H^k(U)\) means \(W^{k,2}(U)\).

Completeness

Theorem. For \(1\le p\le\infty\), \(W^{k,p}(U)\) is Banach. For \(p=2\), \(H^k(U)\) is Hilbert with inner product \(\sum_{|\alpha|\le k}\langle\partial^\alpha f,\partial^\alpha g\rangle_{L^2}\).

Proof. Let \((f_n)\) be Cauchy in \(W^{k,p}\). Then for every \(|\alpha|\le k\), \((\partial^\alpha f_n)\) is Cauchy in \(L^p\), hence converges to some \(g_\alpha\in L^p\). Let \(f=g_0\). For every test function \(\varphi\), the weak derivative identity for \(f_n\) gives \(\int \partial^\alpha f_n\,\varphi=(-1)^{|\alpha|}\int f_n\,\partial^\alpha\varphi\). Passing to the \(L^p\)-limits using Holder duality with compactly supported smooth functions gives \(\int g_\alpha\varphi=(-1)^{|\alpha|}\int f\,\partial^\alpha\varphi\). Thus \(g_\alpha=\partial^\alpha f\), and \(f_n\to f\) in \(W^{k,p}\). The Hilbert claim for \(p=2\) follows from completeness and the displayed inner product.

Mollification

Proposition. If \(f\in W^{k,p}_{\mathrm{loc}}(U)\) and \(\rho_\varepsilon\) is a standard mollifier, then \(\rho_\varepsilon*f\in C^\infty\) on compact subsets \(K\Subset U\), and \(\partial^\alpha(\rho_\varepsilon*f)=\rho_\varepsilon*(\partial^\alpha f)\) for \(|\alpha|\le k\). Moreover \(\rho_\varepsilon*f\to f\) in \(W^{k,p}(K)\).

Proof. The derivative identity is the distributional convolution identity. Approximation in \(L^p(K)\) for each derivative follows from the standard \(L^p\)-continuity of translations and the fact that mollification is an average of small translations. Summing over \(|\alpha|\le k\) gives convergence in the Sobolev norm on compact subsets.

Poincare inequality on an interval

Theorem. If \(u\in W^{1,p}_0(0,L)\), \(1\le p\le\infty\), then \(\|u\|_{L^p(0,L)}\le L\|u'\|_{L^p(0,L)}\).

Proof. For smooth compactly supported \(u\), \(u(x)=\int_0^x u'(t)\,\mathrm{d}t\), so \(|u(x)|\le\int_0^L|u'(t)|\,\mathrm{d}t\). A sharper \(L^p\) estimate follows from Hardy's inequality, and the rough bound \(\|u\|_p\le L\|u'\|_p\) follows by Holder and integration. For general \(u\in W^{1,p}_0\), take smooth compactly supported approximations in \(W^{1,p}\) and pass to the limit.

One-dimensional Sobolev embedding

Theorem. If \(u\in W^{1,p}(a,b)\) with \(p>1\), then \(u\) has a Holder continuous representative with exponent \(1-1/p\), and \(|u(x)-u(y)|\le\|u'\|_{L^p}|x-y|^{1-1/p}\).

Proof. For smooth \(u\), \(u(x)-u(y)=\int_y^x u'(t)\,\mathrm{d}t\). Holder inequality gives the estimate. Approximate a Sobolev function by smooth functions locally or use the absolutely continuous representative theorem; the estimate passes to the limit and produces a continuous representative.

Weak elliptic formulation

Example. The Dirichlet problem \(-\Delta u=f\) on \(U\) is written: find \(u\in H_0^1(U)\) such that \(\int_U\nabla u\cdot\nabla v\,\mathrm{d}x=\int_Ufv\,\mathrm{d}x\) for all \(v\in H_0^1(U)\). Poincare gives coercivity of the left side, and Lax-Milgram gives existence and uniqueness when \(f\in H^{-1}(U)\).
