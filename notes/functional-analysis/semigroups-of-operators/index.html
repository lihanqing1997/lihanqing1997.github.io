25. Semigroups of operators

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/functional-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/functional-analysis/sobolev-spaces/" %}

Strongly continuous semigroups

Definition. A family \((T(t))_{t\ge0}\subset\mathcal L(X)\) is a \(C_0\)-semigroup if \(T(0)=I\), \(T(t+s)=T(t)T(s)\), and \(T(t)x\to x\) as \(t\downarrow0\) for every \(x\in X\).

Generator

Definition. The generator is \(Ax=\lim_{t\downarrow0}(T(t)x-x)/t\) on the set of \(x\) where this norm limit exists. The generator is usually unbounded but closed and densely defined.

Exponential model

Example. If \(A\in\mathcal L(X)\) is bounded, then \(T(t)=e^{tA}=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}t^nA^n/n!\) is a \(C_0\)-semigroup with generator \(A\).

Hille-Yosida philosophy

Theorem. The Hille-Yosida theorem characterizes generators of contraction semigroups by resolvent estimates. It turns an evolution problem \(u'(t)=Au(t)\) into estimates for \((\lambda I-A)^{-1}\).

Heat semigroup

Example. On suitable \(L^p\) spaces, the heat equation is governed by a semigroup generated by the Laplacian. Smoothing for positive time reflects analytic properties of the semigroup.

Evolution viewpoint

Remark. Semigroup theory is functional analysis for time evolution: differential equations become operator exponentials, and domain questions encode boundary conditions and regularity.
