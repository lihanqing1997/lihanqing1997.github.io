20. Semigroups of operators

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/functional-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/functional-analysis/sobolev-spaces/" %}

Semigroups of operators records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the functional analysis note.

Definition

Definition. A strongly continuous semigroup \((T_t)_{t\ge0}\) satisfies \(T_0=I\), \(T_{s+t}=T_sT_t\), and \(T_tx\to x\) as \(t\downarrow0\) for each \(x\).

Proposition

Proposition. The generator \(Ax=\lim_{t\downarrow0}(T_tx-x)/t\) encodes the evolution. Under Hille-Yosida type conditions, closed operators generate strongly continuous contraction semigroups.

Proof

Proof. Resolvent estimates provide approximating bounded operators; exponential formulas construct the semigroup; closedness identifies the generator.

Remark

Remark. Semigroups translate evolution equations into operator theory, linking functional analysis with ODE, PDE, and Markov processes.
