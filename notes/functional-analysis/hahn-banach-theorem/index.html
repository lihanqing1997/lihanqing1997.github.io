8. Hahn-Banach theorem

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/functional-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/functional-analysis/closed-graph-theorem/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/functional-analysis/dual-spaces-and-annihilators/" %}

Hahn-Banach theorem records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the functional analysis note.

Definition

Definition. Hahn-Banach extends bounded linear functionals from subspaces to the whole space without increasing the norm. It is the central extension theorem of functional analysis.

Hahn-Banach theorem

Theorem. If \(M\subset X\) and \(f\in M^*\), then there exists \(F\in X^*\) extending \(f\) with \(\|F\|=\|f\|\).

Proof

Proof. Extend one dimension at a time while respecting a sublinear domination inequality; Zorn's lemma produces a maximal extension, and maximality forces its domain to be all of \(X\).

Remark

Remark. Hahn-Banach creates enough continuous linear functionals to separate points, subspaces, and convex sets. It is duality's existence theorem.
