8. Hahn-Banach theorem

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/functional-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/functional-analysis/closed-graph-theorem/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/functional-analysis/separation-of-convex-sets/" %}

Dominated extension form

Theorem. Let \(p:X\to\mathbb R\) be sublinear and let \(f\) be linear on a subspace \(M\subset X\) with \(f(x)\le p(x)\). Then \(f\) extends to a linear functional \(F\) on \(X\) satisfying \(F(x)\le p(x)\) for all \(x\).

Norm-preserving extension

Corollary. If \(M\subset X\) is a subspace of a normed space and \(f\in M^*\), then there exists \(F\in X^*\) extending \(f\) with \(\|F\|=\|f\|\).

One-dimensional extension step

Proof. To extend from \(M\) to \(M+\mathbb R x_0\), choose \(\alpha=F(x_0)\) so that \(f(m)+t\alpha\le p(m+tx_0)\) for all \(m\in M\) and real \(t\). Sublinearity gives a nonempty interval of allowable \(\alpha\).

Zorn lemma completion

Proof. Partially order dominated extensions by inclusion of domains. Chains have upper bounds by union. Zorn's lemma gives a maximal dominated extension; if the domain were not all of \(X\), the one-dimensional step would extend it further, a contradiction.

Point separation

Corollary. For every nonzero \(x\in X\), there exists \(f\in X^*\) with \(\|f\|=1\) and \(f(x)=\|x\|\). Thus the dual separates points and recovers the norm by \(\|x\|=\sup_{\|f\|\le1}|f(x)|\).

Existence theorem

Remark. Hahn-Banach is not constructive in general. Its role is to guarantee enough continuous linear functionals for duality, weak topology, and separation arguments.
