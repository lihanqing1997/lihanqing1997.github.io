17. Unbounded operators

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/functional-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/functional-analysis/spectral-theory-for-compact-self-adjoint-operators/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/functional-analysis/distributions-and-weak-derivatives/" %}

Unbounded operators records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the functional analysis note.

Definition

Definition. An unbounded operator is defined on a dense domain \(D(T)\subset H\), not necessarily on all of \(H\). Differential operators are the main examples.

Proposition

Proposition. Closedness, symmetry, self-adjointness, and domain specification are part of the data of an unbounded operator. A symmetric operator need not be self-adjoint.

Proof

Proof. Graph norms track convergence of both \(x_n\) and \(Tx_n\). Self-adjointness is checked through equality of domains of the operator and its adjoint.

Remark

Remark. Many PDE and quantum-mechanical operators are unbounded. Ignoring the domain loses the operator.
