22. Locally convex spaces and seminorms

Seminorm families

Definition. A seminorm \(p\) on a vector space \(X\) satisfies \(p(x+y)\le p(x)+p(y)\) and \(p(\alpha x)=|\alpha|p(x)\), but may vanish on nonzero vectors. A family \(\mathcal P\) of seminorms separates points if for every \(x\ne0\) some \(p\in\mathcal P\) has \(p(x)>0\).

Proposition. A separating family \(\mathcal P\) defines a Hausdorff locally convex topology whose basic neighborhoods of \(0\) are \(\{x:p_i(x)<\varepsilon,\ i=1,\ldots,n\}\).

Proof. These sets are balanced, convex, and absorbing, and they are stable under finite intersections. Continuity of linear maps

Theorem. Let \(X\) be defined by seminorms \(\mathcal P\), and let \(Y\) be normed. A linear map \(T:X\to Y\) is continuous iff there exist \(p_1,\ldots,p_n\in\mathcal P\) and \(C<\infty\) such that \(\|Tx\|\le C\max_i p_i(x)\) for all \(x\in X\).

Proof. If the estimate holds, continuity at \(0\) is immediate. Conversely, continuity at \(0\) gives a basic neighborhood \(U=\{x:p_i(x)<\varepsilon,\ i=1,\ldots,n\}\) such that \(T(U)\subset B_Y(0,1)\). For \(x\ne0\), set \(q(x)=\max_i p_i(x)\). If \(q(x)>0\), then \(\varepsilon x/(2q(x))\in U\), so \(\|Tx\|\le2q(x)/\varepsilon\). If \(q(x)=0\), then \(tx\in U\) for every \(t>0\), so \(t\|Tx\|<1\) for all \(t\), forcing \(Tx=0\).

Continuous linear functionals

Corollary. A linear functional \(f:X\to\mathbb F\) is continuous iff it is dominated by a finite maximum of defining seminorms: \(|f(x)|\le C\max_{1\le i\le n}p_i(x)\).

Proof. Apply the preceding theorem with \(Y=\mathbb F\).

Weak topology as locally convex topology

Example. The weak topology \(\sigma(X,X^*)\) is generated by seminorms \(p_f(x)=|f(x)|\), \(f\in X^*\). The weak-star topology \(\sigma(X^*,X)\) is generated by seminorms \(p_x(f)=|f(x)|\), \(x\in X\).

Frechet spaces

Definition. A Frechet space is a complete metrizable locally convex space. If \((p_n)\) is a countable separating family of seminorms, a compatible translation-invariant metric is \(d(x,y)=\sum_{n=1}^\infty2^{-n}p_n(x-y)/(1+p_n(x-y))\).

Proposition. Convergence in this metric is exactly convergence in every seminorm \(p_n\).

Proof. If \(d(x_k,x)\to0\), each summand forces \(p_n(x_k-x)\to0\). Conversely, if every seminorm tends to \(0\), first choose \(N\) so \(\sum_{n>N}2^{-n}<\varepsilon/2\), then choose \(k\) so the first \(N\) summands are collectively below \(\varepsilon/2\).

Spaces of test functions

Remark. Distribution theory needs locally convex spaces because \(C_c^\infty(U)\) is not naturally Banach. Its topology records all derivatives on all compact subsets, and continuity of a distribution means estimates against finitely many such seminorms locally.
