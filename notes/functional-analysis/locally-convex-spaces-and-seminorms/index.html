22. Locally convex spaces and seminorms

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/functional-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/functional-analysis/unbounded-operators/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/functional-analysis/distributions-and-weak-derivatives/" %}

Locally convex spaces

Definition. A locally convex space is a topological vector space whose topology is generated by a family of seminorms. Neighborhoods of zero can be taken convex, balanced, and absorbing.

Seminorm families

Proposition. Given seminorms \((p_i)_{i\in I}\), the sets \(\{x:p_{i_k}(x)<\varepsilon,\ k=1,\dots,n\}\) form a neighborhood basis at zero. The topology is Hausdorff exactly when the seminorms separate points.

Continuous linear functionals

Proposition. A linear functional \(f\) is continuous if and only if \(|f(x)|\le C\max_{1\le k\le n}p_{i_k}(x)\) for finitely many seminorms. Thus continuity is always controlled by finitely many seminorms at a time.

Fréchet spaces

Definition. A Fréchet space is a complete metrizable locally convex space. Spaces of smooth functions with seminorms controlling derivatives on compact sets are central examples.

Test function spaces

Example. The space \(C_c^\infty(U)\) is locally convex but not Banach under a single natural norm. Its topology is designed so distributions are exactly continuous linear functionals on it.

Why this matters

Remark. Normed spaces are too rigid for many spaces in analysis. Locally convex spaces keep Hahn-Banach separation while allowing topologies built from many measurements.
