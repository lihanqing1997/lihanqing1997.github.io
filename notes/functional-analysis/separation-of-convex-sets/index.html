9. Separation of convex sets

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/functional-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/functional-analysis/hahn-banach-theorem/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/functional-analysis/dual-spaces-and-annihilators/" %}

Geometric Hahn-Banach

Theorem. Let \(C\) be a nonempty open convex subset of a real normed space and let \(x_0\notin C\). Then there exists a nonzero continuous linear functional \(f\) and a real \(\alpha\) such that \(f(x)<\alpha\le f(x_0)\) for all \(x\in C\).

Minkowski functional

Definition. For a convex absorbing set \(C\) containing \(0\), the Minkowski functional is \(p_C(x)=\inf\{t>0:x\in tC\}\). It is sublinear and converts geometry of \(C\) into a domination function for Hahn-Banach.

Supporting hyperplanes

Corollary. Closed convex sets can often be supported by continuous linear functionals at boundary points. A supporting functional exposes a linear inequality satisfied by the whole convex set.

Strict separation

Proposition. If one convex set is compact and the other is closed, disjoint, and convex in a locally convex setting, then they can be strictly separated under suitable hypotheses. Compactness supplies a positive gap after separation.

Dual certificates

Example. Separation theorems underlie duality in optimization: infeasibility or optimality can often be certified by a continuous linear functional separating a point from a convex attainable set.

Topology matters

Remark. Separation depends on having enough continuous linear functionals. Hahn-Banach is exactly the theorem that supplies them in normed spaces.
