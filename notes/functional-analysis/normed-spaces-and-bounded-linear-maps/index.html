1. Normed spaces and bounded linear maps

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/functional-analysis/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/functional-analysis/banach-spaces-and-completeness/" %}

Normed spaces and bounded linear maps records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the functional analysis note.

Definition

Definition. A normed space is a vector space with a norm. A linear map \(T:X\to Y\) is bounded if \(\|Tx\|\le C\|x\|\) for some \(C\), and its operator norm is the least such \(C\).

Proposition

Proposition. For linear maps between normed spaces, boundedness, continuity at zero, and continuity everywhere are equivalent.

Proof

Proof. Boundedness directly gives continuity. Continuity at zero controls \(Tx\) for small \(x\), and scaling converts local control into the global inequality.

Remark

Remark. Functional analysis begins when linear algebra is combined with topology. Boundedness is the compatibility condition between the two.
