Argument principle

Theorem. If \(f\) is meromorphic inside and on a contour \(\gamma\), with no zeros or poles on \(\gamma\), then \[ \frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_\gamma \frac{f'(z)}{f(z)}\,\mathrm{d}z=N-P, \] where zeros and poles inside are counted with multiplicity.

Proof through logarithmic derivative

Proof. Near a zero or pole of order \(m\), write \(f(z)=(z-a)^m g(z)\) with \(g(a)\ne0\), allowing \(m<0\) for poles. Then \(f'/f=m/(z-a)+g'/g\), so the residue of \(f'/f\) is \(m\).

Rouché theorem

Theorem. If \(f\) and \(g\) are holomorphic inside and on \(\gamma\) and \(|g|<|f|\) on \(\gamma\), then \(f\) and \(f+g\) have the same number of zeros inside \(\gamma\), counted with multiplicity.

Homotopy proof of Rouché

Proof. For \(0\le t\le1\), the functions \(f+tg\) do not vanish on \(\gamma\). Their winding numbers around zero are constant in \(t\), so the argument principle gives the same zero count for \(f\) and \(f+g\).

Polynomial example

Example. On \(|z|=2\), the term \(z^5\) dominates \(3z^2+1\). Hence \(z^5+3z^2+1\) has five zeros in \(|z|<2\). On smaller contours, a different dominant term may control a local zero count.

Winding interpretation

Remark. The argument principle says that zeros and poles measure how the image curve winds around the origin. Rouché's theorem is stability of that winding number under boundary-small perturbations.
