4. Cauchy theorem

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/complex-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/complex-analysis/contour-integrals/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/complex-analysis/cauchy-integral-formula/" %}

Cauchy theorem records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the complex analysis note.

Definition

Definition. Cauchy's theorem says that integrals of holomorphic functions around suitable closed curves vanish. The precise hypotheses may use simply connected domains, primitives, or homotopy.

Cauchy theorem

Theorem. If \(f\) is holomorphic on a simply connected domain, then \(\int_\gamma f(z)\,\mathrm{d}z=0\) for every closed contour \(\gamma\) in the domain.

Proof

Proof. One proof constructs a primitive by path integration and uses path independence. Another reduces to triangles and uses local estimates from complex differentiability.

Remark

Remark. Cauchy's theorem is the central reason complex analysis is rigid: closed-loop information disappears unless singularities are present.
