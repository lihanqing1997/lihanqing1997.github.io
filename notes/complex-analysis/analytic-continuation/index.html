16. Analytic continuation

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/complex-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/complex-analysis/harmonic-functions/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/complex-analysis/normal-families/" %}

Analytic continuation records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the complex analysis note.

Definition

Definition. Analytic continuation extends a holomorphic germ along paths by matching power series on overlapping disks.

Proposition

Proposition. Continuation along paths is unique when it exists, but the final value may depend on the homotopy class of the path if the domain has monodromy.

Proof

Proof. Uniqueness follows from the identity theorem on overlaps. Path dependence appears by continuing branches such as logarithm around loops.

Remark

Remark. Analytic continuation reveals that a holomorphic function is better understood as a germ plus its possible continuations, not just a formula on one domain.
