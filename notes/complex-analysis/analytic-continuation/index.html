Continuation along overlaps

Definition. An analytic continuation extends a holomorphic function from one domain to a larger connected domain so that the extensions agree on overlaps. The identity theorem makes such continuations unique whenever the overlap is connected and nonempty.

Continuation along chains

Proposition. A germ can be continued along a path by a chain of overlapping discs. At each step, the power series in the next disc is determined by agreement on the overlap.

Monodromy theorem

Theorem. If analytic continuation of a germ along paths in a simply connected domain is always possible, then the continuation is single-valued. Homotopic paths give the same branch by uniqueness and deformation through overlapping chains.

Branch phenomena

Example. The logarithm can be continued locally on \(\mathbb C\setminus\{0\}\), but continuation around a loop encircling zero changes the value by \(2\pi i\). A simply connected slit domain removes this ambiguity.

Natural boundaries

Remark. Some power series cannot be continued past any point of their circle of convergence. Analytic continuation is powerful, but singularities may form a genuine barrier.

Germs

Definition. A germ at \(a\) is an equivalence class of holomorphic functions agreeing on some neighborhood of \(a\). Analytic continuation is best understood as transporting germs along paths.
