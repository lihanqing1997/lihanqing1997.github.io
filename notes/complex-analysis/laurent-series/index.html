9. Laurent series

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/complex-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/complex-analysis/isolated-singularities/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/complex-analysis/residue-theorem/" %}

Laurent series records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the complex analysis note.

Definition

Definition. A Laurent series \(\sum_{n\in\mathbb Z}a_n(z-z_0)^n\) represents holomorphic functions on annuli and separates regular and principal parts.

Laurent expansion theorem

Theorem. A function holomorphic on an annulus has a unique Laurent expansion there, with coefficients given by contour integrals \(a_n=(2\pi i)^{-1}\int_C f(z)(z-z_0)^{-n-1}\,\mathrm{d}z\).

Proof

Proof. Apply Cauchy's formula on two boundary circles and expand the kernels into geometric series valid inside the annulus. Uniqueness follows from coefficient extraction by integration.

Remark

Remark. Laurent series make singularities computable. The coefficient \(a_{-1}\) is the residue, the part seen by contour integration.
