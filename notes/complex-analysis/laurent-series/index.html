11. Laurent series

Annular expansions

Theorem. If \(f\) is holomorphic on an annulus \(r<|z-a|

Principal part

Definition. The negative-power part \(\sum_{n<0}c_n(z-a)^n\) is the principal part. It records precisely the obstruction to holomorphic extension at \(a\).

Coefficient independence

Proposition. The coefficient integral is independent of the circle \(\gamma\) inside the annulus. This follows from Cauchy's theorem applied to the annular region between two such circles.

Classification by Laurent coefficients

Corollary. A singularity is removable when all negative coefficients vanish, a pole when finitely many negative coefficients are nonzero, and essential when infinitely many negative coefficients are nonzero.

Residue coefficient

Definition. The coefficient \(c_{-1}\) is the residue of \(f\) at \(a\), denoted \(\operatorname{Res}(f,a)\). It is the only Laurent coefficient detected by contour integration around \(a\).

Essential singularity example

Example. On \(0<|z|<\infty\), \(e^{1/z}=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}z^{-n}/n!\). Its residue at \(0\) is \(1\), and the infinitely many negative powers show that \(0\) is essential.
