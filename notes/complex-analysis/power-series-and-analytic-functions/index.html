2. Power series and analytic functions

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/complex-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/complex-analysis/complex-differentiability/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/complex-analysis/contour-integrals/" %}

Power series and analytic functions records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the complex analysis note.

Definition

Definition. A power series \(\sum a_n(z-z_0)^n\) converges inside a disk and defines a holomorphic function there. A function is analytic if it is locally represented by such a series.

Proposition

Proposition. Power series may be differentiated term by term inside their radius of convergence, and the derived series has the same radius.

Proof

Proof. Uniform convergence on compact subdisks follows from comparison with a geometric series. Difference quotients may then be controlled uniformly to justify termwise differentiation.

Remark

Remark. Cauchy theory later proves that every holomorphic function is analytic, a result with no real-variable analogue.
