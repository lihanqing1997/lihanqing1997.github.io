3. Contour integrals

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/complex-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/complex-analysis/power-series-and-analytic-functions/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/complex-analysis/cauchy-theorem/" %}

Contour integrals records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the complex analysis note.

Definition

Definition. A contour integral integrates a complex function along a parametrized curve: \(\int_\gamma f(z)\,\mathrm{d}z=\int_a^b f(\gamma(t))\gamma'(t)\,\mathrm{d}t\).

Proposition

Proposition. Contour integrals are invariant under orientation-preserving reparametrization and satisfy length estimates \(|\int_\gamma f\,\mathrm{d}z|\le \sup_\gamma |f|\,\operatorname{length}(\gamma)\).

Proof

Proof. Use the chain rule under reparametrization. The length estimate follows by taking absolute values inside the real integral defining the contour integral.

Remark

Remark. Complex integration is not just integration in two coordinates. The interaction with holomorphicity creates global constraints from local differentiability.
