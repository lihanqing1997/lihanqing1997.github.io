25. Meromorphic functions on compact surfaces

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/complex-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/complex-analysis/mittag-leffler-theorem-and-meromorphic-functions/" %}

Compact Riemann surfaces

Definition. A compact Riemann surface is a compact connected one-dimensional complex manifold. Local coordinates make holomorphic and meromorphic functions meaningful exactly as in plane domains.

Meromorphic functions are maps to the sphere

Proposition. A meromorphic function on a compact Riemann surface is the same as a holomorphic map to \(\widehat{\mathbb C}\). Poles are points mapped to \(\infty\).

No nonconstant holomorphic functions

Theorem. Every holomorphic function on a compact Riemann surface is constant. Its absolute value attains a maximum, so the maximum principle forces constancy.

Divisors of meromorphic functions

Definition. The divisor of a nonzero meromorphic function records zeros with positive multiplicity and poles with negative multiplicity. On a compact surface, the total degree of this divisor is zero.

Residue theorem on compact surfaces

Theorem. The sum of residues of a meromorphic one-form on a compact Riemann surface is zero. There is no boundary; local residue contributions must cancel globally.

Sphere example

Example. On \(\widehat{\mathbb C}\), meromorphic functions are rational functions. The equality between total zero order and total pole order is the usual degree count for rational functions.
