Cauchy estimates

Theorem. If \(|f|\le M\) on \(|\zeta-a|=R\), then \(|f^{(n)}(a)|\le n!M/R^n\). This follows directly from the derivative form of Cauchy's integral formula and the length estimate.

Taylor series radius

Corollary. If \(f\) is holomorphic on \(D(a,R)\), then its Taylor series at \(a\) converges to \(f\) on every smaller disc. Coefficients are controlled by Cauchy's estimates, and the integral formula identifies the sum.

Liouville from estimates

Example. If \(f\) is entire and bounded by \(M\), then \(|f'(a)|\le M/R\) for every \(R>0\), hence \(f'(a)=0\). Since \(a\) is arbitrary, \(f\) is constant.

Derivative bounds

Proposition. Bounds on a holomorphic function automatically give bounds on all derivatives in smaller discs. This is a major contrast with real differentiable functions, where a function can be small while its derivative is large.

Removable singularity criterion

Corollary. If \(f\) is holomorphic on a punctured disc and bounded near the puncture, then the singularity is removable. Cauchy's estimates force the negative Laurent coefficients to vanish.

Analytic rigidity

Remark. Cauchy estimates are the quantitative form of rigidity: holomorphic functions cannot oscillate arbitrarily fast without becoming large nearby.
