5. Cauchy integral formula

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/complex-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/complex-analysis/cauchy-theorem/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/complex-analysis/maximum-principle/" %}

Cauchy integral formula records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the complex analysis note.

Definition

Definition. The Cauchy integral formula reconstructs values of a holomorphic function inside a contour from boundary values on the contour.

Cauchy integral formula

Theorem. If \(f\) is holomorphic inside and on a positively oriented circle \(C\), then \(f(a)=\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_C \frac{f(z)}{z-a}\,\mathrm{d}z\). Higher derivatives are obtained by differentiating the kernel.

Proof

Proof. Subtract \(f(a)\), divide by \(z-a\), and apply Cauchy's theorem to the holomorphic remainder. The kernel integral equals \(2\pi i\) by direct parametrization.

Remark

Remark. The formula turns boundary control into interior control. It immediately implies analyticity, derivative estimates, and Liouville-type results.
