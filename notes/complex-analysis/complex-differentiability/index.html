1. Complex differentiability

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/complex-analysis/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/complex-analysis/power-series-and-analytic-functions/" %}

Complex differentiability records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the complex analysis note.

Definition

Definition. A function \(f:U\to\mathbb C\) is complex differentiable at \(z\) if the difference quotient has a limit independent of the direction in \(\mathbb C\). Holomorphic means complex differentiable on an open set.

Proposition

Proposition. Complex differentiability is much stronger than real differentiability: if \(f=u+iv\) is real differentiable, complex differentiability is equivalent to the Cauchy-Riemann equations plus compatibility of the real derivative with multiplication by \(i\).

Proof

Proof. Compare directional difference quotients along the real and imaginary axes. Equality of the two complex limits yields the Cauchy-Riemann equations, and the converse follows by linear approximation.

Remark

Remark. The rigidity of complex analysis begins here: one complex derivative already imposes two real first-order equations.
