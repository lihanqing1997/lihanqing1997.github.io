Riemann mapping theorem records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the complex analysis note.

Definition

Definition. The Riemann mapping theorem classifies simply connected proper plane domains up to conformal equivalence with the unit disk.

Riemann mapping theorem

Theorem. Every nonempty simply connected proper open subset of \(\mathbb C\) is biholomorphic to the unit disk.

Proof

Proof. The proof uses normal families: choose a normalized family of injective holomorphic maps into the disk, extract an extremal limit, and show maximality forces surjectivity.

Remark

Remark. The theorem is powerful but special to one complex dimension. It says topology plus openness almost determines conformal type for simply connected plane domains.
