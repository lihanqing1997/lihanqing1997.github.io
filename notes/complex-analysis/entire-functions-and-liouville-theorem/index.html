18. Entire functions and Liouville theorem

Entire functions and Liouville theorem records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the complex analysis note.

Definition

Definition. An entire function is holomorphic on all of \(\mathbb C\). Entire functions are constrained by growth conditions at infinity.

Liouville theorem

Theorem. A bounded entire function is constant. Consequently, every nonconstant polynomial has a complex root.

Proof

Proof. Cauchy estimates bound \(|f'(z)|\) by the sup norm on large circles divided by the radius. Letting the radius tend to infinity gives \(f'=0\). Apply the result to \(1/p\) if a polynomial has no zeros.

Remark

Remark. Liouville's theorem is a global rigidity statement extracted from local Cauchy estimates.
