20. Schwarz lemma and automorphisms of the disk

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/complex-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/complex-analysis/meromorphic-functions-on-compact-surfaces/" %}

Schwarz lemma and automorphisms of the disk records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the complex analysis note.

Definition

Definition. Schwarz lemma studies holomorphic self-maps of the unit disk fixing the origin. It is a local normalization result with global consequences.

Schwarz lemma

Theorem. If \(f:\mathbb D\to\mathbb D\) is holomorphic and \(f(0)=0\), then \(|f(z)|\le |z|\) and \(|f'(0)|\le1\), with equality only for rotations.

Proof

Proof. Apply the maximum principle to \(f(z)/z\), after defining the removable value at zero by \(f'(0)\). Equality forces the quotient to be constant.

Remark

Remark. Schwarz lemma is the prototype of metric contraction in complex analysis. It leads to the Poincaré metric and automorphism classification.
