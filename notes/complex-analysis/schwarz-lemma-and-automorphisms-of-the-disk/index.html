17. Schwarz lemma and automorphisms of the disk

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/complex-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/complex-analysis/mobius-transformations/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/complex-analysis/harmonic-functions/" %}

Schwarz lemma

Theorem. If \(f:\mathbb D\to\mathbb D\) is holomorphic and \(f(0)=0\), then \(|f(z)|\le |z|\) and \(|f'(0)|\le1\). If equality occurs at a nonzero point or in the derivative, then \(f(z)=e^{i\theta}z\).

Proof by maximum principle

Proof. Define \(g(z)=f(z)/z\) for \(z\ne0\), and set \(g(0)=f'(0)\). The removable singularity theorem makes \(g\) holomorphic. On \(|z|=r\), \(|g(z)|\le1/r\); letting \(r\uparrow1\) gives \(|g|\le1\).

Automorphisms of the disc

Theorem. Every biholomorphic automorphism of \(\mathbb D\) has the form \[ e^{i\theta}\frac{z-a}{1-\overline a z},\qquad |a|<1. \] Compose with the automorphism sending \(a\) to \(0\), then apply Schwarz's lemma.

Schwarz-Pick estimate

Theorem. Holomorphic maps \(f:\mathbb D\to\mathbb D\) contract the pseudohyperbolic metric: \[ \left|\frac{f(z)-f(w)}{1-\overline{f(w)}f(z)}\right|\le \left|\frac{z-w}{1-\overline w z}\right|. \]

Fixed points

Corollary. A non-identity automorphism of the disc has tightly constrained fixed points. Schwarz's lemma shows that an automorphism fixing \(0\) is a rotation.

Geometric meaning

Remark. Schwarz-Pick turns holomorphic self-maps into contractions for the intrinsic geometry of the disc. This is the beginning of complex hyperbolic geometry.
