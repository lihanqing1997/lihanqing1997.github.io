Normal families records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the complex analysis note.

Definition

Definition. A family of holomorphic functions is normal if every sequence has a subsequence converging uniformly on compact sets, possibly to a holomorphic limit or infinity in the meromorphic setting.

Montel theorem

Theorem. A locally uniformly bounded family of holomorphic functions is normal. This is Montel's theorem.

Proof

Proof. Cauchy estimates give uniform bounds on derivatives on compact subsets. Arzelà-Ascoli then gives subsequential compact convergence, and Cauchy's theorem identifies the limit as holomorphic.

Remark

Remark. Normal families are compactness for holomorphic functions. They support Riemann mapping, value distribution, and dynamical arguments.
