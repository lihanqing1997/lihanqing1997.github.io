10. Residue theorem

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/complex-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/complex-analysis/laurent-series/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/complex-analysis/argument-principle-and-rouche-theorem/" %}

Residue theorem records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the complex analysis note.

Definition

Definition. The residue of \(f\) at an isolated singularity is the coefficient of \((z-z_0)^{-1}\) in its Laurent expansion.

Residue theorem

Theorem. If \(f\) is holomorphic except for finitely many isolated singularities inside a contour \(\gamma\), then \(\int_\gamma f(z)\,\mathrm{d}z=2\pi i\sum \operatorname{Res}(f,a_j)\).

Proof

Proof. Remove small circles around each singularity. Cauchy's theorem applies on the punctured region, and each small circle integral extracts the \((z-a_j)^{-1}\) coefficient.

Remark

Remark. Residues turn difficult real integrals and counting problems into local coefficient computations.
