Linear fractional maps

Definition. A Möbius transformation has the form \(T(z)=(az+b)/(cz+d)\), with \(ad-bc\ne0\), acting on the Riemann sphere \(\widehat{\mathbb C}\). Matrices differing by a nonzero scalar define the same transformation.

Circle-line preservation

Theorem. Möbius transformations map generalized circles, meaning circles and lines in \(\widehat{\mathbb C}\), to generalized circles. This can be checked because they are generated by translations, dilations-rotations, and inversion \(z\mapsto1/z\).

Three-point determination

Proposition. There is a unique Möbius transformation sending any ordered triple of distinct points in \(\widehat{\mathbb C}\) to any other ordered triple. This follows from preservation of the cross-ratio.

Cross-ratio

Definition. The cross-ratio \([z_1,z_2,z_3,z_4]\) is invariant under Möbius transformations. It is the basic projective coordinate on four points of the sphere.

Disk and half-plane

Example. The Cayley map \(z\mapsto (z-i)/(z+i)\) sends the upper half-plane biholomorphically to the unit disc. This makes many disc and half-plane problems equivalent.

Automorphism groups

Remark. Möbius transformations are the conformal automorphisms of the Riemann sphere. Subgroups preserving the disc or half-plane are central in hyperbolic geometry.
