13. Mobius transformations

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/complex-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/complex-analysis/conformal-maps/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/complex-analysis/riemann-mapping-theorem/" %}

Mobius transformations records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the complex analysis note.

Definition

Definition. A Möbius transformation has the form \(z\mapsto (az+b)/(cz+d)\) with \(ad-bc\ne0\), acting on the Riemann sphere.

Proposition

Proposition. Möbius transformations are exactly the biholomorphic automorphisms of the Riemann sphere, and they send generalized circles to generalized circles.

Proof

Proof. Matrix multiplication gives composition, and normalization by scalar multiples identifies transformations with projective linear maps. Circle preservation follows from algebraic equations involving \(z\) and \(\bar z\).

Remark

Remark. These transformations are the linear fractional symmetries of complex analysis. They are the coordinate changes of the sphere.
