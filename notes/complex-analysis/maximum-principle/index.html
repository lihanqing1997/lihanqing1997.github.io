6. Maximum principle

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/complex-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/complex-analysis/cauchy-integral-formula/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/complex-analysis/zeros-and-identity-theorem/" %}

Maximum principle records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the complex analysis note.

Definition

Definition. The maximum principle says a nonconstant holomorphic function cannot attain a strict interior maximum of its modulus.

Maximum modulus principle

Theorem. If \(U\) is connected and \(|f|\) has a local maximum inside \(U\), then \(f\) is constant. Consequently, maxima on compact domains occur on the boundary.

Proof

Proof. Use the mean-value property from the Cauchy formula. If one point equals the average of nearby values in modulus, equality in the triangle inequality forces the nearby values to have the same argument and modulus.

Remark

Remark. Maximum principles convert analytic information into boundary estimates. They are central in harmonic analysis, PDE, and conformal mapping.
