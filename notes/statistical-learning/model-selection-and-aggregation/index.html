15. Model selection and aggregation

Model selection and aggregation records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the statistical learning note.

Definition

Definition. Model selection chooses among candidate classes or tuning parameters. Aggregation combines candidate predictors, often by weights.

Proposition

Proposition. Penalized criteria and exponential weighting can satisfy oracle inequalities comparing performance to the best candidate plus a complexity penalty.

Proof

Proof. Use concentration to control empirical-population deviations uniformly over candidates, then choose penalties matching the deviation scale. Exponential weights are analyzed by convex duality.

Remark

Remark. Model selection is learning over models. The penalty is the price of searching.
