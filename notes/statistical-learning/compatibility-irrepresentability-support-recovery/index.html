21. Compatibility, irrepresentability, and support recovery

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/statistical-learning/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/statistical-learning/nonconvex-sparse-penalties/" %}

Compatibility condition

Definition. For a design matrix \(X\) and support \(S\), the compatibility constant \(\phi_0(S)\) is the largest number such that \[ {\|X\Delta\|_2^2\over n}\ge {\phi_0(S)^2\|\Delta_S\|_1^2\over |S|} \] for all \(\Delta\) in the cone \(\|\Delta_{S^c}\|_1\le3\|\Delta_S\|_1\).

Proposition. The compatibility condition is weaker than a restricted eigenvalue lower bound in \(\ell_2\), but it is exactly matched to \(\ell_1\)-oracle inequalities for the Lasso.

Proof. On the cone, \(\|\Delta_S\|_1\le\sqrt{|S|}\|\Delta_S\|_2\). A restricted eigenvalue bound controlling \(\|\Delta_S\|_2\) therefore implies the displayed compatibility bound. The converse need not hold because compatibility controls only the active \(\ell_1\) size.

Irrepresentability

Definition. For Gram matrix \(\Sigma=X^\top X/n\), the irrepresentable condition for signed support recovery is \[ \|\Sigma_{S^cS}\Sigma_{SS}^{-1}\operatorname{sign}(\beta_S^*)\|_\infty\le1-\eta \] for some \(\eta>0\).

Theorem. In the noiseless KKT geometry of Lasso, exact signed support recovery requires the inactive gradients to be strictly inside the subgradient cube. This is encoded by the irrepresentable condition plus a beta-min lower bound.

Proof. Assume the active solution is known. The KKT equations solve the active coordinates and impose \(\|X_{S^c}^\top(y-X_S\hat\beta_S)/n\|_\infty\le\lambda\) on inactive coordinates. Substituting the active solution gives a deterministic leakage term \(\Sigma_{S^cS}\Sigma_{SS}^{-1}\operatorname{sign}(\beta_S^*)\). Strict containment leaves room for noise; beta-min prevents active coefficients from being shrunk through zero.

Basic inequality for the Lasso

Theorem. In \(y=X\beta^*+\varepsilon\), if \(\lambda\ge2\|X^\top\varepsilon/n\|_\infty\), then the Lasso error \(\Delta=\hat\beta-\beta^*\) satisfies \(\|\Delta_{S^c}\|_1\le3\|\Delta_S\|_1\) and \[ {1\over n}\|X\Delta\|_2^2+\lambda\|\Delta\|_1\lesssim \lambda\|\Delta_S\|_1. \]

Proof. Compare the penalized objective at \(\hat\beta\) and \(\beta^*\). The stochastic term is bounded by \(\lambda\|\Delta\|_1/2\). Rearranging separates active and inactive coordinates and yields the cone condition and inequality.
