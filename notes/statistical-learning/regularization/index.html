Definition

Definition. Regularization adds a penalty or constraint controlling complexity, such as norm, smoothness, sparsity, or stability.

Proposition

Proposition. Penalized ERM trades empirical fit against complexity, and under suitable decomposability or curvature assumptions yields oracle inequalities.

Proof

Proof. Compare the penalized objective at the estimator and at an oracle comparator. Rearrangement gives a basic inequality, then curvature and penalty structure bound the estimation error.

Remark

Remark. Regularization is where optimization, approximation, and statistics meet.
