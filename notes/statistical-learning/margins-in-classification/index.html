9. Margins in classification

Margins in classification records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the statistical learning note.

Definition

Definition. A margin measures confidence of a classifier, often as \(Y f(X)\) for labels \(Y\in\{-1,1\}\). Larger positive margins imply more robust classification.

Proposition

Proposition. Margin bounds can control classification error by empirical margin error plus a complexity term depending on the function class and margin threshold.

Proof

Proof. Upper-bound zero-one loss by a Lipschitz surrogate margin loss, then apply uniform convergence bounds to the surrogate class.

Remark

Remark. Margins explain why classifiers can generalize even when exact training classification is easy: confidence matters, not just correctness.
