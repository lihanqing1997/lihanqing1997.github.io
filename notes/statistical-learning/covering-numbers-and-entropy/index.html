Covering numbers and entropy records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the statistical learning note.

Definition

Definition. A covering number counts how many balls of a given radius are needed to cover a function class under a metric or empirical metric. Its logarithm is metric entropy.

Proposition

Proposition. Entropy integrals control suprema of empirical or Gaussian processes under suitable boundedness and sub-Gaussian assumptions.

Proof

Proof. Chain the function class by successively finer nets. Sum the stochastic increments across scales, using concentration at each scale.

Remark

Remark. Entropy connects statistical learning to empirical-process theory; the present page records the learning-theory interface only.
