Bias, variance, and approximation records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the statistical learning note.

Definition

Definition. Approximation error is the gap between the Bayes rule and the best function in the class. Estimation error is the price of choosing from data.

Proposition

Proposition. Increasing model complexity can decrease approximation error while increasing estimation error. Optimal learning balances these two terms under the sample size and noise level.

Proof

Proof. Write excess risk relative to the Bayes rule as a sum through the best-in-class predictor. Bound each term by approximation theory and empirical-process or concentration arguments.

Remark

Remark. Bias-variance language is informal unless tied to a loss and estimator, but the tradeoff is real across learning theory.
