PAC-Bayes theory gives high-probability risk bounds for randomized predictors. Its proof is an information inequality plus concentration, not ordinary Bayesian posterior consistency.

Randomized predictors

PAC-Bayes compares a data-dependent distribution over hypotheses with a prior distribution chosen before seeing the sample.

Definition. Let \(\mathcal H\) be a hypothesis space, \(P_0\) a prior probability on \(\mathcal H\), and \(Q\) a data-dependent posterior probability on \(\mathcal H\). The Gibbs risk is \[ R(Q)=\int R(h)\,Q(\mathrm{d}h), \qquad R_n(Q)=\int R_n(h)\,Q(\mathrm{d}h). \]

Remark. The word posterior here means any data-dependent distribution \(Q\). It need not be the Bayesian posterior from a likelihood.

Change of measure

The variational form of KL is the algebraic heart of PAC-Bayes.

Lemma. For any measurable \(\phi\) and any \(Q\ll P_0\), \[ Q\phi\le \operatorname{KL}(Q\|P_0)+\log P_0e^\phi. \]

Proof. By the Donsker-Varadhan variational identity, \[ \log P_0e^\phi=\sup_Q\{Q\phi-\operatorname{KL}(Q\|P_0)\}. \] Rearranging this inequality for the chosen \(Q\) gives the claim. Equivalently, apply Jensen's inequality to \(e^{\phi}/(\mathrm{d}Q/\mathrm{d}P_0)\) under \(Q\).

A basic PAC-Bayes inequality

The bound holds simultaneously over all posteriors because the random quantity integrated over the prior is controlled before choosing \(Q\).

Theorem. Assume \(0\le\ell(h,Z)\le1\). With probability at least \(1-\delta\), for every \(Q\ll P_0\), \[ R(Q)\le R_n(Q)+ \sqrt{\frac{\operatorname{KL}(Q\|P_0)+\log(2\sqrt n/\delta)}{2n}} \] up to standard constants depending on the chosen Bernoulli-kl relaxation.

Proof. For each fixed \(h\), concentration controls the deviation between \(R_n(h)\) and \(R(h)\) in exponential form. Integrate the exponential inequality with respect to \(P_0\) and apply Markov's inequality to obtain a high-probability bound on \(\log P_0e^\phi\). The change-of-measure lemma transfers the result from \(P_0\) to any \(Q\). Finally, relax the Bernoulli-kl form to the displayed square-root form.

Choosing the posterior

PAC-Bayes bounds can be optimized over \(Q\), giving a regularized empirical objective.

Proposition. Minimizing \[ Q R_n+\frac{1}{\eta n}\operatorname{KL}(Q\|P_0) \] over \(Q\) yields the Gibbs distribution \[ Q_\eta(\mathrm{d}h)\propto e^{-\eta nR_n(h)}P_0(\mathrm{d}h). \]

Proof. Use the same variational identity with \(\phi=-\eta nR_n(h)\). The minimizing distribution is the exponentially tilted prior, and the minimum value is \(-(\eta n)^{-1}\log P_0e^{-\eta nR_n}\).
