20. PAC-Bayes bounds

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/statistical-learning/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/statistical-learning/distribution-shift-and-domain-adaptation/" %}

PAC-Bayes bounds records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the statistical learning note.

Definition

Definition. PAC-Bayes theory bounds the risk of randomized predictors drawn from a posterior distribution over hypotheses, relative to a prior distribution chosen independently of the sample.

Proposition

Proposition. With high probability over the sample, many PAC-Bayes inequalities control posterior expected risk by empirical posterior risk plus a complexity term involving \(\operatorname{KL}(Q\|P)\).

Proof

Proof. Apply an exponential concentration inequality to each hypothesis, integrate with respect to the prior, then change measure from the prior to the posterior using the variational form of KL divergence.

Remark

Remark. PAC-Bayes bounds connect learning theory, information theory, and Bayesian language without requiring the posterior to be a literal Bayesian posterior.
