Kernel methods and RKHS records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the statistical learning note.

Definition

Definition. A reproducing kernel Hilbert space is a Hilbert space of functions where evaluation is continuous and represented by a kernel \(K(x,\cdot)\).

Representer theorem

Theorem. The representer theorem says many regularized empirical risk problems over an RKHS have solutions in the finite span of kernel sections at training points.

Proof

Proof. Decompose any candidate into its projection onto the data span plus an orthogonal remainder. The loss depends only on evaluations, while the norm penalty increases with the remainder.

Remark

Remark. Kernel methods convert nonlinear function learning into linear Hilbert-space geometry.
