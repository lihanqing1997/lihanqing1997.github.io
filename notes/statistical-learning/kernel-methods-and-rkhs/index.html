Kernel methods turn nonlinear prediction into linear prediction in a Hilbert space. The RKHS norm supplies both geometry and regularization.

Reproducing kernels

A positive definite kernel defines a Hilbert space of functions in which evaluation is continuous.

Definition. A symmetric function \(K:\mathcal X\times\mathcal X\to\mathbb R\) is positive definite if \[ \sum_{i,j=1}^m a_ia_jK(x_i,x_j)\ge0 \] for all finite choices. Its RKHS \(\mathcal H_K\) satisfies the reproducing property \[ f(x)=\langle f,K_x\rangle_{\mathcal H_K},\qquad K_x(\cdot)=K(x,\cdot). \]

Proposition. For every \(x\), \(|f(x)|\le \|f\|_{\mathcal H_K}\sqrt{K(x,x)}\).

Proof. Apply Cauchy-Schwarz to the reproducing identity: \[ |f(x)|=|\langle f,K_x\rangle|\le\|f\|_{\mathcal H_K}\|K_x\|_{\mathcal H_K}. \] The norm of \(K_x\) is \(\sqrt{\langle K_x,K_x\rangle}=\sqrt{K(x,x)}\).

Representer theorem

Regularized empirical problems in an RKHS reduce to finite-dimensional coefficient problems.

Theorem. Let \(J(f)=\Phi(\|f\|_{\mathcal H_K})\) with \(\Phi\) strictly increasing. Any minimizer of \[ \sum_{i=1}^n \ell(f(x_i),y_i)+\lambda J(f) \] over \(\mathcal H_K\) lies in \(\operatorname{span}\{K_{x_1},\ldots,K_{x_n}\}\).

Proof. Decompose \(f=f_S+f_\perp\), where \(f_S\) lies in the data span and \(f_\perp\) is orthogonal to it. The reproducing property gives \(f(x_i)=\langle f,K_{x_i}\rangle=\langle f_S,K_{x_i}\rangle\), so the empirical loss depends only on \(f_S\). Meanwhile \(\|f\|^2=\|f_S\|^2+\|f_\perp\|^2\), and the strictly increasing penalty is minimized by \(f_\perp=0\).

Kernel ridge regression

For squared loss, the representer theorem gives an explicit linear system.

Proposition. Kernel ridge regression solves \[ \min_{f\in\mathcal H_K}\frac1n\sum_{i=1}^n(y_i-f(x_i))^2+\lambda\|f\|_{\mathcal H_K}^2. \] Writing \(f(\cdot)=\sum_i\alpha_iK(x_i,\cdot)\), the coefficients satisfy \[ (K_n+n\lambda I)\alpha=y, \] where \((K_n)_{ij}=K(x_i,x_j)\).

Proof. Substitute the representer expansion. The fitted vector is \(K_n\alpha\), and the RKHS norm is \(\alpha^\top K_n\alpha\). Differentiating \[ n^{-1}\|y-K_n\alpha\|_2^2+\lambda\alpha^\top K_n\alpha \] and using symmetry of \(K_n\) gives \(K_n(K_n+n\lambda I)\alpha=K_ny\). The displayed normal equation follows on the range of \(K_n\), with the regularized solution selecting the canonical representative.

Effective dimension

Kernel rates depend on the spectrum of the kernel operator, not only on ambient dimension.

Definition. For kernel integral operator \(T\) and regularization \(\lambda>0\), the effective dimension is \[ \mathcal N(\lambda)=\operatorname{tr}\{T(T+\lambda I)^{-1}\}. \]

Remark. Fast eigenvalue decay means the RKHS behaves like a low-dimensional space at the resolution \(\lambda\). This quantity appears in variance terms for kernel ridge regression.
