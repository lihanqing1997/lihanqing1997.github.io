2. Bayes rules and excess risk

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/statistical-learning/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/statistical-learning/supervised-learning-setup/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/statistical-learning/empirical-risk-minimization/" %}

Bayes rules and excess risk records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the statistical learning note.

Definition

Definition. A Bayes rule minimizes population risk over all measurable predictors. Excess risk is \(R(f)-R(f^*)\), the loss relative to the Bayes rule.

Proposition

Proposition. For squared-error regression, the Bayes rule is \(f^*(x)=E[Y\mid X=x]\). For classification under zero-one loss, it predicts the most likely class conditional on \(X=x\).

Proof

Proof. Minimize the conditional risk pointwise in \(x\). For squared loss, expand the square around the conditional mean; for classification, choose the label with largest conditional probability.

Remark

Remark. The Bayes rule defines the statistical target even when it is not contained in the chosen model class.
