3. Empirical risk minimization

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/statistical-learning/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/statistical-learning/bayes-rules-and-excess-risk/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/statistical-learning/concentration-inequalities/" %}

Empirical risk minimization records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the statistical learning note.

Definition

Definition. Empirical risk minimization chooses \(\widehat f\in\mathcal F\) approximately minimizing observed average loss over a function class.

Proposition

Proposition. If \(\sup_{f\in\mathcal F}|R_n(f)-R(f)|\) is small, then the excess risk of an empirical minimizer over \(\mathcal F\) is controlled by twice this uniform deviation plus optimization error.

Proof

Proof. Compare \(R(\widehat f)\) to \(R_n(\widehat f)\), use empirical optimality to compare with \(R_n(f)\), then return from empirical to population risk.

Remark

Remark. ERM is simple because one inequality separates statistical learning from computation. The hard part is bounding the uniform deviation.
