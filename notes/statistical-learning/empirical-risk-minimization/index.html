3. Empirical risk minimization

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/statistical-learning/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/statistical-learning/bayes-rules-and-excess-risk/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/statistical-learning/concentration-inequalities/" %}

Empirical risk minimization is the basic bridge from data to predictors. Its validity comes from uniform laws of large numbers, and its sharp forms come from localization around low-risk functions.

Exact and approximate minimizers

Optimization error should be visible in the notation. An algorithm rarely returns a mathematical exact minimizer.

Definition. Given a loss class \(\mathcal G=\{\ell_f:f\in\mathcal F\}\), an \(\eta_n\)-ERM satisfies \[ P_n\ell_{\hat f_n}\le \inf_{f\in\mathcal F}P_n\ell_f+\eta_n. \]

Proposition. If \(\mathcal G\) is \(P\)-Glivenko-Cantelli and \(\eta_n\to0\) in probability, then \[ R(\hat f_n)-\inf_{f\in\mathcal F}R(f)\to0 \] in probability.

Proof. Use the basic ERM inequality \[ R(\hat f_n)-\inf_{\mathcal F}R \le 2\sup_{g\in\mathcal G}|P_ng-Pg|+\eta_n. \] The right side converges to zero by the Glivenko-Cantelli assumption and the optimization condition.

Finite classes

For finite classes, uniform convergence is just concentration plus a union bound.

Theorem. If \(0\le \ell_f\le1\) and \(|\mathcal F|=M<\infty\), then with probability at least \(1-\delta\), \[ \sup_{f\in\mathcal F}|R_n(f)-R(f)| \le \sqrt{\frac{\log(2M/\delta)}{2n}}. \]

Proof. Hoeffding's inequality gives \[ P(|R_n(f)-R(f)|>t)\le2e^{-2nt^2} \] for each fixed \(f\). Taking a union bound over \(M\) functions and solving \(2Me^{-2nt^2}=\delta\) gives the bound.

Oracle inequalities

A learning bound is strongest when it compares the estimator with the best member of a reference class.

Proposition. On the same event as the finite-class theorem, every ERM satisfies \[ R(\hat f_n)\le \inf_{f\in\mathcal F}R(f) +2\sqrt{\frac{\log(2M/\delta)}{2n}}. \]

Proof. Apply the basic ERM inequality with \(\eta_n=0\) and the uniform deviation bound from the finite-class theorem.

Remark. The phrase oracle inequality means the right side is the performance of the best inaccessible oracle choice plus an explicit price for learning it from data.

Localization

Global uniform convergence often wastes information because high-risk functions do not compete with the empirical minimizer.

Definition. For a radius \(r>0\), a localized class may be written \[ \mathcal G(r)=\{g_f-g_{f^*}:P(g_f-g_{f^*})\le r\}. \] Localized analyses bound the empirical process on \(\mathcal G(r)\) and solve a fixed-point inequality for \(r\).

Remark. Fast rates in least squares, classification with margin, and strongly convex regularized ERM all use this idea in different languages.
