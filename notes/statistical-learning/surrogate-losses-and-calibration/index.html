10. Surrogate losses and calibration

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/statistical-learning/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/statistical-learning/margins-in-classification/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/statistical-learning/regularization/" %}

Surrogate losses and calibration records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the statistical learning note.

Definition

Definition. Surrogate losses replace discontinuous zero-one loss by convex or smooth losses such as hinge, logistic, or exponential loss.

Proposition

Proposition. A surrogate loss is classification-calibrated if minimizing its conditional risk recovers the Bayes classifier for zero-one loss.

Proof

Proof. Analyze the conditional risk as a function of the prediction score and class probability. Calibration follows when the sign of the optimal score matches the Bayes decision.

Remark

Remark. Surrogates make optimization tractable, but calibration is needed to justify their causal link to classification risk.
