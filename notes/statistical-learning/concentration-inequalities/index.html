4. Concentration inequalities

Concentration inequalities records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the statistical learning note.

Definition

Definition. Concentration inequalities bound random fluctuations of empirical averages around expectations. They convert independence and boundedness or variance conditions into high-probability control.

Proposition

Proposition. Hoeffding and Bernstein inequalities show that bounded empirical means deviate from their expectations at sub-Gaussian or variance-sensitive rates.

Proof

Proof. Use exponential Markov inequality and factorization of moment generating functions under independence. Optimize the exponential parameter to obtain the tail bound.

Remark

Remark. Concentration is the finite-sample backbone of generalization theory.
