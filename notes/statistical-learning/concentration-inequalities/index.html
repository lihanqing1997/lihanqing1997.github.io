4. Concentration inequalities

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/statistical-learning/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/statistical-learning/empirical-risk-minimization/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/statistical-learning/bias-variance-and-approximation/" %}

Concentration inequalities turn random empirical quantities into deterministic error bars. They are the finite-sample engine behind learning bounds.

Hoeffding inequality

Bounded independent variables concentrate at a sub-Gaussian scale.

Theorem. If \(X_1,\ldots,X_n\) are independent, \(a_i\le X_i\le b_i\), and \(S_n=\sum_i(X_i-\mathbb E X_i)\), then \[ P(S_n\ge t)\le \exp\left\{-\frac{2t^2}{\sum_{i=1}^n(b_i-a_i)^2}\right\}. \]

Proof. For each \(\lambda>0\), Hoeffding's lemma gives \[ \mathbb E e^{\lambda(X_i-\mathbb E X_i)} \le \exp\{\lambda^2(b_i-a_i)^2/8\}. \] Independence multiplies moment generating functions. Chernoff's bound gives \[ P(S_n\ge t)\le \exp\{-\lambda t+\lambda^2\sum_i(b_i-a_i)^2/8\}. \] Optimizing over \(\lambda\) yields the displayed bound.

Bernstein inequality

Variance-sensitive inequalities improve Hoeffding when the variance is small.

Theorem. If \(X_i\) are independent, centered, \(|X_i|\le B\), and \(\sum_i\mathbb E X_i^2\le v\), then \[ P\left(\sum_iX_i\ge t\right) \le \exp\left\{-\frac{t^2}{2(v+Bt/3)}\right\}. \]

Proof. The exponential-moment estimate \[ \log\mathbb E e^{\lambda X_i}\le \frac{\lambda^2\mathbb E X_i^2}{2(1-\lambda B/3)} \] holds for \(0<\lambda<3/B\). Summing over \(i\), applying Chernoff's method, and optimizing \(\lambda=t/(v+Bt/3)\) gives the inequality.

Bounded differences

Many learning statistics are not simple sums, but they are stable under replacing one observation.

Theorem. Let \(F(Z_1,\ldots,Z_n)\) satisfy \[ \sup_{z_1,\ldots,z_n,z_i'}|F(z_1,\ldots,z_i,\ldots,z_n)-F(z_1,\ldots,z_i',\ldots,z_n)|\le c_i. \] Then \[ P(F-\mathbb EF\ge t)\le \exp\left\{-\frac{2t^2}{\sum_i c_i^2}\right\}. \]

Proof. Let \(M_i=\mathbb E(F\mid Z_1,\ldots,Z_i)\). The martingale increments satisfy \(|M_i-M_{i-1}|\le c_i\). Applying Azuma-Hoeffding to this Doob martingale gives the displayed inequality.

From fixed functions to classes

A fixed-function concentration bound becomes a learning bound only after controlling the size of the class.

Proposition. If a tail bound \(P(|P_ng-Pg|>t)\le a(t)\) holds for every \(g\in\mathcal G\), and \(\mathcal G\) is finite, then \[ P\left(\sup_{g\in\mathcal G}|P_ng-Pg|>t\right)\le |\mathcal G|a(t). \]

Proof. The event that the supremum exceeds \(t\) is contained in the union of the events \(\{|P_ng-Pg|>t\}\) over \(g\in\mathcal G\). Apply the union bound.
