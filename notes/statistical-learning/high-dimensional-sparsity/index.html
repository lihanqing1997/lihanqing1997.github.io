14. High-dimensional sparsity

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/statistical-learning/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/statistical-learning/support-vector-machines/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/statistical-learning/model-selection-and-aggregation/" %}

High-dimensional sparsity records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the statistical learning note.

Definition

Definition. High-dimensional learning allows the number of features to be comparable to or larger than sample size. Sparsity assumes the target depends on few effective coordinates.

Proposition

Proposition. Under restricted eigenvalue or compatibility conditions, \(\ell_1\)-regularized estimators satisfy oracle-type bounds scaling with sparsity and logarithmic dimension.

Proof

Proof. Use the basic inequality, decomposability of the \(\ell_1\) norm, concentration of empirical correlations, and restricted curvature on sparse cones.

Remark

Remark. The theory separates dimension from effective dimension, but only under structural assumptions on the design and target.
