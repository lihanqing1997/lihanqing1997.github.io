19. Distribution shift and domain adaptation

Distribution shift and domain adaptation records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the statistical learning note.

Definition

Definition. Distribution shift occurs when training and target distributions differ. Domain adaptation asks when target risk can be controlled using source data plus assumptions linking the domains.

Proposition

Proposition. Target risk can be bounded by source risk, a discrepancy between source and target feature distributions, and the error of the best joint predictor.

Proof

Proof. Add and subtract risks under source and target distributions for a comparator. Bound the distributional difference by an integral probability metric or hypothesis discrepancy.

Remark

Remark. Without assumptions, source performance does not identify target performance. Shift is fundamentally a causal and statistical problem, not only an engineering inconvenience.
