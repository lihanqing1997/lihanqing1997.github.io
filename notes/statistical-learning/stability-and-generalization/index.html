16. Stability and generalization

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/statistical-learning/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/statistical-learning/model-selection-and-aggregation/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/statistical-learning/boosting/" %}

Stability and generalization records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the statistical learning note.

Definition

Definition. Algorithmic stability measures how much a learning algorithm's output changes when one training example is modified.

Proposition

Proposition. Uniform stability implies a bound on expected generalization gap between empirical and population risk.

Proof

Proof. Replace one sample at a time by an independent copy and telescope the difference. Stability controls each replacement's effect on loss.

Remark

Remark. Stability is a property of the algorithm, while VC and Rademacher bounds are mainly properties of the function class.
