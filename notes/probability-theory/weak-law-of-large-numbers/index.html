13. Weak law of large numbers

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Back" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/characteristic-functions/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/borel-cantelli-lemma/" %}

The weak law of large numbers says sample averages stabilize in probability. It is the first expression of the idea that independent noise cancels at scale \(n^{-1}\).

Sample averages

The object is the empirical mean of many independent observations.

Definition. For random variables \(X_1,\dots,X_n\), write \(\overline X_n=n^{-1}\sum_{i=1}^nX_i\). The weak law asks when \(\overline X_n-\mathbb E X_1\to0\) in probability.

Chebyshev weak law

Finite variance gives the simplest proof.

Theorem. If \(X_i\) are pairwise independent, identically distributed, \(\mathbb E X_1=\mu\), and \(\operatorname{Var}(X_1)=\sigma^2\lt\infty\), then \(\overline X_n\to\mu\) in probability.

Proof. Pairwise independence gives \(\operatorname{Var}(\overline X_n)=n^{-2}\sum_i\operatorname{Var}(X_i)=\sigma^2/n\). Chebyshev's inequality yields \[ P(|\overline X_n-\mu|\gt\varepsilon)\le \frac{\sigma^2}{n\varepsilon^2}\to0. \]

Truncation without variance

The finite first moment law is deeper because large observations must be controlled.

Proposition. If \(X_i\) are i.i.d. with \(\mathbb E|X_1|\lt\infty\), then \(\overline X_n\to\mathbb E X_1\) in probability.

Proof. Let \(Y_{n,i}=X_i1_{\{|X_i|\le n\}}\). The contribution of \(X_i-Y_{n,i}\) has expectation controlled by \(\mathbb E[|X_1|1_{\{|X_1|\gt n\}}]\to0\). The truncated variables have variances small enough after averaging because \(n^{-2}\sum_i\mathbb E[Y_{n,i}^2]\le n^{-1}\mathbb E[X_1^2 1_{\{|X_1|\le n\}}]\to0\) after the standard tail decomposition. Chebyshev then handles the truncated average.

Remark. The weak law is about convergence in probability; the strong law requires almost-sure control over all large \(n\) simultaneously.
