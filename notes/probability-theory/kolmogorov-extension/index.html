Kolmogorov extension theorem

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Back" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/martingale-clt/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/path-spaces-finite-dimensional-distributions/" %}

Definition. A family of finite-dimensional distributions \(\mu_{t_1,\ldots,t_n}\) is consistent if it is invariant under permutation of coordinates and marginalization. It should behave like the finite-dimensional laws of a process \((X_t)_{t\in T}\).

Result. Kolmogorov's extension theorem says that a consistent family of finite-dimensional probability measures on standard Borel spaces defines a probability measure on the product path space \(S^T\) whose coordinate process has exactly those finite-dimensional distributions.

Proof.

Define probabilities on cylinder sets by the prescribed finite-dimensional laws. Consistency makes this set function well-defined and finitely additive. The extension theorem for measures extends it to the \(\sigma\)-field generated by cylinders.

Remarks

The theorem builds processes from their finite-dimensional laws. Path regularity, such as continuity, usually requires extra estimates after this construction.
