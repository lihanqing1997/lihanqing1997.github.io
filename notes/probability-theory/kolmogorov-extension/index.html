Kolmogorov Extension Theorem

This page records the core mathematical facts for Kolmogorov Extension Theorem.

Definition. A family of finite-dimensional distributions \(\mu_{t_1,\ldots,t_n}\) is consistent if it is invariant under permutation of coordinates and marginalization. It should behave like the finite-dimensional laws of a process \((X_t)_{t\in T}\).

Central result. Kolmogorov's extension theorem says that a consistent family of finite-dimensional probability measures on standard Borel spaces defines a probability measure on the product path space \(S^T\) whose coordinate process has exactly those finite-dimensional distributions.

Proof. Define probabilities on cylinder sets by the prescribed finite-dimensional laws. Consistency makes this set function well-defined and finitely additive. The extension theorem for measures extends it to the \(\sigma\)-field generated by cylinders.

How to read it

The theorem builds processes from their finite-dimensional laws. Path regularity, such as continuity, usually requires extra estimates after this construction.

The point is not to memorize the displayed formulas separately, but to understand how the definition, the result, and the proof mechanism fit together.
