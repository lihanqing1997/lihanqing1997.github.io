20. Poisson convergence and Poisson processes

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Back" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/berry-esseen-rates/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/stable-infinitely-divisible-laws/" %}

Poisson limits describe rare events. The same principle constructs Poisson processes, where counts on disjoint sets are independent and proportional to intensity.

Rare-event convergence

Many small Bernoulli risks with small total mass produce a Poisson law.

Definition. A Poisson random variable with parameter \(\lambda\ge0\) satisfies \(P(N=k)=e^{-\lambda}\lambda^k/k!\). A sequence of Bernoulli sums is in the law of small numbers when individual success probabilities vanish but their sum tends to \(\lambda\).

Law of small numbers

The Poisson distribution is the limit of rare independent successes.

Theorem. If \(X_{n,k}\) are independent Bernoulli variables with \(p_{n,k}=P(X_{n,k}=1)\), \(\max_kp_{n,k}\to0\), and \(\sum_kp_{n,k}\to\lambda\), then \(\sum_kX_{n,k}\Rightarrow \operatorname{Poisson}(\lambda)\).

Proof. The generating function is \[ \mathbb E z^{\sum_kX_{n,k}}=\prod_k(1+p_{n,k}(z-1)). \] Taking logs gives \(\sum_kp_{n,k}(z-1)+o(1)\) because \(\sum_kp_{n,k}^2\le(\max_kp_{n,k})\sum_kp_{n,k}\to0\). Hence the generating functions converge to \(\exp(\lambda(z-1))\), the Poisson generating function.

Poisson processes

A Poisson process is a random counting measure with independent increments.

Proposition. A Poisson process of rate \(\lambda\) is a counting process \(N_t\) with \(N_0=0\), independent increments, and \(N_t-N_s\sim\operatorname{Poisson}(\lambda(t-s))\) for \(s\le t\).

Proof. The finite-dimensional distributions are determined by independent Poisson increments on disjoint intervals. Consistency under merging adjacent intervals follows from additivity of independent Poisson variables. Kolmogorov extension gives a process; standard construction by exponential waiting times gives a càdlàg counting version.

Remark. Poisson random measures generalize this from time intervals to arbitrary measurable spaces with an intensity measure.
