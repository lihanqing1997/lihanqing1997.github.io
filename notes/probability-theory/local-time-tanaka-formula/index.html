53. Local time and Tanaka formula

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Back" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/stochastic-differential-equations/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/white-noise-stochastic-heat-equation/" %}

Local time measures how long a semimartingale spends at a spatial level. Tanaka's formula is Itô's formula for the nonsmooth convex function \(x\mapsto |x-a|\).

Occupation density

Local time is density of occupation measure with respect to space.

Definition. For Brownian motion, local time \(L_t^a\) is the jointly measurable process satisfying \[ \int_0^t f(B_s)\,\mathrm{d}s=\int_{\mathbb R}f(a)L_t^a\,\mathrm{d}a \] for nonnegative measurable \(f\).

Tanaka formula

The kink of the absolute value creates local time.

Theorem. For Brownian motion, \[ |B_t-a|=|B_0-a|+\int_0^t\operatorname{sgn}(B_s-a)\,\mathrm{d}B_s+L_t^a. \]

Proof. Approximate \(x\mapsto |x-a|\) by smooth convex functions \(f_\varepsilon\). Itô's formula gives a martingale term plus \(\frac12\int_0^t f_\varepsilon''(B_s-a)\,\mathrm{d}s\). As \(\varepsilon\downarrow0\), \(f_\varepsilon'\to\operatorname{sgn}\) and \(f_\varepsilon''\) approximates twice the Dirac mass at \(a\). The limiting second-order term is \(L_t^a\).

Semimartingale version

Local time extends to continuous semimartingales with quadratic variation replacing time.

Proposition. If \(X\) is a continuous semimartingale, then \[ |X_t-a|=|X_0-a|+\int_0^t\operatorname{sgn}(X_s-a)\,\mathrm{d}X_s+L_t^a(X). \]

Proof. The same smoothing proof applies, but the second-order term is \(\frac12\int f_\varepsilon''(X_s-a)\,\mathrm{d}[X]_s\). Its limit defines local time relative to the quadratic variation measure.

Remark. Local time is central in reflected diffusions, one-dimensional SDEs with nonsmooth coefficients, and fine Brownian path analysis.
