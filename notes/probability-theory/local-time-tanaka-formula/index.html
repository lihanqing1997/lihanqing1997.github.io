Local Time and Tanaka Formula

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Back" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/stochastic-differential-equations/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/white-noise-stochastic-heat-equation/" %}

This page records the core mathematical facts for Local Time and Tanaka Formula.

Definition. Local time \(L_t^a\) measures how much time a semimartingale spends near level \(a\), normalized by quadratic variation. For Brownian motion, it is characterized by the occupation density formula \[\int_0^tg(B_s)ds=\int_{\mathbb R}g(a)L_t^a\,da.\]

Central result. Tanaka's formula for Brownian motion is \[|B_t-a|=|B_0-a|+\int_0^t\operatorname{sgn}(B_s-a)dB_s+L_t^a.\]

Proof. Approximate \(|x-a|\) by smooth convex functions \(f_\varepsilon\). Itô formula gives a second-derivative term \(\frac12\int f_\varepsilon''(B_s-a)ds\). As \(\varepsilon\downarrow0\), \(f_\varepsilon'\to\operatorname{sgn}\) and the second-derivative term converges to local time.

How to read it

Local time is the correction required when Itô formula is applied to non-smooth convex functions.

The point is not to memorize the displayed formulas separately, but to understand how the definition, the result, and the proof mechanism fit together.
