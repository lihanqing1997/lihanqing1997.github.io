Local time and Tanaka formula

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Back" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/stochastic-differential-equations/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/white-noise-stochastic-heat-equation/" %}

This topic belongs to stochastic calculus. Ordinary calculus must be modified because martingale increments have non-negligible quadratic variation. Here the focus is Local time and Tanaka formula.

Start with the precise object. The definition states what the object is and what assumptions make the later formulas meaningful.

Definition. Local time \(L_t^a\) measures how much time a semimartingale spends near level \(a\), normalized by quadratic variation. For Brownian motion, it is characterized by the occupation density formula \[\int_0^tg(B_s)ds=\int_{\mathbb R}g(a)L_t^a\,da.\]

These assumptions are part of the mathematics. They tell us where each expression lives and prevent informal notation from hiding a real domain issue.

The result below is the fact one usually uses in calculations or proofs. Read it as the operational content of the definition.

Result. Tanaka's formula for Brownian motion is \[|B_t-a|=|B_0-a|+\int_0^t\operatorname{sgn}(B_s-a)dB_s+L_t^a.\]

Proof.

The proof is easiest to read by separating the elementary case from the extension step.

Approximate \(|x-a|\) by smooth convex functions \(f_\varepsilon\). Itô formula gives a second-derivative term \(\frac12\int f_\varepsilon''(B_s-a)ds\). As \(\varepsilon\downarrow0\), \(f_\varepsilon'\to\operatorname{sgn}\) and the second-derivative term converges to local time.

Remarks

Local time is the correction required when Itô formula is applied to non-smooth convex functions.
