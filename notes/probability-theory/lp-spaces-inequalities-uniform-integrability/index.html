The \(L^p\) spaces package integrability into normed spaces. Their inequalities control approximation, convergence, and uniform integrability.

Lp spaces and norms

\(L^p\) identifies random variables that agree almost surely.

Definition. For \(1\le p\lt\infty\), \(L^p\) consists of random variables with \(\|X\|_p=(\mathbb E|X|^p)^{1/p}\lt\infty\), modulo almost-sure equality. \(L^\infty\) consists of essentially bounded variables with essential supremum norm.

Holder, Jensen, and Minkowski

The basic inequalities make \(L^p\) usable as analytic spaces.

Theorem. If \(p,q\gt1\) and \(1/p+1/q=1\), then \(\mathbb E|XY|\le\|X\|_p\|Y\|_q\). Consequently, \(\|X+Y\|_p\le\|X\|_p+\|Y\|_p\) for \(p\ge1\). If \(\phi\) is convex, \(\phi(\mathbb E X)\le\mathbb E\phi(X)\) whenever both sides are meaningful.

Proof. Holder follows from Young's inequality \(ab\le a^p/p+b^q/q\) after normalizing by \(\|X\|_p\) and \(\|Y\|_q\). Minkowski follows by applying Holder to \(|X+Y|^p\le |X||X+Y|^{p-1}+|Y||X+Y|^{p-1}\). Jensen follows first for finite convex combinations and then by approximation or supporting-line arguments.

Uniform integrability

Uniform integrability is the missing hypothesis that upgrades convergence in probability to \(L^1\)-convergence.

Proposition. A family \(\mathcal X\subset L^1\) is uniformly integrable if \[ \lim_{K\to\infty}\sup_{X\in\mathcal X}\mathbb E[|X|1_{\{|X|\gt K\}}]=0. \] If \(X_n\to X\) in probability and \(\{X_n\}\) is uniformly integrable, then \(X\in L^1\) and \(X_n\to X\) in \(L^1\).

Proof. Truncate \(X_n\) and \(X\) at level \(K\). Bounded convergence in probability implies convergence of the truncated variables in \(L^1\) along the full sequence. The tails are uniformly small for \(X_n\), and Fatou's lemma gives the corresponding tail control for \(X\). Let \(n\to\infty\) for fixed \(K\), then send \(K\to\infty\).

Remark. A useful sufficient condition is an \(L^{1+\delta}\) bound: if \(\sup_X\mathbb E|X|^{1+\delta}\lt\infty\), then \(\mathcal X\) is uniformly integrable.
