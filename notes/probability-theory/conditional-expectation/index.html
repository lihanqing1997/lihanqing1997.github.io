Conditional expectation is the central operator for probability with partial information. It is simultaneously an integral identity, an \(L^2\)-projection, and the algebra behind martingales.

Defining identity

The defining property is equality of integrals over observed events.

Definition. For \(X\in L^1(\Omega,\mathcal F,P)\) and \(\mathcal G\subseteq\mathcal F\), \(\mathbb E[X\mid\mathcal G]\) is a \(\mathcal G\)-measurable integrable random variable \(Y\) such that \[ \int_GY\,\mathrm{d}P=\int_GX\,\mathrm{d}P,\qquad G\in\mathcal G. \]

Existence, uniqueness, and tower property

The operator is characterized by the defining identity.

Theorem. Conditional expectation exists and is unique almost surely. It is linear, positive, contractive on \(L^p\) for \(1\le p\le\infty\), and if \(\mathcal H\subseteq\mathcal G\), then \[ \mathbb E[\mathbb E[X\mid\mathcal G]\mid\mathcal H]=\mathbb E[X\mid\mathcal H]. \]

Proof. Existence follows from Radon-Nikodym: the signed measure \(A\mapsto\int_AX\,\mathrm{d}P\) on \(\mathcal G\) is absolutely continuous with respect to \(P|_{\mathcal G}\). Uniqueness follows by testing the difference of two versions on \(\{Y_1\gt Y_2\}\) and \(\{Y_2\gt Y_1\}\). The tower property follows because the left-hand side is \(\mathcal H\)-measurable and has the same integrals as \(X\) over all \(H\in\mathcal H\).

Pull-out and projection properties

Measurable factors known under \(\mathcal G\) can be pulled out of conditioning.

Proposition. If \(Z\) is bounded and \(\mathcal G\)-measurable, then \[ \mathbb E[ZX\mid\mathcal G]=Z\mathbb E[X\mid\mathcal G]. \] If \(X\in L^2\), then \(\mathbb E[X\mid\mathcal G]\) is the orthogonal projection of \(X\) onto \(L^2(\mathcal G)\).

Proof. For indicators \(Z=1_G\), the pull-out identity follows from the defining integral identity on sets in \(\mathcal G\). Simple approximation and bounded convergence give bounded \(Z\). In \(L^2\), for every \(Y\in L^2(\mathcal G)\), the pull-out identity gives \(\mathbb E[(X-\mathbb E[X\mid\mathcal G])Y]=0\), which is exactly orthogonality to the closed subspace \(L^2(\mathcal G)\).

Remark. Conditioning on a random variable \(Y\) means conditioning on \(\sigma(Y)\). Versions can often be written as measurable functions \(g(Y)\).
