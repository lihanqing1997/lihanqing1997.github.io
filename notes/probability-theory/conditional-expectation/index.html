Conditional Expectation

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Back" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/conditional-probability-regular-conditional-distributions/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/modes-of-convergence/" %}

This page records the core mathematical facts for Conditional Expectation.

Definition. For an integrable \(X\) and a sub-\(\sigma\)-field \(\mathcal G\), the conditional expectation \(\mathbb E(X\mid\mathcal G)\) is the \(\mathcal G\)-measurable integrable random variable \(Y\) such that \(\int_GY\,dP=\int_GX\,dP\) for all \(G\in\mathcal G\).

Central result. Conditional expectation is a projection: if \(X\in L^2\), then \(\mathbb E(X\mid\mathcal G)\) is the orthogonal projection of \(X\) onto the closed subspace \(L^2(\mathcal G)\). Also, \(\mathbb E\{\mathbb E(X\mid\mathcal G)\mid\mathcal H\}=\mathbb E(X\mid\mathcal H)\) whenever \(\mathcal H\subset\mathcal G\).

Proof. Existence follows from the Radon-Nikodym theorem applied to the signed measure \(G\mapsto\int_GX\,dP\) on \(\mathcal G\). Uniqueness follows because two versions with the same integrals over all \(G\in\mathcal G\) must be equal almost surely. The projection property follows by testing against all \(\mathcal G\)-measurable square-integrable functions.

How to read it

Conditioning means replacing unavailable information by the best measurable summary relative to the information \(\mathcal G\). Martingales are built by repeatedly applying this projection.

The point is not to memorize the displayed formulas separately, but to understand how the definition, the result, and the proof mechanism fit together.
