Probability is built from small classes of sets: rectangles, cylinders, intervals, and algebras. Extension and monotone-class theorems are the tools that let identities proved on generators become identities on full \(\sigma\)-fields.

Premeasures and generated sigma-fields

One usually specifies a probability on an algebra, then extends it.

Definition. An algebra \(\mathcal A\) of subsets of \(\Omega\) is closed under complements and finite unions. A premeasure \(\mu_0:\mathcal A\to[0,\infty]\) is finitely additive and countably additive whenever the disjoint union remains in \(\mathcal A\). The generated \(\sigma\)-field is denoted \(\sigma(\mathcal A)\).

Caratheodory extension

Extension is the formal justification for constructing measures from generators.

Theorem. If \(\mu_0\) is a \(\sigma\)-finite premeasure on an algebra \(\mathcal A\), then there is a unique measure \(\mu\) on \(\sigma(\mathcal A)\) extending \(\mu_0\).

Proof. Define the outer measure \(\mu^\ast(E)=\inf\{\sum_n\mu_0(A_n):E\subseteq\bigcup_nA_n,\ A_n\in\mathcal A\}\). Caratheodory-measurable sets form a \(\sigma\)-field, \(\mu^\ast\) restricted to it is a measure, and every set in \(\mathcal A\) is measurable with the original value. Under \(\sigma\)-finiteness, if two extensions agree on \(\mathcal A\), a pi-lambda argument on finite-measure pieces shows they agree on \(\sigma(\mathcal A)\).

Monotone classes

The monotone-class theorem turns algebraic verification into measurable verification.

Proposition. If \(\mathcal A\) is an algebra and \(\mathcal M\) is a monotone class containing \(\mathcal A\), then \(\sigma(\mathcal A)\subseteq\mathcal M\).

Proof. Let \(\mathcal M_0\) be the smallest monotone class containing \(\mathcal A\). One checks that \(\mathcal M_0\) is itself an algebra: closure under complements and finite intersections is verified by fixing one set and applying monotone closure. An algebra that is also a monotone class is a \(\sigma\)-field, because countable unions are limits of increasing finite unions. Hence \(\mathcal M_0=\sigma(\mathcal A)\).

Remark. In probability, monotone classes are the quiet engine behind uniqueness of distributions, Fubini arguments, independence checks, and conditional expectation identities.
