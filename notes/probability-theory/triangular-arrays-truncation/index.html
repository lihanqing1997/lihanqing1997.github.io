16. Triangular arrays and truncation methods

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Back" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/strong-law-of-large-numbers/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/central-limit-theorem/" %}

Triangular arrays allow the summands to change with \(n\). They are the natural language for infinitesimal approximations, rare events, and non-identically distributed central limit theorems.

Triangular arrays

Each row is a different probabilistic experiment.

Definition. A triangular array is a family \(\{X_{n,k}:1\le k\le k_n,\ n\ge1\}\). It is row-wise independent if \(X_{n,1},\dots,X_{n,k_n}\) are independent for each fixed \(n\). It is infinitesimal if for every \(\varepsilon\gt0\), \[ \max_{k\le k_n}P(|X_{n,k}|\gt\varepsilon)\to0. \]

Lindeberg condition

The Lindeberg condition rules out a single large summand dominating a row.

Theorem. For centered row-wise independent variables with row variance \(s_n^2=\sum_k\operatorname{Var}(X_{n,k})\), the Lindeberg condition is \[ \frac1{s_n^2}\sum_k\mathbb E[X_{n,k}^2 1_{\{|X_{n,k}|\gt\varepsilon s_n\}}]\to0 \] for every \(\varepsilon\gt0\). Under this condition and \(s_n^2\to1\), \(\sum_kX_{n,k}\Rightarrow N(0,1)\).

Proof. Use characteristic functions. Taylor expand \(e^{itx}\) up to second order on \(|x|\le\varepsilon\), and control the remainder by Lindeberg's condition on large terms. Row independence turns characteristic functions into products, and the log product converges to \(-t^2/2\). Lévy's continuity theorem gives the normal limit.

Truncation method

Truncation separates small regular fluctuations from rare large values.

Proposition. If \(X_{n,k}\) are replaced by \(X_{n,k}1_{\{|X_{n,k}|\le a_n\}}\), then proving that the sum of discarded terms is negligible in probability allows one to transfer limit theorems from the truncated array to the original array.

Proof. By Markov's inequality, \[ P\left(\sum_k|X_{n,k}|1_{\{|X_{n,k}|\gt a_n\}}\gt\varepsilon\right) \le \varepsilon^{-1}\sum_k\mathbb E[|X_{n,k}|1_{\{|X_{n,k}|\gt a_n\}}]. \] If the right side tends to zero, the discarded tail is negligible. The truncated variables then have bounded moments suitable for variance or characteristic-function estimates.

Remark. Many proofs in probability are just carefully optimized truncation arguments with the cutoff chosen at the scale of the desired limit.
