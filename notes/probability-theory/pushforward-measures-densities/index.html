Pushforward Measures & Densities

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Back" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/distributions-random-variables/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/expected-value/" %}

This page links three ideas: distributions are pushforward measures, expectations are computed by a change-of-variable formula, and densities are Radon-Nikodym derivatives.

Definition: pushforward measure. Let \((S,\mathcal S)\) and \((E,\mathcal E)\) be measurable spaces, let \(\mu\) be a measure on \(S\), and let \(T:S\to E\) be measurable. The pushforward of \(\mu\) by \(T\) is the measure \(T_\#\mu\) on \(E\) defined by

\[(T_\#\mu)(B)=\mu(T^{-1}(B)),\qquad B\in\mathcal E.\]

If \(X:\Omega\to\mathbb R\) is a random variable, then its distribution is exactly \(X_\#P\).

Proposition: change of variables. If \(f:E\to[0,\infty]\) is measurable, then

\[\int_E f(y)\,(T_\#\mu)(dy)=\int_S f(T(s))\,\mu(ds).\]

The same identity holds for integrable real-valued \(f\).

Proof. For \(f=1_B\), both sides equal \(\mu(T^{-1}(B))\). By linearity, the formula holds for nonnegative simple functions.

For general \(f\ge0\), choose simple \(f_n\uparrow f\). Then \(f_n\circ T\uparrow f\circ T\), and monotone convergence gives the identity. For integrable real-valued \(f\), apply the nonnegative case to \(f^+\) and \(f^-\).

Definition: Radon-Nikodym derivative. For measures \(\mu,\nu\) on \((E,\mathcal E)\), write \(\mu\ll\nu\) if \(\nu(A)=0\) implies \(\mu(A)=0\). If there is a measurable \(h\ge0\) such that

\[\mu(A)=\int_A h\,d\nu,\qquad A\in\mathcal E,\]

then \(h\) is the Radon-Nikodym derivative of \(\mu\) with respect to \(\nu\), written \(h=d\mu/d\nu\). In probability, a density is \(d\mu_X/dx\), where \(dx\) is Lebesgue measure.

Theorem: finite Radon-Nikodym theorem. If \(\mu,\nu\) are finite measures and \(\mu\ll\nu\), then there is a measurable \(h\ge0\) with \(\mu(A)=\int_Ah\,d\nu\) for all \(A\). It is unique up to \(\nu\)-null sets.

Proof. Let \(\mathcal C=\{f\ge0:\int_A f\,d\nu\le\mu(A)\text{ for all }A\in\mathcal E\}\). This class is closed under maxima, since \(\max(f,g)=f\) on \(\{f\ge g\}\) and \(g\) on its complement. Choose \(f_n\in\mathcal C\) with \(\int f_n\,d\nu\uparrow\sup_{f\in\mathcal C}\int f\,d\nu\), and replace \(f_n\) by \(f_1\vee\cdots\vee f_n\). Then \(f_n\uparrow h\in\mathcal C\).

Set \(\lambda(A)=\mu(A)-\int_Ah\,d\nu\). If \(\lambda(E)>0\), choose \(\varepsilon>0\) so that the signed measure \(\lambda-\varepsilon\nu\) has positive total mass. By the Hahn decomposition theorem, there is a positive set \(P\) with \(\lambda(B)\ge\varepsilon\nu(B)\) for all \(B\subset P\), and \(\lambda(P)>0\). For every \(A\),

\[\int_A(h+\varepsilon1_P)\,d\nu\le\int_Ah\,d\nu+\lambda(A\cap P)\le\int_Ah\,d\nu+\lambda(A)=\mu(A).\]

Thus \(h+\varepsilon1_P\in\mathcal C\), and its integral is larger than that of \(h\), a contradiction. Hence \(\lambda=0\).

If \(h_1,h_2\) both work, then \(\int_A(h_1-h_2)\,d\nu=0\) for all \(A\). Taking \(A=\{h_1>h_2\}\) and \(A=\{h_2>h_1\}\) gives \(h_1=h_2\) \(\nu\)-almost everywhere.

Mesures images et densités

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Retour" prev_label="Page précédente" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/distributions-random-variables/" next_label="Page suivante" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/expected-value/" %}

Cette page relie trois idées : une loi est une mesure image, l'espérance se calcule par changement de variable, et une densité est une dérivée de Radon-Nikodym.

Définition : mesure image. Soient \((S,\mathcal S)\) et \((E,\mathcal E)\) des espaces mesurables, \(\mu\) une mesure sur \(S\), et \(T:S\to E\) mesurable. La mesure image de \(\mu\) par \(T\) est la mesure \(T_\#\mu\) définie par

\[(T_\#\mu)(B)=\mu(T^{-1}(B)),\qquad B\in\mathcal E.\]

Si \(X:\Omega\to\mathbb R\) est une variable aléatoire, sa loi est exactement \(X_\#P\).

Proposition : changement de variable. Si \(f:E\to[0,\infty]\) est mesurable, alors

\[\int_E f(y)\,(T_\#\mu)(dy)=\int_S f(T(s))\,\mu(ds).\]

La même identité vaut pour \(f\) réelle intégrable.

Preuve. Pour \(f=1_B\), les deux côtés valent \(\mu(T^{-1}(B))\). Par linéarité, la formule vaut pour les fonctions simples positives.

Pour \(f\ge0\), prendre des fonctions simples \(f_n\uparrow f\). Alors \(f_n\circ T\uparrow f\circ T\), et la convergence monotone donne l'identité. Pour \(f\) intégrable, appliquer le cas positif à \(f^+\) et \(f^-\).

Définition : dérivée de Radon-Nikodym. Pour deux mesures \(\mu,\nu\) sur \((E,\mathcal E)\), écrire \(\mu\ll\nu\) si \(\nu(A)=0\) implique \(\mu(A)=0\). S'il existe \(h\ge0\) mesurable tel que

\[\mu(A)=\int_A h\,d\nu,\qquad A\in\mathcal E,\]

alors \(h\) est la dérivée de Radon-Nikodym de \(\mu\) par rapport à \(\nu\), notée \(h=d\mu/d\nu\). En probabilité, une densité est \(d\mu_X/dx\), où \(dx\) est la mesure de Lebesgue.

Théorème : Radon-Nikodym pour mesures finies. Si \(\mu,\nu\) sont finies et \(\mu\ll\nu\), alors il existe \(h\ge0\) mesurable tel que \(\mu(A)=\int_Ah\,d\nu\) pour tout \(A\). Cette fonction est unique \(\nu\)-presque partout.

Preuve. Soit \(\mathcal C=\{f\ge0:\int_A f\,d\nu\le\mu(A)\text{ pour tout }A\}\). Cette classe est stable par maximum. Choisir \(f_n\in\mathcal C\) avec \(\int f_n\,d\nu\) tendant vers le supremum, puis remplacer \(f_n\) par \(f_1\vee\cdots\vee f_n\). Alors \(f_n\uparrow h\in\mathcal C\).

Poser \(\lambda(A)=\mu(A)-\int_Ah\,d\nu\). Si \(\lambda(E)>0\), choisir \(\varepsilon>0\) tel que la mesure signée \(\lambda-\varepsilon\nu\) ait une masse totale positive. Par le théorème de décomposition de Hahn, il existe un ensemble positif \(P\) tel que \(\lambda(B)\ge\varepsilon\nu(B)\) pour tout \(B\subset P\), avec \(\lambda(P)>0\). Pour tout \(A\),

\[\int_A(h+\varepsilon1_P)\,d\nu\le\int_Ah\,d\nu+\lambda(A\cap P)\le\int_Ah\,d\nu+\lambda(A)=\mu(A).\]

Donc \(h+\varepsilon1_P\in\mathcal C\) et son intégrale est plus grande que celle de \(h\), contradiction. Ainsi \(\lambda=0\).

Si \(h_1,h_2\) conviennent, alors \(\int_A(h_1-h_2)\,d\nu=0\) pour tout \(A\). En prenant \(A=\{h_1>h_2\}\) puis \(A=\{h_2>h_1\}\), on obtient \(h_1=h_2\) \(\nu\)-p.p.

Guide Durrett : sections 1.2-1.4 ; arrière-plan mesure-théorique pour Radon-Nikodym.

推前测度与密度

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="返回" prev_label="上一页" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/distributions-random-variables/" next_label="下一页" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/expected-value/" %}

这一页把三个概念连在一起：分布就是推前测度，期望可由换元公式计算，密度就是 Radon-Nikodym 导数。

定义：推前测度. 设 \((S,\mathcal S)\)、\((E,\mathcal E)\) 为可测空间，\(\mu\) 是 \(S\) 上的测度，\(T:S\to E\) 可测。由 \(T\) 推出的推前测度 \(T_\#\mu\) 定义为

\[(T_\#\mu)(B)=\mu(T^{-1}(B)),\qquad B\in\mathcal E.\]

若 \(X:\Omega\to\mathbb R\) 是随机变量，则 \(X\) 的分布正是 \(X_\#P\)。

命题：换元公式. 若 \(f:E\to[0,\infty]\) 可测，则

\[\int_E f(y)\,(T_\#\mu)(dy)=\int_S f(T(s))\,\mu(ds).\]

若 \(f\) 为实值可积函数，同一公式仍成立。

证明. 当 \(f=1_B\) 时，两边均等于 \(\mu(T^{-1}(B))\)。由线性性，公式对非负简单函数成立。

对一般 \(f\ge0\)，取简单函数 \(f_n\uparrow f\)。则 \(f_n\circ T\uparrow f\circ T\)，由单调收敛定理得到公式。对实值可积 \(f\)，分别对 \(f^+\) 与 \(f^-\) 应用非负情形。

定义：Radon-Nikodym 导数. 对 \((E,\mathcal E)\) 上的测度 \(\mu,\nu\)，若 \(\nu(A)=0\) 推出 \(\mu(A)=0\)，记作 \(\mu\ll\nu\)。若存在可测函数 \(h\ge0\) 使得

\[\mu(A)=\int_A h\,d\nu,\qquad A\in\mathcal E,\]

则称 \(h\) 为 \(\mu\) 关于 \(\nu\) 的 Radon-Nikodym 导数，记作 \(h=d\mu/d\nu\)。在概率论中，密度就是 \(d\mu_X/dx\)，其中 \(dx\) 为 Lebesgue 测度。

定理：有限测度的 Radon-Nikodym 定理. 若 \(\mu,\nu\) 为有限测度且 \(\mu\ll\nu\)，则存在可测 \(h\ge0\)，使对所有 \(A\)，\(\mu(A)=\int_Ah\,d\nu\)。该 \(h\) 在 \(\nu\)-零测集外唯一。

证明. 令 \(\mathcal C=\{f\ge0:\int_A f\,d\nu\le\mu(A)\text{ 对所有 }A\text{ 成立}\}\)。这个类对取最大值封闭。取 \(f_n\in\mathcal C\) 使 \(\int f_n\,d\nu\) 趋于上确界，并用 \(f_1\vee\cdots\vee f_n\) 替代 \(f_n\)。于是 \(f_n\uparrow h\in\mathcal C\)。

定义 \(\lambda(A)=\mu(A)-\int_Ah\,d\nu\)。若 \(\lambda(E)>0\)，取 \(\varepsilon>0\) 使符号测度 \(\lambda-\varepsilon\nu\) 的总质量为正。由 Hahn 分解定理，存在正集 \(P\)，使对任意 \(B\subset P\)，\(\lambda(B)\ge\varepsilon\nu(B)\)，且 \(\lambda(P)>0\)。对任意 \(A\)，

\[\int_A(h+\varepsilon1_P)\,d\nu\le\int_Ah\,d\nu+\lambda(A\cap P)\le\int_Ah\,d\nu+\lambda(A)=\mu(A).\]

于是 \(h+\varepsilon1_P\in\mathcal C\)，并且其积分严格大于 \(h\) 的积分，矛盾。因此 \(\lambda=0\)。

若 \(h_1,h_2\) 都满足条件，则对任意 \(A\)，\(\int_A(h_1-h_2)\,d\nu=0\)。分别取 \(A=\{h_1>h_2\}\) 和 \(A=\{h_2>h_1\}\)，得 \(h_1=h_2\) \(\nu\)-几乎处处。

Durrett 参考：第 1.2-1.4 节；Radon-Nikodym 导数需要测度论背景。
