26. Uniform integrability and L1/Lp martingale convergence

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Back" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/optional-stopping-sampling/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/square-integrable-martingales-quadratic-variation/" %}

Uniform integrability is the exact condition for \(L^1\) martingale convergence. It prevents mass from escaping into rare but huge terminal values.

Uniformly integrable martingales

A martingale bounded in \(L^1\) need not be uniformly integrable.

Definition. A family \(\{X_i\}\subset L^1\) is uniformly integrable if \(\sup_i\mathbb E|X_i|\lt\infty\) and the tails \(\sup_i\mathbb E[|X_i|1_{\{|X_i|\gt K\}}]\) vanish as \(K\to\infty\).

UI convergence theorem

Uniform integrability is equivalent to convergence in \(L^1\) for martingales.

Theorem. For a martingale \(M_n\), the following are equivalent: \(\{M_n\}\) is uniformly integrable; \(M_n\to M_\infty\) almost surely and in \(L^1\); and there exists \(M_\infty\in L^1\) such that \(M_n=\mathbb E[M_\infty\mid\mathcal F_n]\).

Proof. Uniform integrability gives \(L^1\)-boundedness, hence almost-sure convergence by the martingale convergence theorem. UI plus a.s. convergence gives \(L^1\)-convergence by Vitali's theorem. If \(M_n\to M_\infty\) in \(L^1\), then for \(A\in\mathcal F_n\), \(\int_A M_m\,\mathrm{d}P=\int_A M_n\,\mathrm{d}P\) for \(m\ge n\); letting \(m\to\infty\) gives \(M_n=\mathbb E[M_\infty\mid\mathcal F_n]\). The representation implies UI because conditional expectations of a fixed \(L^1\) variable form a uniformly integrable family.

Closed martingales

A martingale represented as conditional expectations of a terminal variable is called closed.

Proposition. If \(M_n=\mathbb E[X\mid\mathcal F_n]\) for \(X\in L^p\), \(p\ge1\), then \(M_n\to\mathbb E[X\mid\mathcal F_\infty]\) in \(L^p\) for \(p\gt1\), and in \(L^1\) under uniform integrability.

Proof. The subspaces \(L^p(\mathcal F_n)\) increase to \(L^p(\mathcal F_\infty)\). Conditional expectation is a contraction, and approximation of \(X\) by \(\mathcal F_m\)-measurable variables reduces convergence to the case where the martingale is eventually constant.

Remark. Uniform integrability is the martingale version of compactness in \(L^1\).
