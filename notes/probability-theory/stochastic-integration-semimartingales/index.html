Stochastic Integration with Respect to Semimartingales

This page records the core mathematical facts for Stochastic Integration with Respect to Semimartingales.

Definition. For a semimartingale \(X=M+A\), define \(\int H\,dX=\int H\,dM+\int H\,dA\), where the martingale integral is built by localization and the finite-variation integral is pathwise Lebesgue-Stieltjes.

Central result. If \(X,Y\) are continuous semimartingales, then integration by parts holds: \[X_tY_t=X_0Y_0+\int_0^tX_s\,dY_s+\int_0^tY_s\,dX_s+[X,Y]_t.\]

Proof. Apply the discrete identity \(\Delta(XY)=X\Delta Y+Y\Delta X+\Delta X\Delta Y\) along partitions. The first two sums converge to stochastic integrals with predictable left endpoints, while the last sum converges to covariation.

How to read it

The extra covariation term is the product-rule signature of stochastic calculus.

The point is not to memorize the displayed formulas separately, but to understand how the definition, the result, and the proof mechanism fit together.
