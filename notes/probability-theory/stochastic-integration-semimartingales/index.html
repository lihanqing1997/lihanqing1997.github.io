49. Stochastic integration with semimartingales

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Back" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/stochastic-integration-brownian-motion/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/ito-formula/" %}

Integration with respect to semimartingales extends Brownian integration to the natural general class of stochastic integrators.

Predictable simple integration

The definition begins with left-continuous information and increments of the integrator.

Definition. For a semimartingale \(X\) and predictable simple \(H_t=\sum_kH_k1_{(t_k,t_{k+1}]}\), define \[ (H\cdot X)_t=\sum_kH_k(X_{t_{k+1}\wedge t}-X_{t_k\wedge t}). \]

Decomposition of the integral

The semimartingale decomposition defines the integral by combining martingale and finite-variation integration.

Theorem. If \(X=M+A\), where \(M\) is a local martingale and \(A\) has finite variation, then \[ H\cdot X=H\cdot M+H\cdot A \] for predictable integrands in the appropriate integrability class.

Proof. For simple \(H\), the identity is immediate from increments. For finite-variation \(A\), the integral is the pathwise Lebesgue-Stieltjes integral. For \(M\), localize and construct the martingale integral by \(L^2\) approximation on bounded pieces. Compatibility on overlaps ensures the localized definitions patch together.

Associativity

Integrating in stages gives the same result as multiplying integrands.

Proposition. If \(Y=H\cdot X\) and \(K\) is predictable with suitable integrability, then \[ K\cdot Y=(KH)\cdot X. \]

Proof. For simple integrands the identity follows by expanding sums over common refinements of the partitions. The general case follows by approximation in the semimartingale topology, using continuity of the stochastic integral with respect to predictable integrands.

Remark. This associativity is what makes stochastic differential notation algebraically useful.
