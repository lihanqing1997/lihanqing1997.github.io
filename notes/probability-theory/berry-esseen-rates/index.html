19. Berry-Esseen and rates of convergence

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Back" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/central-limit-theorem/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/poisson-convergence-processes/" %}

Berry-Esseen theory quantifies the error in the central limit theorem. It measures how fast distribution functions approach the Gaussian distribution.

Kolmogorov distance

Rates are usually stated in the supremum distance between distribution functions.

Definition. For distribution functions \(F,G\), the Kolmogorov distance is \[ d_K(F,G)=\sup_x|F(x)-G(x)|. \] For standardized sums \(Z_n\), Berry-Esseen bounds \(d_K(\mathcal L(Z_n),\Phi)\).

Berry-Esseen theorem

A finite third absolute moment gives the canonical \(n^{-1/2}\) rate.

Theorem. If \(X_i\) are i.i.d. with \(\mathbb E X_i=0\), \(\mathbb E X_i^2=\sigma^2\gt0\), and \(\rho=\mathbb E|X_i|^3\lt\infty\), then \[ \sup_x\left|P\left(\frac{\sum_{i=1}^nX_i}{\sigma\sqrt n}\le x\right)-\Phi(x)\right| \le C\frac{\rho}{\sigma^3\sqrt n}. \]

Proof. The proof smooths indicators by Fourier inversion. One bounds the difference between the characteristic function of the normalized sum and \(e^{-t^2/2}\) using a third-order Taylor expansion, integrates this difference over \(|t|\le T\), and controls the smoothing error by \(1/T\). Choosing \(T\asymp \sqrt n\,\sigma^3/\rho\) yields the stated rate.

Why the third moment appears

The first neglected term in the characteristic-function expansion is cubic.

Proposition. If \(\mathbb E|X|^3\lt\infty\), then \[ \varphi(u)=1-\frac{\sigma^2u^2}{2}+O(|u|^3\mathbb E|X|^3) \] near zero for centered \(X\).

Proof. Use Taylor's formula with remainder \(|e^{ix}-(1+ix-x^2/2)|\le C|x|^3\). Taking expectations gives the expansion because \(\mathbb E X=0\) and \(\mathbb E X^2=\sigma^2\).

Remark. The \(n^{-1/2}\) rate is generally sharp under third-moment assumptions; lattice corrections and skewness can appear in finer Edgeworth expansions.
