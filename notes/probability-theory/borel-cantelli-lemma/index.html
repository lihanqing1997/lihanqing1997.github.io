15. Borel-Cantelli lemmas and zero-one laws

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Back" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/weak-law-of-large-numbers/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/strong-law-of-large-numbers/" %}

Borel-Cantelli lemmas connect summability of probabilities with almost-sure occurrence infinitely often. They are the basic zero-one mechanism behind many almost-sure results.

Infinitely often events

The event of infinitely many occurrences is a tail event built from limsup sets.

Definition. For events \(A_n\), define \[ \{A_n\ \mathrm{i.o.}\}=\limsup_nA_n=\bigcap_{m\ge1}\bigcup_{n\ge m}A_n. \] It is the event that \(A_n\) occurs for infinitely many \(n\).

Two Borel-Cantelli lemmas

Summable probabilities prevent infinitely many occurrences; independence gives the converse.

Theorem. If \(\sum_nP(A_n)\lt\infty\), then \(P(A_n\ \mathrm{i.o.})=0\). If the \(A_n\) are independent and \(\sum_nP(A_n)=\infty\), then \(P(A_n\ \mathrm{i.o.})=1\).

Proof. For the first part, \[ P(\limsup A_n)\le P\left(\bigcup_{n\ge m}A_n\right)\le\sum_{n\ge m}P(A_n)\to0. \] For the independent converse, \(P(\cap_{n=m}^N A_n^c)=\prod_{n=m}^N(1-P(A_n))\le \exp(-\sum_{n=m}^NP(A_n))\to0\). Hence with probability one at least one \(A_n\) occurs after every \(m\), which is exactly infinitely often.

Zero-one laws

Tail events often have probability only zero or one.

Proposition. If \(X_n\) are independent and \(T\in\bigcap_m\sigma(X_m,X_{m+1},\dots)\), then \(P(T)\in\{0,1\}\).

Proof. Kolmogorov's zero-one law follows because a tail event is independent of every finite initial sigma-field. A monotone-class argument gives independence from \(\sigma(X_1,X_2,\dots)\), which contains the tail event itself. Thus \(T\) is independent of itself, so \(P(T)=P(T)^2\).

Remark. Events such as convergence of \(\sum X_n\), infinitely many exceedances, or limsup growth rates are usually tail events.
