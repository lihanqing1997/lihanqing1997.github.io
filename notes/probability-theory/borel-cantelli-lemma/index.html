Borel-Cantelli Lemmas and Zero-One Laws

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Back" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/weak-law-of-large-numbers/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/strong-law-of-large-numbers/" %}

This page records the core mathematical facts for Borel-Cantelli Lemmas and Zero-One Laws.

Definition. For events \(A_n\), the event \(A_n\ \mathrm{i.o.}\) is \(\limsup_nA_n=\bigcap_m\bigcup_{n\ge m}A_n\). It means infinitely many of the events occur.

Central result. If \(\sum_nP(A_n)\lt\infty\), then \(P(A_n\ \mathrm{i.o.})=0\). If the \(A_n\) are independent and \(\sum_nP(A_n)=\infty\), then \(P(A_n\ \mathrm{i.o.})=1\). Tail events of independent sequences have probability \(0\) or \(1\).

Proof. For the first part, \(P(\bigcup_{n\ge m}A_n)\le\sum_{n\ge m}P(A_n)\to0\). For the independent converse, \(P(\cap_{n=m}^N A_n^c)=\prod_{n=m}^N(1-P(A_n))\le\exp(-\sum_{n=m}^NP(A_n))\to0\). The zero-one law follows because tail events are independent of every finite initial block, hence independent of the whole generated \(\sigma\)-field.

How to read it

Borel-Cantelli converts summability into almost sure eventual behavior. It is a small theorem with enormous reach.

The point is not to memorize the displayed formulas separately, but to understand how the definition, the result, and the proof mechanism fit together.
