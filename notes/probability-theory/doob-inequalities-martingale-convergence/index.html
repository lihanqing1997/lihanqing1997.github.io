Doob inequalities are maximal inequalities for martingales. They turn control of the terminal value into control of the whole path.

Maximal process

The maximal process records the largest excursion up to time \(n\).

Definition. For a process \(X_k\), write \(X_n^\ast=\max_{0\le k\le n}|X_k|\). For a submartingale \(X_k\), maximal inequalities bound \(P(\max_{k\le n}X_k\ge\lambda)\).

Doob maximal inequality

Positive submartingales cannot cross a high level too often without terminal mass.

Theorem. If \(X_n\ge0\) is a submartingale, then for \(\lambda\gt0\), \[ \lambda P\left(\max_{k\le n}X_k\ge\lambda\right)\le \mathbb E[X_n1_{\{\max_{k\le n}X_k\ge\lambda\}}]\le\mathbb E X_n. \]

Proof. Let \(\tau=\inf\{k:X_k\ge\lambda\}\wedge n\). On \(\{\tau\le n\}\), \(X_\tau\ge\lambda\). Optional sampling for the bounded stopping time \(\tau\) gives \(\mathbb E[X_n\mid\mathcal F_\tau]\ge X_\tau\) for submartingales. Therefore \(\lambda P(\tau\le n)\le\mathbb E[X_\tau1_{\{\tau\le n\}}]\le\mathbb E[X_n1_{\{\tau\le n\}}]\).

Convergence theorem

Uniform \(L^1\) control of a submartingale forces almost-sure convergence.

Proposition. If \(X_n\) is a submartingale with \(\sup_n\mathbb E X_n^+\lt\infty\), then \(X_n\) converges almost surely to a finite random variable.

Proof. Doob's upcrossing inequality bounds the expected number of upcrossings of any interval \([a,b]\) by \((b-a)^{-1}\sup_n\mathbb E(X_n-a)^+\). Hence the number of upcrossings is finite almost surely for every rational interval. A real sequence with finitely many upcrossings of every rational interval has a limit in \([-\infty,\infty]\). The positive-part bound excludes \(+\infty\), and integrability excludes \(-\infty\) on a set of positive probability.

Remark. The \(L^p\) maximal inequality for \(p\gt1\) gives \(\|M_n^\ast\|_p\le q\|M_n\|_p\), where \(q=p/(p-1)\).
