Brownian motion generates a rich family of martingales. These martingales encode Gaussian exponential moments, harmonic functions, and quadratic variation.

Basic Brownian martingales

Brownian motion and its compensated square are martingales.

Definition. With respect to its natural filtration, \(B_t\) is a martingale and \(B_t^2-t\) is a martingale.

Gaussian increment proof

Independent centered increments give the martingale identities.

Theorem. For \(s\le t\), \(\mathbb E[B_t\mid\mathcal F_s]=B_s\) and \(\mathbb E[B_t^2-t\mid\mathcal F_s]=B_s^2-s\).

Proof. Write \(B_t=B_s+(B_t-B_s)\), where the increment is independent of \(\mathcal F_s\), centered, and has variance \(t-s\). The first identity is immediate. For the second, expand \(B_t^2\); the cross term has conditional mean zero and the increment square has conditional mean \(t-s\).

Exponential martingales

Gaussian moment generating functions produce positive martingales.

Proposition. For \(\theta\in\mathbb R\), \[ M_t^\theta=\exp\left(\theta B_t-\frac12\theta^2t\right) \] is a martingale.

Proof. For \(s\le t\), split \(B_t=B_s+(B_t-B_s)\). Conditional on \(\mathcal F_s\), the increment is \(N(0,t-s)\), so \[ \mathbb E[e^{\theta(B_t-B_s)}]=e^{\theta^2(t-s)/2}. \] This cancels the deterministic factor between times \(s\) and \(t\), leaving \(M_s^\theta\).

Remark. Exponential martingales are the prototype for Girsanov changes of measure.
