Semimartingales

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Back" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/quadratic-variation-covariation/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/stochastic-integration-brownian-motion/" %}

Definition. A semimartingale is a process \(X=M+A\), where \(M\) is a local martingale and \(A\) has finite variation on compact intervals. Semimartingales are the largest natural class of integrators for stochastic integration.

Result. If \(X\) is a semimartingale and \(H\) is a bounded predictable simple process, then \(\int H\,dX\) is again a semimartingale. The class is stable under stopping and localization.

Proof.

For simple \(H=\sum_iH_i1_{(t_i,t_{i+1}]}\), the integral is \(\sum_iH_i(X_{t_{i+1}}-X_{t_i})\). The martingale part remains a martingale transform because \(H_i\) is known at time \(t_i\); the finite-variation part remains finite variation because bounded predictable weights preserve total variation.

Remarks

Semimartingales are exactly the processes for which the stochastic integral has good stability and approximation properties.
