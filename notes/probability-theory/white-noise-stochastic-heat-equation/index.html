White Noise and Stochastic Heat Equation

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Back" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/local-time-tanaka-formula/" %}

This page records the core mathematical facts for White Noise and Stochastic Heat Equation.

Definition. Space-time white noise \(\xi\) is a centered Gaussian generalized field with covariance \(\mathbb E\xi(\varphi)\xi(\psi)=\int\varphi(t,x)\psi(t,x)dtdx\). It is not an ordinary function.

Central result. For the additive stochastic heat equation \(\partial_tu=\frac12\Delta u+\xi\), the mild solution is \[u(t,x)=\int_0^t\int_{\mathbb R^d}p_{t-s}(x-y)W(ds,dy),\] whenever the stochastic convolution is square-integrable.

Proof. The heat-kernel formulation solves the deterministic part by variation of constants. Itô isometry for the martingale measure gives \(\mathbb E|u(t,x)|^2=\int_0^t\int p_{t-s}(x-y)^2dyds\). Finite variance is the condition for the stochastic integral to exist.

How to read it

White noise forces probability theory to treat random distributions, not only random functions. This is the entrance to modern SPDE theory.

The point is not to memorize the displayed formulas separately, but to understand how the definition, the result, and the proof mechanism fit together.
