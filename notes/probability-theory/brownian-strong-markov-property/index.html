39. Strong Markov property of Brownian motion

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Back" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/reflection-principle-hitting-times/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/brownian-martingales/" %}

The strong Markov property says Brownian motion restarts at stopping times, not only deterministic times. It is the core reason hitting-time arguments work.

Strong Markov statement

After a stopping time, the future increments are fresh Brownian motion.

Definition. If \(\tau\) is a stopping time for the completed right-continuous Brownian filtration and \(\tau\lt\infty\) a.s., then \[ (B_{\tau+t}-B_\tau)_{t\ge0} \] is a Brownian motion independent of \(\mathcal F_\tau\).

Proof by approximation

The result is first proved for simple stopping times, then by approximation from above.

Theorem. Brownian motion has the strong Markov property at every a.s. finite stopping time.

Proof. For stopping times taking countably many deterministic values, the result follows by decomposing over \(\{\tau=t_k\}\) and using independent increments after each deterministic \(t_k\). For a general stopping time, approximate \(\tau\) from above by dyadic stopping times \(\tau_n\downarrow\tau\). Continuity of Brownian paths gives \(B_{\tau_n+t}-B_{\tau_n}\to B_{\tau+t}-B_\tau\), and right-continuity of the filtration identifies the limiting independence and finite-dimensional distributions.

Applications to hitting times

The process restarts when it reaches a level or exits a domain.

Proposition. If \(T_a\lt\infty\), then \((B_{T_a+t}-a)_{t\ge0}\) is standard Brownian motion independent of \(\mathcal F_{T_a}\).

Proof. Apply the strong Markov property to \(\tau=T_a\). On \(\{T_a\lt\infty\}\), continuity gives \(B_{T_a}=a\), so subtracting \(a\) centers the restarted process at zero. The conditional independence and Brownian increment laws are exactly the strong Markov conclusion.

Remark. Strong Markov arguments are usually the cleanest way to decompose paths into independent excursions or cycles.
