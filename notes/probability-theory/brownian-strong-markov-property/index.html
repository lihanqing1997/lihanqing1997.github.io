Strong Markov Property of Brownian Motion

This page records the core mathematical facts for Strong Markov Property of Brownian Motion.

Definition. The strong Markov property says that Brownian motion restarts after a stopping time. If \(\tau\) is a stopping time, then \((B_{\tau+t}-B_\tau)_{t\ge0}\) is a Brownian motion independent of \(\mathcal F_\tau\), on \(\{\tau\lt\infty\}\).

Central result. Brownian motion has the strong Markov property for every almost surely finite stopping time relative to its usual completed right-continuous filtration.

Proof. First prove the result for stopping times taking finitely many deterministic values, where it follows from independent increments. Approximate a general stopping time from above by dyadic-valued stopping times. Right-continuity of paths and of the filtration allows passage to the limit.

How to read it

The strong Markov property is what makes hitting-time arguments and Brownian potential theory work.

The point is not to memorize the displayed formulas separately, but to understand how the definition, the result, and the proof mechanism fit together.
