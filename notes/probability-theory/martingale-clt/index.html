28. Martingale CLT and applications

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Back" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/square-integrable-martingales-quadratic-variation/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/kolmogorov-extension/" %}

Martingale central limit theorems replace independence with conditional mean-zero increments. They are indispensable for dependent data and stochastic approximation.

Martingale difference arrays

The conditional variance process replaces deterministic variance.

Definition. A martingale difference array is a row-wise adapted family \((X_{n,k},\mathcal F_{n,k})\) with \(\mathbb E[X_{n,k}\mid\mathcal F_{n,k-1}]=0\). Its conditional variance is \(V_n=\sum_k\mathbb E[X_{n,k}^2\mid\mathcal F_{n,k-1}]\).

Martingale central limit theorem

Conditional variance convergence plus conditional Lindeberg gives Gaussian fluctuations.

Theorem. If \(V_n\to\sigma^2\) in probability and \[ \sum_k\mathbb E[X_{n,k}^2 1_{\{|X_{n,k}|\gt\varepsilon\}}\mid\mathcal F_{n,k-1}]\to0 \] in probability for every \(\varepsilon\gt0\), then \(\sum_kX_{n,k}\Rightarrow N(0,\sigma^2)\).

Proof. Use conditional characteristic functions. Taylor expand \(\mathbb E[e^{itX_{n,k}}\mid\mathcal F_{n,k-1}]\) as \(1-\frac12t^2\mathbb E[X_{n,k}^2\mid\mathcal F_{n,k-1}]+r_{n,k}\). The conditional Lindeberg condition makes \(\sum_kr_{n,k}\to0\) in probability. Multiplying conditional factors yields convergence of characteristic functions to \(e^{-\sigma^2t^2/2}\).

Stable convergence variant

Random limiting variances lead to mixed normal limits.

Proposition. If \(V_n\to V\) in probability for a nonnegative random variable \(V\), then under strengthened conditional characteristic-function assumptions the limit is conditionally normal with characteristic function \(\mathbb E e^{-t^2V/2}\).

Proof. The same proof gives conditional characteristic functions converging to \(e^{-t^2V/2}\). Taking expectations gives the mixed normal characteristic function. Stable convergence requires checking convergence after multiplication by bounded variables measurable with respect to the limiting background sigma-field.

Remark. Many asymptotic normality proofs in statistics can be read as martingale CLTs after revealing observations sequentially.
