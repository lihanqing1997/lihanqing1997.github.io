39. Reflection principle and hitting times

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Back" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/brownian-path-properties/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/brownian-strong-markov-property/" %}

The reflection principle is a symmetry argument for Brownian paths. It converts maximum and hitting-time questions into one-dimensional Gaussian probabilities.

First hitting times

Brownian hitting times are stopping times for the natural filtration.

Definition. For \(a\in\mathbb R\), define \(T_a=\inf\{t\ge0:B_t=a\}\). For continuous adapted Brownian motion, \(T_a\) is a stopping time.

Reflection principle

Paths crossing a level can be reflected after first hitting it.

Theorem. For \(a\gt0\) and \(t\gt0\), \[ P\left(\sup_{0\le s\le t}B_s\ge a,\ B_t\le b\right)=P(B_t\ge 2a-b) \] for \(b\le a\). Consequently \(P(\sup_{s\le t}B_s\ge a)=2P(B_t\ge a)\).

Proof. On the event \(T_a\le t\), reflect the path after \(T_a\): \(\widetilde B_s=B_s\) for \(s\le T_a\) and \(\widetilde B_s=2a-B_s\) for \(s>T_a\). The strong Markov property and symmetry of Brownian increments imply the reflected path has the same Wiener measure. The endpoint condition \(B_t\le b\) becomes \(\widetilde B_t\ge2a-b\), giving the identity.

Hitting-time distribution

The first passage density follows by differentiating the reflection formula.

Proposition. For \(a\gt0\), \[ P(T_a\le t)=2P(B_t\ge a) \] and \(T_a\) has density \[ f_{T_a}(t)=\frac{a}{\sqrt{2\pi t^3}}e^{-a^2/(2t)},\qquad t\gt0. \]

Proof. The distribution identity is the maximum formula. Since \(B_t\sim N(0,t)\), \(P(T_a\le t)=2(1-\Phi(a/\sqrt t))\). Differentiate with respect to \(t\), using \(\phi'(x)=-x\phi(x)\) through the chain rule, to get the density.

Remark. The same principle also gives the joint law of \((B_t,\sup_{s\le t}B_s)\).
