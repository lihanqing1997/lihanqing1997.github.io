Reflection Principle and Hitting Times

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Back" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/brownian-path-properties/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/brownian-strong-markov-property/" %}

This page records the core mathematical facts for Reflection Principle and Hitting Times.

Definition. Let \(\tau_a=\inf\{t\ge0:B_t=a\}\). The reflection principle relates paths that cross \(a\) before time \(t\) to reflected paths ending above \(a\).

Central result. For \(a>0\), \[P\left(\sup_{s\le t}B_s\ge a\right)=2P(B_t\ge a).\] Hence \(P(\tau_a\le t)=2\{1-\Phi(a/\sqrt t)\}\).

Proof. On the event \(\tau_a\le t\), reflect the path after \(\tau_a\): \(B_s\) becomes \(B_s\) for \(s\le\tau_a\) and \(2a-B_s\) for \(s>\tau_a\). Brownian symmetry and the strong Markov property preserve path probabilities, and this bijection maps paths ending below \(a\) to paths ending above \(a\).

How to read it

The reflection principle turns a path maximum into a one-time Gaussian calculation.

The point is not to memorize the displayed formulas separately, but to understand how the definition, the result, and the proof mechanism fit together.
