Ergodic Theorems

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Back" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/recurrence-transience-invariant-measures/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/renewal-random-walks/" %}

This page records the core mathematical facts for Ergodic Theorems.

Definition. Ergodic theorems turn time averages into space averages. For a stationary process \((X_n)\), one studies \(n^{-1}\sum_{k=1}^nf(X_k)\).

Central result. If an irreducible positive recurrent Markov chain has invariant distribution \(\pi\), then for \(\pi\)-integrable \(f\), \[\frac1n\sum_{k=1}^nf(X_k)\to\int f\,d\pi\quad\text{a.s.}\] from every initial state under standard irreducibility assumptions.

Proof. Use regeneration at returns to a fixed state. The path splits into i.i.d. cycles with finite mean length. Apply the strong law to the total reward in cycles and to cycle lengths, then handle the final incomplete cycle as negligible compared with \(n\).

How to read it

Ergodicity is the mathematical bridge between one long run and an ensemble distribution.

The point is not to memorize the displayed formulas separately, but to understand how the definition, the result, and the proof mechanism fit together.
