Ergodic theorems

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Back" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/recurrence-transience-invariant-measures/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/renewal-random-walks/" %}

Definition. Ergodic theorems turn time averages into space averages. For a stationary process \((X_n)\), one studies \(n^{-1}\sum_{k=1}^nf(X_k)\).

Result. If an irreducible positive recurrent Markov chain has invariant distribution \(\pi\), then for \(\pi\)-integrable \(f\), \[\frac1n\sum_{k=1}^nf(X_k)\to\int f\,d\pi\quad\text{a.s.}\] from every initial state under standard irreducibility assumptions.

Proof.

Use regeneration at returns to a fixed state. The path splits into i.i.d. cycles with finite mean length. Apply the strong law to the total reward in cycles and to cycle lengths, then handle the final incomplete cycle as negligible compared with \(n\).

Remarks

Ergodicity is the mathematical bridge between one long run and an ensemble distribution.
