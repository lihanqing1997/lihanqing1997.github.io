Stochastic Integration with Respect to Brownian Motion

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Back" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/semimartingales/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/stochastic-integration-semimartingales/" %}

This page records the core mathematical facts for Stochastic Integration with Respect to Brownian Motion.

Definition. For a simple adapted process \(H_t=\sum_iH_i1_{(t_i,t_{i+1}]}(t)\), define \(\int_0^T H_t\,dB_t=\sum_iH_i(B_{t_{i+1}}-B_{t_i})\). The construction extends by \(L^2\)-completion to predictable \(H\) with \(\mathbb E\int_0^TH_t^2dt\lt\infty\).

Central result. Itô isometry: \[\mathbb E\left(\int_0^T H_t\,dB_t\right)^2=\mathbb E\int_0^TH_t^2dt.\] The stochastic integral is a centered square-integrable martingale.

Proof. For simple integrands, expand the square. Cross terms vanish because Brownian increments over disjoint intervals are independent and have conditional mean zero. The diagonal terms give \(\mathbb EH_i^2(t_{i+1}-t_i)\). Completion extends the identity to all \(L^2\) predictable integrands.

How to read it

The stochastic integral is not a pathwise Riemann-Stieltjes integral. It is first an isometry in probability space.

The point is not to memorize the displayed formulas separately, but to understand how the definition, the result, and the proof mechanism fit together.
