Itô formula

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Back" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/stochastic-integration-semimartingales/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/applications-ito-formula/" %}

Definition. A continuous Itô process has the form \[ X_t=X_0+\int_0^tb_s\,ds+\int_0^t\sigma_s\,dB_s, \] where \(b\) is progressively measurable with \(\int_0^t|b_s|ds\lt \infty\), and \(\sigma\) is progressively measurable with \(\int_0^t\sigma_s^2ds\lt \infty\), locally in time. Its quadratic variation is \[ [X]_t=\int_0^t\sigma_s^2\,ds. \]

Itô formula. If \(f\in C^2(\mathbb R)\), then \[ f(X_t)=f(X_0)+\int_0^tf'(X_s)\,dX_s +\frac12\int_0^tf''(X_s)\,d[X]_s. \] Equivalently, \[ df(X_t)=f'(X_t)b_t\,dt+f'(X_t)\sigma_t\,dB_t +\frac12f''(X_t)\sigma_t^2\,dt. \]

Proof. First localize so that \(b,\sigma,X\), and the first two derivatives of \(f\) on the range of \(X\), are bounded. Take a partition \(\pi=\{0=t_0\lt \cdots\lt t_n=t\}\). Taylor’s formula gives \[ f(X_{t_{i+1}})-f(X_{t_i}) =f'(X_{t_i})\Delta_iX+\frac12f''(X_{t_i})(\Delta_iX)^2+r_i, \] where \(|r_i|\le \omega_f(|\Delta_iX|)(\Delta_iX)^2\), and \(\omega_f\) is a modulus of continuity of \(f''\) on the localized compact range. Since \(X\) is continuous, \(\max_i|\Delta_iX|\to0\) in probability, so \(\sum_i r_i\to0\) in probability because \(\sum_i(\Delta_iX)^2\) is tight and converges to \([X]_t\).

The first-order sum \(\sum_i f'(X_{t_i})\Delta_iX\) converges to \(\int_0^tf'(X_s)dX_s\) by the definition of stochastic integration for semimartingales. For the second-order sum, decompose \(\Delta_iX=\Delta_iA+\Delta_iM\), where \(A=\int bds\) has finite variation and \(M=\int\sigma dB\) is continuous local martingale. Terms involving \(\Delta_iA\) vanish in quadratic variation, while \(\sum_i(\Delta_iM)^2\to[M]_t=\int_0^t\sigma_s^2ds\). Hence the second-order sum converges to \(\int_0^tf''(X_s)d[X]_s\). Removing the localization gives the general formula.

What changes from ordinary calculus

For deterministic \(C^1\) paths, \((\Delta X)^2\) is negligible. For Brownian-driven paths, \(\Delta B\) is of order \(\sqrt{\Delta t}\), so \((\Delta B)^2\) is of order \(\Delta t\). This is the source of the extra \(\frac12f''\,d[X]\) term.

Formule d’Itô

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Retour" prev_label="Page précédente" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/stochastic-integration-semimartingales/" next_label="Page suivante" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/applications-ito-formula/" %}

Définition. Un processus d’Itô continu s’écrit \[ X_t=X_0+\int_0^tb_s\,ds+\int_0^t\sigma_s\,dB_s, \] où \(b\) est progressivement mesurable avec \(\int_0^t|b_s|ds\lt \infty\), et \(\sigma\) est progressivement mesurable avec \(\int_0^t\sigma_s^2ds\lt \infty\), localement en temps. Sa variation quadratique est \[ [X]_t=\int_0^t\sigma_s^2\,ds. \]

Formule d’Itô. Si \(f\in C^2(\mathbb R)\), alors \[ f(X_t)=f(X_0)+\int_0^tf'(X_s)\,dX_s +\frac12\int_0^tf''(X_s)\,d[X]_s. \] De façon équivalente, \[ df(X_t)=f'(X_t)b_t\,dt+f'(X_t)\sigma_t\,dB_t +\frac12f''(X_t)\sigma_t^2\,dt. \]

Preuve. On localise d’abord pour que \(b,\sigma,X\) et les deux premières dérivées de \(f\) sur l’image de \(X\) soient bornés. Pour une partition \(\pi=\{0=t_0\lt \cdots\lt t_n=t\}\), la formule de Taylor donne \[ f(X_{t_{i+1}})-f(X_{t_i}) =f'(X_{t_i})\Delta_iX+\frac12f''(X_{t_i})(\Delta_iX)^2+r_i, \] avec \(|r_i|\le \omega_f(|\Delta_iX|)(\Delta_iX)^2\), où \(\omega_f\) est un module de continuité de \(f''\) sur le compact localisé. Comme \(X\) est continu, \(\max_i|\Delta_iX|\to0\) en probabilité; donc \(\sum_i r_i\to0\) en probabilité, puisque \(\sum_i(\Delta_iX)^2\) est tendue et converge vers \([X]_t\).

La somme du premier ordre \(\sum_i f'(X_{t_i})\Delta_iX\) converge vers \(\int_0^tf'(X_s)dX_s\) par définition de l’intégrale semimartingale. Pour la somme du second ordre, on écrit \(\Delta_iX=\Delta_iA+\Delta_iM\), où \(A=\int bds\) est à variation finie et \(M=\int\sigma dB\) est une martingale locale continue. Les termes contenant \(\Delta_iA\) disparaissent en variation quadratique, tandis que \(\sum_i(\Delta_iM)^2\to[M]_t=\int_0^t\sigma_s^2ds\). La somme du second ordre converge donc vers \(\int_0^tf''(X_s)d[X]_s\). On enlève ensuite la localisation.

Différence avec le calcul ordinaire

Pour des trajectoires déterministes \(C^1\), \((\Delta X)^2\) est négligeable. Pour des trajectoires conduites par Brownian motion, \(\Delta B\) est de taille \(\sqrt{\Delta t}\), donc \((\Delta B)^2\) est de taille \(\Delta t\). C’est l’origine du terme supplémentaire \(\frac12f''\,d[X]\).

Itô 公式

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="返回" prev_label="上一页" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/stochastic-integration-semimartingales/" next_label="下一页" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/applications-ito-formula/" %}

定义. 连续 Itô 过程是指形如 \[ X_t=X_0+\int_0^tb_s\,ds+\int_0^t\sigma_s\,dB_s \] 的过程，其中 \(b\) 渐进可测且局部满足 \(\int_0^t|b_s|ds\lt \infty\)，\(\sigma\) 渐进可测且局部满足 \(\int_0^t\sigma_s^2ds\lt \infty\)。它的二次变差为 \[ [X]_t=\int_0^t\sigma_s^2\,ds. \]

Itô 公式. 若 \(f\in C^2(\mathbb R)\)，则 \[ f(X_t)=f(X_0)+\int_0^tf'(X_s)\,dX_s +\frac12\int_0^tf''(X_s)\,d[X]_s. \] 等价地， \[ df(X_t)=f'(X_t)b_t\,dt+f'(X_t)\sigma_t\,dB_t +\frac12f''(X_t)\sigma_t^2\,dt. \]

证明. 先局部化，使得 \(b,\sigma,X\) 以及 \(f\) 在 \(X\) 的取值范围上的前两阶导数都有界。取分割 \(\pi=\{0=t_0\lt \cdots\lt t_n=t\}\)。Taylor 公式给出 \[ f(X_{t_{i+1}})-f(X_{t_i}) =f'(X_{t_i})\Delta_iX+\frac12f''(X_{t_i})(\Delta_iX)^2+r_i, \] 其中 \(|r_i|\le \omega_f(|\Delta_iX|)(\Delta_iX)^2\)，\(\omega_f\) 是 \(f''\) 在局部化紧集上的连续模。由于 \(X\) 连续，\(\max_i|\Delta_iX|\to0\) 依概率成立；又 \(\sum_i(\Delta_iX)^2\) 紧且收敛到 \([X]_t\)，所以 \(\sum_i r_i\to0\) 依概率成立。

一阶和 \(\sum_i f'(X_{t_i})\Delta_iX\) 按半鞅随机积分的定义收敛到 \(\int_0^tf'(X_s)dX_s\)。对二阶和，写 \(\Delta_iX=\Delta_iA+\Delta_iM\)，其中 \(A=\int bds\) 有限变差，\(M=\int\sigma dB\) 是连续局部鞅。含 \(\Delta_iA\) 的项在二次变差中消失，而 \(\sum_i(\Delta_iM)^2\to[M]_t=\int_0^t\sigma_s^2ds\)。所以二阶和收敛到 \(\int_0^tf''(X_s)d[X]_s\)。最后去掉局部化即可。

与普通微积分的差别

对确定性的 \(C^1\) 路径，\((\Delta X)^2\) 可忽略。对 Brownian motion 驱动的路径，\(\Delta B\) 的量级是 \(\sqrt{\Delta t}\)，所以 \((\Delta B)^2\) 的量级是 \(\Delta t\)。这就是额外项 \(\frac12f''\,d[X]\) 的来源。
