Itô formula

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Back" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/stochastic-integration-semimartingales/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/applications-ito-formula/" %}

Definition. Let \(X_t=X_0+\int_0^tb_sds+\int_0^t\sigma_sdB_s\) be a continuous Itô process. The path has drift part of order \(dt\) and martingale part of order \(\sqrt{dt}\).

Result. For \(f\in C^2(\mathbb R)\), \[df(X_t)=f'(X_t)dX_t+\frac12f''(X_t)d[X]_t,\] or equivalently \[f(X_t)=f(X_0)+\int_0^tf'(X_s)dX_s+\frac12\int_0^tf''(X_s)d[X]_s.\]

Proof.

Taylor expand over a partition: \(\Delta f=f'\Delta X+\frac12f''(\Delta X)^2+r\). The finite-variation square terms vanish, the martingale square terms converge to \([X]\), and the remainder is negligible by continuity and localization. Passing to the limit gives the formula.

Remarks

Itô formula is ordinary Taylor expansion plus the rule \((dB_t)^2=dt\).
