Girsanov theorem and change of measure

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Back" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/applications-ito-formula/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/stochastic-differential-equations/" %}

Definition. Girsanov's theorem describes how Brownian drift changes under an equivalent probability measure. Let \(\theta_t\) be adapted and satisfy a condition such as Novikov's criterion. Define \(Z_t=\exp\{-\int_0^t\theta_sdB_s-\frac12\int_0^t\theta_s^2ds\}\).

Result. If \((Z_t)\) is a martingale and \(dQ=Z_TdP\) on \(\mathcal F_T\), then \[\widetilde B_t=B_t+\int_0^t\theta_sds\] is a Brownian motion under \(Q\) on \([0,T]\).

Proof.

Use exponential martingales to compute conditional characteristic functions of increments under \(Q\). The density tilts the Gaussian increment by a linear exponential factor, shifting its mean while preserving its variance. Localization and approximation extend the argument to adapted \(\theta\).

Remarks

Girsanov is the continuous-time likelihood-ratio theorem. It is indispensable for diffusion statistics and stochastic control.
