51. Girsanov theorem and change of measure

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Back" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/applications-ito-formula/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/stochastic-differential-equations/" %}

Definition. For a progressively measurable process \(\theta\) with \(\int_0^T\theta_s^2\mathrm{d}s\lt \infty\), define the stochastic exponential \[ Z_t=\mathcal E\left(\int_0^\cdot \theta_s\,\mathrm{d}B_s\right)_t =\exp\left\{\int_0^t\theta_s\,\mathrm{d}B_s-\frac12\int_0^t\theta_s^2\,\mathrm{d}s\right\}. \] If \(\mathbb E Z_T=1\), then \(Q\) defined by \(\mathrm{d}Q=Z_T\,\mathrm{d}P\) is a probability measure equivalent to \(P\) on \(\mathcal F_T\).

Girsanov theorem. Under the above assumptions, if \(Z\) is a true martingale, then \[ \widetilde B_t=B_t-\int_0^t\theta_s\,\mathrm{d}s,\qquad 0\le t\le T, \] is a Brownian motion under \(Q\).

Proof. The density process \(Z_t=\mathbb E_P(Z_T\mid\mathcal F_t)\) satisfies \(\mathrm{d}Z_t=Z_t\theta_t\,\mathrm{d}B_t\). For bounded predictable \(H\), Bayes’ rule gives \[ \mathbb E_Q\left(\int_s^tH_u\,\mathrm{d}\widetilde B_u\mid\mathcal F_s\right) =\frac1{Z_s}\mathbb E_P\left[Z_t\int_s^tH_u(\mathrm{d}B_u-\theta_u\,\mathrm{d}u)\mid\mathcal F_s\right]=0, \] where the last equality follows from integration by parts applied to \(Z\int H\,\mathrm{d}\widetilde B\). Hence \(\widetilde B\) is a continuous \(Q\)-local martingale. Its quadratic variation is unchanged by adding a finite-variation drift: \[ [\widetilde B]_t=[B]_t=t. \] Lévy’s characterization then implies that \(\widetilde B\) is \(Q\)-Brownian motion.

Novikov condition

A common sufficient condition for \(\mathbb E Z_T=1\) is \[ \mathbb E\exp\left\{\frac12\int_0^T\theta_s^2\mathrm{d}s\right\}\lt \infty. \] Girsanov says that an absolutely continuous change of measure can turn drift into a likelihood ratio.

51. Théorème de Girsanov et changement de mesure

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Retour" prev_label="Page précédente" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/applications-ito-formula/" next_label="Page suivante" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/stochastic-differential-equations/" %}

Définition. Pour un processus progressivement mesurable \(\theta\) tel que \(\int_0^T\theta_s^2\mathrm{d}s\lt \infty\), on définit l’exponentielle stochastique \[ Z_t=\mathcal E\left(\int_0^\cdot \theta_s\,\mathrm{d}B_s\right)_t =\exp\left\{\int_0^t\theta_s\,\mathrm{d}B_s-\frac12\int_0^t\theta_s^2\,\mathrm{d}s\right\}. \] Si \(\mathbb E Z_T=1\), alors \(Q\) défini par \(\mathrm{d}Q=Z_T\,\mathrm{d}P\) est une probabilité équivalente à \(P\) sur \(\mathcal F_T\).

Théorème de Girsanov. Sous les hypothèses précédentes, si \(Z\) est une vraie martingale, alors \[ \widetilde B_t=B_t-\int_0^t\theta_s\,\mathrm{d}s,\qquad 0\le t\le T, \] est un mouvement brownien sous \(Q\).

Preuve. Le processus de densité \(Z_t=\mathbb E_P(Z_T\mid\mathcal F_t)\) vérifie \(\mathrm{d}Z_t=Z_t\theta_t\,\mathrm{d}B_t\). Pour \(H\) prévisible borné, la règle de Bayes donne \[ \mathbb E_Q\left(\int_s^tH_u\,\mathrm{d}\widetilde B_u\mid\mathcal F_s\right) =\frac1{Z_s}\mathbb E_P\left[Z_t\int_s^tH_u(\mathrm{d}B_u-\theta_u\,\mathrm{d}u)\mid\mathcal F_s\right]=0, \] la dernière égalité venant de l’intégration par parties appliquée à \(Z\int H\,\mathrm{d}\widetilde B\). Donc \(\widetilde B\) est une martingale locale continue sous \(Q\). Sa variation quadratique ne change pas lorsqu’on ajoute une dérive à variation finie : \[ [\widetilde B]_t=[B]_t=t. \] La caractérisation de Lévy implique alors que \(\widetilde B\) est un mouvement brownien sous \(Q\).

Condition de Novikov

Une condition suffisante courante pour \(\mathbb E Z_T=1\) est \[ \mathbb E\exp\left\{\frac12\int_0^T\theta_s^2\mathrm{d}s\right\}\lt \infty. \] Le théorème de Girsanov dit qu’un changement de mesure absolument continu peut transformer une dérive en rapport de vraisemblance.

51. Girsanov 定理与测度变换

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="返回" prev_label="上一页" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/applications-ito-formula/" next_label="下一页" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/stochastic-differential-equations/" %}

定义. 对渐进可测过程 \(\theta\)，若 \(\int_0^T\theta_s^2\mathrm{d}s\lt \infty\)，定义随机指数 \[ Z_t=\mathcal E\left(\int_0^\cdot \theta_s\,\mathrm{d}B_s\right)_t =\exp\left\{\int_0^t\theta_s\,\mathrm{d}B_s-\frac12\int_0^t\theta_s^2\,\mathrm{d}s\right\}. \] 若 \(\mathbb E Z_T=1\)，则由 \(\mathrm{d}Q=Z_T\,\mathrm{d}P\) 定义的 \(Q\) 是 \(\mathcal F_T\) 上与 \(P\) 等价的概率测度。

Girsanov 定理. 在上述假设下，若 \(Z\) 是真鞅，则 \[ \widetilde B_t=B_t-\int_0^t\theta_s\,\mathrm{d}s,\qquad 0\le t\le T, \] 在 \(Q\) 下是 Brownian motion。

证明. 密度过程 \(Z_t=\mathbb E_P(Z_T\mid\mathcal F_t)\) 满足 \(\mathrm{d}Z_t=Z_t\theta_t\,\mathrm{d}B_t\)。对有界可预测 \(H\)，Bayes 公式给出 \[ \mathbb E_Q\left(\int_s^tH_u\,\mathrm{d}\widetilde B_u\mid\mathcal F_s\right) =\frac1{Z_s}\mathbb E_P\left[Z_t\int_s^tH_u(\mathrm{d}B_u-\theta_u\,\mathrm{d}u)\mid\mathcal F_s\right]=0, \] 最后一步来自对 \(Z\int H\,\mathrm{d}\widetilde B\) 做分部积分。因此 \(\widetilde B\) 在 \(Q\) 下是连续局部鞅。加入有限变差漂移不改变二次变差： \[ [\widetilde B]_t=[B]_t=t. \] 由 Lévy 表征，\(\widetilde B\) 是 \(Q\)-Brownian motion。

Novikov 条件

保证 \(\mathbb E Z_T=1\) 的常用充分条件是 \[ \mathbb E\exp\left\{\frac12\int_0^T\theta_s^2\mathrm{d}s\right\}\lt \infty. \] Girsanov 定理说明：绝对连续测度变换可以把漂移转化为似然比。
