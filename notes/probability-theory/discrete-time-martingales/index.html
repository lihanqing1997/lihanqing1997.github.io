Martingales formalize fair games relative to a filtration. Their power comes from combining conditional expectation with time.

Martingales and submartingales

The defining condition says the best future prediction is the current value.

Definition. An adapted integrable process \((M_n,\mathcal F_n)\) is a martingale if \(\mathbb E[M_{n+1}\mid\mathcal F_n]=M_n\). It is a submartingale if the conditional expectation is at least \(M_n\), and a supermartingale if it is at most \(M_n\).

Martingale transforms

Predictable betting strategies preserve fairness.

Theorem. If \(M_n\) is a martingale and \(H_n\) is bounded and \(\mathcal F_{n-1}\)-measurable, then \[ (H\cdot M)_n=\sum_{k=1}^nH_k(M_k-M_{k-1}) \] is a martingale.

Proof. The increment \(H_k(M_k-M_{k-1})\) is integrable and adapted. Its conditional expectation given \(\mathcal F_{k-1}\) is \(H_k\mathbb E[M_k-M_{k-1}\mid\mathcal F_{k-1}]=0\), using predictability of \(H_k\). Summing zero-mean conditional increments gives the martingale property.

Doob decomposition

Every submartingale decomposes into a martingale plus predictable increasing drift.

Proposition. In discrete time, an integrable submartingale \(X_n\) has a unique decomposition \(X_n=M_n+A_n\), where \(M_n\) is a martingale, \(A_0=0\), and \(A_n\) is predictable increasing.

Proof. Define \(A_n=\sum_{k=1}^n \mathbb E[X_k-X_{k-1}\mid\mathcal F_{k-1}]\). Submartingality makes the summands nonnegative, and predictability is immediate. Then \(M_n=X_n-A_n\) has conditional mean \(M_{n-1}\). Uniqueness follows because the difference of two such decompositions is both a predictable finite-variation martingale and must have zero increments.

Remark. Submartingales are martingales with compensators; this idea becomes central in continuous time.
