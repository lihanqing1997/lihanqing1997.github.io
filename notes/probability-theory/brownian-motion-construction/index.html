Construction of Brownian motion

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Back" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/gaussian-vectors-processes/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/brownian-path-properties/" %}

Definition. A standard Brownian motion \((B_t)_{t\ge0}\) satisfies \(B_0=0\), has independent increments, \(B_t-B_s\sim N(0,t-s)\), and has continuous paths.

Result. Brownian motion exists. Its finite-dimensional distributions are centered Gaussian with covariance \(\mathbb E(B_sB_t)=s\wedge t\), and they admit a continuous modification.

Proof.

The covariance kernel \(s\wedge t\) is positive semidefinite, so Kolmogorov extension gives a Gaussian process with the desired finite-dimensional laws. Moment estimates \(\mathbb E|B_t-B_s|^4=3|t-s|^2\) imply, by Kolmogorov's continuity criterion, the existence of a continuous modification.

Remarks

Construction has two layers: finite-dimensional Gaussian consistency creates the process; moment estimates move it into a continuous path space.
