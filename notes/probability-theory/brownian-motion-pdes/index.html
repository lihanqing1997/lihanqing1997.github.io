Brownian motion and parabolic PDEs are linked by the heat semigroup. Probabilistic conditioning becomes analytic evolution.

Heat semigroup

Brownian transition densities solve the heat equation.

Definition. For bounded measurable \(f\), define \(P_tf(x)=\mathbb E_x f(B_t)\). In \(\mathbb R^d\), \[ P_tf(x)=\int_{\mathbb R^d}f(y)(2\pi t)^{-d/2}e^{-|y-x|^2/(2t)}\,\mathrm{d}y. \]

Generator of Brownian motion

The infinitesimal generator is one half of the Laplacian.

Theorem. For \(f\in C_b^2(\mathbb R^d)\), \[ \lim_{t\downarrow0}\frac{P_tf(x)-f(x)}{t}=\frac12\Delta f(x). \]

Proof. Use Taylor expansion \(f(x+h)=f(x)+\nabla f(x)\cdot h+\frac12h^\top D^2f(x)h+o(|h|^2)\). With \(h=B_t-B_0\), the linear term has mean zero and \(\mathbb E[h_ih_j]=t\delta_{ij}\), yielding \(\frac12t\Delta f(x)+o(t)\). Divide by \(t\).

Dirichlet problem

Exit distributions represent harmonic functions.

Proposition. If \(D\) is a regular bounded domain, \(\tau_D=\inf\{t:B_t\notin D\}\), and \(g\) is continuous on \(\partial D\), then \[ u(x)=\mathbb E_x g(B_{\tau_D}) \] solves \(\Delta u=0\) in \(D\) with boundary values \(g\).

Proof. For smooth \(u\), Itô's formula gives \(u(B_{t\wedge\tau_D})=u(x)+\int_0^{t\wedge\tau_D}\nabla u(B_s)\,\mathrm{d}B_s+\frac12\int_0^{t\wedge\tau_D}\Delta u(B_s)\,\mathrm{d}s\). Harmonicity makes the drift vanish, so optional stopping identifies \(u(x)\) with expected boundary values. The general result follows by approximation and regularity of the domain.

Remark. Feynman-Kac adds potentials and source terms, turning PDE solutions into weighted Brownian expectations.
