Brownian motion and PDEs

{% include probability-note-nav.html back_label="Back" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/probability-theory/multidimensional-brownian-motion/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/probability-theory/continuous-time-martingales-local-martingales/" %}

Definition. Brownian motion connects probability to the heat equation. If \(P_tf(x)=\mathbb E_xf(B_t)\), then \(P_t\) is the heat semigroup with generator \(\frac12\Delta\).

Result. If \(f\) is bounded and continuous, then \(u(t,x)=\mathbb E_xf(B_t)\) solves \(\partial_tu=\frac12\Delta u\) with initial condition \(u(0,x)=f(x)\), in the appropriate classical or weak sense.

Proof.

For smooth \(f\), Itô's formula gives \(df(B_t)=\nabla f(B_t)dB_t+\frac12\Delta f(B_t)dt\). Taking expectations removes the martingale term and yields the heat equation. General \(f\) follows by approximation and semigroup continuity.

Remarks

The PDE sees infinitesimal averaging; Brownian motion supplies the random paths whose averaging produces the solution.
