21. Bregman divergences

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/f-divergences-information-monotonicity/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/dually-flat-manifolds/" %}

Bregman divergences are generated by convex functions. They are the analytic model for dually flat information geometry.

Bregman divergence

Definition. For a smooth strictly convex function \(F\) on a convex domain, \[ B_F(\theta:\theta')=F(\theta)-F(\theta')-(\theta-\theta')^\top\nabla F(\theta'). \]

Nonnegativity

Proposition. Convexity implies \(B_F(\theta:\theta')\ge0\), with equality exactly when \(\theta=\theta'\) for strictly convex \(F\).

Hessian metric

Theorem. The local metric induced by \(B_F\) is the Hessian metric \[ g_{ij}(\theta)=\partial_i\partial_jF(\theta). \]

Geometry encoded by convexity

Remark. Bregman geometry replaces Euclidean squared distance by the gap between a convex function and its tangent plane.
