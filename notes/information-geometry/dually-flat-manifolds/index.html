22. Dually flat manifolds

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/bregman-divergences/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/legendre-duality-potential-functions/" %}

Dually flat manifolds are the main exactly computable spaces in information geometry. Both dual affine connections are flat, but in different coordinate systems.

Dual flatness

Definition. A statistical manifold \((M,g,\nabla,\nabla^\ast)\) is dually flat if both \(\nabla\) and \(\nabla^\ast\) are flat and torsion-free.

Affine coordinate systems

Proposition. On a simply connected dually flat manifold, there exist \(\nabla\)-affine coordinates \(\theta\) and \(\nabla^\ast\)-affine coordinates \(\eta\). The two coordinate systems are dual with respect to the metric.

Hessian metric form

Theorem. In \(\theta\)-affine coordinates, the metric is locally Hessian: \[ g_{ij}(\theta)=\partial_i\partial_jF(\theta) \] for a convex potential \(F\).

Why this matters

Remark. Dually flat geometry supplies global coordinates, explicit divergences, projection theorems, and Pythagorean identities. It is the workhorse case of information geometry.
