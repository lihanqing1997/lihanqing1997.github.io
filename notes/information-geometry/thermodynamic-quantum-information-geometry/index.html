30. Thermodynamic and quantum information geometry

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/chernoff-information-geodesic-testing/" %}

Thermodynamic and quantum examples show why information geometry reaches beyond classical parametric statistics, while still using the same metric and divergence ideas.

Gibbs family

Definition. For observables \(X_1,\ldots,X_d\), a Gibbs family has the form \[ \rho_\lambda=\frac{1}{Z(\lambda)}\exp\{-\lambda^iX_i\}, \qquad Z(\lambda)=\operatorname{tr}\exp\{-\lambda^iX_i\}. \]

Covariance metric

Proposition. In the classical commuting case, the Fisher metric of a Gibbs family is the covariance matrix of the sufficient statistics: \[ g_{ij}=\operatorname{Cov}_\lambda(X_i,X_j)=\partial_i\partial_j\log Z(\lambda). \]

Thermodynamic length

Remark. The Fisher length of a path of equilibrium states is called thermodynamic length in statistical mechanics. It measures how many distinguishable equilibrium states are traversed along the path.

Quantum extension

Remark. For density matrices, noncommutativity produces several possible quantum information metrics, such as Bures-type metrics. The classical Fisher metric is recovered in the commuting case.
