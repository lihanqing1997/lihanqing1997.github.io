Information geometry extends beyond classical statistical models. Thermodynamic geometry and quantum information geometry keep the same themes: states, distinguishability, monotonicity, and convex duality.

Thermodynamic potentials

Equilibrium thermodynamics has a Hessian geometry built from convex potentials.

Definition. For an exponential-family form of equilibrium states, the log-partition function \(\psi(\theta)\) generates expectation coordinates and a Hessian metric \[ g_{ij}=\partial_i\partial_j\psi. \] This metric is the covariance matrix of the sufficient statistics or observables.

Proposition. Thermodynamic susceptibilities are entries of the Fisher/Hessian metric.

Proof. Differentiating expectation parameters \(\eta_i=\partial_i\psi\) with respect to natural parameters gives \(\partial_j\eta_i=\partial_i\partial_j\psi\). In exponential-family thermodynamics this derivative is the covariance of the corresponding observables, i.e. a susceptibility.

Quantum states

Quantum information geometry replaces probability distributions by density matrices.

Definition. A finite-dimensional quantum state is a positive semidefinite matrix \(\rho\) with \(\operatorname{tr}\rho=1\). A quantum channel is a completely positive trace-preserving linear map \(\Phi\).

Remark. Noncommutativity makes tangent directions and likelihood ratios more subtle than in the classical simplex.

Monotone quantum metrics

Unlike the classical case, monotone quantum metrics are not unique.

Classification. Petz's theorem classifies monotone Riemannian metrics on quantum state spaces by operator-monotone functions. The key mechanism is that monotonicity under every quantum channel forces the metric to be represented by an operator mean acting on left and right multiplication by \(\rho\). The symmetric logarithmic derivative metric and the Bogoliubov-Kubo-Mori metric are important examples.

Classical limit

When all density matrices commute, quantum geometry reduces to classical information geometry.

Proposition. On a commuting family of density matrices, every monotone quantum metric restricts to a constant multiple of the classical Fisher metric.

Proof. Commuting density matrices are simultaneously diagonalizable and therefore equivalent to probability vectors. Quantum channels preserving the commutative algebra include classical Markov kernels. Cencov uniqueness then forces the classical restriction to be Fisher up to scale.
