19. f-divergences and information monotonicity

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/kl-divergence-canonical-example/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/bregman-divergences/" %}

The f-divergences are the canonical separable divergences monotone under Markov maps. They connect information geometry back to the data-processing principle.

f-divergence

Definition. For a convex function \(f\) with \(f(1)=0\), the f-divergence is \[ D_f(P:Q)=\int q(x)f\!\left(\frac{p(x)}{q(x)}\right)\,\mathrm{d}x. \]

Information monotonicity

Theorem. For every Markov kernel \(K\), \[ D_f(PK:QK)\le D_f(P:Q). \] Thus randomized postprocessing cannot increase an f-divergence.

Proof by Jensen

Proof. The likelihood ratio after applying a Markov kernel is a conditional expectation of the original likelihood ratio. Convexity of \(f\) and Jensen's inequality give the contraction.

Local Fisher metric

Corollary. All smooth f-divergences induce the Fisher metric up to the scalar factor \(f''(1)\). Their higher-order terms may induce different alpha-geometric behavior.
