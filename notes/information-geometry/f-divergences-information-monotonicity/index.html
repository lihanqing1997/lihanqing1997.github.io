\(f\)-divergences are a large family of divergences satisfying data processing. Locally, all smooth \(f\)-divergences generate Fisher information up to scale.

Convex generators

An \(f\)-divergence compares likelihood ratios through a convex function.

Definition. For convex \(f\) with \(f(1)=0\), \[ D_f(P\|Q)=\int f\left(\frac{\mathrm{d}P}{\mathrm{d}Q}\right)\,\mathrm{d}Q \] when \(P\ll Q\), with standard extensions otherwise.

Example. KL divergence corresponds to \(f(u)=u\log u\). Squared Hellinger distance corresponds, up to convention, to \(f(u)=(\sqrt u-1)^2\).

Data processing

Convexity gives monotonicity under Markov kernels.

Theorem. For any Markov kernel \(K\), \[ D_f(KP\|KQ)\le D_f(P\|Q). \]

Proof. The likelihood ratio after applying \(K\) is a conditional expectation of the original likelihood ratio under the \(Q\)-joint law. Jensen's inequality for convex \(f\) gives the pointwise conditional bound, and integrating with respect to \(KQ\) gives the result.

Local Fisher metric

All smooth \(f\)-divergences share the Fisher metric locally.

Proposition. For a regular model, \[ D_f(p_\theta\|p_{\theta+\mathrm{d}\theta}) =\frac{f''(1)}{2}g_{ij}\mathrm{d}\theta^i\mathrm{d}\theta^j+o(\|\mathrm{d}\theta\|^2). \]

Proof. The likelihood ratio between nearby distributions is \(1+s_i\mathrm{d}\theta^i+O(\|\mathrm{d}\theta\|^2)\). Taylor expand \(f\) at \(1\). The constant term vanishes and the linear term integrates to zero by normalization. The quadratic term gives \(f''(1)\mathbb E(s_is_j)\mathrm{d}\theta^i\mathrm{d}\theta^j/2\).

Why KL is still special

KL is not the only monotone divergence, but it has special additive and variational properties.

Remark. The chain rule, variational formulas, exponential-family projections, and likelihood asymptotics make KL especially central even though Fisher local geometry is shared by many divergences.
