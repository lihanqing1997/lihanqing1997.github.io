27. Exponential families as dually flat manifolds

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/information-projections/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/mixture-families-mixture-geometry/" %}

Regular exponential families are the central dually flat statistical manifolds. Natural and expectation parameters are dual affine coordinates, and KL divergence is the canonical Bregman divergence.

Natural form

The log-partition function is the convex potential.

Definition. A regular exponential family has densities \[ p_\theta(x)=\exp\{\theta^iT_i(x)-\psi(\theta)+c(x)\}. \] The natural parameter is \(\theta\), and \(\psi(\theta)=\log\int e^{\theta^iT_i(x)+c(x)}\,\mathrm{d}\mu(x)\).

Proposition. The expectation parameter is \[ \eta_i=\mathbb E_\theta T_i=\partial_i\psi(\theta). \]

Proof. Differentiate the normalizing identity \(1=\int e^{\theta^iT_i-\psi(\theta)+c}\,\mathrm{d}\mu\). The derivative gives \(0=\mathbb E_\theta T_i-\partial_i\psi(\theta)\).

Fisher metric as Hessian

The cumulant potential generates the Fisher metric.

Theorem. For a regular exponential family, \[ g_{ij}=\partial_i\partial_j\psi(\theta)=\operatorname{Cov}_\theta(T_i,T_j). \]

Proof. The score is \(s_i=T_i-\partial_i\psi\). Its covariance is \(\operatorname{Cov}(T_i,T_j)\). Differentiating \(\eta_i=\partial_i\psi=\mathbb E T_i\) gives \(\partial_j\eta_i=\operatorname{Cov}(T_i,T_j)\), hence the Hessian identity.

KL as Bregman divergence

KL between two members is the Bregman divergence of \(\psi\).

Proposition. For \(p_{\theta_1},p_{\theta_2}\), \[ D(p_{\theta_1}\|p_{\theta_2}) =\psi(\theta_2)-\psi(\theta_1)-(\theta_2^i-\theta_1^i)\eta_i(\theta_1). \]

Proof. Take the expectation under \(p_{\theta_1}\) of \(\log p_{\theta_1}-\log p_{\theta_2}\). Terms involving \(c(x)\) cancel, and \(\mathbb E_{\theta_1}T_i=\eta_i(\theta_1)\).

Flatness

Natural parameters are exponential-affine; expectation parameters are mixture-affine.

Remark. This dual flatness is why exponential families are the canonical playground for information projections and maximum entropy.
