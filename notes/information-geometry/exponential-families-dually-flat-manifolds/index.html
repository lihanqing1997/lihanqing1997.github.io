27. Exponential families as dually flat manifolds

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/information-projections/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/mixture-families-mixture-geometry/" %}

Regular exponential families are the fundamental statistical examples of dually flat manifolds. The log-partition function is the primal potential.

Exponential family form

Definition. A full regular exponential family has densities \[ p_\theta(x)=\exp\{\theta\cdot T(x)-F(\theta)+h(x)\}, \] where \(F(\theta)=\log\int e^{\theta\cdot T(x)+h(x)}\,\mathrm{d}x\).

Expectation coordinates

Proposition. The dual coordinates are \[ \eta_i=\partial_iF(\theta)=\mathbb E_\theta T_i(X). \] Thus natural parameters and expectation parameters are Legendre dual.

Fisher metric as Hessian

Theorem. For a regular exponential family, \[ g_{ij}(\theta)=\partial_i\partial_jF(\theta)=\operatorname{Cov}_\theta(T_i,T_j). \]

Proof by partition differentiation

Proof. Differentiating \(F=\log Z\) once gives the mean of the sufficient statistic. Differentiating again gives its covariance. The score is \(T-\mathbb E_\theta T\), so the covariance is Fisher information.
