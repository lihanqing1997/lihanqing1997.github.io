3. Fisher information metric

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/tangent-spaces-score-representation/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/fisher-metric-local-distinguishability/" %}

The Fisher information matrix is not merely a matrix attached to parameters. It is the coordinate expression of an intrinsic Riemannian metric on the model.

Metric tensor

Definition. The Fisher information metric is \[ g_{ij}(\theta)=\mathbb E_\theta[\partial_i\ell_\theta\,\partial_j\ell_\theta]. \] For tangent vectors \(u=u^i\partial_i\) and \(v=v^j\partial_j\), \[ g_\theta(u,v)=\mathbb E_\theta[(u^is_i)(v^js_j)]. \]

Negative Hessian identity

Proposition. Under standard regularity conditions, \[ g_{ij}(\theta)=-\mathbb E_\theta[\partial_i\partial_j\ell_\theta]. \] Thus Fisher information is both a score covariance and an expected curvature of log likelihood.

Proof by differentiating the score mean

Proof. The identity \(\mathbb E_\theta s_i=0\) gives \[ 0=\partial_j\mathbb E_\theta s_i=\mathbb E_\theta[\partial_j s_i]+\mathbb E_\theta[s_is_j]. \] Since \(\partial_j s_i=\partial_i\partial_j\ell_\theta\), rearranging gives the result.

Positive definiteness

Remark. The metric can be degenerate if the parametrization has redundant directions. Positive definiteness corresponds to local identifiability in the score representation.
