3. Fisher information metric

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/tangent-spaces-score-representation/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/fisher-metric-local-distinguishability/" %}

The Fisher information metric is the intrinsic Riemannian metric of a regular statistical model. It measures the squared length of infinitesimal distributional change through score covariance.

Metric tensor

The Fisher metric is the \(L^2(P_\theta)\) inner product of tangent scores.

Definition. The Fisher information metric is \[ g_{ij}(\theta)=\mathbb E_\theta[s_i s_j] =\int \partial_i\ell_\theta\,\partial_j\ell_\theta\,p_\theta\,\mathrm{d}\mu. \] For tangent vectors \(u,v\), \(g_\theta(u,v)=\mathbb E_\theta[(u^is_i)(v^js_j)]\).

Proposition. The metric is symmetric and positive semidefinite. It is positive definite iff no nonzero tangent direction has zero score in \(L^2(P_\theta)\).

Proof. Symmetry follows from multiplication. For any \(u\), \[ g_\theta(u,u)=\mathbb E_\theta(u^is_i)^2\ge0. \] It is zero exactly when \(u^is_i=0\) almost surely.

Expected Hessian identity

Under regularity, information is both score variance and expected log-likelihood curvature.

Proposition. If differentiation under the integral is valid, then \[ g_{ij}(\theta)=-\mathbb E_\theta[\partial_i\partial_j\ell_\theta]. \]

Proof. The score has mean zero. Differentiate \(\mathbb E_\theta s_i=0\) with respect to \(\theta^j\). The expectation derivative identity gives \[ 0=\mathbb E_\theta(\partial_j s_i)+\mathbb E_\theta(s_is_j). \] Since \(\partial_js_i=\partial_j\partial_i\ell_\theta\), rearranging gives the formula.

Tensor transformation law

The Fisher matrix changes by the usual pullback rule, so the metric is coordinate-free.

Theorem. Under a reparametrization \(\theta=\theta(\phi)\), \[ g^{(\phi)}_{ab} =\frac{\partial\theta^i}{\partial\phi^a} \frac{\partial\theta^j}{\partial\phi^b}g^{(\theta)}_{ij}. \]

Proof. The score transformation is \(s_a=(\partial\theta^i/\partial\phi^a)s_i\). Taking the \(L^2(P)\) inner product of \(s_a\) and \(s_b\) gives the displayed transformation.

Information and local identifiability

Degenerate Fisher information signals directions invisible to first order.

Remark. A singular Fisher metric may arise from overparametrization, mixture singularities, boundary points, or nonregular models. Classical information geometry usually starts away from these singularities.
