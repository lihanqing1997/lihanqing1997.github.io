25. Dual geodesics and Pythagorean theorem

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/canonical-divergence/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/information-projections/" %}

Dually flat geometry has a Pythagorean theorem for canonical divergence. Orthogonality mixes the two affine structures.

Primal and dual geodesics

Definition. A \(\nabla\)-geodesic is straight in \(\theta\)-coordinates, while a \(\nabla^\ast\)-geodesic is straight in \(\eta\)-coordinates.

Dual orthogonality

Definition. A primal geodesic and a dual geodesic meeting at \(Q\) are orthogonal if their tangent vectors at \(Q\) have zero Fisher inner product.

Pythagorean identity

Theorem. If the \(\nabla\)-geodesic from \(Q\) to \(R\) is orthogonal at \(Q\) to the \(\nabla^\ast\)-geodesic from \(Q\) to \(P\), then \[ D(P:R)=D(P:Q)+D(Q:R). \]

Proof by Bregman algebra

Proof. Expanding the three canonical divergences leaves a cross term \((\theta(R)-\theta(Q))\cdot(\eta(P)-\eta(Q))\). Dual orthogonality makes this term vanish.
