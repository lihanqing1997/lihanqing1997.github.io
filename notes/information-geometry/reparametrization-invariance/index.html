6. Reparametrization invariance

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/fisher-rao-length-distance/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/markov-morphisms-monotonicity/" %}

Information geometry distinguishes intrinsic statistical statements from artifacts of the chosen coordinates. Reparametrization invariance is the first test.

Tensor transformation law

Proposition. If \(\phi=\phi(\theta)\) is a smooth change of coordinates, then the Fisher metric transforms as \[ g^{(\phi)}_{ab}=\frac{\partial\theta^i}{\partial\phi^a}\frac{\partial\theta^j}{\partial\phi^b}g^{(\theta)}_{ij}. \] Thus Fisher information is a covariant two-tensor.

Proof by score chain rule

Proof. The score in \(\phi\)-coordinates is \(\partial_a\ell=(\partial\theta^i/\partial\phi^a)\partial_i\ell\). Taking the covariance of two such score components gives the displayed transformation law.

Invariant scalar quantities

Corollary. Quantities such as \(g_\theta(u,v)\), curve length, and angles between tangent vectors are independent of the coordinate chart.

Warning about matrices

Remark. The Fisher information matrix changes under reparametrization. The metric tensor does not; matrices are only its coordinate representations.

说明
