7. Markov morphisms and monotonicity

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/reparametrization-invariance/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/cencov-theorem/" %}

Markov morphisms formalize stochastic transformations of observations. Monotonicity says that processing data cannot increase distinguishability between distributions.

Markov kernels as maps

A Markov kernel maps probability laws on one space to probability laws on another.

Definition. Given a Markov kernel \(K(\mathrm{d}y\mid x)\), the pushforward of \(P\) is \[ (KP)(B)=\int K(B\mid x)P(\mathrm{d}x). \] This includes deterministic statistics as the special case \(K(B\mid x)=\mathbf 1\{T(x)\in B\}\).

Remark. Markov morphisms are the categorical maps of statistical experiments: they represent randomization, coarse-graining, and observation channels.

Data processing for KL

KL divergence decreases under Markov kernels.

Theorem. For probability laws \(P,Q\) and a Markov kernel \(K\), \[ D_{\mathrm{KL}}(KP\|KQ)\le D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P\|Q). \]

Proof. In the dominated finite case, write the likelihood ratio after applying \(K\) as a conditional expectation of the original likelihood ratio under the joint law. Jensen's inequality for \(u\log u\) gives the inequality. The general case follows by standard approximation and disintegration.

Monotone metrics

A metric is information monotone if stochastic maps cannot increase squared lengths of tangent vectors.

Definition. A Riemannian metric \(g\) on probability models is monotone if for every Markov kernel \(K\), \[ g_{KP}(Ku,Ku)\le g_P(u,u) \] for tangent vectors \(u\) that can be pushed forward by \(K\).

Proposition. The Fisher metric is monotone under Markov kernels.

Proof. Represent a tangent vector by a score \(s\). After applying the channel, the pushed-forward score is the conditional expectation \(\mathbb E(s\mid Y)\). Therefore \[ \mathbb E\{\mathbb E(s\mid Y)^2\}\le \mathbb E(s^2) \] by Jensen or conditional-variance decomposition.

Sufficiency and equality

Equality in monotonicity is tied to preservation of the relevant likelihood ratios.

Remark. For sufficient statistics, no information is lost and Fisher lengths along the model are preserved. This is the geometric form of the factorization principle.
