7. Markov morphisms and monotonicity

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/reparametrization-invariance/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/cencov-theorem/" %}

A stochastic map sends distributions to distributions. Information-geometric structures are especially meaningful when they contract under such coarse-graining maps.

Markov morphism

Definition. For finite alphabets, a Markov morphism is a stochastic matrix \(K(y\mid x)\) acting on probability laws by \[ (Kp)(y)=\sum_xK(y\mid x)p(x). \] It represents randomized observation, coarse-graining, or postprocessing.

Monotone metric

Definition. A Riemannian metric on probability models is monotone if every Markov morphism is length-contracting on tangent vectors: \[ g_{Kp}(Ku,Ku)\le g_p(u,u). \]

Fisher monotonicity

Theorem. The Fisher metric is monotone under Markov morphisms. In statistical language, observing a randomized function of the data cannot increase infinitesimal distinguishability.

Proof idea by conditional expectation

Proof. The pushed-forward score is the conditional expectation of the original score given the coarser observation. The \(L^2\) norm of a conditional expectation is bounded by the original \(L^2\) norm, giving contraction of Fisher length.
