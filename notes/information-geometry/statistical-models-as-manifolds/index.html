1. Statistical models as manifolds

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/tangent-spaces-score-representation/" %}

Information geometry begins by treating a regular parametric family of probability laws as a smooth manifold whose points are distributions rather than only parameter values.

Parametric statistical model

Definition. A regular parametric statistical model is a family \[ \mathcal P=\{p_\theta:\theta\in\Theta\subset\mathbb R^d\} \] of positive densities with respect to a common measure, such that \(\Theta\) is open and the map \(\theta\mapsto p_\theta\) is smooth enough to differentiate under the integral sign.

Identifiability and charts

Proposition. If the parametrization is identifiable, then \(\theta\mapsto p_\theta\) is a coordinate chart on the statistical model. A reparametrization \(\phi=\phi(\theta)\) changes coordinates but not the underlying point \(p\in\mathcal P\).

Tangent vectors as model perturbations

Definition. At \(p_\theta\), the coordinate tangent vector \(\partial_i\) is represented by the infinitesimal perturbation \(\partial_i p_\theta\), or equivalently by the score component \(\partial_i\ell_\theta\), where \(\ell_\theta(x)=\log p_\theta(x)\).

Geometric viewpoint

Remark. The parameter space is only a chart. The geometry belongs to the family of distributions, and good constructions should transform tensorially under changes of coordinates.
