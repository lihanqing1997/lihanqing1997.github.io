Information geometry begins by treating a statistical model as a smooth space of probability laws. Parameters are coordinates; the intrinsic object is the family of distributions and the tensors that survive reparametrization.

Regular statistical models

A statistical manifold is a model with enough smoothness to differentiate probability laws and compare nearby distributions.

Definition. Let \((\mathcal X,\mathcal A,\mu)\) be a measured space. A \(d\)-dimensional regular statistical model is a family \[ \mathcal P=\{p_\theta:\theta\in\Theta\subset\mathbb R^d\} \] of positive densities such that \(\Theta\) is open, \(\theta\mapsto p_\theta(x)\) is smooth, and differentiation under \(\int p_\theta\,\mathrm{d}\mu=1\) is justified.

Remark. The positivity assumption avoids boundary singularities and lets every model point have the same support. Many important models with boundaries need separate treatment.

Charts and identifiability

The parameter is a chart only when different parameter values give different distributions.

Proposition. If \(\theta\mapsto p_\theta\) is injective and has full-rank differential, then \(\Theta\) is a coordinate chart for the image \(\mathcal P\). If \(\phi=\phi(\theta)\) is a smooth reparametrization, the same distribution is described by \(p_{\theta(\phi)}\).

Proof. Injectivity identifies each point of \(\mathcal P\) with one parameter value. Full rank gives a smooth immersed image, and a smooth inverse on the image gives a chart. A reparametrization composes the chart with a diffeomorphism, so it changes coordinates without changing the point \(p\in\mathcal P\).

Curves and tangent vectors

Tangent vectors are infinitesimal changes of distributions, not merely arrows in parameter space.

Definition. A smooth curve \(t\mapsto p_{\theta(t)}\) through \(p_\theta\) has tangent represented by \[ \dot p_0=\dot\theta^i\partial_i p_\theta, \qquad \dot\ell_0=\dot\theta^i\partial_i\log p_\theta. \] The second representation is the score representation of the tangent vector.

Proposition. Every tangent score \(u=\dot\theta^i\partial_i\ell_\theta\) satisfies \(\mathbb E_\theta u=0\).

Proof. Differentiate the normalization identity along the curve: \[ 0=\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}t}\int p_{\theta(t)}\,\mathrm{d}\mu\bigg|_{t=0} =\int \dot p_0\,\mathrm{d}\mu =\int \dot\ell_0\,p_\theta\,\mathrm{d}\mu. \]

Intrinsic viewpoint

A construction is geometric only if it transforms tensorially under changes of chart.

Remark. This principle is why information geometry emphasizes the Fisher metric, affine connections, duality, and divergences rather than formulas tied to one convenient parametrization.
