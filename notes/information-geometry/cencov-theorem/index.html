8. Cencov theorem

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/markov-morphisms-monotonicity/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/probability-simplex/" %}

Cencov’s theorem explains why Fisher geometry is canonical on finite statistical models: it is essentially the only Riemannian geometry compatible with stochastic maps.

Finite simplex setting

Definition. Let \(\Delta_n^\circ\) be the open probability simplex. A family of metrics \(g^{(n)}\) on these simplices is statistically invariant if it is preserved by congruent embeddings and monotone under Markov morphisms.

Cencov uniqueness theorem

Theorem. Up to a positive scalar multiple, the Fisher information metric is the unique statistically invariant Riemannian metric on finite probability simplices.

Meaning of uniqueness

Remark. The theorem is not merely a coordinate-invariance statement. It says that stochastic coarse-graining selects Fisher geometry among all possible Riemannian geometries on probability distributions.

Scope

Remark. Quantum information geometry has a richer family of monotone metrics. The classical finite simplex is unusually rigid.
