28. Mixture families and mixture geometry

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/exponential-families-dually-flat-manifolds/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/natural-gradient/" %}

Mixture geometry uses probability distributions themselves as affine objects. A mixture family is flat for the mixture connection because convex combinations are straight.

Mixture families

A mixture family is linear in densities or probability masses.

Definition. A finite-dimensional mixture family has densities \[ p_\eta(x)=p_0(x)+\eta^aF_a(x) \] on the domain where this expression is positive and normalized. Equivalently, it may be the convex hull of fixed component distributions.

Remark. Mixture coordinates are expectation-like coordinates because linear constraints on probabilities are affine in them.

Mixture geodesics

Straightness in mixture geometry means ordinary convex interpolation of distributions.

Proposition. The curve \(p_t=(1-t)p_0+tp_1\) is a mixture geodesic wherever it remains in the positive model.

Proof. In mixture-affine coordinates, \(p_t\) is a straight line: its second derivative in those coordinates is zero. Therefore its acceleration under the mixture connection vanishes.

Duality with exponential families

Mixture-flat and exponential-flat submanifolds meet through KL Pythagorean identities.

Theorem. In a dually flat model, if a mixture-flat submanifold and an exponential-flat submanifold meet orthogonally, then KL divergence decomposes by the generalized Pythagorean theorem.

Proof. Mixture-flat and exponential-flat mean that the relevant connecting curves are geodesics for dual connections. Orthogonality under the Fisher metric cancels the three-point term in the canonical divergence identity, giving the KL decomposition.

Statistics of mixtures

Mixture models in the ordinary statistical sense can be singular even though mixture geometry is affine.

Remark. Finite mixtures with unknown components often have label switching and nonidentifiability. The mixture connection is simple for convex combinations of fixed distributions, but mixture-model parameter spaces can be singular.
