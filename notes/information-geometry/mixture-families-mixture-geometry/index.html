28. Mixture families and mixture geometry

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/exponential-families-dually-flat-manifolds/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/natural-gradient/" %}

Mixture families are affine under convex combination of densities. They are dual to exponential families in the standard information-geometric picture.

Mixture family

Definition. A finite mixture-affine family can be written as \[ p_\eta(x)=p_0(x)+\sum_{i=1}^d\eta_i v_i(x), \] with constraints ensuring positivity and normalization. Straight lines in \(\eta\) are mixture geodesics.

Mixture connection

Proposition. In mixture-affine coordinates, the mixture connection has vanishing Christoffel symbols. Hence mixture geodesics are ordinary affine segments of densities.

Duality with exponential structure

Theorem. In exponential-family manifolds, expectation coordinates are mixture-affine and natural coordinates are exponential-affine. The corresponding connections are dual with respect to the Fisher metric.

Convexity caution

Remark. Mixture-affine sets are convex in density space, while exponential-affine sets are convex in log-density coordinates. The two notions of flatness are different and complementary.
