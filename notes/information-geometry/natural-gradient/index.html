28. Natural gradient

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/mixture-families-mixture-geometry/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/chernoff-information-geodesic-testing/" %}

The natural gradient is the gradient defined by the Fisher metric. It corrects ordinary coordinate gradients by the local statistical geometry of the model.

Riemannian gradient

Definition. For an objective \(L(\theta)\), the natural gradient \(\widetilde\nabla L\) is the vector field satisfying \[ g_\theta(\widetilde\nabla L,v)=\mathrm{d}L_\theta(v) \] for all tangent vectors \(v\). In coordinates, \[ (\widetilde\nabla L)^i=g^{ij}\partial_jL. \]

Steepest descent interpretation

Proposition. The natural gradient is the steepest ascent direction when step size is measured by infinitesimal Fisher distance rather than Euclidean parameter distance.

Proof by constrained linearization

Proof. Maximize the first-order gain \(\partial_iL\,v^i\) subject to \(g_{ij}v^iv^j\le\varepsilon^2\). The Lagrange multiplier equation gives \(v\propto g^{-1}\nabla L\).

Relation to mirror descent

Remark. On dually flat manifolds, natural-gradient steps and mirror-descent steps are closely related through the potential function and its Legendre dual.
