17. Connections from third derivatives of divergence

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/divergences-local-geometry/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/kl-divergence-canonical-example/" %}

The fundamental divergence construction differentiates once more after the metric. Third derivatives of a divergence define a pair of conjugate connections.

Divergence-induced connections

Theorem. For a smooth divergence \(D(\theta:\theta')\), define \[ \Gamma_{ij,k}=-\partial_i\partial_j\partial'_kD(\theta:\theta')|_{\theta'=\theta}, \qquad \Gamma^\ast_{ij,k}=-\partial'_i\partial'_j\partial_kD(\theta:\theta')|_{\theta'=\theta}. \] Together with the induced metric, these form dual connections.

Cubic tensor from asymmetry

Corollary. The difference \[ C_{ijk}=\Gamma^\ast_{ij,k}-\Gamma_{ij,k} \] is a symmetric cubic tensor in the statistical cases of interest.

Proof idea by diagonal identities

Proof. Because \(D(\theta:\theta)=0\) along the diagonal, first derivatives vanish there. Differentiating the diagonal identities repeatedly relates pure and mixed derivatives and gives the duality equation with respect to the induced metric.

Coordinate-free content

Remark. Although the formulas use coordinates, the resulting metric and connections transform as geometric objects when the divergence is intrinsically defined.
