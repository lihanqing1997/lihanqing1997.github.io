Divergence third derivatives explain why information geometry naturally has two affine connections. The first and second arguments of a divergence are geometrically asymmetric.

Diagonal derivative calculus

The diagonal \(p=q\) is where a contrast function becomes local geometry.

Definition. For coordinates \(\theta\) on the first argument and \(\theta'\) on the second, diagonal evaluation means setting \(\theta'=\theta\) after differentiating. Unprimed indices differentiate the first argument; primed indices differentiate the second.

Remark. Differentiating before restricting to the diagonal is essential. Restricting first would make \(D(p,p)=0\) identically and lose all geometry.

Connection coefficients

The third derivatives of a divergence define two sets of Christoffel symbols.

Proposition. Let \(D\) generate metric \(g_{ij}=-\partial_i\partial_{j'}D|_{\mathrm{diag}}\). Define \[ \Gamma_{ijk}=-\partial_i\partial_j\partial_{k'}D|_{\mathrm{diag}}, \qquad \Gamma^*_{ijk}=-\partial_{i'}\partial_{j'}\partial_kD|_{\mathrm{diag}}. \] Then \(\Gamma\) and \(\Gamma^*\) are coefficients of torsion-free dual connections.

Proof. Symmetry of second derivatives in the unprimed pair gives \(\Gamma_{ijk}=\Gamma_{jik}\), and similarly for the primed pair, so both connections are torsion-free. Differentiating the metric expression along the diagonal gives \(\partial_kg_{ij}=\Gamma_{kij}+\Gamma^*_{kji}\), which is the coordinate duality condition.

KL divergence case

For KL divergence, the induced pair is the exponential and mixture connections.

Theorem. On a regular positive model, \(D_{\mathrm{KL}}(p_\theta\|p_{\theta'})\) induces the Fisher metric and the standard dual pair of exponential and mixture connections.

Proof. The second mixed derivative of KL gives the Fisher metric by the usual score-Hessian identity. The two possible third derivatives keep track of whether differentiation acts on the source or target argument; these yield the two Amari connections \(\alpha=\pm1\) under standard sign conventions.

Choice of divergence

The divergence fixes both local metric and preferred affine structure.

Remark. A Riemannian metric alone does not determine the exponential and mixture connections. A divergence contains third-order information, and this third-order information is exactly what specifies the dual affine structure.
