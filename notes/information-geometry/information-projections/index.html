26. Information projections

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/dual-geodesics-pythagorean-theorem/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/exponential-families-dually-flat-manifolds/" %}

Information projections minimize a divergence over a model or constraint set. Their first-order conditions are geometric orthogonality conditions.

I- and M-projections

The names refer to the two KL directions and their relation with exponential and mixture geometry.

Definition. For a set \(\mathcal C\), an I-projection often means a minimizer of \(D(P\|Q)\) or \(D(Q\|P)\) depending on convention. To avoid ambiguity, this note always states the objective explicitly: \[ \min_{Q\in\mathcal C}D(P\|Q),\qquad \min_{Q\in\mathcal C}D(Q\|P). \]

Remark. The ambiguity of terminology is common. The geometry is unambiguous once the KL order and the affine structure of \(\mathcal C\) are specified.

Moment constraints and exponential form

Minimizing KL subject to moment constraints gives exponential tilting.

Theorem. Minimizing \(D(Q\|P)\) subject to \(\mathbb E_Q T_j=c_j\) gives, when a solution exists, \[ \frac{\mathrm{d}Q_\lambda}{\mathrm{d}P} =\exp\{\lambda^jT_j-\psi(\lambda)\}. \]

Proof. Use Lagrange multipliers for normalization and moment constraints. The first variation of \(\int q\log(q/p)\,\mathrm{d}\mu\) plus linear constraint terms gives \(\log(q/p)+1-\alpha-\lambda^jT_j=0\). Solving yields the exponential tilt, with \(\psi\) normalizing the density.

Projection onto exponential families

Maximum likelihood can be read as a KL projection in expectation coordinates.

Proposition. For an exponential family, the likelihood equations set model expectation parameters equal to empirical sufficient-statistic averages.

Proof. The log likelihood is \(\theta^i\bar T_i-\psi(\theta)\) up to constants. Differentiating gives \(\bar T_i-\partial_i\psi(\theta)\). Setting this to zero yields \(\eta_i(\theta)=\bar T_i\).

Alternating projections

Iterative proportional fitting alternates between dual affine constraint sets.

Remark. When constraint sets are mixture-flat or exponential-flat, alternating KL projections can be analyzed through repeated Pythagorean identities.
