26. Information projections

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/dual-geodesics-pythagorean-theorem/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/exponential-families-dually-flat-manifolds/" %}

Information projections are divergence minimizers. In dually flat manifolds they behave like orthogonal projections, with the appropriate primal or dual geometry.

Projection by divergence minimization

Definition. For a subset \(S\subset M\), an information projection of \(P\) onto \(S\) is a minimizer of either \(D(P:Q)\) or \(D(Q:P)\), depending on the chosen orientation.

Projection theorem

Theorem. If \(S\) is affine for one connection and suitable convexity conditions hold, then the corresponding projection is unique and satisfies an orthogonality condition with the dual geodesic from the projection point to \(P\).

Maximum likelihood and maximum entropy

Corollary. Maximum likelihood and maximum entropy can be interpreted as dual information projections in exponential-family geometry. One fixes empirical constraints; the other minimizes divergence within a model family.

Iterative projections

Remark. Alternating projection algorithms are information-geometric analogues of Euclidean alternating projections, with Bregman or KL divergence replacing squared distance.
