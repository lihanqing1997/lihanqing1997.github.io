Legendre duality turns one affine coordinate system into its dual. In information geometry it relates natural parameters, expectation parameters, and entropy-like potentials.

Legendre transform

A strictly convex potential defines a dual potential by optimizing a linear perturbation.

Definition. For a strictly convex differentiable \(\psi(\theta)\), \[ \psi^*(\eta)=\sup_\theta\{\theta^i\eta_i-\psi(\theta)\}. \] When \(\eta_i=\partial_i\psi(\theta)\), the supremum is attained at \(\theta\).

Proposition. The gradients are inverse maps: \[ \eta=\nabla\psi(\theta),\qquad \theta=\nabla\psi^*(\eta). \]

Proof. The first-order condition for the supremum is \(\eta=\nabla\psi(\theta)\). Differentiating the identity \(\psi^*(\eta)=\theta(\eta)\cdot\eta-\psi(\theta(\eta))\) gives \(\nabla\psi^*(\eta)=\theta(\eta)\), because the terms involving \(\mathrm{d}\theta/\mathrm{d}\eta\) cancel by the first-order condition.

Dual Hessians

The two Hessian metrics are matrix inverses.

Theorem. If \(g_{ij}=\partial_i\partial_j\psi\) and \(g^{ij}_*=\partial^i\partial^j\psi^*\), then the Hessian matrices are inverse under the coordinate transformation \(\eta=\nabla\psi(\theta)\).

Proof. Differentiate \(\eta_i=\partial_i\psi(\theta)\) to get \(\mathrm{d}\eta=g\,\mathrm{d}\theta\). Differentiate \(\theta=\nabla\psi^*(\eta)\) to get \(\mathrm{d}\theta=g_*\,\mathrm{d}\eta\). Composing the two differentials yields \(g_*g=I\).

Fenchel equality

Dual potentials satisfy an exact equality at matching coordinates.

Proposition. If \(\eta=\nabla\psi(\theta)\), then \[ \psi(\theta)+\psi^*(\eta)=\theta^i\eta_i. \]

Proof. At the maximizing \(\theta\), the definition of \(\psi^*\) gives \(\psi^*(\eta)=\theta^i\eta_i-\psi(\theta)\).

Statistical meaning

In exponential families, the log-partition function and negative entropy are Legendre dual.

Remark. Natural parameters are \(\theta\), expectation parameters are \(\eta=\mathbb E_\theta T(X)\), the cumulant function is \(\psi\), and the dual potential is a negative entropy function on the mean-parameter space.
