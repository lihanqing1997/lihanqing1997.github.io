22. Legendre duality and potential functions

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/dually-flat-manifolds/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/canonical-divergence/" %}

Dually flat coordinates are linked by Legendre transform. Potential functions encode both the metric and the coordinate duality.

Legendre transform

Definition. For a strictly convex potential \(F\), define the dual potential \[ F^\ast(\eta)=\sup_\theta\{\theta\cdot\eta-F(\theta)\}. \] The dual coordinate is \(\eta=\nabla F(\theta)\).

Inverse coordinate maps

Theorem. If \(F\) is Legendre type, then \[ \eta_i=\partial_iF(\theta),\qquad \theta^i=\partial^iF^\ast(\eta), \] and \(F^{\ast\ast}=F\).

Dual Hessians

Proposition. The Hessian matrices are inverse: \[ \nabla^2F(\theta)\,\nabla^2F^\ast(\eta)=I \] when \(\eta=\nabla F(\theta)\).

Metric interpretation

Remark. Contravariant coordinates in one system become covariant coordinates in the dual system. This is the coordinate algebra behind dual orthogonality.
