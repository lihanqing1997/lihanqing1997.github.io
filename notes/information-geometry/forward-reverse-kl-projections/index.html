Forward and reverse KL projections solve different variational problems. Their geometric behavior is best understood through dual affine structures.

Two projection problems

The order of KL arguments changes the projection.

Definition. For a set \(\mathcal M\) of distributions and a distribution \(P\), the forward KL projection is \[ \operatorname*{argmin}_{Q\in\mathcal M}D(P\|Q), \] while the reverse KL projection is \[ \operatorname*{argmin}_{Q\in\mathcal M}D(Q\|P). \]

Remark. The terminology is not universal. What matters is always the order of the two KL arguments.

First-order condition

Projection is characterized by orthogonality of the divergence gradient to feasible directions.

Proposition. If \(Q_{\hat\theta}\) minimizes \(D(P\|Q_\theta)\) over a smooth model, then \[ \partial_i D(P\|Q_\theta)|_{\hat\theta}=0. \] Equivalently, \(\int \partial_i\log q_{\hat\theta}\,\mathrm{d}P=\int \partial_i\log q_{\hat\theta}\,\mathrm{d}Q_{\hat\theta}=0\) when the model is normalized.

Proof. Differentiate \(D(P\|Q_\theta)=\int\log p\,\mathrm{d}P-\int\log q_\theta\,\mathrm{d}P\). At an interior minimum the derivative vanishes. The second equality uses the score mean-zero identity under \(Q_{\hat\theta}\).

Mode seeking and mass covering

The asymmetry has visible consequences in approximation.

Proposition. If \(\mathcal M\) is a restricted unimodal family approximating a multimodal \(P\), minimizing \(D(Q\|P)\) penalizes placing mass where \(P\) is small, while minimizing \(D(P\|Q)\) penalizes failing to cover mass of \(P\).

Proof. Reverse KL integrates \(\log(Q/P)\) under \(Q\), so regions where \(Q>0\) and \(P\) is tiny are expensive; it can avoid a mode by putting little \(Q\)-mass there. Forward KL integrates \(\log(P/Q)\) under \(P\), so regions with \(P>0\) and tiny \(Q\) are expensive; it must cover the support carrying \(P\)-mass.

Pythagorean identities

In flat geometries, KL projections satisfy exact additive identities.

Remark. Projection onto an exponential family and projection onto a mixture family use dual geodesics. Orthogonality under the Fisher metric gives generalized Pythagorean theorems for KL divergence.
