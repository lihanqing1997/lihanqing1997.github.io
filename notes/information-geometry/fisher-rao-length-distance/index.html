5. Fisher-Rao length and distance

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/fisher-metric-local-distinguishability/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/reparametrization-invariance/" %}

Once Fisher information is viewed as a metric tensor, it defines lengths of curves and a Riemannian distance on connected components of the statistical manifold.

Length of a statistical path

Definition. For a smooth curve \(\theta(t)\), \(0\le t\le1\), its Fisher-Rao length is \[ L(\theta)=\int_0^1\sqrt{g_{ij}(\theta(t))\dot\theta^i(t)\dot\theta^j(t)}\,\mathrm{d}t. \] The Fisher-Rao distance is the infimum of such lengths over curves joining two model points.

Coordinate invariance

Proposition. The length functional is invariant under smooth reparametrization. The coordinate change in \(g_{ij}\) is exactly canceled by the coordinate change in velocity components \(\dot\theta^i\).

Geodesic equation

Remark. Distance-minimizing curves satisfy the Levi-Civita geodesic equation of the Fisher metric when minimizers exist and remain in the regular part of the model.

Square-root simplification

Corollary. On the finite probability simplex, the map \(p\mapsto2\sqrt p\) embeds the Fisher metric into a sphere. This gives an explicit Fisher-Rao distance for categorical distributions.
