11. Affine connections and covariant derivatives

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/examples-fisher-geometry/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/geodesics-parallel-transport/" %}

A Riemannian metric measures lengths, but a connection tells us how to differentiate vector fields and compare tangent vectors at nearby points. Information geometry studies families of affine connections adapted to statistical models.

Affine connection

A connection is a rule for differentiating vector fields along vector fields.

Definition. An affine connection \(\nabla\) on a manifold assigns to vector fields \(X,Y\) another vector field \(\nabla_XY\), linear in \(X\), linear over constants in \(Y\), and satisfying the Leibniz rule \[ \nabla_X(fY)=X(f)Y+f\nabla_XY. \]

Remark. In coordinates, \(\nabla_{\partial_i}\partial_j=\Gamma^k_{ij}\partial_k\). The functions \(\Gamma^k_{ij}\) are Christoffel symbols, but they are not tensor components.

Torsion

Most statistical connections used here are torsion-free.

Definition. The torsion tensor is \[ T^\nabla(X,Y)=\nabla_XY-\nabla_YX-[X,Y]. \] A connection is torsion-free if \(T^\nabla=0\).

Proposition. In a coordinate frame, torsion-free means \(\Gamma^k_{ij}=\Gamma^k_{ji}\).

Proof. Coordinate vector fields commute: \([\partial_i,\partial_j]=0\). Thus \(T(\partial_i,\partial_j)=(\Gamma^k_{ij}-\Gamma^k_{ji})\partial_k\). Vanishing torsion is equivalent to equality of the lower indices.

Covariant derivative of tensors

Connections extend from vector fields to tensors by compatibility with contraction and product rules.

Definition. For a covector field \(\omega=\omega_j\mathrm{d}\theta^j\), \[ (\nabla_i\omega)_j=\partial_i\omega_j-\Gamma^k_{ij}\omega_k. \] For a vector field \(Y=Y^k\partial_k\), \[ (\nabla_iY)^k=\partial_iY^k+\Gamma^k_{ij}Y^j. \]

Remark. These formulas ensure that \(\nabla_i(\omega_jY^j)=\partial_i(\omega_jY^j)\), so scalar contractions differentiate ordinarily.

Why connections enter statistics

Different affine structures correspond to different ways of declaring curves of distributions straight.

Remark. Mixture families are flat for the mixture connection; exponential families are flat for the exponential connection. Their duality is one of the central ideas of information geometry.
