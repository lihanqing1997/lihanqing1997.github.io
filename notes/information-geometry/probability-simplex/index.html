9. The probability simplex

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/cencov-theorem/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/examples-fisher-geometry/" %}

The probability simplex is the simplest full statistical manifold. It reveals the Fisher metric, square-root embedding, and spherical geometry in explicit form.

Open simplex

Definition. The open simplex is \[ \Delta_n^\circ=\{p\in\mathbb R^{n+1}:p_i>0,\ \sum_{i=0}^n p_i=1\}. \] A tangent vector \(u\) at \(p\) satisfies \(\sum_i u_i=0\).

Fisher metric on the simplex

Proposition. For tangent vectors \(u,v\), the Fisher metric is \[ g_p(u,v)=\sum_i\frac{u_iv_i}{p_i}. \]

Square-root embedding

Theorem. The map \(\Psi(p)=2(\sqrt{p_0},\ldots,\sqrt{p_n})\) sends \(\Delta_n^\circ\) isometrically into the sphere of radius \(2\) in Euclidean space.

Proof by differentiating square roots

Proof. The differential is \(\mathrm{d}\Psi_p(u)=(u_i/\sqrt{p_i})_i\). Its Euclidean inner product with \(\mathrm{d}\Psi_p(v)\) is \(\sum_i u_iv_i/p_i\), exactly the Fisher metric.
