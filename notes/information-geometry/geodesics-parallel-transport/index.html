12. Geodesics and parallel transport

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/affine-connections-covariant-derivatives/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/dual-connections/" %}

A connection determines straight curves and a rule for transporting tangent vectors. In information geometry, different connections produce different statistically meaningful straight lines.

Autoparallel geodesic

Definition. A curve \(\gamma(t)\) is a \(\nabla\)-geodesic, or autoparallel curve, if \[ \nabla_{\dot\gamma}\dot\gamma=0. \] In coordinates this becomes \(\ddot\theta^k+\Gamma^k_{ij}\dot\theta^i\dot\theta^j=0\).

Parallel vector field

Definition. A vector field \(V(t)\) along \(\gamma\) is parallel if \[ \nabla_{\dot\gamma}V=0. \] Parallel transport maps tangent vectors from one point of the curve to another.

Flat connections

Proposition. If a torsion-free connection has coordinates in which all Christoffel symbols vanish, then its geodesics are affine straight lines in those coordinates and its parallel transport is path-independent on simply connected domains.

Riemannian versus affine geodesics

Remark. The Levi-Civita geodesics minimize Fisher-Rao length locally. Exponential and mixture geodesics need not minimize length; they encode affine statistical structure.
