12. Geodesics and parallel transport

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/affine-connections-covariant-derivatives/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/dual-connections/" %}

Geodesics and parallel transport are the dynamical objects determined by a connection. They describe straightness, not necessarily shortest distance unless the connection is Levi-Civita.

Geodesic equation

A geodesic has zero covariant acceleration.

Definition. A curve \(\theta(t)\) is a \(\nabla\)-geodesic if \[ \nabla_{\dot\theta}\dot\theta=0. \] In coordinates, \[ \ddot\theta^k+\Gamma^k_{ij}(\theta)\dot\theta^i\dot\theta^j=0. \]

Proposition. For a flat connection with affine coordinates, geodesics are straight lines in those coordinates.

Proof. In affine coordinates for a flat connection, \(\Gamma^k_{ij}=0\). The geodesic equation becomes \(\ddot\theta^k=0\), whose solutions are \(\theta^k(t)=a^k+tb^k\).

Parallel transport

Parallel transport carries tangent vectors along a curve while keeping covariant derivative zero.

Definition. A vector field \(V(t)\) along \(\theta(t)\) is parallel if \[ \nabla_{\dot\theta}V=0. \] In coordinates, \(\dot V^k+\Gamma^k_{ij}\dot\theta^iV^j=0\).

Remark. For the Levi-Civita connection, parallel transport preserves inner products. For general statistical connections, this preservation may hold only in dual pairing with the conjugate connection.

Levi-Civita geodesics

For the metric connection, geodesics are locally length minimizing.

Theorem. The Levi-Civita connection is the unique torsion-free connection satisfying \(\nabla g=0\). Its geodesics solve the Euler-Lagrange equations of the energy functional \[ E(\theta)=\frac12\int g_{ij}\dot\theta^i\dot\theta^j\,\mathrm{d}t. \]

Proof. Metric compatibility and torsion-freeness determine the Christoffel symbols by the Koszul formula. Varying the energy functional and integrating by parts yields the geodesic equation with these symbols.

Statistical straightness

Mixture and exponential geodesics can be straight without minimizing Fisher-Rao length.

Remark. This is why information geometry distinguishes affine geodesics from Riemannian distance geodesics. The former encode statistical linearity; the latter encode Fisher length.
