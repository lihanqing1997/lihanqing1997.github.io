30. Chernoff information and geodesic testing

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/natural-gradient/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/thermodynamic-quantum-information-geometry/" %}

Binary hypothesis testing has a clean information-geometric picture: the Chernoff point lies on an exponential geodesic between the two hypotheses.

Exponential geodesic between hypotheses

Definition. For densities \(p\) and \(q\), the exponential geodesic is the normalized path \[ r_t(x)=\frac{p(x)^{1-t}q(x)^t}{\int p^{1-t}q^t\,\mathrm{d}x},\qquad 0\le t\le1. \]

Chernoff information

Definition. The Chernoff information is \[ C(p,q)=-\inf_{0\le t\le1}\log\int p(x)^{1-t}q(x)^t\,\mathrm{d}x. \]

Chernoff point

Proposition. At an interior optimizing \(t^\star\), the point \(r_{t^\star}\) balances the KL divergences to the two endpoints in the sense that \[ D(r_{t^\star}:p)=D(r_{t^\star}:q). \]

Link to information theory

Remark. The same quantity is the optimal Bayesian error exponent in iid binary testing. Information geometry adds the picture of a balancing point on an exponential geodesic.
