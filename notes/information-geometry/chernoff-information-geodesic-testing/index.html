Chernoff information links hypothesis testing error exponents with exponential geodesics between distributions. It is one of the clearest operational appearances of information geometry.

Chernoff coefficient

The Chernoff exponent is obtained by optimizing a log-normalizer along an exponential path.

Definition. For densities \(p,q\), \[ C(P,Q)=-\min_{0\le t\le1}\log\int p^{1-t}q^t\,\mathrm{d}\mu. \] The density proportional to \(p^{1-t}q^t\) lies on the exponential geodesic joining \(p\) and \(q\).

Remark. At \(t=1/2\), the same expression gives the Bhattacharyya coefficient. Chernoff information optimizes over the point on the exponential geodesic.

Testing exponent

Chernoff information is the best symmetric exponential error rate for testing two simple hypotheses.

Theorem. For testing \(P^n\) versus \(Q^n\) with equal priors, the optimal Bayes error probability satisfies \[ -\lim_{n\to\infty}\frac1n\log P_e^{(n)}=C(P,Q). \]

Proof. The likelihood-ratio test is optimal by Neyman-Pearson. Upper bounds follow from exponential Markov inequalities applied to likelihood ratios, producing \(\int p^{1-t}q^t\). Lower bounds use a change-of-measure argument at the tilted Chernoff distribution, showing that no test can beat the optimized exponent.

Chernoff point

The optimizing point balances the two KL divergences back to the endpoints.

Proposition. If \(t^*\in(0,1)\) is the optimizer and \(r_{t^*}\propto p^{1-t^*}q^{t^*}\), then \[ D(R_{t^*}\|P)=D(R_{t^*}\|Q) \] under differentiability and interior optimality.

Proof. Let \(\psi(t)=\log\int p^{1-t}q^t\,\mathrm{d}\mu\). The derivative is \(\psi'(t)=\mathbb E_{R_t}\log(q/p)\). Interior optimality gives \(\psi'(t^*)=0\). Expanding the two KL divergences from \(R_{t^*}\) to \(P\) and \(Q\) shows their difference is exactly \(\mathbb E_{R_{t^*}}\log(q/p)\).

Geometric interpretation

The Chernoff point is where the exponential geodesic meets a decision boundary of equal divergence.

Remark. This picture is especially clean in exponential families, where the Chernoff path is a straight line in natural parameters and the optimizing point balances normal directions to likelihood-ratio level sets.
