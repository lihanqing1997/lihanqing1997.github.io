18. KL divergence as canonical example

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/connections-from-divergence/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/f-divergences-information-monotonicity/" %}

Kullback-Leibler divergence is the canonical statistical divergence. Its local geometry is exactly Fisher geometry with exponential and mixture affine structures.

KL divergence on a model

Definition. For two model points, \[ D_{\mathrm{KL}}(p_\theta:p_{\theta'})=\int p_\theta(x)\log\frac{p_\theta(x)}{p_{\theta'}(x)}\,\mathrm{d}x. \]

Metric recovery

Proposition. The second-order diagonal derivative of KL divergence is the Fisher metric: \[ -\partial_i\partial'_jD_{\mathrm{KL}}(\theta:\theta')|_{\theta'=\theta}=g_{ij}(\theta). \]

Connection recovery

Theorem. The third-order divergence construction applied to KL divergence yields the mixture and exponential dual connections, with the precise sign depending on whether one uses \(D(p:q)\) or \(D(q:p)\).

Forward and reverse roles

Remark. Forward and reverse KL have the same local metric but interchange the two affine connections. This is why KL asymmetry is geometrically meaningful.
