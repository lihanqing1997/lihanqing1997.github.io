16. Divergences and local geometry

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/alpha-connections/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/connections-from-divergence/" %}

A divergence is an asymmetric contrast function between model points. Its second and third derivatives generate the metric and dual connections.

Statistical divergence

Definition. A divergence on a manifold \(M\) is a smooth function \(D(p:q)\ge0\) with \(D(p:q)=0\) if and only if \(p=q\). It need not be symmetric and need not satisfy the triangle inequality.

Metric from second order contact

Proposition. In local coordinates, a divergence induces a metric by \[ g_{ij}(\theta)=-\partial_i\partial'_jD(\theta:\theta')\big|_{\theta'=\theta}. \] The Hessian in either argument gives the same tensor on the diagonal under standard divergence regularity.

Why asymmetry matters

Remark. The symmetric second-order part gives the metric. The asymmetric third-order part determines the dual affine connections.

KL example preview

Remark. For KL divergence, this construction recovers Fisher information and the exponential-mixture dual connection pair.
