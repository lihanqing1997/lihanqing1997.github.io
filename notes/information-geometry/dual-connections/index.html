13. Dual connections

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/geodesics-parallel-transport/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/statistical-manifolds-cubic-tensor/" %}

The central extra structure of information geometry is a pair of affine connections dual with respect to the Fisher metric.

Conjugate connections

Definition. Two connections \(\nabla\) and \(\nabla^\ast\) are dual with respect to \(g\) if \[ Xg(Y,Z)=g(\nabla_XY,Z)+g(Y,\nabla_X^\ast Z) \] for all vector fields \(X,Y,Z\).

Levi-Civita midpoint

Proposition. If \(\nabla\) and \(\nabla^\ast\) are torsion-free dual connections, then their average is the Levi-Civita connection: \[ \nabla^{(0)}=\frac12(\nabla+\nabla^\ast). \]

Proof by metric compatibility

Proof. Averaging the duality identity with its swapped version shows that \(\nabla^{(0)}\) is metric-compatible. Torsion-freeness is preserved by averaging. The fundamental theorem of Riemannian geometry identifies the result with the Levi-Civita connection.

Duality principle

Remark. Information geometry often proves statements in pairs: one statement for \(\nabla\), another dual statement for \(\nabla^\ast\), connected by the metric.
