10. Examples of Fisher geometry

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/probability-simplex/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/affine-connections-covariant-derivatives/" %}

Concrete models show how Fisher geometry encodes scale, curvature, and parameter interactions. The same formulas can look very different in different coordinates.

Bernoulli family

The one-dimensional Bernoulli model has an explicit Fisher line element.

Example. For \(P_\theta(Y=1)=\theta\), \(0<\theta<1\), \[ g(\theta)=\frac{1}{\theta(1-\theta)}. \]

Proof. The score is \(Y/\theta-(1-Y)/(1-\theta)\). Its variance under Bernoulli\((\theta)\) is \(1/\theta+1/(1-\theta)=1/\{\theta(1-\theta)\}\).

Normal location-scale family

The normal family is a basic curved surface with orthogonal location and scale directions.

Proposition. For \(N(\mu,\sigma^2)\), \[ g_{\mu\mu}=\frac{1}{\sigma^2},\qquad g_{\mu\sigma}=0,\qquad g_{\sigma\sigma}=\frac{2}{\sigma^2}. \]

Proof. The scores are \(s_\mu=(X-\mu)/\sigma^2\) and \(s_\sigma=-1/\sigma+(X-\mu)^2/\sigma^3\). Using central moments \(\mathbb E(X-\mu)^2=\sigma^2\), \(\mathbb E(X-\mu)^3=0\), and \(\mathbb E(X-\mu)^4=3\sigma^4\) gives the entries.

Categorical family

The categorical family is the probability simplex with Fisher metric.

Example. For \(p=(p_1,\ldots,p_n)\), tangent vectors satisfy \(\sum_i u_i=0\) and the Fisher metric is \(g_p(u,u)=\sum_i u_i^2/p_i\).

Remark. This example is the local form behind multinomial asymptotics and chi-square approximations.

Poisson family

The Poisson mean has information inverse to the mean.

Proposition. For \(X\sim\operatorname{Poisson}(\lambda)\), \[ g(\lambda)=\frac1\lambda. \]

Proof. The score is \(X/\lambda-1\). Its variance is \(\operatorname{Var}(X)/\lambda^2=\lambda/\lambda^2=1/\lambda\).
