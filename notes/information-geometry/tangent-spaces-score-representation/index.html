2. Tangent spaces and score representation

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/statistical-models-as-manifolds/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/fisher-information-metric/" %}

The score representation embeds tangent vectors into the Hilbert space of square-integrable mean-zero random variables. This is the analytic bridge between geometry and likelihood theory.

Score vectors

The coordinate scores form the basic tangent frame.

Definition. For a regular model, the coordinate score functions are \[ s_i(x;\theta)=\partial_i\ell_\theta(x)=\partial_i\log p_\theta(x). \] A tangent vector \(u=u^i\partial_i\) is represented by the random variable \(u^is_i\in L_0^2(P_\theta)\), where \(L_0^2(P_\theta)=\{h:\mathbb E_\theta h=0,\ \mathbb E_\theta h^2<\infty\}\).

Proposition. The map \(u^i\partial_i\mapsto u^is_i\) is linear. It is injective exactly when the Fisher information matrix is positive definite.

Proof. Linearity is immediate. The squared \(L^2(P_\theta)\)-norm of the image is \(u^ig_{ij}u^j\). This vanishes for nonzero \(u\) precisely when the Fisher matrix has a null direction.

Differentiating expectations

Scores describe derivatives of expectations by covariance identities.

Proposition. For a smooth statistic \(h_\theta(x)\), \[ \partial_i\mathbb E_\theta h_\theta =\mathbb E_\theta(\partial_i h_\theta)+\mathbb E_\theta(h_\theta s_i). \]

Proof. Write the expectation as \(\int h_\theta p_\theta\,\mathrm{d}\mu\) and differentiate under the integral: \[ \partial_i\int h_\theta p_\theta\,\mathrm{d}\mu =\int(\partial_i h_\theta)p_\theta\,\mathrm{d}\mu+\int h_\theta(\partial_i p_\theta)\,\mathrm{d}\mu. \] Since \(\partial_i p_\theta=s_ip_\theta\), the identity follows.

Centering and nuisance directions

The tangent-space formalism naturally separates identifiable directions from redundant ones.

Definition. A direction \(u\) is null at \(p_\theta\) if \(u^is_i=0\) in \(L^2(P_\theta)\). Null directions do not change the distribution to first order and should be quotiented out of the tangent space.

Remark. In semiparametric statistics, the same idea becomes projection onto tangent spaces and orthogonal complements, producing efficient scores and information bounds.

Coordinate transformations

Scores transform covariantly, so tangent coordinates and score functions remain consistent under reparametrization.

Proposition. If \(\phi=\phi(\theta)\), then \[ \partial_{\phi^a}\ell=\frac{\partial\theta^i}{\partial\phi^a}\partial_{\theta^i}\ell. \]

Proof. This is the ordinary chain rule applied to \(\ell_{\theta(\phi)}(x)\). It shows that score components transform as a covector frame, while tangent vector components transform contravariantly.
