2. Tangent spaces and score representation

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/statistical-models-as-manifolds/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/fisher-information-metric/" %}

The tangent space of a statistical model can be represented inside a Hilbert space of centered random variables. Scores are the coordinate basis of this representation.

Score functions

Definition. The score vector is \[ s_\theta(X)=\nabla_\theta\ell_\theta(X),\qquad s_i=\partial_i\ell_\theta. \] Under the usual regularity condition \(\partial_i\int p_\theta(x)\,\mathrm{d}x=0\), each score component is centered: \(\mathbb E_\theta s_i=0\).

Tangent space in (L^2_0(P_ heta))

Proposition. For a regular model, tangent vectors at \(p_\theta\) are represented by the span of score components inside \[ L^2_0(P_\theta)=\{u:\mathbb E_\theta u=0,\\ \mathbb E_\theta u^2<\infty\}. \] A vector \(v=v^i\partial_i\) corresponds to the score perturbation \(v^is_i\).

Proof by differentiating normalization

Proof. Because \(\int p_\theta\,\mathrm{d}x=1\), differentiation gives \(\int\partial_i p_\theta\,\mathrm{d}x=0\). Since \(\partial_i p_\theta=p_\theta\partial_i\ell_\theta\), this is \(\mathbb E_\theta s_i=0\). Linear combinations represent arbitrary coordinate tangent directions.

Statistical meaning

Remark. A tangent vector describes a local alternative. Its score representation is the infinitesimal log-likelihood ratio in that direction.
