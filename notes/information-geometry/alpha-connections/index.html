15. Alpha-connections

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/statistical-manifolds-cubic-tensor/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/divergences-local-geometry/" %}

The alpha-connections interpolate between exponential, Levi-Civita, and mixture geometry. They are the standard one-parameter family of information-geometric affine structures.

Alpha family

Definition. For a statistical manifold \((M,g,C)\), the alpha-connection \(\nabla^{(\alpha)}\) is determined by \[ \Gamma^{(\alpha)}_{ij,k}=\Gamma^{(0)}_{ij,k}-\frac{\alpha}{2}C_{ijk}. \]

Duality

Theorem. For every \(\alpha\), the connections \(\nabla^{(\alpha)}\) and \(\nabla^{(-\alpha)}\) are conjugate with respect to the Fisher metric.

Special values

Proposition. The connection \(\nabla^{(0)}\) is Levi-Civita. The connection \(\nabla^{(1)}\) is the exponential connection, and \(\nabla^{(-1)}\) is the mixture connection, up to the sign convention used by the source.

Convention warning

Remark. Some books reverse the signs of the \(e\)- and \(m\)-connections. What matters is the dual pair, not the naming convention.
