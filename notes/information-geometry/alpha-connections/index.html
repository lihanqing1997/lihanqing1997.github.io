15. Alpha-connections

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/statistical-manifolds-cubic-tensor/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/divergences-local-geometry/" %}

The \(\alpha\)-connections form the standard one-parameter family of affine connections in information geometry. They interpolate between mixture, Levi-Civita, and exponential geometry.

Definition by cubic tensor

The whole family is obtained by shifting Levi-Civita by the cubic tensor.

Definition. The \(\alpha\)-connection is defined by \[ \Gamma^{(\alpha)}_{ijk} =\Gamma^{(0)}_{ijk}-\frac{\alpha}{2}C_{ijk}. \] The dual of \(\nabla^{(\alpha)}\) is \(\nabla^{(-\alpha)}\).

Proposition. The duality relation follows from the symmetry of \(C\): \[ Xg(Y,Z)=g(\nabla_X^{(\alpha)}Y,Z)+g(Y,\nabla_X^{(-\alpha)}Z). \]

Proof. The Levi-Civita connection already satisfies metric compatibility. The two added cubic terms appear with opposite signs and cancel because \(C(X,Y,Z)=C(X,Z,Y)\).

Exponential and mixture endpoints

The values \(\alpha=1\) and \(\alpha=-1\) are statistically special.

Definition. With the common convention, \(\nabla^{(1)}\) is the exponential connection and \(\nabla^{(-1)}\) is the mixture connection. Exponential families are flat for \(\nabla^{(1)}\); mixture families are flat for \(\nabla^{(-1)}\).

Remark. Some authors reverse the signs. What matters is the dual pair and the identification of exponential versus mixture affine coordinates.

Geodesics

Different \(\alpha\) values declare different curves of distributions to be straight.

Proposition. On the positive simplex, \(\alpha=1\) geodesics are straight in log-probability coordinates up to normalization, while \(\alpha=-1\) geodesics are straight in probability coordinates.

Proof. The simplex has mixture coordinates \(p_i\), in which mixture geodesics are ordinary line segments. Exponential coordinates are log ratios, and exponential geodesics are ordinary line segments in those coordinates. These are precisely affine coordinates for the corresponding flat connections.

Levi-Civita midpoint

The Fisher-Rao geodesics arise at \(\alpha=0\), not at the exponential or mixture endpoints.

Remark. The \(\alpha=0\) geometry is metric and length-based. The \(\alpha=\pm1\) geometries are affine and projection-based. Both viewpoints are useful but answer different questions.
