14. Statistical manifolds and cubic tensor

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/dual-connections/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/alpha-connections/" %}

A statistical manifold is a Riemannian manifold with a symmetric cubic tensor. The cubic tensor measures how far the preferred affine connections differ from Levi-Civita geometry.

Amari-Chentsov tensor

Definition. For a regular statistical model, the cubic tensor is \[ C_{ijk}=\mathbb E_\theta[s_is_js_k]. \] It is symmetric in all indices.

Statistical manifold

Definition. A statistical manifold is a pair \((M,g,C)\), where \(g\) is a Riemannian metric and \(C\) is a totally symmetric covariant three-tensor.

Connections from cubic tensor

Proposition. Given \((M,g,C)\), define a family of lowered Christoffel symbols by \[ \Gamma^{(\alpha)}_{ij,k}=\Gamma^{(0)}_{ij,k}-\frac{\alpha}{2}C_{ijk}, \] where \(\Gamma^{(0)}\) is Levi-Civita. The connections \(\nabla^{(\alpha)}\) and \(\nabla^{(-\alpha)}\) are dual.

Role of skewness

Remark. The Fisher metric records second moments of scores; the cubic tensor records third-order asymmetry of likelihood perturbations.

命题. 表述. Given \((M,g,C)\), define a family of lowered Christoffel symbols by \[ \Gamma^{(\alpha)}_{ij,k}=\Gamma^{(0)}_{ij,k}-\frac{\alpha}{2}C_{ijk}, \] where \(\Gamma^{(0)}\) is Levi-Civita. The connections \(\nabla^{(\alpha)}\) and \(\nabla^{(-\alpha)}\) are dual.
