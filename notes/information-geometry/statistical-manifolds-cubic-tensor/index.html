14. Statistical manifolds and cubic tensor

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-geometry/dual-connections/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-geometry/alpha-connections/" %}

A statistical manifold is not only Riemannian. It carries a cubic tensor that measures the difference between dual affine connections and the Levi-Civita connection.

Amari-Chentsov tensor

The cubic tensor is the third-order score moment in a parametric model.

Definition. The Amari-Chentsov tensor is \[ C_{ijk}(\theta)=\mathbb E_\theta[s_is_js_k]. \] It is symmetric in all indices under regularity conditions.

Proposition. Under reparametrization, \(C_{ijk}\) transforms as a covariant 3-tensor.

Proof. Each score component transforms by the chain rule with one Jacobian factor. Multiplying three score components and taking expectation gives three Jacobian factors, which is exactly the covariant tensor transformation law.

Difference of dual connections

The cubic tensor records how far the exponential and mixture connections are from Levi-Civita.

Definition. For a statistical manifold, the \(\alpha\)-connections satisfy \[ \Gamma^{(\alpha)}_{ijk} =\Gamma^{(0)}_{ijk}-\frac{\alpha}{2}C_{ijk}, \] where \(\Gamma_{ijk}=g_{k\ell}\Gamma^\ell_{ij}\).

Remark. Here \(\alpha=0\) is Levi-Civita, \(\alpha=1\) is the exponential connection, and \(\alpha=-1\) is the mixture connection, up to convention.

Symmetry and torsion

Because the cubic tensor is symmetric, the \(\alpha\)-connections remain torsion-free.

Proposition. If \(\nabla^{(0)}\) is torsion-free and \(C_{ijk}\) is symmetric in \(i,j\), then every \(\nabla^{(\alpha)}\) is torsion-free.

Proof. Torsion in coordinates is the antisymmetric part of \(\Gamma^k_{ij}\) in \(i,j\). The Levi-Civita part has no antisymmetric component, and the added tensor term is symmetric in \(i,j\), so the antisymmetric part remains zero.

Skewness of the model

The cubic tensor captures third-order asymmetry of the model around a distribution.

Remark. For normal location families the cubic tensor vanishes in the location direction; for exponential families it encodes third cumulants of sufficient statistics.
