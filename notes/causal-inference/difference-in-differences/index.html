16. Difference-in-differences

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/causal-inference/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/causal-inference/instrumental-variables/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/causal-inference/regression-discontinuity-designs/" %}

Difference-in-differences records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the causal inference note.

Definition

Definition. Difference-in-differences compares outcome changes over time between treated and comparison groups.

Proposition

Proposition. Under parallel trends, the treated group's untreated counterfactual change equals the comparison group's observed change, identifying the average treatment effect on the treated.

Proof

Proof. Write the treated post-treatment counterfactual as treated baseline outcome plus the comparison trend. Subtract observed treated baseline and compare with observed treated post outcome.

Remark

Remark. The key assumption is about untreated trends, not equality of levels. Event-study plots are diagnostics, not proofs.
