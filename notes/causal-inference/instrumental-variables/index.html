15. Instrumental variables

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/causal-inference/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/causal-inference/efficient-influence-functions/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/causal-inference/difference-in-differences/" %}

Instrumental variables records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the causal inference note.

Definition

Definition. An instrument affects treatment, is independent of unmeasured outcome causes, and affects the outcome only through treatment.

Proposition

Proposition. Under monotonicity and exclusion restrictions, the Wald ratio identifies a local average treatment effect among compliers.

Proof

Proof. Decompose observed treatment and outcome by principal strata. The instrument changes treatment only for compliers, so the reduced-form outcome difference equals the treatment difference times the complier effect.

Remark

Remark. Instrumental variables identify a specific causal margin. The estimand is not generally the population ATE.
