Consistency, exchangeability, and positivity records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the causal inference note.

Definition

Definition. The standard identification triad consists of consistency, exchangeability, and positivity. Together they connect observed conditional means to potential-outcome means.

Proof

Proof. Use exchangeability to replace \(Y(a)\) by its conditional distribution among units with \(A=a\), then use consistency to replace \(Y(a)\) by observed \(Y\) in that group and average over \(X\).

Remark

Remark. Positivity is not technical decoration: without overlap, some counterfactual means require extrapolation rather than identification.
