17. Regression discontinuity designs

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/causal-inference/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/causal-inference/difference-in-differences/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/causal-inference/longitudinal-treatment-regimes/" %}

Regression discontinuity designs records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the causal inference note.

Definition

Definition. Regression discontinuity uses a treatment rule that changes discontinuously at a threshold of a running variable. Units just above and below the cutoff are compared.

Proposition

Proposition. If potential outcome regressions are continuous at the cutoff, the jump in observed conditional mean identifies the causal effect at the cutoff.

Proof

Proof. Take one-sided limits of \(E[Y\mid R=r]\) as \(r\) approaches the cutoff. Consistency and continuity replace these limits by potential-outcome limits.

Remark

Remark. Regression discontinuity is local by nature. Bandwidth choices define the bias-variance and locality tradeoff.
