5. Matching and stratification

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/causal-inference/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/causal-inference/propensity-scores/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/causal-inference/directed-acyclic-graphs/" %}

Matching and stratification records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the causal inference note.

Definition

Definition. Matching and stratification compare treated and control units with similar covariates or propensity scores. They aim to approximate randomized comparisons within balanced groups.

Proposition

Proposition. If strata are sufficiently fine and identification assumptions hold within strata, stratified differences estimate weighted averages of conditional treatment effects.

Proof

Proof. Condition on stratum membership, apply the adjustment formula inside each stratum, and average with the target population's stratum weights.

Remark

Remark. Design-based causal inference emphasizes balance diagnostics before outcome modeling. Matching changes the effective target population.
