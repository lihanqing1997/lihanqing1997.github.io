1. Potential outcomes

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/causal-inference/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/causal-inference/consistency-exchangeability-and-positivity/" %}

Potential outcomes records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the causal inference note.

Definition

Definition. Potential outcomes \(Y(1)\) and \(Y(0)\) represent the outcomes that would be observed under treatment and control. Only one potential outcome is observed for each unit.

Proposition

Proposition. Causal effects are contrasts of potential outcomes, such as \(E[Y(1)-Y(0)]\). The fundamental missing-data problem is that \(Y(1)\) and \(Y(0)\) are never jointly observed on the same unit.

Proof

Proof. The observed outcome satisfies consistency \(Y=AY(1)+(1-A)Y(0)\). Identification therefore requires assumptions connecting unobserved potential outcomes to observed data.

Remark

Remark. Potential outcomes make causal questions precise before modeling. They separate the estimand from the estimator.
