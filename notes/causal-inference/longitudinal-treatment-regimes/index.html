Longitudinal treatment regimes records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the causal inference note.

Definition

Definition. A longitudinal regime assigns treatment decisions over time as functions of evolving history. Time-varying confounding can be affected by earlier treatment.

Proposition

Proposition. Sequential exchangeability, consistency, and positivity identify regime-specific mean outcomes by the longitudinal g-formula.

Proof

Proof. Iterate conditional expectations backward through time, replacing each observed treatment mechanism by the regime rule while preserving the observed history distribution under identification assumptions.

Remark

Remark. Time-varying confounding is why ordinary regression adjustment can fail: variables can be both confounders and mediators.
