d-separation records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the causal inference note.

Definition

Definition. d-separation is the graphical criterion for reading conditional independences in DAGs. Paths are blocked by conditioning on noncolliders or by failing to condition on colliders or their descendants.

Global Markov property

Theorem. In a Markovian DAG, d-separation of sets \(A\) and \(B\) by \(C\) implies \(A\perp B\mid C\).

Proof

Proof. Reduce dependence flow to path structures in the factorized distribution. Moralization or ancestral graph arguments convert d-separation into ordinary graph separation.

Remark

Remark. Collider bias is the most common conceptual trap: conditioning can open paths that were previously blocked.
