Front-door adjustment records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the causal inference note.

Definition

Definition. The front-door criterion identifies an effect through a mediator that intercepts all directed paths from treatment to outcome and has controlled confounding relations.

Proposition

Proposition. Under the front-door conditions, the causal effect of \(A\) on \(Y\) is identifiable even when the treatment-outcome relation is confounded.

Proof

Proof. First identify the effect of treatment on the mediator, then identify the mediator-outcome relation after adjusting for treatment, and combine the two pieces by summing or integrating over mediator values.

Remark

Remark. Front-door identification is important because it shows hidden treatment-outcome confounding is not always fatal.
