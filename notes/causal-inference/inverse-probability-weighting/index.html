Inverse probability weighting records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the causal inference note.

Definition

Definition. Inverse probability weighting estimates causal means by reweighting observed outcomes by the inverse probability of receiving the observed treatment.

Proposition

Proposition. Under exchangeability and positivity, \(E[A Y/e(X)]=E[Y(1)]\) and \(E[(1-A)Y/(1-e(X))]=E[Y(0)]\).

Proof

Proof. Condition on \(X\). Since \(E[A\mid X]=e(X)\), the weight cancels treatment assignment probability, and consistency replaces observed outcomes with potential outcomes.

Remark

Remark. IPW creates a pseudo-population with balanced covariates, but small propensities can cause large variance and instability.
