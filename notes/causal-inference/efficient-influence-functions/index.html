Efficient influence functions records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the causal inference note.

Definition

Definition. Efficient influence functions describe first-order sensitivity of causal parameters in semiparametric models and determine efficiency bounds.

Proposition

Proposition. For the ATE under nonparametric point-treatment assumptions, the efficient influence function is the difference of augmented treatment-specific scores minus the ATE.

Proof

Proof. Derive pathwise derivatives of the parameter along regular submodels and identify the unique gradient in the tangent space inner product. Orthogonal projection removes nuisance directions.

Remark

Remark. Influence-function language connects causal inference to statistical decision theory and semiparametric efficiency.
