12. Outcome regression and g-formula

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/causal-inference/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/causal-inference/inverse-probability-weighting/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/causal-inference/doubly-robust-estimation/" %}

Outcome regression and g-formula records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the causal inference note.

Definition

Definition. Outcome regression models or estimates \(m_a(X)=E[Y\mid A=a,X]\). The g-formula averages these conditional means over the target covariate distribution.

Proposition

Proposition. Under standard point-treatment identification assumptions, \(E[Y(a)]=E[m_a(X)]\).

Proof

Proof. This is exactly the adjustment formula. Estimate the conditional mean, then integrate it against the empirical or target covariate law.

Remark

Remark. Outcome regression extrapolates where treatment groups have weak overlap. Good prediction is not automatically good causal estimation if confounding structure is wrong.
