6. Directed acyclic graphs

Directed acyclic graphs records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the causal inference note.

Definition

Definition. A directed acyclic graph encodes qualitative causal relations through nodes and arrows, with no directed cycles. Parents of a node are its direct graphical causes in the model.

Proposition

Proposition. Under the Markov property, each node is independent of its nondescendants conditional on its parents. Under faithfulness, graphical separation corresponds to statistical independence.

Proof

Proof. Factor the joint density according to the graph. Conditional independence follows by separating factors involving parents, descendants, and nondescendants.

Remark

Remark. DAGs are not merely pictures; they are formal languages for assumptions. The graph does not prove the assumptions.
