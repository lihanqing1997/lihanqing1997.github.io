3. Average treatment effects

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/causal-inference/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/causal-inference/consistency-exchangeability-and-positivity/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/causal-inference/propensity-scores/" %}

Average treatment effects records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the causal inference note.

Definition

Definition. The average treatment effect is \(\operatorname{ATE}=E[Y(1)-Y(0)]\). Conditional and subgroup effects condition this contrast on covariates or populations.

Proposition

Proposition. Under identification assumptions, ATE can be written as an outcome-regression functional, an inverse-propensity functional, or a doubly robust functional.

Proof

Proof. Start from the adjustment formula, then use iterated expectation. For the weighting form, divide observed outcomes by treatment probabilities and condition on covariates.

Remark

Remark. Different formulas identify the same estimand but lead to estimators with different robustness and efficiency properties.
