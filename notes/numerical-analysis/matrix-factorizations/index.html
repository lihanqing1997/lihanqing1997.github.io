Matrix factorizations expose geometry: triangular factors encode elimination, orthogonal factors preserve lengths, and singular values reveal rank and conditioning.

QR factorization

Definition. A QR factorization writes \(A=QR\), where \(Q\) has orthonormal columns and \(R\) is upper triangular. Householder reflections and Givens rotations compute QR while preserving Euclidean norms.

Cholesky factorization

Proposition. If \(A\) is symmetric positive definite, then \(A=LL^\top\) with \(L\) lower triangular and positive diagonal. Cholesky is efficient and stable for positive definite systems, but it is not a remedy for indefiniteness.

Singular value decomposition

Theorem. Every real matrix has an SVD \(A=U\Sigma V^\top\), with orthogonal \(U,V\) and nonnegative singular values on the diagonal of \(\Sigma\). Singular values determine operator norm, rank, numerical rank, and least-squares sensitivity.

Rank-revealing factorizations

Definition. A rank-revealing factorization separates numerically large directions from small ones. The goal is not exact algebraic rank, which is unstable under perturbation, but a scale-dependent effective rank.

Orthogonality as stability

Remark. Algorithms built from orthogonal transformations are favored because they do not magnify Euclidean errors. This is why QR and SVD are preferred over normal equations in ill-conditioned least-squares problems.
