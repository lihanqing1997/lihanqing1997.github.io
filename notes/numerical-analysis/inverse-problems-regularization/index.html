Inverse problems try to recover causes from observed effects. They are often ill posed, so numerical analysis must include regularization from the start.

Ill-posed inverse problem

Definition. An inverse problem is ill posed when existence, uniqueness, or continuous dependence on data fails. Compact forward operators often have singular values tending to zero, which amplifies noise in inverse reconstruction.

Tikhonov regularization

Definition. Tikhonov regularization replaces \(Ax\approx b\) by minimizing \(\|Ax-b\|^2+\lambda\|Lx\|^2\). The parameter \(\lambda\) balances data fit against smoothness, size, or another structural penalty.

Spectral filtering

Proposition. In the SVD basis, regularization multiplies each noisy inverse coefficient by a filter factor. Truncated SVD discards small singular values; Tikhonov shrinks them continuously.

Bias-variance tradeoff

Theorem. Regularization introduces bias to control noise amplification. Under source conditions and noise bounds, an appropriate choice of \(\lambda\) yields convergence rates determined by smoothness and singular-value decay.

Statistical link

Remark. Many inverse problems are also statistical estimation problems. Numerical regularization, Bayesian priors, penalized likelihood, and minimax rates often describe the same stabilization phenomenon in different languages.
