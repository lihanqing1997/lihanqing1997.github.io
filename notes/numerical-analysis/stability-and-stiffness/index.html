Stability for time-stepping methods is about how numerical errors propagate along a computed trajectory.

Test equation

Definition. The scalar test equation \(\mathrm{d}y/\mathrm{d}t=\lambda y\) is used to study linear stability. A method produces \(y_{n+1}=R(h\lambda)y_n\), where \(R\) is the stability function.

Absolute stability region

Definition. The absolute stability region is \(\{z\in\mathbb C: |R(z)|\le1\}\). For decaying modes with \(\operatorname{Re}\lambda\lt0\), the product \(h\lambda\) should lie in this region.

Stiffness

Definition. A problem is stiff when stability forces explicit methods to take much smaller steps than accuracy alone would require. Stiffness is therefore a relation among the equation, the method, and the desired accuracy.

A-stability

Theorem. A method is A-stable if its stability region contains the entire left half-plane. Backward Euler is A-stable; no explicit Runge-Kutta method is A-stable.

Implicit cost

Remark. Implicit methods improve stability by requiring nonlinear or linear solves at each step. The practical method is therefore a coupled object: time discretization plus equation solver plus error controller.
