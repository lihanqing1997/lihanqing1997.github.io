Here Newton's method is treated as a solver for nonlinear equations, not as an optimization method for minimizing an objective.

Newton iteration

Definition. For \(F:\mathbb R^d\to\mathbb R^d\), Newton's method solves \(J_F(x_k)s_k=-F(x_k)\) and updates \(x_{k+1}=x_k+s_k\), where \(J_F\) is the Jacobian.

Local quadratic convergence

Theorem. If \(F(x^\star)=0\), \(J_F(x^\star)\) is nonsingular, and \(J_F\) is locally Lipschitz, then Newton iterates started sufficiently close to \(x^\star\) converge quadratically.

Kantorovich theorem

Theorem. Kantorovich-type results give computable conditions under which Newton iterates exist, remain in a controlled ball, and converge. They combine an inverse-Jacobian bound with a Lipschitz bound on the Jacobian.

Damping and line search

Definition. A damped Newton method uses \(x_{k+1}=x_k+\alpha_k s_k\), with \(0\lt\alpha_k\le1\), chosen to reduce a merit function such as \(\|F(x)\|^2\). Damping trades local speed for global reliability.

Relation to optimization Newton

Remark. Optimization Newton applies the same linearization idea to \(F=\nabla f\). Numerical analysis emphasizes residuals, Jacobian solves, conditioning, and globalization for equations, while optimization adds objective decrease and second-order optimality.
