30. Numerical analysis and statistics

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/numerical-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/numerical-analysis/inverse-problems-regularization/" %}

The final page records the interface with statistics without turning numerical analysis into a statistics note.

Computation-aware inference

Definition. Computation-aware inference treats the reported estimator, posterior summary, or test statistic as the output of an approximate algorithm. Its error has statistical components and numerical components.

Likelihood computation

Proposition. Numerical likelihoods may involve quadrature, ODE solvers, matrix factorizations, or iterative optimization. If the numerical tolerance is larger than the statistical uncertainty, inference can be dominated by computation rather than sampling.

Monte Carlo versus deterministic error

Definition. Monte Carlo error is stochastic and usually scales like \(n^{-1/2}\) under independence. Deterministic discretization error often scales like \(h^p\). Hybrid methods must track both scales rather than reporting a single undifferentiated error.

Approximate linear algebra in statistics

Remark. Large-scale regression, Gaussian processes, inverse problems, and Bayesian computation depend on approximate linear algebra. Preconditioning and numerical rank can affect not only speed but also the effective statistical model being fit.

Boundary with optimization

Remark. Optimization asks how to minimize or maximize an objective. Numerical analysis asks whether the finite computation accurately represents the mathematical problem. In statistical work the two meet, but they answer different questions.
