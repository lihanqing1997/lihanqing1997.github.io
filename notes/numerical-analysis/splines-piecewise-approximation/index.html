Splines replace one high-degree global polynomial by many low-degree pieces joined with controlled smoothness.

Spline space

Definition. A spline of degree \(r\) with knots \(a=t_0\lt t_1\lt\cdots\lt t_m=b\) is a function that is polynomial of degree at most \(r\) on each interval and satisfies prescribed continuity conditions at the knots.

Local support

Proposition. B-spline bases have local support: changing one coefficient affects only a few neighboring intervals. This locality gives sparse matrices, stable evaluation, and good behavior under local refinement.

Cubic splines

Theorem. The natural cubic spline interpolant minimizes \(\int_a^b |s''(x)|^2\,\mathrm{d}x\) among twice continuously differentiable functions interpolating the same data and satisfying the same endpoint conditions. Thus smoothness is encoded variationally.

Approximation order

Proposition. For sufficiently smooth \(f\), spline approximation error decays like a power of the maximal knot spacing. The rate is limited by both polynomial degree and smoothness of the target.

Why piecewise methods matter

Remark. Piecewise approximation handles functions with local features much better than global polynomial approximation. This is one reason splines sit naturally between interpolation, finite elements, and nonparametric smoothing.
