9. Brenier theorem

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/one-dimensional-transport/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/displacement-interpolation/" %}

Brenier theorem records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the optimal transport note.

Definition

Definition. For quadratic cost in Euclidean space, optimal transport maps are gradients of convex functions when the source measure is absolutely continuous.

Brenier theorem

Theorem. If \(\mu\) is absolutely continuous on \(\mathbb R^d\) and \(\nu\) has finite second moment, there is a unique optimal map for quadratic cost, and it has the form \(T=\nabla\phi\) for a convex function \(\phi\).

Proof

Proof. Quadratic cost converts \(c\)-cyclical monotonicity into ordinary cyclical monotonicity of the map. Rockafellar's theorem identifies cyclically monotone maps as subgradients of convex functions; absolute continuity selects a gradient almost everywhere.

Remark

Remark. Brenier's theorem is the main bridge between optimal transport and convex analysis.
