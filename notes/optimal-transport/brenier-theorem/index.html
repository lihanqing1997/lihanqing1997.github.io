8. Quadratic transport and Brenier's theorem

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/one-dimensional-transport/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/monge-ampere-equation/" %}

For squared Euclidean distance, cyclical monotonicity becomes ordinary convexity. This identifies the optimal map as a gradient and gives a multidimensional replacement for monotone rearrangement.

Convex subgradients

Definition. For a proper lower-semicontinuous convex function \(u:\mathbb R^d\to(-\infty,\infty]\), its subdifferential at \(x\) is \[ \partial u(x)=\{y:u(z)\ge u(x)+y\cdot(z-x)\ \text{for every }z\}. \] If \(u\) is differentiable at \(x\), then \(\partial u(x)=\{\nabla u(x)\}\).

Rockafellar theorem. A set \(\Gamma\subset\mathbb R^d\times\mathbb R^d\) is cyclically monotone for the pairing, meaning \[ \sum_i x_i\cdot y_i\ge\sum_i x_i\cdot y_{\sigma(i)}, \] if and only if it is contained in \(\partial u\) for some proper lower-semicontinuous convex \(u\).

For \(c(x,y)=\tfrac12|x-y|^2\), expanding the squares shows that \(c\)-cyclical monotonicity is exactly cyclical monotonicity of the pairing. The quadratic terms cancel under permutations.

Brenier's theorem

Brenier's theorem. Let \(\mu,\nu\in\mathcal P_2(\mathbb R^d)\), and suppose \(\mu\) is absolutely continuous with respect to Lebesgue measure. Then there exists a convex lower-semicontinuous \(u\) such that \[ T=\nabla u,\qquad T_\#\mu=\nu. \] The plan \((\operatorname{id},T)_\#\mu\) is the unique optimal plan for the cost \(\tfrac12|x-y|^2\). Consequently \(T\) is the unique optimal map \(\mu\)-almost everywhere.

Proof. Existence of an optimal plan \(\pi\) follows from page 3. Its support is \(c\)-cyclically monotone, so after expanding the quadratic cost it is cyclically monotone for \(x\cdot y\). Rockafellar's theorem gives a convex \(u\) with \(\operatorname{supp}\pi\subset\partial u\).

A convex function on \(\mathbb R^d\) is locally Lipschitz in the interior of its effective domain and therefore differentiable Lebesgue-almost everywhere by Rademacher's theorem. Since \(\mu\ll\mathcal L^d\), \(\partial u(x)=\{\nabla u(x)\}\) for \(\mu\)-almost every \(x\). Disintegrating \(\pi=\mu(\mathrm dx)\pi_x(\mathrm dy)\), the support inclusion forces \(\pi_x=\delta_{\nabla u(x)}\) almost surely. Hence \(\pi=(\operatorname{id},\nabla u)_\#\mu\) and \((\nabla u)_\#\mu=\nu\).

If \(\widetilde\pi\) is another optimal plan, the average \((\pi+\widetilde\pi)/2\) is optimal and the same argument makes it a graph plan. But the conditional law of this average is \(\tfrac12\delta_{\nabla u(x)}+\tfrac12\widetilde\pi_x\), which can be a Dirac mass only when \(\widetilde\pi_x=\delta_{\nabla u(x)}\) almost surely. Thus the optimal plan is unique.

Converse optimality

Proposition. If \(u\) is convex, \(\nabla u\) exists \(\mu\)-almost everywhere, and \(T=\nabla u\) pushes \(\mu\) to \(\nu\), then \(T\) is optimal for quadratic cost.

Proof. The graph of \(\partial u\) is cyclically monotone by summing the subgradient inequalities around a cycle. Therefore the graph of \(T\) is \(c\)-cyclically monotone for quadratic cost. The optimal support theorem from page 5, or equivalently the convex conjugate inequality \(u(x)+u^*(y)\ge x\cdot y\) with equality at \(y=\nabla u(x)\), supplies dual potentials touching the cost on the graph. Complementary slackness proves optimality.

Inverse transport and Legendre duality

If both \(\mu\) and \(\nu\) are absolutely continuous, the reverse optimal map is \(\nabla u^*\), where \[ u^*(y)=\sup_x\{x\cdot y-u(x)\}. \] At differentiability points, \[ y=\nabla u(x)\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad x=\nabla u^*(y). \] Thus the forward and reverse maps are inverse almost everywhere with respect to the corresponding measures.

Gaussian transport

Proposition. Let \(\mu=N(m_0,\Sigma_0)\), \(\nu=N(m_1,\Sigma_1)\), with positive-definite covariances. The Brenier map is \[ T(x)=m_1+A(x-m_0), \quad A=\Sigma_0^{-1/2} (\Sigma_0^{1/2}\Sigma_1\Sigma_0^{1/2})^{1/2} \Sigma_0^{-1/2}, \] and \[ W_2^2(\mu,\nu) =|m_0-m_1|^2+ \operatorname{tr}\!\left( \Sigma_0+\Sigma_1 -2(\Sigma_0^{1/2}\Sigma_1\Sigma_0^{1/2})^{1/2} \right). \]

Proof. The matrix \(A\) is symmetric positive definite, hence \(T=\nabla u\) for the convex quadratic \[ u(x)=m_1\cdot x+\tfrac12(x-m_0)^{\mathsf T}A(x-m_0). \] Direct multiplication gives \(A\Sigma_0A^{\mathsf T}=\Sigma_1\), so \(T_\#\mu=\nu\). The converse proposition proves optimality. Expanding \(\mathbb E|X-T(X)|^2\) and using the covariance identity yields the distance formula.

Polar factorization

Polar factorization theorem. Let \(\mu\ll\mathcal L^d\) and let \(S\in L^2(\mu;\mathbb R^d)\). If \(\nu=S_\#\mu\), then \[ S=\nabla u\circ s \] \(\mu\)-almost everywhere, where \(\nabla u\) is the Brenier map from \(\mu\) to \(\nu\) and \(s_\#\mu=\mu\). Under the usual nondegeneracy assumptions the factorization is unique.

This is the nonlinear analogue of matrix polar decomposition: the measure-preserving map \(s\) plays the role of the orthogonal factor, and the gradient of a convex function plays the role of the positive factor.

Why absolute continuity matters

Example. If \(\mu=\delta_0\) and \(\nu=\tfrac12(\delta_{-1}+\delta_1)\), the optimal plan cannot be induced by a map. More subtly, a source supported on a lower-dimensional surface may meet a multivalued part of \(\partial u\) on a set of positive \(\mu\)-mass. Absolute continuity removes this ambiguity because convex functions are differentiable Lebesgue-almost everywhere.

8. Transport quadratique et théorème de Brenier

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Retour" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Page précédente" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/one-dimensional-transport/" next_label="Page suivante" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/monge-ampere-equation/" %}

Pour la distance euclidienne quadratique, la monotonie cyclique devient la convexité ordinaire. L'application optimale est donc un gradient, ce qui fournit l'analogue multidimensionnel du réarrangement monotone.

Sous-gradients convexes

Définition. Pour une fonction convexe propre semi-continue inférieurement \(u\), \[ \partial u(x)=\{y:u(z)\ge u(x)+y\cdot(z-x)\ \text{pour tout }z\}. \] Si \(u\) est différentiable en \(x\), alors \(\partial u(x)=\{\nabla u(x)\}\).

Théorème de Rockafellar. Un ensemble \(\Gamma\) est cycliquement monotone pour l'appariement, c'est-à-dire \[ \sum_i x_i\cdot y_i\ge\sum_i x_i\cdot y_{\sigma(i)}, \] si et seulement s'il est contenu dans \(\partial u\) pour une fonction convexe propre semi-continue inférieurement \(u\).

Pour \(c(x,y)=\tfrac12|x-y|^2\), le développement des carrés montre que la \(c\)-monotonie cyclique est exactement la monotonie cyclique de \(x\cdot y\).

Théorème de Brenier

Théorème de Brenier. Soient \(\mu,\nu\in\mathcal P_2(\mathbb R^d)\), avec \(\mu\ll\mathcal L^d\). Il existe une fonction convexe semi-continue inférieurement \(u\) telle que \[ T=\nabla u,\qquad T_\#\mu=\nu. \] Le plan \((\operatorname{id},T)_\#\mu\) est l'unique plan optimal pour \(\tfrac12|x-y|^2\), et \(T\) est l'unique application optimale \(\mu\)-presque partout.

Preuve. Un plan optimal \(\pi\) existe par la page 3. Son support est \(c\)-cycliquement monotone; après développement du coût quadratique, le théorème de Rockafellar donne une fonction convexe \(u\) telle que \(\operatorname{supp}\pi\subset\partial u\).

Une fonction convexe est localement lipschitzienne dans l'intérieur de son domaine et donc différentiable presque partout par le théorème de Rademacher. Comme \(\mu\ll\mathcal L^d\), \(\partial u(x)=\{\nabla u(x)\}\) pour \(\mu\)-presque tout \(x\). En désintégrant \(\pi=\mu(\mathrm dx)\pi_x(\mathrm dy)\), l'inclusion du support impose \(\pi_x=\delta_{\nabla u(x)}\). Ainsi \(\pi=(\operatorname{id},\nabla u)_\#\mu\).

Si \(\widetilde\pi\) est aussi optimale, leur moyenne est optimale et doit également être portée par un graphe. Sa loi conditionnelle est \(\tfrac12\delta_{\nabla u(x)}+\tfrac12\widetilde\pi_x\), qui ne peut être une masse de Dirac que si \(\widetilde\pi_x=\delta_{\nabla u(x)}\). L'unicité suit.

Optimalité réciproque

Proposition. Si \(u\) est convexe, \(\nabla u\) existe \(\mu\)-presque partout et \(T=\nabla u\) vérifie \(T_\#\mu=\nu\), alors \(T\) est optimal pour le coût quadratique.

Preuve. Le graphe de \(\partial u\) est cycliquement monotone, comme on le voit en sommant les inégalités de sous-gradient le long d'un cycle. Il est donc \(c\)-cycliquement monotone. L'inégalité de Fenchel \(u(x)+u^*(y)\ge x\cdot y\), avec égalité pour \(y=\nabla u(x)\), fournit des potentiels duaux qui touchent le coût sur le graphe. La complémentarité prouve l'optimalité.

Transport inverse et dualité de Legendre

Si \(\mu\) et \(\nu\) sont toutes deux absolument continues, l'application optimale inverse est \(\nabla u^*\), où \(u^*(y)=\sup_x\{x\cdot y-u(x)\}\). Aux points de différentiabilité, \[ y=\nabla u(x)\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad x=\nabla u^*(y). \]

Transport gaussien

Proposition. Pour \(\mu=N(m_0,\Sigma_0)\), \(\nu=N(m_1,\Sigma_1)\) à covariances définies positives, \[ T(x)=m_1+A(x-m_0),\quad A=\Sigma_0^{-1/2}(\Sigma_0^{1/2}\Sigma_1\Sigma_0^{1/2})^{1/2}\Sigma_0^{-1/2}, \] et \[ W_2^2=|m_0-m_1|^2+ \operatorname{tr}\!\left(\Sigma_0+\Sigma_1 -2(\Sigma_0^{1/2}\Sigma_1\Sigma_0^{1/2})^{1/2}\right). \]

Preuve. \(A\) est symétrique définie positive, donc \(T\) est le gradient d'une fonction quadratique convexe. L'identité \(A\Sigma_0A^{\mathsf T}=\Sigma_1\) donne \(T_\#\mu=\nu\), puis la proposition précédente donne l'optimalité. Le développement de \(\mathbb E|X-T(X)|^2\) fournit la formule.

Factorisation polaire

Théorème de factorisation polaire. Si \(\mu\ll\mathcal L^d\), \(S\in L^2(\mu;\mathbb R^d)\), et \(\nu=S_\#\mu\), alors \[ S=\nabla u\circ s \] \(\mu\)-presque partout, où \(\nabla u\) est l'application de Brenier de \(\mu\) vers \(\nu\) et \(s_\#\mu=\mu\).

C'est l'analogue non linéaire de la décomposition polaire des matrices : \(s\) joue le rôle du facteur orthogonal et le gradient convexe celui du facteur positif.

Rôle de l'absolue continuité

Exemple. Pour \(\mu=\delta_0\) et \(\nu=\tfrac12(\delta_{-1}+\delta_1)\), aucun plan optimal ne provient d'une application. Plus généralement, une mesure source portée par une sous-variété peut rencontrer une partie multivoque de \(\partial u\) sur un ensemble de masse positive. L'absolue continuité élimine cette ambiguïté par la différentiabilité presque partout des fonctions convexes.

8. 二次传输与布雷尼耶定理

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="返回" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="上一页" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/one-dimensional-transport/" next_label="下一页" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/monge-ampere-equation/" %}

对欧几里得平方距离，循环单调性转化为普通凸性。这把最优映射识别为凸函数的梯度，并给出单调重排的多维对应物。

凸函数的次梯度

定义. 对真下半连续凸函数 \(u:\mathbb R^d\to(-\infty,\infty]\)，定义 \[ \partial u(x)=\{y:u(z)\ge u(x)+y\cdot(z-x)\ \text{对所有 }z\}. \] 若 \(u\) 在 \(x\) 可微，则 \(\partial u(x)=\{\nabla u(x)\}\)。

罗卡费拉定理. 集合 \(\Gamma\subset\mathbb R^d\times\mathbb R^d\) 关于内积循环单调，即 \[ \sum_i x_i\cdot y_i\ge\sum_i x_i\cdot y_{\sigma(i)}, \] 当且仅当存在真下半连续凸函数 \(u\)，使 \(\Gamma\subset\partial u\)。

对 \(c(x,y)=\tfrac12|x-y|^2\)，展开平方项后，置换下纯 \(x\) 项和纯 \(y\) 项相消，故 \(c\)-循环单调性恰好等价于内积的循环单调性。

布雷尼耶定理

布雷尼耶定理. 设 \(\mu,\nu\in\mathcal P_2(\mathbb R^d)\)，并且 \(\mu\ll\mathcal L^d\)。则存在下半连续凸函数 \(u\)，使得 \[ T=\nabla u,\qquad T_\#\mu=\nu. \] 计划 \((\operatorname{id},T)_\#\mu\) 是代价 \(\tfrac12|x-y|^2\) 的唯一最优计划。因此 \(T\) 在 \(\mu\)-几乎处处意义下是唯一最优映射。

证明. 第 3 页保证存在最优计划 \(\pi\)。其支撑为 \(c\)-循环单调集；展开二次代价后，罗卡费拉定理给出凸函数 \(u\)，使 \(\operatorname{supp}\pi\subset\partial u\)。

凸函数在其有效域内部局部利普希茨，故由拉德马赫定理几乎处处可微。由于 \(\mu\ll\mathcal L^d\)，对 \(\mu\)-几乎处处的 \(x\)，有 \(\partial u(x)=\{\nabla u(x)\}\)。分解 \(\pi=\mu(\mathrm dx)\pi_x(\mathrm dy)\)，支撑包含关系强制 \(\pi_x=\delta_{\nabla u(x)}\)。所以 \(\pi=(\operatorname{id},\nabla u)_\#\mu\)，并且 \((\nabla u)_\#\mu=\nu\)。

若 \(\widetilde\pi\) 也是最优计划，则平均计划 \((\pi+\widetilde\pi)/2\) 仍最优，也必须是图像型计划。但其条件分布为 \(\tfrac12\delta_{\nabla u(x)}+\tfrac12\widetilde\pi_x\)，只有当 \(\widetilde\pi_x=\delta_{\nabla u(x)}\) 时才是狄拉克测度，故最优计划唯一。

反向最优性

命题. 若 \(u\) 凸，\(\nabla u\) 在 \(\mu\)-几乎处处存在，且 \(T=\nabla u\) 满足 \(T_\#\mu=\nu\)，则 \(T\) 对二次代价最优。

证明. 沿一个循环求和次梯度不等式，可见 \(\partial u\) 的图像循环单调，故 \(T\) 的图像关于二次代价 \(c\)-循环单调。也可用芬歇尔不等式 \(u(x)+u^*(y)\ge x\cdot y\)，它在 \(y=\nabla u(x)\) 时取等号，从而产生在图像上接触代价的对偶势。互补松弛给出最优性。

逆传输与勒让德对偶

若 \(\mu,\nu\) 都绝对连续，则反向最优映射为 \(\nabla u^*\)，其中 \[ u^*(y)=\sup_x\{x\cdot y-u(x)\}. \] 在可微点， \[ y=\nabla u(x)\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad x=\nabla u^*(y). \]

高斯传输

命题. 设 \(\mu=N(m_0,\Sigma_0)\)、\(\nu=N(m_1,\Sigma_1)\)，协方差均正定。则 \[ T(x)=m_1+A(x-m_0),\quad A=\Sigma_0^{-1/2}(\Sigma_0^{1/2}\Sigma_1\Sigma_0^{1/2})^{1/2}\Sigma_0^{-1/2}, \] 且 \[ W_2^2=|m_0-m_1|^2+ \operatorname{tr}\!\left(\Sigma_0+\Sigma_1 -2(\Sigma_0^{1/2}\Sigma_1\Sigma_0^{1/2})^{1/2}\right). \]

证明. \(A\) 对称正定，因此 \(T\) 是某个凸二次函数的梯度。直接计算得 \(A\Sigma_0A^{\mathsf T}=\Sigma_1\)，故 \(T_\#\mu=\nu\)，由上一命题知其最优。展开 \(\mathbb E|X-T(X)|^2\) 并利用协方差恒等式即得距离公式。

极分解

极分解定理. 设 \(\mu\ll\mathcal L^d\)、\(S\in L^2(\mu;\mathbb R^d)\)，并令 \(\nu=S_\#\mu\)。则 \[ S=\nabla u\circ s \] 在 \(\mu\)-几乎处处成立，其中 \(\nabla u\) 是从 \(\mu\) 到 \(\nu\) 的布雷尼耶映射，且 \(s_\#\mu=\mu\)。

这是矩阵极分解的非线性对应：保测映射 \(s\) 扮演正交因子的角色，凸函数梯度则扮演正因子的角色。

绝对连续性为何重要

例. 若 \(\mu=\delta_0\)、\(\nu=\tfrac12(\delta_{-1}+\delta_1)\)，最优计划不可能由映射诱导。更一般地，支撑在低维曲面上的源测度可能在正质量集合上落入 \(\partial u\) 的多值部分。绝对连续性利用凸函数的勒贝格几乎处处可微性排除了这种歧义。
