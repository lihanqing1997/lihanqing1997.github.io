16. Monge-Ampere equation

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/talagrand-and-transport-inequalities/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/entropic-regularization/" %}

Monge-Ampere equation records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the optimal transport note.

Definition

Definition. When the Brenier map \(T=\nabla\phi\) pushes a density \(\rho_0\) to \(\rho_1\), the convex potential formally satisfies a Monge-Ampère equation.

Proposition

Proposition. For smooth positive densities, \(\rho_0(x)=\rho_1(\nabla\phi(x))\det D^2\phi(x)\).

Proof

Proof. Apply the change-of-variables formula to \(T=\nabla\phi\). The Jacobian determinant of the gradient map is \(\det D^2\phi\).

Remark

Remark. Regularity theory for optimal maps is largely regularity theory for this nonlinear PDE under convexity constraints.
