9. The Monge--Ampère equation

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/brenier-theorem/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/regularity-of-optimal-maps-and-general-costs/" %}

Brenier's theorem turns quadratic transport into a convex potential \(u\). When the measures have densities, the pushforward condition becomes a nonlinear partial differential equation for \(u\). Its determinant measures how the gradient map changes volume.

Derivation from change of variables

Jacobian equation. Let \(\mu=f(x)\,\mathrm dx\) on a domain \(\Omega\), \(\nu=g(y)\,\mathrm dy\) on \(\Lambda\), and suppose \(T:\Omega\to\Lambda\) is a \(C^1\)-diffeomorphism with \(T_\#\mu=\nu\). Then \[ f(x)=g(T(x))|\det DT(x)| \] for almost every \(x\). If \(T=\nabla u\) with \(u\) convex and \(D^2u>0\), then \[ \boxed{\det D^2u(x)=\frac{f(x)}{g(\nabla u(x))}}, \qquad \nabla u(\Omega)=\Lambda. \]

Proof. For every bounded continuous \(h\), \[ \int_\Lambda h(y)g(y)\,\mathrm dy =\int_\Omega h(T(x))f(x)\,\mathrm dx. \] Changing variables \(y=T(x)\) in the left side gives \[ \int_\Omega h(T(x))g(T(x))|\det DT(x)|\,\mathrm dx. \] Equality for all \(h\) yields the Jacobian identity. For a convex potential, \(DT=D^2u\) is positive semidefinite, so the absolute value may be removed. The image condition is the second boundary condition, not a pointwise Dirichlet condition.

Ellipticity and convexity

The operator \(u\mapsto\det D^2u\) is elliptic only on convex functions. Indeed, differentiating the determinant at a positive-definite matrix \(A\) in direction \(H\) gives \[ D(\det)_A[H]=\operatorname{cof}(A):H =\det(A)\operatorname{tr}(A^{-1}H), \] whose coefficient matrix \(\operatorname{cof}(A)\) is positive definite. Convexity is therefore both the transport structure and the ellipticity condition.

Linearized Monge--Ampère operator. At a smooth strictly convex solution \(u\), the linearization of \(\det D^2u=F\) is \[ L_uv=\operatorname{cof}(D^2u):D^2v. \] Moreover, each row of \(\operatorname{cof}(D^2u)\) is divergence free, so \[ L_uv=\nabla\cdot(\operatorname{cof}(D^2u)\nabla v). \]

Alexandrov solutions

Optimal potentials need not be twice differentiable. The correct weak formulation uses the geometry of the subgradient rather than distributional determinants.

Definition. For a convex \(u:\Omega\to\mathbb R\), its Monge--Ampère measure is \[ M_u(E)=\mathcal L^d(\partial u(E)), \qquad E\subset\Omega\ \text{Borel}. \] We call \(u\) an Alexandrov solution of \(\det D^2u=\rho\) if \(M_u(E)=\int_E\rho(x)\,\mathrm dx\) for every Borel \(E\).

Consistency. If \(u\in C^2(\Omega)\) is strictly convex, then \[ M_u(E)=\int_E\det D^2u(x)\,\mathrm dx. \]

Proof. Strict convexity makes \(\partial u(x)=\{\nabla u(x)\}\), and the area formula for the injective gradient map gives \[ \mathcal L^d(\nabla u(E)) =\int_E|\det D(\nabla u)(x)|\,\mathrm dx =\int_E\det D^2u(x)\,\mathrm dx. \]

Transport formulation of weak solutions

Brenier--Alexandrov correspondence. Let \(\Omega,\Lambda\) be bounded convex domains, let \(f,g\) be positive densities with equal total mass, and let \(u\) be the Brenier potential satisfying \((\nabla u)_\#(f\,\mathrm dx)=g\,\mathrm dy\). Then \(u\) solves \[ g(\nabla u)\det D^2u=f \] in the Alexandrov sense, together with \(\partial u(\Omega)\subset\overline\Lambda\) and the full-mass image condition. Conversely, an Alexandrov solution satisfying the transport boundary condition induces the optimal map.

The equation and the image condition must be kept together. The determinant alone controls local volume change but does not specify which target domain receives the mass.

One-dimensional and radial examples

One dimension. The equation is \[ u''(x)=\frac{f(x)}{g(u'(x))}. \] Since \(T=u'\), integration gives \(F_\nu(T(x))=F_\mu(x)\), hence \(T=F_\nu^{-1}\circ F_\mu\). Thus the Monge--Ampère equation reduces exactly to monotone rearrangement.

Radial measures. If \(f,g\) are radial and \(T(x)=t(r)x/r\), mass conservation inside balls gives \[ \int_0^r f_0(s)s^{d-1}\,\mathrm ds = \int_0^{t(r)}g_0(s)s^{d-1}\,\mathrm ds. \] Differentiation yields \[ f_0(r)r^{d-1}=g_0(t(r))t(r)^{d-1}t'(r), \] which equals the determinant formula because the radial and tangential eigenvalues of \(DT\) are \(t'(r)\) and \(t(r)/r\).

Quadratic potentials. For \(u(x)=\tfrac12x^{\mathsf T}Ax+b\cdot x\) with \(A>0\), \(\det D^2u=\det A\). The gradient map sends ellipsoids to ellipsoids and recovers the Gaussian transport matrix from page 8.

Regularity questions

The equation suggests that smooth positive \(f,g\) should produce a smooth potential, but this is false without geometry. Convexity of the domains, bounds away from zero and infinity, and boundary regularity are essential. The next page separates interior regularity, global regularity, and the additional curvature condition required by general costs.

9. L'équation de Monge--Ampère

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Retour" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Page précédente" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/brenier-theorem/" next_label="Page suivante" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/regularity-of-optimal-maps-and-general-costs/" %}

Le théorème de Brenier transforme le transport quadratique en un potentiel convexe \(u\). Lorsque les mesures ont des densités, la condition d'image devient une équation aux dérivées partielles non linéaire dont le déterminant mesure la variation de volume du gradient.

Dérivation par changement de variables

Équation jacobienne. Soient \(\mu=f(x)\,\mathrm dx\) sur \(\Omega\), \(\nu=g(y)\,\mathrm dy\) sur \(\Lambda\), et \(T\) un \(C^1\)-difféomorphisme tel que \(T_\#\mu=\nu\). Alors \[ f(x)=g(T(x))|\det DT(x)|. \] Si \(T=\nabla u\), \(u\) convexe et \(D^2u>0\), \[ \det D^2u(x)=\frac{f(x)}{g(\nabla u(x))}, \qquad \nabla u(\Omega)=\Lambda. \]

Preuve. Pour toute fonction continue bornée \(h\), \[ \int_\Lambda h(y)g(y)\,\mathrm dy =\int_\Omega h(T(x))f(x)\,\mathrm dx. \] Le changement \(y=T(x)\) dans le membre gauche le transforme en \(\int_\Omega h(T(x))g(T(x))|\det DT(x)|\,\mathrm dx\). L'égalité pour tout \(h\) donne l'identité. Pour un potentiel convexe, \(D^2u\ge0\), donc la valeur absolue disparaît. La condition d'image est une seconde condition au bord, non une condition de Dirichlet ponctuelle.

Ellipticité et convexité

La dérivée du déterminant en une matrice définie positive \(A\) est \[ D(\det)_A[H]=\operatorname{cof}(A):H =\det(A)\operatorname{tr}(A^{-1}H). \] La matrice des coefficients est positive. L'opérateur n'est donc elliptique que sur le cône des fonctions convexes.

Opérateur linéarisé. Au voisinage d'une solution strictement convexe, \[ L_uv=\operatorname{cof}(D^2u):D^2v =\nabla\cdot(\operatorname{cof}(D^2u)\nabla v), \] car chaque ligne de \(\operatorname{cof}(D^2u)\) est sans divergence.

Solutions d'Alexandrov

Définition. Pour \(u\) convexe, sa mesure de Monge--Ampère est \[ M_u(E)=\mathcal L^d(\partial u(E)). \] On dit que \(u\) est solution d'Alexandrov de \(\det D^2u=\rho\) si \(M_u(E)=\int_E\rho(x)\,\mathrm dx\) pour tout borélien \(E\).

Compatibilité classique. Si \(u\in C^2\) est strictement convexe, \[ M_u(E)=\int_E\det D^2u(x)\,\mathrm dx. \]

Preuve. Alors \(\partial u(x)=\{\nabla u(x)\}\), et la formule de l'aire appliquée au gradient injectif donne \[ \mathcal L^d(\nabla u(E)) =\int_E|\det D(\nabla u)|\,\mathrm dx =\int_E\det D^2u\,\mathrm dx. \]

Formulation faible par le transport

Correspondance de Brenier--Alexandrov. Soient \(\Omega,\Lambda\) convexes bornés, et \(f,g\) des densités positives de même masse. Le potentiel de Brenier satisfaisant \((\nabla u)_\#(f\,\mathrm dx)=g\,\mathrm dy\) résout \[ g(\nabla u)\det D^2u=f \] au sens d'Alexandrov, avec la condition d'image. Réciproquement, une solution d'Alexandrov satisfaisant cette condition induit l'application optimale.

L'équation et la condition d'image sont inséparables : le déterminant règle le changement local de volume, mais ne désigne pas le domaine cible.

Exemples unidimensionnel et radial

Dimension un. L'équation devient \(u''(x)=f(x)/g(u'(x))\). Comme \(T=u'\), son intégration donne \(F_\nu(T(x))=F_\mu(x)\), donc \(T=F_\nu^{-1}\circ F_\mu\).

Mesures radiales. Pour \(T(x)=t(r)x/r\), la conservation de la masse des boules donne \[ \int_0^r f_0(s)s^{d-1}\,\mathrm ds =\int_0^{t(r)}g_0(s)s^{d-1}\,\mathrm ds. \] Après dérivation, \[ f_0(r)r^{d-1}=g_0(t(r))t(r)^{d-1}t'(r), \] en accord avec les valeurs propres radiale \(t'(r)\) et tangentielles \(t(r)/r\) de \(DT\).

Potentiels quadratiques. Pour \(u(x)=\tfrac12x^{\mathsf T}Ax+b\cdot x\), \(A>0\), on a \(\det D^2u=\det A\). Le gradient envoie les ellipsoïdes sur des ellipsoïdes et retrouve le transport gaussien.

Questions de régularité

Des densités positives lisses ne suffisent pas à elles seules. La convexité des domaines, des bornes uniformes sur les densités et la régularité du bord sont essentielles. La page suivante distingue régularité intérieure, régularité globale et condition de courbure propre aux coûts généraux.

9. 蒙日--安培方程

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="返回" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="上一页" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/brenier-theorem/" next_label="下一页" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/regularity-of-optimal-maps-and-general-costs/" %}

布雷尼耶定理把二次传输转化为凸势 \(u\)。当两个测度具有密度时，推前条件变成关于 \(u\) 的非线性偏微分方程；其中行列式刻画梯度映射造成的体积变化。

由换元公式导出

雅可比方程. 设 \(\mu=f(x)\,\mathrm dx\) 支撑于 \(\Omega\)，\(\nu=g(y)\,\mathrm dy\) 支撑于 \(\Lambda\)，且 \(T:\Omega\to\Lambda\) 为满足 \(T_\#\mu=\nu\) 的 \(C^1\) 微分同胚，则 \[ f(x)=g(T(x))|\det DT(x)|. \] 若 \(T=\nabla u\)、\(u\) 凸且 \(D^2u>0\)，则 \[ \boxed{\det D^2u(x)=\frac{f(x)}{g(\nabla u(x))}}, \qquad \nabla u(\Omega)=\Lambda. \]

证明. 对每个有界连续函数 \(h\)， \[ \int_\Lambda h(y)g(y)\,\mathrm dy =\int_\Omega h(T(x))f(x)\,\mathrm dx. \] 在左边作换元 \(y=T(x)\)，得到 \(\int_\Omega h(T(x))g(T(x))|\det DT(x)|\,\mathrm dx\)。对所有 \(h\) 的等式给出雅可比恒等式。凸势满足 \(DT=D^2u\ge0\)，故可去掉绝对值。像集条件是第二边界条件，而非逐点狄利克雷边界条件。

椭圆性与凸性

行列式在正定矩阵 \(A\) 处沿方向 \(H\) 的导数为 \[ D(\det)_A[H]=\operatorname{cof}(A):H =\det(A)\operatorname{tr}(A^{-1}H). \] 系数矩阵 \(\operatorname{cof}(A)\) 正定。因此蒙日--安培算子只在凸函数锥上椭圆；凸性既是传输结构，也是椭圆性条件。

线性化蒙日--安培算子. 在光滑严格凸解 \(u\) 处， \[ L_uv=\operatorname{cof}(D^2u):D^2v =\nabla\cdot(\operatorname{cof}(D^2u)\nabla v), \] 因为 \(\operatorname{cof}(D^2u)\) 的每一行散度为零。

亚历山德罗夫解

最优势未必二阶可微。正确的弱形式应使用次梯度的几何，而不是直接把行列式解释为分布。

定义. 对凸函数 \(u:\Omega\to\mathbb R\)，定义其蒙日--安培测度 \[ M_u(E)=\mathcal L^d(\partial u(E)). \] 若对每个博雷尔集 \(E\)，\(M_u(E)=\int_E\rho(x)\,\mathrm dx\)，则称 \(u\) 是 \(\det D^2u=\rho\) 的亚历山德罗夫解。

与经典解的一致性. 若 \(u\in C^2(\Omega)\) 严格凸，则 \[ M_u(E)=\int_E\det D^2u(x)\,\mathrm dx. \]

证明. 严格凸时 \(\partial u(x)=\{\nabla u(x)\}\)。对单射梯度映射应用面积公式， \[ \mathcal L^d(\nabla u(E)) =\int_E|\det D(\nabla u)(x)|\,\mathrm dx =\int_E\det D^2u(x)\,\mathrm dx. \]

弱解的传输表述

布雷尼耶--亚历山德罗夫对应. 设 \(\Omega,\Lambda\) 为有界凸域，\(f,g\) 为总质量相同的正密度。满足 \((\nabla u)_\#(f\,\mathrm dx)=g\,\mathrm dy\) 的布雷尼耶势在亚历山德罗夫意义下解 \[ g(\nabla u)\det D^2u=f, \] 并满足传输像集条件。反之，满足该像集条件的亚历山德罗夫解诱导最优映射。

方程与像集条件不可分离：行列式只控制局部体积变化，却不能单独指定质量应到达哪个目标域。

一维与径向例子

一维. 方程化为 \(u''(x)=f(x)/g(u'(x))\)。由于 \(T=u'\)，积分得到 \(F_\nu(T(x))=F_\mu(x)\)，从而 \(T=F_\nu^{-1}\circ F_\mu\)，正好恢复单调重排。

径向测度. 若 \(T(x)=t(r)x/r\)，球内质量守恒给出 \[ \int_0^r f_0(s)s^{d-1}\,\mathrm ds =\int_0^{t(r)}g_0(s)s^{d-1}\,\mathrm ds. \] 求导得 \[ f_0(r)r^{d-1}=g_0(t(r))t(r)^{d-1}t'(r). \] 这与 \(DT\) 的径向特征值 \(t'(r)\) 和切向特征值 \(t(r)/r\) 所给出的行列式完全一致。

二次势. 若 \(u(x)=\tfrac12x^{\mathsf T}Ax+b\cdot x\)、\(A>0\)，则 \(\det D^2u=\det A\)。梯度映射把椭球送到椭球，并恢复第 8 页的高斯传输矩阵。

正则性问题

方程看似暗示光滑正密度应产生光滑势，但缺少几何条件时并非如此。区域凸性、密度的一致上下界和边界正则性都不可缺少。下一页将分别讨论内部正则性、整体正则性以及一般代价所需的额外曲率条件。
