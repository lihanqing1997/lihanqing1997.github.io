24. Sinkhorn divergences and semi-discrete transport

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/entropic-regularization/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/schrodinger-bridges-and-entropic-dynamics/" %}

Two complementary constructions improve the usability of transport without abandoning its geometry. Sinkhorn divergences remove the self-interaction bias of entropy. Semi-discrete transport keeps the source continuous and the target finite, converting the dual problem into a smooth concave optimization over weighted geometric cells.

Debiasing entropic transport

Definition. The Sinkhorn divergence is \[ S_\varepsilon(\mu,\nu) = \operatorname{OT}_\varepsilon(\mu,\nu) -\frac12\operatorname{OT}_\varepsilon(\mu,\mu) -\frac12\operatorname{OT}_\varepsilon(\nu,\nu). \] The two self-costs remove the entropic preference for diffuse couplings.

Definiteness. For the usual negative-type ground costs, including standard powers of Euclidean distance in their admissible range, \(S_\varepsilon(\mu,\nu)\ge0\) and \[ S_\varepsilon(\mu,\nu)=0\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad\mu=\nu. \] Symmetry follows when the cost is symmetric.

The statement is cost dependent: debiasing an arbitrary asymmetric or non-negative-type cost need not produce a metric-like divergence. Even in the favorable case, the triangle inequality is not generally asserted.

Interpolation between transport and kernel discrepancies

Two limiting regimes. Under suitable integrability, \[ \lim_{\varepsilon\downarrow0}S_\varepsilon(\mu,\nu) =\operatorname{OT}_0(\mu,\nu), \] while \[ \lim_{\varepsilon\to\infty}S_\varepsilon(\mu,\nu) = \int c\,\mathrm d\mu\,\mathrm d\nu -\frac12\int c\,\mathrm d\mu\,\mathrm d\mu -\frac12\int c\,\mathrm d\nu\,\mathrm d\nu. \] The latter is an energy-distance or maximum-mean-discrepancy form when \(-c\) induces a positive kernel modulo constants.

Proof idea. The small-\(\varepsilon\) statement follows from the zero-temperature convergence of each term, noting that the unregularized self-costs vanish. For large \(\varepsilon\), each entropic optimizer approaches the product coupling. Substitute the three product couplings into the debiased combination; the leading entropic constants cancel.

Statistical smoothing and bias

For fixed \(\varepsilon>0\), positive kernels make dual potentials smooth functions of the marginals, so \(S_\varepsilon(\widehat\mu_n,\widehat\nu_m)\) often has root-sample Gaussian limits under substantially weaker dimensional conditions than unregularized transport. If \(\varepsilon=\varepsilon_n\downarrow0\), inference must balance three terms: \[ \text{total error} = \text{sampling error} +\text{entropic bias} +\text{numerical error}. \] Sending \(\varepsilon_n\) to zero too quickly restores the nonsmooth and dimension-sensitive behavior of ordinary transport.

The semi-discrete setting

Definition. Let the source be \(\mu=\rho(x)\,\mathrm dx\) on \(\Omega\subset\mathbb R^d\), and let \[ \nu=\sum_{j=1}^Nb_j\delta_{y_j},\qquad b_j>0. \] Semi-discrete transport seeks a measurable partition of \(\Omega\) into cells of masses \(b_j\), sending each cell to \(y_j\).

Laguerre cells and the dual objective

Definition. For weights \(\psi\in\mathbb R^N\), define \[ L_j(\psi) = \{x:c(x,y_j)-\psi_j \le c(x,y_k)-\psi_k\ \text{for every }k\}. \] These are Laguerre cells. For quadratic cost they are power cells, bounded by affine hyperplanes.

Semi-discrete dual. The dual problem is the unconstrained concave maximization \[ \max_{\psi\in\mathbb R^N} \Phi(\psi), \qquad \Phi(\psi) = \sum_{j=1}^Nb_j\psi_j +\int_\Omega\min_j\{c(x,y_j)-\psi_j\}\,\mathrm d\mu(x). \] At a maximizer, \(\mu(L_j(\psi))=b_j\) for every \(j\), and the optimal map is \[ T(x)=y_j\quad\text{for }x\in L_j(\psi). \]

Proof. In the Kantorovich dual, optimize the source potential pointwise: \[ \varphi(x)=\min_j\{c(x,y_j)-\psi_j\}. \] Substitution gives \(\Phi\). If cell boundaries have zero \(\mu\)-mass, differentiation under the integral yields \[ \frac{\partial\Phi}{\partial\psi_j} =b_j-\mu(L_j(\psi)). \] Thus stationarity is exactly the cell-mass condition. Complementary slackness then sends each cell to its defining target point.

Power diagrams for quadratic cost

For \(c(x,y)=\tfrac12|x-y|^2\), \[ L_j(\psi) = \left\{ x:x\cdot(y_k-y_j) \le\frac{|y_k|^2-|y_j|^2}{2}+\psi_j-\psi_k \ \forall k \right\}. \] Hence each cell is a convex polyhedron. Changing \(\psi_j\) moves its facets without moving the sites \(y_j\); the weights are exactly the variables required to match prescribed cell masses.

Hessian and Newton geometry

Hessian structure. Assume \(\rho\) is continuous and positive and cells meet regularly. For \(i\ne j\), \[ \frac{\partial^2\Phi}{\partial\psi_i\partial\psi_j} = \int_{F_{ij}} \frac{\rho(x)} {|\nabla_xc(x,y_i)-\nabla_xc(x,y_j)|} \,\mathrm d\mathcal H^{d-1}(x), \] where \(F_{ij}=L_i\cap L_j\) is the common facet, and \[ \frac{\partial^2\Phi}{\partial\psi_i^2} =-\sum_{j\ne i}\frac{\partial^2\Phi}{\partial\psi_i\partial\psi_j}. \] Thus the Hessian is a negative weighted graph Laplacian.

Proof idea. Perturbing \(\psi_j\) moves only facets adjacent to \(L_j\). The coarea formula converts normal facet speed into first-order cell-mass change, producing the boundary integral. Total mass is invariant under adding a common constant to all weights, so row sums vanish. Positivity of facet weights makes the Hessian negative semidefinite, with constants as its gauge nullspace when the adjacency graph is connected.

After fixing a gauge such as \(\sum_j\psi_j=0\), Newton's method solves \[ D^2\Phi(\psi)\,\Delta\psi=-\nabla\Phi(\psi). \] Damping keeps all cells nonempty and ensures ascent. The Laplacian structure explains both fast local convergence and ill-conditioning when cells nearly disappear.

A one-dimensional cell calculation

Example. Let \(y_1<y_2\) and \(c(x,y)=\tfrac12|x-y|^2\). The boundary \(r\) between the two cells satisfies \[ \frac12|r-y_1|^2-\psi_1 = \frac12|r-y_2|^2-\psi_2, \] hence \[ r=\frac{y_1+y_2}{2}+\frac{\psi_1-\psi_2}{y_2-y_1}. \] The mass condition \(F_\mu(r)=b_1\) determines the weight difference. Thus the semi-discrete dual recovers the quantile boundary exactly.

Consistency under discretization

If discrete targets \(\nu_N\to\nu\) in \(W_2\), stability gives convergence of semi-discrete optimal values. Under uniqueness and regularity, the cell maps converge in measure to the Brenier map. This makes semi-discrete transport both a geometric solver and a principled map estimator, provided the discretization and density estimation errors are controlled.

24. Divergences de Sinkhorn et transport semi-discret

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Retour" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Page précédente" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/entropic-regularization/" next_label="Page suivante" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/schrodinger-bridges-and-entropic-dynamics/" %}

Deux constructions complémentaires améliorent le transport sans abandonner sa géométrie. Les divergences de Sinkhorn retirent le biais d'auto-interaction de l'entropie. Le transport semi-discret garde une source continue et une cible finie, et transforme le dual en optimisation concave sur des cellules pondérées.

Débiaisage du transport entropique

Définition. \[ S_\varepsilon(\mu,\nu) =\operatorname{OT}_\varepsilon(\mu,\nu) -\frac12\operatorname{OT}_\varepsilon(\mu,\mu) -\frac12\operatorname{OT}_\varepsilon(\nu,\nu). \]

Définitude. Pour les coûts usuels de type négatif, \(S_\varepsilon\ge0\) et \(S_\varepsilon(\mu,\nu)=0\) si et seulement si \(\mu=\nu\). Cette propriété dépend du coût, et l'inégalité triangulaire n'est pas affirmée en général.

Entre transport et distances à noyau

Deux limites. \[ \lim_{\varepsilon\downarrow0}S_\varepsilon=\operatorname{OT}_0, \] tandis que \[ \lim_{\varepsilon\to\infty}S_\varepsilon = \int c\,\mathrm d\mu\,\mathrm d\nu -\frac12\int c\,\mathrm d\mu\,\mathrm d\mu -\frac12\int c\,\mathrm d\nu\,\mathrm d\nu. \]

Idée de la preuve. La petite régularisation utilise la convergence de chaque coût et l'annulation des auto-coûts non régularisés. À grande régularisation, chaque plan tend vers le produit; les constantes entropiques dominantes s'annulent dans la combinaison débiaisée.

Lissage statistique et biais

À \(\varepsilon>0\) fixé, la dépendance lisse des potentiels donne souvent des limites gaussiennes à vitesse racine. Si \(\varepsilon_n\downarrow0\), il faut équilibrer erreur d'échantillonnage, biais entropique et erreur numérique.

Cadre semi-discret

Définition. La source est \(\mu=\rho\,\mathrm dx\), la cible \[ \nu=\sum_{j=1}^Nb_j\delta_{y_j}. \] On cherche une partition de la source en cellules de masses \(b_j\), chacune envoyée vers \(y_j\).

Cellules de Laguerre et dual

Définition. \[ L_j(\psi)=\{x:c(x,y_j)-\psi_j\le c(x,y_k)-\psi_k\ \forall k\}. \]

Dual semi-discret. \[ \max_{\psi\in\mathbb R^N} \Phi(\psi),\qquad \Phi(\psi)=\sum_jb_j\psi_j+ \int\min_j\{c(x,y_j)-\psi_j\}\,\mathrm d\mu(x). \] Au maximum, \(\mu(L_j(\psi))=b_j\), et \(T(x)=y_j\) sur \(L_j(\psi)\).

Preuve. Dans le dual, l'optimisation ponctuelle donne \(\varphi(x)=\min_j(c(x,y_j)-\psi_j)\). Si les frontières sont négligeables, \[ \frac{\partial\Phi}{\partial\psi_j}=b_j-\mu(L_j(\psi)). \] La stationnarité donne les masses, puis la complémentarité donne l'application.

Diagrammes de puissance

Pour \(c(x,y)=|x-y|^2/2\), les cellules sont les polyèdres \[ x\cdot(y_k-y_j)\le\frac{|y_k|^2-|y_j|^2}{2}+\psi_j-\psi_k. \] Les poids déplacent les facettes jusqu'à obtenir les masses prescrites.

Hessien et géométrie de Newton

Structure du hessien. Pour \(i\ne j\), \[ \frac{\partial^2\Phi}{\partial\psi_i\partial\psi_j} = \int_{F_{ij}} \frac{\rho(x)} {|\nabla_xc(x,y_i)-\nabla_xc(x,y_j)|} \,\mathrm d\mathcal H^{d-1}, \] et la diagonale est l'opposé de la somme hors diagonale. Le hessien est donc l'opposé d'un laplacien de graphe pondéré.

La formule vient de la vitesse normale des facettes et de la coaire. Après la jauge \(\sum_j\psi_j=0\), Newton résout \(D^2\Phi\,\Delta\psi=-\nabla\Phi\). Un amortissement empêche la disparition des cellules.

Calcul unidimensionnel

Exemple. Pour \(y_1<y_2\), la frontière vérifie \[ r=\frac{y_1+y_2}{2}+\frac{\psi_1-\psi_2}{y_2-y_1}. \] La condition \(F_\mu(r)=b_1\) détermine la différence des poids et retrouve la frontière quantile.

Convergence de la discrétisation

Si \(\nu_N\to\nu\) dans \(W_2\), les valeurs semi-discrètes convergent. Sous unicité et régularité, les applications cellulaires convergent en mesure vers l'application de Brenier.

24. 辛克霍恩散度与半离散传输

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="返回" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="上一页" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/entropic-regularization/" next_label="下一页" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/schrodinger-bridges-and-entropic-dynamics/" %}

两种互补构造在不放弃传输几何的前提下改善了可用性。辛克霍恩散度消除熵带来的自相互作用偏差；半离散传输保持源测度连续、目标测度有限，把对偶问题化为加权几何单元上的光滑凹优化。

熵传输的去偏

定义. 辛克霍恩散度为 \[ S_\varepsilon(\mu,\nu) = \operatorname{OT}_\varepsilon(\mu,\nu) -\frac12\operatorname{OT}_\varepsilon(\mu,\mu) -\frac12\operatorname{OT}_\varepsilon(\nu,\nu). \] 两个自身代价消除了熵对弥散耦合的偏好。

正定性. 对通常的负型底层代价，包括适当范围内的欧几里得距离幂，有 \(S_\varepsilon(\mu,\nu)\ge0\)，且 \[ S_\varepsilon(\mu,\nu)=0\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad\mu=\nu. \] 该结论依赖代价；对任意不对称或非负型代价，去偏后未必有这种性质。即使在良好情形，也通常不声称三角不等式。

传输与核差异之间的插值

两个极限区域. 在适当可积条件下， \[ \lim_{\varepsilon\downarrow0}S_\varepsilon(\mu,\nu) =\operatorname{OT}_0(\mu,\nu), \] 而 \[ \lim_{\varepsilon\to\infty}S_\varepsilon(\mu,\nu) = \int c\,\mathrm d\mu\,\mathrm d\nu -\frac12\int c\,\mathrm d\mu\,\mathrm d\mu -\frac12\int c\,\mathrm d\nu\,\mathrm d\nu. \] 当 \(-c\) 在模常数意义下诱导正定核时，后式是能量距离或最大均值差异形式。

证明思路. 小 \(\varepsilon\) 结论来自三个正则代价各自的零温收敛，并注意未正则自身代价为零。大 \(\varepsilon\) 时，每个熵最优计划趋于乘积耦合；把三个乘积耦合代入去偏组合，主导熵常数相消。

统计平滑与偏差

固定 \(\varepsilon>0\) 时，正核使对偶势随边缘光滑变化，因此 \(S_\varepsilon(\widehat\mu_n,\widehat\nu_m)\) 常在比未正则传输更弱的维数条件下具有根号样本量高斯极限。若 \(\varepsilon_n\downarrow0\)，总误差必须分为 \[ \text{抽样误差}+\text{熵偏差}+\text{数值误差}. \] \(\varepsilon_n\) 下降过快会重新引入普通传输的非光滑性与维数敏感性。

半离散设定

定义. 设源测度 \(\mu=\rho(x)\,\mathrm dx\) 支撑于 \(\Omega\subset\mathbb R^d\)，目标测度为 \[ \nu=\sum_{j=1}^Nb_j\delta_{y_j},\qquad b_j>0. \] 半离散传输寻找 \(\Omega\) 的可测分割，使第 \(j\) 个单元质量为 \(b_j\)，并把该单元全部送往 \(y_j\)。

拉盖尔单元与对偶目标

定义. 对权重 \(\psi\in\mathbb R^N\)，定义 \[ L_j(\psi) = \{x:c(x,y_j)-\psi_j \le c(x,y_k)-\psi_k\ \text{对每个 }k\}. \] 这些集合称为拉盖尔单元。对二次代价，它们是由仿射超平面围成的幂单元。

半离散对偶. 对偶问题是无约束凹极大化 \[ \max_{\psi\in\mathbb R^N}\Phi(\psi), \qquad \Phi(\psi) = \sum_jb_j\psi_j +\int_\Omega\min_j\{c(x,y_j)-\psi_j\}\,\mathrm d\mu(x). \] 在极大点，\(\mu(L_j(\psi))=b_j\)，且最优映射为 \[ T(x)=y_j\quad\text{当 }x\in L_j(\psi). \]

证明. 在康托罗维奇对偶中逐点优化源势， \[ \varphi(x)=\min_j\{c(x,y_j)-\psi_j\}. \] 代回即得 \(\Phi\)。若单元边界的 \(\mu\)-质量为零，则 \[ \frac{\partial\Phi}{\partial\psi_j}=b_j-\mu(L_j(\psi)). \] 驻定性就是单元质量条件，互补松弛再说明每个单元应被送往其定义目标点。

二次代价的幂图

对 \(c(x,y)=\tfrac12|x-y|^2\)， \[ L_j(\psi) = \left\{ x:x\cdot(y_k-y_j) \le\frac{|y_k|^2-|y_j|^2}{2}+\psi_j-\psi_k \ \forall k \right\}. \] 因此每个单元为凸多面体。改变 \(\psi_j\) 会移动单元面但不移动站点 \(y_j\)，权重正是匹配指定单元质量所需的变量。

黑塞矩阵与牛顿几何

黑塞矩阵结构. 假设 \(\rho\) 连续为正且单元正则相交。对 \(i\ne j\)， \[ \frac{\partial^2\Phi}{\partial\psi_i\partial\psi_j} = \int_{F_{ij}} \frac{\rho(x)} {|\nabla_xc(x,y_i)-\nabla_xc(x,y_j)|} \,\mathrm d\mathcal H^{d-1}(x), \] 且 \[ \frac{\partial^2\Phi}{\partial\psi_i^2} =-\sum_{j\ne i}\frac{\partial^2\Phi}{\partial\psi_i\partial\psi_j}. \] 因此黑塞矩阵是负的加权图拉普拉斯矩阵。

证明思路. 扰动 \(\psi_j\) 只移动与 \(L_j\) 相邻的面。余面积公式把面的法向速度转化为单元质量的一阶变化，得到边界积分。给所有权重加同一个常数不改变单元，因此行和为零。若邻接图连通，常数方向就是唯一规范零空间。

固定 \(\sum_j\psi_j=0\) 后，牛顿法求解 \[ D^2\Phi(\psi)\Delta\psi=-\nabla\Phi(\psi). \] 阻尼保持所有单元非空并保证上升。拉普拉斯结构同时解释了快速局部收敛，以及单元接近消失时的病态条件数。

一维单元计算

例. 设 \(y_1<y_2\)，\(c(x,y)=\tfrac12|x-y|^2\)。两单元边界 \(r\) 满足 \[ \frac12|r-y_1|^2-\psi_1 =\frac12|r-y_2|^2-\psi_2, \] 因此 \[ r=\frac{y_1+y_2}{2}+\frac{\psi_1-\psi_2}{y_2-y_1}. \] 质量条件 \(F_\mu(r)=b_1\) 决定权重差，恰好恢复分位数边界。

离散化相合性

若离散目标 \(\nu_N\to\nu\) 于 \(W_2\)，稳定性保证半离散最优值收敛。在唯一性与正则性条件下，单元映射依测度收敛到布雷尼耶映射。因此，只要控制离散化与密度估计误差，半离散传输既是几何求解器，也是有理论依据的映射估计器。
