Kantorovich duality records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the optimal transport note.

Definition

Definition. The dual problem maximizes \(\int \varphi\,\mathrm{d}\mu+\int\psi\,\mathrm{d}\nu\) over functions satisfying \(\varphi(x)+\psi(y)\le c(x,y)\).

Kantorovich duality

Theorem. Under standard lower semicontinuity and integrability assumptions, the primal transport value equals the Kantorovich dual value.

Proof

Proof. Weak duality follows by integrating the inequality against any coupling. Strong duality follows from convex analysis, tight approximation, and closure of admissible potentials.

Remark

Remark. Dual potentials are certificates of optimality. They are the analogues of Lagrange multipliers for marginal constraints.
