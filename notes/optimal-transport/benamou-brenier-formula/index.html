11. Benamou-Brenier formula

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/displacement-interpolation/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/continuity-equation/" %}

Benamou-Brenier formula records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the optimal transport note.

Definition

Definition. The dynamic formulation represents \(W_2\) by minimizing kinetic energy over density-velocity pairs satisfying the continuity equation.

Benamou-Brenier formula

Theorem. For suitable measures on \(\mathbb R^d\), \(W_2^2(\mu_0,\mu_1)=\inf\int_0^1\int |v_t(x)|^2\,\mathrm{d}\mu_t(x)\,\mathrm{d}t\), subject to \(\partial_t\mu_t+\nabla\cdot(v_t\mu_t)=0\).

Proof

Proof. A static optimal map induces a constant-speed particle flow with the right action. Conversely, any admissible flow yields a coupling of endpoints whose transport cost is bounded by the action.

Remark

Remark. This formula turns optimal transport into fluid mechanics and makes Wasserstein geometry variational.
