14. The Benamou--Brenier formula and tangent geometry

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/continuity-equation/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/wasserstein-barycenters-and-frechet-geometry/" %}

The static problem minimizes endpoint displacement. The Benamou--Brenier formula gives the same distance by minimizing kinetic energy over every mass-preserving flow joining the endpoints. It is the bridge from transport plans to fluid mechanics and the source of the formal Riemannian structure of \(\mathcal P_2\).

Dynamic action

Definition. An admissible dynamic pair between \(\mu_0,\mu_1\in\mathcal P_2(\mathbb R^d)\) consists of a narrowly continuous curve \(\mu_t\) and a velocity field \(v_t\in L^2(\mu_t)\) satisfying \[ \partial_t\mu_t+\nabla\cdot(v_t\mu_t)=0, \qquad \mu_{t=0}=\mu_0,\quad\mu_{t=1}=\mu_1. \] Its action is \[ \mathcal A(\mu,v)=\int_0^1\int_{\mathbb R^d}|v_t(x)|^2\,\mathrm d\mu_t(x)\,\mathrm dt. \]

The dynamic formula

Benamou--Brenier formula. For \(\mu_0,\mu_1\in\mathcal P_2(\mathbb R^d)\), \[ \boxed{ W_2^2(\mu_0,\mu_1) = \inf_{(\mu_t,v_t)} \int_0^1\int|v_t|^2\,\mathrm d\mu_t\,\mathrm dt }, \] where the infimum is over all admissible dynamic pairs.

Proof. For any admissible pair, page 13 gives \[ W_2(\mu_0,\mu_1) \le\int_0^1|\dot\mu_t|\,\mathrm dt \le\int_0^1\|v_t\|_{L^2(\mu_t)}\,\mathrm dt. \] Cauchy--Schwarz on the unit time interval yields \[ W_2^2(\mu_0,\mu_1) \le\int_0^1\|v_t\|_{L^2(\mu_t)}^2\,\mathrm dt =\mathcal A(\mu,v). \] Hence \(W_2^2\) is a lower bound on every action.

For the reverse inequality, take the displacement geodesic generated by an optimal plan. It is absolutely continuous with constant metric speed \(W_2(\mu_0,\mu_1)\). Its minimal velocity field satisfies \[ \|v_t\|_{L^2(\mu_t)}=|\dot\mu_t|=W_2(\mu_0,\mu_1) \] for almost every \(t\). Its action is therefore exactly \(W_2^2(\mu_0,\mu_1)\), attaining the lower bound.

Convex momentum formulation

If \(\mu_t=\rho_t\,\mathrm dx\), introduce momentum \(m_t=\rho_tv_t\). The constraints become linear: \[ \partial_t\rho+\nabla\cdot m=0. \] The action becomes \[ \int_0^1\int\frac{|m_t(x)|^2}{\rho_t(x)}\,\mathrm dx\,\mathrm dt, \] with the lower-semicontinuous convention \(0/0=0\) and \(|m|^2/0=+\infty\) for \(m\ne0\). The perspective function \((\rho,m)\mapsto|m|^2/\rho\) is convex, so the dynamic transport problem is a convex optimization problem in \((\rho,m)\).

Convexity. Since \(q(v)=|v|^2\) is convex, its perspective \(\rho q(m/\rho)=|m|^2/\rho\) is convex for \(\rho>0\). The lower-semicontinuous extension preserves convexity at \(\rho=0\).

Equations for a smooth geodesic

A minimal velocity belongs to the tangent space and is therefore a gradient, \(v_t=\nabla\phi_t\), under smoothness assumptions. First variations of the action subject to the continuity equation give \[ \partial_t\phi_t+\frac12|\nabla\phi_t|^2=0. \] Taking a gradient yields the inviscid Burgers equation \[ \partial_tv_t+(v_t\cdot\nabla)v_t=0. \] Together with \[ \partial_t\rho_t+\nabla\cdot(\rho_tv_t)=0, \] this is pressureless Euler flow: particles keep constant velocity along straight characteristics.

Derivation. Add a Lagrange multiplier \(\phi\) for the continuity equation and integrate by parts: \[ \int\left\{\frac12\rho|v|^2+\phi(\partial_t\rho+\nabla\cdot(\rho v))\right\} = \int\rho\left\{\frac12|v|^2-\partial_t\phi-v\cdot\nabla\phi\right\}, \] up to endpoint terms. Variation in \(v\) gives \(v=\nabla\phi\) after the corresponding sign convention; variation in \(\rho\) then gives the Hamilton--Jacobi equation.

Otto's tangent metric

Definition. For a sufficiently regular \(\mu\), \[ T_\mu\mathcal P_2 =\overline{\{\nabla\phi:\phi\in C_c^\infty(\mathbb R^d)\}}^{L^2(\mu)}. \] The formal Riemannian inner product is \[ \langle\nabla\phi,\nabla\psi\rangle_{T_\mu} =\int\nabla\phi\cdot\nabla\psi\,\mathrm d\mu. \]

A tangent vector is represented by the distribution \(-\nabla\cdot(\mu\nabla\phi)\), but the gradient field is the useful minimal representative. The metric norm of the tangent vector is \(\|\nabla\phi\|_{L^2(\mu)}\). The Benamou--Brenier formula is therefore the usual energy formula for Riemannian distance, interpreted on an infinite-dimensional space.

First variations and Wasserstein gradients

Formal gradient formula. Suppose a functional \(\mathcal F\) has first variation \(\delta\mathcal F/\delta\rho\), meaning \[ \frac{\mathrm d}{\mathrm d\varepsilon}\mathcal F(\rho+\varepsilon\sigma)\bigg|_{\varepsilon=0} =\int\frac{\delta\mathcal F}{\delta\rho}\,\sigma\,\mathrm dx. \] Along \(\partial_t\rho+\nabla\cdot(\rho v)=0\), \[ \frac{\mathrm d}{\mathrm dt}\mathcal F(\rho_t) =\int\nabla\!\left(\frac{\delta\mathcal F}{\delta\rho}\right)\cdot v_t\,\rho_t\,\mathrm dx. \] Hence \[ \operatorname{grad}_{W_2}\mathcal F(\rho) =\nabla\!\left(\frac{\delta\mathcal F}{\delta\rho}\right). \]

Proof. Substitute \(\partial_t\rho=-\nabla\cdot(\rho v)\) into the first-variation formula and integrate by parts. The resulting \(L^2(\rho)\) pairing identifies the Riemannian gradient.

Examples of the action

Translation. For \(\mu_t=(x\mapsto x+ta)_\#\mu_0\), \(v_t=a\), so \[ \mathcal A=\int_0^1|a|^2\,\mathrm dt=|a|^2=W_2^2(\mu_0,\mu_1). \]

General \(p\). The same argument gives \[ W_p^p(\mu_0,\mu_1) =\inf\int_0^1\int|v_t|^p\,\mathrm d\mu_t\,\mathrm dt \] for \(1<p<\infty\). Only \(p=2\) produces a quadratic inner-product geometry.

14. Formule de Benamou--Brenier et géométrie tangente

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Retour" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Page précédente" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/continuity-equation/" next_label="Page suivante" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/wasserstein-barycenters-and-frechet-geometry/" %}

Le problème statique minimise le déplacement entre extrémités. La formule de Benamou--Brenier obtient la même distance en minimisant l'énergie cinétique parmi tous les flots conservant la masse. Elle relie transport, mécanique des fluides et géométrie riemannienne formelle de \(\mathcal P_2\).

Action dynamique

Définition. Une paire dynamique admissible satisfait \[ \partial_t\mu_t+\nabla\cdot(v_t\mu_t)=0,\qquad \mu_{t=0}=\mu_0,\quad\mu_{t=1}=\mu_1, \] et son action est \[ \mathcal A(\mu,v)=\int_0^1\int|v_t|^2\,\mathrm d\mu_t\,\mathrm dt. \]

Formule dynamique

Formule de Benamou--Brenier. \[ W_2^2(\mu_0,\mu_1) =\inf_{(\mu_t,v_t)} \int_0^1\int|v_t|^2\,\mathrm d\mu_t\,\mathrm dt. \]

Preuve. Pour toute paire admissible, la page 13 et Cauchy--Schwarz donnent \[ W_2(\mu_0,\mu_1) \le\int_0^1\|v_t\|_{L^2(\mu_t)}\,\mathrm dt \le\mathcal A(\mu,v)^{1/2}. \] Donc \(W_2^2\) minore toute action. Réciproquement, une géodésique de déplacement possède une vitesse minimale de norme constante \[ \|v_t\|_{L^2(\mu_t)}=|\dot\mu_t|=W_2(\mu_0,\mu_1). \] Son action vaut exactement \(W_2^2\).

Formulation convexe par la quantité de mouvement

Si \(\mu_t=\rho_t\,\mathrm dx\), posons \(m_t=\rho_tv_t\). Alors \[ \partial_t\rho+\nabla\cdot m=0,\qquad \mathcal A=\int_0^1\int\frac{|m|^2}{\rho}\,\mathrm dx\,\mathrm dt. \] La perspective \((\rho,m)\mapsto|m|^2/\rho\) est convexe, avec l'extension semi-continue \(0/0=0\) et \(|m|^2/0=+\infty\) si \(m\ne0\). Le problème dynamique est donc convexe en \((\rho,m)\).

Équations d'une géodésique lisse

Une vitesse minimale est un gradient \(v_t=\nabla\phi_t\). Les variations de l'action sous la contrainte de continuité donnent \[ \partial_t\phi_t+\frac12|\nabla\phi_t|^2=0, \] puis \[ \partial_tv_t+(v_t\cdot\nabla)v_t=0. \] Avec \(\partial_t\rho+\nabla\cdot(\rho v)=0\), on obtient le système d'Euler sans pression : les particules gardent une vitesse constante le long des caractéristiques.

Dérivation. Ajoutons un multiplicateur \(\phi\) pour la contrainte et intégrons par parties : \[ \int\left\{\frac12\rho|v|^2+\phi(\partial_t\rho+\nabla\cdot(\rho v))\right\} = \int\rho\left\{\frac12|v|^2-\partial_t\phi-v\cdot\nabla\phi\right\}, \] hors termes aux extrémités. La variation en \(v\) donne \(v=\nabla\phi\), au signe près, et celle en \(\rho\) donne Hamilton--Jacobi.

Métrique tangente d'Otto

Définition. \[ T_\mu\mathcal P_2 =\overline{\{\nabla\phi:\phi\in C_c^\infty\}}^{L^2(\mu)}, \qquad \langle\nabla\phi,\nabla\psi\rangle_{T_\mu} =\int\nabla\phi\cdot\nabla\psi\,\mathrm d\mu. \]

Le vecteur tangent est la distribution \(-\nabla\cdot(\mu\nabla\phi)\), représentée minimalement par \(\nabla\phi\). La formule dynamique est alors la formule d'énergie habituelle de la distance riemannienne, dans un espace de dimension infinie.

Premières variations et gradients de Wasserstein

Formule formelle. Si \(\mathcal F\) a pour première variation \(\delta\mathcal F/\delta\rho\), alors le long de la continuité \[ \frac{\mathrm d}{\mathrm dt}\mathcal F(\rho_t) =\int\nabla\!\left(\frac{\delta\mathcal F}{\delta\rho}\right)\cdot v_t\,\rho_t\,\mathrm dx. \] Ainsi \[ \operatorname{grad}_{W_2}\mathcal F =\nabla\!\left(\frac{\delta\mathcal F}{\delta\rho}\right). \]

Preuve. On substitue \(\partial_t\rho=-\nabla\cdot(\rho v)\) dans la première variation et on intègre par parties.

Exemples d'action

Translation. Pour \(v_t=a\), \(\mathcal A=|a|^2=W_2^2(\mu_0,\mu_1)\).

Exposant général. Pour \(1<p<\infty\), \[ W_p^p(\mu_0,\mu_1)=\inf\int_0^1\int|v_t|^p\,\mathrm d\mu_t\,\mathrm dt. \] Seul \(p=2\) fournit une géométrie issue d'un produit scalaire.

14. 贝纳穆--布雷尼耶公式与切空间几何

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="返回" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="上一页" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/continuity-equation/" next_label="下一页" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/wasserstein-barycenters-and-frechet-geometry/" %}

静态问题最小化端点位移。贝纳穆--布雷尼耶公式则在所有连接端点的保质量流中最小化动能，并得到同一距离。它把传输计划、流体力学与 \(\mathcal P_2\) 的形式黎曼结构连接起来。

动态作用量

定义. 连接 \(\mu_0,\mu_1\) 的可行动态对 \((\mu_t,v_t)\) 满足 \[ \partial_t\mu_t+\nabla\cdot(v_t\mu_t)=0,\qquad \mu_{t=0}=\mu_0,\quad\mu_{t=1}=\mu_1. \] 其作用量为 \[ \mathcal A(\mu,v)=\int_0^1\int|v_t(x)|^2\,\mathrm d\mu_t(x)\,\mathrm dt. \]

动态公式

贝纳穆--布雷尼耶公式. \[ \boxed{ W_2^2(\mu_0,\mu_1) = \inf_{(\mu_t,v_t)} \int_0^1\int|v_t|^2\,\mathrm d\mu_t\,\mathrm dt }. \]

证明. 对任意可行动态对，第 13 页给出 \[ W_2(\mu_0,\mu_1) \le\int_0^1|\dot\mu_t|\,\mathrm dt \le\int_0^1\|v_t\|_{L^2(\mu_t)}\,\mathrm dt. \] 在单位时间区间应用柯西--施瓦茨不等式，得到 \(W_2^2\le\mathcal A(\mu,v)\)。反之，取最优计划生成的位移测地线。其最小速度满足 \[ \|v_t\|_{L^2(\mu_t)}=|\dot\mu_t|=W_2(\mu_0,\mu_1) \] 几乎处处，故作用量恰为 \(W_2^2\)，达到下界。

动量的凸表述

若 \(\mu_t=\rho_t\,\mathrm dx\)，令动量 \(m_t=\rho_tv_t\)。约束变为线性的 \[ \partial_t\rho+\nabla\cdot m=0, \] 作用量变为 \[ \int_0^1\int\frac{|m_t(x)|^2}{\rho_t(x)}\,\mathrm dx\,\mathrm dt. \] 透视函数 \((\rho,m)\mapsto|m|^2/\rho\) 为凸函数，并以 \(0/0=0\)、\(|m|^2/0=+\infty\) 作下半连续延拓。因此动态最优传输关于 \((\rho,m)\) 是凸优化问题。

光滑测地线的方程

在光滑条件下，最小速度为梯度 \(v_t=\nabla\phi_t\)。在连续性方程约束下对作用量求一阶变分，得到 \[ \partial_t\phi_t+\frac12|\nabla\phi_t|^2=0. \] 再取梯度， \[ \partial_tv_t+(v_t\cdot\nabla)v_t=0. \] 与 \(\partial_t\rho+\nabla\cdot(\rho v)=0\) 合并，就是无压欧拉方程：粒子沿直线特征线保持速度。

推导. 为连续性方程引入拉格朗日乘子 \(\phi\)，分部积分得 \[ \int\left\{\frac12\rho|v|^2+\phi(\partial_t\rho+\nabla\cdot(\rho v))\right\} = \int\rho\left\{\frac12|v|^2-\partial_t\phi-v\cdot\nabla\phi\right\}, \] 忽略端点项。对 \(v\) 变分给出 \(v=\nabla\phi\)，符号随约定而变；对 \(\rho\) 变分给出哈密顿--雅可比方程。

奥托切空间度量

定义. \[ T_\mu\mathcal P_2 =\overline{\{\nabla\phi:\phi\in C_c^\infty(\mathbb R^d)\}}^{L^2(\mu)}, \qquad \langle\nabla\phi,\nabla\psi\rangle_{T_\mu} =\int\nabla\phi\cdot\nabla\psi\,\mathrm d\mu. \]

切向量在分布意义下是 \(-\nabla\cdot(\mu\nabla\phi)\)，而梯度场是其最小表示。贝纳穆--布雷尼耶公式因此正是无穷维黎曼空间中用能量定义距离的公式。

一阶变分与瓦瑟斯坦梯度

形式梯度公式. 若泛函 \(\mathcal F\) 的一阶变分为 \(\delta\mathcal F/\delta\rho\)，则沿连续性方程 \[ \frac{\mathrm d}{\mathrm dt}\mathcal F(\rho_t) =\int\nabla\!\left(\frac{\delta\mathcal F}{\delta\rho}\right)\cdot v_t\,\rho_t\,\mathrm dx. \] 因而 \[ \operatorname{grad}_{W_2}\mathcal F =\nabla\!\left(\frac{\delta\mathcal F}{\delta\rho}\right). \]

证明. 在一阶变分公式中代入 \(\partial_t\rho=-\nabla\cdot(\rho v)\)，再分部积分即可。

作用量例子

平移. 若 \(v_t=a\)，则 \(\mathcal A=|a|^2=W_2^2(\mu_0,\mu_1)\)。

一般指数. 对 \(1<p<\infty\)，同样有 \[ W_p^p(\mu_0,\mu_1)=\inf\int_0^1\int|v_t|^p\,\mathrm d\mu_t\,\mathrm dt. \] 只有 \(p=2\) 产生来自内积的二次几何。
