18. Sinkhorn scaling

Sinkhorn scaling records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the optimal transport note.

Definition

Definition. Sinkhorn scaling alternately rescales rows and columns of a positive kernel matrix to match prescribed marginals.

Proposition

Proposition. For strictly positive kernels and positive marginals with equal total mass, Sinkhorn iterations converge to scaling vectors solving the entropic transport marginal constraints.

Proof

Proof. The iteration is coordinate ascent in the dual or alternating Bregman projection. Positivity and convexity prevent mass from escaping and force convergence to the unique scaling up to normalization.

Remark

Remark. Sinkhorn belongs in the computational interface of optimal transport: it is a numerical method, but its convergence is convex-dual structure.
