20. Empirical measures and Wasserstein convergence

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/talagrand-and-transport-inequalities/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/inference-for-transport-costs-maps-and-barycenters/" %}

When a distribution is observed through samples, its empirical measure is the canonical plug-in estimator in Wasserstein space. Consistency is simple, but rates depend sharply on dimension, moment assumptions, and the local structure of the underlying law.

Empirical measures

Definition. For i.i.d. \(X_1,\ldots,X_n\sim\mu\), \[ \widehat\mu_n=\frac1n\sum_{i=1}^n\delta_{X_i}. \] Its integral against \(f\) is the empirical average \(\widehat\mu_nf=n^{-1}\sum_i f(X_i)\).

Strong Wasserstein consistency. If \(\mu\in\mathcal P_p(\mathbb R^d)\), then \[ W_p(\widehat\mu_n,\mu)\longrightarrow0 \qquad\text{almost surely}. \]

Proof. Varadarajan's theorem, or the strong law on a countable convergence-determining class, gives \(\widehat\mu_n\Rightarrow\mu\) almost surely. The scalar strong law gives \[ \int|x|^p\,\mathrm d\widehat\mu_n(x) =\frac1n\sum_{i=1}^n|X_i|^p \longrightarrow\mathbb E|X_1|^p. \] The topology theorem on page 11 converts weak convergence plus moment convergence into \(W_p\)-convergence.

Dimension-dependent expected rates

Generic compact-support rates. Suppose \(\mu\) is supported in a bounded subset of \(\mathbb R^d\). Up to constants depending on \(d,p\) and the support diameter, \[ \mathbb E W_p^p(\widehat\mu_n,\mu) \lesssim \begin{cases} n^{-1/2},&d<2p,\\ n^{-1/2}\log(1+n),&d=2p,\\ n^{-p/d},&d>2p. \end{cases} \] Comparable bounds hold under sufficiently high finite moments, with additional tail terms.

Proof mechanism. Partition space into dyadic cubes. A transport plan first corrects mass discrepancies on coarse cubes, then recursively within each cube. The cost at scale \(2^{-j}\) is controlled by \(2^{-jp}\) times the total empirical mass fluctuation over the \(2^{jd}\) cells. Binomial fluctuations contribute roughly \(\min\{1,\sqrt{2^{jd}/n}\}\). Summing \[ \sum_{j\ge0}2^{-jp}\min\left\{1,\sqrt{\frac{2^{jd}}n}\right\} \] yields the three regimes. For unbounded support, apply the argument on dyadic annuli and control the remaining tail with moments.

These are distribution-free rates and need not be sharp for smooth low-dimensional laws. They expose the central obstruction: at fine scales there are exponentially many cells relative to the dimension.

Why the curse of dimensionality appears

Quantization lower bound. If \(\mu\) has a density bounded below on a set of positive \(d\)-dimensional volume, then every probability measure \(\nu_n\) supported on at most \(n\) points satisfies \[ W_p(\mu,\nu_n)\ge c\,n^{-1/d}. \]

Proof. Let the support points be \(z_1,\ldots,z_n\). For any transport to these points, each \(x\) must move at least \(\min_i|x-z_i|\), so \[ W_p^p(\mu,\nu_n)\ge\int\min_i|x-z_i|^p\,\mathrm d\mu(x). \] The union of \(n\) balls of radius \(r\) has volume at most \(n\omega_dr^d\). Choose \(r\asymp n^{-1/d}\) so these balls cover at most half of a fixed positive-volume region. On the uncovered part the distance is at least \(r\), giving the lower bound.

Since \(\widehat\mu_n\) has at most \(n\) support points, no estimator constrained to the raw empirical support can beat this geometric approximation barrier uniformly over full-dimensional distributions.

Discrete and low-dimensional exceptions

Finite support. If \(\mu\) is supported on a fixed finite set with pairwise distances bounded above and below, then \(W_p^p(\widehat\mu_n,\mu)\) is comparable to the \(\ell^1\) error of the empirical cell probabilities, which is \(O_{\mathbb P}(n^{-1/2})\). The effective dimension is the number of atoms, not the ambient Euclidean dimension.

One dimension. The identity \[ W_p^p(\widehat\mu_n,\mu) =\int_0^1|F_n^{-1}(u)-F^{-1}(u)|^p\,\mathrm du \] reduces the problem to quantile-process theory. If \(F\) has a positive smooth density on a compact interval, \(W_p(\widehat\mu_n,\mu)=O_{\mathbb P}(n^{-1/2})\), much faster than the worst-case bound for \(W_p\) obtained by taking a \(p\)-th root above.

Concentration around the mean

Bounded-support concentration. If \(\mu\) has support diameter \(D\), changing one observation changes \(W_p^p(\widehat\mu_n,\mu)\) by at most \(D^p/n\). Therefore McDiarmid's inequality gives \[ \mathbb P\left( W_p^p(\widehat\mu_n,\mu) -\mathbb EW_p^p(\widehat\mu_n,\mu)\ge t \right) \le\exp\left(-\frac{2nt^2}{D^{2p}}\right). \]

Proof. If \(X_i\) is replaced by \(X_i'\), couple the two empirical measures by matching all unchanged observations and moving only mass \(1/n\) from \(X_i\) to \(X_i'\). Their \(W_p^p\) distance is at most \(D^p/n\). The triangle inequality for optimal values, or direct replacement in a coupling to \(\mu\), gives the same bounded difference for the statistic. Apply McDiarmid's inequality.

Two-sample empirical transport

For independent samples with laws \(\mu,\nu\), \[ |W_p(\widehat\mu_n,\widehat\nu_m)-W_p(\mu,\nu)| \le W_p(\widehat\mu_n,\mu)+W_p(\widehat\nu_m,\nu). \] Thus one-sample rates immediately imply consistency and deviation bounds away from the null. Under \(\mu=\nu\), however, the first-order derivative of the distance may vanish and the limiting scale can change. Page 21 treats this nonregular behavior.

Tail assumptions are structural

A small empirical error in weak topology can hide a large transport error in the tails. Finite \(p\)-th moment is sufficient for consistency but generally not for a clean finite-sample rate. Polynomial moments yield polynomial tail terms, exponential moments yield exponential concentration, and compact support removes tail error entirely. Any statistical claim about empirical Wasserstein distance must therefore state both dimension and tail assumptions.

20. Mesures empiriques et convergence de Wasserstein

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Retour" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Page précédente" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/talagrand-and-transport-inequalities/" next_label="Page suivante" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/inference-for-transport-costs-maps-and-barycenters/" %}

Lorsqu'une loi est observée par échantillonnage, sa mesure empirique est l'estimateur plug-in canonique dans l'espace de Wasserstein. La cohérence est simple, mais les vitesses dépendent fortement de la dimension, des moments et de la structure locale de la loi.

Mesures empiriques

Définition. Pour \(X_1,\ldots,X_n\) i.i.d. de loi \(\mu\), \[ \widehat\mu_n=\frac1n\sum_{i=1}^n\delta_{X_i}. \]

Cohérence forte. Si \(\mu\in\mathcal P_p(\mathbb R^d)\), \[ W_p(\widehat\mu_n,\mu)\to0 \quad\text{presque sûrement}. \]

Preuve. Le théorème de Varadarajan donne \(\widehat\mu_n\Rightarrow\mu\) presque sûrement. La loi forte donne \[ \int|x|^p\,\mathrm d\widehat\mu_n=\frac1n\sum_i|X_i|^p\to\mathbb E|X_1|^p. \] Le critère topologique de la page 11 conclut.

Vitesses dépendant de la dimension

Bornes génériques à support compact. \[ \mathbb EW_p^p(\widehat\mu_n,\mu)\lesssim \begin{cases} n^{-1/2},&d<2p,\\ n^{-1/2}\log(1+n),&d=2p,\\ n^{-p/d},&d>2p. \end{cases} \]

Mécanisme de preuve. Partitionnons l'espace en cubes dyadiques. On corrige les écarts de masse des grandes échelles vers les petites. À l'échelle \(2^{-j}\), le coût est contrôlé par \(2^{-jp}\) fois la fluctuation totale sur \(2^{jd}\) cellules, de taille \(\min\{1,\sqrt{2^{jd}/n}\}\). La somme \[ \sum_j2^{-jp}\min\left\{1,\sqrt{\frac{2^{jd}}n}\right\} \] produit les trois régimes. Pour un support non borné, on travaille sur des couronnes et contrôle la queue par les moments.

Origine de la malédiction de la dimension

Borne de quantification. Si \(\mu\) a une densité minorée sur un ensemble de volume positif, toute mesure \(\nu_n\) portée par au plus \(n\) points vérifie \[ W_p(\mu,\nu_n)\ge c\,n^{-1/d}. \]

Preuve. Toute masse doit parcourir au moins \(\min_i|x-z_i|\). L'union de \(n\) boules de rayon \(r\) a volume au plus \(n\omega_dr^d\). Pour \(r\asymp n^{-1/d}\), une partie de volume fixé reste hors de ces boules et se déplace d'au moins \(r\).

Exceptions discrètes et unidimensionnelles

Support fini. Sur un nombre fixé d'atomes séparés, \(W_p^p\) est comparable à l'erreur \(\ell^1\) des fréquences empiriques, d'ordre \(n^{-1/2}\). La dimension pertinente est le nombre d'atomes.

Dimension un. \[ W_p^p(\widehat\mu_n,\mu) =\int_0^1|F_n^{-1}-F^{-1}|^p\,\mathrm du. \] Pour une densité positive lisse sur un intervalle compact, \(W_p=O_{\mathbb P}(n^{-1/2})\).

Concentration autour de la moyenne

Support borné. Si le diamètre du support est \(D\), \[ \mathbb P\left(W_p^p-\mathbb EW_p^p\ge t\right) \le\exp\left(-\frac{2nt^2}{D^{2p}}\right). \]

Preuve. Le remplacement d'une observation déplace seulement une masse \(1/n\) sur une distance au plus \(D\), donc change la statistique d'au plus \(D^p/n\). L'inégalité de McDiarmid conclut.

Transport empirique à deux échantillons

Pour deux échantillons indépendants, \[ |W_p(\widehat\mu_n,\widehat\nu_m)-W_p(\mu,\nu)| \le W_p(\widehat\mu_n,\mu)+W_p(\widehat\nu_m,\nu). \] Hors de l'hypothèse nulle, les vitesses à un échantillon suffisent. Sous \(\mu=\nu\), la dérivée du premier ordre peut s'annuler et l'échelle limite change; la page suivante étudie ce phénomène.

Rôle structurel des queues

Un faible écart étroit peut cacher un grand coût de transport dans les queues. Le moment d'ordre \(p\) suffit à la cohérence, mais pas à une vitesse non asymptotique simple. Toute affirmation statistique doit préciser simultanément dimension et hypothèses de queue.

20. 经验测度与瓦瑟斯坦收敛

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="返回" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="上一页" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/talagrand-and-transport-inequalities/" next_label="下一页" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/inference-for-transport-costs-maps-and-barycenters/" %}

当分布通过样本观测时，经验测度是瓦瑟斯坦空间中的标准代入估计量。相合性很直接，但收敛速率尖锐地依赖维数、矩条件以及真实分布的局部结构。

经验测度

定义. 对独立同分布样本 \(X_1,\ldots,X_n\sim\mu\)，定义 \[ \widehat\mu_n=\frac1n\sum_{i=1}^n\delta_{X_i}. \]

强瓦瑟斯坦相合性. 若 \(\mu\in\mathcal P_p(\mathbb R^d)\)，则 \[ W_p(\widehat\mu_n,\mu)\longrightarrow0 \qquad\text{几乎必然}. \]

证明. 瓦拉达拉金定理，或在一个可数的收敛决定函数族上应用强大数定律，给出 \(\widehat\mu_n\Rightarrow\mu\) 几乎必然。标量强大数定律又给出 \[ \int|x|^p\,\mathrm d\widehat\mu_n =\frac1n\sum_i|X_i|^p\to\mathbb E|X_1|^p. \] 第 11 页的拓扑定理把弱收敛与矩收敛转化为 \(W_p\) 收敛。

依赖维数的期望速率

紧支撑下的一般速率. 若 \(\mu\) 支撑在 \(\mathbb R^d\) 的有界集合中，则 \[ \mathbb EW_p^p(\widehat\mu_n,\mu) \lesssim \begin{cases} n^{-1/2},&d<2p,\\ n^{-1/2}\log(1+n),&d=2p,\\ n^{-p/d},&d>2p. \end{cases} \] 在足够高阶有限矩条件下也有类似结果，但会出现额外尾项。

证明机制. 把空间划分为二进立方体。传输计划先纠正粗尺度单元的质量差，再逐层进入细尺度。在尺度 \(2^{-j}\)，代价由 \(2^{-jp}\) 乘以 \(2^{jd}\) 个单元上的总经验质量波动控制；二项波动量级约为 \(\min\{1,\sqrt{2^{jd}/n}\}\)。求和 \[ \sum_{j\ge0}2^{-jp}\min\left\{1,\sqrt{\frac{2^{jd}}n}\right\} \] 得到三个区域。无界支撑时在二进环带上重复论证，并用矩控制尾部。

维数灾难的几何来源

量化下界. 若 \(\mu\) 在某个正 \(d\) 维体积集合上的密度有正下界，则每个至多支撑于 \(n\) 点的概率测度 \(\nu_n\) 都满足 \[ W_p(\mu,\nu_n)\ge c\,n^{-1/d}. \]

证明. 若支撑点为 \(z_1,\ldots,z_n\)，则每个 \(x\) 至少移动 \(\min_i|x-z_i|\)。\(n\) 个半径 \(r\) 的球总体积不超过 \(n\omega_dr^d\)。取 \(r\asymp n^{-1/d}\)，可使一个固定正体积部分未被覆盖；该部分至少移动距离 \(r\)，从而得到下界。

离散与低维例外

有限支撑. 若 \(\mu\) 支撑在固定有限集合上且点间距离有一致上下界，则 \(W_p^p(\widehat\mu_n,\mu)\) 与经验频率的 \(\ell^1\) 误差同阶，量级为 \(O_{\mathbb P}(n^{-1/2})\)。有效维数是原子数，而不是环境欧几里得维数。

一维. \[ W_p^p(\widehat\mu_n,\mu) =\int_0^1|F_n^{-1}(u)-F^{-1}(u)|^p\,\mathrm du. \] 若 \(F\) 在紧区间上具有光滑正密度，则 \(W_p(\widehat\mu_n,\mu)=O_{\mathbb P}(n^{-1/2})\)，显著快于一般最坏情形。

围绕期望的集中

有界支撑集中界. 若支撑直径为 \(D\)，则 \[ \mathbb P\left( W_p^p(\widehat\mu_n,\mu)-\mathbb EW_p^p(\widehat\mu_n,\mu)\ge t \right) \le\exp\left(-\frac{2nt^2}{D^{2p}}\right). \]

证明. 替换一个观测时，只需把质量 \(1/n\) 从旧观测移到新观测，距离至多为 \(D\)，故统计量变化至多 \(D^p/n\)。应用麦克迪阿米德不等式即可。

双样本经验传输

对来自 \(\mu,\nu\) 的独立样本， \[ |W_p(\widehat\mu_n,\widehat\nu_m)-W_p(\mu,\nu)| \le W_p(\widehat\mu_n,\mu)+W_p(\widehat\nu_m,\nu). \] 因此在备择假设下，单样本速率立即给出相合性与偏差界。然而在 \(\mu=\nu\) 的零假设下，距离的一阶导数可能消失，极限尺度会改变。第 21 页讨论这种非正则行为。

尾部假设的结构作用

弱拓扑中的小误差可能在尾部隐藏很大传输代价。有限 \(p\) 阶矩足以保证相合性，却通常不足以给出简洁有限样本速率。多项式矩产生多项式尾项，指数矩产生指数集中，紧支撑则完全消除尾误差。因此经验瓦瑟斯坦距离的统计结论必须同时陈述维数与尾部条件。
