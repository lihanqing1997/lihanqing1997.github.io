5. c-transforms and c-convexity

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/kantorovich-duality/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/cyclical-monotonicity/" %}

c-transforms and c-convexity records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the optimal transport note.

Definition

Definition. The \(c\)-transform of \(\varphi\) is \(\varphi^c(y)=\inf_x\{c(x,y)-\varphi(x)\}\). A function is \(c\)-convex if it is a \(c\)-transform of another function.

Proposition

Proposition. Dual optimizers can be chosen in \(c\)-conjugate form, with \(\psi=\varphi^c\) and \(\varphi=\psi^c\) under suitable hypotheses.

Proof

Proof. Given any feasible pair, replacing one potential by its \(c\)-transform preserves feasibility and improves or preserves the dual objective. Iterating yields conjugate potentials.

Remark

Remark. The \(c\)-transform generalizes Legendre-Fenchel conjugacy and is the language of optimal maps for general costs.
