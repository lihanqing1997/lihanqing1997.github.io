5. \(c\)-convexity and cyclical monotonicity

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/kantorovich-duality/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/discrete-transport-and-assignment-problems/" %}

Duality says that optimal mass moves only where \(\varphi(x)+\psi(y)=c(x,y)\). The \(c\)-transform organizes all such touching pairs, while cyclical monotonicity describes them without mentioning the potentials. Together they are the geometric language of optimality.

The \(c\)-transform

Definition. For \(\varphi:X\to[-\infty,\infty]\), define its \(c\)-transform on \(Y\) by \[ \varphi^c(y)=\inf_{x\in X}\{c(x,y)-\varphi(x)\}. \] For \(\psi:Y\to[-\infty,\infty]\), define \(\psi^c(x)=\inf_y\{c(x,y)-\psi(y)\}\). A function is \(c\)-concave if it equals the \(c\)-transform of some function. Some authors use the sign-reversed term \(c\)-convex; the present note follows the dual inequality \(\varphi+\psi\le c\).

Proposition. The \(c\)-transform reverses order. Moreover, \[ \varphi\le\varphi^{cc}, \qquad \varphi^{ccc}=\varphi^c. \] A function \(\varphi\) is \(c\)-concave exactly when \(\varphi=\varphi^{cc}\), up to the usual properness qualifications.

Proof. If \(\varphi_1\le\varphi_2\), then \(c-\varphi_1\ge c-\varphi_2\), so taking infima gives \(\varphi_1^c\ge\varphi_2^c\). For every \(x,y\), \[ \varphi^c(y)\le c(x,y)-\varphi(x), \] hence \(\varphi(x)\le c(x,y)-\varphi^c(y)\). Taking the infimum over \(y\) gives \(\varphi\le\varphi^{cc}\). Apply the order reversal to this inequality to obtain \(\varphi^c\ge\varphi^{ccc}\); applying the double-transform inequality to \(\varphi^c\) gives the reverse inequality. If \(\varphi=\psi^c\), then \(\varphi^{cc}=\psi^{ccc}=\psi^c=\varphi\). The converse is immediate from \(\varphi=(\varphi^c)^c\).

Reduction of the dual problem

Proposition. If \((\varphi,\psi)\) is dual feasible, then \(\psi\le\varphi^c\). Replacing \(\psi\) by \(\varphi^c\), and then \(\varphi\) by \(\varphi^{cc}\), preserves feasibility and cannot decrease the dual objective. Consequently the dual problem may be written \[ K_c(\mu,\nu) =\sup_{\varphi=\varphi^{cc}} \left\{\int\varphi\,\mathrm d\mu+\int\varphi^c\,\mathrm d\nu\right\}. \]

Proof. Feasibility gives \(\psi(y)\le c(x,y)-\varphi(x)\) for every \(x\); taking the infimum yields \(\psi\le\varphi^c\). By definition, \(\varphi(x)+\varphi^c(y)\le c(x,y)\), so the replacement is feasible. The same argument on the other side replaces \(\varphi\) by \((\varphi^c)^c=\varphi^{cc}\). Both replacements increase only one potential, hence do not lower the objective.

The \(c\)-superdifferential

Definition. For a \(c\)-concave \(\varphi\), its \(c\)-superdifferential is \[ \partial^c\varphi =\{(x,y):\varphi(x)+\varphi^c(y)=c(x,y)\}. \] The fiber \(\partial^c\varphi(x)=\{y:(x,y)\in\partial^c\varphi\}\) contains the destinations at which the \(c\)-support associated with \(y\) touches \(\varphi\) at \(x\).

Corollary. If \(\varphi\) is an optimal Kantorovich potential and \(\pi\) is an optimal plan, then \[ \operatorname{supp}\pi\subset\partial^c\varphi \] whenever the potentials and cost are continuous; in general the inclusion holds \(\pi\)-almost surely.

Cyclical monotonicity

Definition. A set \(\Gamma\subset X\times Y\) is \(c\)-cyclically monotone if for every \(N\ge1\), every \((x_i,y_i)\in\Gamma\), and every permutation \(\sigma\) of \(\{1,\ldots,N\}\), \[ \sum_{i=1}^N c(x_i,y_i) \le \sum_{i=1}^N c(x_i,y_{\sigma(i)}). \] It is enough to test cyclic permutations after decomposing a permutation into disjoint cycles.

Proposition. Every \(c\)-superdifferential \(\partial^c\varphi\) is \(c\)-cyclically monotone.

Proof. If \((x_i,y_i)\in\partial^c\varphi\), then \[ c(x_i,y_i)=\varphi(x_i)+\varphi^c(y_i). \] Feasibility gives \(\varphi(x_i)+\varphi^c(y_{\sigma(i)})\le c(x_i,y_{\sigma(i)})\). Sum over \(i\). Since permutation does not change \(\sum_i\varphi^c(y_i)\), the desired inequality follows.

Optimal support theorem. Under the standard lower-semicontinuity and finite-cost hypotheses, every optimal plan is concentrated on a \(c\)-cyclically monotone Borel set. Conversely, if a finite-cost plan is concentrated on a \(c\)-cyclically monotone set and that set admits an integrable \(c\)-potential, then the plan is optimal.

Proof. For the forward direction, take optimal dual potentials. Complementary slackness places the plan on \(\partial^c\varphi\), which is cyclically monotone. For the converse, the \(c\)-version of Rockafellar's construction produces a \(c\)-concave \(\varphi\) with \(\Gamma\subset\partial^c\varphi\). Thus \(\varphi+\varphi^c=c\) on the support. Integrating gives equality of primal and dual values, so weak duality proves optimality.

Rockafellar's construction

Fix \((x_0,y_0)\in\Gamma\). A potential containing a \(c\)-cyclically monotone set in its \(c\)-superdifferential can be built as \[ \varphi(x)=\inf_{\substack{N\ge1\\(x_i,y_i)\in\Gamma}} \left[ c(x,y_N)-c(x_N,y_N) +\sum_{i=0}^{N-1}\{c(x_{i+1},y_i)-c(x_i,y_i)\} \right]. \] Cyclical monotonicity prevents contradictory negative cycles. Comparing a chain ending at \(x\) with the same chain extended by \((x,y)\in\Gamma\) yields \(\varphi(z)-\varphi(x)\le c(z,y)-c(x,y)\), which is precisely the supporting inequality defining \((x,y)\in\partial^c\varphi\).

Quadratic cost and ordinary convexity

Proposition. For \(c(x,y)=\tfrac12|x-y|^2\) on \(\mathbb R^d\), let \[ u(x)=\tfrac12|x|^2-\varphi(x). \] Then \(\varphi\) is \(c\)-concave exactly when \(u\) is convex, and \[ y\in\partial^c\varphi(x) \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad y\in\partial u(x). \]

Proof. Expand \[ \tfrac12|x-y|^2-\varphi(x) =u(x)-x\cdot y+\tfrac12|y|^2. \] The \(c\)-transform is therefore a Legendre--Fenchel transform up to quadratic terms. The touching equality becomes \[ u(z)\ge u(x)+y\cdot(z-x)\qquad\text{for every \(z\)}, \] which is exactly \(y\in\partial u(x)\).

Two-point condition. For quadratic cost, the two-cycle inequality is \[ |x_1-y_1|^2+|x_2-y_2|^2 \le |x_1-y_2|^2+|x_2-y_1|^2, \] which simplifies to \[ (x_1-x_2)\cdot(y_1-y_2)\ge0. \] In one dimension this forbids crossings: \(x_1<x_2\) implies \(y_1\le y_2\). In higher dimensions, all cycle lengths are needed to characterize gradients of convex functions.

5. \(c\)-convexité et monotonie cyclique

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Retour" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Page précédente" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/kantorovich-duality/" next_label="Page suivante" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/discrete-transport-and-assignment-problems/" %}

La dualité impose que la masse optimale se déplace seulement là où \(\varphi(x)+\psi(y)=c(x,y)\). La \(c\)-transformée organise ces points de contact, tandis que la monotonie cyclique les décrit sans faire intervenir les potentiels. Ces deux notions constituent le langage géométrique de l'optimalité.

La \(c\)-transformée

Définition. Pour \(\varphi:X\to[-\infty,\infty]\), sa \(c\)-transformée sur \(Y\) est \[ \varphi^c(y)=\inf_{x\in X}\{c(x,y)-\varphi(x)\}. \] On définit de même \(\psi^c(x)=\inf_y\{c(x,y)-\psi(y)\}\). Une fonction est \(c\)-concave si elle est la \(c\)-transformée d'une autre fonction. Le terme \(c\)-convexe est aussi employé avec une convention de signe opposée.

Proposition. La \(c\)-transformée renverse l'ordre et \[ \varphi\le\varphi^{cc}, \qquad \varphi^{ccc}=\varphi^c. \] Une fonction propre est \(c\)-concave exactement lorsqu'elle vérifie \(\varphi=\varphi^{cc}\).

Preuve. Si \(\varphi_1\le\varphi_2\), alors \(c-\varphi_1\ge c-\varphi_2\), donc \(\varphi_1^c\ge\varphi_2^c\). Pour tout \(x,y\), \[ \varphi^c(y)\le c(x,y)-\varphi(x), \] donc \(\varphi(x)\le c(x,y)-\varphi^c(y)\). L'infimum en \(y\) donne \(\varphi\le\varphi^{cc}\). Le renversement de l'ordre et la même inégalité appliquée à \(\varphi^c\) donnent \(\varphi^{ccc}=\varphi^c\). Enfin, si \(\varphi=\psi^c\), alors \(\varphi^{cc}=\psi^{ccc}=\psi^c=\varphi\), et la réciproque est immédiate.

Réduction du problème dual

Proposition. Si \((\varphi,\psi)\) est admissible, alors \(\psi\le\varphi^c\). Remplacer \(\psi\) par \(\varphi^c\), puis \(\varphi\) par \(\varphi^{cc}\), conserve l'admissibilité et ne diminue pas l'objectif. Ainsi \[ K_c(\mu,\nu) =\sup_{\varphi=\varphi^{cc}} \left\{\int\varphi\,\mathrm d\mu+\int\varphi^c\,\mathrm d\nu\right\}. \]

Preuve. L'admissibilité donne \(\psi(y)\le c(x,y)-\varphi(x)\) pour tout \(x\), puis \(\psi\le\varphi^c\) en prenant l'infimum. Par définition, \(\varphi(x)+\varphi^c(y)\le c(x,y)\). Le même raisonnement de l'autre côté remplace \(\varphi\) par \(\varphi^{cc}\). Chaque opération augmente un seul potentiel.

Surdifférentiel \(c\)-convexe

Définition. Pour une fonction \(c\)-concave \(\varphi\), son surdifférentiel \(c\)-convexe est \[ \partial^c\varphi =\{(x,y):\varphi(x)+\varphi^c(y)=c(x,y)\}. \] La fibre \(\partial^c\varphi(x)\) rassemble les destinations dont le \(c\)-support touche \(\varphi\) en \(x\).

Corollaire. Si \(\varphi\) est un potentiel optimal et \(\pi\) un plan optimal, alors \(\operatorname{supp}\pi\subset\partial^c\varphi\) dans le cadre continu, et l'inclusion vaut \(\pi\)-presque sûrement en général.

Monotonie cyclique

Définition. Un ensemble \(\Gamma\subset X\times Y\) est \(c\)-cycliquement monotone si, pour tous \((x_i,y_i)\in\Gamma\) et toute permutation \(\sigma\), \[ \sum_{i=1}^N c(x_i,y_i) \le \sum_{i=1}^N c(x_i,y_{\sigma(i)}). \] Il suffit de vérifier les permutations cycliques, toute permutation se décomposant en cycles disjoints.

Proposition. Tout surdifférentiel \(\partial^c\varphi\) est \(c\)-cycliquement monotone.

Preuve. Pour \((x_i,y_i)\in\partial^c\varphi\), \[ c(x_i,y_i)=\varphi(x_i)+\varphi^c(y_i). \] L'admissibilité donne \(\varphi(x_i)+\varphi^c(y_{\sigma(i)})\le c(x_i,y_{\sigma(i)})\). La somme et l'invariance de \(\sum_i\varphi^c(y_i)\) par permutation concluent.

Théorème du support optimal. Sous les hypothèses usuelles de semi-continuité et de coût fini, tout plan optimal est concentré sur un ensemble borélien \(c\)-cycliquement monotone. Réciproquement, un plan de coût fini concentré sur un tel ensemble est optimal dès que cet ensemble admet un potentiel \(c\)-convexe intégrable.

Preuve. Dans le sens direct, la complémentarité place le plan dans \(\partial^c\varphi\), qui est cycliquement monotone. Réciproquement, la construction de Rockafellar adaptée au coût produit une fonction \(c\)-concave \(\varphi\) telle que \(\Gamma\subset\partial^c\varphi\). L'égalité \(\varphi+\varphi^c=c\) sur le support donne l'égalité des valeurs primale et duale après intégration; la dualité faible conclut.

Construction de Rockafellar

Fixons \((x_0,y_0)\in\Gamma\). On peut construire un potentiel par \[ \varphi(x)=\inf_{\substack{N\ge1\\(x_i,y_i)\in\Gamma}} \left[ c(x,y_N)-c(x_N,y_N) +\sum_{i=0}^{N-1}\{c(x_{i+1},y_i)-c(x_i,y_i)\} \right]. \] La monotonie cyclique exclut les cycles négatifs contradictoires. Prolonger une chaîne par \((x,y)\in\Gamma\) fournit \(\varphi(z)-\varphi(x)\le c(z,y)-c(x,y)\), qui est exactement l'inégalité de support définissant \((x,y)\in\partial^c\varphi\).

Coût quadratique et convexité ordinaire

Proposition. Pour \(c(x,y)=\tfrac12|x-y|^2\) sur \(\mathbb R^d\), posons \[ u(x)=\tfrac12|x|^2-\varphi(x). \] Alors \(\varphi\) est \(c\)-concave exactement lorsque \(u\) est convexe, et \[ y\in\partial^c\varphi(x) \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad y\in\partial u(x). \]

Preuve. Le développement \[ \tfrac12|x-y|^2-\varphi(x) =u(x)-x\cdot y+\tfrac12|y|^2 \] identifie la \(c\)-transformée à une transformée de Legendre--Fenchel à des termes quadratiques près. L'égalité de contact équivaut à \[ u(z)\ge u(x)+y\cdot(z-x)\quad\text{pour tout \(z\)}, \] donc à \(y\in\partial u(x)\).

Condition à deux points. Pour le coût quadratique, l'inégalité à deux cycles se réduit à \[ (x_1-x_2)\cdot(y_1-y_2)\ge0. \] En dimension un, elle interdit les croisements : \(x_1<x_2\) implique \(y_1\le y_2\). En dimension supérieure, tous les cycles sont nécessaires pour caractériser les gradients de fonctions convexes.

5. \(c\)-凸性与循环单调性

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="返回" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="上一页" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/kantorovich-duality/" next_label="下一页" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/discrete-transport-and-assignment-problems/" %}

对偶理论说明，最优质量只沿着满足 \(\varphi(x)+\psi(y)=c(x,y)\) 的位置移动。\(c\)-变换组织这些接触点，循环单调性则在不显式提及势函数的情况下描述它们。二者共同构成最优性的几何语言。

\(c\)-变换

定义. 对 \(\varphi:X\to[-\infty,\infty]\)，定义其在 \(Y\) 上的 \(c\)-变换 \[ \varphi^c(y)=\inf_{x\in X}\{c(x,y)-\varphi(x)\}. \] 类似地，\(\psi^c(x)=\inf_y\{c(x,y)-\psi(y)\}\)。若某函数是另一函数的 \(c\)-变换，则称其为 \(c\)-凹函数。另一些文献采用相反符号并称之为 \(c\)-凸函数；本笔记遵循 \(\varphi+\psi\le c\) 的对偶约定。

命题. \(c\)-变换反转函数的序，并且 \[ \varphi\le\varphi^{cc}, \qquad \varphi^{ccc}=\varphi^c. \] 在通常的真函数条件下，\(\varphi\) 为 \(c\)-凹函数当且仅当 \(\varphi=\varphi^{cc}\)。

证明. 若 \(\varphi_1\le\varphi_2\)，则 \(c-\varphi_1\ge c-\varphi_2\)，取下确界得 \(\varphi_1^c\ge\varphi_2^c\)。对每个 \(x,y\)， \[ \varphi^c(y)\le c(x,y)-\varphi(x), \] 故 \(\varphi(x)\le c(x,y)-\varphi^c(y)\)。对 \(y\) 取下确界即得 \(\varphi\le\varphi^{cc}\)。利用序反转并对 \(\varphi^c\) 再用双重变换不等式，得到 \(\varphi^{ccc}=\varphi^c\)。若 \(\varphi=\psi^c\)，则 \(\varphi^{cc}=\psi^{ccc}=\psi^c=\varphi\)；反向显然。

对偶问题的约化

命题. 若 \((\varphi,\psi)\) 对偶可行，则 \(\psi\le\varphi^c\)。先以 \(\varphi^c\) 替代 \(\psi\)，再以 \(\varphi^{cc}\) 替代 \(\varphi\)，可行性保持且目标值不减。因此 \[ K_c(\mu,\nu) =\sup_{\varphi=\varphi^{cc}} \left\{\int\varphi\,\mathrm d\mu+\int\varphi^c\,\mathrm d\nu\right\}. \]

证明. 可行性给出对每个 \(x\)，\(\psi(y)\le c(x,y)-\varphi(x)\)；取下确界即得 \(\psi\le\varphi^c\)。由定义，\(\varphi(x)+\varphi^c(y)\le c(x,y)\)，故替换后仍可行。对另一侧重复同一论证即把 \(\varphi\) 替换为 \(\varphi^{cc}\)。两次操作都只增大一个势。

\(c\)-上微分

定义. 对 \(c\)-凹函数 \(\varphi\)，定义其 \(c\)-上微分 \[ \partial^c\varphi =\{(x,y):\varphi(x)+\varphi^c(y)=c(x,y)\}. \] 纤维 \(\partial^c\varphi(x)\) 包含所有使相应 \(c\)-支撑函数在 \(x\) 处接触 \(\varphi\) 的目的地。

推论. 若 \(\varphi\) 是最优康托罗维奇势，\(\pi\) 是最优计划，则在连续情形 \(\operatorname{supp}\pi\subset\partial^c\varphi\)；一般情形下该包含关系在 \(\pi\)-几乎处处意义成立。

循环单调性

定义. 若对任意 \((x_i,y_i)\in\Gamma\) 及任意置换 \(\sigma\)，都有 \[ \sum_{i=1}^N c(x_i,y_i) \le \sum_{i=1}^N c(x_i,y_{\sigma(i)}), \] 则称 \(\Gamma\subset X\times Y\) 为 \(c\)-循环单调集。由于每个置换都可分解为不交循环，只需检验循环置换。

命题. 每个 \(c\)-上微分 \(\partial^c\varphi\) 都是 \(c\)-循环单调的。

证明. 若 \((x_i,y_i)\in\partial^c\varphi\)，则 \[ c(x_i,y_i)=\varphi(x_i)+\varphi^c(y_i). \] 可行性给出 \(\varphi(x_i)+\varphi^c(y_{\sigma(i)})\le c(x_i,y_{\sigma(i)})\)。对 \(i\) 求和，并注意置换不改变 \(\sum_i\varphi^c(y_i)\)，即得结论。

最优支撑定理. 在标准的下半连续与有限代价条件下，每个最优计划都集中于某个博雷尔 \(c\)-循环单调集。反之，若有限代价计划集中于一个 \(c\)-循环单调集，且该集合存在可积的 \(c\)-势，则此计划最优。

证明. 正向由最优对偶势与互补松弛得到：计划集中于 \(\partial^c\varphi\)，而后者循环单调。反向使用罗卡费拉构造的 \(c\)-版本，得到 \(c\)-凹函数 \(\varphi\) 使 \(\Gamma\subset\partial^c\varphi\)。于是支撑上 \(\varphi+\varphi^c=c\)，积分后原值与对偶值相等，弱对偶说明计划最优。

罗卡费拉构造

固定 \((x_0,y_0)\in\Gamma\)，可以构造 \[ \varphi(x)=\inf_{\substack{N\ge1\\(x_i,y_i)\in\Gamma}} \left[ c(x,y_N)-c(x_N,y_N) +\sum_{i=0}^{N-1}\{c(x_{i+1},y_i)-c(x_i,y_i)\} \right]. \] 循环单调性排除了相互矛盾的负环。把链条延长一个 \((x,y)\in\Gamma\)，可得 \(\varphi(z)-\varphi(x)\le c(z,y)-c(x,y)\)，这正是 \((x,y)\in\partial^c\varphi\) 的支撑不等式。

二次代价与普通凸性

命题. 对 \(\mathbb R^d\) 上的 \(c(x,y)=\tfrac12|x-y|^2\)，令 \[ u(x)=\tfrac12|x|^2-\varphi(x). \] 则 \(\varphi\) 为 \(c\)-凹函数当且仅当 \(u\) 为凸函数，并且 \[ y\in\partial^c\varphi(x) \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad y\in\partial u(x). \]

证明. 展开 \[ \tfrac12|x-y|^2-\varphi(x) =u(x)-x\cdot y+\tfrac12|y|^2. \] 因此 \(c\)-变换在相差二次项后就是勒让德--芬歇尔变换。接触等式等价于 \[ u(z)\ge u(x)+y\cdot(z-x)\qquad\text{对每个 \(z\) 成立}, \] 也就是 \(y\in\partial u(x)\)。

两点条件. 对二次代价，两点循环不等式化简为 \[ (x_1-x_2)\cdot(y_1-y_2)\ge0. \] 在一维中，这排除了交叉：\(x_1<x_2\) 蕴含 \(y_1\le y_2\)。在高维中，要刻画凸函数的梯度，则必须使用所有长度的循环。
