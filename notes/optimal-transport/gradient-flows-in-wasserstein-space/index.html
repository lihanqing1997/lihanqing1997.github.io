Gradient flows in Wasserstein space records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the optimal transport note.

Definition

Definition. A Wasserstein gradient flow is a steepest-descent curve for a functional on probability measures, using Wasserstein distance as the underlying metric.

Proposition

Proposition. The Fokker-Planck equation is the Wasserstein gradient flow of relative entropy under suitable convexity and regularity assumptions.

Proof

Proof. Compute the first variation of entropy, identify the velocity field as minus its Wasserstein gradient, and insert it into the continuity equation.

Remark

Remark. Wasserstein gradient flows connect diffusion PDE, entropy, convexity, and geometry of probability measures.
