12. Continuity equation

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/benamou-brenier-formula/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/gradient-flows-in-wasserstein-space/" %}

Continuity equation records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the optimal transport note.

Definition

Definition. The continuity equation \(\partial_t\mu_t+\nabla\cdot(v_t\mu_t)=0\) expresses conservation of mass under a velocity field.

Proposition

Proposition. Weak solutions satisfy \(\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}t}\int \phi\,\mathrm{d}\mu_t=\int \nabla\phi\cdot v_t\,\mathrm{d}\mu_t\) for smooth test functions \(\phi\).

Proof

Proof. Formally multiply the PDE by \(\phi\), integrate in space, and integrate by parts. The weak form is then taken as the definition for measure-valued curves.

Remark

Remark. The continuity equation is the tangent-vector language of Wasserstein space.
