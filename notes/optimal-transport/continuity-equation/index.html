13. Absolutely continuous curves and the continuity equation

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/displacement-interpolation/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/benamou-brenier-formula/" %}

A curve of measures has no pointwise derivative in the usual vector-space sense. Its infinitesimal motion is instead described by a velocity field transporting mass through the continuity equation. Metric absolute continuity and this Eulerian description are equivalent.

The distributional continuity equation

Definition. A narrowly continuous curve \(t\mapsto\mu_t\in\mathcal P(\mathbb R^d)\) and a Borel velocity field \(v_t(x)\) solve \[ \partial_t\mu_t+\nabla\cdot(v_t\mu_t)=0 \] on \((0,T)\) if, for every \(\zeta\in C_c^\infty((0,T)\times\mathbb R^d)\), \[ \int_0^T\int_{\mathbb R^d} \{\partial_t\zeta(t,x)+\nabla\zeta(t,x)\cdot v_t(x)\} \,\mathrm d\mu_t(x)\,\mathrm dt=0. \]

Equivalently, for every \(\phi\in C_c^\infty(\mathbb R^d)\), the map \(t\mapsto\int\phi\,\mathrm d\mu_t\) is absolutely continuous and \[ \frac{\mathrm d}{\mathrm dt}\int\phi\,\mathrm d\mu_t =\int\nabla\phi(x)\cdot v_t(x)\,\mathrm d\mu_t(x) \] for almost every \(t\).

Flows of particles

Proposition. Suppose \(v_t\) is smooth and \(X_t(x)\) solves \[ \frac{\mathrm d}{\mathrm dt}X_t(x)=v_t(X_t(x)),\qquad X_0(x)=x. \] Then \(\mu_t=X_{t\#}\mu_0\) solves the continuity equation.

Proof. For a smooth compactly supported \(\phi\), \[ \frac{\mathrm d}{\mathrm dt}\int\phi\,\mathrm d\mu_t =\frac{\mathrm d}{\mathrm dt}\int\phi(X_t(x))\,\mathrm d\mu_0(x) =\int\nabla\phi(X_t(x))\cdot v_t(X_t(x))\,\mathrm d\mu_0(x). \] Pushing forward the last integral gives \(\int\nabla\phi\cdot v_t\,\mathrm d\mu_t\), which is the weak equation.

Metric absolute continuity

Definition. A curve \(\mu:[0,T]\to(\mathcal P_p(\mathbb R^d),W_p)\) belongs to \(AC^p\) if there exists \(m\in L^p(0,T)\) such that \[ W_p(\mu_s,\mu_t)\le\int_s^t m(r)\,\mathrm dr \] whenever \(s\le t\). Its metric derivative is \[ |\dot\mu_t|=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{W_p(\mu_{t+h},\mu_t)}{|h|}, \] which exists for almost every \(t\), belongs to \(L^p\), and is the smallest admissible \(m\).

Velocity characterization

Eulerian characterization. Let \(1<p<\infty\). A curve \(\mu_t\) belongs to \(AC^p([0,T];\mathcal P_p(\mathbb R^d))\) if and only if there exists a Borel field \(v_t\in L^p(\mu_t;\mathbb R^d)\) solving the continuity equation and satisfying \[ \int_0^T\|v_t\|_{L^p(\mu_t)}^p\,\mathrm dt<\infty. \] Every such field obeys \[ |\dot\mu_t|\le\|v_t\|_{L^p(\mu_t)} \quad\text{a.e.} \] There is a unique minimal velocity in the appropriate tangent class for which equality holds.

Proof, velocity implies absolute continuity. The superposition principle represents the solution by a probability \(\boldsymbol\eta\) on absolutely continuous paths \(\gamma\) such that \((e_t)_\#\boldsymbol\eta=\mu_t\) and \(\dot\gamma_t=v_t(\gamma_t)\) almost everywhere. The joint law of \((\gamma_s,\gamma_t)\) couples \(\mu_s,\mu_t\), so \[ \begin{aligned} W_p(\mu_s,\mu_t) &\le\left(\int|\gamma_t-\gamma_s|^p\,\mathrm d\boldsymbol\eta\right)^{1/p}\\ &\le\int_s^t \left(\int|\dot\gamma_r|^p\,\mathrm d\boldsymbol\eta\right)^{1/p}\mathrm dr\\ &=\int_s^t\|v_r\|_{L^p(\mu_r)}\,\mathrm dr. \end{aligned} \] This proves absolute continuity and the metric-derivative bound.

Proof, absolute continuity implies velocity. For small \(h\), choose optimal plans between \(\mu_t\) and \(\mu_{t+h}\) and view \((y-x)/h\) as a discrete velocity. The \(L^p\) norm of this velocity is \(W_p(\mu_t,\mu_{t+h})/|h|\), which is locally bounded by the metric derivative. Weak compactness in \(L^p(\mu_t)\), together with differentiation of test-function integrals, produces a limiting field \(v_t\) satisfying the continuity equation. Lower semicontinuity gives \(\|v_t\|_{L^p(\mu_t)}\le|\dot\mu_t|\). Combined with the first inequality, equality follows for the minimal representative.

The superposition principle

Superposition principle. If \((\mu_t,v_t)\) solves the continuity equation and \(\int_0^T\int|v_t|\,\mathrm d\mu_t\,\mathrm dt<\infty\), then there exists a probability measure \(\boldsymbol\eta\) on \(AC([0,T];\mathbb R^d)\) such that \[ (e_t)_\#\boldsymbol\eta=\mu_t,\qquad \dot\gamma_t=v_t(\gamma_t) \] for \(\boldsymbol\eta\)-almost every path and almost every time.

This theorem is a measure-valued extension of the method of characteristics. Even when the vector field has no unique classical flow, the solution is still a probability distribution over integral curves.

Minimal velocities and tangent directions

Velocity fields are not unique. A field \(w_t\) satisfying \[ \nabla\cdot(w_t\mu_t)=0 \] may be added without changing the curve. For \(p=2\), the minimal velocity is the orthogonal projection onto \[ T_{\mu_t}\mathcal P_2 =\overline{\{\nabla\phi:\phi\in C_c^\infty(\mathbb R^d)\}}^{L^2(\mu_t)}. \] Divergence-free components are orthogonal to all gradients and contribute kinetic energy without moving the measure at first order.

Translation, dilation, and displacement flows

Translation. If \(\mu_t=(x\mapsto x+ta)_\#\mu_0\), then \(v_t\equiv a\), the continuity equation holds, and \(|\dot\mu_t|=|a|\).

Dilation. If \(\mu_t=(x\mapsto e^t x)_\#\mu_0\), then \(v_t(x)=x\). Hence \[ |\dot\mu_t|^2=\int|x|^2\,\mathrm d\mu_t(x) \] when this field is the minimal \(L^2\) representative.

A displacement geodesic. If \(\mu_t=((1-t)\operatorname{id}+tT)_\#\mu_0\), then along the particle starting at \(x\), velocity is \(T(x)-x\). In Eulerian coordinates, \[ v_t=(T-\operatorname{id})\circ T_t^{-1} \] wherever \(T_t\) is invertible. Its kinetic norm is constant and equals \(W_2(\mu_0,\mu_1)\).

13. Courbes absolument continues et équation de continuité

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Retour" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Page précédente" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/displacement-interpolation/" next_label="Page suivante" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/benamou-brenier-formula/" %}

Une courbe de mesures n'a pas de dérivée ponctuelle dans un espace vectoriel. Son mouvement infinitésimal est décrit par un champ de vitesse transportant la masse à travers l'équation de continuité. Cette description eulérienne équivaut à l'absolue continuité métrique.

Équation de continuité au sens des distributions

Définition. Une courbe étroitement continue \(\mu_t\) et un champ borélien \(v_t\) résolvent \[ \partial_t\mu_t+\nabla\cdot(v_t\mu_t)=0 \] si, pour tout \(\zeta\in C_c^\infty((0,T)\times\mathbb R^d)\), \[ \int_0^T\int \{\partial_t\zeta+\nabla\zeta\cdot v_t\} \,\mathrm d\mu_t\,\mathrm dt=0. \]

De manière équivalente, \[ \frac{\mathrm d}{\mathrm dt}\int\phi\,\mathrm d\mu_t =\int\nabla\phi\cdot v_t\,\mathrm d\mu_t \] pour toute fonction test spatiale et presque tout \(t\).

Flots de particules

Proposition. Si \(X_t\) résout \(\dot X_t(x)=v_t(X_t(x))\), \(X_0(x)=x\), alors \(\mu_t=X_{t\#}\mu_0\) résout l'équation de continuité.

Preuve. Pour \(\phi\) lisse, \[ \frac{\mathrm d}{\mathrm dt}\int\phi(X_t(x))\,\mathrm d\mu_0(x) =\int\nabla\phi(X_t(x))\cdot v_t(X_t(x))\,\mathrm d\mu_0(x) =\int\nabla\phi\cdot v_t\,\mathrm d\mu_t. \]

Absolue continuité métrique

Définition. Une courbe \(\mu_t\) appartient à \(AC^p\) s'il existe \(m\in L^p(0,T)\) tel que \[ W_p(\mu_s,\mu_t)\le\int_s^t m(r)\,\mathrm dr. \] Sa dérivée métrique est \[ |\dot\mu_t|=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{W_p(\mu_{t+h},\mu_t)}{|h|}, \] définie presque partout et minimale parmi les fonctions \(m\).

Caractérisation par les vitesses

Caractérisation eulérienne. Pour \(1<p<\infty\), \(\mu_t\in AC^p\) si et seulement s'il existe \(v_t\in L^p(\mu_t)\) résolvant l'équation de continuité et \[ \int_0^T\|v_t\|_{L^p(\mu_t)}^p\,\mathrm dt<\infty. \] Tout tel champ vérifie \(|\dot\mu_t|\le\|v_t\|_{L^p(\mu_t)}\). Un représentant minimal réalise l'égalité.

Preuve, du champ à la courbe. Le principe de superposition représente la solution par une probabilité \(\boldsymbol\eta\) sur des trajectoires telles que \((e_t)_\#\boldsymbol\eta=\mu_t\) et \(\dot\gamma_t=v_t(\gamma_t)\). La loi de \((\gamma_s,\gamma_t)\) est un couplage; Minkowski donne \[ W_p(\mu_s,\mu_t) \le\int_s^t\|v_r\|_{L^p(\mu_r)}\,\mathrm dr. \]

Preuve, de la courbe au champ. Entre \(\mu_t\) et \(\mu_{t+h}\), les plans optimaux définissent des vitesses discrètes \((y-x)/h\), de norme \(W_p(\mu_t,\mu_{t+h})/|h|\). La compacité faible dans \(L^p\) et la dérivation des intégrales de fonctions tests produisent une limite \(v_t\) satisfaisant l'équation. La semi-continuité donne \(\|v_t\|_{L^p}\le|\dot\mu_t|\); l'inégalité opposée précédente donne l'égalité pour le champ minimal.

Principe de superposition

Principe de superposition. Si \((\mu_t,v_t)\) résout l'équation et \(\int_0^T\int|v_t|\,\mathrm d\mu_t\,\mathrm dt<\infty\), il existe une probabilité \(\boldsymbol\eta\) sur \(AC([0,T];\mathbb R^d)\) telle que \[ (e_t)_\#\boldsymbol\eta=\mu_t,\qquad \dot\gamma_t=v_t(\gamma_t) \] presque partout.

C'est une extension du procédé des caractéristiques : même sans flot classique unique, la solution est une distribution de probabilité sur les courbes intégrales.

Vitesses minimales et directions tangentes

Les vitesses ne sont pas uniques : on peut ajouter \(w_t\) tel que \(\nabla\cdot(w_t\mu_t)=0\). Pour \(p=2\), le champ minimal est la projection orthogonale sur \[ T_{\mu_t}\mathcal P_2 =\overline{\{\nabla\phi:\phi\in C_c^\infty\}}^{L^2(\mu_t)}. \] Les composantes sans divergence ajoutent de l'énergie sans déplacer la mesure au premier ordre.

Flots de translation, de dilatation et de déplacement

Translation. Pour \(\mu_t=(x\mapsto x+ta)_\#\mu_0\), \(v_t\equiv a\) et \(|\dot\mu_t|=|a|\).

Dilatation. Pour \(\mu_t=(x\mapsto e^tx)_\#\mu_0\), \(v_t(x)=x\) et \(|\dot\mu_t|^2=\int|x|^2\,\mathrm d\mu_t\) lorsque ce champ est minimal.

Géodésique de déplacement. Pour \(\mu_t=T_{t\#}\mu_0\), la vitesse lagrangienne est \(T(x)-x\), et \[ v_t=(T-\operatorname{id})\circ T_t^{-1}. \] Sa norme cinétique est constante et vaut \(W_2(\mu_0,\mu_1)\).

13. 绝对连续曲线与连续性方程

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="返回" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="上一页" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/displacement-interpolation/" next_label="下一页" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/benamou-brenier-formula/" %}

测度曲线没有通常向量空间意义下的逐点导数。其无穷小运动由速度场通过连续性方程描述。度量意义的绝对连续性与这种欧拉描述彼此等价。

分布意义下的连续性方程

定义. 若窄连续曲线 \(t\mapsto\mu_t\) 与博雷尔速度场 \(v_t(x)\) 对每个 \(\zeta\in C_c^\infty((0,T)\times\mathbb R^d)\) 满足 \[ \int_0^T\int \{\partial_t\zeta(t,x)+\nabla\zeta(t,x)\cdot v_t(x)\} \,\mathrm d\mu_t(x)\,\mathrm dt=0, \] 则称它们解 \[ \partial_t\mu_t+\nabla\cdot(v_t\mu_t)=0. \]

等价地，对每个空间测试函数 \(\phi\)， \[ \frac{\mathrm d}{\mathrm dt}\int\phi\,\mathrm d\mu_t =\int\nabla\phi\cdot v_t\,\mathrm d\mu_t \] 对几乎每个 \(t\) 成立。

粒子流

命题. 若 \(X_t(x)\) 解 \[ \frac{\mathrm d}{\mathrm dt}X_t(x)=v_t(X_t(x)),\qquad X_0(x)=x, \] 则 \(\mu_t=X_{t\#}\mu_0\) 解连续性方程。

证明. 对光滑紧支撑 \(\phi\)，链式法则给出 \[ \frac{\mathrm d}{\mathrm dt}\int\phi(X_t(x))\,\mathrm d\mu_0(x) =\int\nabla\phi(X_t(x))\cdot v_t(X_t(x))\,\mathrm d\mu_0(x) =\int\nabla\phi\cdot v_t\,\mathrm d\mu_t. \]

度量绝对连续性

定义. 若存在 \(m\in L^p(0,T)\)，使得 \[ W_p(\mu_s,\mu_t)\le\int_s^t m(r)\,\mathrm dr, \] 则称 \(\mu:[0,T]\to\mathcal P_p\) 属于 \(AC^p\)。其度量导数为 \[ |\dot\mu_t|=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{W_p(\mu_{t+h},\mu_t)}{|h|}, \] 它几乎处处存在，并且是所有可行 \(m\) 中最小的。

速度场刻画

欧拉刻画. 设 \(1<p<\infty\)。曲线 \(\mu_t\in AC^p\) 当且仅当存在 \(v_t\in L^p(\mu_t;\mathbb R^d)\) 解连续性方程，并满足 \[ \int_0^T\|v_t\|_{L^p(\mu_t)}^p\,\mathrm dt<\infty. \] 每个这样的速度场都满足 \[ |\dot\mu_t|\le\|v_t\|_{L^p(\mu_t)} \] 几乎处处；存在唯一的最小切向速度使等号成立。

由速度场推出绝对连续性. 叠加原理把解表示为绝对连续路径 \(\gamma\) 上的概率测度 \(\boldsymbol\eta\)，满足 \((e_t)_\#\boldsymbol\eta=\mu_t\)、\(\dot\gamma_t=v_t(\gamma_t)\)。\((\gamma_s,\gamma_t)\) 的分布耦合 \(\mu_s,\mu_t\)，故 \[ W_p(\mu_s,\mu_t) \le\int_s^t\|v_r\|_{L^p(\mu_r)}\,\mathrm dr. \]

由绝对连续性推出速度场. 在 \(\mu_t\) 与 \(\mu_{t+h}\) 间取最优计划，把 \((y-x)/h\) 看作离散速度，其 \(L^p\) 范数为 \(W_p(\mu_t,\mu_{t+h})/|h|\)。\(L^p\) 弱紧性与测试函数积分的微分给出极限速度 \(v_t\) 和连续性方程。下半连续性给出 \(\|v_t\|_{L^p}\le|\dot\mu_t|\)，结合前向不等式即得最小速度处的等号。

叠加原理

叠加原理. 若 \((\mu_t,v_t)\) 解连续性方程且 \[ \int_0^T\int|v_t|\,\mathrm d\mu_t\,\mathrm dt<\infty, \] 则存在 \(AC([0,T];\mathbb R^d)\) 上的概率测度 \(\boldsymbol\eta\)，使 \[ (e_t)_\#\boldsymbol\eta=\mu_t,\qquad \dot\gamma_t=v_t(\gamma_t) \] 对几乎每条路径和几乎每个时刻成立。

该定理是特征线方法的测度值推广。即使向量场不存在唯一经典流，解仍可表示为积分曲线上的概率分布。

最小速度与切向方向

速度场并不唯一。若 \(\nabla\cdot(w_t\mu_t)=0\)，则加上 \(w_t\) 不改变曲线。对 \(p=2\)，最小速度是到 \[ T_{\mu_t}\mathcal P_2 =\overline{\{\nabla\phi:\phi\in C_c^\infty\}}^{L^2(\mu_t)} \] 的正交投影。无散分量与所有梯度正交，只增加动能而不在一阶上移动测度。

平移、伸缩与位移流

平移. 若 \(\mu_t=(x\mapsto x+ta)_\#\mu_0\)，则 \(v_t\equiv a\)，且 \(|\dot\mu_t|=|a|\)。

伸缩. 若 \(\mu_t=(x\mapsto e^tx)_\#\mu_0\)，则 \(v_t(x)=x\)。当该场最小时，\(|\dot\mu_t|^2=\int|x|^2\,\mathrm d\mu_t\)。

位移测地线. 若 \(\mu_t=T_{t\#}\mu_0\)，拉格朗日速度为 \(T(x)-x\)。在 \(T_t\) 可逆处， \[ v_t=(T-\operatorname{id})\circ T_t^{-1}, \] 其动能范数恒定并等于 \(W_2(\mu_0,\mu_1)\)。
