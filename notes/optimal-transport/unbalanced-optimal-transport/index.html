26. Unbalanced optimal transport

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/schrodinger-bridges-and-entropic-dynamics/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/multi-marginal-and-martingale-transport/" %}

Classical transport requires equal total mass and preserves every particle. Unbalanced transport permits mass to be created, removed, or attenuated, charging separately for spatial motion and variation of mass. The resulting geometry combines Wasserstein and Fisher--Rao structures.

Relaxing the marginal constraints

Definition. Let \(\mu,\nu\) be finite nonnegative measures, not necessarily of equal mass. For divergences \(D_1,D_2\) on positive measures, define \[ \operatorname{UOT}(\mu,\nu) = \inf_{\pi\in\mathcal M_+(X\times Y)} \left\{ \int c\,\mathrm d\pi +\tau_1D_1(\pi_X\,\|\,\mu) +\tau_2D_2(\pi_Y\,\|\,\nu) \right\}. \] The marginals of \(\pi\) need not equal \(\mu,\nu\); the divergences penalize their mismatch.

Transported mass is represented by \(\pi\). Source mass not used by \(\pi_X\) may be destroyed, and missing target mass relative to \(\pi_Y\) may be created. The parameters \(\tau_1,\tau_2\) determine whether it is cheaper to move mass or modify it.

KL-penalized unbalanced transport

Dual formulation. For \(D_i=\operatorname{KL}\), \[ \operatorname{UOT}(\mu,\nu) = \sup_{\varphi+\psi\le c} \left[ -\tau_1\int\{e^{-\varphi/\tau_1}-1\}\,\mathrm d\mu -\tau_2\int\{e^{-\psi/\tau_2}-1\}\,\mathrm d\nu \right]. \]

Proof. The entropy conjugacy formula is \[ \tau\operatorname{KL}(\alpha\,\|\,\mu) = \sup_f\left\{\int f\,\mathrm d\alpha -\tau\int(e^{f/\tau}-1)\,\mathrm d\mu\right\}. \] Apply it to \(\pi_X,\pi_Y\), then minimize over \(\pi\ge0\). Finiteness requires \(c+f+g\ge0\). Setting \(\varphi=-f,\psi=-g\) gives the constraint and dual objective.

Unlike balanced dual potentials, \(\varphi,\psi\) cannot be shifted by opposite constants without changing the objective: the total transported mass is itself an optimized quantity.

Entropic scaling for unequal mass

Adding coupling entropy with parameter \(\varepsilon\) again gives a Gibbs plan \(P=\operatorname{diag}(u)K\operatorname{diag}(v)\). For discrete KL marginal penalties, generalized Sinkhorn updates take the form \[ u\leftarrow\left(a\oslash Kv\right)^{\tau_1/(\tau_1+\varepsilon)}, \qquad v\leftarrow\left(b\oslash K^{\mathsf T}u\right)^{\tau_2/(\tau_2+\varepsilon)}. \] Exponents below one perform only a partial marginal correction. As \(\tau_i\to\infty\), the exponents approach one and balanced Sinkhorn is recovered.

The continuity equation with reaction

Definition. A dynamic unbalanced path consists of a measure curve \(\mu_t\), a velocity \(v_t\), and a reaction rate \(\alpha_t\) satisfying \[ \partial_t\mu_t+\nabla\cdot(v_t\mu_t)=\alpha_t\mu_t. \] In one common normalization, its action is \[ \mathcal A_\delta(\mu,v,\alpha) = \int_0^1\int \{|v_t|^2+\delta^2|\alpha_t|^2\} \,\mathrm d\mu_t\,\mathrm dt. \]

The left side moves existing mass; the right side changes its amplitude. The parameter \(\delta\) sets the relative price of reaction. The induced distance is a form of the Hellinger--Kantorovich or Wasserstein--Fisher--Rao metric, with constants depending on normalization.

Pure growth and Fisher--Rao geometry

Scalar mass distance. If all mass stays at one point and only its amount changes from \(m_0\) to \(m_1\), the minimal action is \[ 4\delta^2(\sqrt{m_1}-\sqrt{m_0})^2. \]

Proof. The reaction equation is \(m'=\alpha m\). Put \(r=\sqrt m\), so \(\alpha=2r'/r\). Then \[ \delta^2\int_0^1\alpha_t^2m_t\,\mathrm dt =4\delta^2\int_0^1|r_t'|^2\,\mathrm dt. \] The minimum over curves with fixed endpoints is attained by linear \(r_t\), giving the formula.

Cone geometry and Dirac measures

Dirac formula. In the normalization above, \[ \operatorname{HK}_\delta^2(a\delta_x,b\delta_y) = 4\delta^2\left[ a+b-2\sqrt{ab}\, \cos_+\!\left(\frac{d(x,y)}{2\delta}\right) \right], \] where \(\cos_+(r)=\cos(\min\{r,\pi/2\})\).

The square root of mass is a radial coordinate on a metric cone over \(X\). Nearby Dirac masses are partly transported; beyond the cutoff angle it is cheaper to annihilate at \(x\) and recreate at \(y\). This gives a precise geometric meaning to the transport--reaction tradeoff.

Dynamic Hellinger--Kantorovich formula

Dynamic representation. The squared Hellinger--Kantorovich distance is the infimum of \(\mathcal A_\delta\) over all reaction--continuity triples connecting the endpoint measures. Minimizers exist under standard compactness assumptions, and the distance metrizes weak convergence together with convergence of total mass on bounded spaces.

Proof mechanism. Lift each measure to the cone by representing mass as squared radius. Ordinary quadratic transport on the cone simultaneously changes position and radius. Projecting cone geodesics back to \(X\) yields the reaction--continuity equation and its action. Conversely, every finite-action triple admits a cone lift. The Benamou--Brenier formula on the cone gives equality.

Unbalanced barycenters

An unbalanced barycenter minimizes \[ \nu\longmapsto\sum_i\lambda_i\operatorname{HK}_\delta^2(\nu,\mu_i) \] or an analogous static UOT objective. It can average measures with different total mass and can suppress features present in only a few inputs rather than transporting mass over a long distance to accommodate them.

Gradient flows with mass variation

The formal tangent norm now contains transport and reaction: \[ \|(\nabla\phi,\alpha)\|_\mu^2 =\int(|\nabla\phi|^2+\delta^2\alpha^2)\,\mathrm d\mu. \] Gradient flows combine spatial drift with a reaction term. For a first variation \(F=\delta\mathcal E/\delta\rho\), one obtains, up to normalization, \[ \partial_t\rho =\nabla\cdot(\rho\nabla F)-\delta^{-2}\rho F. \] The first term transports mass down the energy gradient; the second creates or destroys it according to the sign of \(F\).

Balanced and pure-reaction limits

As reaction becomes infinitely expensive, the dynamic constraint approaches mass conservation and the geometry tends toward Wasserstein distance on equal-mass measures. If spatial motion becomes infinitely expensive, only Fisher--Rao change remains. Unbalanced transport is therefore not a small variant of classical transport but a geometry interpolating two distinct mechanisms.

26. Transport optimal non équilibré

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Retour" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Page précédente" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/schrodinger-bridges-and-entropic-dynamics/" next_label="Page suivante" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/multi-marginal-and-martingale-transport/" %}

Le transport classique exige des masses totales égales et conserve chaque particule. Le transport non équilibré permet création, destruction ou atténuation, avec des coûts séparés pour le mouvement spatial et la variation de masse. Sa géométrie combine Wasserstein et Fisher--Rao.

Relaxation des contraintes marginales

Définition. \[ \operatorname{UOT}(\mu,\nu) = \inf_{\pi\ge0}\left\{ \int c\,\mathrm d\pi +\tau_1D_1(\pi_X\,\|\,\mu) +\tau_2D_2(\pi_Y\,\|\,\nu)\right\}. \] Les marginales de \(\pi\) ne sont plus imposées; les divergences pénalisent leur écart aux données.

Transport non équilibré pénalisé par KL

Dual. \[ \operatorname{UOT}(\mu,\nu) = \sup_{\varphi+\psi\le c}\left[ -\tau_1\int(e^{-\varphi/\tau_1}-1)\,\mathrm d\mu -\tau_2\int(e^{-\psi/\tau_2}-1)\,\mathrm d\nu \right]. \]

Preuve. On utilise \[ \tau\operatorname{KL}(\alpha\,\|\,\mu) =\sup_f\left\{\int f\,\mathrm d\alpha-\tau\int(e^{f/\tau}-1)\,\mathrm d\mu\right\}. \] Après application aux deux marginales, la minimisation en \(\pi\ge0\) exige \(c+f+g\ge0\). Le changement \(\varphi=-f,\psi=-g\) donne la formule.

Équilibrage entropique

Avec une entropie de couplage \(\varepsilon\), les mises à jour généralisées sont \[ u\leftarrow(a\oslash Kv)^{\tau_1/(\tau_1+\varepsilon)},\qquad v\leftarrow(b\oslash K^{\mathsf T}u)^{\tau_2/(\tau_2+\varepsilon)}. \] Les exposants inférieurs à un ne corrigent que partiellement les marginales. Lorsque \(\tau_i\to\infty\), on retrouve Sinkhorn équilibré.

Équation de continuité avec réaction

Définition. \[ \partial_t\mu_t+\nabla\cdot(v_t\mu_t)=\alpha_t\mu_t, \] avec action \[ \mathcal A_\delta =\int_0^1\int(|v_t|^2+\delta^2|\alpha_t|^2)\,\mathrm d\mu_t\,\mathrm dt. \] Le paramètre \(\delta\) règle le prix relatif de la réaction.

Croissance pure et géométrie de Fisher--Rao

Distance entre masses scalaires. Sans mouvement spatial, le coût minimal pour passer de \(m_0\) à \(m_1\) est \[ 4\delta^2(\sqrt{m_1}-\sqrt{m_0})^2. \]

Preuve. \(m'=\alpha m\). Avec \(r=\sqrt m\), \(\alpha=2r'/r\), donc l'action est \(4\delta^2\int|r'|^2\). Le chemin linéaire en \(r\) minimise cette énergie.

Géométrie conique

Formule pour deux masses de Dirac. \[ \operatorname{HK}_\delta^2(a\delta_x,b\delta_y) = 4\delta^2\left[ a+b-2\sqrt{ab}\cos_+\!\left(\frac{d(x,y)}{2\delta}\right) \right]. \]

La racine carrée de la masse est la coordonnée radiale d'un cône métrique. Au-delà de l'angle de coupure, il est moins coûteux d'annihiler puis de recréer la masse que de la transporter.

Formule dynamique de Hellinger--Kantorovich

Représentation dynamique. Le carré de la distance HK est l'infimum de \(\mathcal A_\delta\) parmi les triples satisfaisant l'équation avec réaction.

Mécanisme. On relève les mesures sur le cône, où la masse devient rayon carré. Le transport quadratique sur le cône modifie simultanément position et rayon. La projection donne l'équation avec réaction; le relèvement inverse et Benamou--Brenier sur le cône donnent l'égalité.

Barycentres et flots non équilibrés

Un barycentre HK peut moyenner des masses totales différentes. Le produit tangent contient transport et réaction. Pour \(F=\delta\mathcal E/\delta\rho\), le flot formel est \[ \partial_t\rho=\nabla\cdot(\rho\nabla F)-\delta^{-2}\rho F. \]

Limites équilibrée et réactionnelle

Lorsque la réaction devient infiniment chère, on retrouve Wasserstein sur les mesures de même masse. Lorsque le déplacement devient infiniment cher, il ne reste que Fisher--Rao. La théorie interpole donc deux mécanismes géométriques distincts.

26. 非平衡最优传输

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="返回" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="上一页" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/schrodinger-bridges-and-entropic-dynamics/" next_label="下一页" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/multi-marginal-and-martingale-transport/" %}

经典传输要求总质量相等并保存每个粒子。非平衡传输允许质量产生、消失或衰减，并分别为位置移动与质量变化收费。所得几何结合了瓦瑟斯坦结构与费雪--拉奥结构。

放松边缘约束

定义. 设 \(\mu,\nu\) 为有限非负测度，总质量可以不同。对正测度间散度 \(D_1,D_2\)，定义 \[ \operatorname{UOT}(\mu,\nu) = \inf_{\pi\in\mathcal M_+(X\times Y)} \left\{ \int c\,\mathrm d\pi +\tau_1D_1(\pi_X\,\|\,\mu) +\tau_2D_2(\pi_Y\,\|\,\nu) \right\}. \] \(\pi\) 的边缘不必等于 \(\mu,\nu\)，散度惩罚其不匹配。

\(\pi\) 表示实际被传输的质量。未被 \(\pi_X\) 使用的源质量可以消失，相对于 \(\pi_Y\) 缺少的目标质量可以产生。参数 \(\tau_1,\tau_2\) 决定移动质量还是改变质量更便宜。

KL 惩罚的非平衡传输

对偶表述. 当 \(D_i=\operatorname{KL}\) 时， \[ \operatorname{UOT}(\mu,\nu) = \sup_{\varphi+\psi\le c} \left[ -\tau_1\int\{e^{-\varphi/\tau_1}-1\}\,\mathrm d\mu -\tau_2\int\{e^{-\psi/\tau_2}-1\}\,\mathrm d\nu \right]. \]

证明. 使用熵共轭公式 \[ \tau\operatorname{KL}(\alpha\,\|\,\mu) = \sup_f\left\{\int f\,\mathrm d\alpha -\tau\int(e^{f/\tau}-1)\,\mathrm d\mu\right\}. \] 分别用于 \(\pi_X,\pi_Y\)，再对 \(\pi\ge0\) 极小化。有限性要求 \(c+f+g\ge0\)。令 \(\varphi=-f,\psi=-g\) 即得结论。

与平衡对偶势不同，给 \(\varphi,\psi\) 加上相反常数会改变目标值，因为总传输质量本身也是待优化变量。

不等质量的熵缩放

再加入参数为 \(\varepsilon\) 的耦合熵，仍得到吉布斯计划 \(P=\operatorname{diag}(u)K\operatorname{diag}(v)\)。离散 KL 边缘惩罚下的广义辛克霍恩更新为 \[ u\leftarrow\left(a\oslash Kv\right)^{\tau_1/(\tau_1+\varepsilon)}, \qquad v\leftarrow\left(b\oslash K^{\mathsf T}u\right)^{\tau_2/(\tau_2+\varepsilon)}. \] 小于一的指数只部分修正边缘；当 \(\tau_i\to\infty\) 时，指数趋于一，恢复平衡辛克霍恩。

带反应项的连续性方程

定义. 非平衡动态路径由测度曲线 \(\mu_t\)、速度 \(v_t\) 与反应率 \(\alpha_t\) 组成，满足 \[ \partial_t\mu_t+\nabla\cdot(v_t\mu_t)=\alpha_t\mu_t. \] 在一种常用规范下，作用量为 \[ \mathcal A_\delta(\mu,v,\alpha) = \int_0^1\int \{|v_t|^2+\delta^2|\alpha_t|^2\} \,\mathrm d\mu_t\,\mathrm dt. \]

左侧移动现有质量，右侧改变其幅度。参数 \(\delta\) 决定反应的相对价格。由此诱导的度量是海灵格--康托罗维奇或瓦瑟斯坦--费雪--拉奥距离的一种规范。

纯增长与费雪--拉奥几何

标量质量距离. 若全部质量固定在同一点，只从 \(m_0\) 变为 \(m_1\)，则最小作用量为 \[ 4\delta^2(\sqrt{m_1}-\sqrt{m_0})^2. \]

证明. 反应方程为 \(m'=\alpha m\)。令 \(r=\sqrt m\)，则 \(\alpha=2r'/r\)，从而 \[ \delta^2\int_0^1\alpha_t^2m_t\,\mathrm dt =4\delta^2\int_0^1|r_t'|^2\,\mathrm dt. \] 固定端点时，线性 \(r_t\) 最小化该能量，得到公式。

锥几何与狄拉克测度

狄拉克公式. 在上述规范下， \[ \operatorname{HK}_\delta^2(a\delta_x,b\delta_y) = 4\delta^2\left[ a+b-2\sqrt{ab}\, \cos_+\!\left(\frac{d(x,y)}{2\delta}\right) \right], \] 其中 \(\cos_+(r)=\cos(\min\{r,\pi/2\})\)。

质量平方根是以 \(X\) 为底的度量锥上的径向坐标。邻近狄拉克质量会部分传输；超过截断角后，在 \(x\) 消灭并在 \(y\) 重建比搬运更便宜。这精确刻画了传输与反应的权衡。

海灵格--康托罗维奇动态公式

动态表示. 海灵格--康托罗维奇距离平方等于所有连接端点的反应--连续性三元组的 \(\mathcal A_\delta\) 下确界。在标准紧性条件下极小化解存在；在有界空间上，该距离刻画弱收敛与总质量收敛。

证明机制. 把每个测度提升到度量锥，使质量成为半径平方。锥上的普通二次传输同时改变位置与半径；把锥测地线投影回 \(X\) 得到反应--连续性方程及其作用量。反之，每个有限作用三元组都有锥提升。锥上的贝纳穆--布雷尼耶公式给出等式。

非平衡重心与梯度流

非平衡重心可以平均总质量不同的测度。其形式切向范数同时包含传输与反应。若 \(F=\delta\mathcal E/\delta\rho\)，相差规范常数后，梯度流为 \[ \partial_t\rho =\nabla\cdot(\rho\nabla F)-\delta^{-2}\rho F. \] 第一项沿能量梯度移动质量，第二项按 \(F\) 的符号产生或消灭质量。

平衡极限与纯反应极限

当反应无限昂贵时，动态约束趋于质量守恒，几何在等质量测度上趋于瓦瑟斯坦距离。若空间运动无限昂贵，则只剩费雪--拉奥变化。因此非平衡传输不是经典理论的小修补，而是在两个不同机制之间插值的新几何。
