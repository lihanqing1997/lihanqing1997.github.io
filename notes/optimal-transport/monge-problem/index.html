2. Monge problem

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/transport-plans-and-couplings/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/kantorovich-problem/" %}

Monge problem records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the optimal transport note.

Definition

Definition. The Monge problem seeks a measurable map \(T:X\to Y\) pushing \(\mu\) to \(\nu\), written \(T_\#\mu=\nu\), minimizing \(\int c(x,T(x))\,\mathrm{d}\mu(x)\).

Proposition

Proposition. Every transport map induces a coupling \((\operatorname{id},T)_\#\mu\), but not every coupling comes from a map. Monge's problem may fail to have feasible maps or minimizers.

Proof

Proof. The induced coupling has correct marginals by definition of pushforward. Failure examples occur when an atom of \(\mu\) must be split to match a non-atomic or differently atomic \(\nu\).

Remark

Remark. Monge's formulation is geometrically natural but analytically rigid. Kantorovich relaxation is the convex repair.
