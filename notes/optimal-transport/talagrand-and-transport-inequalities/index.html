15. Talagrand and transport inequalities

Talagrand and transport inequalities records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the optimal transport note.

Definition

Definition. Transport inequalities bound Wasserstein distance by entropy or other divergence quantities, typically comparing a measure to a reference law.

Talagrand inequality

Theorem. For the standard Gaussian measure \(\gamma\), Talagrand's inequality gives \(W_2^2(\mu,\gamma)\le 2\operatorname{KL}(\mu\mid\gamma)\).

Proof

Proof. One proof uses displacement convexity of entropy along optimal transport from \(\mu\) to \(\gamma\), combined with Gaussian integration by parts.

Remark

Remark. Transport inequalities link concentration of measure, information theory, and geometry. They are central but belong here as transport structure, not only probability.
