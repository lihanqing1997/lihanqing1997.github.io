19. Transport inequalities and concentration

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/diffusion-and-interaction-equations/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/empirical-measures-in-wasserstein-distance/" %}

Transport inequalities convert information divergence into geometric distance. Through Kantorovich duality they become exponential bounds for Lipschitz observables, and through gradient flows they connect entropy, Fisher information, curvature, and concentration.

Relative entropy and transport inequalities

Definition. For \(\nu,\mu\in\mathcal P(X)\), \[ \operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\mu) = \begin{cases} \displaystyle\int\log\!\left(\frac{\mathrm d\nu}{\mathrm d\mu}\right)\mathrm d\nu, &\nu\ll\mu,\\ +\infty,&\text{otherwise}. \end{cases} \] A measure \(\mu\) satisfies \(T_1(C)\) if \[ W_1(\nu,\mu)\le\sqrt{2C\,\operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\mu)} \] for every \(\nu\), and satisfies \(T_2(C)\) if \[ W_2^2(\nu,\mu)\le2C\,\operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\mu). \]

Since \(W_1\le W_2\), \(T_2(C)\) implies \(T_1(C)\). Pinsker's inequality, \[ \|\nu-\mu\|_{\mathrm{TV}}^2\le\frac12\operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\mu), \] is an analogous comparison in total variation, but it does not encode the geometry of the underlying space.

Entropy variational formula

Donsker--Varadhan formula. For \(\nu\ll\mu\), \[ \operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\mu) =\sup_f\left\{\int f\,\mathrm d\nu-\log\int e^f\,\mathrm d\mu\right\}, \] where the supremum may be taken over bounded measurable \(f\).

Proof. Let \(h=\mathrm d\nu/\mathrm d\mu\). For any \(f\), define the tilted law \(\mathrm d\mu_f=e^f\mathrm d\mu/\int e^f\mathrm d\mu\). Nonnegativity of \(\operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\mu_f)\) gives \[ \int f\,\mathrm d\nu-\log\int e^f\,\mathrm d\mu \le\int\log h\,\mathrm d\nu. \] Truncations of \(f=\log h\) approach equality.

\(T_1\) and sub-Gaussian observables

Bobkov--Götze characterization. A probability measure \(\mu\) satisfies \(T_1(C)\) if and only if every \(1\)-Lipschitz \(f\) satisfies \[ \log\int e^{\lambda(f-\mu f)}\,\mathrm d\mu \le\frac{C\lambda^2}{2} \qquad\text{for every }\lambda\in\mathbb R. \]

Proof from \(T_1\). For the exponentially tilted law \(\nu_\lambda\propto e^{\lambda f}\mu\), Kantorovich--Rubinstein duality gives \[ \nu_\lambda f-\mu f\le W_1(\nu_\lambda,\mu) \le\sqrt{2C\,\operatorname{KL}(\nu_\lambda\,\|\,\mu)}. \] Writing \(\Lambda(\lambda)=\log\mu e^{\lambda(f-\mu f)}\), one has \(\Lambda'(\lambda)=\nu_\lambda(f-\mu f)\) and \(\operatorname{KL}(\nu_\lambda\,\|\,\mu)=\lambda\Lambda'(\lambda)-\Lambda(\lambda)\). The resulting differential inequality integrates to \(\Lambda(\lambda)\le C\lambda^2/2\).

Reverse implication. For any \(\nu\), the entropy variational formula applied to \(\lambda(f-\mu f)\) gives \[ \lambda(\nu f-\mu f) \le\operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\mu)+\frac{C\lambda^2}{2}. \] Optimize over \(\lambda>0\), then take the supremum over \(1\)-Lipschitz \(f\) and use Kantorovich--Rubinstein duality.

Concentration. Under \(T_1(C)\), every \(1\)-Lipschitz \(f\) satisfies \[ \mu(f-\mu f\ge r)\le e^{-r^2/(2C)}. \] This follows from the exponential moment bound and Chernoff optimization.

Talagrand's Gaussian \(T_2\) inequality

Gaussian transport inequality. For the standard Gaussian measure \(\gamma=N(0,I_d)\) and every \(\nu\in\mathcal P_2(\mathbb R^d)\), \[ W_2^2(\nu,\gamma)\le2\operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\gamma). \]

Proof. It suffices by approximation to assume that \(\nu=f\gamma\) is smooth and positive and that the Brenier map \(T=\nabla u\) pushing \(\gamma\) to \(\nu\) is smooth with \(DT>0\). The Jacobian identity gives \[ f(T(x))e^{-|T(x)|^2/2}\det DT(x)=e^{-|x|^2/2}, \] hence \[ \log f(T(x)) =\frac{|T(x)|^2-|x|^2}{2}-\log\det DT(x). \] Because \(\log a\le a-1\) eigenvalue by eigenvalue, \[ \log\det DT\le\operatorname{tr}(DT-I). \] Therefore \[ \begin{aligned} \operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\gamma) &=\int\log f(T(x))\,\mathrm d\gamma(x)\\ &\ge\frac12\int|T-x|^2\,\mathrm d\gamma +\int x\cdot(T-x)\,\mathrm d\gamma -\int\operatorname{tr}(DT-I)\,\mathrm d\gamma. \end{aligned} \] Gaussian integration by parts gives \[ \int x\cdot(T-x)\,\mathrm d\gamma =\int\operatorname{div}(T-x)\,\mathrm d\gamma =\int\operatorname{tr}(DT-I)\,\mathrm d\gamma. \] The last two terms cancel. Since \(T\) is optimal, \[ \operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\gamma) \ge\frac12\int|T-x|^2\,\mathrm d\gamma =\frac12W_2^2(\nu,\gamma). \]

Logarithmic Sobolev inequality and \(T_2\)

Otto--Villani implication. If \(\mu\) satisfies the logarithmic Sobolev inequality \[ \operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\mu) \le\frac C2 I(\nu\,\|\,\mu), \] where \[ I(\nu\,\|\,\mu)=\int\left|\nabla\log\frac{\mathrm d\nu}{\mathrm d\mu}\right|^2\,\mathrm d\nu, \] then \(\mu\) satisfies \(T_2(C)\).

Proof architecture. Follow the entropy gradient flow from \(\nu\) toward \(\mu\). Along the flow, \[ -\frac{\mathrm d}{\mathrm dt}\operatorname{KL}(\nu_t\,\|\,\mu)=I(\nu_t\,\|\,\mu), \qquad |\dot\nu_t|=\sqrt{I(\nu_t\,\|\,\mu)}. \] Hence \[ W_2(\nu,\mu)\le\int_0^\infty\sqrt{I(\nu_t\,\|\,\mu)}\,\mathrm dt. \] The logarithmic Sobolev inequality forces exponential entropy decay and converts this integral into \(\sqrt{2C\,\operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\mu)}\).

The HWI inequality

HWI inequality. If \(\mu=e^{-V}\mathrm dx/Z\) with \(D^2V\ge\kappa I\), then \[ \operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\mu) \le W_2(\nu,\mu)\sqrt{I(\nu\,\|\,\mu)} -\frac{\kappa}{2}W_2^2(\nu,\mu). \]

Proof. Relative entropy is displacement \(\kappa\)-convex. Apply the convexity inequality along the geodesic from \(\nu\) to \(\mu\), divide by \(t\), and let \(t\downarrow0\). The directional derivative at \(\nu\) is the \(L^2(\nu)\) pairing of \(\nabla\log(\mathrm d\nu/\mathrm d\mu)\) with the geodesic velocity. Cauchy--Schwarz bounds it by \(W_2(\nu,\mu)\sqrt I\), yielding the formula.

Tensorization

If each \(\mu_i\) satisfies \(T_2(C_i)\) on \(X_i\), then the product measure satisfies \(T_2(\max_iC_i)\) for the squared product metric. The proof disintegrates a competitor coordinate by coordinate, applies the one-coordinate inequalities conditionally, and uses the chain rule for relative entropy. This dimension-free tensorization is a central reason transport inequalities yield concentration for high-dimensional product measures.

19. Inégalités de transport et concentration

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Retour" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Page précédente" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/diffusion-and-interaction-equations/" next_label="Page suivante" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/empirical-measures-in-wasserstein-distance/" %}

Les inégalités de transport transforment une divergence d'information en distance géométrique. Par dualité, elles deviennent des bornes exponentielles pour les observables lipschitziennes; par les flots de gradient, elles relient entropie, information de Fisher, courbure et concentration.

Entropie relative et inégalités de transport

Définition. \[ \operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\mu) =\int\log\!\left(\frac{\mathrm d\nu}{\mathrm d\mu}\right)\mathrm d\nu \] si \(\nu\ll\mu\), et vaut \(+\infty\) sinon. La mesure \(\mu\) satisfait \(T_1(C)\) si \[ W_1(\nu,\mu)\le\sqrt{2C\,\operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\mu)}, \] et \(T_2(C)\) si \[ W_2^2(\nu,\mu)\le2C\,\operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\mu). \]

Formule variationnelle de l'entropie

Formule de Donsker--Varadhan. \[ \operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\mu) =\sup_f\left\{\int f\,\mathrm d\nu-\log\int e^f\,\mathrm d\mu\right\}. \]

Preuve. Pour \(\mathrm d\mu_f=e^f\mathrm d\mu/\int e^f\mathrm d\mu\), la positivité de \(\operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\mu_f)\) donne une inégalité. Des troncatures de \(f=\log(\mathrm d\nu/\mathrm d\mu)\) atteignent la limite.

\(T_1\) et observables sous-gaussiennes

Caractérisation de Bobkov--Götze. \(T_1(C)\) équivaut à \[ \log\int e^{\lambda(f-\mu f)}\,\mathrm d\mu\le\frac{C\lambda^2}{2} \] pour toute fonction \(1\)-lipschitzienne \(f\).

Preuve. Sous \(T_1\), appliquons Kantorovitch--Rubinstein à la loi inclinée \(\nu_\lambda\propto e^{\lambda f}\mu\). Pour \(\Lambda(\lambda)=\log\mu e^{\lambda(f-\mu f)}\), \[ \Lambda'(\lambda)\le\sqrt{2C\{\lambda\Lambda'(\lambda)-\Lambda(\lambda)\}}, \] ce qui s'intègre en \(\Lambda(\lambda)\le C\lambda^2/2\). Réciproquement, la formule variationnelle donne \[ \lambda(\nu f-\mu f)\le\operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\mu)+C\lambda^2/2. \] On optimise en \(\lambda\), puis en \(f\).

Concentration. \[ \mu(f-\mu f\ge r)\le e^{-r^2/(2C)} \] pour toute \(f\) \(1\)-lipschitzienne.

Inégalité gaussienne \(T_2\) de Talagrand

Inégalité gaussienne. Pour \(\gamma=N(0,I_d)\), \[ W_2^2(\nu,\gamma)\le2\operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\gamma). \]

Preuve. Supposons \(\nu=f\gamma\) lisse et \(T\) l'application de Brenier de \(\gamma\) vers \(\nu\). L'identité jacobienne donne \[ \log f(T(x))=\frac{|T(x)|^2-|x|^2}{2}-\log\det DT(x). \] Comme \(\log\det DT\le\operatorname{tr}(DT-I)\), \[ \operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\gamma) \ge\frac12\int|T-x|^2\,\mathrm d\gamma +\int x\cdot(T-x)\,\mathrm d\gamma -\int\operatorname{tr}(DT-I)\,\mathrm d\gamma. \] L'intégration par parties gaussienne identifie les deux derniers termes, qui s'annulent. Le premier vaut \(\tfrac12W_2^2(\nu,\gamma)\). L'approximation traite le cas général.

Inégalité logarithmique de Sobolev et \(T_2\)

Implication d'Otto--Villani. Si \[ \operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\mu)\le\frac C2I(\nu\,\|\,\mu), \] alors \(\mu\) satisfait \(T_2(C)\).

Architecture de la preuve. Suivons le flot de gradient de l'entropie depuis \(\nu\) vers \(\mu\). On a \[ -\frac{\mathrm d}{\mathrm dt}\operatorname{KL}=I,\qquad |\dot\nu_t|=\sqrt I. \] La longueur du flot majore \(W_2(\nu,\mu)\), et l'inégalité logarithmique de Sobolev transforme l'intégrale de \(\sqrt I\) en \(\sqrt{2C\,\operatorname{KL}}\).

Inégalité HWI

HWI. Si \(\mu=e^{-V}\mathrm dx/Z\), \(D^2V\ge\kappa I\), \[ \operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\mu) \le W_2(\nu,\mu)\sqrt{I(\nu\,\|\,\mu)} -\frac\kappa2W_2^2(\nu,\mu). \]

Preuve. L'entropie relative est \(\kappa\)-convexe par déplacement. On applique cette convexité sur la géodésique de \(\nu\) vers \(\mu\), puis on dérive en zéro. Cauchy--Schwarz borne la dérivée directionnelle par \(W_2\sqrt I\).

Tensorisation

Si chaque \(\mu_i\) satisfait \(T_2(C_i)\), leur produit satisfait \(T_2(\max_iC_i)\) pour la métrique produit quadratique. La preuve désintègre coordonnée par coordonnée et utilise la règle de chaîne de l'entropie. Cette tensorisation sans perte dimensionnelle explique les conséquences de concentration en grande dimension.

19. 传输不等式与集中性

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="返回" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="上一页" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/diffusion-and-interaction-equations/" next_label="下一页" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/empirical-measures-in-wasserstein-distance/" %}

传输不等式把信息散度转化为几何距离。通过康托罗维奇对偶，它们给出利普希茨观测量的指数界；通过梯度流，它们把熵、费雪信息、曲率与集中性联系起来。

相对熵与传输不等式

定义. 对 \(\nu,\mu\in\mathcal P(X)\)， \[ \operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\mu) = \begin{cases} \displaystyle\int\log\!\left(\frac{\mathrm d\nu}{\mathrm d\mu}\right)\mathrm d\nu, &\nu\ll\mu,\\ +\infty,&\text{否则}. \end{cases} \] 若对所有 \(\nu\)， \[ W_1(\nu,\mu)\le\sqrt{2C\,\operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\mu)}, \] 则称 \(\mu\) 满足 \(T_1(C)\)；若 \[ W_2^2(\nu,\mu)\le2C\,\operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\mu), \] 则称其满足 \(T_2(C)\)。

由于 \(W_1\le W_2\)，\(T_2(C)\) 蕴含 \(T_1(C)\)。平斯克不等式 \[ \|\nu-\mu\|_{\mathrm{TV}}^2\le\tfrac12\operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\mu) \] 是全变差中的对应比较，但不反映底空间几何。

熵的变分公式

唐斯克--瓦拉丹公式. \[ \operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\mu) =\sup_f\left\{\int f\,\mathrm d\nu-\log\int e^f\,\mathrm d\mu\right\}. \]

证明. 令 \(h=\mathrm d\nu/\mathrm d\mu\)，并定义指数倾斜分布 \(\mathrm d\mu_f=e^f\mathrm d\mu/\int e^f\mathrm d\mu\)。\(\operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\mu_f)\ge0\) 给出上界；取 \(f=\log h\) 的截断可逼近等号。

\(T_1\) 与次高斯观测量

博布科夫--格策刻画. \(\mu\) 满足 \(T_1(C)\)，当且仅当每个 \(1\)-利普希茨函数 \(f\) 都满足 \[ \log\int e^{\lambda(f-\mu f)}\,\mathrm d\mu \le\frac{C\lambda^2}{2} \quad\text{对所有 }\lambda\in\mathbb R. \]

由 \(T_1\) 推出矩母函数界. 对指数倾斜分布 \(\nu_\lambda\propto e^{\lambda f}\mu\) 应用康托罗维奇--鲁宾斯坦对偶。记 \(\Lambda(\lambda)=\log\mu e^{\lambda(f-\mu f)}\)，则 \[ \Lambda'(\lambda)\le\sqrt{2C\{\lambda\Lambda'(\lambda)-\Lambda(\lambda)\}}, \] 积分后得 \(\Lambda(\lambda)\le C\lambda^2/2\)。

反向. 熵变分公式给出 \[ \lambda(\nu f-\mu f) \le\operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\mu)+\frac{C\lambda^2}{2}. \] 对 \(\lambda>0\) 优化，再对所有 \(1\)-利普希茨 \(f\) 取上确界，即得 \(T_1\)。

集中不等式. 在 \(T_1(C)\) 下， \[ \mu(f-\mu f\ge r)\le e^{-r^2/(2C)} \] 对每个 \(1\)-利普希茨 \(f\) 成立。

塔拉格朗高斯 \(T_2\) 不等式

高斯传输不等式. 对标准高斯测度 \(\gamma=N(0,I_d)\)， \[ W_2^2(\nu,\gamma)\le2\operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\gamma). \]

证明. 先设 \(\nu=f\gamma\) 光滑为正，且从 \(\gamma\) 到 \(\nu\) 的布雷尼耶映射 \(T\) 光滑、\(DT>0\)。雅可比恒等式给出 \[ \log f(T(x)) =\frac{|T(x)|^2-|x|^2}{2}-\log\det DT(x). \] 对每个特征值用 \(\log a\le a-1\)，得到 \(\log\det DT\le\operatorname{tr}(DT-I)\)。于是 \[ \operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\gamma) \ge\frac12\int|T-x|^2\,\mathrm d\gamma +\int x\cdot(T-x)\,\mathrm d\gamma -\int\operatorname{tr}(DT-I)\,\mathrm d\gamma. \] 高斯分部积分给出 \[ \int x\cdot(T-x)\,\mathrm d\gamma =\int\operatorname{tr}(DT-I)\,\mathrm d\gamma, \] 后两项抵消。由于 \(T\) 最优，第一项为 \(\tfrac12W_2^2(\nu,\gamma)\)。一般情形由逼近得到。

对数索博列夫不等式与 \(T_2\)

奥托--维拉尼蕴含关系. 若 \[ \operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\mu) \le\frac C2I(\nu\,\|\,\mu), \] 其中 \[ I(\nu\,\|\,\mu) =\int\left|\nabla\log\frac{\mathrm d\nu}{\mathrm d\mu}\right|^2\,\mathrm d\nu, \] 则 \(\mu\) 满足 \(T_2(C)\)。

证明结构. 从 \(\nu\) 沿相对熵梯度流走向 \(\mu\)。流上 \[ -\frac{\mathrm d}{\mathrm dt}\operatorname{KL}=I,\qquad |\dot\nu_t|=\sqrt I. \] 因而 \(W_2(\nu,\mu)\) 不超过 \(\int_0^\infty\sqrt{I(\nu_t\,\|\,\mu)}\,\mathrm dt\)。对数索博列夫不等式迫使熵指数衰减，并把该积分控制为 \(\sqrt{2C\,\operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\mu)}\)。

HWI 不等式

HWI 不等式. 若 \(\mu=e^{-V}\mathrm dx/Z\) 且 \(D^2V\ge\kappa I\)，则 \[ \operatorname{KL}(\nu\,\|\,\mu) \le W_2(\nu,\mu)\sqrt{I(\nu\,\|\,\mu)} -\frac{\kappa}{2}W_2^2(\nu,\mu). \]

证明. 相对熵位移 \(\kappa\)-凸。沿从 \(\nu\) 到 \(\mu\) 的测地线应用凸性，除以 \(t\) 后令 \(t\downarrow0\)。在 \(\nu\) 处的方向导数是 \(\nabla\log(\mathrm d\nu/\mathrm d\mu)\) 与测地速度的 \(L^2(\nu)\) 内积，柯西--施瓦茨不等式将其控制为 \(W_2\sqrt I\)。

张量化

若每个 \(\mu_i\) 在 \(X_i\) 上满足 \(T_2(C_i)\)，则其乘积测度关于平方乘积度量满足 \(T_2(\max_iC_i)\)。证明逐坐标分解竞争测度，条件地应用单坐标不等式，并使用相对熵的链式法则。这种无维数损失的张量化，是传输不等式产生高维集中现象的关键原因。
