3. Kantorovich problem

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/monge-problem/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/kantorovich-duality/" %}

Kantorovich problem records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the optimal transport note.

Definition

Definition. The Kantorovich problem minimizes \(\int c(x,y)\,\mathrm{d}\pi(x,y)\) over all couplings \(\pi\in\Pi(\mu,\nu)\).

Proposition

Proposition. If the cost is lower semicontinuous and bounded below by integrable functions, an optimal coupling exists under standard tightness assumptions.

Proof

Proof. Take a minimizing sequence of couplings. Tightness gives a weakly convergent subsequence, marginal constraints pass to the limit, and lower semicontinuity gives optimality.

Remark

Remark. Kantorovich's relaxation turns transport into infinite-dimensional linear programming. Duality then becomes available.
