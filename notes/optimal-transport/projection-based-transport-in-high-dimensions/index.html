22. Projection-based transport in high dimensions

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/inference-for-transport-costs-maps-and-barycenters/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/entropic-regularization/" %}

Projection-based distances replace one difficult high-dimensional transport problem by a family of low-dimensional ones. Their appeal is mathematical as well as computational: one-dimensional transport is explicit, empirical rates improve, and the Cramér--Wold theorem preserves identifiability when all directions are retained.

Sliced Wasserstein distance

Definition. For \(\theta\in\mathbb S^{d-1}\), let \(P_\theta(x)=\theta^{\mathsf T}x\), and let \(\sigma\) be normalized surface measure on the sphere. Define \[ \operatorname{SW}_p^p(\mu,\nu) =\int_{\mathbb S^{d-1}} W_p^p(P_{\theta\#}\mu,P_{\theta\#}\nu)\,\mathrm d\sigma(\theta). \] The max-sliced distance is \[ \operatorname{MSW}_p(\mu,\nu) =\sup_{\theta\in\mathbb S^{d-1}} W_p(P_{\theta\#}\mu,P_{\theta\#}\nu). \]

Metric property. \(\operatorname{SW}_p\) and \(\operatorname{MSW}_p\) are metrics on \(\mathcal P_p(\mathbb R^d)\).

Proof. Symmetry and nonnegativity are immediate. Minkowski's inequality in \(L^p(\mathbb S^{d-1})\), combined with the triangle inequality for each one-dimensional \(W_p\), proves the sliced triangle inequality; the max version follows by taking suprema. If \(\operatorname{SW}_p(\mu,\nu)=0\), projected laws agree for almost every \(\theta\). Continuity of characteristic functions in \(\theta\) extends equality to every direction. The Cramér--Wold theorem then gives \(\mu=\nu\). The max version is immediate.

Comparison with ordinary Wasserstein distance

Projection contraction. \[ \operatorname{SW}_p(\mu,\nu) \le\operatorname{MSW}_p(\mu,\nu) \le W_p(\mu,\nu). \]

Proof. The first inequality is \(L^p\)-average at most supremum. Since \(P_\theta\) is \(1\)-Lipschitz, the pushforward of any coupling gives \[ W_p(P_{\theta\#}\mu,P_{\theta\#}\nu)\le W_p(\mu,\nu). \] Take the supremum over \(\theta\).

A reverse inequality requires restrictions such as compact support or regularity, and its constant generally deteriorates with dimension. Projection distances are therefore weaker quantitatively even though they identify the law.

Quantile representation

Every projected problem is one-dimensional: \[ \operatorname{SW}_p^p(\mu,\nu) = \int_{\mathbb S^{d-1}}\int_0^1 |F_{\mu,\theta}^{-1}(u)-F_{\nu,\theta}^{-1}(u)|^p \,\mathrm du\,\mathrm d\sigma(\theta). \] Here \(F_{\mu,\theta}\) is the distribution function of \(\theta^{\mathsf T}X\). This formula replaces a high-dimensional coupling by sorting along directions.

Identifiability through the Radon transform

If \(\mu\) has density \(f\), the density of \(P_{\theta\#}\mu\) is the Radon transform \[ \mathcal Rf(\theta,t) =\int_{\{x:\theta^{\mathsf T}x=t\}}f(x)\,\mathrm d\mathcal H^{d-1}(x). \] The Fourier-slice theorem states that the one-dimensional Fourier transform in \(t\) equals \(\widehat f(s\theta)\). Thus all projected laws determine the full characteristic function and hence the original distribution.

Empirical behavior

Reduction to one-dimensional rates. For the empirical measure \(\widehat\mu_n\), \[ \mathbb E\operatorname{SW}_p^p(\widehat\mu_n,\mu) = \int_{\mathbb S^{d-1}} \mathbb E W_p^p(P_{\theta\#}\widehat\mu_n,P_{\theta\#}\mu) \,\mathrm d\sigma(\theta). \] Any one-dimensional bound that is uniform in \(\theta\) therefore transfers directly to the sliced distance without the \(n^{-p/d}\) full-dimensional quantization barrier.

Proof. Projection commutes with empirical measures: \[ P_{\theta\#}\widehat\mu_n =\frac1n\sum_i\delta_{\theta^{\mathsf T}X_i}. \] The identity then follows from Tonelli's theorem.

The constants may still depend on dimension through projected moments or through uniformity over directions. Slicing mitigates the geometric curse of matching but does not make high-dimensional statistics entirely dimension free.

The sliced Wasserstein process

For inference, one studies the random field \[ \theta\longmapsto W_p(P_{\theta\#}\widehat\mu_n,P_{\theta\#}\mu). \] Uniform weak convergence over \(\theta\) requires empirical-process control of projected distribution or quantile functions. Continuous functionals of this process yield limit laws for average-sliced, max-sliced, and randomly sliced statistics. Max-slicing is nonsmooth because it also estimates the maximizing direction.

Monte Carlo approximation over directions

Random slicing. For independent uniform directions \(\theta_1,\ldots,\theta_L\), \[ \widehat{\operatorname{SW}}_{p,L}^p =\frac1L\sum_{\ell=1}^L W_p^p(P_{\theta_\ell\#}\mu,P_{\theta_\ell\#}\nu) \] is unbiased for \(\operatorname{SW}_p^p\). If the integrand has finite variance, its directional Monte Carlo error is \(O_{\mathbb P}(L^{-1/2})\).

With empirical measures there are two independent errors: sample error in the projected laws and numerical integration error over directions. Their budgets should be balanced rather than conflated.

Projection-robust transport

Definition. For \(1\le k\le d\), define \[ \operatorname{PRW}_{p,k}(\mu,\nu) = \sup_{E\in\operatorname{Gr}(k,d)} W_p(P_{E\#}\mu,P_{E\#}\nu), \] where \(P_E\) is orthogonal projection and \(\operatorname{Gr}(k,d)\) is the Grassmannian. At \(k=1\) this is max-sliced transport; at \(k=d\) it is ordinary \(W_p\).

The parameter \(k\) interpolates statistical tractability and geometric fidelity. Optimization over \(E\) is nonconvex, and a data-dependent maximizing subspace contributes additional uncertainty.

Gaussian example

Projected Gaussians. If \(\mu=N(m_0,\Sigma_0)\), \(\nu=N(m_1,\Sigma_1)\), then \[ W_2^2(P_{\theta\#}\mu,P_{\theta\#}\nu) = \{\theta^{\mathsf T}(m_0-m_1)\}^2 + \left( \sqrt{\theta^{\mathsf T}\Sigma_0\theta} - \sqrt{\theta^{\mathsf T}\Sigma_1\theta} \right)^2. \] Slicing averages this explicit directional discrepancy; max-slicing searches for the direction where mean or variance differs most.

Limitations

Different directions are not coupled consistently by separate one-dimensional optimal plans, so sliced geodesics need not correspond to a single flow in \(\mathbb R^d\). A discrepancy concentrated in a small set of directions can be diluted by averaging. Finally, using finitely many random directions makes the computed quantity a random approximation to the metric. These are structural differences, not merely implementation details.

22. Transport par projections en grande dimension

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Retour" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Page précédente" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/inference-for-transport-costs-maps-and-barycenters/" next_label="Page suivante" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/entropic-regularization/" %}

Les distances par projections remplacent un problème de transport difficile en grande dimension par une famille de problèmes de faible dimension. Le transport unidimensionnel est explicite, les vitesses empiriques s'améliorent, et le théorème de Cramér--Wold conserve l'identifiabilité lorsque toutes les directions sont utilisées.

Distance de Wasserstein tranchée

Définition. Pour \(P_\theta(x)=\theta^{\mathsf T}x\), \[ \operatorname{SW}_p^p(\mu,\nu) =\int_{\mathbb S^{d-1}} W_p^p(P_{\theta\#}\mu,P_{\theta\#}\nu)\,\mathrm d\sigma(\theta), \] et \[ \operatorname{MSW}_p(\mu,\nu) =\sup_{\theta\in\mathbb S^{d-1}} W_p(P_{\theta\#}\mu,P_{\theta\#}\nu). \]

Propriété métrique. \(\operatorname{SW}_p\) et \(\operatorname{MSW}_p\) sont des distances sur \(\mathcal P_p(\mathbb R^d)\).

Preuve. Minkowski dans \(L^p(\mathbb S^{d-1})\), combiné avec l'inégalité triangulaire unidimensionnelle, donne celle de \(\operatorname{SW}_p\). Si la distance est nulle, les lois projetées coïncident presque partout en \(\theta\), puis partout par continuité des fonctions caractéristiques. Le théorème de Cramér--Wold donne \(\mu=\nu\).

Comparaison avec \(W_p\)

Contraction par projection. \[ \operatorname{SW}_p(\mu,\nu)\le\operatorname{MSW}_p(\mu,\nu)\le W_p(\mu,\nu). \]

Preuve. Une moyenne \(L^p\) est dominée par le supremum, et \(P_\theta\) est \(1\)-lipschitzienne, donc contracte \(W_p\).

Une inégalité inverse demande des restrictions et ses constantes se dégradent généralement avec la dimension. Les distances projetées sont donc quantitativement plus faibles, bien qu'elles identifient la loi.

Représentation quantile

\[ \operatorname{SW}_p^p =\int_{\mathbb S^{d-1}}\int_0^1 |F_{\mu,\theta}^{-1}(u)-F_{\nu,\theta}^{-1}(u)|^p \,\mathrm du\,\mathrm d\sigma(\theta). \] Le couplage de grande dimension est remplacé par des tris directionnels.

Identifiabilité et transformée de Radon

Pour une densité \(f\), la densité projetée est \[ \mathcal Rf(\theta,t) =\int_{\theta^{\mathsf T}x=t}f(x)\,\mathrm d\mathcal H^{d-1}(x). \] Le théorème de la tranche de Fourier identifie sa transformée unidimensionnelle à \(\widehat f(s\theta)\). Toutes les projections déterminent donc la fonction caractéristique complète.

Comportement empirique

Réduction unidimensionnelle. \[ \mathbb E\operatorname{SW}_p^p(\widehat\mu_n,\mu) =\int\mathbb EW_p^p(P_{\theta\#}\widehat\mu_n,P_{\theta\#}\mu)\,\mathrm d\sigma(\theta). \] Toute borne unidimensionnelle uniforme en \(\theta\) se transfère donc sans barrière \(n^{-p/d}\).

Les constantes peuvent toutefois dépendre de la dimension par les moments projetés ou l'uniformité en direction. Le tranchage réduit la difficulté géométrique sans rendre toute statistique indépendante de la dimension.

Processus de Wasserstein tranché

L'inférence étudie le champ aléatoire \(\theta\mapsto W_p(P_{\theta\#}\widehat\mu_n,P_{\theta\#}\mu)\). Sa convergence uniforme demande un contrôle de processus empiriques des fonctions de répartition ou quantiles projetés. Les fonctionnelles moyenne, maximum et Monte Carlo ont alors des lois limites distinctes.

Approximation par directions aléatoires

Tranchage aléatoire. Pour \(\theta_\ell\) uniformes indépendantes, \[ \widehat{\operatorname{SW}}_{p,L}^p =\frac1L\sum_{\ell=1}^LW_p^p(P_{\theta_\ell\#}\mu,P_{\theta_\ell\#}\nu) \] est sans biais, avec erreur \(O_{\mathbb P}(L^{-1/2})\) si la variance directionnelle est finie.

Transport robuste par projection

Définition. \[ \operatorname{PRW}_{p,k}(\mu,\nu) =\sup_{E\in\operatorname{Gr}(k,d)} W_p(P_{E\#}\mu,P_{E\#}\nu). \] Pour \(k=1\), on retrouve le max-tranché; pour \(k=d\), \(W_p\).

Exemple gaussien

Gaussiennes projetées. \[ W_2^2(P_{\theta\#}\mu,P_{\theta\#}\nu) =\{\theta^{\mathsf T}(m_0-m_1)\}^2+ \left(\sqrt{\theta^{\mathsf T}\Sigma_0\theta} -\sqrt{\theta^{\mathsf T}\Sigma_1\theta}\right)^2. \]

Limites structurelles

Les couplages optimaux des projections ne sont pas nécessairement compatibles avec un couplage global. Une différence concentrée dans peu de directions peut être diluée par la moyenne, et un nombre fini de directions rend la distance calculée aléatoire. Ce sont des différences mathématiques, pas seulement numériques.

22. 高维投影型传输

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="返回" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="上一页" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/inference-for-transport-costs-maps-and-barycenters/" next_label="下一页" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/entropic-regularization/" %}

投影型距离把一个困难的高维传输问题替换为一族低维问题。其价值不仅在计算方面：一维传输具有显式公式，经验速率得到改善，而在保留所有方向时，克拉梅尔--沃尔德定理保证分布仍可识别。

切片瓦瑟斯坦距离

定义. 对 \(\theta\in\mathbb S^{d-1}\)，令 \(P_\theta(x)=\theta^{\mathsf T}x\)，\(\sigma\) 为球面上的归一化测度。定义 \[ \operatorname{SW}_p^p(\mu,\nu) =\int_{\mathbb S^{d-1}} W_p^p(P_{\theta\#}\mu,P_{\theta\#}\nu)\,\mathrm d\sigma(\theta). \] 最大切片距离为 \[ \operatorname{MSW}_p(\mu,\nu) =\sup_{\theta\in\mathbb S^{d-1}} W_p(P_{\theta\#}\mu,P_{\theta\#}\nu). \]

度量性质. \(\operatorname{SW}_p\) 与 \(\operatorname{MSW}_p\) 都是 \(\mathcal P_p(\mathbb R^d)\) 上的度量。

证明. 对每个方向使用一维 \(W_p\) 的三角不等式，再在球面 \(L^p\) 空间中应用闵可夫斯基不等式，得到切片三角不等式。若 \(\operatorname{SW}_p=0\)，则几乎每个方向的投影分布相同；特征函数关于方向的连续性把等式扩展到所有方向，克拉梅尔--沃尔德定理给出 \(\mu=\nu\)。最大切片情形类似。

与普通瓦瑟斯坦距离的比较

投影压缩. \[ \operatorname{SW}_p(\mu,\nu) \le\operatorname{MSW}_p(\mu,\nu) \le W_p(\mu,\nu). \]

证明. \(L^p\) 平均不超过上确界。每个 \(P_\theta\) 都是 \(1\)-利普希茨映射，任意耦合推前后立即给出 \(W_p(P_{\theta\#}\mu,P_{\theta\#}\nu)\le W_p(\mu,\nu)\)。

反向不等式需要紧支撑或正则性等限制，其常数通常随维数恶化。因此投影距离虽能识别分布，却在定量上弱于完整 \(W_p\)。

分位数表示

每个投影问题都是一维的： \[ \operatorname{SW}_p^p(\mu,\nu) = \int_{\mathbb S^{d-1}}\int_0^1 |F_{\mu,\theta}^{-1}(u)-F_{\nu,\theta}^{-1}(u)|^p \,\mathrm du\,\mathrm d\sigma(\theta). \] 高维耦合由各方向上的排序代替。

拉东变换与可识别性

若 \(\mu\) 的密度为 \(f\)，则投影密度是拉东变换 \[ \mathcal Rf(\theta,t) =\int_{\{x:\theta^{\mathsf T}x=t\}}f(x)\,\mathrm d\mathcal H^{d-1}(x). \] 傅里叶切片定理说明其关于 \(t\) 的一维傅里叶变换等于 \(\widehat f(s\theta)\)。因此全部投影分布决定完整特征函数。

经验行为

约化为一维速率. \[ \mathbb E\operatorname{SW}_p^p(\widehat\mu_n,\mu) = \int_{\mathbb S^{d-1}} \mathbb EW_p^p(P_{\theta\#}\widehat\mu_n,P_{\theta\#}\mu) \,\mathrm d\sigma(\theta). \] 因而任何关于 \(\theta\) 一致的一维上界都可直接转移，而不出现完整匹配的 \(n^{-p/d}\) 量化障碍。

证明. 投影与经验测度可交换： \[ P_{\theta\#}\widehat\mu_n =\frac1n\sum_i\delta_{\theta^{\mathsf T}X_i}. \] 再用托内利定理交换期望与球面积分。

常数仍可能通过投影矩或方向一致性依赖维数。切片缓解了几何匹配的维数灾难，却不能使全部高维统计完全与维数无关。

切片瓦瑟斯坦过程

推断需要研究随机场 \[ \theta\longmapsto W_p(P_{\theta\#}\widehat\mu_n,P_{\theta\#}\mu). \] 对 \(\theta\) 的一致弱收敛要求控制方向索引下的投影分布函数或分位数经验过程。对该过程取平均、上确界或随机方向积分，会产生不同极限分布；最大切片还包含估计最大方向的非光滑性。

方向的蒙特卡洛近似

随机切片. 若 \(\theta_1,\ldots,\theta_L\) 独立均匀分布于球面，则 \[ \widehat{\operatorname{SW}}_{p,L}^p =\frac1L\sum_{\ell=1}^L W_p^p(P_{\theta_\ell\#}\mu,P_{\theta_\ell\#}\nu) \] 是 \(\operatorname{SW}_p^p\) 的无偏估计。若方向积分被积函数具有有限方差，则方向蒙特卡洛误差为 \(O_{\mathbb P}(L^{-1/2})\)。

投影鲁棒传输

定义. 对 \(1\le k\le d\)，定义 \[ \operatorname{PRW}_{p,k}(\mu,\nu) = \sup_{E\in\operatorname{Gr}(k,d)} W_p(P_{E\#}\mu,P_{E\#}\nu). \] \(k=1\) 时为最大切片距离，\(k=d\) 时恢复普通 \(W_p\)。

参数 \(k\) 在统计可处理性与几何保真度之间插值。对 \(E\) 的优化是非凸的，数据依赖的最大子空间还会引入额外统计不确定性。

高斯例子

投影高斯分布. 若 \(\mu=N(m_0,\Sigma_0)\)、\(\nu=N(m_1,\Sigma_1)\)，则 \[ W_2^2(P_{\theta\#}\mu,P_{\theta\#}\nu) = \{\theta^{\mathsf T}(m_0-m_1)\}^2 + \left( \sqrt{\theta^{\mathsf T}\Sigma_0\theta} - \sqrt{\theta^{\mathsf T}\Sigma_1\theta} \right)^2. \] 切片距离平均方向差异，最大切片距离则寻找均值或方差差异最大的方向。

局限

各方向的一维最优耦合未必能组合成 \(\mathbb R^d\) 中的单个联合耦合，因此切片测地线通常不对应一个全局流。只集中在少数方向的差异可能被平均稀释；有限随机方向又使计算量本身成为随机近似。这些都是结构差异，而不只是实现细节。
