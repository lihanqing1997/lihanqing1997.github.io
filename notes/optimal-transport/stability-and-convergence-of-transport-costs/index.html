Stability and convergence of transport costs records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the optimal transport note.

Definition

Definition. Stability asks when convergence of measures implies convergence of optimal values and optimizers.

Proposition

Proposition. Under weak convergence plus uniform moment control, Wasserstein distances and optimal transport costs with appropriate growth conditions are stable. Optimal plans are tight and subsequential limits are optimal.

Proof

Proof. Use tightness of couplings, lower semicontinuity for the liminf inequality, and recovery couplings or gluing for the limsup inequality.

Remark

Remark. Stability is essential when transport is estimated from data or approximated computationally.
