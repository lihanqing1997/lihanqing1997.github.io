1. Transport plans and couplings

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/monge-problem/" %}

Transport plans and couplings records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the optimal transport note.

Definition

Definition. For probability measures \(\mu\) on \(X\) and \(\nu\) on \(Y\), a coupling is a probability measure \(\pi\) on \(X\times Y\) with marginals \(\mu\) and \(\nu\).

Proposition

Proposition. The set \(\Pi(\mu,\nu)\) of couplings is convex and weakly compact under tightness hypotheses such as Polish spaces with tight marginals.

Proof

Proof. Convexity is immediate from linearity of marginals. Compactness follows from Prokhorov tightness and closedness of the marginal constraints under weak convergence.

Remark

Remark. Couplings are the relaxed objects of optimal transport. They allow splitting mass and make existence theorems possible.
