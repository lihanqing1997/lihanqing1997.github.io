10. Displacement interpolation

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/brenier-theorem/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/benamou-brenier-formula/" %}

Displacement interpolation records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the optimal transport note.

Definition

Definition. Displacement interpolation moves mass along optimal transport geodesics rather than linearly mixing measures. For quadratic cost, it is \(\mu_t=((1-t)\operatorname{id}+tT)_\#\mu_0\).

Proposition

Proposition. When \(T\) is an optimal map between \(\mu_0\) and \(\mu_1\), the curve \((\mu_t)_{t\in[0,1]}\) is a constant-speed geodesic in \(W_2\).

Proof

Proof. Construct the dynamic coupling by sending each particle along the straight segment from \(x\) to \(T(x)\). Optimality of subsegments follows from cyclical monotonicity.

Remark

Remark. Displacement interpolation is why Wasserstein space has a geometry distinct from linear mixture geometry.
