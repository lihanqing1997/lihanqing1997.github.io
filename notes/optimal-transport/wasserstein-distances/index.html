11. Wasserstein distances and the topology of probability measures

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/regularity-of-optimal-maps-and-general-costs/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/displacement-interpolation/" %}

A Wasserstein distance does more than assign a transport cost. It equips a space of probability measures with a metric that simultaneously records weak convergence and control of tails. The geometry developed later rests on this topology.

Definition and finiteness

Definition. Let \((X,d)\) be Polish and \(1\le p<\infty\). For \(\mu,\nu\in\mathcal P_p(X)\), define \[ W_p(\mu,\nu) =\left( \inf_{\pi\in\Pi(\mu,\nu)} \int_{X\times X}d(x,y)^p\,\mathrm d\pi(x,y) \right)^{1/p}. \] An optimizer exists because \(d^p\) is continuous and nonnegative.

Finiteness. For any base point \(x_0\), \[ W_p(\mu,\nu)^p \le 2^{p-1}\left( \int d(x,x_0)^p\,\mathrm d\mu +\int d(y,x_0)^p\,\mathrm d\nu \right). \]

Proof. Use the product coupling \(\mu\otimes\nu\) and the inequality \(d(x,y)^p\le2^{p-1}\{d(x,x_0)^p+d(y,x_0)^p\}\).

The metric axioms

Theorem. \(W_p\) is a metric on \(\mathcal P_p(X)\).

Proof. Nonnegativity and symmetry are immediate. If \(W_p(\mu,\nu)=0\), an optimal plan \(\pi\) satisfies \(d(x,y)=0\) almost surely, so it is concentrated on the diagonal and its two marginals coincide.

For the triangle inequality, choose \(\pi_{12}\in\Pi(\mu_1,\mu_2)\) and \(\pi_{23}\in\Pi(\mu_2,\mu_3)\), and glue them to \(\gamma\) on \(X^3\). Its \((1,3)\)-marginal is a coupling of \(\mu_1,\mu_3\). Minkowski's inequality gives \[ \begin{aligned} W_p(\mu_1,\mu_3) &\le\left(\int d(x_1,x_3)^p\,\mathrm d\gamma\right)^{1/p}\\ &\le\left(\int d(x_1,x_2)^p\,\mathrm d\gamma\right)^{1/p} +\left(\int d(x_2,x_3)^p\,\mathrm d\gamma\right)^{1/p}. \end{aligned} \] Taking infima over the two plans proves \(W_p(\mu_1,\mu_3)\le W_p(\mu_1,\mu_2)+W_p(\mu_2,\mu_3)\).

Elementary comparisons

Proposition. If \(1\le p\le q\) and \(\mu,\nu\in\mathcal P_q(X)\), then \[ W_p(\mu,\nu)\le W_q(\mu,\nu). \] Also, \[ W_p(\mu,\delta_{x_0})^p=\int d(x,x_0)^p\,\mathrm d\mu(x). \]

Proof. For every coupling, the \(L^p\)-norm of \(d(x,y)\) is at most its \(L^q\)-norm; take infima. A coupling with \(\delta_{x_0}\) is unique, namely \(\mu(\mathrm dx)\delta_{x_0}(\mathrm dy)\), giving the second formula.

On a space of finite diameter \(D\), interpolation gives \(W_q^q\le D^{q-p}W_p^p\). On unbounded spaces no reverse comparison is possible without stronger moment bounds.

Characterization of \(W_p\)-convergence

Topology theorem. For \(\mu_n,\mu\in\mathcal P_p(X)\), the following are equivalent:

(i) \(W_p(\mu_n,\mu)\to0\).

(ii) \(\mu_n\Rightarrow\mu\) and, for one base point \(x_0\), \[ \int d(x,x_0)^p\,\mathrm d\mu_n(x) \to \int d(x,x_0)^p\,\mathrm d\mu(x). \]

(iii) \(\mu_n\Rightarrow\mu\) and the \(p\)-th moments are uniformly integrable: \[ \lim_{R\to\infty}\sup_n \int_{\{d(x,x_0)>R\}}d(x,x_0)^p\,\mathrm d\mu_n(x)=0. \]

Proof. If \(W_p(\mu_n,\mu)\to0\), take nearly optimal couplings \((X_n,X)\). Then \(d(X_n,X)\to0\) in \(L^p\), hence in probability, which implies weak convergence. The reverse triangle inequality \[ |d(X_n,x_0)-d(X,x_0)|\le d(X_n,X) \] and the \(L^p\) norm inequality give convergence of \(p\)-th moment norms and uniform integrability.

Conversely, weak convergence admits couplings with \(X_n\to X\) almost surely by the Skorokhod representation theorem. Condition (iii) makes \(d(X_n,X)^p\) uniformly integrable because it is bounded by \(2^{p-1}\{d(X_n,x_0)^p+d(X,x_0)^p\}\). Vitali's theorem yields \(\mathbb E d(X_n,X)^p\to0\), hence \(W_p(\mu_n,\mu)\to0\). Equivalence of (ii) and (iii) follows by truncating the moment function and using weak convergence on bounded continuous truncations.

Completeness and separability

Theorem. If \((X,d)\) is complete and separable, then \((\mathcal P_p(X),W_p)\) is complete and separable.

Proof. For completeness, take a \(W_p\)-Cauchy sequence and extract a subsequence with \(W_p(\mu_{n_k},\mu_{n_{k+1}})\le2^{-k}\). Glue nearly optimal successive couplings to random variables \(X_k\) on one space. Then \[ \sum_k\|d(X_k,X_{k+1})\|_{L^p}<\infty. \] Hence \(X_k\) is almost surely Cauchy along a further argument and converges to an \(X\)-valued variable \(X_\infty\), using completeness of \(X\). Minkowski and tail summation give \(\|d(X_k,X_\infty)\|_{L^p}\to0\), so \(W_p(\mu_{n_k},\mathcal L(X_\infty))\to0\). The Cauchy property brings the full sequence to the same limit.

For separability, choose a countable dense subset \(D\subset X\). Finitely supported probability measures on \(D\) with rational weights form a countable family. Truncation to a compact high-mass set, quantization by nearby points of \(D\), and rational approximation of weights show that this family is \(W_p\)-dense.

Relative compactness

Compactness criterion. A family \(\mathcal K\subset\mathcal P_p(X)\) is relatively compact in \(W_p\) if it is tight and its \(p\)-th moments are uniformly integrable. On a proper metric space, a uniform bound on a moment of order \(q>p\) implies these conditions.

The extra tail condition is essential. Weak compactness alone permits a small amount of mass to escape to infinity while carrying nonvanishing \(p\)-transport cost, as the example on page 3 showed.

The \(W_\infty\) distance

For bounded-support measures one may also define \[ W_\infty(\mu,\nu)=\inf_{\pi\in\Pi(\mu,\nu)} \operatorname*{ess\,sup}_{(x,y)\sim\pi}d(x,y). \] It minimizes the largest displacement rather than an average displacement. It is the limit of \(W_p\) as \(p\to\infty\) under suitable boundedness assumptions, but its topology and geodesics can be more rigid.

11. Distances de Wasserstein et topologie des mesures de probabilité

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Retour" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Page précédente" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/regularity-of-optimal-maps-and-general-costs/" next_label="Page suivante" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/displacement-interpolation/" %}

Une distance de Wasserstein ne se contente pas de mesurer un coût. Elle munit un espace de probabilités d'une métrique qui encode simultanément convergence étroite et contrôle des queues.

Définition et finitude

Définition. Sur un espace polonais \((X,d)\), pour \(1\le p<\infty\), \[ W_p(\mu,\nu) =\left(\inf_{\pi\in\Pi(\mu,\nu)} \int d(x,y)^p\,\mathrm d\pi(x,y)\right)^{1/p}. \] Un minimiseur existe, car \(d^p\) est continu et positif.

Finitude. Pour tout \(x_0\), \[ W_p^p(\mu,\nu)\le2^{p-1}\left( \int d(x,x_0)^p\,\mathrm d\mu+ \int d(y,x_0)^p\,\mathrm d\nu\right). \]

Preuve. On utilise le couplage produit et \(d(x,y)^p\le2^{p-1}\{d(x,x_0)^p+d(y,x_0)^p\}\).

Axiomes métriques

Théorème. \(W_p\) est une distance sur \(\mathcal P_p(X)\).

Preuve. Positivité et symétrie sont immédiates. Si \(W_p=0\), un plan optimal est porté par la diagonale, donc ses marginales sont égales.

Pour l'inégalité triangulaire, recollons \(\pi_{12}\in\Pi(\mu_1,\mu_2)\) et \(\pi_{23}\in\Pi(\mu_2,\mu_3)\) en une mesure \(\gamma\) sur \(X^3\). Sa marginale \((1,3)\) est admissible et l'inégalité de Minkowski donne \[ W_p(\mu_1,\mu_3) \le\|d(x_1,x_2)+d(x_2,x_3)\|_{L^p(\gamma)} \le\|d(x_1,x_2)\|_{L^p}+\|d(x_2,x_3)\|_{L^p}. \] L'infimum sur les deux plans conclut.

Comparaisons élémentaires

Proposition. Si \(1\le p\le q\), alors \(W_p\le W_q\), et \[ W_p(\mu,\delta_{x_0})^p=\int d(x,x_0)^p\,\mathrm d\mu(x). \]

Preuve. Sur un espace probabilisé, la norme \(L^p\) est dominée par la norme \(L^q\); on prend l'infimum sur les couplages. Le couplage avec une masse de Dirac est unique, ce qui donne la seconde formule.

Caractérisation de la convergence

Théorème topologique. Les propriétés suivantes sont équivalentes :

(i) \(W_p(\mu_n,\mu)\to0\).

(ii) \(\mu_n\Rightarrow\mu\) et les moments d'ordre \(p\) convergent.

(iii) \(\mu_n\Rightarrow\mu\) et \[ \lim_{R\to\infty}\sup_n\int_{\{d(x,x_0)>R\}}d(x,x_0)^p\,\mathrm d\mu_n=0. \]

Preuve. Si \(W_p\to0\), des couplages presque optimaux donnent \(d(X_n,X)\to0\) dans \(L^p\), donc en probabilité et en loi. L'inégalité \(|d(X_n,x_0)-d(X,x_0)|\le d(X_n,X)\) donne la convergence des moments et l'intégrabilité uniforme.

Réciproquement, le théorème de représentation de Skorokhod fournit un couplage tel que \(X_n\to X\) presque sûrement. L'intégrabilité uniforme des moments entraîne celle de \(d(X_n,X)^p\); le théorème de Vitali donne \(\mathbb E d(X_n,X)^p\to0\), donc \(W_p\to0\). L'équivalence des deux formulations de moments s'obtient par troncature.

Complétude et séparabilité

Théorème. Si \(X\) est complet et séparable, \((\mathcal P_p(X),W_p)\) l'est aussi.

Preuve. D'une suite de Cauchy, extrayons \(\mu_{n_k}\) telle que \(W_p(\mu_{n_k},\mu_{n_{k+1}})\le2^{-k}\). Recolons des couplages presque optimaux pour obtenir des variables \(X_k\) sur un même espace avec \(\sum_k\|d(X_k,X_{k+1})\|_{L^p}<\infty\). Elles convergent vers une variable \(X_\infty\), et la somme des queues donne \(W_p(\mu_{n_k},\mathcal L(X_\infty))\to0\). La propriété de Cauchy traite la suite entière.

Pour la séparabilité, les mesures finies à poids rationnels portées par un sous-ensemble dense dénombrable forment une famille dénombrable et dense, par troncature, quantification et approximation des poids.

Compacité relative

Critère de compacité. Une famille de \(\mathcal P_p(X)\) est relativement compacte pour \(W_p\) si elle est tendue et si ses moments d'ordre \(p\) sont uniformément intégrables. Sur un espace propre, une borne uniforme sur un moment d'ordre \(q>p\) suffit.

Distance \(W_\infty\)

Pour des mesures à support borné, \[ W_\infty(\mu,\nu)=\inf_{\pi\in\Pi(\mu,\nu)} \operatorname*{ess\,sup}_{(x,y)\sim\pi}d(x,y). \] Elle minimise le déplacement maximal. Sous des hypothèses de bornitude, elle est la limite de \(W_p\) lorsque \(p\to\infty\), mais sa géométrie est plus rigide.

11. 瓦瑟斯坦距离与概率测度的拓扑

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="返回" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="上一页" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/regularity-of-optimal-maps-and-general-costs/" next_label="下一页" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/displacement-interpolation/" %}

瓦瑟斯坦距离不只是一个传输代价。它在概率测度空间上定义度量，同时记录弱收敛与尾部控制。后续的几何理论都建立在这一拓扑之上。

定义与有限性

定义. 设 \((X,d)\) 为波兰空间，\(1\le p<\infty\)。对 \(\mu,\nu\in\mathcal P_p(X)\)，定义 \[ W_p(\mu,\nu) =\left(\inf_{\pi\in\Pi(\mu,\nu)} \int d(x,y)^p\,\mathrm d\pi(x,y)\right)^{1/p}. \] 由于 \(d^p\) 连续非负，最优计划存在。

有限性. 对任意基点 \(x_0\)， \[ W_p^p(\mu,\nu)\le2^{p-1}\left( \int d(x,x_0)^p\,\mathrm d\mu+ \int d(y,x_0)^p\,\mathrm d\nu\right). \]

证明. 对乘积耦合使用 \(d(x,y)^p\le2^{p-1}\{d(x,x_0)^p+d(y,x_0)^p\}\)。

度量公理

定理. \(W_p\) 是 \(\mathcal P_p(X)\) 上的度量。

证明. 非负性与对称性显然。若 \(W_p(\mu,\nu)=0\)，最优计划满足 \(d(x,y)=0\) 几乎处处，故集中于对角线，两边缘相同。

为证三角不等式，拼接 \(\pi_{12}\in\Pi(\mu_1,\mu_2)\) 与 \(\pi_{23}\in\Pi(\mu_2,\mu_3)\) 得到 \(X^3\) 上的 \(\gamma\)。其 \((1,3)\) 边缘耦合 \(\mu_1,\mu_3\)。闵可夫斯基不等式给出 \[ W_p(\mu_1,\mu_3) \le\|d(x_1,x_2)+d(x_2,x_3)\|_{L^p(\gamma)} \le\|d(x_1,x_2)\|_{L^p}+\|d(x_2,x_3)\|_{L^p}. \] 对两计划取下确界即得结论。

基本比较

命题. 若 \(1\le p\le q\)，则 \(W_p(\mu,\nu)\le W_q(\mu,\nu)\)，并且 \[ W_p(\mu,\delta_{x_0})^p=\int d(x,x_0)^p\,\mathrm d\mu(x). \]

证明. 在概率空间上，随机变量的 \(L^p\) 范数不超过 \(L^q\) 范数；对耦合取下确界。与狄拉克测度的耦合唯一，因此第二式直接成立。

\(W_p\) 收敛的刻画

拓扑定理. 下列条件等价：

(i) \(W_p(\mu_n,\mu)\to0\)。

(ii) \(\mu_n\Rightarrow\mu\)，且 \(p\) 阶矩收敛。

(iii) \(\mu_n\Rightarrow\mu\)，且 \(p\) 阶矩一致可积： \[ \lim_{R\to\infty}\sup_n \int_{\{d(x,x_0)>R\}}d(x,x_0)^p\,\mathrm d\mu_n=0. \]

证明. 若 \(W_p\to0\)，取近似最优耦合 \((X_n,X)\)，则 \(d(X_n,X)\to0\) 于 \(L^p\)，从而依概率收敛并推出弱收敛。反三角不等式 \[ |d(X_n,x_0)-d(X,x_0)|\le d(X_n,X) \] 给出矩收敛与一致可积性。

反之，由斯科罗霍德表示定理，可在同一空间上实现 \(X_n\to X\) 几乎处处。条件 (iii) 使 \(d(X_n,X)^p\) 一致可积，因为它被 \(2^{p-1}\{d(X_n,x_0)^p+d(X,x_0)^p\}\) 控制。维塔利定理给出 \(\mathbb E d(X_n,X)^p\to0\)，故 \(W_p\to0\)。条件 (ii) 与 (iii) 的等价性由截断矩函数得到。

完备性与可分性

定理. 若 \((X,d)\) 完备且可分，则 \((\mathcal P_p(X),W_p)\) 也完备且可分。

证明. 对 \(W_p\)-柯西列抽取子列，使 \(W_p(\mu_{n_k},\mu_{n_{k+1}})\le2^{-k}\)。拼接相邻近似最优耦合，得到同一空间上的 \(X_k\)，满足 \(\sum_k\|d(X_k,X_{k+1})\|_{L^p}<\infty\)。于是 \(X_k\) 收敛到 \(X_\infty\)，尾和估计给出 \(W_p(\mu_{n_k},\mathcal L(X_\infty))\to0\)，再由柯西性得到全列收敛。

为证可分，取 \(X\) 的可数稠密子集。支撑在该子集上、具有有理权重的有限支撑概率测度构成可数族；通过截断、量化和权重有理逼近可证明其在 \(W_p\) 下稠密。

相对紧性

紧性判据. \(\mathcal P_p(X)\) 中的测度族若一致紧且 \(p\) 阶矩一致可积，则在 \(W_p\) 中相对紧。在恰当度量空间上，一致有界的 \(q>p\) 阶矩蕴含这两个条件。

\(W_\infty\) 距离

对有界支撑测度，还可定义 \[ W_\infty(\mu,\nu)=\inf_{\pi\in\Pi(\mu,\nu)} \operatorname*{ess\,sup}_{(x,y)\sim\pi}d(x,y). \] 它最小化最大位移而非平均位移。在适当有界条件下，它是 \(p\to\infty\) 时 \(W_p\) 的极限，但其拓扑与测地线通常更为刚性。
