7. Wasserstein distances

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/cyclical-monotonicity/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/one-dimensional-transport/" %}

Wasserstein distances records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the optimal transport note.

Definition

Definition. For \(p\ge1\), the Wasserstein distance is \(W_p(\mu,\nu)=\inf_{\pi\in\Pi(\mu,\nu)}(\int d(x,y)^p\,\mathrm{d}\pi)^{1/p}\), on measures with finite \(p\)-moment.

Proposition

Proposition. The function \(W_p\) is a metric on the space of probability measures with finite \(p\)-moment. Convergence in \(W_p\) is weak convergence plus convergence of \(p\)-moments.

Proof

Proof. The triangle inequality is proved by gluing optimal or nearly optimal couplings and applying Minkowski inequality. The convergence characterization uses truncation and tightness.

Remark

Remark. Wasserstein distance metrizes not only distributional closeness but also geometry of the underlying space.
