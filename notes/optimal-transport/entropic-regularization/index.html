23. Entropic regularization and Sinkhorn scaling

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/projection-based-transport-in-high-dimensions/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/sinkhorn-divergences-and-semi-discrete-transport/" %}

Entropy turns the linear transport problem into a strictly convex smooth problem. Its optimizer has a multiplicative Gibbs form, and enforcing the two marginals reduces to alternating diagonal scalings. The regularization is therefore simultaneously variational, probabilistic, and algorithmic.

The entropic transport problem

Definition. For \(\varepsilon>0\), define \[ \operatorname{OT}_\varepsilon(\mu,\nu) = \inf_{\pi\in\Pi(\mu,\nu)} \left\{ \int c\,\mathrm d\pi +\varepsilon\operatorname{KL}(\pi\,\|\,\mu\otimes\nu) \right\}. \] The reference \(\mu\otimes\nu\) makes the entropy term invariant under changes of coordinates and equal to the mutual information of a coupling.

Existence and uniqueness. If the feasible set contains a finite-objective plan and \(c\) is bounded below with the usual lower-semicontinuity assumptions, the entropic problem has a unique minimizer \(\pi_\varepsilon\).

Proof. Compactness of \(\Pi(\mu,\nu)\), lower semicontinuity of the cost integral, and lower semicontinuity of relative entropy give existence by the direct method. Relative entropy is strictly convex on densities with respect to \(\mu\otimes\nu\), while the cost is linear. Two distinct finite-objective minimizers would have a midpoint with strictly smaller entropy and the same averaged cost, contradicting minimality.

Dual formulation

Entropic duality. Under integrability conditions, \[ \operatorname{OT}_\varepsilon(\mu,\nu) = \sup_{\varphi,\psi} \left[ \int\varphi\,\mathrm d\mu+\int\psi\,\mathrm d\nu -\varepsilon\int \left\{ e^{(\varphi(x)+\psi(y)-c(x,y))/\varepsilon}-1 \right\} \,\mathrm d\mu(x)\,\mathrm d\nu(y) \right]. \] At optimality, \[ \frac{\mathrm d\pi_\varepsilon}{\mathrm d(\mu\otimes\nu)}(x,y) = \exp\!\left(\frac{\varphi_\varepsilon(x)+\psi_\varepsilon(y)-c(x,y)}{\varepsilon}\right). \]

Proof. Write \(\pi=r\,\mu\otimes\nu\) and introduce multipliers \(\varphi,\psi\) for the marginals. Pointwise minimization in \(r\ge0\) uses the convex conjugate \[ \sup_{r\ge0}\{sr-\varepsilon(r\log r-r+1)\} =\varepsilon(e^{s/\varepsilon}-1). \] Substituting \(s=\varphi+\psi-c\) gives the dual. Equality in the conjugacy relation gives the exponential density of the optimizer.

The Schrödinger system

Let \(K_\varepsilon(x,y)=e^{-c(x,y)/\varepsilon}\), \(u(x)=e^{\varphi_\varepsilon(x)/\varepsilon}\), and \(v(y)=e^{\psi_\varepsilon(y)/\varepsilon}\). Then \[ \pi_\varepsilon(\mathrm dx,\mathrm dy) =u(x)K_\varepsilon(x,y)v(y)\,\mu(\mathrm dx)\nu(\mathrm dy). \] The marginal constraints become \[ u(x)\int K_\varepsilon(x,y)v(y)\,\mathrm d\nu(y)=1, \qquad v(y)\int K_\varepsilon(x,y)u(x)\,\mathrm d\mu(x)=1. \] This pair of nonlinear integral equations is the Schrödinger system.

Discrete matrix scaling

For discrete marginals \(a\in\Delta_m\), \(b\in\Delta_n\), let \(K_{ij}=e^{-C_{ij}/\varepsilon}>0\). Absorbing the marginal weights into scaling vectors, the unique optimizer has form \[ P_\varepsilon=\operatorname{diag}(u)K\operatorname{diag}(v), \] with \[ u\odot(Kv)=a,\qquad v\odot(K^{\mathsf T}u)=b. \]

Sinkhorn iterations. Starting from \(v^{(0)}>0\), alternate \[ u^{(k+1)}=a\oslash(Kv^{(k)}), \qquad v^{(k+1)}=b\oslash(K^{\mathsf T}u^{(k+1)}), \] where \(\oslash\) is componentwise division. Every row update enforces the first marginal exactly, and every column update enforces the second.

Why Sinkhorn converges

Sinkhorn scaling theorem. If \(K\) has strictly positive entries and \(a,b\) are strictly positive probability vectors, there exist positive \(u,v\), unique up to \((u,v)\mapsto(\lambda u,\lambda^{-1}v)\), such that \(\operatorname{diag}(u)K\operatorname{diag}(v)\in U(a,b)\). Sinkhorn iterates converge to this scaled matrix.

Proof perspectives. In dual coordinates, each update exactly maximizes the strictly concave dual objective in one block of potentials. The objective increases and is bounded above; strict concavity modulo additive constants identifies the limit. Equivalently, each update is a Kullback--Leibler projection onto one affine marginal set. Alternating Bregman projections converge to the unique intersection minimizer. A quantitative proof uses contraction of the positive kernel in Hilbert's projective metric.

Small-regularization limit

Zero-temperature limit. As \(\varepsilon\downarrow0\), \[ \operatorname{OT}_\varepsilon(\mu,\nu)\longrightarrow\operatorname{OT}_0(\mu,\nu). \] Every weak limit point of \(\pi_\varepsilon\) is an unregularized optimal plan. If several unregularized optimizers exist, \(\pi_\varepsilon\) selects the one minimizing \(\operatorname{KL}(\pi\,\|\,\mu\otimes\nu)\), when this selection problem is finite.

Proof. Since the entropy term is nonnegative, \(\operatorname{OT}_\varepsilon\ge\operatorname{OT}_0\). Evaluate the regularized objective at a finite-entropy optimal plan, or approximate an optimal plan by finite-entropy plans, to obtain the limsup inequality. Compactness gives limit points of \(\pi_\varepsilon\); lower semicontinuity of the cost makes them optimal. Comparing two unregularized optimizers at order \(\varepsilon\) gives the entropy selection rule.

Large-regularization limit

As \(\varepsilon\to\infty\), minimizing mutual information dominates the cost. The unique zero-information coupling is \(\mu\otimes\nu\), so \(\pi_\varepsilon\Rightarrow\mu\otimes\nu\) under mild conditions. Entropic transport therefore interpolates between optimal geometric dependence and independence.

Smooth dependence on the marginals

For fixed \(\varepsilon>0\) and positive kernels, dual potentials are unique modulo constants and vary smoothly with positive discrete marginals. The envelope theorem gives \[ \nabla_a\operatorname{OT}_\varepsilon=\varphi_\varepsilon, \qquad \nabla_b\operatorname{OT}_\varepsilon=\psi_\varepsilon \] in normalized coordinates. This smoothness is the main statistical and optimization advantage of regularization, but it becomes poorly conditioned as \(\varepsilon\downarrow0\).

Log-domain stabilization

When \(\varepsilon\) is small, entries of \(K=e^{-C/\varepsilon}\) may underflow. Writing \(u=e^{f/\varepsilon}\), \(v=e^{g/\varepsilon}\) transforms scaling into log-sum-exp updates: \[ f_i=\varepsilon\log a_i-\varepsilon\log\sum_j \exp\!\left(\frac{g_j-C_{ij}}{\varepsilon}\right). \] Subtracting additive constants from \(f,g\) preserves the plan and prevents numerical overflow. This is the stable realization of the dual gauge invariance.

Regularization bias

\(\operatorname{OT}_\varepsilon(\mu,\mu)\) is generally positive, so the regularized cost is not a distance and is biased toward diffuse couplings. Smaller \(\varepsilon\) reduces geometric bias but slows scaling and increases statistical and numerical sensitivity. The next page studies debiasing and separates the fully discrete and semi-discrete computational geometries.

23. Régularisation entropique et équilibrage de Sinkhorn

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Retour" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Page précédente" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/projection-based-transport-in-high-dimensions/" next_label="Page suivante" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/sinkhorn-divergences-and-semi-discrete-transport/" %}

L'entropie transforme le problème linéaire en un problème strictement convexe et lisse. L'optimiseur possède une forme multiplicative de Gibbs, et l'imposition des deux marginales devient un équilibrage diagonal alterné.

Problème entropique

Définition. \[ \operatorname{OT}_\varepsilon(\mu,\nu) =\inf_{\pi\in\Pi(\mu,\nu)} \left\{\int c\,\mathrm d\pi +\varepsilon\operatorname{KL}(\pi\,\|\,\mu\otimes\nu)\right\}. \]

Existence et unicité. Sous les hypothèses usuelles de semi-continuité et de finitude, le problème admet un unique minimiseur \(\pi_\varepsilon\).

Preuve. La compacité de \(\Pi(\mu,\nu)\) et la semi-continuité des deux termes donnent l'existence. L'entropie relative est strictement convexe sur les densités, tandis que le coût est linéaire; la moyenne de deux minimiseurs distincts aurait donc une valeur plus faible.

Formulation duale

Dualité entropique. \[ \operatorname{OT}_\varepsilon = \sup_{\varphi,\psi}\left[ \int\varphi\,\mathrm d\mu+\int\psi\,\mathrm d\nu -\varepsilon\int\left\{ e^{(\varphi+\psi-c)/\varepsilon}-1\right\}\mathrm d\mu\,\mathrm d\nu \right], \] et \[ \frac{\mathrm d\pi_\varepsilon}{\mathrm d(\mu\otimes\nu)} =e^{(\varphi_\varepsilon+\psi_\varepsilon-c)/\varepsilon}. \]

Preuve. Écrivons \(\pi=r\,\mu\otimes\nu\), introduisons les multiplicateurs des marginales, puis minimisons ponctuellement en \(r\). La conjugaison \[ \sup_{r\ge0}\{sr-\varepsilon(r\log r-r+1)\} =\varepsilon(e^{s/\varepsilon}-1) \] donne le dual et l'égalité de conjugaison donne la densité exponentielle.

Système de Schrödinger

Avec \(K_\varepsilon=e^{-c/\varepsilon}\), \(u=e^{\varphi/\varepsilon}\), \(v=e^{\psi/\varepsilon}\), \[ \pi_\varepsilon(\mathrm dx,\mathrm dy) =u(x)K_\varepsilon(x,y)v(y)\,\mu(\mathrm dx)\nu(\mathrm dy), \] et les marginales imposent \[ u(x)\int K_\varepsilon(x,y)v(y)\,\mathrm d\nu(y)=1,\quad v(y)\int K_\varepsilon(x,y)u(x)\,\mathrm d\mu(x)=1. \]

Équilibrage matriciel discret

Pour \(K_{ij}=e^{-C_{ij}/\varepsilon}\), \[ P_\varepsilon=\operatorname{diag}(u)K\operatorname{diag}(v), \] avec \(u\odot(Kv)=a\), \(v\odot(K^{\mathsf T}u)=b\).

Itérations de Sinkhorn. \[ u^{(k+1)}=a\oslash(Kv^{(k)}),\qquad v^{(k+1)}=b\oslash(K^{\mathsf T}u^{(k+1)}). \]

Convergence de Sinkhorn

Théorème d'équilibrage. Si \(K,a,b\) sont strictement positifs, il existe des facteurs positifs \(u,v\), uniques à la transformation \((u,v)\mapsto(\lambda u,\lambda^{-1}v)\) près, et les itérations convergent vers la matrice équilibrée unique.

Trois lectures de la preuve. Chaque mise à jour maximise exactement le dual concave dans un bloc. Elle est aussi une projection de Kullback--Leibler sur un ensemble affine de marginale; les projections de Bregman alternées convergent vers l'intersection. Enfin, la métrique projective de Hilbert fournit une contraction quantitative du noyau positif.

Limite de faible régularisation

Limite de température nulle. Lorsque \(\varepsilon\downarrow0\), \[ \operatorname{OT}_\varepsilon\to\operatorname{OT}_0. \] Toute limite de \(\pi_\varepsilon\) est un plan optimal non régularisé; parmi plusieurs optimiseurs, l'entropie sélectionne celui de plus faible \(\operatorname{KL}(\cdot\,\|\,\mu\otimes\nu)\).

Preuve. L'entropie positive donne la borne inférieure. L'évaluation en un plan optimal d'entropie finie, ou son approximation, donne la borne supérieure. Compacité et semi-continuité identifient les limites. La comparaison au premier ordre en \(\varepsilon\) donne la sélection entropique.

Grande régularisation et indépendance

Lorsque \(\varepsilon\to\infty\), le terme d'information mutuelle domine. Son unique minimum est le couplage indépendant \(\mu\otimes\nu\); le plan régularisé interpole donc entre dépendance géométrique optimale et indépendance.

Dépendance lisse et stabilisation logarithmique

À \(\varepsilon\) fixé, les potentiels varient régulièrement avec les marginales positives et sont les gradients de la valeur régularisée. Pour éviter le sous-flux numérique quand \(\varepsilon\) est petit, on travaille avec \(u=e^{f/\varepsilon}\), \(v=e^{g/\varepsilon}\) et des mises à jour log-sum-exp. L'ajout de constantes opposées à \(f,g\) exprime l'invariance de jauge.

Biais de régularisation

\(\operatorname{OT}_\varepsilon(\mu,\mu)\) est généralement positif : le coût régularisé n'est pas une distance. Diminuer \(\varepsilon\) réduit le biais géométrique mais dégrade conditionnement, vitesse de convergence et stabilité statistique. La page suivante étudie le débiaisage.

23. 熵正则化与辛克霍恩缩放

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="返回" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="上一页" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/projection-based-transport-in-high-dimensions/" next_label="下一页" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/sinkhorn-divergences-and-semi-discrete-transport/" %}

熵把线性传输问题变成严格凸且光滑的问题。其最优解具有乘法吉布斯形式，而施加两个边缘约束可以化为交替对角缩放。因此该正则化同时具有变分、概率与算法含义。

熵正则传输问题

定义. 对 \(\varepsilon>0\)，定义 \[ \operatorname{OT}_\varepsilon(\mu,\nu) = \inf_{\pi\in\Pi(\mu,\nu)} \left\{ \int c\,\mathrm d\pi +\varepsilon\operatorname{KL}(\pi\,\|\,\mu\otimes\nu) \right\}. \] 以 \(\mu\otimes\nu\) 为参照使熵项在坐标变换下保持自然，并且它就是耦合的互信息。

存在性与唯一性. 若可行集中存在有限目标值计划，且 \(c\) 满足通常的下半连续和下界条件，则熵正则问题存在唯一极小化解 \(\pi_\varepsilon\)。

证明. \(\Pi(\mu,\nu)\) 的紧性、代价积分的下半连续性和相对熵的下半连续性给出存在性。相对熵关于相对于 \(\mu\otimes\nu\) 的密度严格凸，代价则线性；两个不同有限目标值极小化解的中点会具有严格更小的熵和平均代价，矛盾。

对偶表述

熵对偶. 在适当可积条件下， \[ \operatorname{OT}_\varepsilon(\mu,\nu) = \sup_{\varphi,\psi} \left[ \int\varphi\,\mathrm d\mu+\int\psi\,\mathrm d\nu -\varepsilon\int \left\{ e^{(\varphi(x)+\psi(y)-c(x,y))/\varepsilon}-1 \right\} \,\mathrm d\mu(x)\,\mathrm d\nu(y) \right], \] 且在最优处 \[ \frac{\mathrm d\pi_\varepsilon}{\mathrm d(\mu\otimes\nu)}(x,y) = \exp\!\left(\frac{\varphi_\varepsilon(x)+\psi_\varepsilon(y)-c(x,y)}{\varepsilon}\right). \]

证明. 写 \(\pi=r\,\mu\otimes\nu\)，为边缘约束引入乘子 \(\varphi,\psi\)。关于 \(r\ge0\) 的逐点极小化使用凸共轭 \[ \sup_{r\ge0}\{sr-\varepsilon(r\log r-r+1)\} =\varepsilon(e^{s/\varepsilon}-1). \] 取 \(s=\varphi+\psi-c\) 即得对偶；共轭取等条件给出指数型最优密度。

薛定谔系统

令 \(K_\varepsilon(x,y)=e^{-c(x,y)/\varepsilon}\)、\(u=e^{\varphi_\varepsilon/\varepsilon}\)、\(v=e^{\psi_\varepsilon/\varepsilon}\)，则 \[ \pi_\varepsilon(\mathrm dx,\mathrm dy) =u(x)K_\varepsilon(x,y)v(y)\,\mu(\mathrm dx)\nu(\mathrm dy). \] 边缘约束化为 \[ u(x)\int K_\varepsilon(x,y)v(y)\,\mathrm d\nu(y)=1, \qquad v(y)\int K_\varepsilon(x,y)u(x)\,\mathrm d\mu(x)=1. \] 这对非线性积分方程称为薛定谔系统。

离散矩阵缩放

对离散边缘 \(a,b\)，令 \(K_{ij}=e^{-C_{ij}/\varepsilon}>0\)。吸收边缘权重后，唯一最优解形如 \[ P_\varepsilon=\operatorname{diag}(u)K\operatorname{diag}(v), \] 并满足 \[ u\odot(Kv)=a,\qquad v\odot(K^{\mathsf T}u)=b. \]

辛克霍恩迭代. 从 \(v^{(0)}>0\) 出发，交替更新 \[ u^{(k+1)}=a\oslash(Kv^{(k)}), \qquad v^{(k+1)}=b\oslash(K^{\mathsf T}u^{(k+1)}). \] 每个行更新精确满足第一边缘，每个列更新精确满足第二边缘。

辛克霍恩迭代为何收敛

辛克霍恩缩放定理. 若 \(K\) 的元素严格为正，且 \(a,b\) 为严格正概率向量，则存在正向量 \(u,v\)，在 \((u,v)\mapsto(\lambda u,\lambda^{-1}v)\) 的尺度变换外唯一，使 \(\operatorname{diag}(u)K\operatorname{diag}(v)\in U(a,b)\)。辛克霍恩迭代收敛到该矩阵。

三种证明视角. 在对偶坐标中，每次更新都精确最大化一个势块上的严格凹对偶目标；目标单调上升且有上界。等价地，每次更新是到一个仿射边缘集合的 KL 投影，交替布雷格曼投影收敛到交集中的唯一极小化解。定量证明则使用正核在希尔伯特射影度量中的压缩性。

小正则化极限

零温极限. 当 \(\varepsilon\downarrow0\) 时， \[ \operatorname{OT}_\varepsilon(\mu,\nu)\to\operatorname{OT}_0(\mu,\nu). \] \(\pi_\varepsilon\) 的每个弱极限点都是未正则最优计划。若未正则最优解不唯一，则在选择问题有限时，\(\pi_\varepsilon\) 选择使 \(\operatorname{KL}(\pi\,\|\,\mu\otimes\nu)\) 最小者。

证明. 熵项非负，故正则值不小于未正则值。在一个有限熵最优计划处评价正则目标，或用有限熵计划逼近最优计划，得到上极限不等式。紧性给出 \(\pi_\varepsilon\) 的极限点，代价下半连续性说明它们最优。对两个未正则最优解比较 \(\varepsilon\) 阶项即得熵选择规则。

大正则化极限

当 \(\varepsilon\to\infty\) 时，最小化互信息占主导。唯一零互信息耦合是 \(\mu\otimes\nu\)，故在温和条件下 \(\pi_\varepsilon\Rightarrow\mu\otimes\nu\)。熵传输在最优几何依赖与独立性之间插值。

关于边缘的光滑依赖

固定 \(\varepsilon>0\) 且核为正时，对偶势在相差常数外唯一，并随正离散边缘光滑变化。包络定理给出 \[ \nabla_a\operatorname{OT}_\varepsilon=\varphi_\varepsilon,\qquad \nabla_b\operatorname{OT}_\varepsilon=\psi_\varepsilon. \] 这种光滑性是正则化的主要统计与优化优势，但当 \(\varepsilon\downarrow0\) 时条件数会恶化。

对数域稳定化

小 \(\varepsilon\) 时 \(K=e^{-C/\varepsilon}\) 可能数值下溢。写 \(u=e^{f/\varepsilon}\)、\(v=e^{g/\varepsilon}\)，可把缩放写成对数和指数更新： \[ f_i=\varepsilon\log a_i-\varepsilon\log\sum_j \exp\!\left(\frac{g_j-C_{ij}}{\varepsilon}\right). \] 对 \(f,g\) 加上相反常数不改变传输计划，这正是对偶规范不变性的稳定数值实现。

正则化偏差

\(\operatorname{OT}_\varepsilon(\mu,\mu)\) 通常为正，因此正则代价并非距离，并偏向弥散耦合。减小 \(\varepsilon\) 会降低几何偏差，却使缩放更慢、统计和数值敏感性更高。下一页讨论去偏与半离散几何。
