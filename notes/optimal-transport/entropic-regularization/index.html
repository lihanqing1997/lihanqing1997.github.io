17. Entropic regularization

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/optimal-transport/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/optimal-transport/monge-ampere-equation/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/optimal-transport/sinkhorn-scaling/" %}

Entropic regularization records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the optimal transport note.

Definition

Definition. Entropic regularization adds \(\varepsilon\operatorname{KL}(\pi\mid\mu\otimes\nu)\) to the Kantorovich objective, making the optimizer smoother and strictly convex.

Proposition

Proposition. With positive regularization and finite discrete measures, the optimal coupling has scaling form \(\pi_{ij}=a_i K_{ij} b_j\), where \(K_{ij}=e^{-c_{ij}/\varepsilon}\).

Proof

Proof. First-order optimality for the constrained convex problem gives multiplicative Lagrange multipliers for the row and column constraints.

Remark

Remark. Entropic regularization is computationally powerful, but it changes the geometry. The zero-regularization limit recovers the original transport problem.
