5. Zermelo-Fraenkel set theory and choice

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/foundations-of-mathematics/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/foundations-of-mathematics/naive-set-theory-and-paradoxes/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/foundations-of-mathematics/logicism-formalism-and-intuitionism/" %}

Zermelo-Fraenkel set theory restricts set construction through axioms. With choice, it becomes the usual background theory ZFC.

Separation

Definition. Instead of arbitrary comprehension, separation forms subsets \(\{x\in A:P(x)\}\) of already existing sets. This blocks Russell's paradox while preserving ordinary constructions.

Replacement and power sets

Remark. Replacement allows images of sets under definable functions to be sets. The power-set axiom creates spaces of subsets and functions.

Axiom of choice

Definition. Choice says every family of nonempty sets has a choice function. Equivalent forms include Zorn's lemma and the well-ordering theorem.

Choice in analysis

Example. Hahn-Banach, bases of arbitrary vector spaces, and some compactness theorems use choice principles. Choice can also produce nonmeasurable sets.
