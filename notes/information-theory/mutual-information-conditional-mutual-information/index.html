Mutual information measures dependence by comparing the joint law with the product of its marginals. Conditional mutual information is the corresponding obstruction to conditional independence.

Mutual information

Definition. For finite \(X,Y\), \[ I(X;Y)=D(P_{XY}\|P_XP_Y)=H(X)+H(Y)-H(X,Y). \] The conditional mutual information is \[ I(X;Y\mid Z)=H(X\mid Z)-H(X\mid Y,Z). \]

Chain rules

Proposition. For random variables \(X_1,\ldots,X_n,Y,Z\), \[ I(X_1,\ldots,X_n;Y\mid Z)=\sum_{i=1}^n I(X_i;Y\mid Z,X_1,\ldots,X_{i-1}). \] Also \(I(X;Y\mid Z)\ge0\), with equality if and only if \(X\) and \(Y\) are conditionally independent given \(Z\).

Proof from relative entropy

Proof. The identity \(I(X;Y)=D(P_{XY}\|P_XP_Y)\) makes nonnegativity immediate. Conditional mutual information is the average of \(D(P_{XY\mid Z=z}\|P_{X\mid Z=z}P_{Y\mid Z=z})\) over \(z\). The chain rule follows by expanding conditional entropies and canceling adjacent terms.

Independence certificate

Remark. The equation \(I(X;Y\mid Z)=0\) is often more useful than the factorization itself: it turns conditional independence into an inequality statement stable under limiting and convexity arguments.
