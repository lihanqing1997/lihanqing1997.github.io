The converse proves that capacity is not merely a random-coding artifact. No coding scheme can force reliable transmission above the single-letter capacity.

Converse statement

Theorem. For a discrete memoryless channel with capacity \(C\), any sequence of \((n,M_n)\) codes with average error \(P_e^{(n)}\to0\) must satisfy \[ \limsup_{n\to\infty}\frac1n\log M_n\le C. \]

Fano step

Lemma. If \(M\) is uniform on \(\{1,\ldots,M_n\}\), then reliable decoding implies \[ \log M_n\le I(M;Y^n)+o(n). \]

Single-letterization

Proof. By data processing, \(I(M;Y^n)\le I(X^n;Y^n)\). For a memoryless channel, \[ I(X^n;Y^n)\le\sum_{i=1}^n I(X_i;Y_i)\le nC. \] Combining this with Fano's inequality gives \(\log M_n\le nC+o(n)\).

Converse philosophy

Remark. Most impossibility proofs in information theory have this shape: reduce success to a lower bound on information, upper-bound that information by data processing and single-letter inequalities, and compare the two bounds.
