22. Gaussian extremality and Gaussian channels

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-theory/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-theory/continuous-alphabets-differential-entropy/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-theory/rate-distortion-function/" %}

Gaussian distributions are extremal for entropy under covariance constraints. This extremality yields the capacity of additive Gaussian channels.

Maximum entropy at fixed covariance

Theorem. If \(X\in\mathbb R^d\) has covariance matrix \(\Sigma\), then \[ h(X)\le \frac12\log\big((2\pi e)^d\det\Sigma\big), \] with equality if and only if \(X\) is Gaussian with covariance \(\Sigma\).

AWGN capacity

Theorem. For the additive channel \(Y=X+Z\), where \(Z\sim N(0,N)\) is independent noise and \(\mathbb E X^2\le P\), the capacity per use is \[ C=\frac12\log\left(1+\frac PN\right). \]

Proof by entropy bound

Proof. For any input satisfying the power constraint, \[ I(X;Y)=h(Y)-h(Z)\le \frac12\log(2\pi e(P+N))-\frac12\log(2\pi eN). \] Equality is attained by Gaussian \(X\sim N(0,P)\), independent of \(Z\).

Role of Gaussianity

Remark. The theorem is not saying Gaussian noise is easiest; it says that once the noise is Gaussian and the input power is fixed, Gaussian input maximizes output entropy and therefore mutual information.
