11. Information spectrum

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-theory/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-theory/sanov-theorem-information-projections/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-theory/prefix-codes-kraft-mcmillan/" %}

Information spectrum methods keep the random information density instead of replacing it immediately by its expectation. This gives limit theorems beyond iid models.

Information density

Definition. For a pair \((X,Y)\) with joint law \(P_{XY}\), the information density is \[ i(X;Y)=\log\frac{P_{Y\mid X}(Y\mid X)}{P_Y(Y)}. \] For a sequence of pairs, the normalized information density is \(n^{-1}i(X^n;Y^n)\).

Definition. The spectral inf-mutual information rate is the largest \(a\) such that \[ P\left\{\frac1n i(X^n;Y^n)

Memoryless specialization

Proposition. For iid pairs \((X_i,Y_i)\), both spectral mutual information rates equal \(I(X;Y)\). For iid sources, both spectral entropy rates equal \(H(X)\).

Proof by concentration

Proof. In the iid case the normalized information density is an empirical average of integrable random variables. The law of large numbers collapses the spectrum to a point. For general non-iid sequences, the spectrum records the possible limiting range instead of forcing a single expectation.
