The entropy power inequality is the continuous counterpart of a volume-growth statement. It is one of the deepest structural inequalities in classical information theory.

Entropy power

Definition. For \(X\in\mathbb R^d\) with differential entropy \(h(X)\), define entropy power \[ N(X)=\frac1{2\pi e}\exp\left(\frac{2}{d}h(X)\right). \] For a Gaussian vector with covariance \(\sigma^2 I_d\), \(N(X)=\sigma^2\).

Entropy power inequality

Theorem. If \(X\) and \(Y\) are independent \(\mathbb R^d\)-valued random vectors with densities, then \[ N(X+Y)\ge N(X)+N(Y). \] Equality holds in the scalar nondegenerate case when \(X\) and \(Y\) are Gaussian.

Brunn-Minkowski analogy

Remark. The inequality resembles \(|A+B|^{1/d}\ge |A|^{1/d}+|B|^{1/d}\) for sets after translating volume into entropy power. This is why EPI is often viewed as a probabilistic Brunn-Minkowski theorem.

Consequences

Corollary. EPI implies Gaussian extremality statements and gives a short route to capacity formulas for Gaussian noise channels. It also connects to Fisher information through de Bruijn's identity and Stam's inequality.
