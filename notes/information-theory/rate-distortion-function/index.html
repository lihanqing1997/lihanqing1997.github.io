23. Rate-distortion function

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-theory/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-theory/gaussian-extremality-gaussian-channels/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-theory/rate-distortion-theorem/" %}

Rate-distortion theory asks how many bits are needed when exact reconstruction is replaced by controlled approximation.

Distortion measure

Definition. Let \(d:\mathcal X\times\widehat{\mathcal X}\to[0,\infty)\) be a distortion measure. A reproduction \(\widehat X\) is admissible at level \(D\) if \(\mathbb E d(X,\widehat X)\le D\).

Rate-distortion function

Definition. The rate-distortion function of \(X\) is \[ R(D)=\inf_{P_{\widehat X\mid X}:\,\mathbb E d(X,\widehat X)\le D}I(X;\widehat X). \]

Convexity and monotonicity

Proposition. The function \(D\mapsto R(D)\) is nonincreasing and convex on its effective domain. It is zero once \(D\) is at least the distortion achievable by a reproduction independent of \(X\).

Proof by time sharing

Proof. Nonincreasingness is immediate from enlarging the feasible set. Convexity follows by time sharing two test channels: choose one of two reproduction kernels with an independent coin, producing averaged distortion and averaged mutual information.
