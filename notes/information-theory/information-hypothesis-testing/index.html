Hypothesis testing gives relative entropy another operational identity: it is the sharp exponent for one error probability when the other is constrained.

Binary testing errors

Definition. For testing \(P\) against \(Q\), a test \(\phi\in[0,1]\) has type-I error \(\alpha=P\phi\) and type-II error \(\beta=Q(1-\phi)\), depending on the convention for rejection. Only the pair of achievable errors matters.

Chernoff-Stein lemma

Theorem. For iid testing between \(P^n\) and \(Q^n\), if the type-I error is bounded above by a fixed \(\varepsilon\in(0,1)\), then the optimal type-II error exponent is \[ \lim_{n\to\infty}-\frac1n\log\beta_n^\star=D(P\|Q), \] with the convention determined by which hypothesis is protected.

Chernoff information

Theorem. When the two error probabilities are balanced, the optimal Bayesian error exponent is the Chernoff information \[ C(P,Q)=-\inf_{0\le s\le1}\log\sum_x P(x)^sQ(x)^{1-s}. \]

Proof idea by likelihood ratios

Proof. Neyman-Pearson tests threshold the likelihood ratio. Under iid sampling, normalized log-likelihood ratios obey laws of large numbers and large-deviation bounds. Their means under \(P\) and \(Q\) are relative entropies, which determine the exponents.
