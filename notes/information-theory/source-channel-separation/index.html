Separation theorems say that source representation and channel transmission can be optimized separately under the classical iid assumptions.

Lossless separation

Theorem. For an iid source with entropy \(H(X)\) and a discrete memoryless channel of capacity \(C\), reliable transmission is possible if \(H(X)C\).

Lossy separation

Theorem. For distortion level \(D\), lossy communication is possible when \(R(D)C\), under the standard memoryless source and channel assumptions.

Proof by concatenation and converse

Proof. The direct part concatenates a source code of rate below channel capacity with a channel code of rate above the source-coding requirement. The converse combines the channel upper bound \(I\le nC\) with the source lower bound \(I\ge nR(D)\), after single-letterization.

Scope of the theorem

Remark. Separation is a theorem with assumptions, not a universal design law. Dependence, delay constraints, multi-terminal settings, or semantic constraints can break the clean source/channel split.
