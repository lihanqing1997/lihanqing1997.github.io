30. Information, estimation, and statistical experiments

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-theory/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-theory/information-hypothesis-testing/" %}

The final synthesis page records how information quantities enter statistical impossibility results without duplicating the statistical inference and decision theory notes.

Fano lower bound for estimation

Proposition. Let \(\theta_1,\ldots,\theta_N\) be \(2\delta\)-separated under a loss metric and let \(V\) be uniform on these indices. If data \(X\) are generated from \(P_{\theta_V}\), then every estimator has risk at least of order \(\delta\) whenever \[ I(V;X)\ll \log N. \]

Le Cam two-point principle

Proposition. For two separated parameters, testing affinity, total variation, Hellinger distance, or KL divergence controls the minimum estimation error. A typical form is \[ \inf_{\widehat\theta}\sup_{j=0,1}P_{\theta_j}\{d(\widehat\theta,\theta_j)\ge\delta\}\ge \frac12(1-\|P_{\theta_0}-P_{\theta_1}\|_{\mathrm{TV}}). \]

Experiments and information loss

Definition. A Markov transition from one experiment to another is a randomized reduction of observations. Data processing says such a reduction cannot increase pairwise divergences or mutual information. Equality corresponds to statistical sufficiency; approximate equality leads toward deficiency distance.

Connections to other notes

Remark. This page is a map rather than a replacement: LAN, minimax risk, deficiency, posterior contraction, and empirical-process entropy are developed in their own notes. Information theory supplies reusable inequalities and operational interpretations that those notes call upon.
