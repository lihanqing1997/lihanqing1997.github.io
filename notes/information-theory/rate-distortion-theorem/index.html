The rate-distortion theorem gives the operational meaning of (R(D)): it is the sharp asymptotic compression rate under average distortion constraint.

Lossy block code

Definition. An \((n,M,D)\) lossy code maps \(X^n\) to one of \(M\) codewords in \(\widehat{\mathcal X}^n\) such that \[ \frac1n\mathbb E\sum_{i=1}^n d(X_i,\widehat X_i)\le D. \]

Achievability by covering

Proof. Choose a test channel nearly attaining \(R(D)\). Generate reproduction codewords from its marginal law. With about \(\exp(nI(X;\widehat X))\) random codewords, the source sequence is jointly typical with at least one reproduction sequence with high probability, giving average distortion near \(D\).

Converse by single-letterization

Proof. For any code, let \(J\) be the message. Then \(\log M\ge I(X^n;J)\ge I(X^n;\widehat X^n)\). Chain rules and convexity reduce \(n^{-1}I(X^n;\widehat X^n)\) to a single-letter lower bound at the average distortion, giving \(R\ge R(D)\).
