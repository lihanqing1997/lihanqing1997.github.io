28. Entropy and Fisher information

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-theory/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-theory/entropy-power-inequality/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-theory/information-hypothesis-testing/" %}

Entropy and Fisher information meet along Gaussian smoothing. This link explains why central-limit phenomena and information inequalities often share the same proofs.

Fisher information of a density

Definition. For a smooth density \(f\) on \(\mathbb R^d\), the Fisher information for translation is \[ J(X)=\int \|\nabla\log f(x)\|^2 f(x)\,\mathrm{d}x. \]

de Bruijn identity

Theorem. Let \(Z\sim N(0,I_d)\) be independent of \(X\). Then, under regularity conditions, \[ \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}t}h(X+\sqrt t Z)=\frac12 J(X+\sqrt t Z). \]

Stam inequality

Corollary. For independent random vectors, \[ \frac1{J(X+Y)}\ge \frac1{J(X)}+\frac1{J(Y)} \] in the scalar form, with suitable matrix versions in higher dimension. This is the Fisher-information analogue of EPI.

Statistical meaning

Remark. Fisher information controls local parametric sensitivity, while entropy controls global spread. Gaussian convolution reveals a differential relation between these two notions, giving a clean bridge to Cramér-Rao and asymptotic normality themes in the statistics notes.
