8. Entropy rates of stochastic processes

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-theory/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-theory/asymptotic-equipartition-property/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-theory/method-of-types/" %}

For dependent sequences, the central object is entropy per symbol. Stationarity restores enough structure for limits to exist.

Entropy rate

Definition. For a discrete stochastic process \(X_1,X_2,\ldots\), define \[ \overline H_n=\frac1n H(X_1,\ldots,X_n),\qquad h_n=H(X_n\mid X_1, \ldots,X_{n-1}). \] When the limit exists, the entropy rate is \(\overline H=\lim_n \overline H_n\).

Stationary limit theorem

Theorem. If the process is stationary, then \(h_n\) decreases to a limit and \[ \lim_{n\to\infty}\frac1nH(X_1^n)=\lim_{n\to\infty}H(X_n\mid X_1^{n-1}). \]

Proof by monotonicity

Proof. Conditioning reduces entropy, so \(H(X_n\mid X_1^{n-1})\) is monotone after rewriting by stationarity. The block entropy satisfies \[ H(X_1^n)=\sum_{i=1}^n H(X_i\mid X_1^{i-1}), \] so \(n^{-1}H(X_1^n)\) is the Cesàro average of the conditional entropies and has the same limit.

Markov chains

Corollary. If \((X_n)\) is a stationary Markov chain with transition matrix \(P(y\mid x)\) and stationary law \(\pi\), then \[ \overline H=\sum_x\pi(x)H(P(\cdot\mid x)). \]
