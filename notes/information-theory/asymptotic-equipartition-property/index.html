The asymptotic equipartition property is the bridge from single-letter entropy to high-dimensional geometry. It says that most long samples have nearly the same probability.

Typical set

Definition. For iid \(X_1,\ldots,X_n\sim P\), the \(\varepsilon\)-typical set is \[ A_\varepsilon^{(n)}=\left\{x^n:\left|-\frac1n\log P^n(x^n)-H(P)\right|\le\varepsilon\right\}. \]

AEP theorem

Theorem. If \(X_i\) are iid with finite entropy, then \[ -\frac1n\log P^n(X^n)\to H(P) \] in probability and almost surely. Consequently, for large \(n\), \(P^n(A_\varepsilon^{(n)})\to1\) and \[ \exp(n(H-\varepsilon))\lesssim |A_\varepsilon^{(n)}|\lesssim \exp(n(H+\varepsilon)). \]

Proof by the law of large numbers

Proof. Since \(-\log P^n(X^n)=\sum_{i=1}^n -\log P(X_i)\), the normalized information density is an empirical average with mean \(H(P)\). The law of large numbers gives convergence. The cardinality bounds follow because every typical sequence has probability between \(\exp(-n(H+\varepsilon))\) and \(\exp(-n(H-\varepsilon))\).

Operational meaning

Remark. The AEP explains why entropy is the compression rate: a high-probability set has exponential size \(\exp(nH)\), so about \(nH\) nats are necessary and sufficient to name its elements.
