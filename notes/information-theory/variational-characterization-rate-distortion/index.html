25. Variational characterization of rate distortion

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-theory/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-theory/rate-distortion-theorem/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-theory/source-channel-separation/" %}

The minimizer in the rate-distortion problem satisfies a Gibbs-type fixed point. This is the analytic core behind the Blahut-Arimoto algorithm.

Lagrange form

Proposition. For slope parameter \(s\le0\), minimizers of \[ I(X;\widehat X)-s\,\mathbb E d(X,\widehat X) \] trace supporting hyperplanes of the convex curve \(R(D)\).

Gibbs form of the test channel

Theorem. At an interior optimum, the reproduction kernel has the form \[ P_{\widehat X\mid X}(\hat x\mid x)=\frac{P_{\widehat X}(\hat x)\exp(s d(x,\hat x))}{\sum_{z}P_{\widehat X}(z)\exp(s d(x,z))},\qquad s\le0. \]

Proof by first variation

Proof. Differentiate the Lagrangian with respect to the conditional probabilities \(P_{\widehat X\mid X}(\cdot\mid x)\), adding a normalization multiplier for each \(x\). Solving the stationarity equations gives the displayed exponential tilt, coupled with the consistency equation for \(P_{\widehat X}\).

Blahut-Arimoto fixed point

Remark. Alternating between the tilted conditional law and its induced reproduction marginal yields the Blahut-Arimoto iteration. The algorithm is secondary here; the important fact is the convex variational structure.
