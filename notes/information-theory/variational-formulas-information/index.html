6. Variational formulas for information

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-theory/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-theory/fano-inequality-inverse-bounds/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-theory/asymptotic-equipartition-property/" %}

Information quantities are convex dual objects. Variational formulas turn entropy and relative entropy into optimization principles, useful for large deviations and exponential bounds.

Donsker-Varadhan formula

Theorem. For probability measures \(P\ll Q\) on a finite space, \[ D(P\|Q)=\sup_f\left\{\mathbb E_P f-\log \mathbb E_Q e^f\right\}, \] where the supremum ranges over all real functions \(f\). The optimizer is \(f=\log(\mathrm{d}P/\mathrm{d}Q)+c\).

Entropy duality

Corollary. The preceding identity is equivalent to \[ \log \mathbb E_Q e^f=\sup_P\{\mathbb E_P f-D(P\|Q)\}. \] Thus log-moment generating functions and relative entropy are convex conjugates.

Proof by Gibbs inequality

Proof. For any \(f\), define \(Q_f(x)=e^{f(x)}Q(x)/\mathbb E_Qe^f\). Gibbs inequality gives \(D(P\|Q_f)\ge0\), or \[ D(P\|Q)\ge \mathbb E_P f-\log\mathbb E_Qe^f. \] Equality holds when \(Q_f=P\).

Information projection

Remark. Minimizing \(D(P\|Q)\) over a convex constraint set gives an information projection. This is the geometric core behind maximum entropy distributions and Sanov conditional limit theorems.
