2. Relative entropy and the log-sum inequality

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-theory/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-theory/entropy-conditional-entropy/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-theory/mutual-information-conditional-mutual-information/" %}

Relative entropy is not a metric, but it is the central directed measure of separation between probability laws. Its most useful elementary proof engine is the log-sum inequality.

Relative entropy

Definition. For probability measures \(P,Q\) on a finite alphabet, with \(P\ll Q\), \[ D(P\|Q)=\sum_x P(x)\log\frac{P(x)}{Q(x)}. \] If \(P\) charges a point where \(Q\) has mass zero, set \(D(P\|Q)=+\infty\).

Log-sum inequality

Theorem. For nonnegative numbers \((a_i,b_i)\) with \(a=\sum_i a_i\) and \(b=\sum_i b_i\), \[ \sum_i a_i\log\frac{a_i}{b_i}\ge a\log\frac{a}{b}, \] with equality exactly when \(a_i/b_i\) is constant on the active indices.

Gibbs inequality

Corollary. Taking \(a_i=P(i)\), \(b_i=Q(i)\), and \(a=b=1\) gives \(D(P\|Q)\ge0\). Equality holds if and only if \(P=Q\). Joint convexity of \(D(P\|Q)\) follows from the same inequality applied after mixing indexed pairs of measures.

Proof by convexity

Proof. The function \(t\mapsto t\log t\) is convex. Write \[ \sum_i b_i\frac{a_i}{b_i}\log\frac{a_i}{b_i}\ge b\,\frac{a}{b}\log\frac{a}{b} \] by Jensen's inequality with weights \(b_i/b\). The equality condition is the equality condition in Jensen's inequality.
