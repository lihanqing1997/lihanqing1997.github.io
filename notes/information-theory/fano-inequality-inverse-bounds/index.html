5. Fano inequality and inverse information bounds

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-theory/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-theory/data-processing-sufficiency/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-theory/variational-formulas-information/" %}

Fano inequality converts small information into unavoidable error. It is one of the main converse tools connecting information theory with statistical lower bounds.

Decoding error

Definition. Let \(M\) be uniform on \(\{1,\ldots,N\}\), let \(Y\) be an observation, and let \(\widehat M=\widehat M(Y)\). The probability of error is \(P_e=P(\widehat M\ne M)\).

Fano inequality

Theorem. For every decoder, \[ H(M\mid Y)\le h_2(P_e)+P_e\log(N-1), \] where \(h_2(u)=-u\log u-(1-u)\log(1-u)\). Consequently, \[ P_e\ge 1-\frac{I(M;Y)+1}{\log N} \] when logarithms are base two. With natural logarithms, replace \(1\) by \(\log 2\).

Proof by error indicator

Proof. Let \(E=\mathbf 1\{M\ne\widehat M\}\). Since \(E\) is determined by \((M,Y)\), \[ H(M\mid Y)=H(E,M\mid Y)-H(E\mid M,Y)\le H(E)+H(M\mid E,Y). \] On \(E=0\), \(M\) is determined by \(Y\). On \(E=1\), at most \(N-1\) values remain. This gives the displayed bound.

Statistical lower bounds

Remark. In minimax estimation one chooses a separated finite packing of parameters, places a uniform prior on it, and applies Fano to the induced testing problem. The resulting lower bound is controlled by mutual information or average KL divergence.
