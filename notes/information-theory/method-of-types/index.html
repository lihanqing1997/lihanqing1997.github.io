The method of types replaces sums over sequences by sums over empirical distributions. It is the finite-alphabet combinatorial form of large deviations.

Types and type classes

Definition. For \(x^n\in\mathcal X^n\), its type \(\widehat P_{x^n}\) is the empirical distribution \[ \widehat P_{x^n}(a)=\frac1n\sum_{i=1}^n\mathbf 1\{x_i=a\}. \] The type class \(T_Q\) is the set of strings having type \(Q\).

Counting theorem

Theorem. For a type \(Q\) on alphabet \(\mathcal X\), \[ \frac{\exp(nH(Q))}{(n+1)^{|\mathcal X|}}\le |T_Q|\le \exp(nH(Q)). \] If \(X^n\sim P^n\), then every \(x^n\in T_Q\) has probability \(\exp[-n(H(Q)+D(Q\|P))]\).

Proof by multinomial coefficients

Proof. The size of \(T_Q\) is \(n!/\prod_a(nQ(a))!\). Stirling bounds give the exponential term \(\exp(nH(Q))\) with only polynomial loss. The probability identity follows by writing \(\prod_a P(a)^{nQ(a)}\) and expanding \(H(Q)+D(Q\|P)=-\sum_aQ(a)\log P(a)\).

Why types matter

Remark. Type bounds explain why KL divergence appears in statistics: the number of strings with empirical law \(Q\) is about \(e^{nH(Q)}\), while each has probability about \(e^{-n(H(Q)+D(Q\|P))}\). The entropy terms cancel, leaving \(e^{-nD(Q\|P)}\).
