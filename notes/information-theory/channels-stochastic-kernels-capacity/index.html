17. Channels as stochastic kernels and capacity

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/information-theory/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/information-theory/kolmogorov-complexity-algorithmic-information/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/information-theory/channel-coding-achievability/" %}

A channel is a Markov kernel. Its capacity is a variational quantity: the largest mutual information induced by an input distribution.

Discrete memoryless channel

Definition. A discrete memoryless channel is a stochastic matrix \(W(y\mid x)\). For input law \(P_X\), it induces \(P_XW\) on outputs and mutual information \[ I(P_X,W)=\sum_{x,y}P_X(x)W(y\mid x)\log\frac{W(y\mid x)}{(P_XW)(y)}. \]

Capacity

Definition. The channel capacity is \[ C=\max_{P_X} I(P_X,W). \] The maximum exists for finite alphabets because the simplex is compact and \(I(P_X,W)\) is continuous.

Concavity in the input law

Proposition. For fixed \(W\), the map \(P_X\mapsto I(P_X,W)\) is concave. For fixed \(P_X\), mutual information is convex in the channel law.

Proof by entropy form

Proof. Write \(I(X;Y)=H(Y)-H(Y\mid X)\). As \(P_X\) varies, \(H(Y)\) is concave in \(P_X\), while \(H(Y\mid X)=\sum_xP_X(x)H(W(\cdot\mid x))\) is linear. This proves concavity.
