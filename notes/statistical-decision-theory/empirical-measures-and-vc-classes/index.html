Le Cam's product-experiment theory connects naturally to empirical measures. VC bounds provide uniform control needed for minimum-distance estimators.

Empirical measure indexed by sets

Definition. For observations \(X_1,\ldots,X_n\), the empirical measure is \(P_n(A)=n^{-1}\sum_i1_A(X_i)\). Uniform control over \(A\in\mathcal C\) concerns \(\sup_{A\in\mathcal C}|P_nA-PA|\).

VC uniform law

Theorem. If \(\mathcal C\) is a VC class, then \(\sup_{A\in\mathcal C}|P_nA-PA|\to0\) under broad measurability conditions, and exponential inequalities give finite-sample control.

Symmetrization and growth

Proof. Symmetrization reduces deviations to Rademacher averages, while Sauer-Shelah bounds the number of traces of \(\mathcal C\) on the sample by a polynomial in \(n\).

Minimum-distance estimation

Estimators based on minimizing empirical distances over VC classes inherit consistency and rates from these uniform empirical bounds.
