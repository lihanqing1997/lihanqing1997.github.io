Contiguity is the asymptotic version of absolute continuity that is strong enough to transfer (o_p(1)) statements between changing measures.

Contiguous sequences

Definition. A sequence \(Q_n\) is contiguous with respect to \(P_n\), written \(Q_n\triangleleft P_n\), if \(P_n(A_n)\to0\) implies \(Q_n(A_n)\to0\) for every event sequence \(A_n\).

Likelihood-ratio tightness criterion

Lemma. If \(Q_n\ll P_n\) with \(L_n=\mathrm{d}Q_n/\mathrm{d}P_n\), then \(Q_n\triangleleft P_n\) when the laws of \(L_n\) under \(P_n\) are tight and have no mass escaping to infinity in the limit.

Transfer of negligible events

Proof. Write \(Q_n(A_n)=E_{P_n}L_n1_{A_n}\). On \(\{L_n\le M\}\) this is at most \(MP_n(A_n)\); the complement is controlled by tightness of \(L_n\).

Local alternatives

LAN alternatives \(P_{\theta_0+h/\sqrt n}^{(n)}\) are typically mutually contiguous with \(P_{\theta_0}^{(n)}\). This is why local power can be computed under one base measure and transferred to another.
