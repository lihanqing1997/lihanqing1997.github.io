Deficiency turns exact Blackwell comparison into approximate comparison. It is the basic metric language for asymptotic equivalence of experiments.

Deficiency

Definition. For experiments \(\mathcal E=\{P_\theta\}\) and \(\mathcal F=\{Q_\theta\}\), the deficiency of \(\mathcal E\) relative to \(\mathcal F\) is

\[ \delta(\mathcal E,\mathcal F)=\inf_K\sup_{\theta\in\Theta}\|P_\theta K-Q_\theta\|_{\mathrm{TV}}. \]

Le Cam distance

Definition. The symmetric Le Cam distance is \(\Delta(\mathcal E,\mathcal F)=\max\{\delta(\mathcal E,\mathcal F),\delta(\mathcal F,\mathcal E)\}\). Small distance means each experiment can simulate the other with small total-variation error.

Risk transfer by total variation

Proof. If losses are bounded by one and \(P_\theta K\) is within \(\varepsilon\) of \(Q_\theta\) in total variation, then the risk of any rule changes by at most \(\varepsilon\), uniformly in \(\theta\).

Asymptotic equivalence

A sequence \(\mathcal E_n\) is asymptotically equivalent to \(\mathcal F_n\) when \(\Delta(\mathcal E_n,\mathcal F_n)\to0\). Then bounded-loss decision problems have the same asymptotic value.
