For i.i.d. models, differentiability in quadratic mean is the clean condition that turns single-observation Hellinger geometry into LAN.

Quadratic mean differentiability

Definition. A dominated model \(p_\theta\) is differentiable in quadratic mean at \(\theta_0\) if there is score \(\dot\ell_{\theta_0}\) such that

\[ \int\left(\sqrt{p_{\theta_0+h}}-\sqrt{p_{\theta_0}} -\frac12h^\top\dot\ell_{\theta_0}\sqrt{p_{\theta_0}}\right)^2\mathrm{d}\mu=o(\|h\|^2). \]

DQM implies LAN

Theorem. For i.i.d. observations, DQM and nonsingular Fisher information imply LAN with central sequence \(\Delta_n=n^{-1/2}\sum_i\dot\ell_{\theta_0}(X_i)\).

Square-root density expansion

Proof. The DQM expansion controls Hellinger error for local shifts \(h/\sqrt n\). Summing independent score terms gives the central sequence CLT and the quadratic likelihood term.

Tangent Hilbert space

The closure of score directions in \(L_2^0(P_{\theta_0})\) is the local tangent space. Fisher information is the squared norm of tangent vectors.
