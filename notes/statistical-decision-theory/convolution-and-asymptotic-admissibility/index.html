Convolution theorems say that a regular estimator cannot have an arbitrary limit distribution: it must contain the efficient Gaussian noise, possibly plus extra independent noise.

Regular estimator limit

Definition. An estimator sequence is regular at \(\theta_0\) if the distributional limit of \(\sqrt n(T_n-\theta_0-h/\sqrt n)\) under \(P_{\theta_0+h/\sqrt n}\) exists and does not depend on \(h\) except through the natural centering.

Convolution form

Theorem. In a LAN model, any regular estimator has limit law of the form

\[ N(0,I_{\theta_0}^{-1}) * M, \]

Translation equivariance in the limit

Proof. Regularity makes the limiting rule equivariant in the Gaussian shift experiment. The structure of equivariant estimators in a Gaussian shift gives the efficient Gaussian component convolved with an independent residual law.

Admissibility pressure

Extra convolution noise increases risk for convex losses. This is why asymptotically efficient estimators are natural candidates for asymptotic admissibility.
