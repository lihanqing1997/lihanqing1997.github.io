Sufficiency says that an experiment can be compressed without changing any decision problem. In Le Cam language, sufficiency is exact equivalence by transitions.

Statistic as a transition

Definition. A statistic \(T:\mathcal X\to\mathcal Y\) sends \(\mathcal E=\{P_\theta\}\) to \(T\mathcal E=\{P_\theta T^{-1}\}\). This map is a Markov transition from observations to statistic values.

Sufficiency as lossless reduction

Theorem. A statistic \(T\) is sufficient when there exists a transition \(K\) from \(\mathcal Y\) back to \(\mathcal X\) such that \(K(P_\theta T^{-1})=P_\theta\) for every \(\theta\). Then \(T\mathcal E\) and \(\mathcal E\) have the same attainable risks.

Reconstruction kernel argument

Proof. Any rule using \(X\) can be simulated from \(T(X)\) by first drawing \(X'\) from the conditional kernel \(K(\cdot\mid T)\), then applying the original rule. The risk is unchanged for every \(\theta\).

Conditional distributions

In dominated models this is the familiar factorization or conditional-distribution criterion: the conditional law of \(X\) given \(T\) does not depend on \(\theta\).
