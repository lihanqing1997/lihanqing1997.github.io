Gaussian shift experiments are the canonical local limits of smooth statistical models. Their risks become the benchmark for asymptotic efficiency.

Finite-dimensional Gaussian shift

Definition. In the experiment \(\mathcal G=\{N(h,I^{-1}):h\in H\}\), one observes \(Y=h+Z\), where \(Z\sim N(0,I^{-1})\). The parameter \(h\) is local displacement.

Likelihood ratio in the shift experiment

Proposition. Relative to \(h=0\), the log likelihood ratio is

\[ \ell_h(Y)=h^\top IY-\frac12h^\top Ih. \]

Gaussian completion of the square

Proof. Compare the two normal densities. The quadratic terms in \(Y\) cancel, leaving the linear term \(h^\top IY\) and the curvature term \(-h^\top Ih/2\).

Risk benchmark

When \(\mathcal E_n\) converges locally to \(\mathcal G\), no regular procedure can beat the corresponding Gaussian-shift risk in shrinking neighborhoods.
