Shrinkage and Invariance

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/statistical-decision-theory/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/statistical-decision-theory/admissibility-and-complete-classes/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/statistical-decision-theory/lower-bounds/" %}

Normal mean example

If \(X\sim N(\theta,\sigma^2)\) and \(L(\theta,a)=(a-\theta)^2\), the estimator \(X\) has constant risk \(\sigma^2\). With prior \(\theta\sim N(0,\tau^2)\), the Bayes estimator is

\[ \delta(X)=\frac{\tau^2}{\tau^2+\sigma^2}X. \]

This shrinks toward zero. It lowers variance but introduces bias. Risk analysis decides whether the tradeoff is worthwhile.

James-Stein phenomenon

Theorem (James-Stein). If \(X\sim N_p(\theta,\sigma^2I)\) and loss is \(\|\delta-\theta\|^2\), then the usual estimator \(\delta_0(X)=X\) is inadmissible for \(p\ge3\).

For \(X\sim N_p(\theta,\sigma^2I)\) and squared error loss \(\|\delta-\theta\|^2\), the usual estimator \(X\) is inadmissible when \(p\ge3\). Shrinkage estimators can dominate it. This surprising result shows that coordinatewise intuition can fail in multivariate decision problems.

James-Stein estimator

The classical James-Stein estimator is

\[ \delta_{\mathrm{JS}}(X)= \left(1-\frac{(p-2)\sigma^2}{\|X\|^2}\right)X. \]

For \(p\ge3\), it has strictly smaller risk than \(X\) for every \(\theta\). The positive-part version replaces the shrinkage factor by its positive part and improves the estimator further, though it is no longer exactly generalized Bayes in the same simple way.

Proof sketch. Stein's identity evaluates the risk difference between \(X\) and estimators of the form \(X+g(X)\). Taking \(g(x)=-(p-2)\sigma^2x/\|x\|^2\) yields a negative risk difference for every \(\theta\) when \(p\ge3\).

Bias-variance accounting

Shrinkage improves total risk because risk is not coordinatewise. Pulling all coordinates toward a center adds squared bias, but it can reduce variance by more than the added bias. The gain is visible only when the loss aggregates several coordinates.

Invariance

If a problem is unchanged under a group of transformations, it is natural to restrict attention to equivariant rules. Invariance can reduce the decision problem and often produces minimax rules. But invariance is a modeling judgment: it encodes which transformations should not matter.

Equivariant rules

If a group \(G\) acts on samples, parameters, and actions, a rule is equivariant when \(\delta(gX)=g\delta(X)\). Equivariance forces the risk to be constant on orbits of the group. In location problems, translation equivariance often leads to estimators of the form \(X+c\) or residual-based rules.

Geometry

Shrinkage works because high-dimensional noise spreads in many directions. Pulling the estimator toward a reasonable center can reduce total squared error even if it creates bias in every coordinate.

Shrinkage et Invariance

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Retour" back_url="/notes/statistical-decision-theory/" prev_label="Page précédente" prev_url="/notes/statistical-decision-theory/admissibility-and-complete-classes/" next_label="Page suivante" next_url="/notes/statistical-decision-theory/lower-bounds/" %}

Moyenne normale

Si \(X\sim N(\theta,\sigma^2)\) et \(L(\theta,a)=(a-\theta)^2\), l'estimateur \(X\) a risque constant \(\sigma^2\). Avec \(\theta\sim N(0,\tau^2)\), l'estimateur bayésien est

\[ \delta(X)=\frac{\tau^2}{\tau^2+\sigma^2}X. \]

Il rétrécit l'estimation vers zéro, réduisant la variance mais ajoutant un biais.

Phénomène de James-Stein

Théorème (James-Stein). Si \(X\sim N_p(\theta,\sigma^2I)\), sous perte \(\|\delta-\theta\|^2\), l'estimateur \(X\) est inadmissible pour \(p\ge3\).

Pour \(X\sim N_p(\theta,\sigma^2I)\), sous perte quadratique, l'estimateur \(X\) est inadmissible lorsque \(p\ge3\). Des estimateurs de shrinkage peuvent le dominer.

Estimateur de James-Stein

L'estimateur classique est

\[ \delta_{\mathrm{JS}}(X)= \left(1-\frac{(p-2)\sigma^2}{\|X\|^2}\right)X. \]

Pour \(p\ge3\), son risque est strictement plus petit que celui de \(X\) pour tout \(\theta\). La version positive-part améliore encore le risque.

Preuve esquissée. L'identité de Stein calcule la différence de risque pour les estimateurs \(X+g(X)\). Avec \(g(x)=-(p-2)\sigma^2x/\|x\|^2\), cette différence est négative pour tout \(\theta\) lorsque \(p\ge3\).

Biais et variance

Le shrinkage ajoute du biais mais peut réduire la variance totale davantage. Le gain apparaît lorsque la perte agrège plusieurs coordonnées.

Invariance

Si le problème est inchangé par un groupe de transformations, on peut se restreindre aux règles équivariantes. L'invariance simplifie le problème et produit souvent des règles minimax.

Règles équivariantes

Si un groupe \(G\) agit sur les données, les paramètres et les actions, une règle est équivariante lorsque \(\delta(gX)=g\delta(X)\). Le risque est alors constant sur les orbites du groupe.

Géométrie

En grande dimension, le bruit se répartit dans de nombreuses directions. Tirer l'estimateur vers un centre plausible peut réduire l'erreur quadratique totale.

Shrinkage 与不变性

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="返回" back_url="/notes/statistical-decision-theory/" prev_label="上一页" prev_url="/notes/statistical-decision-theory/admissibility-and-complete-classes/" next_label="下一页" next_url="/notes/statistical-decision-theory/lower-bounds/" %}

正态均值例子

若 \(X\sim N(\theta,\sigma^2)\)，平方损失为 \(L(\theta,a)=(a-\theta)^2\)，通常估计 \(X\) 风险恒为 \(\sigma^2\)。若 \(\theta\sim N(0,\tau^2)\)，Bayes 估计为

\[ \delta(X)=\frac{\tau^2}{\tau^2+\sigma^2}X. \]

它向零收缩，降低方差但引入偏差。风险分析决定这种权衡是否值得。

James-Stein 现象

定理（James-Stein）. 若 \(X\sim N_p(\theta,\sigma^2I)\)，损失为 \(\|\delta-\theta\|^2\)，则通常估计量 \(\delta_0(X)=X\) 在 \(p\ge3\) 时不可容许。

若 \(X\sim N_p(\theta,\sigma^2I)\)，平方损失为 \(\|\delta-\theta\|^2\)，当 \(p\ge3\) 时，通常估计 \(X\) 不可容许。Shrinkage 估计量可以支配它。

James-Stein 估计量

经典 James-Stein 估计量为

\[ \delta_{\mathrm{JS}}(X)= \left(1-\frac{(p-2)\sigma^2}{\|X\|^2}\right)X. \]

当 \(p\ge3\) 时，它在每个 \(\theta\) 上的风险都严格小于 \(X\)。positive-part 版本把收缩因子替换成其正部，并进一步改进风险。

证明思路. Stein 恒等式可以计算形如 \(X+g(X)\) 的估计量与 \(X\) 的风险差。取 \(g(x)=-(p-2)\sigma^2x/\|x\|^2\) 时，当 \(p\ge3\)，风险差对所有 \(\theta\) 都为负。

偏差-方差计算

shrinkage 之所以能改善总风险，是因为风险不是逐坐标判断的。向中心拉动会增加平方偏差，但可能更大幅度降低方差。当损失聚合多个坐标时，这种收益才会显现。

不变性

若一个问题在某个变换群下不变，自然可以考虑等变规则。不变性可以降低问题复杂度，并常常产生 minimax 规则。但它也是建模判断：它说明哪些变换不应改变问题。

等变规则

若群 \(G\) 同时作用在样本、参数和行动上，规则满足 \(\delta(gX)=g\delta(X)\) 时称为等变。等变性使风险在群轨道上恒定，从而显著简化决策问题。

几何直觉

高维噪声分散在许多方向上。把估计量拉向合理中心，即使每个坐标都有偏差，也可能降低总平方误差。
