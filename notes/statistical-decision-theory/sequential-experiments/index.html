Sequential experiments allow the amount of data to depend on observations. The decision object is not a fixed sample but a stopped experiment.

Stopped experiment

Definition. A sequential experiment consists of observations adapted to a filtration and a stopping time \(\tau\). The observed data are \(\mathcal F_\tau\), and decision rules must be \(\mathcal F_\tau\)-measurable.

Likelihood process

Proposition. For two parameters, the likelihood ratio stopped at \(\tau\) is the likelihood-ratio martingale evaluated at \(\tau\), provided the stopping rule is nonanticipating.

Optional-stopping mechanism

Proof. Likelihood ratios form nonnegative martingales under the reference law. Stopping at \(\tau\) preserves the change-of-measure identity on \(\mathcal F_\tau\).

Brownian approximation

Under local Gaussian approximations, sequential experiments are often approximated by observing Brownian motion with drift up to a stopping time.
