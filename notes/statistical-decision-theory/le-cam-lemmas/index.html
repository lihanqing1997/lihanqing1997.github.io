Le Cam's lemmas organize changes of measure in local asymptotics. They tell how likelihood-ratio limits transform distributions under nearby alternatives.

First lemma and contiguity

Lemma. If \(L_n=\mathrm{d}Q_n/\mathrm{d}P_n\) converges in distribution under \(P_n\) to \(L\) with \(E L=1\), then \(Q_n\triangleleft P_n\). If also \(L>0\) almost surely in the right sense, mutual contiguity follows.

Third lemma

Theorem. If \((T_n,L_n)\Rightarrow (T,L)\) under \(P_n\), then under \(Q_n\) the limit law of \(T_n\) is tilted by \(L\):

\[ E_Q\varphi(T)=E_P\{L\varphi(T)\}. \]

Proof by bounded test functions

Proof. For bounded \(\varphi\), \(E_{Q_n}\varphi(T_n)=E_{P_n}L_n\varphi(T_n)\). Joint convergence and uniform integrability of the likelihood-ratio coordinate pass this identity to the limit.

Local power calculations

In LAN, the third lemma shifts the normal limit of a statistic under \(P_{\theta_0}\) to its limit under \(P_{\theta_0+h/\sqrt n}\).
