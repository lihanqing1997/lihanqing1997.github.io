Local asymptotic minimax theory transfers optimal risks from the Gaussian shift limit back to shrinking neighborhoods in the original model.

Local risk

Definition. For an estimator \(T_n\) of \(\theta\), local risk around \(\theta_0\) is evaluated over \(\theta=\theta_0+h/\sqrt n\) with \(h\) restricted to bounded sets.

Local asymptotic minimax lower bound

Theorem. Under LAN and regularity of the loss \(\ell\),

\[ \lim_{c\to\infty}\liminf_n\inf_{T_n}\sup_{\|h\|\le c} E_{\theta_0+h/\sqrt n}\ell\{\sqrt n(T_n-\theta_0-h/\sqrt n)\} \ge R_{\mathcal G}^{\star}. \]

Limit-experiment proof

Proof. Any sequence of procedures with bounded local risk has subsequential risk limits in the Gaussian shift experiment. The optimal Gaussian risk therefore lower-bounds all possible local asymptotic risks.

Efficiency interpretation

An estimator is locally asymptotically minimax if it attains the Gaussian shift bound. Efficient regular estimators do this under quadratic loss in smooth finite-dimensional models.
