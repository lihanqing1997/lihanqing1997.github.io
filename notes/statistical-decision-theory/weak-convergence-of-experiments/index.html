Weak convergence of experiments is convergence of all finite likelihood-ratio coordinates. It is the minimum structure needed for local decision limits.

Finite likelihood-ratio vectors

Definition. For \(\theta_0,\theta_1,\ldots,\theta_k\), consider the vector \((\mathrm{d}P_{\theta_j,n}/\mathrm{d}P_{\theta_0,n})_{j=1}^k\) under \(P_{\theta_0,n}\). Weak convergence of experiments requires convergence of these vectors for every finite set.

Determination of finite experiment limits

Theorem. For finite parameter sets, convergence in distribution of likelihood-ratio vectors under one base point determines the weak limit experiment.

Recovering the remaining laws

Proof. The law under another parameter is obtained by weighting with the corresponding likelihood-ratio coordinate. Thus the whole finite experiment is encoded by the base law and the likelihood-ratio vector.

From finite coordinates to local theory

LAN is a special case where the likelihood-ratio vector converges to the likelihood-ratio vector of a Gaussian shift experiment.
