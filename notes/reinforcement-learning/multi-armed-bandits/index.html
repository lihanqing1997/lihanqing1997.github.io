16. Multi-armed bandits

Multi-armed bandits records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the reinforcement learning note.

Definition

Definition. A multi-armed bandit has actions with unknown reward distributions and no state dynamics. It is the simplest exploration-exploitation model.

Proposition

Proposition. Upper confidence bound algorithms choose actions by empirical mean plus uncertainty radius and achieve logarithmic problem-dependent regret for suboptimal arms.

Proof

Proof. Concentration bounds ensure that confidence intervals contain true means with high probability. Suboptimal arms are pulled often only while uncertainty is large enough to mask their gap.

Remark

Remark. Bandits isolate exploration without transition dynamics, making them a clean statistical core of RL.
