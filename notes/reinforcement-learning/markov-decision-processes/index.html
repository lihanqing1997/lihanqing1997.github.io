1. Markov decision processes

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/reinforcement-learning/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/reinforcement-learning/policies-and-value-functions/" %}

Markov decision processes records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the reinforcement learning note.

Definition

Definition. A Markov decision process consists of states, actions, transition probabilities, rewards, and a discount or horizon. The next-state distribution depends on the present state and action.

Proposition

Proposition. For a fixed policy, the controlled process is a Markov chain, and its value function satisfies a linear Bellman equation.

Proof

Proof. Condition on the first transition. The total discounted return splits into immediate reward plus discounted future return, whose conditional expectation is the value at the next state.

Remark

Remark. MDPs are the mathematical environment model of reinforcement learning. The Markov assumption is the structural simplification that makes dynamic programming possible.
