Entropy regularization changes hard maximization into soft maximization. It encourages exploration, smooths policy improvement, and connects reinforcement learning with convex duality.

Regularized objective

The policy is rewarded for both external reward and entropy.

Definition. For temperature \(\alpha>0\), the entropy-regularized return includes \[ \sum_{t\ge0}\gamma^t\{R_{t+1}+\alpha H(\pi(\cdot\mid S_t))\}, \] where \(H(\pi(\cdot\mid s))=-\int\pi(\mathrm{d}a\mid s)\log\pi(a\mid s)\) in the discrete or density case.

Remark. Larger \(\alpha\) favors more random policies; as \(\alpha\downarrow0\), the ordinary control objective is recovered.

Soft Bellman operator

The maximum is replaced by log-sum-exp or its continuous analogue.

Definition. For finite actions, the soft optimality operator is \[ (T_\alpha v)(s)=\alpha\log\sum_a\exp\left(\frac{r(s,a)+\gamma\sum_{s'}P(s'\mid s,a)v(s')}{\alpha}\right). \]

Proposition. The soft Bellman operator is a \(\gamma\)-contraction in sup norm.

Proof. The log-sum-exp map is 1-Lipschitz with respect to the sup norm: shifting all action scores by at most \(\varepsilon\) shifts the log-sum-exp by at most \(\varepsilon\). The only dependence on \(v\) is through \(\gamma Pv\), whose sup-norm change is at most \(\gamma\|u-v\|_\infty\).

Soft greedy policy

The soft maximizer is a Gibbs distribution over action values.

Theorem. For action scores \(q(s,a)\), the solution of \[ \max_{\pi(\cdot\mid s)}\left\{\sum_a\pi(a\mid s)q(s,a)+\alpha H(\pi(\cdot\mid s))\right\} \] is \[ \pi_\alpha(a\mid s)=\frac{\exp(q(s,a)/\alpha)}{\sum_b\exp(q(s,b)/\alpha)}. \]

Proof. Use a Lagrange multiplier for the constraint \(\sum_a\pi(a\mid s)=1\). The first-order condition is \(q(s,a)-\alpha(1+\log\pi(a\mid s))-\lambda=0\), hence \(\pi(a\mid s)\propto e^{q(s,a)/\alpha}\).

KL control

Entropy regularization is equivalent to KL regularization relative to a reference policy in many formulations.

Remark. Replacing entropy by \(-\operatorname{KL}(\pi(\cdot\mid s)\|\rho(\cdot\mid s))\) gives soft policies tilted from the reference \(\rho\). This is the form used in trust-region and mirror-descent views of policy optimization.
