The Bellman optimality equations replace policy averaging by maximization. They characterize the best achievable value and the policies that attain it.

Optimal Bellman operator

The optimal operator performs the best one-step action choice plus optimal continuation.

Definition. For bounded \(v\), \[ (T_*v)(s)=\sup_{a\in\mathcal A}\left\{r(s,a)+\gamma\int v(s')P(\mathrm{d}s'\mid s,a)\right\}. \] For finite action sets, the supremum is a maximum.

Theorem. The optimal value \(v_*\) is the unique bounded fixed point of \(T_*\).

Proof. The operator \(T_*\) is a \(\gamma\)-contraction: for any \(u,v\), \[ |(T_*u)(s)-(T_*v)(s)|\le\gamma\|u-v\|_\infty \] because the same action comparison bounds the difference of two suprema. Banach's theorem gives a unique fixed point. Dynamic programming shows \(v_*\) satisfies the equation by optimizing the first action and then optimizing the tail.

Optimal action values

The \(q\)-form moves the maximization to the next state.

Definition. The optimal action-value equation is \[ q_*(s,a)=r(s,a)+\gamma\int \max_{a'}q_*(s',a')P(\mathrm{d}s'\mid s,a). \]

Proposition. The operator \[ (T_*^Qq)(s,a)=r(s,a)+\gamma\int\max_{a'}q(s',a')P(\mathrm{d}s'\mid s,a) \] is a \(\gamma\)-contraction in sup norm and has unique fixed point \(q_*\).

Proof. The map \(q\mapsto\max_a q(s,a)\) is nonexpansive in sup norm. Combining this with the Markov integral and the factor \(\gamma\) gives contraction. The fixed point is the optimal \(q\)-function by the same one-step optimality argument.

Greedy policies

A fixed point of the optimality equation immediately gives an optimal policy.

Theorem. If \(\pi\) is greedy with respect to \(v_*\), meaning \[ \pi(s)\in\operatorname*{argmax}_{a}\left\{r(s,a)+\gamma\int v_*(s')P(\mathrm{d}s'\mid s,a)\right\}, \] then \(v_\pi=v_*\).

Proof. For a greedy policy, \(T_\pi v_*=T_*v_*=v_*\). Thus \(v_*\) is a fixed point of \(T_\pi\). The policy Bellman operator has a unique bounded fixed point, so \(v_\pi=v_*\).

Optimality and nonuniqueness

The optimal value is unique, but optimal policies need not be.

Remark. When several actions attain the Bellman maximum at a state, any measurable selection among them is optimal. This is why value functions are often better identified than policies.
