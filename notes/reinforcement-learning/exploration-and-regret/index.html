15. Exploration and regret

Exploration and regret records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the reinforcement learning note.

Definition

Definition. Exploration chooses actions to learn about uncertain rewards or transitions. Regret compares accumulated reward to that of an optimal policy or action.

Proposition

Proposition. In bandit problems, optimism and posterior sampling achieve sublinear regret under standard stochastic assumptions.

Proof

Proof. Confidence intervals or posterior samples balance estimated reward with uncertainty. Concentration controls how often a suboptimal action appears plausibly optimal.

Remark

Remark. Exploration is statistical experimental design inside sequential decision making.
