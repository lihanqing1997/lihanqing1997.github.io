21. Partially observable problems

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/reinforcement-learning/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/reinforcement-learning/entropy-regularized-reinforcement-learning/" %}

Partial observability breaks the ordinary Markov property at the observation level. The correct state for planning is the belief distribution over hidden states.

POMDP model

The agent sees observations generated from a latent Markov state.

Definition. A partially observable Markov decision process has latent states \(S_t\), actions \(A_t\), transitions \(P(S_{t+1}\mid S_t,A_t)\), observations \(O_t\) generated from an observation kernel, and rewards depending on latent states and actions. The history \(H_t=(O_0,A_0,\ldots,O_t)\) is observed; \(S_t\) is not.

Remark. A policy based only on the current observation is generally not Markov-optimal, because the observation may not contain all information about the latent state.

Belief states

The posterior distribution of the latent state is a sufficient statistic for control.

Definition. The belief state is \[ b_t(B)=P(S_t\in B\mid H_t). \] Bayes filtering updates \(b_t\) using the action, transition kernel, and new observation likelihood.

Theorem. The belief process is fully observed and Markov controlled: \(b_{t+1}\) depends on the past only through \((b_t,A_t,O_{t+1})\).

Proof. Given \(b_t\) and \(A_t\), the predictive law of \(S_{t+1}\) is obtained by integrating the transition kernel against \(b_t\). Observing \(O_{t+1}\) applies Bayes' rule to this predictive law. The earlier history enters only through \(b_t\), so the belief update is Markov.

Belief MDP

Planning in a POMDP can be reformulated as planning in a continuous-state MDP over beliefs.

Proposition. The expected reward under belief \(b\) and action \(a\) is \[ \bar r(b,a)=\int r(s,a)b(\mathrm{d}s), \] and the belief transition kernel is induced by prediction and Bayes update. Thus the POMDP becomes a fully observed belief MDP.

Proof. Conditional on the history, the latent state distribution is \(b\). Taking conditional expectation of reward gives \(\bar r\). The next belief is a measurable function of the current belief, action, and random observation, which defines the belief transition kernel.

Memory and recurrent policies

Approximate solutions often replace exact beliefs by learned memory states.

Remark. Recurrent networks, filters, and predictive-state representations can be viewed as approximations to belief-state inference. Their statistical difficulty is that representation learning and control are coupled.
