Actor-critic methods records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the reinforcement learning note.

Definition

Definition. Actor-critic methods combine a policy parameter update, the actor, with a value-function estimator, the critic.

Proposition

Proposition. If the critic consistently estimates the compatible action-value or advantage function, actor updates approximate policy-gradient ascent.

Proof

Proof. Use the policy gradient theorem and replace the unknown action value by the critic estimate. Compatibility conditions make approximation error orthogonal to policy score directions.

Remark

Remark. Actor-critic algorithms trade bias, variance, and stability through the coupling of two learning processes.
