12. Policy gradient theorem

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/reinforcement-learning/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/reinforcement-learning/eligibility-traces-and-td-lambda/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/reinforcement-learning/baselines-and-variance-reduction/" %}

Policy gradient theorem records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the reinforcement learning note.

Definition

Definition. Policy gradient methods optimize a differentiable parameterized policy by estimating the gradient of expected return.

Policy gradient theorem

Theorem. Under regularity assumptions, \(\nabla J(\theta)=E_{\pi_\theta}[\nabla\log\pi_\theta(A\mid S)Q^{\pi_\theta}(S,A)]\), with the expectation under the discounted occupancy measure.

Proof

Proof. Differentiate trajectory probabilities using the log-derivative identity. Terms involving transition probabilities vanish because they do not depend on policy parameters.

Remark

Remark. The theorem avoids differentiating the environment model and makes model-free policy optimization possible.
