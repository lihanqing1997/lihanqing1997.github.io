10. Q-learning

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/reinforcement-learning/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/reinforcement-learning/temporal-difference-learning/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/reinforcement-learning/eligibility-traces-and-td-lambda/" %}

Q-learning records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the reinforcement learning note.

Definition

Definition. Q-learning is an off-policy temporal-difference method for estimating the optimal action-value function.

Proposition

Proposition. In finite discounted MDPs, tabular Q-learning converges to \(Q^*\) under sufficient exploration and Robbins-Monro step-size conditions.

Proof

Proof. The update is stochastic approximation to the optimal Bellman fixed point for \(Q\). Visiting all state-action pairs infinitely often and contraction of the Bellman optimality operator drive convergence.

Remark

Remark. Q-learning separates behavior from target optimality in the tabular case; function approximation makes this separation much more delicate.
