11. Eligibility traces and TD lambda

Eligibility traces and TD lambda records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the reinforcement learning note.

Definition

Definition. Eligibility traces assign credit to recently visited states or features, interpolating between one-step TD and Monte Carlo returns.

Proposition

Proposition. The forward view of \(TD(\lambda)\) uses \(\lambda\)-returns, while the backward view uses recursively updated eligibility traces; under suitable conditions the two views agree.

Proof

Proof. Expand the \(\lambda\)-return as a geometrically weighted sum of \(n\)-step returns and rearrange terms to obtain the trace recursion.

Remark

Remark. Eligibility traces control bias-variance and credit assignment across time.
