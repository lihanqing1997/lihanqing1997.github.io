5. Contraction and fixed points

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/reinforcement-learning/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/reinforcement-learning/bellman-optimality-equations/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/reinforcement-learning/policy-evaluation/" %}

Contraction and fixed points records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the reinforcement learning note.

Definition

Definition. Discounted Bellman operators are contractions in the sup norm with contraction factor \(\gamma\).

Proposition

Proposition. A discounted Bellman operator has a unique fixed point, and iterating the operator converges geometrically to that fixed point.

Proof

Proof. For two value functions, the immediate reward cancels and the difference is at most \(\gamma\) times the sup-norm difference after transition averaging and maximization or policy averaging.

Remark

Remark. Contraction is the analytic reason discounted dynamic programming is stable and algorithmically tractable.
