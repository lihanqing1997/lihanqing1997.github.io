3. Bellman expectation equations

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/reinforcement-learning/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/reinforcement-learning/policies-and-value-functions/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/reinforcement-learning/bellman-optimality-equations/" %}

Bellman expectation equations records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the reinforcement learning note.

Definition

Definition. The Bellman expectation equation characterizes the value function of a fixed policy as a fixed point of an affine operator.

Proposition

Proposition. For discounted problems, \(V^\pi=T^\pi V^\pi\), where \((T^\pi V)(s)=E_\pi[R_{t+1}+\gamma V(S_{t+1})\mid S_t=s]\).

Proof

Proof. Condition on the first reward and next state, then use the Markov property and stationarity of the policy for the remaining return.

Remark

Remark. Policy evaluation is fixed-point computation. Statistical learning enters when the transition law or expectations are unknown.
