7. Policy iteration

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/reinforcement-learning/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/reinforcement-learning/policy-evaluation/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/reinforcement-learning/value-iteration/" %}

Policy iteration records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the reinforcement learning note.

Definition

Definition. Policy iteration alternates policy evaluation with greedy policy improvement.

Policy iteration theorem

Theorem. In a finite discounted MDP, exact policy iteration monotonically improves value and terminates at an optimal policy after finitely many policy changes.

Proof

Proof. The policy improvement theorem shows a greedy policy has value at least that of the old policy. Finite policy space prevents infinitely many strict improvements.

Remark

Remark. Policy iteration separates prediction from control. Approximate variants inherit errors from both phases.
