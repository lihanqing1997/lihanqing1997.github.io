7. Policy iteration

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/reinforcement-learning/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/reinforcement-learning/policy-evaluation/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/reinforcement-learning/value-iteration/" %}

Policy iteration alternates exact policy evaluation with greedy improvement. In finite discounted MDPs it terminates in an optimal policy after finitely many strict improvements.

Policy improvement

The improvement theorem is the reason greedy steps are safe.

Theorem. Let \(\pi'\) be greedy with respect to \(v_\pi\), so \(T_{\pi'}v_\pi=T_*v_\pi\ge T_\pi v_\pi=v_\pi\). Then \(v_{\pi'}\ge v_\pi\) pointwise.

Proof. Since \(T_{\pi'}\) is monotone, \[ v_\pi\le T_{\pi'}v_\pi\le T_{\pi'}^2v_\pi\le\cdots. \] The iterates converge to the fixed point \(v_{\pi'}\) by contraction, so \(v_{\pi'}\ge v_\pi\).

Exact policy iteration

Exact policy iteration repeats evaluation and improvement.

Definition. Policy iteration constructs \(\pi_{k+1}\) greedy with respect to \(v_{\pi_k}\), after exactly evaluating \(v_{\pi_k}\).

Theorem. In a finite discounted MDP, exact policy iteration reaches an optimal policy in finitely many iterations.

Proof. The policy improvement theorem gives monotone improvement in value. If the value does not improve, the current policy is greedy with respect to its own value, hence satisfies the optimality equation and is optimal. Otherwise at least one state action changes to yield strict improvement. There are only finitely many deterministic stationary policies.

Modified policy iteration

Practical dynamic programming often evaluates only approximately before improving.

Definition. Modified policy iteration applies \(m\) evaluation sweeps \(T_{\pi_k}\) before taking a greedy policy-improvement step. Value iteration and policy iteration are limiting cases with \(m=0\) and exact evaluation.

Remark. The method trades computation per iteration against the number of improvement steps. Error bounds combine contraction during partial evaluation with approximate-greedy error.

Policy oscillation

Tie-breaking matters when several greedy actions have the same value.

Remark. Consistent tie-breaking prevents cycling among equivalent optimal policies in exact finite policy iteration. The value sequence remains monotone even when policies are not unique.
