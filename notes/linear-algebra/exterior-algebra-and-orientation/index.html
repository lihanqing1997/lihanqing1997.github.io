19. Exterior algebra and orientation

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/linear-algebra/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/linear-algebra/tensor-products-and-multilinear-maps/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/linear-algebra/exact-sequences-and-dimension-counts/" %}

Exterior algebra and orientation records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the linear algebra note.

Definition

Definition. The exterior power \(\bigwedge^k V\) encodes alternating \(k\)-linear information. Simple elements \(v_1\wedge\cdots\wedge v_k\) represent oriented \(k\)-dimensional volume.

Proposition

Proposition. The top exterior power \(\bigwedge^n V\) of an \(n\)-dimensional space is one-dimensional. A choice of nonzero element determines an orientation.

Proof

Proof. Alternating relations kill linearly dependent lists and identify changes of basis through the determinant. Dimension follows by taking wedges of increasing basis indices.

Remark

Remark. Exterior algebra is the natural home of determinants and differential forms. It is the linear algebra that later becomes integration on manifolds.
