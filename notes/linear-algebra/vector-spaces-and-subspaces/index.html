1. Vector spaces and subspaces

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/linear-algebra/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/linear-algebra/linear-combinations-span-and-independence/" %}

Vector spaces and subspaces records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the linear algebra note.

Definition

Definition. A vector space over a field \(F\) is an abelian group with scalar multiplication satisfying the distributive and unit axioms. A subspace is a subset closed under addition and scalar multiplication, hence a vector space with inherited operations.

Proposition

Proposition. Intersections of subspaces are subspaces, while sums \(U+W=\{u+w:u\in U,w\in W\}\) are the smallest subspaces containing both \(U\) and \(W\). The direct-sum condition is exactly \(U\cap W=\{0\}\).

Proof

Proof. Closure proves the intersection and sum statements. For the direct-sum criterion, uniqueness of \(u+w\) is equivalent to the impossibility of a nonzero vector lying in both summands.

Remark

Remark. Most linear algebra arguments start by replacing a complicated set by the subspace it spans. This turns geometry into algebra without losing the linear information.
