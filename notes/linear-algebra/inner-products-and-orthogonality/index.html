10. Inner products and orthogonality

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/linear-algebra/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/linear-algebra/jordan-form-and-minimal-polynomials/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/linear-algebra/orthogonal-projections-and-least-squares/" %}

Inner products and orthogonality records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the linear algebra note.

Definition

Definition. An inner product is a positive definite Hermitian or symmetric bilinear form. It defines lengths, angles, orthogonality, and the norm \(\|v\|=\sqrt{\langle v,v\rangle}\).

Proposition

Proposition. Every finite-dimensional inner-product space has an orthonormal basis. Gram-Schmidt turns any independent list into an orthonormal list spanning the same nested subspaces.

Proof

Proof. Subtract from each new vector its projections onto the previously constructed orthonormal vectors, then normalize the nonzero remainder. Orthogonality follows from the projection formula.

Remark

Remark. Orthogonality is the bridge from algebraic coordinates to geometry. It is also why least squares, projections, and spectral theory behave cleanly.
