15. Positive definite matrices

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/linear-algebra/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/linear-algebra/bilinear-and-quadratic-forms/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/linear-algebra/matrix-norms-and-condition-numbers/" %}

Positive definite matrices records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the linear algebra note.

Definition

Definition. A Hermitian matrix \(A\) is positive definite if \(x^*Ax\gt0\) for every nonzero \(x\). Positive semidefinite means the same inequality with \(\ge0\).

Proposition

Proposition. For Hermitian \(A\), positive definiteness is equivalent to all eigenvalues being positive, to existence of a Cholesky factorization, and to positivity of all leading principal minors.

Proof

Proof. The eigenvalue criterion follows from the spectral theorem. Cholesky is obtained by eliminating while preserving positivity of Schur complements.

Remark

Remark. Positive definite matrices are the linear algebra of energy, variance, metrics, and convex quadratic functions. They sit at the interface with optimization and statistics.
