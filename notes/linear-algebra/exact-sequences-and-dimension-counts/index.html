20. Exact sequences and dimension counts

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/linear-algebra/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/linear-algebra/exterior-algebra-and-orientation/" %}

Exact sequences and dimension counts records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the linear algebra note.

Definition

Definition. A sequence \(U\xrightarrow{S}V\xrightarrow{T}W\) is exact at \(V\) if \(\operatorname{im}S=\ker T\). Exactness records that all relations are generated by the previous map.

Proposition

Proposition. For a short exact sequence \(0\to U\to V\to W\to0\) of finite-dimensional spaces, \(\dim V=\dim U+\dim W\).

Proof

Proof. Injectivity identifies \(U\) with a subspace of \(V\), and surjectivity identifies \(W\) with the quotient of \(V\) by that subspace. Apply the quotient dimension formula.

Remark

Remark. Exact sequences turn bookkeeping into structure. They prepare the language used in algebra, topology, functional analysis, and statistical tangent spaces.
