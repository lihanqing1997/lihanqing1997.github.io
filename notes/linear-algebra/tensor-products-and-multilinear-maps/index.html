18. Tensor products and multilinear maps

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/linear-algebra/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/linear-algebra/dual-spaces-and-adjoints/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/linear-algebra/exterior-algebra-and-orientation/" %}

Tensor products and multilinear maps records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the linear algebra note.

Definition

Definition. The tensor product \(V\otimes W\) is a vector space equipped with a bilinear map \((v,w)\mapsto v\otimes w\) that represents all bilinear maps linearly.

Universal property

Theorem. Every bilinear map \(B:V\times W\to Z\) factors uniquely through a linear map \(\widetilde B:V\otimes W\to Z\). This universal property characterizes the tensor product up to unique isomorphism.

Proof

Proof. Construct a free vector space on pairs \((v,w)\) and quotient by the bilinearity relations. The universal map is then forced by the quotient property.

Remark

Remark. Tensors are not just arrays. The coordinate array appears only after choosing bases; the invariant content is the universal multilinear mapping property.
