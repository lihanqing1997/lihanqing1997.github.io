9. Jordan form and minimal polynomials

Jordan form and minimal polynomials records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the linear algebra note.

Definition

Definition. The minimal polynomial of \(T\) is the monic polynomial of least degree annihilating \(T\). Jordan form decomposes an operator into generalized eigenspaces and nilpotent chains.

Jordan decomposition

Theorem. Over an algebraically closed field, an operator has a Jordan form. It is diagonalizable exactly when the minimal polynomial splits into distinct linear factors.

Proof

Proof. Primary decomposition reduces \(V\) to kernels of powers \((T-\lambda I)^k\). On each component, nilpotent cyclic chains produce Jordan blocks.

Remark

Remark. Jordan form is structurally exact but numerically unstable. The theoretical invariant remains essential, even when computation prefers Schur form or invariant subspaces.
