17. Dual spaces and adjoints

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/linear-algebra/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/linear-algebra/matrix-norms-and-condition-numbers/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/linear-algebra/tensor-products-and-multilinear-maps/" %}

Dual spaces and adjoints records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the linear algebra note.

Definition

Definition. The dual space \(V^*\) consists of linear functionals \(\varphi:V\to F\). With inner products, an adjoint \(T^*\) is defined by \(\langle Tv,w\rangle=\langle v,T^*w\rangle\).

Proposition

Proposition. In finite dimension, \(\dim V^*=\dim V\), but the isomorphism \(V\cong V^*\) is not canonical until an inner product or pairing is chosen.

Proof

Proof. A basis of \(V\) gives a dual basis of \(V^*\), proving dimension equality. Non-canonicity follows because changing the basis changes the coordinate identification.

Remark

Remark. Duality organizes constraints, covectors, weak formulations, and transposes. It becomes indispensable in functional analysis and differential geometry.
