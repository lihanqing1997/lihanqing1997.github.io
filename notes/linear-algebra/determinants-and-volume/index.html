6. Determinants and volume

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/linear-algebra/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/linear-algebra/rank-nullity-and-quotient-spaces/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/linear-algebra/eigenvalues-and-invariant-subspaces/" %}

Determinants and volume records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the linear algebra note.

Definition

Definition. The determinant is the alternating multilinear function of the columns of a square matrix normalized by \(\det I=1\). Geometrically it is oriented volume scaling.

Proposition

Proposition. A square matrix is invertible exactly when its determinant is nonzero, and \(\det(AB)=\det A\det B\). Row operations reveal how determinant records linear dependence and volume change.

Proof

Proof. Alternating multilinearity reduces determinant identities to their values on elementary matrices or on columns of the identity. Multiplicativity follows by checking that \(A\mapsto\det(AB)\) has the same defining properties.

Remark

Remark. The determinant is not merely a formula; it is the universal alternating volume form. This viewpoint leads naturally to exterior algebra.
