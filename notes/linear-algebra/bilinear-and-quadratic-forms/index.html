14. Bilinear and quadratic forms

Bilinear and quadratic forms records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the linear algebra note.

Definition

Definition. A bilinear form \(B\) assigns scalars \(B(v,w)\) linearly in each argument. A quadratic form is \(q(v)=B(v,v)\), usually for a symmetric or Hermitian form.

Sylvester law of inertia

Theorem. Over \(\mathbb R\), every symmetric bilinear form has a basis in which its matrix is diagonal with entries \(1\), \(-1\), and \(0\). The numbers of positive and negative entries are invariants.

Proof

Proof. Repeatedly choose a vector with nonzero value of \(q\), split off its orthogonal complement, and proceed by induction. Invariance follows from maximal positive and negative subspaces.

Remark

Remark. Quadratic forms encode conics, curvature approximations, covariance matrices, and second-order optimality. Their classification is linear algebra with geometric consequences.
