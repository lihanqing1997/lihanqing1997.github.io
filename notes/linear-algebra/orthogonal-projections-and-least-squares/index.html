11. Orthogonal projections and least squares

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/linear-algebra/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/linear-algebra/inner-products-and-orthogonality/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/linear-algebra/spectral-theorem-for-normal-operators/" %}

Orthogonal projections and least squares records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the linear algebra note.

Definition

Definition. If \(U\) is a finite-dimensional subspace of an inner-product space, the orthogonal projection \(P_Ux\) is the unique point in \(U\) such that \(x-P_Ux\perp U\).

Projection theorem

Theorem. The projection \(P_Ux\) is the unique minimizer of \(u\mapsto\|x-u\|\) over \(U\). In coordinates this gives the normal equations for least squares.

Proof

Proof. Decompose \(x=u+r\) with \(r\perp U\). Pythagoras gives \(\|x-v\|^2=\|r\|^2+\|u-v\|^2\), minimized only at \(v=u\).

Remark

Remark. This page supplies the geometric core of regression and approximation theory; numerical analysis then asks how to compute the projection stably.
