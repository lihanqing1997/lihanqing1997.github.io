5. Rank, nullity, and quotient spaces

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/linear-algebra/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/linear-algebra/linear-maps-and-matrices/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/linear-algebra/determinants-and-volume/" %}

Rank, nullity, and quotient spaces records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the linear algebra note.

Definition

Definition. The kernel \(\ker T\) consists of vectors killed by \(T\), and the image \(\operatorname{im}T\) consists of values attained by \(T\). The quotient \(V/\ker T\) identifies vectors that have the same image.

Rank-nullity theorem

Theorem. For finite-dimensional \(V\), \(\dim V=\dim\ker T+\dim\operatorname{im}T\). Equivalently, \(V/\ker T\cong\operatorname{im}T\).

Proof

Proof. Choose a basis of \(\ker T\), extend it to a basis of \(V\), and observe that the images of the added basis vectors form a basis of \(\operatorname{im}T\).

Remark

Remark. Rank-nullity is the prototype of an exact-sequence dimension count. Later algebraic and topological invariants repeatedly imitate this argument.
