17. Convex functions and monotone functions

Convexity and monotonicity provide regularity weaker than differentiability but strong enough for one-sided derivatives, inequalities, and optimization.

Convexity

Definition. A function on an interval is convex if \(f((1-t)x+ty)\le(1-t)f(x)+tf(y)\) for \(0\le t\le1\). Convex functions lie below chords and above supporting lines when subgradients exist.

Continuity of convex functions

Proposition. A finite convex function on an open interval is continuous and locally Lipschitz. Bounds on secant slopes over compact subintervals give the local Lipschitz constant.

Monotone functions

Theorem. A monotone real function has left and right limits at every point and only countably many discontinuities. Its jumps have summable total size over bounded intervals.

Distribution functions

Example. Cumulative distribution functions are increasing and right-continuous. Their analytic structure is monotone-function theory with probabilistic normalization.
