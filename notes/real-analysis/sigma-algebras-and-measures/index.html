25. Sigma-algebras and measures

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/real-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/real-analysis/inverse-and-implicit-function-theorems/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/real-analysis/outer-measures-and-lebesgue-measure/" %}

Measure theory starts by deciding which sets are measurable and how size behaves under countable operations. Countability is what makes limits compatible with set algebra.

Sigma-algebras

Definition. A sigma-algebra \(\mathcal A\) on \(X\) contains \(X\), is closed under complements, and is closed under countable unions. It is therefore closed under countable intersections and set differences.

Generated sigma-algebras

Definition. The sigma-algebra \(\sigma(\mathcal C)\) is the smallest sigma-algebra containing \(\mathcal C\). The Borel sigma-algebra on a topological space is generated by open sets.

Measures

Definition. A measure \(\mu\) satisfies \(\mu(\emptyset)=0\) and countable additivity on disjoint measurable sets: \(\mu(\bigcup A_n)=\sum \mu(A_n)\). Probability measures have total mass \(1\).

Continuity of measure

Theorem. If \(A_n\uparrow A\), then \(\mu(A_n)\uparrow \mu(A)\). If \(A_n\downarrow A\) and \(\mu(A_1)<\infty\), then \(\mu(A_n)\downarrow \mu(A)\).

Pi-lambda uniqueness

Lemma. If two finite measures agree on a generating pi-system, then they agree on the generated sigma-algebra. This is one of the most common tools for proving equality of measures.
