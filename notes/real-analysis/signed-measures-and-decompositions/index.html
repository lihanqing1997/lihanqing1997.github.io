32. Signed and complex measures

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/real-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/real-analysis/product-measures-and-fubini-theorem/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/real-analysis/radon-nikodym-derivatives/" %}

Signed and complex measures allow cancellation, but their structure is still controlled by positivity through Hahn, Jordan, and total variation decompositions.

Signed measures

A signed measure is countably additive but may take negative values.

Definition. A signed measure \(\nu\) on \((X,\mathcal A)\) is a countably additive map to \([-\infty,\infty]\) that assumes at most one of \(+\infty\) and \(-\infty\).

Definition. A set \(P\) is positive for \(\nu\) if every measurable subset of \(P\) has nonnegative \(\nu\)-measure; negative sets are defined analogously.

Hahn decomposition

Every signed measure splits the space into positive and negative regions.

Theorem. There exist disjoint measurable sets \(P,N\) with \(P\cup N=X\), where \(P\) is positive and \(N\) is negative for \(\nu\). The decomposition is unique modulo \(\nu\)-null sets.

Proof. Choose sets with nearly maximal positive measure among sets of finite positive measure, remove subsets where negativity remains, and iterate with summable errors. The union of extracted positive pieces is positive, and its complement is negative; otherwise one could improve the construction. Uniqueness follows because the intersection of a positive set and a negative set must have signed measure zero.

Jordan decomposition

A signed measure is the difference of two mutually singular positive measures.

Theorem. There are unique mutually singular positive measures \(\nu^+\) and \(\nu^-\) such that \(\nu=\nu^+-\nu^-\).

Proof. Given a Hahn decomposition \(X=P\cup N\), define \(\nu^+(A)=\nu(A\cap P)\) and \(\nu^-(A)=-\nu(A\cap N)\). These are positive and mutually singular. If another such decomposition exists, comparing on positive and negative Hahn sets gives equality.

Total variation

Total variation measures the size of a signed measure without cancellation.

Definition. The total variation measure is \(|\nu|=\nu^++\nu^-\). Equivalently, \[ |\nu|(A)=\sup\sum_i|\nu(A_i)|, \] where the supremum is over finite measurable partitions of \(A\).

Proof. The Jordan formula gives \(\sum_i|\nu(A_i)|\le\sum_i(\nu^+(A_i)+\nu^-(A_i))=|\nu|(A)\). Taking the partition by \(A\cap P\) and \(A\cap N\) attains equality.

Complex measures

Complex measures reduce to signed measures through real and imaginary parts.

Remark. A complex measure has finite total variation on the whole space by definition. Its variation is the minimal positive measure \(\lambda\) such that \(|\nu(A)|\le\lambda(A)\) for every measurable \(A\).
