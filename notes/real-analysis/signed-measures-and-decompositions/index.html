32. Signed and complex measures

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/real-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/real-analysis/product-measures-and-fubini-theorem/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/real-analysis/radon-nikodym-derivatives/" %}

Signed and complex measures allow cancellation. Their decomposition theory is the measure-theoretic version of positive and negative parts of functions.

Signed measures

Definition. A signed measure is countably additive and takes values in \([-\infty,\infty]\) without taking both infinite signs. Positive measures are the special nonnegative case.

Hahn decomposition

Theorem. There are disjoint measurable sets \(P\) and \(N\) covering the space such that all measurable subsets of \(P\) have nonnegative measure and all measurable subsets of \(N\) have nonpositive measure.

Jordan decomposition

Theorem. Every signed measure decomposes uniquely as \(\nu=\nu^+-\nu^-\) with mutually singular positive measures. The total variation is \(|\nu|=\nu^++\nu^-\).

Integration

Definition. A function is integrable with respect to \(\nu\) if it is integrable with respect to \(|\nu|\). Then integration is defined by the Jordan decomposition.

Total variation distance

Example. For probability measures \(P\) and \(Q\), \(\|P-Q\|_{TV}=\sup_A |P(A)-Q(A)|\). This distance is central in testing and statistical comparison.
