Riemann-Stieltjes integration replaces interval length by increments of an integrator. It is the deterministic ancestor of integration against measures and distribution functions.

Stieltjes sums

The integrator decides how much weight each subinterval receives.

Definition. Given \(f,\alpha:[a,b]\to\mathbb R\), a Riemann-Stieltjes sum is \(\sum_i f(t_i)(\alpha(x_i)-\alpha(x_{i-1}))\). If these sums converge to a common limit over refining partitions, write \(\int_a^b f\,\mathrm{d}\alpha\).

Remark. When \(\alpha(x)=x\), this is the Riemann integral. When \(\alpha\) has jumps, the integral includes weighted point masses.

Monotone integrators and induced measures

Increasing integrators are distribution functions of finite measures on intervals.

Theorem. If \(\alpha\) is increasing and right-continuous, there is a unique finite Borel measure \(\mu_\alpha\) on \([a,b]\) such that \(\mu_\alpha((s,t])=\alpha(t)-\alpha(s)\).

Proof. Define the premeasure on half-open intervals by the displayed formula and extend it to finite disjoint unions. Countable additivity on the interval algebra follows from monotone continuity of \(\alpha\). Caratheodory extension gives a Borel measure, and uniqueness follows because half-open intervals generate the Borel sigma-algebra.

Existence for continuous integrands

Continuous functions integrate against bounded-variation integrators.

Proposition. If \(f\) is continuous and \(\alpha\) has bounded variation on \([a,b]\), then \(\int f\,\mathrm{d}\alpha\) exists.

Proof. Write \(\alpha=\alpha_1-\alpha_2\) as the difference of increasing functions by Jordan decomposition. For increasing \(\alpha_i\), the Stieltjes integral agrees with the Lebesgue integral of \(f\) against \(\mu_{\alpha_i}\), which exists because \(f\) is bounded. Subtract the two integrals.

Integration by parts

Stieltjes integration has a symmetric product rule.

Theorem. If \(f\) and \(g\) have bounded variation and share no common discontinuity, then \[ \int_a^b f\,\mathrm{d}g+\int_a^b g\,\mathrm{d}f=f(b)g(b)-f(a)g(a). \]

Proof. For a partition, expand \(f(x_i)g(x_i)-f(x_{i-1})g(x_{i-1})\) as \(f(t_i)\Delta g_i+g(s_i)\Delta f_i\) plus errors controlled by oscillations. Bounded variation bounds the accumulated error, and absence of common discontinuities makes those errors vanish as the mesh is refined.

Distribution functions

Stieltjes integration is the natural notation for expectations of functions of a real random variable.

Example. If \(F\) is a distribution function and \(X\sim F\), then \(\mathbb E\varphi(X)=\int\varphi\,\mathrm{d}F\) whenever the expectation exists. At an atom \(x\), the jump \(F(x)-F(x-)\) contributes \(\varphi(x)\) times the atom mass.
