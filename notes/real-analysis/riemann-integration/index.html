18. Riemann and Darboux integration

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/real-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/real-analysis/convex-functions-and-monotone-functions/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/real-analysis/riemann-stieltjes-integration/" %}

Riemann integration measures area by refining partitions of the domain. Darboux sums expose the exact criterion for when upper and lower approximations meet.

Upper and lower Darboux sums

The Riemann integral exists when all sufficiently fine upper and lower step approximations agree.

Definition. For a bounded \(f:[a,b]\to\mathbb R\) and partition \(P\), define \(U(f,P)=\sum_i M_i\Delta x_i\) and \(L(f,P)=\sum_i m_i\Delta x_i\), where \(M_i=\sup_{[x_{i-1},x_i]}f\) and \(m_i=\inf_{[x_{i-1},x_i]}f\).

Theorem. \(f\) is Riemann integrable if and only if for every \(\varepsilon>0\) there is a partition \(P\) with \(U(f,P)-L(f,P)\lt \varepsilon\).

Proof. Refining partitions lowers upper sums and raises lower sums. The upper integral is the infimum of upper sums and the lower integral is the supremum of lower sums. Equality of these two values is equivalent to being able to make their difference arbitrarily small.

Continuous functions are integrable

Uniform continuity on a compact interval controls oscillation on small subintervals.

Theorem. Every continuous function on \([a,b]\) is Riemann integrable.

Proof. By uniform continuity, choose \(\delta\) so that \(|x-y|\lt \delta\Rightarrow |f(x)-f(y)|\lt \varepsilon/(b-a)\). For any partition with mesh below \(\delta\), the oscillation on each subinterval is below \(\varepsilon/(b-a)\), hence \(U(f,P)-L(f,P)\lt \varepsilon\).

Lebesgue criterion for Riemann integrability

Riemann integrability is exactly continuity except on a null set.

Theorem. A bounded function on \([a,b]\) is Riemann integrable if and only if its set of discontinuities has Lebesgue measure zero.

Proof. Let \(\omega_f(x)\) be the oscillation of \(f\) at \(x\). The discontinuity set is \(\bigcup_{m\ge1}\{\omega_f\ge1/m\}\). Small Darboux gap implies each such set can be covered by intervals of arbitrarily small total length. Conversely, if discontinuities have measure zero, cover the bad set by short intervals and use uniform continuity on the remaining compact pieces to make the Darboux gap small.

Algebra and order

Integrable functions form a stable ordered vector space.

Proposition. If \(f,g\) are Riemann integrable, then \(af+bg\), \(fg\), \(|f|\), \(\max(f,g)\), and \(\min(f,g)\) are Riemann integrable.

Proof. The discontinuities of these combinations are contained in the union of the discontinuity sets of \(f\) and \(g\), except for operations involving continuous maps on \(\mathbb R^2\). Since finite unions of null sets are null, the Lebesgue criterion applies.

Relation to Lebesgue integration

The Riemann integral is a special case of the Lebesgue integral.

Theorem. If \(f\) is Riemann integrable on \([a,b]\), then \(f\) is Lebesgue integrable and the two integrals agree.

Proof. Riemann integrability gives upper and lower step functions converging in integral gap. These step functions are Lebesgue measurable and squeeze \(f\). Monotone and dominated convergence identify the common limit with the Lebesgue integral.
