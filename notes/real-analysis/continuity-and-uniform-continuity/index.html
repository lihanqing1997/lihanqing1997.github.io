5. Continuity and uniform continuity

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/real-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/real-analysis/metric-spaces-and-compactness/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/real-analysis/differentiability-and-taylor-expansion/" %}

Continuity and uniform continuity records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the real analysis note.

Definition

Definition. Continuity at a point means small input changes near that point force small output changes. Uniform continuity chooses one input scale that works simultaneously for all points in the domain.

Extreme value and Heine-Cantor theorem

Theorem. A continuous function from a compact metric space to a metric space is uniformly continuous and attains its maximum and minimum when the target is ordered like \(\mathbb R\).

Proof

Proof. Compactness turns local continuity neighborhoods into a finite subcover. The finite minimum of local radii gives uniform continuity, and sequential compactness gives extrema.

Remark

Remark. Uniformity is the difference between local analysis and global control. Many interchange theorems require uniform rather than pointwise estimates.
