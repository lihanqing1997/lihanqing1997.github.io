46. Fourier series and orthogonality

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/real-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/real-analysis/convolution-and-mollifiers/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/real-analysis/fourier-transform-on-euclidean-space/" %}

Fourier series express functions in an orthogonal trigonometric basis. The subject combines real analysis with Hilbert-space geometry.

Fourier coefficients

Definition. For periodic \(f\in L^2[-\pi,\pi]\), the coefficient is \(\hat f(k)=(2\pi)^{-1}\int_{-\pi}^{\pi}f(x)e^{-ikx}\,\mathrm{d}x\).

Bessel and Parseval

Theorem. Bessel's inequality says \(\sum |\hat f(k)|^2\le\|f\|_2^2\). Completeness of the trigonometric system gives Parseval equality.

Partial sums

Definition. Fourier partial sums are orthogonal projections onto finitely many frequencies. In \(L^2\), these projections converge to the original function.

Fejer theorem

Theorem. Cesaro means of Fourier partial sums converge uniformly to continuous periodic functions. Positivity of the Fejer kernel stabilizes convergence.

Series estimators

Remark. Orthogonal-series methods in statistics estimate finitely many coefficients and control truncation bias plus estimation variance.
