20. Fourier series and orthogonality

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/real-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/real-analysis/approximation-by-smooth-and-simple-functions/" %}

Fourier series and orthogonality records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the real analysis note.

Definition

Definition. Fourier series expand periodic functions in the orthogonal system \(e^{inx}\). Coefficients are projections onto trigonometric basis functions.

Proposition

Proposition. In \(L^2\) on the circle, trigonometric polynomials are dense and Fourier partial sums converge in \(L^2\) to the original function.

Proof

Proof. Orthogonality gives projection formulas and Parseval identities for finite sums. Density of trigonometric polynomials completes the passage to all \(L^2\) functions.

Remark

Remark. Fourier analysis shows the power of Hilbert-space geometry inside real analysis. Pointwise convergence is subtler and depends on additional regularity.
