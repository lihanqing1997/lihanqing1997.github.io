46. Fourier series and orthogonality

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/real-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/real-analysis/convolution-and-mollifiers/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/real-analysis/fourier-transform-on-euclidean-space/" %}

Fourier series expand periodic functions in an orthogonal trigonometric basis. The cleanest theory lives first in \(L^2\), where orthogonality gives projection.

Trigonometric system

Complex exponentials form the natural orthonormal system on the circle.

Definition. On \([-\pi,\pi]\), set \(e_n(x)=(2\pi)^{-1/2}e^{inx}\), \(n\in\mathbb Z\). The Fourier coefficient of \(f\in L^1\) is \(\widehat f(n)=(2\pi)^{-1/2}\int_{-\pi}^{\pi}f(x)e^{-inx}\,\mathrm{d}x\).

Proposition. The functions \(e_n\) are orthonormal in \(L^2([-\pi,\pi])\).

Proof. \(\langle e_n,e_m\rangle=(2\pi)^{-1}\int_{-\pi}^{\pi}e^{i(n-m)x}\,\mathrm{d}x\). The integral is \(1\) if \(n=m\) and \(0\) otherwise.

Bessel and Parseval

Orthogonal projection controls the size of coefficients.

Theorem. For \(f\in L^2\), \(\sum_{n\in\mathbb Z}|\widehat f(n)|^2\le\|f\|_2^2\). If the trigonometric system is complete, equality holds.

Proof. For a finite set \(F\), the projection \(P_Ff=\sum_{n\in F}\widehat f(n)e_n\) satisfies \(\|f\|_2^2=\|P_Ff\|_2^2+\|f-P_Ff\|_2^2\). Thus \(\sum_{n\in F}|\widehat f(n)|^2\le\|f\|_2^2\). Let \(F\uparrow\mathbb Z\). Completeness makes \(P_Ff\to f\), giving equality.

Dirichlet kernels

Partial sums are convolutions with oscillatory kernels.

Definition. The \(N\)-th symmetric partial sum is \(S_Nf=f*D_N\), where \(D_N(x)=\sum_{|n|\le N}e^{inx}\) up to normalization.

Remark. The \(L^1\)-norm of \(D_N\) grows like \(\log N\), so pointwise convergence of Fourier partial sums is subtle and cannot be treated like a positive approximate identity.

Fejer means

Averaged partial sums have a positive kernel and converge better.

Theorem. If \(f\in C(\mathbb T)\), the Fejer means \(\sigma_N f\) converge uniformly to \(f\). If \(f\in L^p\), \(1\le p\lt \infty\), then \(\sigma_Nf\to f\) in \(L^p\).

Proof. Fejer means are convolutions with the Fejer kernel \(K_N\), which is nonnegative, has integral one, and concentrates near zero. Thus \(K_N\) is an approximate identity. Uniform continuity gives uniform convergence for continuous \(f\), and translation continuity in \(L^p\) gives \(L^p\) convergence.

Orthogonal projection viewpoint

Fourier series are least-squares approximations in \(L^2\).

Remark. The \(N\)-term Fourier partial sum is the orthogonal projection onto the span of frequencies \(|n|\le N\). This is the same Hilbert-space geometry behind regression projections and conditional expectation.
