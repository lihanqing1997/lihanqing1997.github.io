2. Sequences and subsequences

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/real-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/real-analysis/ordered-fields-and-completeness/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/real-analysis/series-and-absolute-convergence/" %}

Sequences and subsequences records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the real analysis note.

Definition

Definition. A sequence \((x_n)\) converges to \(x\) if every neighborhood of \(x\) eventually contains \(x_n\). A subsequence records the behavior along a strictly increasing index map.

Bolzano-Weierstrass theorem

Theorem. In \(\mathbb R\), every bounded sequence has a convergent subsequence. This Bolzano-Weierstrass theorem is compactness in sequential form.

Proof

Proof. Bisect a bounded interval repeatedly, choosing a half interval containing infinitely many terms. The nested intervals determine a limit point, and indices are chosen successively inside them.

Remark

Remark. Subsequences distinguish convergence from mere boundedness. They are the first compactness tool before general topology formalizes compactness itself.
