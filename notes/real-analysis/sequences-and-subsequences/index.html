6. Sequences, subsequences, and asymptotic notation

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/real-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/real-analysis/completeness-principles/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/real-analysis/series-and-absolute-convergence/" %}

Sequences are the basic syntax of limits. They also model later notions such as convergence almost surely, in probability, in distribution, and in norm.

Sequential convergence

Definition. In a metric space \((X,d)\), \(x_n\to x\) means that every ball around \(x\) eventually contains all \(x_n\). Metric limits are unique because two different points can be separated by disjoint small balls.

Subsequences and cluster points

Definition. A subsequence is \((x_{n_k})\) with \(n_k\) strictly increasing. A cluster point is a limit of some subsequence. Compactness can often be read as the assertion that cluster points always exist.

Diagonal extraction

Lemma. If requirements are indexed by \(j=1,2,\ldots\), one can refine subsequences successively and take the diagonal. The diagonal eventually satisfies every fixed requirement. This is the standard proof device behind separability reductions.

Asymptotic scales

Definition. Write \(x_n=O(a_n)\) when \(|x_n|/a_n\) is bounded, and \(x_n=o(a_n)\) when that ratio tends to \(0\). Stochastic analogues replace boundedness and convergence by boundedness and convergence in probability.

Limsup estimates

Proposition. If the expressions are meaningful, \(\limsup(x_n+y_n)\le \limsup x_n+\limsup y_n\). If \(y_n\to y\), then \(\limsup(x_n+y_n)=\limsup x_n+y\). Such deterministic estimates are the grammar of asymptotic comparison.
