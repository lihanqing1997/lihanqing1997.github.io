15. Product measures and Fubini theorem

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/real-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/real-analysis/convergence-theorems/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/real-analysis/radon-nikodym-derivatives/" %}

Product measures and Fubini theorem records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the real analysis note.

Definition

Definition. Product measure assigns size to rectangles by \((\mu\otimes\nu)(A\times B)=\mu(A)\nu(B)\) and extends this assignment to a product \(\sigma\)-algebra.

Fubini theorem

Theorem. For integrable \(f\) on a product space, iterated integrals exist almost everywhere and equal the integral over the product: \(\int f\,\mathrm{d}(\mu\otimes\nu)=\int\int f(x,y)\,\mathrm{d}\nu(y)\,\mathrm{d}\mu(x)\).

Proof

Proof. Prove the statement for indicators of measurable rectangles, extend by monotone class to nonnegative measurable functions, then apply positive and negative parts for integrable functions.

Remark

Remark. Fubini turns high-dimensional integration into repeated one-dimensional integration, but integrability hypotheses are essential for signed functions.
