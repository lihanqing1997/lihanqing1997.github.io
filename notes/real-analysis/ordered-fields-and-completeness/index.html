2. Ordered fields and Archimedean order

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/real-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/real-analysis/natural-numbers-integers-and-rationals/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/real-analysis/dedekind-cuts-and-real-line/" %}

An ordered field combines algebra and order, but analysis needs more: every bounded increasing approximation should have a target. Completeness is the axiom that turns an ordered field into the real continuum.

Ordered-field compatibility

Definition. An ordered field is a field \(F\) with a total order such that \(x\le y \Rightarrow x+z\le y+z\) and multiplication by positive elements preserves order. In such a field, inequalities may be added, multiplied by positive numbers, and inverted with the direction reversed for positive elements.

Suprema and infima

Definition. For \(A \subset F\), an element \(s\) is \(\sup A\) if it is an upper bound and is less than every other upper bound. Infima are defined dually. The rationals show that ordered-field axioms alone do not guarantee these objects exist.

Archimedean property

Proposition. The real line is Archimedean: for every \(x \in \mathbb R\) there is \(n \in \mathbb N\) with \(x0\) some \(n\) satisfies \(1/n<\varepsilon\). This rules out infinitely large or infinitesimal real numbers.

Completeness principles

Theorem. For the real line, the least-upper-bound property, monotone convergence of bounded monotone sequences, nested closed interval completeness, Bolzano-Weierstrass, and Cauchy completeness are equivalent. Different proofs choose different forms of the same no-gaps principle.

Nested interval proof

Proof. If \([a_n,b_n]\) are nested and \(b_n-a_n \to 0\), let \(x=\sup_n a_n\). Then \(a_m\le x\le b_m\) for every \(m\), so \(x\) lies in all intervals. Any second point has distance at most \(b_m-a_m\) for every \(m\), hence is equal to \(x\).
