Borel measures live on the topology-generated sigma-algebra. Radon measures are Borel measures that can be approximated from compact and open sets.

Borel sigma-algebra

The Borel sets are generated by open sets and therefore encode topology.

Definition. If \(X\) is a topological space, \(\mathcal B(X)\) is the sigma-algebra generated by open subsets of \(X\). A Borel measure is a measure on \(\mathcal B(X)\).

Proposition. On a metric space, \(\mathcal B(X)\) is generated by open balls, and if \(X\) is separable it is generated by balls with centers in a countable dense set and rational radii.

Proof. Open sets are unions of open balls. In a separable metric space, every open ball is a countable union of rational-radius balls with centers in a fixed countable dense subset contained slightly inside it. Thus the countable collection generates all open sets.

Regularity

Regularity means measurable sets can be approximated by topologically nice sets.

Definition. A finite Borel measure \(\mu\) on a metric space is regular if \(\mu(A)=\inf\{\mu(U):A\subset U,\ U\text{ open}\}\) and \(\mu(A)=\sup\{\mu(K):K\subset A,\ K\text{ compact}\}\) for Borel \(A\).

Theorem. Every finite Borel measure on \(\mathbb R^d\) is regular.

Proof. Let \(\mathcal C\) be the Borel sets satisfying inner and outer regularity. Intervals and boxes satisfy regularity by elementary approximation from slightly larger open boxes and slightly smaller compact boxes. The class \(\mathcal C\) is a monotone class under finite measure continuity, so the monotone class theorem extends regularity to all Borel sets.

Radon measures

Radon measures are the right measures for locally compact spaces.

Definition. A Radon measure on a locally compact Hausdorff space is a Borel measure finite on compact sets, inner regular on open sets, and outer regular on Borel sets.

Proposition. Every locally finite Borel measure on \(\mathbb R^d\) is Radon.

Proof. Restrict the measure to compact boxes, where it is finite and regular. Exhaust \(\mathbb R^d\) by increasing compact boxes and pass regularity through the exhaustion using monotone convergence.

Support

The support is the closed set where the measure cannot be locally ignored.

Definition. The support of a Borel measure \(\mu\) on a metric space is the complement of the largest open set of measure zero. Equivalently, \(x\in\operatorname{supp}\mu\) if every open ball around \(x\) has positive measure.

Proof. The union of all open null sets is open and null when the space has a countable base, because it is the union of countably many basis elements with measure zero. Taking its complement gives the stated characterization.

Weak convergence bridge

Regularity makes measures visible to continuous test functions.

Remark. On metric spaces, regularity is what allows approximation of indicators by continuous functions. This is the technical heart behind portmanteau theorems and weak convergence.
