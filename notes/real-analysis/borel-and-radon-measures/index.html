27. Borel and Radon measures on metric spaces

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/real-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/real-analysis/outer-measures-and-lebesgue-measure/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/real-analysis/lebesgue-measurable-functions/" %}

Topology lets measures be approximated by open, closed, and compact sets. Radon regularity is the formal version of that compatibility.

Borel measures

Definition. A Borel measure is a measure on the Borel sigma-algebra \(\mathcal B(X)\) of a topological space \(X\). In metric spaces, open balls generate the Borel sets.

Regularity

Definition. Outer regularity approximates a set from outside by open sets; inner regularity approximates from inside by compact sets. Regularity turns abstract measurability into topological approximation.

Radon measures

Definition. On locally compact spaces, a Radon measure is finite on compact sets and regular. On Polish spaces, finite Borel measures have strong regularity properties, which are crucial for weak convergence.

Regular approximation theorem

Theorem. For Lebesgue measure and finite-measure \(A\subset\mathbb R^d\), every \(\varepsilon>0\) admits compact \(K\) and open \(U\) with \(K\subset A\subset U\) and \(\lambda(U\setminus K)<\varepsilon\).

Measures determined by tests

Remark. Finite Radon measures on nice spaces are often determined by integrals against continuous test functions. This is the analytic basis of weak convergence of measures.
