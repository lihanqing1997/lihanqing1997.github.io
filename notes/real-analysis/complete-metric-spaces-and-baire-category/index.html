9. Complete metric spaces and Baire category

Completeness supports fixed-point arguments and category arguments. Baire category is the hidden engine behind several fundamental theorems of functional analysis.

Completion

Construction. Every metric space can be embedded densely into a complete metric space by taking Cauchy sequences modulo zero distance. Uniformly continuous maps into complete spaces extend naturally to completions.

Meagre and residual sets

Definition. A set is nowhere dense if its closure has empty interior. A countable union of nowhere dense sets is meagre, and the complement of a meagre set is residual.

Baire category theorem

Theorem. A complete metric space is not meagre in itself. Equivalently, a countable intersection of dense open sets is dense. The proof uses nested balls with radii tending to zero.

Functional-analysis consequence

Remark. Uniform boundedness, open mapping, and closed graph theorems are Baire category results in Banach spaces. Thus this real-analysis theorem becomes a structural theorem about linear operators.
