Lebesgue measurable functions records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the real analysis note.

Definition

Definition. A function is measurable if inverse images of Borel sets are measurable. Equivalently, threshold sets \(\{x:f(x)>a\}\) are measurable for all real \(a\).

Proposition

Proposition. Pointwise limits of measurable functions are measurable, and measurable nonnegative functions can be approximated increasingly by simple functions.

Proof

Proof. Measurability of limsup and liminf follows by countable unions and intersections of threshold events. Simple approximation partitions the range into dyadic intervals.

Remark

Remark. Measurable functions are exactly the functions compatible with the countable operations of measure theory. This makes integration possible beyond continuous functions.
