17. Lp spaces

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/real-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/real-analysis/radon-nikodym-derivatives/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/real-analysis/modes-of-convergence/" %}

Lp spaces records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the real analysis note.

Definition

Definition. For \(1\le p<\infty\), \(L^p\) consists of measurable functions with \(\int |f|^p\,\mathrm{d}\mu<\infty\), modulo equality almost everywhere.

Proposition

Proposition. Minkowski and Hölder inequalities make \(L^p\) a normed space, and \(L^p\) is complete for \(1\le p\le\infty\).

Proof

Proof. Hölder follows from convexity or Young's inequality. Minkowski follows by applying Hölder to \(|f+g|^p\), and completeness follows by extracting an almost everywhere summable subsequence from a Cauchy sequence.

Remark

Remark. The \(L^p\) scale measures size by integrability rather than pointwise bounds. It is the shared language of analysis, probability, and PDE.
