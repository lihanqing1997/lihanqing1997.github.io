42. L^p duality and weak convergence

Duality turns functions into linear tests. Weak convergence studies convergence through all such tests rather than through norm distance.

Endpoint caution

Remark. The dual of \(L^1\) is \(L^\infty\) under sigma-finiteness, but the dual of \(L^\infty\) is generally larger than \(L^1\). Endpoint spaces require care.

Weak convergence

Definition. A sequence \(f_n\) converges weakly to \(f\) in \(L^p\) if \(\int f_n g\,\mathrm{d}\mu\to \int f g\,\mathrm{d}\mu\) for every dual test \(g\).

Weak lower semicontinuity

Theorem. If \(f_n\) converges weakly to \(f\) in \(L^p\), then \(\|f\|_p\le\liminf \|f_n\|_p\). Convex norms are weakly lower semicontinuous.

Tests as dual pairings

Remark. A statistical test \(\phi\) acts on a probability measure by \(P\mapsto \int \phi\,\mathrm{d}P\). This is the same function-measure duality that underlies weak convergence and decision theory.
