42. L^p duality and weak convergence

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/real-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/real-analysis/inequalities-in-lp-spaces/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/real-analysis/egorov-lusin-and-approximation-theorems/" %}

Duality identifies continuous linear functionals on \(L^p\) spaces. Weak convergence then becomes convergence against all dual test functions.

Continuous functionals from \(L^q\)

Holder gives the basic dual pairing.

Proposition. If \(1\le p\lt \infty\), \(1/p+1/q=1\), and \(g\in L^q\), then \(T_g(f)=\int fg\,\mathrm{d}\mu\) defines a bounded linear functional on \(L^p\) with \(\|T_g\|\le\|g\|_q\).

Proof. Linearity is immediate. Holder gives \(|T_g(f)|\le\|f\|_p\|g\|_q\), so \(T_g\) is bounded with the stated norm estimate.

Duality for \(1\lt p\lt \infty\)

For sigma-finite measure spaces, every functional arises from an \(L^q\) function.

Theorem. If \(1\lt p\lt \infty\) and the measure space is sigma-finite, then \((L^p)^*=L^q\), isometrically under the pairing \(\int fg\,\mathrm{d}\mu\).

Proof. Given a bounded linear functional \(T\), define a signed measure \(\nu(A)=T(\mathbf 1_A)\) first on finite-measure sets and extend by sigma-finiteness. Boundedness gives \(\nu\ll\mu\), so \(\nu=g\mu\) by Radon-Nikodym. Testing \(T\) on simple functions gives \(T(f)=\int fg\). A standard extremal choice \(f=\operatorname{sgn}(g)|g|^{q-1}\) on finite pieces gives \(\|T\|=\|g\|_q\).

Weak convergence in \(L^p\)

Weak convergence is convergence under all continuous linear tests.

Definition. \(f_n\rightharpoonup f\) weakly in \(L^p\) if \(\int f_ng\,\mathrm{d}\mu\to\int fg\,\mathrm{d}\mu\) for every \(g\in L^q\), where \(q\) is conjugate to \(p\).

Proposition. Strong \(L^p\) convergence implies weak \(L^p\) convergence.

Proof. If \(\|f_n-f\|_p\to0\), then for each \(g\in L^q\), Holder gives \(|\int(f_n-f)g|\le\|f_n-f\|_p\|g\|_q\to0\).

Reflexivity

Reflexivity gives weak compactness of bounded sets.

Theorem. For \(1\lt p\lt \infty\), \(L^p\) is reflexive.

Proof. The dual of \(L^p\) is \(L^q\), and the dual of \(L^q\) is \(L^p\). Under these identifications, the canonical embedding of \(L^p\) into its double dual maps \(f\) to the functional \(g\mapsto\int fg\), which is onto.

Weak lower semicontinuity

Norms behave well under weak limits.

Proposition. If \(f_n\rightharpoonup f\) in \(L^p\), then \(\|f\|_p\le\liminf_n\|f_n\|_p\).

Proof. For \(f\ne0\), choose \(g\in L^q\) with \(\|g\|_q=1\) and \(\int fg\) arbitrarily close to \(\|f\|_p\). Weak convergence gives \(\int f_ng\to\int fg\), while Holder gives \(|\int f_ng|\le\|f_n\|_p\). Take the liminf and then optimize over \(g\).
