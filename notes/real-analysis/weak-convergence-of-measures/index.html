Weak convergence of measures is convergence seen through bounded continuous test functions. It is the topology behind distributional convergence in probability and statistics.

Bounded-continuous test functions

Weak convergence tests measures by continuous observables.

Definition. Probability measures \(\mu_n\) on a metric space \(S\) converge weakly to \(\mu\), written \(\mu_n\Rightarrow\mu\), if \(\int f\,\mathrm{d}\mu_n\to\int f\,\mathrm{d}\mu\) for every bounded continuous \(f:S\to\mathbb R\).

Remark. Bounded continuous functions are insensitive to small topological perturbations but still rich enough to determine probability measures on metric spaces.

Portmanteau theorem

Weak convergence has many equivalent formulations.

Theorem. For probability measures on a metric space, \(\mu_n\Rightarrow\mu\) if and only if \(\limsup_n\mu_n(F)\le\mu(F)\) for every closed \(F\), equivalently \(\liminf_n\mu_n(G)\ge\mu(G)\) for every open \(G\), equivalently \(\mu_n(A)\to\mu(A)\) for every \(\mu\)-continuity set \(A\).

Proof. Approximate indicators of closed sets from above by bounded continuous functions such as \(x\mapsto(1-d(x,F)/\varepsilon)^+\), then let \(\varepsilon\downarrow0\). Open-set inequalities follow by complements. Continuity sets are squeezed between their interiors and closures. Conversely, bounded continuous functions are approximated by layer-cake integrals of continuity sets.

Continuous mapping theorem

Weak convergence is preserved by continuous transformations.

Theorem. If \(\mu_n\Rightarrow\mu\) on \(S\) and \(T:S\to E\) is continuous, then \(\mu_n\circ T^{-1}\Rightarrow\mu\circ T^{-1}\).

Proof. For every bounded continuous \(g:E\to\mathbb R\), \(g\circ T\) is bounded and continuous on \(S\). Hence \(\int g\,\mathrm{d}(\mu_n\circ T^{-1})=\int g\circ T\,\mathrm{d}\mu_n\to\int g\circ T\,\mathrm{d}\mu=\int g\,\mathrm{d}(\mu\circ T^{-1})\).

Convergence on the real line

Distribution functions provide a one-dimensional criterion.

Theorem. Probability measures \(\mu_n\) on \(\mathbb R\) converge weakly to \(\mu\) if and only if \(F_n(x)\to F(x)\) at every continuity point of \(F\).

Proof. The forward direction follows from portmanteau applied to \((-\infty,x]\) and \((-\infty,x)\). For the reverse direction, approximate bounded continuous functions by step functions whose discontinuity points are continuity points of \(F\), controlling the tails by choosing large compact intervals.

Statistical reading

Weak convergence is convergence of laws, not necessarily convergence of realized values.

Remark. Central limit theorems, bootstrap consistency, empirical process convergence, and posterior weak convergence are all statements in this topology or its function-space analogues.
