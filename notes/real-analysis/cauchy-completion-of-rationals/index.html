Cauchy completion builds real numbers from rational approximating sequences. It is the metric counterpart of Dedekind's order construction.

Cauchy sequences and null sequences

A sequence is Cauchy when its tail becomes internally indistinguishable, even before a limit is known.

Definition. A rational sequence \((q_n)\) is Cauchy if for every \(\varepsilon>0\) there is \(N\) such that \(|q_n-q_m|\lt \varepsilon\) for all \(m,n\ge N\). A null sequence satisfies \(q_n\to0\) in \(\mathbb Q\).

Lemma. Every Cauchy sequence of rationals is bounded.

Proof. Choose \(N\) such that \(|q_n-q_N|\lt 1\) for \(n\ge N\). The tail is bounded by \(|q_N|+1\), and the finitely many initial terms have a maximum absolute value.

Equivalence classes of approximations

Two rational Cauchy sequences represent the same real number when their difference vanishes.

Definition. Let \(\mathcal C\) be the set of rational Cauchy sequences and \(\mathcal N\) the set of rational null sequences. Define \((q_n)\sim(r_n)\) if \((q_n-r_n)\in\mathcal N\). The completion is \(\mathcal C/\mathcal N\).

Proposition. Coordinatewise addition and multiplication are well-defined on \(\mathcal C/\mathcal N\).

Proof. Sums and products of Cauchy sequences are Cauchy; products use boundedness of Cauchy sequences. If \(q_n-r_n\to0\) and \(u_n-v_n\to0\), then \((q_n+u_n)-(r_n+v_n)\to0\), and \(q_nu_n-r_nv_n=q_n(u_n-v_n)+v_n(q_n-r_n)\to0\) by boundedness.

Order from eventual positivity

Positivity is defined by being eventually separated from zero.

Definition. A class \([q_n]\) is positive if there exist \(\delta>0\) and \(N\) such that \(q_n>\delta\) for all \(n\ge N\). Define \([q_n]\le[r_n]\) if \([r_n-q_n]\) is positive or zero.

Proposition. This order is total and compatible with the field operations.

Proof. For a nonzero class \([q_n]\), the sequence cannot have subsequences arbitrarily close to both positive and negative values; otherwise Cauchyness would force convergence to zero. Thus exactly one of the class or its negative is positive. Compatibility follows from the corresponding rational inequalities for sufficiently large indices.

Completeness of the completed field

The construction is designed so that Cauchy approximations converge.

Theorem. The metric field \(\mathcal C/\mathcal N\) is complete.

Proof. Let \(x_k\) be a Cauchy sequence of classes. Choose rational representatives \(q^{(k)}_n\), and pass to a subsequence, still denoted \(x_k\), with \(|x_{k+1}-x_k|\lt 2^{-k}\). Pick rational representatives \(a_k\) of \(x_k\) accurate within \(2^{-k}\). Then \((a_k)\) is a rational Cauchy sequence and its class is the limit of \(x_k\). The original sequence has the same limit because it is Cauchy.

Equivalence with Dedekind reals

The metric and order repairs give the same real line.

Remark. A Cauchy class maps to the cut of rationals eventually below its representatives. Conversely, a cut is represented by any rational sequence increasing through the cut and approaching its boundary. These maps are inverse order-preserving field isomorphisms.
