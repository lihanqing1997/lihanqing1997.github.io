41. Inequalities in L^p spaces

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/real-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/real-analysis/lp-spaces/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/real-analysis/lp-duality-and-weak-convergence/" %}

Inequalities convert integrability into quantitative control. They are the practical grammar of analysis and probability.

Jensen inequality

Theorem. For convex \(\phi\) and probability measure \(P\), \(\phi(\int X\,\mathrm{d}P)\le \int \phi(X)\,\mathrm{d}P\) whenever the expressions are meaningful.

Markov and Chebyshev

Proposition. For \(f\ge0\), \(\mu(f\ge t)\le t^{-1}\int f\,\mathrm{d}\mu\). Taking \(f=|g|^p\) gives moment tail bounds.

Young convolution inequality

Theorem. For suitable exponents, convolution satisfies \(\|f*g\|_r\le\|f\|_p\|g\|_q\). This is the basic bound behind smoothing by kernels.

Tail integration

Example. For nonnegative \(X\), \(\mathbb E X^p=p\int_0^\infty t^{p-1}P(X>t)\,\mathrm{d}t\). Moment and tail estimates are two sides of the same formula.
