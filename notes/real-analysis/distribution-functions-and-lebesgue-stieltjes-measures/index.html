35. Distribution functions and Lebesgue-Stieltjes measures

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/real-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/real-analysis/lebesgue-decomposition-and-singularity/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/real-analysis/lebesgue-differentiation-theorem/" %}

Distribution functions are monotone right-continuous encodings of measures on the real line. Lebesgue-Stieltjes measures make this correspondence exact.

Distribution functions

A distribution function records mass accumulated from the left.

Definition. A distribution function is an increasing right-continuous function \(F:\mathbb R\to\mathbb R\) with finite limits at \(\pm\infty\). For probability laws, these limits are \(0\) and \(1\).

Proposition. If \(\mu\) is a finite Borel measure on \(\mathbb R\), then \(F(x)=\mu((-\infty,x])\) is increasing and right-continuous.

Proof. Monotonicity is immediate from inclusion. If \(x_n\downarrow x\), then \((-\infty,x_n]\downarrow(-\infty,x]\), and continuity from above for finite measures gives \(F(x_n)\downarrow F(x)\).

Measure generated by a distribution function

Right-continuity is the convention that fixes endpoint masses.

Theorem. For every distribution function \(F\), there is a unique finite Borel measure \(\mu_F\) such that \(\mu_F((a,b])=F(b)-F(a)\).

Proof. Define a premeasure on finite disjoint unions of half-open intervals by summing increments of \(F\). Monotonicity and right-continuity give countable additivity on the algebra. Caratheodory extension gives a Borel measure, and uniqueness follows since half-open intervals form a pi-system generating \(\mathcal B(\mathbb R)\).

Atoms and jumps

Point masses are exactly jumps of the distribution function.

Proposition. \(\mu_F(\{x\})=F(x)-F(x-)\), where \(F(x-)=\lim_{y\uparrow x}F(y)\).

Proof. The singleton \(\{x\}\) is the decreasing intersection of intervals \((x-1/n,x]\). Continuity from above gives \(\mu_F(\{x\})=\lim_n(F(x)-F(x-1/n))=F(x)-F(x-)\).

Integration against \(F\)

Stieltjes notation is integration with respect to \(\mu_F\).

Definition. For measurable \(g\), write \(\int g\,\mathrm{d}F\) for \(\int g\,\mathrm{d}\mu_F\), whenever the latter integral is defined.

Remark. In probability, if \(X\) has distribution function \(F\), then \(\mathbb E g(X)=\int g\,\mathrm{d}F\). This notation includes discrete, continuous, and mixed laws.

Weak convergence via distribution functions

Continuity points are the right places to test convergence.

Theorem. For probability measures on \(\mathbb R\), \(\mu_n\Rightarrow\mu\) if and only if \(F_n(x)\to F(x)\) at every continuity point of \(F\).

Proof. Weak convergence implies the inequalities of the portmanteau theorem, which give convergence at continuity points of half-lines. Conversely, convergence of distribution functions at continuity points determines integrals of bounded continuous functions by approximating such functions with finite linear combinations of interval indicators whose endpoints are continuity points.
