35. Distribution functions and Lebesgue-Stieltjes measures

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/real-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/real-analysis/lebesgue-decomposition-and-singularity/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/real-analysis/lebesgue-differentiation-theorem/" %}

Distribution functions are monotone right-continuous functions. Lebesgue-Stieltjes measures turn them into measures on the real line.

Distribution functions

Definition. A distribution function \(F\) is increasing, right-continuous, and satisfies limits \(0\) at \(-\infty\) and \(1\) at \(\infty\). It corresponds to \(F(x)=P((-\infty,x])\).

Lebesgue-Stieltjes measure

Theorem. Every increasing right-continuous \(F\) defines a unique Borel measure \(\mu_F\) such that \(\mu_F((a,b])=F(b)-F(a)\).

Atoms and jumps

Proposition. The point mass at \(x\) is \(\mu_F(\{x\})=F(x)-F(x-)\). Thus jumps of \(F\) are exactly atoms of the measure.

Densities

Theorem. The measure \(\mu_F\) is absolutely continuous with respect to Lebesgue measure exactly when \(F\) is absolutely continuous. Then \(F(x)=\int_{-\infty}^x f(t)\,\mathrm{d}t\).

Quantiles

Definition. The generalized inverse \(F^{-1}(u)=\inf\{x:F(x)\ge u\}\) works even with jumps. If \(U\) is uniform on \((0,1)\), then \(F^{-1}(U)\) has distribution \(F\).
