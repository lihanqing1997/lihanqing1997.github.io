19. Approximation by smooth and simple functions

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/real-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/real-analysis/modes-of-convergence/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/real-analysis/fourier-series-and-orthogonality/" %}

Approximation by smooth and simple functions records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the real analysis note.

Definition

Definition. Approximation theorems replace complicated measurable or integrable functions by simple, continuous, or smooth functions under appropriate norms and measures.

Proposition

Proposition. On many regular measure spaces, simple functions are dense in \(L^p\), and smooth compactly supported functions are dense in \(L^p(\mathbb R^d)\) for \(1\le p<\infty\).

Proof

Proof. First approximate by simple functions, then approximate measurable sets by regular open and compact sets, and finally smooth indicators by convolution with mollifiers.

Remark

Remark. Approximation is how abstract integration becomes calculable. It also lets theorems be proved first for nice functions and then extended by density.
