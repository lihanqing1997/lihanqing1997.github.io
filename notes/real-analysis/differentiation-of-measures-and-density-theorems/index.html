37. Differentiation of measures and density theorems

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/real-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/real-analysis/lebesgue-differentiation-theorem/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/real-analysis/modes-of-convergence/" %}

Differentiation can be applied to measures, not only functions. Local ratios of small-ball masses recover Radon-Nikodym densities.

Local density ratios

Definition. For measures \(\nu\) and \(\mu\), ratios such as \(\nu(B(x,r))/\mu(B(x,r))\) describe the local density of \(\nu\) relative to \(\mu\).

Differentiation of measures

Theorem. Under standard Radon hypotheses on \(\mathbb R^d\), if \(\nu\ll\mu\), then small-ball density ratios converge to \(\mathrm{d}\nu/\mathrm{d}\mu\) for \(\mu\)-almost every point.

Singular part detection

Proposition. If \(\nu\perp\mu\), the mass of \(\nu\) can concentrate on a set of \(\mu\)-measure zero. Local density ratios then reveal the singular component rather than a finite density.

Vitali covering

Lemma. Vitali-type covering lemmas select disjoint balls from arbitrary fine covers while controlling the remaining set. They are the geometric tools behind differentiation theorems.

Statistical reading

Remark. Likelihood ratios compare measures globally; local density ratios compare them around small neighborhoods. Smooth statistical models turn these comparisons into score and information expansions.
