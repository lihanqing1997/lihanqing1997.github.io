Asymptotic Tools

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/statistical-inference/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/statistical-inference/likelihood-and-mle/" %}

Asymptotic inference studies approximations as \(n\to\infty\). The goal is not that \(n\) is literally infinite, but that large-sample limits reveal the first-order behavior of estimators, tests, and confidence sets.

Modes of convergence

\(X_n\to X\) in probability if, for every \(\varepsilon>0\), \(P(|X_n-X|>\varepsilon)\to0\). It means random error becomes negligible.

\(X_n\rightsquigarrow X\) in distribution if \(E f(X_n)\to E f(X)\) for every bounded continuous \(f\). It means the laws of \(X_n\) converge, not necessarily the variables on the same sample space.

\(X_n\to X\) almost surely if \(P(X_n\to X)=1\). This is pathwise convergence and is stronger than convergence in probability.

Stochastic orders

\(X_n=o_p(a_n)\) means \(X_n/a_n\to0\) in probability. \(X_n=O_p(a_n)\) means \(X_n/a_n\) is tight: for every \(\varepsilon>0\), there is \(M<\infty\) such that \(\sup_nP(|X_n/a_n|>M)<\varepsilon\).

These symbols let us write Taylor expansions with random remainders. For example, if \(R_n=o_p(n^{-1/2})\), then \(\sqrt n R_n=o_p(1)\), so \(R_n\) does not affect a \(\sqrt n\)-limit.

Definition. A sequence \(T_n\) is asymptotically linear for \(\theta_0\) with influence function \(\phi\) if \(P\phi=0\), \(P\|\phi\|^2<\infty\), and

\[ T_n-\theta_0=P_n\phi+o_p(n^{-1/2}). \]

Equivalently, \(\sqrt n(T_n-\theta_0)=n^{-1/2}\sum_{i=1}^n\phi(X_i)+o_p(1)\).

Continuous mapping and Slutsky

If \(X_n\rightsquigarrow X\) and \(g\) is continuous, then \(g(X_n)\rightsquigarrow g(X)\). This is the continuous mapping theorem.

If \(X_n\rightsquigarrow X\) and \(Y_n\to c\) in probability, then

\[ X_n+Y_n\rightsquigarrow X+c,\qquad X_nY_n\rightsquigarrow cX. \]

This is Slutsky's lemma. It justifies replacing unknown deterministic limits by consistent random estimates in asymptotic distributions.

Central limit theorem

If \(X_1,X_2,\ldots\) are i.i.d. with \(EX_1=\mu\) and \(\operatorname{Var}(X_1)=\sigma^2<\infty\), then

\[ \sqrt n(\bar X_n-\mu)\rightsquigarrow N(0,\sigma^2). \]

For vectors, if \(E X_i=\mu\) and \(\operatorname{Var}(X_i)=\Sigma\), then \(\sqrt n(\bar X_n-\mu)\rightsquigarrow N(0,\Sigma)\). Statistical asymptotic normality usually comes from applying this vector CLT to scores, estimating equations, or influence functions.

Delta method

Let \(T_n\) estimate \(\theta\in\mathbb R^d\) and suppose

\[ \sqrt n(T_n-\theta)\rightsquigarrow Z. \]

If \(g:\mathbb R^d\to\mathbb R^k\) is differentiable at \(\theta\), with derivative matrix \(\dot g_\theta\), then

\[ \sqrt n\{g(T_n)-g(\theta)\}\rightsquigarrow \dot g_\theta Z. \]

In particular, if \(Z\sim N(0,V)\), then \(\dot g_\theta Z\sim N(0,\dot g_\theta V\dot g_\theta^\top)\).

Theorem. If \(T_n\) is asymptotically linear with influence function \(\phi\), then

\[ \sqrt n(T_n-\theta_0)\rightsquigarrow N(0,P\phi\phi^\top). \]

Proof. By asymptotic linearity, \(\sqrt n(T_n-\theta_0)=n^{-1/2}\sum_i\phi(X_i)+o_p(1)\). The vector CLT gives \(n^{-1/2}\sum_i\phi(X_i)\rightsquigarrow N(0,P\phi\phi^\top)\). Slutsky's lemma removes the \(o_p(1)\) remainder.

Why the delta method is true

Differentiability gives

\[ g(T_n)-g(\theta)=\dot g_\theta(T_n-\theta)+r(T_n), \qquad \frac{\|r(T_n)\|}{\|T_n-\theta\|}\to0 \]

whenever \(T_n\to\theta\). Since \(\sqrt n(T_n-\theta)=O_p(1)\), we have \(T_n-\theta=O_p(n^{-1/2})\) and \(\sqrt n r(T_n)=o_p(1)\). Slutsky then gives the result.

How these tools are used

The common pattern is: find a linear expansion

\[ \sqrt n(T_n-\theta_0)=\frac1{\sqrt n}\sum_{i=1}^n\phi(X_i)+o_p(1), \]

then apply the CLT to the sum and Slutsky to the negligible term. The function \(\phi\) is often called an influence function.

Outils Asymptotiques

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Retour" back_url="/notes/statistical-inference/" next_label="Page suivante" next_url="/notes/statistical-inference/likelihood-and-mle/" %}

L'inférence asymptotique étudie les approximations quand \(n\to\infty\). L'idée n'est pas que \(n\) soit infini, mais que la limite révèle le comportement de premier ordre des estimateurs, tests et régions de confiance.

Modes de convergence

\(X_n\to X\) en probabilité si, pour tout \(\varepsilon>0\), \(P(|X_n-X|>\varepsilon)\to0\). L'erreur aléatoire devient négligeable.

\(X_n\rightsquigarrow X\) en loi si \(E f(X_n)\to E f(X)\) pour toute fonction continue bornée \(f\). Les lois convergent, même si les variables ne convergent pas point par point.

\(X_n\to X\) presque sûrement si \(P(X_n\to X)=1\). Cette convergence trajectorielle implique la convergence en probabilité.

Ordres stochastiques

\(X_n=o_p(a_n)\) signifie \(X_n/a_n\to0\) en probabilité. \(X_n=O_p(a_n)\) signifie que \(X_n/a_n\) est tendue : pour tout \(\varepsilon>0\), il existe \(M<\infty\) tel que \(\sup_nP(|X_n/a_n|>M)<\varepsilon\).

Ces notations permettent d'écrire des développements de Taylor avec des restes aléatoires. Si \(R_n=o_p(n^{-1/2})\), alors \(\sqrt nR_n=o_p(1)\).

Définition. Une suite \(T_n\) est asymptotiquement linéaire pour \(\theta_0\), de fonction d'influence \(\phi\), si \(P\phi=0\), \(P\|\phi\|^2<\infty\), et

\[ T_n-\theta_0=P_n\phi+o_p(n^{-1/2}). \]

Application continue et Slutsky

Si \(X_n\rightsquigarrow X\) et si \(g\) est continue, alors \(g(X_n)\rightsquigarrow g(X)\). C'est le théorème de l'application continue.

Si \(X_n\rightsquigarrow X\) et \(Y_n\to c\) en probabilité, alors

\[ X_n+Y_n\rightsquigarrow X+c,\qquad X_nY_n\rightsquigarrow cX. \]

Le lemme de Slutsky justifie le remplacement de limites déterministes inconnues par des estimateurs consistants.

Théorème central limite

Si \(X_1,X_2,\ldots\) sont i.i.d., \(EX_1=\mu\), et \(\operatorname{Var}(X_1)=\sigma^2<\infty\), alors

\[ \sqrt n(\bar X_n-\mu)\rightsquigarrow N(0,\sigma^2). \]

Pour des vecteurs, \(\sqrt n(\bar X_n-\mu)\rightsquigarrow N(0,\Sigma)\). La normalité asymptotique vient souvent de ce TCL appliqué aux scores, équations d'estimation ou fonctions d'influence.

Méthode delta

Supposons que \(T_n\) estime \(\theta\in\mathbb R^d\) et que

\[ \sqrt n(T_n-\theta)\rightsquigarrow Z. \]

Si \(g:\mathbb R^d\to\mathbb R^k\) est différentiable en \(\theta\), de dérivée \(\dot g_\theta\), alors

\[ \sqrt n\{g(T_n)-g(\theta)\}\rightsquigarrow \dot g_\theta Z. \]

Théorème. Si \(T_n\) est asymptotiquement linéaire avec fonction d'influence \(\phi\), alors

\[ \sqrt n(T_n-\theta_0)\rightsquigarrow N(0,P\phi\phi^\top). \]

Preuve. L'expansion linéaire donne \(\sqrt n(T_n-\theta_0)=n^{-1/2}\sum_i\phi(X_i)+o_p(1)\). Le TCL vectoriel donne la limite normale de la somme, puis Slutsky élimine le reste.

Preuve de l'idée

La différentiabilité donne

\[ g(T_n)-g(\theta)=\dot g_\theta(T_n-\theta)+r(T_n), \qquad \frac{\|r(T_n)\|}{\|T_n-\theta\|}\to0. \]

Comme \(\sqrt n(T_n-\theta)=O_p(1)\), on obtient \(\sqrt n r(T_n)=o_p(1)\). Slutsky donne la limite.

Usage typique

On cherche souvent une expansion linéaire

\[ \sqrt n(T_n-\theta_0)=\frac1{\sqrt n}\sum_{i=1}^n\phi(X_i)+o_p(1). \]

Le TCL traite la somme, et Slutsky élimine le terme négligeable. La fonction \(\phi\) est souvent une fonction d'influence.

渐近工具

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="返回" back_url="/notes/statistical-inference/" next_label="下一页" next_url="/notes/statistical-inference/likelihood-and-mle/" %}

渐近推断研究 \(n\to\infty\) 时的近似。这里并不是说样本量真的无限大，而是用极限揭示估计量、检验和置信集的一阶行为。

收敛方式

若对任意 \(\varepsilon>0\)，\(P(|X_n-X|>\varepsilon)\to0\)，则 \(X_n\to X\) 依概率收敛。它表示随机误差变得可以忽略。

若对任意有界连续函数 \(f\)，有 \(E f(X_n)\to E f(X)\)，则 \(X_n\rightsquigarrow X\) 依分布收敛。它描述的是分布收敛，而不要求同一样本空间上的逐点收敛。

若 \(P(X_n\to X)=1\)，则称 \(X_n\to X\) 几乎必然收敛。这比依概率收敛更强。

随机阶

\(X_n=o_p(a_n)\) 表示 \(X_n/a_n\to0\) 依概率成立。\(X_n=O_p(a_n)\) 表示 \(X_n/a_n\) 是紧的：对任意 \(\varepsilon>0\)，存在 \(M<\infty\)，使得 \(\sup_nP(|X_n/a_n|>M)<\varepsilon\)。

这些符号使我们能够写带随机余项的 Taylor 展开。若 \(R_n=o_p(n^{-1/2})\)，则 \(\sqrt nR_n=o_p(1)\)，所以它不影响 \(\sqrt n\)-极限。

定义. 若 \(P\phi=0\)、\(P\|\phi\|^2<\infty\)，并且

\[ T_n-\theta_0=P_n\phi+o_p(n^{-1/2}), \]

则称 \(T_n\) 在 \(\theta_0\) 处渐近线性，\(\phi\) 称为影响函数。

连续映射与 Slutsky

若 \(X_n\rightsquigarrow X\)，且 \(g\) 连续，则 \(g(X_n)\rightsquigarrow g(X)\)。这就是连续映射定理。

若 \(X_n\rightsquigarrow X\)，且 \(Y_n\to c\) 依概率，则

\[ X_n+Y_n\rightsquigarrow X+c,\qquad X_nY_n\rightsquigarrow cX. \]

这就是 Slutsky 引理。它允许我们在渐近分布中用一致估计量替换未知常数。

中心极限定理

若 \(X_1,X_2,\ldots\) 独立同分布，\(EX_1=\mu\)，\(\operatorname{Var}(X_1)=\sigma^2<\infty\)，则

\[ \sqrt n(\bar X_n-\mu)\rightsquigarrow N(0,\sigma^2). \]

向量版本中，若方差矩阵为 \(\Sigma\)，则 \(\sqrt n(\bar X_n-\mu)\rightsquigarrow N(0,\Sigma)\)。统计中的渐近正态性通常来自把这个向量 CLT 用在 score、估计方程或影响函数上。

Delta 方法

设 \(T_n\) 估计 \(\theta\in\mathbb R^d\)，且

\[ \sqrt n(T_n-\theta)\rightsquigarrow Z. \]

若 \(g:\mathbb R^d\to\mathbb R^k\) 在 \(\theta\) 处可微，导数矩阵为 \(\dot g_\theta\)，则

\[ \sqrt n\{g(T_n)-g(\theta)\}\rightsquigarrow \dot g_\theta Z. \]

若 \(Z\sim N(0,V)\)，则极限为 \(N(0,\dot g_\theta V\dot g_\theta^\top)\)。

定理. 若 \(T_n\) 具有影响函数 \(\phi\) 的渐近线性展开，则

\[ \sqrt n(T_n-\theta_0)\rightsquigarrow N(0,P\phi\phi^\top). \]

证明. 渐近线性给出 \(\sqrt n(T_n-\theta_0)=n^{-1/2}\sum_i\phi(X_i)+o_p(1)\)。向量 CLT 给出求和项收敛到 \(N(0,P\phi\phi^\top)\)，再由 Slutsky 引理去掉 \(o_p(1)\) 余项。

为什么成立

可微性给出

\[ g(T_n)-g(\theta)=\dot g_\theta(T_n-\theta)+r(T_n), \qquad \frac{\|r(T_n)\|}{\|T_n-\theta\|}\to0. \]

由于 \(\sqrt n(T_n-\theta)=O_p(1)\)，有 \(T_n-\theta=O_p(n^{-1/2})\)，从而 \(\sqrt n r(T_n)=o_p(1)\)。再用 Slutsky 即可。

典型用法

常见目标是找到线性展开

\[ \sqrt n(T_n-\theta_0)=\frac1{\sqrt n}\sum_{i=1}^n\phi(X_i)+o_p(1). \]

然后对求和项使用 CLT，对可忽略项使用 Slutsky。函数 \(\phi\) 通常称为影响函数。
