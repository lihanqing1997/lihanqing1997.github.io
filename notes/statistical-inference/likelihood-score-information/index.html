Likelihood, score, and information

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/statistical-inference/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/statistical-inference/statistical-models-experiments/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/statistical-inference/sufficiency-ancillarity-exponential-families/" %}

Likelihood is the central object of parametric inference. It treats the observed data as fixed and compares parameter values by how much probability density they assign to the observation.

Definition. In a dominated model with i.i.d. observations, the likelihood and log-likelihood are \[ L_n(\theta)=\prod_{i=1}^np_\theta(X_i),\qquad \ell_n(\theta)=\sum_{i=1}^n\log p_\theta(X_i). \] The score is \(S_n(\theta)=\dot\ell_n(\theta)\), and the observed Hessian is \(H_n(\theta)=\ddot\ell_n(\theta)\).

Fisher information

For one observation write \(s_\theta(X)=\partial_\theta\log p_\theta(X)\). Under regularity conditions, the Fisher information is \[ I(\theta)=E_\theta s_\theta(X)s_\theta(X)^\top. \]

Information identity. If differentiation may be passed under the integral sign and \(p_\theta\) is twice differentiable, then \[ E_\theta s_\theta(X)=0,\qquad I(\theta)=-E_\theta\partial_\theta^2\log p_\theta(X). \]

Proof. Since \(\int p_\theta\,\mathrm{d}\mu=1\), differentiating gives \[ 0=\int \dot p_\theta\,\mathrm{d}\mu =\int \frac{\dot p_\theta}{p_\theta}p_\theta\,\mathrm{d}\mu =E_\theta s_\theta(X). \] Differentiate \(E_\theta s_\theta=0\) once more. The derivative of the density contributes \(s_\theta s_\theta^\top\), while the derivative of the score contributes \(\partial_\theta s_\theta=\partial_\theta^2\log p_\theta\). Thus \(0=E_\theta\partial_\theta^2\log p_\theta+E_\theta s_\theta s_\theta^\top\).

Reparametrization

If \(\eta=g(\theta)\) is one-to-one and differentiable, information transforms by the chain rule. For scalar \(\theta\), \[ I_\eta(\eta)=I_\theta(\theta)\left(\frac{\mathrm{d}\theta}{\mathrm{d}\eta}\right)^2. \] This is why standard errors should be transformed with the delta method rather than by informal algebra alone.

Vraisemblance, score et information

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Retour" back_url="/notes/statistical-inference/" prev_label="Page précédente" prev_url="/notes/statistical-inference/statistical-models-experiments/" next_label="Page suivante" next_url="/notes/statistical-inference/sufficiency-ancillarity-exponential-families/" %}

La vraisemblance est l’objet central de l’inférence paramétrique. Les données observées sont fixées, et les valeurs du paramètre sont comparées par la densité qu’elles attribuent à l’observation.

Définition. Dans un modèle dominé avec observations i.i.d., \[ L_n(\theta)=\prod_{i=1}^np_\theta(X_i),\qquad \ell_n(\theta)=\sum_{i=1}^n\log p_\theta(X_i). \] Le score est \(S_n(\theta)=\dot\ell_n(\theta)\), et la Hessienne observée est \(H_n(\theta)=\ddot\ell_n(\theta)\).

Information de Fisher

Pour une observation, \(s_\theta(X)=\partial_\theta\log p_\theta(X)\). Sous conditions de régularité, \[ I(\theta)=E_\theta s_\theta(X)s_\theta(X)^\top. \]

Identité d’information. Si l’on peut différencier sous le signe intégral et si \(p_\theta\) est deux fois différentiable, alors \[ E_\theta s_\theta(X)=0,\qquad I(\theta)=-E_\theta\partial_\theta^2\log p_\theta(X). \]

Preuve. De \(\int p_\theta\,\mathrm{d}\mu=1\), on tire \[ 0=\int \dot p_\theta\,\mathrm{d}\mu =\int \frac{\dot p_\theta}{p_\theta}p_\theta\,\mathrm{d}\mu =E_\theta s_\theta(X). \] En dérivant encore \(E_\theta s_\theta=0\), la dérivée de la densité donne \(s_\theta s_\theta^\top\), et la dérivée du score donne \(\partial_\theta^2\log p_\theta\). Donc \(0=E_\theta\partial_\theta^2\log p_\theta+E_\theta s_\theta s_\theta^\top\).

Reparamétrisation

Si \(\eta=g(\theta)\) est bijective et différentiable, l’information se transforme par la règle de chaîne. Pour un paramètre scalaire, \[ I_\eta(\eta)=I_\theta(\theta)\left(\frac{\mathrm{d}\theta}{\mathrm{d}\eta}\right)^2. \]

似然、score 与信息

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="返回" back_url="/notes/statistical-inference/" prev_label="上一页" prev_url="/notes/statistical-inference/statistical-models-experiments/" next_label="下一页" next_url="/notes/statistical-inference/sufficiency-ancillarity-exponential-families/" %}

似然是参数推断的核心对象。它把观测数据视为固定，然后比较不同参数值对该观测赋予的概率密度大小。

定义. 在支配模型和 i.i.d. 观测下，似然与 log-likelihood 为 \[ L_n(\theta)=\prod_{i=1}^np_\theta(X_i),\qquad \ell_n(\theta)=\sum_{i=1}^n\log p_\theta(X_i). \] score 为 \(S_n(\theta)=\dot\ell_n(\theta)\)，观测 Hessian 为 \(H_n(\theta)=\ddot\ell_n(\theta)\)。

Fisher 信息

对单个观测，记 \(s_\theta(X)=\partial_\theta\log p_\theta(X)\)。在正则条件下，Fisher 信息为 \[ I(\theta)=E_\theta s_\theta(X)s_\theta(X)^\top. \]

信息恒等式. 若可以在积分号下求导，且 \(p_\theta\) 二阶可微，则 \[ E_\theta s_\theta(X)=0,\qquad I(\theta)=-E_\theta\partial_\theta^2\log p_\theta(X). \]

证明. 由 \(\int p_\theta\,\mathrm{d}\mu=1\)，求导得 \[ 0=\int \dot p_\theta\,\mathrm{d}\mu =\int \frac{\dot p_\theta}{p_\theta}p_\theta\,\mathrm{d}\mu =E_\theta s_\theta(X). \] 再对 \(E_\theta s_\theta=0\) 求导。密度的导数贡献 \(s_\theta s_\theta^\top\)，score 的导数贡献 \(\partial_\theta^2\log p_\theta\)。因此 \(0=E_\theta\partial_\theta^2\log p_\theta+E_\theta s_\theta s_\theta^\top\)。

重参数化

若 \(\eta=g(\theta)\) 一一且可微，信息按链式法则变换。对标量参数， \[ I_\eta(\eta)=I_\theta(\theta)\left(\frac{\mathrm{d}\theta}{\mathrm{d}\eta}\right)^2. \] 因此标准误的变换应使用 delta method，而不只是形式代数。
