First-order asymptotics describe the main Gaussian limit. Higher-order asymptotics study the error of that approximation and how to improve it.

Definition. An Edgeworth expansion approximates a distribution function by \[ P\{Z_n\le x\}=\Phi(x)+n^{-1/2}p_1(x)\phi(x)+n^{-1}p_2(x)\phi(x)+\cdots+o(n^{-1}), \] where the polynomials \(p_j\) depend on cumulants of the summands or statistic.

Cornish-Fisher idea. If an Edgeworth expansion is available, approximate quantiles can be corrected by solving the expansion for \(x\). This gives Cornish-Fisher corrections to normal critical values.

Proof. Write the desired quantile as \(x=z_\alpha+n^{-1/2}a+n^{-1}b+\cdots\). Substitute into the Edgeworth expansion and match coefficients of powers of \(n^{-1/2}\). The first coefficient \(a\) cancels the \(n^{-1/2}\) distributional error at \(z_\alpha\); later coefficients cancel higher-order errors.

Bartlett correction

Likelihood ratio statistics often have expectation \(r+c/n+o(n^{-1})\) instead of \(r\). Multiplying by \(1-c/(rn)\) can improve chi-square approximation from first-order to second-order accuracy.
