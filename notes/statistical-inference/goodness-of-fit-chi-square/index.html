Goodness-of-fit tests ask whether a whole distributional model is compatible with the data. This page keeps the inference-level tools; empirical-process details are handled in the empirical processes note.

Definition. For multinomial cell counts \(N_j\) with expected counts \(np_j(\theta)\), Pearson’s statistic is \[ X^2=\sum_{j=1}^k\frac{\{N_j-np_j(\hat\theta)\}^2}{np_j(\hat\theta)}. \]

Pearson limit. If the null model is correct, all cell probabilities are positive, and \(d\) parameters are estimated regularly, then \[ X^2\rightsquigarrow\chi^2_{k-1-d}. \]

Proof. The vector of cell proportions has a multivariate normal limit on the \(k-1\) dimensional hyperplane where components sum to zero. Estimating \(d\) regular parameters removes \(d\) additional tangent directions. Pearson’s statistic is the squared norm of the residual in the inverse multinomial covariance metric, hence has \(\chi^2_{k-1-d}\) limit.
