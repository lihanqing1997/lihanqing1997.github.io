LAN describes the local geometry of a statistical experiment near a fixed parameter. It is the bridge between likelihood expansions and Gaussian limit experiments.

Definition. The experiments \(P_\theta^{(n)}\) are LAN at \(\theta_0\) with information \(I\) if, for each fixed \(h\), \[ \log\frac{\mathrm{d}P_{\theta_0+h/\sqrt n}^{(n)}}{\mathrm{d}P_{\theta_0}^{(n)}} =h^\top\Delta_n-\frac12h^\top Ih+o_{P_{\theta_0}}(1), \qquad \Delta_n\rightsquigarrow N(0,I). \]

Le Cam first lemma. If likelihood ratios \(\mathrm{d}Q_n/\mathrm{d}P_n\) converge in distribution under \(P_n\) to a positive random variable with mean \(1\), then \(Q_n\) is contiguous with respect to \(P_n\).

Proof. If \(P_n(A_n)\to0\), then for any \(M\), \[ Q_n(A_n)=E_{P_n}\left[1_{A_n}\frac{\mathrm{d}Q_n}{\mathrm{d}P_n}\right] \le M P_n(A_n)+E_{P_n}\left[\frac{\mathrm{d}Q_n}{\mathrm{d}P_n}1_{\{\mathrm{d}Q_n/\mathrm{d}P_n\gt M\}}\right]. \] The first term vanishes. Tightness plus convergence to a mean-one limit makes the tail term small for large \(M\).

Meaning

Under LAN, local alternatives \(\theta_0+h/\sqrt n\) cannot be separated perfectly from \(\theta_0\); they shift the Gaussian central sequence by \(Ih\). This is the basis for local power and efficiency calculations.
