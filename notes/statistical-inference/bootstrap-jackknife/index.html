20. Bootstrap and jackknife

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/statistical-inference/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/statistical-inference/goodness-of-fit-chi-square/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/statistical-inference/higher-order-asymptotics/" %}

Resampling estimates uncertainty by recomputing a statistic under data-based approximations to its sampling distribution.

Nonparametric bootstrap

Definition. The nonparametric bootstrap draws \(X_1^*,\ldots,X_n^*\) i.i.d. from the empirical distribution \(P_n\). The bootstrap statistic \(T_n^*=T(X_1^*,\ldots,X_n^*)\) is studied conditionally on the observed data.

Bootstrap consistency

Theorem. For a smooth functional \(T(P)\), if \(\sqrt n(P_n-P)\rightsquigarrow G\) and \(T\) is Hadamard differentiable at \(P\), then conditionally in probability \(\sqrt n\{T(P_n^*)-T(P_n)\}\rightsquigarrow \dot T_PG\), the same limit as \(\sqrt n\{T(P_n)-T(P)\}\).

Bootstrap intervals

Proposition. Percentile, basic, bootstrap-\(t\), BC, and BCa intervals differ in how they approximate the distribution of the estimation error and correct bias, skewness, or acceleration. Studentization is often essential for reliable coverage.

Jackknife

Definition. The jackknife recomputes leave-one-out estimates \(T_{(-i)}\). Pseudovalues and their empirical variance estimate bias and standard error, especially when \(T\) has an influence-function expansion.

Failure modes

Remark. Ordinary bootstrap can fail for nonsmooth functionals, boundary parameters, cube-root asymptotics, model selection, and some dependent data. Parametric, wild, multiplier, subsampling, or problem-specific bootstrap schemes may then be needed.
