4. Subspaces and quotient spaces

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/general-topology/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/general-topology/continuity-and-homeomorphisms/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/general-topology/product-topology/" %}

Subspaces and quotient spaces records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the general topology note.

Definition

Definition. The subspace topology on \(A\subset X\) has open sets \(A\cap U\). The quotient topology for a surjection \(q:X\to Y\) declares \(V\subset Y\) open exactly when \(q^{-1}(V)\) is open.

Proposition

Proposition. The quotient topology is the finest topology on \(Y\) making \(q\) continuous, while the subspace topology is the coarsest topology on \(A\) making inclusion continuous.

Proof

Proof. Both statements follow from the direction in which inverse images are computed. Universal mapping properties characterize the constructions uniquely.

Remark

Remark. Subspaces restrict spaces; quotients glue points. Most geometric constructions use both operations repeatedly.
