15. Metrization theorems

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/general-topology/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/general-topology/tietze-extension-theorem/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/general-topology/paracompactness-and-partitions-of-unity/" %}

Metrization theorems records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the general topology note.

Definition

Definition. A metrization theorem gives conditions under which a topology comes from a metric. This asks when abstract nearness can be measured by distances.

Proposition

Proposition. A regular second-countable \(T_1\) space is metrizable. More refined theorems replace second countability by \(\sigma\)-locally finite bases.

Proof

Proof. Use countably many continuous functions separating points and closed sets, then embed the space into a product of intervals with a metric inducing the product topology.

Remark

Remark. Metrization is useful because metric spaces allow sequences, completeness, and quantitative estimates. Not every natural topology is metrizable.
