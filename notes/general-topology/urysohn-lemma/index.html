13. Urysohn lemma

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/general-topology/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/general-topology/tychonoff-theorem/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/general-topology/tietze-extension-theorem/" %}

Urysohn lemma records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the general topology note.

Definition

Definition. Urysohn lemma separates disjoint closed sets in a normal space by a continuous real-valued function.

Urysohn lemma

Theorem. If \(A,B\) are disjoint closed subsets of a normal space \(X\), there exists continuous \(f:X\to[0,1]\) with \(f(A)=0\) and \(f(B)=1\).

Proof

Proof. Use normality to build nested open sets indexed by dyadic rationals, then define \(f(x)\) as an infimum of indices whose open sets contain \(x\). The nesting proves continuity.

Remark

Remark. This theorem explains why normal spaces have enough continuous functions to encode separation topologically.
