20. Uniform spaces

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/general-topology/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/general-topology/stone-cech-compactification/" %}

Uniform spaces records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the general topology note.

Definition

Definition. A uniform space specifies which pairs of points are uniformly close through entourages of the diagonal. It abstracts uniform continuity and Cauchy behavior without requiring a metric.

Proposition

Proposition. Every metric space defines a uniformity, and uniform spaces support notions of Cauchy filters, completeness, and completion. A continuous map is uniformly continuous exactly when it pulls entourages back to entourages.

Proof

Proof. Metric entourages are sets \(\{(x,y):d(x,y)<\varepsilon\}\). The uniformity axioms encode symmetry, refinement, and triangle-like composition of entourages.

Remark

Remark. Uniform spaces sit between topology and metric geometry. They explain why some topological spaces have meaningful uniform convergence and completion theories.
