10. Countability axioms

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/general-topology/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/general-topology/local-compactness-and-one-point-compactification/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/general-topology/nets-and-filters/" %}

Countability axioms records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the general topology note.

Definition

Definition. First countability gives each point a countable neighborhood basis. Second countability gives the whole topology a countable basis. Separability means a countable dense subset exists.

Proposition

Proposition. Second countability implies first countability and separability. In metric spaces, separability implies second countability.

Proof

Proof. A countable basis restricts to countable local bases and supplies a countable dense set by choosing one point from each nonempty basis element. In metric spaces, balls centered at dense points with rational radii form a countable basis.

Remark

Remark. Countability axioms explain when sequences are enough and when topology needs nets or filters.
