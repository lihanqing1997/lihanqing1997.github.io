19. Stone-Cech compactification

Stone-Cech compactification records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the general topology note.

Definition

Definition. The Stone-Cech compactification \(\beta X\) is a compact Hausdorff space containing a completely regular space \(X\) densely and extending bounded continuous maps from \(X\).

Proposition

Proposition. Every bounded continuous map from \(X\) to a compact Hausdorff space extends uniquely to \(\beta X\). This universal property characterizes \(\beta X\).

Proof

Proof. Embed \(X\) into a product of compact intervals using all bounded continuous real-valued functions, then take the closure. Extension follows from coordinate projections and compactness.

Remark

Remark. Stone-Cech compactification is large and abstract, but it is the universal compact receptacle for bounded continuous information.
