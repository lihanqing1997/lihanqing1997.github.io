9. Local compactness and one-point compactification

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/general-topology/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/general-topology/connectedness-and-path-connectedness/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/general-topology/countability-axioms/" %}

Local compactness and one-point compactification records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the general topology note.

Definition

Definition. A space is locally compact if each point has a compact neighborhood, usually with Hausdorff assumptions. One-point compactification adds a point at infinity.

Proposition

Proposition. A locally compact noncompact Hausdorff space has a one-point compactification whose neighborhoods of infinity are complements of compact closed subsets.

Proof

Proof. Open-cover compactness is checked by taking one neighborhood of infinity, whose complement is compact, and then extracting a finite subcover there. Hausdorffness uses local compactness to separate compact sets from points.

Remark

Remark. One-point compactification formalizes the idea that Euclidean space becomes a sphere after adding infinity.
