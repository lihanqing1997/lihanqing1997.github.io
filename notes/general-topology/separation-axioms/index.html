6. Separation axioms

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/general-topology/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/general-topology/product-topology/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/general-topology/compactness/" %}

Separation axioms records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the general topology note.

Definition

Definition. Separation axioms measure how well points and closed sets can be distinguished by open neighborhoods. The common hierarchy includes \(T_0\), \(T_1\), Hausdorff, regular, and normal.

Proposition

Proposition. In Hausdorff spaces, limits of nets and sequences are unique when they exist. In normal spaces, disjoint closed sets can be separated by neighborhoods and, under stronger results, by functions.

Proof

Proof. For uniqueness, separate two proposed limits by disjoint neighborhoods and use eventual membership. For normality results, construct nested open sets around closed sets.

Remark

Remark. Separation hypotheses are often invisible in metric spaces because metrics satisfy strong separation automatically.
