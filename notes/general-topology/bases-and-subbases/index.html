2. Bases and subbases

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/general-topology/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/general-topology/topological-spaces-and-open-sets/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/general-topology/continuity-and-homeomorphisms/" %}

Bases and subbases records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the general topology note.

Definition

Definition. A basis is a collection of open sets such that every open set is a union of basis elements and intersections are locally refined by basis elements. A subbasis generates a topology by finite intersections and arbitrary unions.

Proposition

Proposition. A function is continuous if it suffices to check inverse images of subbasic open sets. A topology generated by a basis is the smallest topology containing that basis.

Proof

Proof. Because arbitrary open sets are unions of basis elements, inverse-image and closure properties reduce to basis or subbasis checks. Minimality follows by closure requirements of topologies.

Remark

Remark. Bases make abstract topologies usable. They are also how product, order, metric, and weak topologies are usually specified.
