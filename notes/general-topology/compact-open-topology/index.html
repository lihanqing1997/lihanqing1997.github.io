17. Compact-open topology

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/general-topology/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/general-topology/paracompactness-and-partitions-of-unity/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/general-topology/baire-spaces/" %}

Compact-open topology records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the general topology note.

Definition

Definition. The compact-open topology on a function space has subbasic sets \([K,U]=\{f:f(K)\subset U\}\), where \(K\) is compact and \(U\) is open.

Proposition

Proposition. For locally compact Hausdorff domains, the compact-open topology interacts well with evaluation and currying: maps into function spaces correspond to maps on products under suitable hypotheses.

Proof

Proof. Compactness controls finite subcovers needed for continuity of evaluation on neighborhoods. Local compactness supplies compact neighborhoods around domain points.

Remark

Remark. Function-space topologies encode modes of convergence. Compact-open convergence is uniform convergence on compact sets in many metric examples.
