12. Tychonoff theorem

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/general-topology/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/general-topology/nets-and-filters/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/general-topology/urysohn-lemma/" %}

Tychonoff theorem records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the general topology note.

Definition

Definition. Tychonoff theorem states that arbitrary products of compact spaces are compact in the product topology.

Tychonoff theorem

Theorem. If each \(X_i\) is compact, then \(\prod_i X_i\) is compact. This requires the product topology with finitely many coordinate restrictions in basic opens.

Proof

Proof. One proof uses ultrafilters: project an ultrafilter to each compact coordinate, choose coordinate limits, and show the original ultrafilter converges to the assembled point.

Remark

Remark. Tychonoff is a central compactness theorem and is equivalent to strong choice principles. It underlies Banach-Alaoglu and pointwise compactness results.
