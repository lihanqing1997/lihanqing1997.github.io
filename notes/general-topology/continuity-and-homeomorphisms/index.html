3. Continuity and homeomorphisms

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/general-topology/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/general-topology/bases-and-subbases/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/general-topology/subspaces-and-quotient-spaces/" %}

Continuity and homeomorphisms records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the general topology note.

Definition

Definition. A homeomorphism is a bijective continuous map with continuous inverse. Homeomorphic spaces are topologically the same.

Proposition

Proposition. Topological properties such as compactness, connectedness, separation axioms, and convergence behavior are preserved by homeomorphisms.

Proof

Proof. Translate open-cover, separation, or connectedness definitions through inverse images and images under the homeomorphism. The inverse continuity supplies the reverse implication.

Remark

Remark. Topology studies properties invariant under homeomorphism, not properties depending on coordinates, embedding, or metric formulas.
