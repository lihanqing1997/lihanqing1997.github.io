Paracompactness and partitions of unity records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the general topology note.

Definition

Definition. A space is paracompact if every open cover has a locally finite open refinement. Partitions of unity subordinate to covers distribute global constructions into local pieces.

Proposition

Proposition. Every paracompact Hausdorff space admits partitions of unity subordinate to locally finite open covers, under standard regularity assumptions.

Proof

Proof. First refine the cover to a locally finite family with closures inside chosen opens, then use Urysohn-type functions and normalize the locally finite sum.

Remark

Remark. Partitions of unity are the technical device that makes local-to-global arguments work on manifolds and bundles.
