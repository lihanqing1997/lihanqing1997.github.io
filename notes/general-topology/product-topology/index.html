5. Product topology

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/general-topology/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/general-topology/subspaces-and-quotient-spaces/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/general-topology/separation-axioms/" %}

Product topology records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the general topology note.

Definition

Definition. The product topology on \(\prod_i X_i\) is generated by coordinate projections. Basic opens restrict finitely many coordinates and leave the rest unrestricted.

Proposition

Proposition. A map \(f:Y\to\prod_i X_i\) is continuous exactly when every coordinate map \(\pi_i\circ f\) is continuous.

Proof

Proof. The product topology is initial with respect to the projections. Therefore continuity into the product is precisely continuity after composing with all projections.

Remark

Remark. Finite-coordinate control is why infinite products are compact under Tychonoff and why pointwise convergence is a product topology.
