18. Baire spaces

Baire spaces records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the general topology note.

Definition

Definition. A Baire space is one in which countable intersections of dense open sets are dense. Complete metric spaces and locally compact Hausdorff spaces are Baire spaces.

Proposition

Proposition. In a Baire space, no nonempty open set is meagre. Therefore category-generic properties cannot be proved by countably many nowhere dense exceptions.

Proof

Proof. The proof for complete metric spaces uses nested balls; for locally compact Hausdorff spaces it uses nested compact neighborhoods.

Remark

Remark. Baire category is a topological notion of largeness. It complements measure-theoretic largeness and drives many existence theorems.
