7. Compactness

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/general-topology/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/general-topology/separation-axioms/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/general-topology/connectedness-and-path-connectedness/" %}

Compactness records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the general topology note.

Definition

Definition. A space is compact if every open cover has a finite subcover. Compactness is a finite-extraction principle.

Proposition

Proposition. Continuous images of compact spaces are compact, and compact subsets of Hausdorff spaces are closed.

Proof

Proof. For images, pull back an open cover of the image to an open cover of the domain. For closedness in Hausdorff spaces, separate an exterior point from each point of the compact set and extract a finite subcover.

Remark

Remark. Compactness is one of the main reasons topological abstraction is useful: it makes global conclusions from local open information.
