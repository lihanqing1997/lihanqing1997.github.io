Smooth manifolds and charts records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential geometry note.

Definition

Definition. A smooth \(n\)-manifold is a Hausdorff second-countable space locally modeled on \(\mathbb R^n\) with smooth transition maps between charts.

Proposition

Proposition. Smooth compatibility of charts is an equivalence relation after passing to maximal atlases. Smooth maps between manifolds are maps whose coordinate representations are smooth.

Proof

Proof. Check reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity using composition of smooth transition maps. Smoothness of maps is independent of chart choices because transitions are smooth.

Remark

Remark. Manifolds are spaces that locally admit calculus but need not have global coordinates. This local-global tension drives differential geometry.
