Submanifold geometry records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential geometry note.

Definition

Definition. For a submanifold of a Riemannian manifold, ambient derivatives split into tangential and normal parts. The normal part is the second fundamental form.

Proposition

Proposition. The Gauss equation relates intrinsic curvature of a submanifold to ambient curvature and the second fundamental form.

Proof

Proof. Decompose the ambient Levi-Civita connection into tangent and normal components, then compare curvature expressions before and after projection.

Remark

Remark. Submanifold geometry separates what is intrinsic from what depends on the embedding. This distinction is central in classical surface theory.
