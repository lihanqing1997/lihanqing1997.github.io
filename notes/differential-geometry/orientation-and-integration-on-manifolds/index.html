7. Orientation and integration on manifolds

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/differential-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/differential-geometry/differential-forms/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/differential-geometry/stokes-theorem/" %}

Orientation and integration on manifolds records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential geometry note.

Definition

Definition. An orientation is a consistent choice of positive bases in tangent spaces. A compactly supported top-degree form can be integrated over an oriented manifold by using charts and partitions of unity.

Proposition

Proposition. The integral of a top-degree form is independent of the oriented atlas and partition of unity used to define it.

Proof

Proof. Change of variables controls coordinate changes, with the sign determined by orientation. Partitions of unity reduce global integration to chartwise integration and recombine locally finite sums.

Remark

Remark. Orientation is what makes signed volume meaningful. Without it one integrates densities rather than top forms.
