17. Principal bundles and connections

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/differential-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/differential-geometry/lie-groups-and-lie-algebras/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/differential-geometry/characteristic-classes/" %}

Principal bundles and connections records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential geometry note.

Definition

Definition. A principal \(G\)-bundle is a space locally modeled on \(U\times G\) with a free transitive right \(G\)-action on fibers. A connection chooses horizontal directions invariant under the group action.

Proposition

Proposition. Connections can be described by horizontal distributions or by Lie-algebra-valued connection one-forms satisfying equivariance and normalization conditions.

Proof

Proof. Local trivializations split tangent vectors into base and fiber parts. Transformation rules under changes of trivialization give the connection-form conditions.

Remark

Remark. Bundles and connections provide the natural language for gauge theory, holonomy, characteristic classes, and many geometric structures.
