2. Tangent spaces and differentials

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/differential-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/differential-geometry/smooth-manifolds-and-charts/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/differential-geometry/vector-fields-and-flows/" %}

Tangent spaces and differentials records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential geometry note.

Definition

Definition. The tangent space \(T_pM\) can be defined by derivations at \(p\), equivalence classes of curves through \(p\), or coordinate tangent vectors. The differential \(\mathrm{d}f_p:T_pM\to T_{f(p)}N\) is the induced linear map.

Proposition

Proposition. The curve, derivation, and coordinate definitions of tangent vectors are naturally isomorphic, and the differential satisfies the chain rule.

Proof

Proof. A curve defines a derivation by differentiating functions along it. Coordinates identify derivations with directional derivatives, and compatibility follows from the ordinary chain rule.

Remark

Remark. Tangent spaces are where nonlinear geometry becomes linear at a point. They are the basic fibers of every tensor construction.
