13. Geodesics and exponential map

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/differential-geometry/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/differential-geometry/levi-civita-connection/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/differential-geometry/curvature-tensors/" %}

Geodesics and exponential map records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential geometry note.

Definition

Definition. A geodesic is a curve \(\gamma\) satisfying \(\nabla_{\dot\gamma}\dot\gamma=0\). The exponential map sends an initial tangent vector to the point reached at time one by the corresponding geodesic.

Proposition

Proposition. For each point and tangent vector, there is a unique geodesic with those initial data, at least for small time. The exponential map is a local diffeomorphism near the zero vector.

Proof

Proof. In coordinates, the geodesic equation is a second-order ODE with smooth coefficients. The inverse function theorem applies to the derivative of the exponential map at zero.

Remark

Remark. Geodesics generalize straight lines, but global minimizing behavior can fail after conjugate points or cut loci.
