Characteristic classes records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential geometry note.

Definition

Definition. Characteristic classes assign cohomology classes to vector bundles, measuring obstructions to triviality and encoding curvature information.

Proposition

Proposition. Chern-Weil theory constructs de Rham cohomology classes from invariant polynomials applied to curvature forms, and these classes are independent of the chosen connection.

Proof

Proof. Compare two connections along an affine path; the derivative of the characteristic form is exact by the Bianchi identity and invariance of the polynomial.

Remark

Remark. Characteristic classes are global invariants extracted from local curvature. They are one bridge from differential geometry to topology.
