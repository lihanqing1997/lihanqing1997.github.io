Curvature tensors records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential geometry note.

Definition

Definition. The Riemann curvature tensor measures noncommutativity of covariant derivatives: \(R(X,Y)Z=\nabla_X\nabla_YZ-\nabla_Y\nabla_XZ-\nabla_{[X,Y]}Z\).

Proposition

Proposition. Curvature has algebraic symmetries and satisfies the Bianchi identities. Sectional, Ricci, and scalar curvature are contractions or evaluations of the Riemann tensor.

Proof

Proof. Use torsion-free and metric-compatibility properties of the Levi-Civita connection to derive symmetries. Cyclic summation of covariant derivative commutators gives Bianchi identities.

Remark

Remark. Curvature is the obstruction to flattening geometry by coordinates. It is local but has global consequences through comparison and topology.
