8. Stokes theorem

Stokes theorem records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential geometry note.

Definition

Definition. Stokes theorem relates the integral of an exterior derivative over a manifold to the integral of the form over the boundary.

Stokes theorem

Theorem. For a compact oriented manifold with boundary, \(\int_M \mathrm{d}\omega=\int_{\partial M}\omega\).

Proof

Proof. Use partitions of unity to reduce to forms supported in coordinate half-spaces. There the theorem becomes the fundamental theorem of calculus with sign conventions determined by boundary orientation.

Remark

Remark. Stokes theorem unifies the fundamental theorem of calculus, Green theorem, divergence theorem, and classical Stokes theorem.
