Riemannian metrics records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential geometry note.

Definition

Definition. A Riemannian metric assigns an inner product \(g_p\) to each tangent space smoothly. It defines lengths, angles, gradients, volume, and distance.

Proposition

Proposition. Every smooth manifold admits a Riemannian metric, using local Euclidean metrics glued by a partition of unity.

Proof

Proof. Choose a locally finite atlas and a subordinate partition of unity. Pull local Euclidean inner products to the manifold and sum them with nonnegative partition weights.

Remark

Remark. A Riemannian metric is extra structure, not part of the definition of a smooth manifold. It turns differential topology into geometry.
