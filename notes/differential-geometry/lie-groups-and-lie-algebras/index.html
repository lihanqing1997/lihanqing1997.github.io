Lie groups and Lie algebras records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential geometry note.

Definition

Definition. A Lie group is both a smooth manifold and a group with smooth multiplication and inversion. Its Lie algebra is the tangent space at the identity with bracket from left-invariant vector fields.

Proposition

Proposition. A Lie group homomorphism induces a Lie algebra homomorphism. The exponential map sends Lie algebra elements to one-parameter subgroups locally.

Proof

Proof. Differentiate the group homomorphism at the identity and use compatibility with left translations. One-parameter subgroups solve left-invariant ODEs.

Remark

Remark. Lie theory turns continuous symmetry into linearized algebra plus global topology.
