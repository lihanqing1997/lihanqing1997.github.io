Differential forms records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential geometry note.

Definition

Definition. A differential \(k\)-form is a smooth alternating covariant \(k\)-tensor field. Forms can be pulled back by smooth maps and wedged together.

Proposition

Proposition. The exterior derivative \(\mathrm{d}\) maps \(k\)-forms to \((k+1)\)-forms, satisfies \(\mathrm{d}^2=0\), and obeys the graded Leibniz rule.

Proof

Proof. Define \(\mathrm{d}\) in coordinates by differentiating coefficients and wedging coordinate one-forms. Coordinate independence follows from the chain rule and alternating algebra.

Remark

Remark. Forms are the natural objects to integrate on oriented manifolds. The identity \(\mathrm{d}^2=0\) is the algebraic seed of de Rham cohomology.
