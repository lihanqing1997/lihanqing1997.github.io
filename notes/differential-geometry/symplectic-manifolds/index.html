Symplectic manifolds records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential geometry note.

Definition

Definition. A symplectic manifold is an even-dimensional manifold with a closed nondegenerate two-form \(\omega\). Nondegeneracy identifies vector fields with one-forms.

Darboux theorem

Theorem. Darboux theorem says every symplectic form is locally equivalent to the standard form \(\sum_i \mathrm{d}q_i\wedge\mathrm{d}p_i\). Thus symplectic geometry has no local curvature invariant.

Proof

Proof. Moser's path method constructs a time-dependent vector field whose flow transports one family of symplectic forms to another. Closedness and nondegeneracy make the equation solvable.

Remark

Remark. Symplectic geometry is globally rich precisely because it is locally uniform. Its invariants are global and dynamical rather than local metric curvature.
