Bifurcations records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential equations note.

Definition

Definition. A bifurcation occurs when qualitative dynamics change as a parameter varies, often because a linearized operator loses hyperbolicity.

Proposition

Proposition. Saddle-node, transcritical, pitchfork, and Hopf bifurcations are normal forms describing generic low-dimensional changes under nondegeneracy conditions.

Proof

Proof. Reduce the system to a center manifold containing the neutral directions, then use Taylor expansion and coordinate changes to identify the leading normal form.

Remark

Remark. Bifurcation theory is local geometry of families of differential equations.
