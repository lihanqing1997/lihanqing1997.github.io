Variational methods records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential equations note.

Definition

Definition. Variational methods solve differential equations by finding critical points or minimizers of energy functionals.

Proposition

Proposition. Coercive weakly lower semicontinuous functionals on reflexive Banach spaces attain minimizers on weakly closed constraint sets. Euler-Lagrange equations then give weak PDEs.

Proof

Proof. Take a minimizing sequence, use coercivity for boundedness, extract a weakly convergent subsequence by reflexivity, and use lower semicontinuity to pass to the limit.

Remark

Remark. Variational methods explain why compactness, convexity, and weak convergence are central to PDE existence theory.
