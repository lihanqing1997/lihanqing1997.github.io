5. Flows and phase portraits

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/differential-equations/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/differential-equations/gronwall-inequalities/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/differential-equations/linear-systems-with-constant-coefficients/" %}

Flows and phase portraits records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential equations note.

Definition

Definition. An autonomous ODE \(\dot x=f(x)\) defines a local flow \(\Phi_t(x_0)\), moving initial states along solution curves.

Proposition

Proposition. Where the flow is defined, it satisfies \(\Phi_0=\operatorname{id}\) and \(\Phi_{t+s}=\Phi_t\circ\Phi_s\). Orbits partition the phase space away from nonuniqueness.

Proof

Proof. Uniqueness implies that solving from \(x_0\) for time \(s\) and then for time \(t\) equals solving once for time \(s+t\).

Remark

Remark. The flow viewpoint turns ODEs into geometry of trajectories. It is the language of dynamical systems.
