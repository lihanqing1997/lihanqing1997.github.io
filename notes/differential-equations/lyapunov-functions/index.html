Lyapunov functions records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential equations note.

Definition

Definition. A Lyapunov function is a scalar function that decreases along trajectories, certifying stability without solving the equation explicitly.

Proposition

Proposition. If \(V\) is positive definite near an equilibrium and \(\dot V=\nabla V\cdot f\le0\), then the equilibrium is stable. If \(\dot V<0\) away from the equilibrium, one obtains asymptotic stability under suitable compactness conditions.

Proof

Proof. Sublevel sets of \(V\) trap trajectories. Strict decrease prevents trajectories from remaining away from the equilibrium indefinitely.

Remark

Remark. Lyapunov methods replace explicit solutions by energy estimates, paralleling PDE energy methods.
