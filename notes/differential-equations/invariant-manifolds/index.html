Invariant manifolds records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential equations note.

Definition

Definition. Invariant manifolds are submanifolds carried into themselves by a flow. Stable and unstable manifolds collect points converging to an equilibrium forward or backward in time.

Stable manifold theorem

Theorem. Near a hyperbolic equilibrium, there are stable and unstable manifolds tangent to the stable and unstable eigenspaces of the linearization.

Proof

Proof. Write the nonlinear equation as a perturbation of its linear part and solve a fixed-point problem in a space of decaying curves. The tangent statement follows from the contraction construction.

Remark

Remark. Invariant manifold theory explains how linearized dynamics persist in nonlinear systems when no eigenvalues lie on the imaginary axis.
