17. Wave equation

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/differential-equations/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/differential-equations/heat-equation/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/differential-equations/conservation-laws/" %}

Wave equation records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential equations note.

Definition

Definition. The wave equation \(\partial_t^2u-c^2\Delta u=0\) models propagation with finite speed. Initial displacement and velocity determine the solution.

Proposition

Proposition. Energy for the wave equation is conserved under suitable boundary conditions: \(E(t)=\frac12\int(|\partial_tu|^2+c^2|\nabla u|^2)\,\mathrm{d}x\) is constant.

Proof

Proof. Multiply the equation by \(\partial_tu\), integrate in space, and integrate by parts. Boundary terms vanish under decay, periodic, or compatible boundary conditions.

Remark

Remark. Hyperbolic equations preserve and transport information rather than immediately smoothing it.
