18. Conservation laws

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/differential-equations/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/differential-equations/wave-equation/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/differential-equations/semigroup-methods/" %}

Conservation laws records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential equations note.

Definition

Definition. A scalar conservation law has the form \(\partial_tu+\nabla\cdot F(u)=0\). Weak solutions are necessary because shocks can form from smooth initial data.

Proposition

Proposition. Weak solutions are not unique without entropy conditions. Entropy inequalities select physically relevant solutions and imply contraction principles in many scalar cases.

Proof

Proof. Integrate the conservation law over spacetime regions and move derivatives onto test functions. Entropy conditions are derived from convex entropy pairs and limiting viscosity approximations.

Remark

Remark. Conservation laws show why weak formulation alone may be insufficient; admissibility matters.
