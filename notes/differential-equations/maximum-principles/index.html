Maximum principles records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential equations note.

Definition

Definition. Maximum principles say that solutions or subsolutions of elliptic and parabolic equations attain extrema on the boundary or initial boundary, under sign conditions.

Proposition

Proposition. If \(Lu\ge0\) for a uniformly elliptic operator with suitable zeroth-order sign, then an interior maximum forces rigidity; otherwise maxima occur on the boundary.

Proof

Proof. At an interior maximum, first derivatives vanish and the Hessian is nonpositive. The elliptic operator sign then contradicts strict subsolution behavior; approximation handles weak cases.

Remark

Remark. Maximum principles provide qualitative control without explicit solution formulas.
