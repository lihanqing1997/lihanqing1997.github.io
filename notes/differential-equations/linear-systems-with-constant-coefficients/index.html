Linear systems with constant coefficients records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential equations note.

Definition

Definition. A constant-coefficient linear system has the form \(\dot x=Ax\). Its solutions are given by the matrix exponential \(e^{tA}\).

Proposition

Proposition. The matrix exponential satisfies \(\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}t}e^{tA}=Ae^{tA}\), \(e^{0A}=I\), and gives the unique solution \(x(t)=e^{tA}x_0\).

Proof

Proof. Differentiate the uniformly convergent power series for \(e^{tA}\). Uniqueness follows from linear ODE theory or from applying the inverse exponential to a solution.

Remark

Remark. Spectral information about \(A\) becomes time behavior of the system: decay, growth, oscillation, and resonance.
