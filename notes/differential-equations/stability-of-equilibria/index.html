Stability of equilibria records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential equations note.

Definition

Definition. An equilibrium \(x^\star\) satisfies \(f(x^\star)=0\). It is stable if nearby initial data stay nearby, and asymptotically stable if they also converge to it.

Proposition

Proposition. For \(\dot x=Ax\), the origin is asymptotically stable exactly when every eigenvalue of \(A\) has negative real part.

Proof

Proof. Put \(A\) into Jordan form or use norm estimates for the semigroup \(e^{tA}\). Polynomial factors from Jordan blocks are dominated exactly when exponential real parts are negative.

Remark

Remark. Linear stability is local information for nonlinear systems after linearization, but nonlinear terms can matter in nonhyperbolic cases.
