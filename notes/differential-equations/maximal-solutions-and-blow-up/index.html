Maximal solutions and blow-up records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential equations note.

Definition

Definition. A maximal solution is one that cannot be extended to a larger time interval while still solving the equation. Blow-up means the solution leaves every compact subset in finite time.

Proposition

Proposition. For locally Lipschitz vector fields, a finite maximal endpoint can occur only if the solution exits every compact subset of the domain.

Proof

Proof. If the solution remains in a compact subset, local existence with uniform time radius extends it beyond the supposed endpoint, contradicting maximality.

Remark

Remark. Blow-up is not always literal divergence to infinity; on restricted domains it may mean approaching the boundary.
