Elliptic equations records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential equations note.

Definition

Definition. Elliptic equations generalize equilibrium and potential problems. Their principal symbols are positive or invertible in nonzero directions.

Proposition

Proposition. Uniform ellipticity yields energy estimates, maximum principles, and regularity under appropriate coefficient and boundary hypotheses.

Proof

Proof. Energy estimates come from testing the equation with the solution or truncations. Regularity is obtained by differentiating weakly, freezing coefficients, or using difference quotients.

Remark

Remark. Elliptic theory is the PDE analogue of solving positive definite linear systems in infinite dimensions.
