12. Sobolev spaces and traces

Sobolev spaces and traces records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential equations note.

Definition

Definition. Sobolev spaces control functions and weak derivatives in \(L^p\). Trace theorems define boundary values for Sobolev functions when ordinary pointwise restriction is not meaningful.

Proposition

Proposition. On sufficiently regular domains, the trace map extends continuously from smooth functions to Sobolev spaces such as \(H^1(\Omega)\to L^2(\partial\Omega)\), with sharper target spaces available.

Proof

Proof. Flatten the boundary locally, prove one-dimensional estimates normal to the boundary, and patch the estimates by partitions of unity.

Remark

Remark. Boundary conditions in weak PDE formulations depend on trace theory, not pointwise values alone.
