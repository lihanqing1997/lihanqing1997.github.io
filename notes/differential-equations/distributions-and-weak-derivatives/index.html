Distributions and weak derivatives records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential equations note.

Definition

Definition. A weak derivative is defined by integration against test functions: \(u\) has weak derivative \(v\) if \(\int u\partial_i\varphi\,\mathrm{d}x=-\int v\varphi\,\mathrm{d}x\) for all test \(\varphi\).

Proposition

Proposition. Classical derivatives are weak derivatives, but weak derivatives exist for many nonsmooth functions. Differential equations can therefore be interpreted in distributional form.

Proof

Proof. Classical integration by parts proves agreement for smooth functions. For nonsmooth functions, the identity with all test functions is taken as the definition.

Remark

Remark. Weak derivatives are the entry point to modern PDE theory because solutions are often not classically differentiable.
