Picard iteration and contraction records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential equations note.

Definition

Definition. Picard iteration rewrites an ODE as an integral equation \(x(t)=x_0+\int_{t_0}^t f(s,x(s))\,\mathrm{d}s\) and iterates the right-hand side.

Proposition

Proposition. On a short enough time interval, a Lipschitz vector field makes the Picard operator a contraction on a complete space of continuous curves.

Proof

Proof. The sup norm controls the integral of the difference of two curves by the interval length times the Lipschitz constant. Choose the interval short enough and apply Banach fixed point theorem.

Remark

Remark. This is the model proof where differential equations are solved by functional analysis.
