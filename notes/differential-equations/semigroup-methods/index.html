19. Semigroup methods

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/differential-equations/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/differential-equations/conservation-laws/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/differential-equations/variational-methods/" %}

Semigroup methods records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential equations note.

Definition

Definition. Evolution equations can be written as \(\mathrm{d}u/\mathrm{d}t=Au\), where \(A\) is often an unbounded operator on a Banach space.

Proposition

Proposition. If \(A\) generates a strongly continuous semigroup \(T(t)\), then \(u(t)=T(t)u_0\) is the abstract solution to the linear evolution problem.

Proof

Proof. Resolvent estimates and density conditions identify generators. Once a semigroup exists, differentiability on the generator domain verifies the equation.

Remark

Remark. Semigroup theory unifies ODE, heat flow, transport, and Markov evolution in one operator framework.
