16. Heat equation

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/differential-equations/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/differential-equations/maximum-principles/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/differential-equations/wave-equation/" %}

Heat equation records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential equations note.

Definition

Definition. The heat equation \(\partial_tu-\Delta u=0\) models diffusion and smooths initial data over positive time.

Proposition

Proposition. The heat kernel on \(\mathbb R^d\) gives \(u(t,x)=(4\pi t)^{-d/2}\int e^{-|x-y|^2/(4t)}u_0(y)\,\mathrm{d}y\), solving the equation for suitable initial data.

Proof

Proof. Differentiate the Gaussian kernel to verify the PDE and use approximate identity properties as \(t\downarrow0\) to recover the initial condition.

Remark

Remark. Parabolic equations combine time evolution with elliptic smoothing. They are irreversible in a way wave equations are not.
