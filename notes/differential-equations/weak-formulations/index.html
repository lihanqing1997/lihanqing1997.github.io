Weak formulations records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the differential equations note.

Definition

Definition. A weak formulation multiplies a differential equation by test functions and moves derivatives by integration by parts, lowering the differentiability required of the solution.

Lax-Milgram framework

Theorem. For elliptic equations, weak solutions are often characterized by a bilinear form \(a(u,v)=\ell(v)\) on a Sobolev space. Coercivity and boundedness give existence and uniqueness.

Proof

Proof. Integration by parts produces the variational identity. Lax-Milgram theorem gives a unique weak solution when the bilinear form is coercive and continuous.

Remark

Remark. Weak formulations are not approximations to PDE; they are the correct formulation when classical derivatives are too restrictive.
