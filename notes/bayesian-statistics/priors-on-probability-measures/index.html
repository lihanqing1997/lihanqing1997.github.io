This page records the main definitions, propositions, and proof ideas for Priors on probability measures.

Definition

Definition. A random probability measure is a measurable map into the space of probability measures equipped with a chosen topology and sigma-field.

Proposition

Proposition. Positive prior mass in every weak neighborhood of a law places that law in the weak support of the prior.

Proof

Proof. Support is the smallest closed set of prior probability one.

Remarks

Weak support, total-variation support, and KL support are different and lead to different posterior claims.
