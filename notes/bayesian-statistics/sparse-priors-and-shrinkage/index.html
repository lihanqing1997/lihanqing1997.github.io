Coordinate sparsity

Sparse Bayesian inference encodes the belief that the signal has many exactly or effectively zero coordinates. The prior must both search over supports and estimate coefficients on a selected support.

Definition. A spike-and-slab prior has indicators \(\gamma_j\in\{0,1\}\) and coefficients \[ \beta_j\mid\gamma_j\sim (1-\gamma_j)\delta_0+\gamma_jG_j,\qquad \gamma_j\sim\operatorname{Bernoulli}(q). \] The induced support is \(S_\gamma=\{j:\gamma_j=1\}\).

Proposition. If the indicators are independent, the prior mass of a support \(S\) is \(q^{|S|}(1-q)^{p-|S|}\). Choosing \(q\asymp p^{-a}\) penalizes large models by approximately \(a|S|\log p\).

Proof. The first identity is the Bernoulli product probability. Taking negative logarithms gives \(-|S|\log q-(p-|S|)\log(1-q)\). When \(q\) is small, the second term is lower order for sparse \(S\), while \(-\log q\asymp \log p\).

Continuous shrinkage

Definition. A global-local shrinkage prior has \(\beta_j\mid\lambda_j,\tau\sim N(0,\tau^2\lambda_j^2)\), where \(\tau\) is global shrinkage and \(\lambda_j\) is local shrinkage. The horseshoe prior takes \(\lambda_j\sim C^+(0,1)\) and often \(\tau\sim C^+(0,\tau_0)\).

Remark. Spike-and-slab priors are natural for support recovery. Horseshoe-type priors are natural for adaptive shrinkage and uncertainty quantification when exact zeros are less central.

Model-size priors and beta-min separation

Definition. A complexity prior assigns \(\Pi(|S|=s)\propto e^{-a s\log(ep/s)}\). It matches the combinatorial size \({p\choose s}\) of the sparse model class.

Theorem. Exact support recovery generally requires posterior concentration near the truth and a beta-min condition \(\min_{j\in S_0}|\beta_{0j}|\) larger than the estimation noise and shrinkage bias.

Proof. If an active coefficient is below the local uncertainty scale, the likelihood cannot reliably distinguish it from zero. Conversely, once likelihood separation exceeds the prior penalty for adding the variable, posterior odds favor the true support over supports missing that coordinate.
