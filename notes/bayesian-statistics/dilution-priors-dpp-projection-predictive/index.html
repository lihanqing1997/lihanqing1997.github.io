42. Dilution priors, DPP priors, and projection predictive inference

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/bayesian-model-averaging/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/posterior-compression-density-ratio-diagnostics/" %}

Redundancy problem

When predictors are strongly correlated, many models represent nearly the same predictive information. Ordinary independent model priors can overcount a scientific idea just because it has many redundant encodings.

Definition. A dilution prior assigns smaller total prior mass to clusters of similar models. A determinantal point process prior favors diverse subsets through probabilities proportional to \(\det(K_S)\), where \(K_S\) is a similarity kernel submatrix.

Proposition. If two predictors are nearly identical, a DPP prior with similarity kernel \(K\) assigns small prior mass to including both because \(\det(K_{\{j,k\}})=K_{jj}K_{kk}-K_{jk}^2\) is small when \(K_{jk}^2\approx K_{jj}K_{kk}\).

Proof. For a two-by-two kernel submatrix, compute the determinant. Near collinearity makes the two columns nearly linearly dependent, and determinant measures squared volume.

Projection predictive inference

Definition. Projection predictive inference chooses a smaller model by minimizing predictive discrepancy from a reference posterior, for example \[ \min_{\theta_S}D\{p(\tilde y\mid x,\theta_{\mathrm{ref}})\|p_S(\tilde y\mid x_S,\theta_S)\}. \]

Remark. Projection targets predictive preservation, not exact recovery of the true support. This is essential when redundant predictors make exact support scientifically unstable.

Volume interpretation of DPP priors

Proposition. If columns indexed by \(S\) are represented by feature vectors \(v_j\), then \(\det(V_S^\top V_S)\) is the squared volume of the parallelepiped spanned by those vectors. A DPP prior therefore penalizes nearly collinear sets.

Proof. The Gram determinant formula states that the squared \(s\)-dimensional volume of \(v_{j_1},\ldots,v_{j_s}\) is \(\det(V_S^\top V_S)\). Near linear dependence makes this determinant small.

Projection predictive target

Remark. Projection predictive inference answers a different question from selection consistency: find a small model that preserves the reference posterior predictive distribution. This is often the scientifically stable question under collinearity.
