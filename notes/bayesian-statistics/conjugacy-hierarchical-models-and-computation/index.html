Conjugacy is not merely a computational trick. It identifies families of priors that are stable under likelihood multiplication and therefore makes the update algebra explicit.

Conjugate priors in exponential families

The natural exponential-family form makes the source of conjugacy transparent.

Definition. Suppose \[ p_\eta(x)=\exp\{\eta^\top T(x)-A(\eta)\}h(x). \] A conjugate prior has density \[ \pi_{\alpha,\beta}(\eta)\propto \exp\{\eta^\top\alpha-\beta A(\eta)\} \] on the natural parameter space.

Proposition. After observing \(x_1,\ldots,x_n\), the posterior is in the same family with \[ \alpha_n=\alpha+\sum_{i=1}^n T(x_i),\qquad \beta_n=\beta+n. \]

Proof. Multiply the prior density by the likelihood. The exponent becomes \[ \eta^\top\alpha-\beta A(\eta)+\eta^\top\sum_iT(x_i)-nA(\eta), \] which is the same conjugate form with the updated hyperparameters.

Beta-binomial and gamma-Poisson calculations

The simplest conjugate pairs should be remembered as algebraic templates.

Example. If \(X\mid p\sim\operatorname{Bin}(n,p)\) and \(p\sim\operatorname{Beta}(a,b)\), then \[ p\mid X=x\sim\operatorname{Beta}(a+x,b+n-x). \] If \(X_i\mid\lambda\sim\operatorname{Poisson}(\lambda)\) and \(\lambda\sim\operatorname{Gamma}(a,b)\) with rate \(b\), then \[ \lambda\mid X^{(n)}\sim\operatorname{Gamma}\left(a+\sum_iX_i,b+n\right). \]

Normal-normal shrinkage

Gaussian conjugacy shows the posterior mean as a precision-weighted average of prior information and data information.

Proposition. If \(X_i\mid\theta\sim N(\theta,\sigma^2)\) and \(\theta\sim N(m_0,\tau_0^2)\), then \[ \theta\mid X^{(n)}\sim N(m_n,\tau_n^2), \quad \tau_n^{-2}=\tau_0^{-2}+n\sigma^{-2}, \quad m_n=\tau_n^2(\tau_0^{-2}m_0+n\sigma^{-2}\bar X). \]

Proof. Complete the square in \[ -\frac{(\theta-m_0)^2}{2\tau_0^2}-\frac{n(\bar X-\theta)^2}{2\sigma^2}. \] The coefficient of \(\theta^2\) gives \(\tau_n^{-2}\), and the coefficient of \(\theta\) gives \(\tau_n^{-2}m_n\).

Computation beyond conjugacy

Conjugacy gives exact formulas, but the same posterior is well-defined without conjugacy. Computation then approximates integrals rather than changing the target distribution.

Remark. Laplace approximation, Monte Carlo, Markov chain Monte Carlo, Hamiltonian Monte Carlo, and variational Bayes should all be read as different approximations to posterior expectations under the same Bayes formula.
