4. Conjugate families and exponential families

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/posterior-and-decision/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/prior-construction-jeffreys-weak-information/" %}

This page records the main definitions, propositions, and proof ideas for Conjugate families and exponential families.

Definition

Definition. A prior family is conjugate when posterior updating stays in the same family. For \(p_\theta(x)=h(x)\exp\{\theta^\top T(x)-A(\theta)\}\), the natural conjugate prior has density proportional to \(\exp\{\theta^\top\alpha-\beta A(\theta)\}\).

Proposition

Proposition. After observations \(X_1,\ldots,X_n\), the hyperparameters update as \[ (\alpha,\beta)\mapsto\left(\alpha+\sum_{i=1}^nT(X_i),\beta+n\right). \]

Proof

Proof. Multiplying likelihood and prior collects all terms in the same exponential-family form.

Remarks

Conjugate models give exact algebraic checks for computation, approximation, and hierarchical modeling.
