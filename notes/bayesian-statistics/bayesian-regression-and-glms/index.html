7. Bayesian regression and generalized linear models

This page records the main definitions, propositions, and proof ideas for Bayesian regression and generalized linear models.

Definition

Definition. In a GLM, \(g(\mu_i)=x_i^\top\beta\). Bayesian inference studies \(\Pi(\mathrm{d}\beta\mid y)\propto L(\beta;y)\Pi(\mathrm{d}\beta)\).

Proposition

Proposition. For \(Y\mid\beta\sim N(X\beta,\sigma^2I)\) and \(\beta\sim N(b_0,V_0)\), \[ V_n^{-1}=V_0^{-1}+\sigma^{-2}X^\top X,\quad b_n=V_n(V_0^{-1}b_0+\sigma^{-2}X^\top Y). \]

Proof

Proof. Complete the square in the Gaussian likelihood times Gaussian prior.

Remarks

In nonconjugate GLMs, the same geometry appears through gradients, Hessians, HMC, Laplace approximation, and variational approximations.
