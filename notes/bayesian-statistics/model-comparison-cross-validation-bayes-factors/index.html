Bayesian model comparison can be based on marginal likelihood, predictive performance, or decision loss. These criteria answer different questions and should not be conflated.

Bayes factors and posterior model odds

Bayes factors compare models through their prior predictive densities.

Definition. For models \(M_1,M_2\), the Bayes factor in favor of \(M_1\) over \(M_2\) is \[ B_{12}=\frac{m_1(X)}{m_2(X)}. \] Posterior odds satisfy \[ \frac{\Pi(M_1\mid X)}{\Pi(M_2\mid X)} =B_{12}\frac{\Pi(M_1)}{\Pi(M_2)}. \]

Proof. Apply Bayes formula on the discrete model index. The likelihood for model \(M_j\) after integrating its parameters is precisely \(m_j(X)\).

Laplace approximation and the Occam factor

Marginal likelihood automatically penalizes unused parameter volume.

Proposition. If a \(d\)-dimensional regular model has posterior mode \(\hat\theta\), then formally \[ m(X)\approx p_{\hat\theta}(X)\pi(\hat\theta)(2\pi)^{d/2}\det\{-\nabla^2\log[p_\theta(X)\pi(\theta)]_{\hat\theta}\}^{-1/2}. \]

Proof. Second-order Taylor expansion of the log integrand at \(\hat\theta\) gives a Gaussian integral. The determinant term is the local posterior volume and produces the Occam factor.

Cross-validation and predictive scoring

Cross-validation evaluates predictive accuracy under a chosen scoring rule rather than prior predictive evidence.

Definition. Leave-one-out expected log predictive density estimates \[ \sum_{i=1}^n \log p(y_i\mid y_{-i}) \] where \(p(y_i\mid y_{-i})\) is the posterior predictive density fitted without observation \(i\).

Remark. Bayes factors are sensitive to prior mass on parameters; cross-validation is usually less sensitive to diffuse prior volume but targets predictive performance rather than posterior model probability.

Stacking and model averaging

When no model is true, averaging for prediction can be preferable to selecting one model.

Proposition. Stacking chooses weights to maximize a cross-validated predictive score over convex combinations of predictive distributions.

Proof. The criterion is a concave optimization problem in the weights for logarithmic score because the log of a positive affine combination is concave. The resulting weights target predictive performance rather than posterior model probabilities.
