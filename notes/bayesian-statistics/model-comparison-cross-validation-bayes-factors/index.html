10. Model comparison, cross-validation, and Bayes factors

This page records the main definitions, propositions, and proof ideas for Model comparison, cross-validation, and Bayes factors.

Definition

Definition. Leave-one-out expected log predictive density is \[ \operatorname{elpd}_{\mathrm{loo}}=\sum_{i=1}^n\log p(y_i\mid y_{-i}),\quad p(y_i\mid y_{-i})=\int p_\theta(y_i)\,\mathrm{d}\Pi(\theta\mid y_{-i}). \]

Proposition

Proposition. Bayes factors compare prior-averaged evidence; predictive criteria estimate future predictive accuracy.

Proof

Proof. Marginal likelihood averages the likelihood over the prior, while cross-validation evaluates predictive density for held-out observations.

Remarks

Diffuse priors can lower marginal likelihood even when the best-fitting likelihood value is high.
