24. Dirichlet processes and Pólya urn schemes

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/priors-on-probability-measures/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/polya-trees-histograms-tail-free-priors/" %}

The Dirichlet process is the basic conjugate prior on probability measures. Its finite-dimensional Dirichlet distributions and Pólya urn prediction rule are two faces of the same object.

Finite-dimensional definition

A Dirichlet process is characterized by its masses on finite partitions.

Definition. A random probability measure \(P\) has distribution \(\operatorname{DP}(\alpha G_0)\) if for every finite measurable partition \(A_1,\ldots,A_k\), \[ (P(A_1),\ldots,P(A_k))\sim\operatorname{Dirichlet}(\alpha G_0(A_1),\ldots,\alpha G_0(A_k)). \]

Proposition. \(\mathbb E P(A)=G_0(A)\) and \(\operatorname{Var}(P(A))=G_0(A)(1-G_0(A))/(\alpha+1)\).

Proof. Use the mean and variance formulas for a beta random variable, since \(P(A)\sim\operatorname{Beta}(\alpha G_0(A),\alpha(1-G_0(A)))\).

Conjugacy

Observations update the base measure by adding point masses.

Theorem. If \(P\sim\operatorname{DP}(\alpha G_0)\) and \(X_1,\ldots,X_n\mid P\stackrel{\mathrm{iid}}{\sim}P\), then \[ P\mid X_{1:n}\sim\operatorname{DP}\left(\alpha G_0+\sum_{i=1}^n\delta_{X_i}\right). \]

Proof. For any finite partition, the likelihood contributes multinomial counts to the cell probabilities. Dirichlet-multinomial conjugacy updates each partition parameter by its count. Since this holds for all finite partitions, the posterior process is the displayed Dirichlet process.

Pólya urn prediction

The predictive law is a mixture of the base distribution and the empirical distribution.

Corollary. The posterior predictive distribution is \[ X_{n+1}\mid X_{1:n}\sim \frac{\alpha}{\alpha+n}G_0+\frac1{\alpha+n}\sum_{i=1}^n\delta_{X_i}. \]

Proof. The predictive probability of \(A\) is \(\mathbb E\{P(A)\mid X_{1:n}\}\). Apply the posterior mean formula for the updated Dirichlet process.

Clustering interpretation

Repeated values occur with positive probability because the predictive distribution puts mass on previous observations.

Remark. This clustering property is the basis of Chinese restaurant process representations and Dirichlet process mixture models.
