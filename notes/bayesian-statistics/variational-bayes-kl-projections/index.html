15. Variational Bayes and KL projections

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/laplace-variational-posterior-approximations/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/posterior-consistency-parametric-models/" %}

Variational Bayes is best read as projection of the posterior onto a tractable family. The chosen KL direction determines the geometry and the typical bias of the approximation.

Evidence lower bound

The optimization objective is an exact decomposition of the marginal likelihood.

Definition. For a density \(q\) on \(\Theta\), the evidence lower bound is \[ \mathcal L(q)=\int q(\theta)\log\frac{p_\theta(X)\pi(\theta)}{q(\theta)}\,\mathrm{d}\theta. \]

Proposition. The identity \[ \log m(X)=\mathcal L(q)+\mathrm{KL}(q\,\|\,\Pi(\cdot\mid X)) \] holds whenever the terms are well-defined.

Proof. Use \(\pi(\theta\mid X)=p_\theta(X)\pi(\theta)/m(X)\). Then \[ \mathrm{KL}(q\,\|\,\Pi)=\int q\log\frac{q\,m(X)}{p_\theta(X)\pi(\theta)} =\log m(X)-\mathcal L(q). \]

Mean-field coordinate updates

Mean-field VB turns the variational problem into a sequence of conditional expectation calculations.

Theorem. If \(q(\theta)=\prod_{j=1}^k q_j(\theta_j)\), then the coordinate optimizer satisfies \[ \log q_j^*(\theta_j)=\mathbb E_{-j}\log\{p_\theta(X)\pi(\theta)\}+\mathrm{constant}. \]

Proof. Hold all other factors fixed and keep only terms depending on \(q_j\). The objective is a linear functional of \(q_j\) minus \(\int q_j\log q_j\), subject to normalization. The Euler equation gives the displayed exponential form.

Reverse and forward KL

The direction of KL controls whether the approximation is mode-seeking or mass-covering.

Definition. Reverse KL is \(\mathrm{KL}(q\|\pi)=\int q\log(q/\pi)\). Forward KL is \(\mathrm{KL}(\pi\|q)=\int \pi\log(\pi/q)\).

Remark. Reverse KL heavily penalizes putting mass where the posterior density is small, so it can underestimate uncertainty in multimodal or skewed problems. Forward KL penalizes missing posterior mass but is often harder to optimize.

Variational uncertainty

A good ELBO value does not automatically imply accurate credible sets.

Proposition. If the variational family excludes posterior dependence, then marginal variances can be systematically underestimated even when posterior means are accurate.

Proof. Dependence contributes covariance terms to uncertainty in linear functionals. A product approximation sets cross-covariances to zero, so the induced variance of sums or contrasts may be too small unless the missing covariance happens to vanish.
