15. Variational Bayes and KL projections

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/laplace-variational-posterior-approximations/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/posterior-consistency-parametric-models/" %}

Variational Bayes turns posterior approximation into an optimization problem over a tractable family of probability measures.

Variational family

Definition. Let \(\Pi(\mathrm{d}z|X)\) be the posterior and \(\mathcal Q\) a tractable family. A variational posterior is a minimizer of a divergence from \(\mathcal Q\) to \(\Pi(\cdot|X)\), most commonly reverse KL.

ELBO identity

Proposition. For \(q\ll\Pi\), minimizing \(D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q\|\Pi(\cdot|X))\) is equivalent to maximizing \(\mathbb E_q\log p(X,Z)-\mathbb E_q\log q(Z)\), because the difference is the fixed log marginal likelihood.

Mean-field coordinate update

Theorem. If \(q(z)=\prod_{j=1}^mq_j(z_j)\), the coordinate optimum satisfies \(\log q_j(z_j)=\mathbb E_{q_{-j}}\log p(X,Z)+c_j\), whenever the right-hand side defines a member of the coordinate family.

Forward KL projection

Definition. The forward projection minimizes \(D_{\mathrm{KL}}(\Pi(\cdot|X)\|q)\). It is moment-covering rather than mode-seeking and is closely related to expectation propagation and information projection geometry.

Frequentist validity

Remark. A variational posterior may contract at the right rate when \(\mathcal Q\) approximates the exact posterior well enough in KL and the exact posterior has suitable testing and entropy control. Optimization error must be kept below the statistical scale.
