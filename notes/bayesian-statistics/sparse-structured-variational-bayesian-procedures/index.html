33. Sparse, structured, and variational Bayesian procedures

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/semiparametric-nonparametric-bvm/" %}

This page records the main definitions, propositions, and proof ideas for Sparse, structured, and variational Bayesian procedures.

Definition

Definition. Sparse Bayesian procedures use priors that put mass on low-dimensional structures, such as spike-and-slab priors for many zero coefficients.

Proposition

Proposition. Variational posteriors can inherit contraction rates when the variational family contains distributions close enough to the exact posterior in KL divergence.

Proof

Proof. The variational optimum is controlled by the evidence lower bound; testing bounds then transfer contraction to the approximate posterior.

Remarks

The synthesis is: priors encode structure, computation approximates the posterior, and frequentist analysis checks contraction, selection, and calibration.
