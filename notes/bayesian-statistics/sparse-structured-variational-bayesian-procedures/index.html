Modern Bayesian procedures often combine sparsity, structure, and variational approximation. The mathematical question is whether computationally tractable posteriors still concentrate near the truth with calibrated uncertainty.

Sparse priors

Sparsity priors put most mass on low-dimensional structures inside a high-dimensional space.

Definition. A spike-and-slab prior for \(\theta\in\mathbb R^p\) has indicators \(z_j\sim\operatorname{Bernoulli}(q)\) and \[ \theta_j\mid z_j\sim (1-z_j)\delta_0+z_j G. \]

Proposition. The prior mass of a support \(S\) is \(q^{|S|}(1-q)^{p-|S|}\), before accounting for slab density on the nonzero coefficients.

Proof. The indicators are independent Bernoulli variables, so the probability of exactly the support \(S\) is the product of \(q\) over \(S\) and \(1-q\) over its complement.

Posterior contraction in sparse models

Rates depend on the true sparsity and the logarithmic cost of searching over supports.

Theorem. In many sparse normal means or regression problems, the benchmark squared-error rate is of order \[ s_0\log(p/s_0)/n \] for \(s_0\) nonzero coefficients.

Proof. The variance cost of estimating \(s_0\) coefficients is proportional to \(s_0/n\), and the model-selection cost is the logarithm of the number of possible supports, \(\log {p\choose s_0}\asymp s_0\log(p/s_0)\), divided by \(n\).

Variational sparse Bayes

Mean-field approximations often factorize over inclusion indicators and coefficients.

Definition. A structured variational family may use \[ q(z,\theta)=\prod_{j=1}^p q_j(z_j,\theta_j), \] or richer block factorizations that preserve known dependence.

Remark. A too-factorized family can underestimate posterior dependence and uncertainty, but may still recover support or prediction risk under suitable beta-min and design conditions.

Computation and theory

The ELBO is an optimization objective, while contraction is a statistical property. Both must be checked.

Proposition. If a variational distribution is close in KL to a posterior that contracts at rate \(\varepsilon_n\), then bounded posterior functionals are close up to the variational approximation error.

Proof. Pinsker's inequality converts KL error into total variation error. Total variation controls bounded expectation differences, while posterior contraction controls the target posterior mass outside the \(\varepsilon_n\)-ball.
