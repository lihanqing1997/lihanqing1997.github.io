Adaptive Bayes tries to learn unknown regularity or sparsity while still quantifying uncertainty. Empirical Bayes and hierarchical Bayes differ in whether the hyperparameter is estimated or integrated out.

Hierarchical adaptation

A hyperprior can spread mass over models with different effective complexity.

Definition. Let \(\lambda\) be a regularity, scale, bandwidth, or sparsity hyperparameter. A hierarchical prior has \[ \lambda\sim H,\qquad \theta\mid\lambda\sim\Pi_\lambda. \]

Proposition. If \(H\) assigns at least \(\exp(-C n\varepsilon_{n,\lambda}^2)\) mass near a near-optimal \(\lambda\), then the hierarchical prior can inherit the contraction rate associated with that \(\lambda\).

Proof. The prior mass condition in the general contraction theorem can be satisfied by integrating over a hyperparameter neighborhood. The likelihood tests are unchanged; the hyperprior contributes only to the denominator mass budget.

Empirical Bayes

Empirical Bayes estimates hyperparameters from data and then plugs them into the prior.

Definition. An empirical Bayes posterior has the form \[ \Pi_{\hat\lambda(X)}(\cdot\mid X), \] where \(\hat\lambda(X)\) is often chosen by marginal likelihood or a moment estimate.

Remark. Because \(\hat\lambda\) is data-dependent, frequentist analysis must control the posterior uniformly over the likely range of \(\hat\lambda\).

Marginal likelihood selection

Marginal likelihood balances fit and prior complexity.

Proposition. In many Gaussian sequence problems, marginal likelihood selects a scale or truncation level by trading off fit to observed coefficients with the prior penalty for model size.

Proof. The marginal likelihood factors over coefficients. Terms for included coefficients reward explaining signal; terms for excessive complexity pay a normalizing penalty. The maximizer balances these contributions.

Adaptive credible sets

Adaptive contraction does not automatically give adaptive honest coverage.

Remark. Credible sets may need inflation or self-similarity restrictions. Otherwise, functions with deceptive coefficient patterns can make the posterior radius too small for honest frequentist coverage.
