30. Adaptive Bayes, empirical Bayes, and hierarchical Bayes

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/contraction-rates-density-regression-white-noise/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/semiparametric-nonparametric-bvm/" %}

This page records the main definitions, propositions, and proof ideas for Adaptive Bayes, empirical Bayes, and hierarchical Bayes.

Definition

Definition. A prior is adaptive over a scale of regularity classes if it contracts at the minimax rate for each class without knowing the class in advance.

Proposition

Proposition. Hierarchical Bayes mixes tuning parameters; empirical Bayes estimates them from data. Both adapt when enough mass is placed near the oracle scale.

Proof

Proof. Condition on a good tuning value and then show the hyperprior or data-driven selector reaches that region with high probability.

Remarks

Adaptive estimation is easier than adaptive honest uncertainty quantification.
