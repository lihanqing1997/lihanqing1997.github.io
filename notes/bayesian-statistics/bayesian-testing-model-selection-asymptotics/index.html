21. Bayesian testing and model selection asymptotics

This page records the main definitions, propositions, and proof ideas for Bayesian testing and model selection asymptotics.

Definition

Definition. Posterior odds compare integrated likelihoods over hypotheses \(H_0,H_1\).

Proposition

Proposition. For a regular model of dimension \(d_k\), Laplace approximation gives \[ \log m_k(X)\approx \ell_k(\hat\theta_k)-\frac{d_k}{2}\log n+O_p(1). \]

Proof

Proof. The local Gaussian integral around the MLE contributes the factor \(n^{-d_k/2}\).

Remarks

For nonparametric alternatives, Bayes-factor consistency requires prior mass near the truth and testing power away from it.
