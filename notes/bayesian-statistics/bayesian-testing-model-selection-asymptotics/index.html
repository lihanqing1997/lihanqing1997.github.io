21. Bayesian testing and model selection asymptotics

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/laplace-bvm-and-misspecification/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/function-space-priors-random-series/" %}

Bayesian testing compares posterior probabilities of hypotheses or models. Asymptotically, the answer depends on testing separation, prior mass, and the dimensional penalty in marginal likelihoods.

Posterior odds

Testing is model comparison when the hypotheses are assigned prior probability.

Definition. For hypotheses \(H_0,H_1\), posterior odds are \[ \frac{\Pi(H_1\mid X)}{\Pi(H_0\mid X)} =\frac{\Pi(H_1)}{\Pi(H_0)} \frac{m_1(X)}{m_0(X)}, \] where \(m_j\) is the marginal likelihood under \(H_j\).

Proof. Apply Bayes formula to the binary model index. The likelihood of each hypothesis after integrating nuisance parameters is its marginal likelihood.

Testing consistency

A Bayesian test is consistent when posterior mass moves to the true hypothesis.

Proposition. If under \(H_0\), \(m_1(X)/m_0(X)\to0\) in probability, then \(\Pi(H_1\mid X)\to0\) for fixed positive prior odds.

Proof. Posterior odds equal prior odds times the Bayes factor. Fixed prior odds cannot prevent convergence to zero of the Bayes factor.

Occam penalties

Bayesian model selection naturally penalizes dimension through integration.

Proposition. In regular nested models, Laplace approximation gives a marginal likelihood penalty of order \(n^{-d/2}\) for \(d\) free local dimensions.

Proof. The local posterior volume around the maximum likelihood estimator has radius \(n^{-1/2}\) in each of \(d\) directions. The Gaussian integral therefore contributes \(n^{-d/2}\).

Point nulls and continuous priors

Testing a point null requires assigning it positive prior mass as a model, not merely using a continuous prior over the full parameter.

Remark. If a continuous prior assigns zero mass to \(\theta=\theta_0\), then the posterior probability of the point null remains zero. Bayesian point-null testing therefore uses a mixture prior with an atom at the null and a continuous alternative component.
