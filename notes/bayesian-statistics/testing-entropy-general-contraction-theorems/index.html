This page records the main definitions, propositions, and proof ideas for Testing, entropy, and general contraction theorems.

Definition

Definition. The posterior contracts at rate \(\varepsilon_n\) if \(E_0\Pi(d(\theta,\theta_0)>M\varepsilon_n\mid X^{(n)})\to0\) for large \(M\).

Proposition

Proposition. A GGV-type theorem balances KL prior mass \(e^{-Cn\varepsilon_n^2}\), sieve complement mass \(e^{-Cn\varepsilon_n^2}\), and entropy \(\log N(\varepsilon_n,\Theta_n,d)\lesssim n\varepsilon_n^2\).

Proof

Proof. Cover the bad set by entropy balls, attach tests, and compare numerator and denominator.

Remarks

Entropy measures model size at resolution \(\varepsilon_n\); KL mass measures the Bayesian cost of being near the truth.
