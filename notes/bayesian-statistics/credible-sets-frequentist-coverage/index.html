19. Credible sets and frequentist coverage

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/bernstein-von-mises-theorem/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/laplace-bvm-and-misspecification/" %}

Credible sets are posterior probability statements; confidence sets are repeated-sampling statements. The two agree only under additional conditions.

Credibility and coverage

The same random set can be evaluated under two probability measures.

Definition. A set \(C_n(X)\) has posterior credibility \(1-\alpha\) if \(\Pi(C_n(X)\mid X)\ge1-\alpha\). It has frequentist coverage \(1-\alpha\) at \(\theta_0\) if \[ P_{\theta_0}\{\theta_0\in C_n(X)\}\to1-\alpha. \]

Remark. Posterior probability conditions on the observed data. Coverage averages over repeated data generated at a fixed parameter.

Coverage from BvM

In regular parametric models, BvM supplies the bridge between the two notions.

Theorem. Under BvM, credible ellipsoids based on the efficient center and information matrix have asymptotic frequentist coverage equal to their posterior credibility.

Proof. The local posterior radius converges to the Gaussian quantile, while the sampling distribution of the efficient center has the same Gaussian covariance. Thus the event that \(\theta_0\) lies in the credible ellipsoid has the same limiting probability as the posterior Gaussian ball.

Marginal credible intervals

Marginal intervals can have good first-order coverage even when joint sets require more care.

Proposition. If a scalar functional \(\psi(\theta)\) is differentiable and BvM holds for \(\theta\), then the marginal posterior of \(\sqrt n(\psi(\theta)-\psi(\hat\theta_n))\) is asymptotically normal with variance \(\dot\psi^\top I^{-1}\dot\psi\).

Proof. Apply the delta method to the local posterior variable and to the sampling distribution of \(\hat\theta_n\).

Nonparametric credible sets

In infinite-dimensional models, credible balls may undercover unless inflated or carefully centered.

Remark. Posterior contraction alone does not guarantee coverage. Bias, polished-tail conditions, self-similarity assumptions, or hierarchical adaptation can determine whether credible sets have honest frequentist coverage.
