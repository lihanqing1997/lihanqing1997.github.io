19. Credible sets and frequentist coverage

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/bernstein-von-mises-theorem/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/laplace-bvm-and-misspecification/" %}

This page records the main definitions, propositions, and proof ideas for Credible sets and frequentist coverage.

Definition

Definition. A set \(C_n(X)\) is credible if \(\Pi(C_n(X)\mid X)\ge1-\alpha\). It has frequentist coverage if \(P_{\theta_0}(\theta_0\in C_n(X))\to1-\alpha\).

Proposition

Proposition. Under parametric BvM, regular posterior credible ellipsoids have asymptotic frequentist coverage.

Proof

Proof. The posterior quantiles and sampling quantiles are governed by the same limiting normal law.

Remarks

In nonparametric problems, bias and strong norms can destroy coverage without extra structure or inflation.
