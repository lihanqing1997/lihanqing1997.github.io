16. Posterior consistency in parametric models

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/variational-bayes-kl-projections/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/local-asymptotic-normality-laplace-approximation/" %}

This page records the main definitions, propositions, and proof ideas for Posterior consistency in parametric models.

Definition

Definition. The posterior is consistent at \(\theta_0\) if \(\Pi(U^c\mid X_1,\ldots,X_n)\to0\) for every neighborhood \(U\) of \(\theta_0\).

Proposition

Proposition. In identifiable dominated i.i.d. models, positive prior mass in every KL neighborhood plus separating tests implies posterior consistency.

Proof

Proof. The KL mass protects the denominator; tests control the numerator on separated bad sets.

Remarks

Consistency gives no rate, limit shape, coverage, or misspecification robustness by itself.
