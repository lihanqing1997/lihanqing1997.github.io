16. Posterior consistency in parametric models

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/variational-bayes-kl-projections/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/local-asymptotic-normality-laplace-approximation/" %}

Posterior consistency means that the posterior eventually concentrates near the data-generating parameter. In parametric models, the key ingredients are identifiability, prior mass near the truth, and likelihood separation away from it.

Consistency neighborhoods

A topology on the parameter or model space determines what it means to learn the truth.

Definition. For data \(X^{(n)}\sim P_{\theta_0}^{(n)}\), the posterior is consistent at \(\theta_0\) if for every neighborhood \(U\) of \(\theta_0\), \[ \Pi(U^c\mid X^{(n)})\to0 \] in \(P_{\theta_0}^{(n)}\)-probability or almost surely.

Definition. A prior has Kullback-Leibler support at \(\theta_0\) if every set \(\{\theta:K(P_{\theta_0},P_\theta)<\varepsilon\}\) has positive prior mass.

Likelihood separation

Away from the truth, likelihood ratios should decay exponentially.

Proposition. In an iid dominated model, if \(K(P_{\theta_0},P_\theta)>0\), then \[ \frac1n\log\frac{p_\theta^{(n)}(X^{(n)})}{p_{\theta_0}^{(n)}(X^{(n)})} \to -K(P_{\theta_0},P_\theta) \] almost surely under \(P_{\theta_0}\).

Proof. The left side is the empirical average of \(\log\{p_\theta(X_i)/p_{\theta_0}(X_i)\}\). The strong law of large numbers gives its expectation, which is \(-K(P_{\theta_0},P_\theta)\).

Finite-cover consistency argument

Compactness can turn pointwise likelihood separation into uniform separation.

Theorem. If every closed set away from \(\theta_0\) can be covered by finitely many likelihood-separated neighborhoods, and the prior puts positive mass in every KL neighborhood of \(\theta_0\), then the posterior is consistent at \(\theta_0\).

Proof. The posterior numerator over the bad set is bounded by a finite sum of exponentially small likelihood ratios. The denominator is bounded below by the likelihood integrated over a KL neighborhood, which decays no faster than \(\exp\{-n\varepsilon\}\) with high probability. Choosing the separation larger than \(\varepsilon\) forces the posterior bad mass to vanish.

Nonidentifiability

If two parameters generate the same distribution, consistency can only hold for equivalence classes.

Remark. Bayesian consistency is a statement about the sampling distributions supported by the prior. If the map \(\theta\mapsto P_\theta\) is not injective, the posterior may concentrate on the set of observationally equivalent parameters rather than on a single point.
