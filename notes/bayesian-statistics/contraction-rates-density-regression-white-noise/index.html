30. Contraction rates in density, regression, and white-noise models

This page records the main definitions, propositions, and proof ideas for Contraction rates in density, regression, and white-noise models.

Definition

Definition. In Gaussian white noise, \(\mathrm{d}X(t)=f(t)\,\mathrm{d}t+n^{-1/2}\,\mathrm{d}W(t)\), and loss is often \(\|f-f_0\|_2\).

Proposition

Proposition. For one-dimensional \(\beta\)-smooth truth, the benchmark rate is \(\varepsilon_n=n^{-\beta/(2\beta+1)}\), up to logarithmic factors.

Proof

Proof. Balance approximation bias \(k^{-\beta}\) with stochastic size \((k/n)^{1/2}\).

Remarks

Density, regression, and white-noise models use the same contraction logic with different testing metrics.
