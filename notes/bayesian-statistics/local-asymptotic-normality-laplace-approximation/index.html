LAN is the local quadratic approximation that connects likelihood theory, Laplace approximation, and Gaussian posterior limits.

Local asymptotic normality

LAN studies likelihood ratios under \(n^{-1/2}\)-local perturbations.

Definition. A model is locally asymptotically normal at \(\theta_0\) with information \(I_{\theta_0}\) if for each fixed \(h\), \[ \log\frac{p_{\theta_0+h/\sqrt n}^{(n)}}{p_{\theta_0}^{(n)}}(X^{(n)}) =h^\top\Delta_n-\frac12h^\top I_{\theta_0}h+o_{P_{\theta_0}}(1), \] where \(\Delta_n\Rightarrow N(0,I_{\theta_0})\).

Remark. The random vector \(\Delta_n\) is the normalized score. The quadratic term is deterministic curvature.

Posterior in local coordinates

The posterior becomes approximately Gaussian after changing variables to \(h=\sqrt n(\theta-\theta_0)\).

Theorem. If the posterior localizes in \(n^{-1/2}\)-neighborhoods and the prior density is continuous and positive at \(\theta_0\), LAN implies that the local posterior density is approximately proportional to \[ \exp\{h^\top\Delta_n-\tfrac12h^\top I_{\theta_0}h\}. \]

Proof. Bayes formula in the \(h\)-coordinate multiplies the likelihood ratio by \(\pi(\theta_0+h/\sqrt n)\). Continuity of the prior makes this factor locally constant, while LAN gives the displayed quadratic likelihood ratio.

Completing the square

The LAN kernel is Gaussian after completing the square.

Proposition. The kernel \[ \exp\{h^\top\Delta-\tfrac12h^\top I h\} \] is proportional to the density of \(N(I^{-1}\Delta,I^{-1})\).

Proof. Complete the square: \[ h^\top\Delta-\frac12h^\top Ih =-\frac12(h-I^{-1}\Delta)^\top I(h-I^{-1}\Delta) +\frac12\Delta^\top I^{-1}\Delta. \] The last term does not depend on \(h\).

Laplace approximation as local LAN

In regular parametric models, Laplace approximation can be viewed as deterministic LAN around the posterior mode.

Remark. LAN is probabilistic and centered around the true parameter; Laplace approximation is analytic and centered around the mode. Under regularity, the two pictures agree to first order.
