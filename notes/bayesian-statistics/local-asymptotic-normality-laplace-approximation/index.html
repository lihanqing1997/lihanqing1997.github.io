16. Local asymptotic normality and Laplace approximation

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/posterior-consistency-parametric-models/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/bernstein-von-mises-theorem/" %}

This page records the main definitions, propositions, and proof ideas for Local asymptotic normality and Laplace approximation.

Definition

Definition. LAN at \(\theta_0\) means \[ \log\frac{p_{\theta_0+h/\sqrt n}^{(n)}}{p_{\theta_0}^{(n)}}=h^\top\Delta_n-\frac12h^\top I_{\theta_0}h+o_{P_{\theta_0}}(1). \]

Proposition

Proposition. With a continuous positive prior density, the local posterior of \(h=\sqrt n(\theta-\theta_0)\) is approximately \(N(I_{\theta_0}^{-1}\Delta_n,I_{\theta_0}^{-1})\).

Proof

Proof. Insert the LAN expansion into Bayes formula and complete the square.

Remarks

LAN is the local calculation underneath many Bernstein-von Mises theorems.
