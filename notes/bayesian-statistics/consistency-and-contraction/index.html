Consistency asks whether posterior mass reaches every fixed neighborhood of the truth. Contraction asks how fast the posterior neighborhood can shrink.

KL support and tests

The basic posterior consistency proof has a denominator lower bound and a numerator upper bound.

Definition. A prior has the KL property at \(P_0\) if for every \(\varepsilon>0\), \[ \Pi\{P:K(P_0,P)<\varepsilon\}>0. \]

Theorem. Schwartz's theorem: KL support at \(P_0\) plus uniformly exponentially consistent tests for the complement of a neighborhood \(U\) imply \(\Pi(U^c\mid X^{(n)})\to0\) under \(P_0\).

Proof. The posterior bad mass is a numerator divided by a denominator. KL support gives a lower bound on the denominator of order \(\exp(-n\varepsilon)\) with high probability. The tests split the numerator into type-I and type-II terms, both exponentially small on \(U^c\). Choosing constants appropriately forces the ratio to vanish.

Doob consistency

Doob's theorem is powerful but answers a different question from fixed-truth frequentist consistency.

Theorem. Under identifiability and standard measurability conditions, the posterior is consistent for \(\Pi\)-almost every parameter value.

Proof. Let \(\Theta\) be the random parameter drawn from \(\Pi\), and let \(X_1,X_2,\ldots\) be drawn conditionally from \(P_\Theta^\infty\). Identifiability implies that the tail sigma-field generated by the infinite observation sequence determines \(\Theta\) almost surely. The posterior is the conditional law of \(\Theta\) given the first \(n\) observations; by martingale convergence, these conditional laws converge to the conditional law given the infinite sequence, which is the point mass at the true \(\Theta\) for \(\Pi\)-almost every parameter.

Remark. The exceptional set may depend on the prior and can contain scientifically important truths. Schwartz-type theorems are designed to verify consistency at a fixed \(P_0\).

Contraction rates

A rate \(\varepsilon_n\) is meaningful when posterior mass outside \(M\varepsilon_n\)-balls vanishes.

Definition. The posterior contracts at rate \(\varepsilon_n\) around \(P_0\) for metric \(d\) if, for sufficiently large \(M\), \[ \Pi(P:d(P,P_0)>M\varepsilon_n\mid X^{(n)})\to0 \] in \(P_0\)-probability.

Testing scale

The rate is governed by the smallest scale at which tests, entropy, and prior mass all work.

Remark. The usual inequality \(n\varepsilon_n^2\) appears because testing errors for Hellinger-separated distributions are exponentially small in \(n d^2\), while entropy and prior-mass terms must fit under that exponential budget.
