14. Laplace, variational, and other posterior approximations

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/hamiltonian-monte-carlo-diagnostics/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/posterior-consistency-parametric-models/" %}

This page records the main definitions, propositions, and proof ideas for Laplace, variational, and other posterior approximations.

Definition

Definition. Variational inference minimizes \(\operatorname{KL}(q\|\Pi(\cdot\mid X))\) over a tractable family \(\mathcal Q\).

Proposition

Proposition. If \(\ell(\theta)=\log p_\theta(X)+\log\pi(\theta)\) has mode \(\hat\theta\) and negative Hessian \(H\), then Laplace gives \(\Pi(\mathrm{d}\theta\mid X)\approx N(\hat\theta,H^{-1})\).

Proof

Proof. A second-order Taylor expansion at the posterior mode gives a quadratic log density.

Remarks

Laplace is local and curvature-based; variational approximation is global but family-restricted.
