Approximate inference replaces exact posterior integration by a tractable approximation. The approximation should be understood as a mathematical object with its own error, not as a different posterior.

Laplace approximation

Laplace approximation is local Gaussian approximation at a posterior mode.

Theorem. Let \(h\) have a strict interior maximum at \(\hat\theta\), and let \(H=-\nabla^2 h(\hat\theta)\) be positive definite. Then, formally, \[ \int \exp\{n h(\theta)\}a(\theta)\,\mathrm{d}\theta \approx \exp\{n h(\hat\theta)\}a(\hat\theta)(2\pi/n)^{d/2}\det(H)^{-1/2}. \]

Proof. Taylor expand \(h(\theta)\) around \(\hat\theta\): \[ h(\theta)=h(\hat\theta)-\frac12(\theta-\hat\theta)^\top H(\theta-\hat\theta)+o(\|\theta-\hat\theta\|^2). \] The change of variables \(u=\sqrt n(\theta-\hat\theta)\) reduces the leading term to a Gaussian integral.

Gaussian posterior approximation

In Bayesian computation, Laplace approximation turns curvature into covariance.

Proposition. If \(\hat\theta\) is the posterior mode and \(H_n=-\nabla^2\log\{\pi(\theta)p_\theta(X)\}|_{\hat\theta}\), then the Laplace approximation is \(N(\hat\theta,H_n^{-1})\).

Proof. Apply the quadratic Taylor expansion to the log posterior density and normalize the resulting Gaussian kernel.

Variational approximation

Variational methods choose an approximating distribution from a restricted family.

Definition. For a variational family \(\mathcal Q\), reverse-KL variational Bayes solves \[ q^*=\arg\min_{q\in\mathcal Q}\mathrm{KL}(q\,\|\,\Pi(\cdot\mid X)). \]

Remark. Laplace approximation is local and curvature-based; variational approximation is global over a restricted class but biased toward the geometry induced by the chosen divergence.

Approximation error

Every approximate posterior should be judged by the posterior functionals it is meant to approximate.

Proposition. If \(d_{\mathrm{TV}}(Q,\Pi(\cdot\mid X))\le\varepsilon\) and \(|g|\le M\), then \[ \left|\int g\,\mathrm{d}Q-\int g\,\mathrm{d}\Pi(\cdot\mid X)\right|\le 2M\varepsilon. \]

Proof. The total variation distance is half the \(L^1\) distance between densities under a common dominating measure. The expectation difference is bounded by \(M\) times that \(L^1\) distance.
