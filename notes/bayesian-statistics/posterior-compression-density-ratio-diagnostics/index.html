Compressed posterior summaries

Posterior compression replaces a complicated posterior object by a smaller representation whose fidelity can be checked. In redundant-predictor problems, regions or groups may be more stable than exact supports.

Definition. Let \(P\) be the full posterior predictive or posterior law and \(Q\) a compressed approximation. A density-ratio diagnostic studies \(r=\mathrm{d}P/\mathrm{d}Q\) on the sigma-field relevant to the summary. Exact fidelity is \(r=1\) \(Q\)-almost surely.

Remark. A compressed summary is useful only when it preserves the inferential quantity at stake. Support compression, predictive compression, and credible-region compression are different mathematical targets.

Coresets and weighted likelihoods

Definition. A Bayesian coreset replaces \(\ell_n(\theta)=\sum_{i=1}^n\ell_i(\theta)\) by a weighted sparse sum \(\ell_w(\theta)=\sum_{i=1}^n w_i\ell_i(\theta)\), with many \(w_i=0\).

Proposition. If \(\sup_\theta|\ell_w(\theta)-\ell_n(\theta)|\le\varepsilon\), then the normalizing constants and posterior expectations of bounded functions are stable up to errors controlled by \(e^\varepsilon-1\).

Proof. The likelihood ratio between weighted and full posterior kernels lies between \(e^{-\varepsilon}\) and \(e^\varepsilon\). Normalize both measures and apply the same multiplicative bound to integrals of bounded test functions.
