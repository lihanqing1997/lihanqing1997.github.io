22. Function-space priors and random series

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/bayesian-testing-model-selection-asymptotics/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/priors-on-probability-measures/" %}

This page records the main definitions, propositions, and proof ideas for Function-space priors and random series.

Definition

Definition. A random series prior writes \(f=\sum_{j\ge1}\beta_j\psi_j\), with random coefficients chosen to ensure convergence in the target space.

Proposition

Proposition. If \((\psi_j)\) is an orthonormal basis of a Hilbert space, convergence holds whenever \(\sum_j\beta_j^2<\infty\) almost surely.

Proof

Proof. Parseval identity turns tail convergence in the Hilbert norm into convergence of squared coefficient tails.

Remarks

Approximation error is controlled by the basis; prior concentration is controlled by coefficient tails.
