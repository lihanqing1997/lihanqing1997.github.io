12. Markov chain Monte Carlo

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/monte-carlo-integration-simulation-error/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/hamiltonian-monte-carlo-diagnostics/" %}

This page records the main definitions, propositions, and proof ideas for Markov chain Monte Carlo.

Definition

Definition. A transition kernel \(K\) has invariant distribution \(\Pi\) if \(\int K(\theta,A)\,\mathrm{d}\Pi(\theta)=\Pi(A)\) for every measurable \(A\).

Proposition

Proposition. Metropolis-Hastings uses \[ \alpha(\theta,\theta')=\min\left\{1,\frac{\pi(\theta')q(\theta\mid\theta')}{\pi(\theta)q(\theta'\mid\theta)}\right\} \] to make \(\Pi\) reversible.

Proof

Proof. Detailed balance holds because both directed move densities equal the same minimum.

Remarks

Reliability depends on ergodicity, mixing, autocorrelation, and effective sample size.
