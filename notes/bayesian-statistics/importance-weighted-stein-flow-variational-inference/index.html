Importance-weighted bounds

Definition. For proposal \(q_\phi(z\mid x)\) and samples \(z_1,\ldots,z_K\), the importance-weighted objective is \[ \mathcal L_K=E\log\left({1\over K}\sum_{k=1}^K {p_\theta(x,z_k)\over q_\phi(z_k\mid x)}\right). \]

Proposition. \(\mathcal L_K\le \log p_\theta(x)\), and \(\mathcal L_K\) is nondecreasing in \(K\).

Proof. Jensen gives the lower bound because the average importance weight has expectation \(p_\theta(x)\). Monotonicity follows by conditioning on \(K+1\) samples and averaging over subsets of size \(K\), then applying Jensen to the logarithm.

Stein variational inference

Definition. Stein variational gradient descent transports particles by \(T_\epsilon(z)=z+\epsilon\phi(z)\), choosing \(\phi\) in an RKHS to most decrease \(D(T_{\epsilon\#}Q\|\Pi)\) at \(\epsilon=0\).

Remark. Normalizing flows use explicit invertible maps and Jacobian determinants; Stein methods use interacting particles and kernelized gradients. Both enlarge the variational family beyond mean-field factorization.

Reparameterization gradients and degeneracy

Proposition. If \(z=T_\phi(\epsilon,x)\) with \(\epsilon\sim r\), gradients of reparameterized objectives can be written by differentiating through \(T_\phi\) under the expectation, provided domination conditions hold.

Proof. Express the objective as \(\int F(T_\phi(\epsilon,x),\phi)r(\epsilon)\,\mathrm{d}\epsilon\). If the derivative is dominated by an integrable envelope, dominated convergence permits interchange of differentiation and integration.

Stein identity

Definition. For a smooth target density \(\pi\), the Langevin Stein operator is \[ \mathcal A_\pi\phi(z)=\nabla\log\pi(z)^\top\phi(z)+\nabla\cdot\phi(z). \] Under boundary conditions, \(E_\pi\mathcal A_\pi\phi=0\).
