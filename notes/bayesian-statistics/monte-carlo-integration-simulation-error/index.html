Monte Carlo methods approximate posterior expectations by random averages. The target is mathematical integration; simulation error must therefore be quantified like any other approximation error.

Posterior expectations as integrals

Most Bayesian summaries are expectations under the posterior.

Definition. For a posterior \(\Pi(\mathrm{d}\theta\mid X)\) and integrable function \(g\), the target is \[ I=\int g(\theta)\Pi(\mathrm{d}\theta\mid X). \] Given draws \(\theta_1,\ldots,\theta_N\), the Monte Carlo estimator is \(\hat I_N=N^{-1}\sum_{s=1}^N g(\theta_s)\).

Theorem. If \(\theta_s\) are iid from the posterior and \(\mathbb E|g(\theta)|<\infty\), then \(\hat I_N\to I\) almost surely.

Proof. This is the strong law of large numbers applied to the iid sequence \(g(\theta_s)\).

Monte Carlo central limit theorem

Error bars require second moments, not just consistency.

Proposition. If \(\operatorname{Var}\{g(\theta)\}<\infty\), then \[ \sqrt N(\hat I_N-I)\Rightarrow N(0,\operatorname{Var}\{g(\theta)\}). \]

Proof. Apply the classical central limit theorem to \(g(\theta_s)\). A consistent estimate of the variance gives standard Monte Carlo error estimates.

Importance sampling

Importance sampling changes the simulation law but corrects the integral with weights.

Definition. If \(q\) dominates the posterior density \(\pi\), then \[ I=\frac{\int g(\theta)w(\theta)q(\theta)\,\mathrm{d}\theta}{\int w(\theta)q(\theta)\,\mathrm{d}\theta}, \qquad w(\theta)=\frac{\pi(\theta\mid X)}{q(\theta)}. \]

Remark. Importance sampling fails when the weight distribution has heavy tails. A proposal must cover the posterior tails at least as well as the target.

Effective sample size

Effective sample size is a diagnostic for weight or dependence degeneracy, not a new theorem about the posterior.

Proposition. For normalized importance weights \(\bar w_s=w_s/\sum_r w_r\), the common diagnostic \[ N_{\mathrm{eff}}=\frac{1}{\sum_s\bar w_s^2} \] equals \(N\) for equal weights and approaches \(1\) when one draw dominates.

Proof. The expression is the reciprocal of the squared \(\ell^2\)-norm of the normalized weight vector. It is maximized by the uniform vector and minimized by a point mass.
