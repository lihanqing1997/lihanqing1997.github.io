26. Gaussian process priors and RKHS geometry

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/polya-trees-histograms-tail-free-priors/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/mixture-priors-density-estimation/" %}

Gaussian process priors put Gaussian laws on function spaces. Their geometry is governed by covariance kernels, reproducing-kernel Hilbert spaces, and small-ball probabilities.

Gaussian processes

A Gaussian process is determined by its mean and covariance kernel.

Definition. A random function \(W\) on \(\mathcal X\) is a Gaussian process with mean \(m\) and covariance \(K\) if every finite vector \((W(x_1),\ldots,W(x_k))\) is multivariate normal with mean \(m(x_j)\) and covariance \(K(x_i,x_j)\).

Proposition. A symmetric positive semidefinite kernel \(K\) defines consistent finite-dimensional Gaussian distributions.

Proof. Positive semidefiniteness gives valid covariance matrices on every finite set. Consistency under marginalization follows from the corresponding property of multivariate normal distributions, and Kolmogorov extension gives a stochastic process.

RKHS and support

The RKHS is not the typical sample-path space, but it controls shifts and support.

Definition. The reproducing-kernel Hilbert space \(\mathbb H\) associated with \(K\) is the Hilbert closure of finite linear combinations of \(K(x,\cdot)\), with inner product satisfying \(f(x)=\langle f,K(x,\cdot)\rangle_{\mathbb H}\).

Theorem. For many Gaussian measures on Banach spaces, the support is the closure of the RKHS in the Banach norm.

Proof. The Cameron-Martin space gives directions along which Gaussian measure can be shifted absolutely continuously. Small balls around RKHS elements have positive probability, and closure gives all support points; points outside the closure have separating neighborhoods of zero Gaussian mass.

Concentration function

Posterior contraction for Gaussian priors is often expressed through approximation plus small-ball terms.

Definition. For truth \(f_0\), the concentration function is \[ \varphi_{f_0}(\varepsilon) =\inf_{\|h-f_0\|\le\varepsilon,h\in\mathbb H}\frac12\|h\|_{\mathbb H}^2 -\log \mathbb P(\|W\|\le\varepsilon). \]

Remark. The first term measures RKHS approximation cost; the second measures how much prior mass a small ball receives.

Kernel scale and adaptation

Length-scale hyperparameters change both smoothness and concentration.

Remark. Hierarchical Gaussian process priors place a prior on scale parameters so that the posterior can adapt to unknown regularity, but adaptation requires enough hyperprior mass near the scale that balances bias and variance.
