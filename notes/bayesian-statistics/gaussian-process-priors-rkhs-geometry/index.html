25. Gaussian process priors and RKHS geometry

This page records the main definitions, propositions, and proof ideas for Gaussian process priors and RKHS geometry.

Definition

Definition. A Gaussian process is a random function whose finite-dimensional marginals are multivariate normal. Its RKHS describes deterministic shifts of finite Gaussian cost.

Proposition

Proposition. The concentration function \[ \varphi_{f_0}(\varepsilon)=\inf_{\|h-f_0\|\le\varepsilon}\frac12\|h\|_{\mathbb H}^2-\log P(\|W\|\le\varepsilon) \] controls rates through \(\varphi_{f_0}(\varepsilon_n)\lesssim n\varepsilon_n^2\).

Proof

Proof. The first term is the RKHS approximation cost, and the second is the small-ball probability cost.

Remarks

Rescaling and length-scale priors adapt Gaussian-process geometry to unknown smoothness.
