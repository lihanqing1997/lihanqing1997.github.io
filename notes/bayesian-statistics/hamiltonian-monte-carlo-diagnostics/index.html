13. Hamiltonian Monte Carlo and diagnostics

This page records the main definitions, propositions, and proof ideas for Hamiltonian Monte Carlo and diagnostics.

Definition

Definition. For target \(\pi(q)\) and momentum \(p\sim N(0,M)\), the Hamiltonian is \[ H(q,p)=-\log\pi(q)+\frac12p^\top M^{-1}p. \]

Proposition

Proposition. Leapfrog integration is reversible and volume preserving, so a Metropolis correction based on Hamiltonian error targets the exact posterior.

Proof

Proof. Each leapfrog substep is a determinant-one shear, and the full step is symmetric under momentum reversal.

Remarks

Divergences, low effective sample size, and large \(\hat R\) are geometry or mixing warnings.
