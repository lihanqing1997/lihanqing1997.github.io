Under misspecification, the posterior often concentrates around the KL projection of the truth rather than a true parameter. The limiting covariance then differs from ordinary Fisher information.

Pseudo-true parameters

When the model is wrong, the target becomes the best approximation inside the model.

Definition. Let \(P_0\) be the data-generating law and \(P_\theta\) the model. A pseudo-true parameter is \[ \theta^*=\arg\min_{\theta}K(P_0,P_\theta) =\arg\max_\theta P_0\log p_\theta, \] when the minimizer exists.

Proposition. The expected score at an interior pseudo-true parameter is zero: \(P_0\dot\ell_{\theta^*}=0\).

Proof. Differentiate the criterion \(\theta\mapsto P_0\log p_\theta\) at its interior maximum and use the regularity that permits interchange of differentiation and integration.

Sandwich covariance

The curvature and score variance no longer agree under misspecification.

Definition. Let \[ J=-P_0\ddot\ell_{\theta^*},\qquad V=P_0(\dot\ell_{\theta^*}\dot\ell_{\theta^*}^\top). \] The frequentist covariance of the maximum likelihood estimator is \(J^{-1}VJ^{-1}\), while the local posterior covariance is often \(J^{-1}\).

Remark. This mismatch is why credible sets can have incorrect frequentist coverage under misspecification even when the posterior is asymptotically normal.

Misspecified BvM

The posterior can still be asymptotically Gaussian, but it is centered at the pseudo-true parameter.

Theorem. Under smoothness, prior positivity at \(\theta^*\), posterior localization, and nonsingular \(J\), the posterior of \(\sqrt n(\theta-\hat\theta_n)\) is asymptotically \(N(0,J^{-1})\).

Proof. Expand the empirical log likelihood around \(\hat\theta_n\). The first derivative vanishes at the empirical maximizer, and the Hessian converges to \(-J\). Prior continuity makes the prior locally constant. Laplace approximation then gives the Gaussian posterior covariance \(J^{-1}\).

Robust adjustment

If frequentist calibration is desired under misspecification, posterior covariance may need sandwich correction.

Remark. Bayesian coherence and frequentist calibration are different targets. A sandwich-adjusted posterior is no longer the exact posterior but can be useful as a calibrated uncertainty approximation.
