19. Model misspecification and pseudo-true parameters

This page records the main definitions, propositions, and proof ideas for Model misspecification and pseudo-true parameters.

Definition

Definition. A pseudo-true parameter minimizes KL divergence: \[ \theta^\star\in\operatorname*{argmin}_\theta P_0\log\frac{p_0}{p_\theta}. \]

Proposition

Proposition. In smooth misspecified models, posterior covariance and sampling covariance need not match; the latter has sandwich form.

Proof

Proof. Posterior curvature comes from the expected Hessian, while sampling variance also uses the score variance under the true law.

Remarks

Ordinary credible sets can undercover under misspecification unless corrected or robustified.
