This page records the main definitions, propositions, and proof ideas for Pólya trees, histograms, and tail-free priors.

Definition

Definition. A Pólya tree recursively splits mass by beta variables: \(G(A_{\epsilon0})=G(A_\epsilon)V_\epsilon\) and \(G(A_{\epsilon1})=G(A_\epsilon)(1-V_\epsilon)\).

Proposition

Proposition. Tail-free structure is conjugate: each local beta split updates by left and right descendant counts.

Proof

Proof. Given a parent node, child counts are binomial and beta-binomial conjugacy applies locally.

Remarks

Random histograms are finite truncations that make computation and entropy transparent.
