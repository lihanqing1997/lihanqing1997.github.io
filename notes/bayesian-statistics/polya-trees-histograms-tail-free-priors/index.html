25. Pólya trees, histograms, and tail-free priors

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/dirichlet-processes-polya-urns/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/gaussian-process-priors-rkhs-geometry/" %}

Pólya trees construct random probability measures through recursive random splits. They make the relation between partitions, histograms, and nonparametric priors explicit.

Recursive beta splits

A Pólya tree assigns random masses down a binary partition tree.

Definition. Let \(\mathcal X\) be recursively partitioned into sets \(A_\epsilon\), \(\epsilon\in\{0,1\}^m\). At each node, draw \[ V_\epsilon\sim\operatorname{Beta}(a_{\epsilon0},a_{\epsilon1}) \] and assign conditional masses \(P(A_{\epsilon0}\mid A_\epsilon)=V_\epsilon\), \(P(A_{\epsilon1}\mid A_\epsilon)=1-V_\epsilon\).

Proposition. The mass of a cell is the product of the split probabilities along its path.

Proof. Use the chain rule \(P(A_{\epsilon_1\cdots\epsilon_m})=\prod_{r=1}^m P(A_{\epsilon_1\cdots\epsilon_r}\mid A_{\epsilon_1\cdots\epsilon_{r-1}})\).

Posterior conjugacy

Observed counts update beta splits node by node.

Proposition. If \(n_{\epsilon0}\) and \(n_{\epsilon1}\) are the numbers of observations going left and right from node \(\epsilon\), then \[ V_\epsilon\mid X_{1:n}\sim\operatorname{Beta}(a_{\epsilon0}+n_{\epsilon0},a_{\epsilon1}+n_{\epsilon1}). \]

Proof. Conditional on reaching a node, the split likelihood is binomial in \(V_\epsilon\). Beta-binomial conjugacy gives the update, independently across nodes conditional on the tree counts.

Absolute continuity

The smoothness of a Pólya tree draw depends on how quickly split variances decrease down the tree.

Remark. If split parameters grow sufficiently fast with depth, random measures are often absolutely continuous with respect to the centering measure. If not, draws can be singular or too rough.

Histograms and tail-free priors

Finite truncations of Pólya trees are random histograms on a partition.

Example. Stopping the tree at depth \(M\) gives a random probability vector over \(2^M\) cells. Conditional on that vector, the density is piecewise constant relative to the base partition measure.
