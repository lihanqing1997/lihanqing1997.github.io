38. Advanced variational inference

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/bayesian-glms-high-dimensional-likelihoods/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/importance-weighted-stein-flow-variational-inference/" %}

Variational objective

Variational inference is not a single algorithm. It is a family of projection problems whose statistical behavior depends on the variational family, the KL direction, and the optimization error.

Definition. For a family \(\mathcal Q\), variational Bayes chooses \[ \hat Q\in\arg\min_{Q\in\mathcal Q}D(Q\|\Pi(\cdot\mid X)) \] equivalently maximizing the ELBO \[ \mathcal L(Q)=E_Q\log p(X,\theta)-E_Q\log q(\theta). \]

Proposition. If \(\mathcal Q=\{\prod_{j=1}^m q_j(\theta_j)\}\), the coordinate ascent update satisfies \[ \log q_j^*(\theta_j)=E_{-j}\log p(X,\theta)+\mathrm{constant}. \]

Proof. Hold all other factors fixed and maximize the ELBO over \(q_j\) under the constraint \(\int q_j=1\). The variational derivative gives \(E_{-j}\log p(X,\theta)-\log q_j-1+\lambda=0\), hence the displayed update.

Approximation and optimization gaps

Definition. The variational error decomposes conceptually into an approximation gap \(\inf_{Q\in\mathcal Q}D(Q\|\Pi)\) and an optimization gap \(D(\hat Q\|\Pi)-\inf_{Q\in\mathcal Q}D(Q\|\Pi)\).

Remark. A small ELBO gap does not automatically imply correct posterior uncertainty. Reverse KL can choose one mode, shrink tails, and underestimate dependence.

ELBO gap and posterior risk

Proposition. If \(Q\) is a variational distribution and \(\Pi\) is the exact posterior, then \[ \log m(X)-\mathcal L(Q)=\operatorname{KL}(Q\|\Pi). \] Thus optimizing the ELBO is exactly reverse-KL projection when optimization is exact.

Proof. Write \(\pi(\theta\mid X)=p_\theta(X)\pi(\theta)/m(X)\) and integrate \(\log q-\log\pi(\theta\mid X)\) against \(Q\). Rearranging gives the identity.

Mean-field bias

Remark. Mean-field factorization can be accurate for posterior means and poor for uncertainty. For theoretical work, the object to bound is not only the ELBO gap but the loss relevant to the statistical question.
