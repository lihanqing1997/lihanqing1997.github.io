5. Prior construction, Jeffreys priors, and weak information

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/conjugacy-hierarchical-models-and-computation/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/hierarchical-models-exchangeability/" %}

This page records the main definitions, propositions, and proof ideas for Prior construction, Jeffreys priors, and weak information.

Definition

Definition. For Fisher information \(I(\theta)\), Jeffreys prior has density \(\pi_J(\theta)\propto\det I(\theta)^{1/2}\).

Proposition

Proposition. Under a smooth one-to-one reparametrization \(\phi=g(\theta)\), \[ \pi_J^\phi(\phi)\,\mathrm{d}\phi=\pi_J^\theta(\theta)\,\mathrm{d}\theta. \]

Proof

Proof. Fisher information transforms as \(I_\phi=J^\top I_\theta J\), where \(J=\mathrm{d}\theta/\mathrm{d}\phi\). Taking determinants gives the density transformation.

Remarks

Weakly informative priors are not neutral; they rule out implausible scales and unstable likelihood regions.
