Prior construction balances invariance, information, support, and posterior propriety. Weak information does not mean no information; it means deliberately controlled information.

Jeffreys prior and reparametrization

Jeffreys prior is built from the local curvature of the statistical model.

Definition. For a regular one-parameter model with Fisher information \[ I(\theta)=\mathbb E_\theta\left[\left(\frac{\partial}{\partial\theta}\log p_\theta(X)\right)^2\right], \] Jeffreys prior is \(\pi_J(\theta)\propto I(\theta)^{1/2}\). In dimension \(d\), \(\pi_J(\theta)\propto\det I(\theta)^{1/2}\).

Proposition. Jeffreys prior is invariant under smooth one-to-one reparametrization.

Proof. If \(\phi=g(\theta)\), then \(I_\phi(\phi)=I_\theta(\theta)(\mathrm{d}\theta/\mathrm{d}\phi)^2\) in one dimension, and \(I_\phi=J^\top I_\theta J\) in several dimensions. Taking square roots of determinants gives the Jacobian factor required for density transformation.

Improper priors and posterior propriety

Improper priors can be useful notation, but the posterior must still normalize.

Definition. An improper prior is a sigma-finite measure \(\Pi\) with \(\Pi(\Theta)=\infty\). It defines a posterior only when \[ 0<\int p_\theta(X)\,\Pi(\mathrm{d}\theta)<\infty \] for the observed data, or almost surely under the intended sampling laws.

Example. For \(X_i\sim N(\theta,\sigma^2)\) with known \(\sigma\), the flat prior \(\Pi(\mathrm{d}\theta)=\mathrm{d}\theta\) gives \[ \theta\mid X^{(n)}\sim N(\bar X,\sigma^2/n), \] which is proper for \(n\ge1\).

Weakly informative priors

Weakly informative priors regularize extreme parameter values without attempting to encode detailed prior knowledge.

Remark. A prior may be weak on the natural scale but strong after transformation. In hierarchical and high-dimensional models, weakly informative priors are often used to prevent separation, nonidentifiability, or unrealistic tail behavior.

Prior predictive checking

A prior can be assessed before seeing the data by simulating from the prior predictive distribution.

Proposition. If the prior predictive puts substantial mass on scientifically impossible data, then either the likelihood, the prior, or the parameterization is misspecified for the intended analysis.

Proof. The prior predictive is the distribution of data under the assumed joint Bayesian model. Events with large prior predictive probability are therefore events the model declares plausible before observation.
