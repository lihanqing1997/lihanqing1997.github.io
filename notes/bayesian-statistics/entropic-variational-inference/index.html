Entropic regularization

Entropic VI is useful when the goal is not only tractability but also preserving dependence or spreading mass. The mathematical object is a tradeoff between fidelity to the target and entropy of the approximation.

Definition. An entropically regularized variational problem has the form \[ \min_{Q\in\mathcal Q}\; E_Q[-\log p(X,\theta)]+\rho D(Q\|R)-\tau H(Q), \] where \(R\) is a reference measure and \(H(Q)=-E_Q\log q\) when \(Q\) has density \(q\).

Proposition. If \(\mathcal Q\) is unrestricted and the objective is \(D(Q\|\Pi)-\tau H(Q)\) relative to a base measure, the optimizer is a tempered density proportional to \(\pi^{1/(1+\tau)}\).

Proof. Write the objective as \(\int q\log(q/\pi)\,\mathrm{d}\mu+\tau\int q\log q\,\mathrm{d}\mu\). Taking the variational derivative under \(\int q=1\) gives \((1+\tau)\log q-\log\pi+\mathrm{constant}=0\), hence \(q\propto \pi^{1/(1+\tau)}\).

Mirror descent

Remark. Entropy is the Bregman generator behind exponentiated-gradient and mirror-descent updates. This is the optimization bridge between entropic VI, natural gradient methods, and information geometry.

KL-proximal update

Theorem. A KL-proximal step for minimizing a linearized objective \(E_Q g\) over probability measures satisfies \[ q^+(\theta)\propto q(\theta)\exp\{-\eta g(\theta)\}. \]

Proof. Minimize \(E_Q g+\eta^{-1}\operatorname{KL}(Q\|Q^{old})\) subject to \(\int q=1\). The Euler equation gives \(g+\eta^{-1}(\log q-\log q^{old}+1)+\lambda=0\), which yields the exponential tilt.

Temperature and uncertainty

Remark. Entropic smoothing can prevent premature collapse of the variational approximation, but it also changes the target unless the temperature is sent to zero or corrected by a final projection.
