Hierarchical Bayes introduces latent structure and shared hyperparameters. Exchangeability explains why such models are natural for repeated units that have no intrinsic ordering.

Conditional independence and partial pooling

A hierarchical model separates unit-level variation from population-level variation.

Definition. A basic two-level model has \[ Y_i\mid\theta_i\sim P_{\theta_i},\qquad \theta_i\mid\eta\stackrel{\mathrm{iid}}{\sim}G_\eta,\qquad \eta\sim H. \] Conditional on \(\eta\), the unit parameters are independent; marginally, they are dependent.

Proposition. The posterior distribution of \(\theta_i\) uses information from both \(Y_i\) and the other observations through the hyperparameter \(\eta\).

Proof. The posterior factorizes as \[ \pi(\eta,\theta_{1:n}\mid y)\propto h(\eta)\prod_{i=1}^n g_\eta(\theta_i)p_{\theta_i}(y_i). \] Integrating out \(\eta\) couples the conditional posterior of each \(\theta_i\) to all data \(y_{1:n}\).

Normal means shrinkage

The normal hierarchical model gives the clearest formula for partial pooling.

Example. If \(Y_i\mid\theta_i\sim N(\theta_i,\sigma^2)\) and \(\theta_i\mid\mu,\tau^2\sim N(\mu,\tau^2)\), then for fixed \((\mu,\tau^2)\), \[ \mathbb E(\theta_i\mid Y_i,\mu,\tau^2) =\frac{\tau^2}{\tau^2+\sigma^2}Y_i+\frac{\sigma^2}{\tau^2+\sigma^2}\mu. \] The posterior mean shrinks \(Y_i\) toward the population mean \(\mu\).

Exchangeability and de Finetti representation

Exchangeability turns symmetry into a mixture representation.

Theorem. For an infinite exchangeable sequence \(Y_1,Y_2,\ldots\) taking values in a standard Borel space, there exists a random probability measure \(P\) such that, conditionally on \(P\), the \(Y_i\) are iid with common law \(P\).

Proof. A full proof is a measure-theoretic theorem. The key idea is that empirical measures form a reverse martingale under the exchangeable sigma-field, and conditional distributions given that tail information become iid directing laws.

Hyperpriors and identifiability

Hyperpriors must be chosen with the same care as ordinary priors.

Remark. Weak data information about variance components can make posterior behavior sensitive to hyperprior tails near zero or infinity. In hierarchical models, prior predictive checking is especially important because hyperparameters control the scale of entire groups.
