6. Hierarchical models and exchangeability

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/prior-construction-jeffreys-weak-information/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/bayesian-regression-and-glms/" %}

This page records the main definitions, propositions, and proof ideas for Hierarchical models and exchangeability.

Definition

Definition. A sequence is exchangeable if each finite subvector has a distribution invariant under permutations. A hierarchical model often writes \(\theta_i\mid G\sim G\) and \(X_i\mid\theta_i\sim P_{\theta_i}\).

Proposition

Proposition. De Finetti representation states that infinite exchangeable laws are mixtures of i.i.d. laws: \[ P(\theta_1\in A_1,\ldots,\theta_n\in A_n)=\int\prod_{i=1}^nG(A_i)\,\mathrm{d}\Lambda(G). \]

Proof

Proof. The empirical measure is a martingale object whose limit is the directing random measure; conditional on it, the coordinates become independent.

Remarks

Partial pooling is posterior shrinkage toward a group distribution, strongest when group variance is small or unit-level data are scarce.
