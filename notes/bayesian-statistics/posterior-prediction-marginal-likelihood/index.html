2. Posterior prediction and marginal likelihood

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/bayesian-models-priors-posteriors/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/posterior-and-decision/" %}

This page records the main definitions, propositions, and proof ideas for Posterior prediction and marginal likelihood.

Definition

Definition. The posterior predictive law of a new observation \(Y\) is \(P(Y\in A\mid X)=\int P_\theta(A)\,\mathrm{d}\Pi(\theta\mid X)\). The marginal likelihood is \(m(X)=\int p_\theta(X)\,\mathrm{d}\Pi(\theta)\).

Proposition

Proposition. For models \(M_0,M_1\), \[ \frac{P(M_1\mid X)}{P(M_0\mid X)}=\frac{P(M_1)}{P(M_0)}\frac{m_1(X)}{m_0(X)}. \] The second factor is the Bayes factor.

Proof

Proof. Apply Bayes formula on the enlarged model space and cancel the common normalizing constant in the odds ratio.

Remarks

Prediction averages over posterior uncertainty; plug-in prediction suppresses one source of variability.
