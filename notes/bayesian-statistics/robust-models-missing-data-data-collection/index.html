Robust Bayesian modeling changes the likelihood or prior so that inference is less brittle under outliers, missingness, or data-collection mechanisms.

Heavy-tailed observation models

A common robust move is to replace Gaussian errors with heavier-tailed errors.

Definition. The Student \(t_\nu\) error model can be represented as \[ Y_i\mid\theta,\lambda_i\sim N(\theta,\sigma^2/\lambda_i),\qquad \lambda_i\sim\operatorname{Gamma}(\nu/2,\nu/2). \]

Proposition. Integrating out \(\lambda_i\) gives the Student \(t_\nu\) density for \(Y_i-\theta\).

Proof. The normal density contributes \(\lambda_i^{1/2}\exp\{-\lambda_i(y_i-\theta)^2/(2\sigma^2)\}\). Multiplication by the gamma density gives a gamma kernel in \(\lambda_i\). Integrating the gamma kernel yields a power term of the form \((1+(y_i-\theta)^2/(\nu\sigma^2))^{-(\nu+1)/2}\).

Missing data mechanisms

Bayesian missing-data analysis models the unobserved data jointly with the observed data.

Definition. Let \(Y=(Y_{\mathrm{obs}},Y_{\mathrm{mis}})\) and let \(R\) be the missingness pattern. The mechanism is missing at random if \[ P(R\mid Y_{\mathrm{obs}},Y_{\mathrm{mis}},\psi)=P(R\mid Y_{\mathrm{obs}},\psi). \]

Proposition. Under missing at random and parameter distinctness between \(\theta\) and \(\psi\), likelihood-based inference for \(\theta\) may ignore the missingness mechanism.

Proof. The observed likelihood factors into a term involving \(\theta\) through \(\int p_\theta(y_{\mathrm{obs}},y_{\mathrm{mis}})\,\mathrm{d}y_{\mathrm{mis}}\) and a term involving \(\psi\) through the missingness model. If the parameter spaces are distinct and the prior factorizes, the posterior for \(\theta\) is proportional to the first factor times its prior.

Contamination neighborhoods

Robustness can be formalized by asking how posterior summaries change under local perturbations of the model.

Remark. Mixture contamination, scale mixtures, and bounded-influence likelihoods all reduce sensitivity to observations that would dominate a thin-tailed Gaussian likelihood.

Data-collection design

The sampling design can be included in the joint law rather than treated as an afterthought.

Proposition. If the design is ignorable given observed covariates, then conditioning on the design variables can be sufficient for posterior inference about the response model.

Proof. This is the same factorization logic as missing-data ignorability: once the design likelihood involves no unknown parameter shared with the response model, it contributes only a multiplicative factor independent of the target parameter.
