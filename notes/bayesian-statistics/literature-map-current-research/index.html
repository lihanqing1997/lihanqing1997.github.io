44. Literature map for current research

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/posterior-compression-density-ratio-diagnostics/" %}

Sparse Bayesian inference

For seminar preparation, author names should be attached to mathematical roles rather than memorized as a flat list.

Remark. Castillo and Nickl are central for BvM and uncertainty in high-dimensional and nonparametric Bayes. Ghosal, Ghosh, and van der Vaart anchor posterior contraction theory. Spokoiny is a key reference for finite-sample likelihood expansions. Rousseau, Alquier, Petrone, Grünwald, and Chopin connect Bayesian asymptotics, PAC-Bayes, misspecification, computation, and model averaging themes.

Variational inference and high-dimensional statistics

Remark. Blei, Jordan, and Broderick are central names for variational inference and scalable Bayes. van de Geer and Wainwright are central for high-dimensional statistics, oracle inequalities, restricted eigenvalues, and sparse estimation. Hastie connects statistical learning, regularization, and applied sparse modeling.

Definition. A useful literature map records: problem, model, prior or estimator, main theorem, assumptions, and limitation. This format turns references into answers to questions such as what is new, what fails, and what comparison is fair.

How to classify a paper quickly

Definition. A useful map records four coordinates: model class, prior or approximation family, target of inference, and metric of validity.

Proposition. Most modern Bayesian theory papers can be read as proving one of three implications: prior thickness plus tests imply contraction; contraction plus local quadratic expansion implies Gaussian approximation; or variational/projection error plus posterior control implies approximate decision validity.

Remark. For personal research notes, record which implication a paper proves before recording the technical lemmas. That prevents a literature map from becoming an unstructured bibliography.
