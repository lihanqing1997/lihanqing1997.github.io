Semiparametric and nonparametric BvM theorems ask when some low-dimensional functional has a Gaussian posterior limit even though the full parameter is infinite-dimensional.

Functionals and influence functions

The target is often a functional \(\psi(P)\), not the whole distribution.

Definition. A functional \(\psi\) is pathwise differentiable at \(P_0\) if there is an influence function \(\tilde\psi\in L_0^2(P_0)\) such that along regular paths, \[ \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}t}\psi(P_t)\bigg|_{t=0} =P_0\tilde\psi s, \] where \(s\) is the score of the path.

Remark. The efficient variance bound is \(P_0\tilde\psi^2\).

Semiparametric BvM

A functional BvM says the posterior of the functional is asymptotically normal and efficient.

Theorem. Under posterior contraction of the nuisance parameter, sufficient regularity of \(\psi\), and a no-bias condition, the posterior of \[ \sqrt n(\psi(P)-\hat\psi) \] converges to \(N(0,P_0\tilde\psi^2)\).

Proof. Expand \(\psi(P)-\psi(P_0)\) into its linear influence-function term plus a remainder. Posterior contraction controls the remainder. The likelihood shifts the posterior in the least favorable direction, and the linear term obeys the ordinary Gaussian limit.

Nonparametric failure modes

The full posterior on an infinite-dimensional parameter usually cannot be Gaussian in strong norms.

Remark. Different functionals can have different regularity. Smooth linear functionals may satisfy BvM while the full density or regression function does not.

Credible intervals for functionals

When semiparametric BvM holds, Bayesian credible intervals can be efficient confidence intervals.

Corollary. Posterior quantile intervals for a regular functional have asymptotic frequentist coverage \(1-\alpha\) and length determined by the efficient variance.

Proof. The posterior quantiles converge to Gaussian quantiles with variance \(P_0\tilde\psi^2/n\), and the efficient estimator has the same asymptotic sampling law.
