31. Semiparametric and nonparametric Bernstein-von Mises theory

This page records the main definitions, propositions, and proof ideas for Semiparametric and nonparametric Bernstein-von Mises theory.

Definition

Definition. For a functional \(\psi(\eta)\), semiparametric BvM studies \(\sqrt n(\psi(\eta)-\hat\psi_n)\mid X\).

Proposition

Proposition. A typical condition is an expansion \[ \psi(\eta)-\psi(\eta_0)=P_0\tilde\psi_{\eta_0}(\eta-\eta_0)+\operatorname{rem}(\eta,\eta_0) \] with posterior remainder \(o_p(n^{-1/2})\).

Proof

Proof. Shift the likelihood in the least favorable direction and control the nonlinear remainder by posterior contraction.

Remarks

Full nonparametric BvM usually requires weak spaces where Gaussian limits are tight.
