18. Bernstein-von Mises theorem

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/local-asymptotic-normality-laplace-approximation/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/bayesian-statistics/credible-sets-frequentist-coverage/" %}

This page records the main definitions, propositions, and proof ideas for Bernstein-von Mises theorem.

Definition

Definition. A BvM theorem studies the posterior law of \(\sqrt n(\theta-\hat\theta_n)\) and compares it to a fixed Gaussian law.

Proposition

Proposition. In regular finite-dimensional models, \[ \left\|\Pi(\sqrt n(\theta-\hat\theta_n)\in\cdot\mid X^{(n)})-N(0,I_{\theta_0}^{-1})\right\|_{\mathrm{TV}}\to0. \]

Proof

Proof. Posterior mass is first localized; LAN then gives the Gaussian likelihood ratio and the prior is locally constant.

Remarks

BvM justifies first-order frequentist coverage of regular credible ellipsoids.
