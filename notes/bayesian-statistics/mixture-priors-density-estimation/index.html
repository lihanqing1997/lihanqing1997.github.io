This page records the main definitions, propositions, and proof ideas for Mixture priors and density estimation.

Definition

Definition. For kernel \(k(x\mid z)\) and mixing distribution \(G\), \(f_G(x)=\int k(x\mid z)\,\mathrm{d}G(z)\).

Proposition

Proposition. If good finite mixtures approximate the truth and the prior charges their neighborhoods, the induced density prior has KL support near the truth.

Proof

Proof. Approximate first by a finite mixture, then use continuity of the mixture map.

Remarks

Mixture-prior rates balance approximation, prior mass, and entropy of mixing distributions.
