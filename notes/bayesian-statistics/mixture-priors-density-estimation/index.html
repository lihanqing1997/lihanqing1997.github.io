Mixture priors model densities by averaging kernels against an unknown mixing distribution. They are central because they turn discrete random measures into smooth density priors.

Kernel mixtures

A mixture density is a linear smoothing of a mixing distribution.

Definition. Given kernel \(k(x\mid z)\) and mixing distribution \(G\), the mixture density is \[ p_G(x)=\int k(x\mid z)\,G(\mathrm{d}z). \]

Proposition. If \(G_n\Rightarrow G\) weakly and \(z\mapsto k(x\mid z)\) is bounded continuous for each \(x\), then \(p_{G_n}(x)\to p_G(x)\) pointwise.

Proof. This is the definition of weak convergence applied to the bounded continuous test function \(z\mapsto k(x\mid z)\).

Dirichlet process mixtures

A discrete random mixing measure can still induce a continuous random density.

Definition. A Dirichlet process mixture uses \(G\sim\operatorname{DP}(\alpha G_0)\) and density \(p_G\). Even though \(G\) is discrete almost surely, \(p_G\) is smooth when \(k\) is smooth.

Remark. The mixture map \(G\mapsto p_G\) regularizes the random measure. This is why Dirichlet process mixtures are useful for density estimation.

KL support for mixtures

Consistency requires prior mass near the true density in KL neighborhoods.

Proposition. If the kernel family can approximate \(p_0\) in KL divergence and the prior on \(G\) assigns positive mass to weak neighborhoods of approximating mixing laws, then the induced density prior has KL support at \(p_0\).

Proof. Choose a mixing law \(G^*\) with \(K(p_0,p_{G^*})<\varepsilon\). Continuity of the mixture map in the relevant topology gives a neighborhood of \(G^*\) whose image remains in a KL neighborhood. Positive prior mass of that neighborhood gives KL support.

Rates and kernel bandwidth

Contraction rates depend on approximation by mixtures and on the complexity of the mixing distribution.

Remark. Location-scale normal mixtures can adapt to smooth densities, but the rate calculation must control tails, bandwidth, entropy of mixture classes, and prior mass near approximating mixtures.
