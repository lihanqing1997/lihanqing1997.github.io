Breslow estimator

After estimating \(\beta\), the baseline cumulative hazard can be estimated by matching observed event counts with model-implied expected event counts.

Definition. The Breslow estimator is \[ \widehat\Lambda_0(t)=\int_0^t {\mathrm{d}N_\cdot(u)\over S^{(0)}(u,\hat\beta)} =\sum_{u\le t}{dN_\cdot(u)\over \sum_jY_j(u)\exp(Z_j^\top\hat\beta)}. \]

Proposition. For a fixed covariate vector \(z\), the plug-in survival estimator is \[ \widehat S(t\mid z)=\exp\{-\widehat\Lambda_0(t)\exp(z^\top\hat\beta)\}. \]

Proof. Under the Cox model, the cumulative hazard at covariate value \(z\) is \(\Lambda(t\mid z)=\Lambda_0(t)\exp(z^\top\beta)\). Substitute \(\widehat\Lambda_0\) and \(\hat\beta\), then use \(S=\exp(-\Lambda)\).

Joint uncertainty

Remark. Uncertainty in \(\widehat S(t\mid z)\) has two sources: the martingale noise in \(\widehat\Lambda_0\) and the regression uncertainty in \(\hat\beta\). Delta-method calculations must keep both pieces.

Influence representation

Proposition. Under regularity conditions, \[ \widehat\Lambda_0(t)-\Lambda_0(t) =\int_0^t{1\over s^{(0)}(u,\beta_0)}\,\mathrm{d}\overline M(u) -\dot\Lambda_0(t)^\top(\hat\beta-\beta_0)+o_p(n^{-1/2}), \] where \(\dot\Lambda_0(t)\) is the derivative of the Breslow functional with respect to \(\beta\).

Proof. Expand the Breslow denominator around \(\beta_0\). The numerator minus its compensator gives the martingale integral. The denominator derivative gives the linear term in \(\hat\beta-\beta_0\); the remaining empirical-process terms are controlled uniformly on compact time intervals.

Prediction bands

Remark. Pointwise intervals for \(S(t\mid z)\) are usually built on the log cumulative-hazard or complementary log-log scale, because these transformations keep back-transformed survival probabilities inside \([0,1]\).
