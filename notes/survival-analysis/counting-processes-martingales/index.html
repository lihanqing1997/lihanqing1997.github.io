Intensity model

Counting-process notation is the natural language for Cox theory because risk sets are random and change with time. It turns likelihood derivatives into martingales.

Definition. A counting process \(N(t)\) has intensity \(\lambda(t)\) with respect to a filtration \((\mathcal F_t)\) if \[ M(t)=N(t)-\int_0^t\lambda(u)\,\mathrm{d}u \] is a local martingale. In survival analysis, a subject-level intensity often has the form \(Y_i(t)\alpha_i(t)\), where \(Y_i(t)\) is predictable at-risk status.

Theorem. Doob-Meyer decomposition: if \(N\) is a submartingale counting process satisfying the usual regularity conditions, then \(N(t)=M(t)+A(t)\), where \(M\) is a martingale and \(A\) is predictable increasing. If \(A(t)=\int_0^t\lambda(u)\,\mathrm{d}u\), \(\lambda\) is the intensity.

Remark. The compensator \(A\) is the model-implied cumulative number of events. Martingale residuals \(M=N-A\) are the stochastic error terms in asymptotic proofs.

Martingale integrals

Proposition. If \(H\) is bounded and predictable and \(M\) is a square-integrable martingale with predictable variation \(\langle M\rangle\), then \[ \int_0^t H(u)\,\mathrm{d}M(u) \] is a martingale with predictable variation \(\int_0^tH(u)^2\,\mathrm{d}\langle M\rangle(u)\).

Proof. First prove the claim for simple predictable \(H\) by linearity and optional stopping on disjoint predictable intervals. Then approximate bounded predictable processes in the martingale isometry. The predictable variation identity follows from the defining isometry for stochastic integrals.

Doob-Meyer decomposition

Theorem. If \(N(t)\) is a counting process with predictable intensity \(Y(t)\lambda(t)\), then \[ M(t)=N(t)-\int_0^tY(u)\lambda(u)\,\mathrm{d}u \] is a local martingale, and its predictable variation is \[ \langle M\rangle(t)=\int_0^tY(u)\lambda(u)\,\mathrm{d}u. \]

Proof. By the definition of intensity, the conditional mean increment over \([t,t+\mathrm{d}t)\) is \(Y(t)\lambda(t)\mathrm{d}t+o(\mathrm{d}t)\). Subtracting the compensator removes the predictable drift. For a counting process with unit jumps, the predictable quadratic variation equals the compensator.

Martingale central limit theorem

Proposition. If \(M_{n}\) is a triangular array of square-integrable martingales with jumps tending to zero and \(\langle M_n\rangle(t)\to v(t)\) in probability, then \(M_n(t)\Rightarrow N(0,v(t))\) for each fixed \(t\), with process convergence under the usual tightness conditions.

Remark. In survival analysis, this theorem turns sums of predictable covariates against \(\mathrm{d}M_i(t)\) into Gaussian score limits.
