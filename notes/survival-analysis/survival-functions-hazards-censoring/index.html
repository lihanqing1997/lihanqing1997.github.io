Survival law

Survival analysis studies a nonnegative event time \(T\), often observed incompletely. The mathematical object is not only the distribution of \(T\), but the way information is lost through censoring and delayed observation.

Definition. For \(T\ge0\), the survival function, distribution function, density, hazard, and cumulative hazard are \[ S(t)=P(T>t),\quad F(t)=P(T\le t),\quad f(t)=F'(t),\quad \lambda(t)=\lim_{h\downarrow0}{P(t\le T

Proposition. If \(T\) has absolutely continuous law and \(S(t)>0\), then \(S(t)=\exp\{-\Lambda(t)\}\) and \(f(t)=\lambda(t)S(t)\).

Proof. Since \(S'(t)=-f(t)=-\lambda(t)S(t)\), we have \((\log S)'(t)=-\lambda(t)\). Integrating from \(0\) to \(t\), using \(S(0)=1\) for no atom at zero, gives \(S(t)=\exp\{-\Lambda(t)\}\). The identity for \(f\) follows from the definition of the hazard.

Censoring

The basic observation is usually \(X=T\wedge C\) and \(\Delta=1\{T\le C\}\), where \(C\) is a censoring time. Without assumptions on \(C\), the distribution of \(T\) is not identifiable.

Definition. Right censoring is independent if \(T\perp C\), or conditionally independent given covariates \(Z\), meaning \(T\perp C\mid Z\). In counting-process notation this is often stated as noninformative censoring: after conditioning on the observed history, the censoring mechanism does not change the event intensity.

Remark. Independent censoring is not a cosmetic assumption. It is the statement that the observed risk set at time \(t\) is a representative sample of subjects whose event times exceed \(t\), after conditioning on the covariates included in the model.

Likelihood under independent right censoring

The first useful calculation is the likelihood contribution of a censored observation. It explains why the pair \((X,\Delta)\), rather than \(X\) alone, is the statistical observation.

Proposition. If \(X=T\wedge C\), \(\Delta=1\{T\le C\}\), and \(T\perp C\), then the part of the likelihood involving the law of \(T\) is \[ L_T=\prod_{i=1}^n f_T(x_i)^{\Delta_i}S_T(x_i)^{1-\Delta_i} =\prod_{i=1}^n \lambda_T(x_i)^{\Delta_i}\exp\{-\Lambda_T(x_i)\}. \]

Proof. The joint density of \((X,\Delta)\) factors as \(f_T(x)S_C(x)\) when \(\Delta=1\), and \(S_T(x)f_C(x)\) when \(\Delta=0\). The censoring factors contain no parameter of the \(T\)-law. Finally use \(f=\lambda S\) and \(S=\exp(-\Lambda)\).

Left truncation and delayed entry

Definition. With delayed entry \(A\), subject \(i\) is observed only if \(T_i\ge A_i\). The risk indicator becomes \(Y_i(t)=1\{A_i\le t\le X_i\}\), so likelihood and martingale formulas condition on survival to the entry time.

Remark. Censoring removes the right tail after observation has begun; truncation changes which subjects enter the sample. They are different missingness mechanisms and cannot be interchanged.
