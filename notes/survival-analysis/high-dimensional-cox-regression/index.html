Penalized partial likelihood

High-dimensional Cox regression replaces fixed-dimensional likelihood maximization by a sparse convex program. The geometry is analogous to Lasso, but the loss is the negative partial log likelihood.

Definition. The Lasso Cox estimator solves \[ \hat\beta\in\arg\min_{\beta\in\mathbb R^p}\{-n^{-1}\ell_p(\beta)+\lambda\|\beta\|_1\}. \]

Definition. A Cox restricted eigenvalue condition requires the Hessian of the normalized negative partial log likelihood to have curvature on sparse cones: \[ \delta^\top\nabla^2\{-n^{-1}\ell_p(\beta_0)\}\delta\ge \kappa\|\delta_S\|_2^2 \] for all \(\delta\) satisfying \(\|\delta_{S^c}\|_1\le c\|\delta_S\|_1\).

Theorem. On the score event \(\|n^{-1}U(\beta_0)\|_\infty\le\lambda/2\), and under restricted eigenvalue curvature \(\kappa\), an \(s\)-sparse truth satisfies the typical bounds \[ \|\hat\beta-\beta_0\|_1\lesssim {s\lambda\over\kappa},\qquad (\hat\beta-\beta_0)^\top\nabla^2 L(\beta_0)(\hat\beta-\beta_0)\lesssim {s\lambda^2\over\kappa}. \]

Proof. The optimality inequality gives the basic inequality. The score event controls the stochastic linear term by \(\lambda\|\Delta\|_1/2\), forcing the cone condition. Restricted eigenvalue curvature converts prediction curvature into \(\ell_2\) control on the active coordinates, and the cone converts that into \(\ell_1\) control.

Remark. For support recovery one also needs beta-min and an incoherence or irrepresentability-type condition. Prediction consistency alone does not identify the active set.

Score concentration

Lemma. If covariates are uniformly bounded and martingale Bernstein inequalities apply, then with high probability \[ \|n^{-1}U(\beta_0)\|_\infty\lesssim \sqrt{\log p\over n}. \] Thus the Lasso tuning parameter is typically chosen of this order.

Proof. Each score coordinate is a martingale integral with bounded jumps and predictable variation of order \(n\). Bernstein's inequality gives a coordinate tail bound, and a union bound over \(p\) coordinates gives the logarithmic factor.

Debiased Cox estimator

Definition. A debiased Cox estimator for coordinate \(j\) has the form \[ \tilde\beta_j=\hat\beta_j+\hat\Theta_j^\top n^{-1}U(\hat\beta), \] where \(\hat\Theta_j\) estimates the \(j\)-th row of the inverse sparse information matrix.

Remark. Debiasing requires both an accurate initial sparse estimator and an accurate inverse-information direction. The second requirement is often the harder one.
