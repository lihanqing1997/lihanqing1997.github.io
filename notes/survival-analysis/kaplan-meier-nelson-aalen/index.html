2. Kaplan-Meier and Nelson-Aalen estimators

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/survival-analysis/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/survival-analysis/survival-functions-hazards-censoring/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/survival-analysis/counting-processes-martingales/" %}

Risk sets

Nonparametric survival estimation begins by separating two processes: who is at risk immediately before a time \(t\), and whether an event occurs at \(t\).

Definition. For subject \(i\), let \(N_i(t)=1\{X_i\le t,\Delta_i=1\}\) and \(Y_i(t)=1\{X_i\ge t\}\). At an event time \(t_j\), write \(d_j=\sum_i \Delta_i1\{X_i=t_j\}\) and \(r_j=\sum_i1\{X_i\ge t_j\}\).

Theorem. Under independent right censoring, the Kaplan-Meier estimator is \[ \widehat S(t)=\prod_{t_j\le t}\left(1-{d_j\over r_j}\right), \] and the Nelson-Aalen estimator of the cumulative hazard is \[ \widehat\Lambda(t)=\sum_{t_j\le t}{d_j\over r_j}. \]

Proof. At time \(t_j\), conditional on being in the risk set, the empirical conditional survival probability across the jump is \(1-d_j/r_j\). Multiplying these conditional survival estimates gives the product-limit form. Since \(\mathrm{d}\Lambda(t)\) is the conditional event probability over an infinitesimal interval, its empirical jump at \(t_j\) is \(d_j/r_j\), giving Nelson-Aalen.

Connection between the two estimators

Proposition. When jumps are small, \(-\log \widehat S(t)=\widehat\Lambda(t)+O_p(\sum_{t_j\le t}d_j^2/r_j^2)\).

Proof. Use \(-\log(1-x)=x+O(x^2)\) uniformly for small \(x\), and substitute \(x=d_j/r_j\) in the product formula for \(\widehat S\).

Greenwood variance and log-scale intervals

Theorem. For fixed \(t\) before the last time with a nonempty risk set, Greenwood's formula estimates the variance of the Kaplan-Meier estimator by \[ \widehat{\operatorname{var}}\{\widehat S(t)\} =\widehat S(t)^2\sum_{t_j\le t}{d_j\over r_j(r_j-d_j)}. \]

Proof. Work on the log product scale. Since \(\log\widehat S(t)=\sum_{t_j\le t}\log(1-d_j/r_j)\), a first-order delta calculation gives increments with variance \(d_j/\{r_j(r_j-d_j)\}\). Multiplying by \(\widehat S(t)^2\) returns to the survival scale.

Asymptotic process viewpoint

Proposition. The Nelson-Aalen error admits the martingale representation \[ \widehat\Lambda(t)-\Lambda(t) =\int_0^t {1\over Y_\cdot(u)}\,\mathrm{d}M_\cdot(u)+R_n(t), \] where \(R_n(t)\) is a smaller order term on intervals where \(Y_\cdot(u)\) is of order \(n\).

Remark. This representation is the bridge from product-limit estimators to the counting-process central limit theory used for Cox regression.
