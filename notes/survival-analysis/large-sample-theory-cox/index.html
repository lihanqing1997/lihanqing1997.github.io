Consistency

The asymptotic theory of Cox estimators follows the same M-estimation architecture as likelihood theory, but the stochastic part is a martingale integral.

Theorem. Assume independent observations, bounded covariates, noninformative censoring, identifiability of \(\beta_0\), and uniform convergence of the normalized partial log likelihood \(n^{-1}\ell_p(\beta)\) to a strictly concave limit maximized uniquely at \(\beta_0\). Then the partial likelihood estimator \(\hat\beta\) is consistent.

Proof. Uniform convergence makes the empirical criterion close to its deterministic limit on compact sets. Strict concavity and unique maximization give separation away from every neighborhood of \(\beta_0\). The argmax theorem then yields \(\hat\beta\to\beta_0\).

Asymptotic normality

Theorem. If, in addition, the information matrix \(\mathcal I(\beta_0)\) is nonsingular and the martingale central limit theorem applies to \(n^{-1/2}U(\beta_0)\), then \[ \sqrt n(\hat\beta-\beta_0)\Rightarrow N(0,\mathcal I(\beta_0)^{-1}). \]

Proof. Taylor expand the score: \(0=U(\hat\beta)=U(\beta_0)-I_n(\tilde\beta)(\hat\beta-\beta_0)\). Divide by \(n\), use \(n^{-1}I_n(\tilde\beta)\to\mathcal I(\beta_0)\) and \(n^{-1/2}U(\beta_0)\Rightarrow N(0,\mathcal I(\beta_0))\), then apply Slutsky.

Remark. The proof reveals why martingales enter Cox theory: the score at the truth is a sum of predictable covariates integrated against event martingales.

Information convergence

Lemma. Suppose \(n^{-1}S^{(k)}(t,\beta)\to s^{(k)}(t,\beta)\) uniformly for \(k=0,1,2\) on compact neighborhoods of \(\beta_0\), and \(s^{(0)}\) is bounded away from zero. Then \[ n^{-1}I_n(\beta)\to \int_0^\tau v(t,\beta)s^{(0)}(t,\beta)\lambda_0(t)\,\mathrm{d}t \] uniformly, where \(v=s^{(2)}/s^{(0)}-\{s^{(1)}/s^{(0)}\}^{\otimes2}\).

Proof. Replace empirical risk-set sums by their limits in the covariance formula. The event integral has compensator \(S^{(0)}(t,\beta_0)\lambda_0(t)\mathrm{d}t\), and the martingale remainder is \(o_p(n)\) uniformly under boundedness and stochastic equicontinuity.

Sandwich variance under misspecification

Remark. If the proportional hazards model is used as a working model, the limiting variance need not be the inverse Hessian. The robust form is \(A^{-1}BA^{-1}\), where \(A\) is the limit Hessian and \(B\) is the variance of the score martingale or cluster score.
