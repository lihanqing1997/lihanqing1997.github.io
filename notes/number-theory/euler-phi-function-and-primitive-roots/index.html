Euler phi function and primitive roots records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the number theory note.

Definition

Definition. Euler's function \(\varphi(n)\) counts units modulo \(n\). A primitive root modulo \(n\) is a generator of \((\mathbb Z/n\mathbb Z)^\times\).

Proposition

Proposition. Euler theorem says \(a^{\varphi(n)}\equiv1\pmod n\) for \(\gcd(a,n)=1\). Primitive roots exist for prime powers of odd primes, but not for all moduli.

Proof

Proof. Euler theorem is Lagrange theorem applied to the finite unit group. Primitive-root classification uses cyclicity of finite subgroups of fields and CRT decomposition.

Remark

Remark. The structure of the unit group controls exponentiation, residues, and many cryptographic constructions.
