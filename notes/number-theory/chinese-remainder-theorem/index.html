4. Chinese remainder theorem

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/number-theory/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/number-theory/congruences-and-modular-arithmetic/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/number-theory/quadratic-residues-and-reciprocity/" %}

Chinese remainder theorem records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the number theory note.

Definition

Definition. The Chinese remainder theorem solves simultaneous congruences with coprime moduli by identifying one quotient ring with a product of quotient rings.

Chinese remainder theorem

Theorem. If \(n_1,ldots,n_r\) are pairwise coprime, then \(\mathbb Z/(n_1\cdots n_r)\mathbb Z\cong\prod_i \mathbb Z/n_i\mathbb Z\).

Proof

Proof. Construct the natural reduction map. Coprimality gives surjectivity by Bezout combinations, and equal cardinalities or kernel computation gives injectivity.

Remark

Remark. CRT is a decomposition theorem: arithmetic modulo a composite splits into arithmetic modulo coprime factors.
