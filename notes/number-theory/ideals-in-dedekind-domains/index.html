13. Ideals in Dedekind domains

Ideals in Dedekind domains records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the number theory note.

Definition

Definition. A Dedekind domain is a Noetherian integrally closed domain of dimension one. Rings of integers of number fields are Dedekind domains.

Ideal factorization theorem

Theorem. Every nonzero ideal in a Dedekind domain factors uniquely as a product of nonzero prime ideals.

Proof

Proof. Localizing at prime ideals gives discrete valuation rings where ideals are powers of the maximal ideal. Glue the local exponents to obtain global unique factorization of ideals.

Remark

Remark. Unique factorization of elements may fail, but unique factorization of ideals survives. This is the central repair in algebraic number theory.
