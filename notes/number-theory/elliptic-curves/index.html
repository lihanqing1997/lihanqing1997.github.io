Elliptic curves records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the number theory note.

Definition

Definition. An elliptic curve over a field is a smooth projective genus-one curve with a chosen rational point, often represented by a nonsingular Weierstrass equation.

Mordell-Weil theorem

Theorem. The rational points on an elliptic curve form an abelian group, and over \(\mathbb Q\) this group is finitely generated.

Proof

Proof. The group law comes from line intersection: a line meets a cubic in three points counted with multiplicity. Mordell-Weil finite generation uses height functions and descent.

Remark

Remark. Elliptic curves connect Diophantine equations, complex tori, modular forms, and arithmetic geometry.
