Dirichlet series and Euler products records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the number theory note.

Definition

Definition. A Dirichlet series has the form \(\sum_{n\ge1}a_n n^{-s}\). Multiplicative coefficients often produce Euler products over primes.

Proposition

Proposition. If \(a_n\) is multiplicative and the series converges absolutely, then \(\sum a_n n^{-s}=\prod_p\sum_{k\ge0}a_{p^k}p^{-ks}\).

Proof

Proof. Absolute convergence permits rearranging the series. Unique factorization rewrites every integer as a product of prime powers, matching terms in the product expansion.

Remark

Remark. Euler products are analytic expressions of prime factorization. They are the entry point from arithmetic to complex analysis.
