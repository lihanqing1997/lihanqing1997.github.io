Valuations and p-adic numbers records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the number theory note.

Definition

Definition. The \(p\)-adic absolute value measures divisibility by \(p\): numbers divisible by high powers of \(p\) are small. Completing \(\mathbb Q\) under this absolute value gives \(\mathbb Q_p\).

Proposition

Proposition. The field \(\mathbb Q_p\) is complete, locally compact, and every nonzero element decomposes as \(p^k u\) with \(u\) a \(p\)-adic unit.

Proof

Proof. Cauchy sequences stabilize modulo every power of \(p\). The valuation gives the exponent \(k\), and the remaining factor has valuation zero.

Remark

Remark. The \(p\)-adic numbers make congruence information into geometry. Local methods study equations prime by prime.
