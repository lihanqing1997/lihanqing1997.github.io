Divisibility and Euclidean algorithm records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the number theory note.

Definition

Definition. For integers, \(a\mid b\) means \(b=ac\) for some integer \(c\). The greatest common divisor is the positive generator of the ideal \((a,b)\subset\mathbb Z\).

Proposition

Proposition. The Euclidean algorithm terminates and produces integers \(u,v\) such that \(\gcd(a,b)=ua+vb\).

Proof

Proof. Repeated division with remainder strictly decreases nonnegative remainders. Back-substitution expresses the final nonzero remainder as an integer combination of the original inputs.

Remark

Remark. The gcd is both an arithmetic object and an ideal-theoretic object. This dual view extends to Euclidean domains and PIDs.
