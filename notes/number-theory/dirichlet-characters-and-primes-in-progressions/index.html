Dirichlet characters and primes in progressions records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the number theory note.

Definition

Definition. A Dirichlet character modulo \(q\) is a periodic completely multiplicative function adapted to the unit group modulo \(q\).

Dirichlet theorem

Theorem. If \(\gcd(a,q)=1\), then there are infinitely many primes \(p\equiv a\pmod q\).

Proof

Proof. Characters separate residue classes. Dirichlet \(L\)-functions encode primes in those classes, and nonvanishing at \(s=1\) supplies divergence of the corresponding prime sum.

Remark

Remark. Characters are Fourier analysis on finite abelian groups. They transform congruence questions into analytic statements about \(L\)-functions.
