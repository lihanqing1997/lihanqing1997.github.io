15. Units and Dirichlet unit theorem

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/number-theory/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/number-theory/class-group-and-failure-of-unique-factorization/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/number-theory/valuations-and-p-adic-numbers/" %}

Units and Dirichlet unit theorem records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the number theory note.

Definition

Definition. A unit in \(\mathcal O_K\) is an invertible algebraic integer. Units form a finitely generated abelian group with roots of unity as torsion.

Dirichlet unit theorem

Theorem. Dirichlet's unit theorem says \(\mathcal O_K^\times\cong\mu_K\times\mathbb Z^{r_1+r_2-1}\), where \(r_1\) and \(r_2\) count real and complex embeddings.

Proof

Proof. Embed units logarithmically into a hyperplane using archimedean absolute values. Lattice discreteness and covolume finiteness give the free rank.

Remark

Remark. Units are solutions to norm equations and encode the archimedean geometry of a number field.
