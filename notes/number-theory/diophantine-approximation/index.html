Diophantine approximation records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the number theory note.

Definition

Definition. Diophantine approximation studies how well real or algebraic numbers can be approximated by rationals, often through inequalities involving denominators.

Dirichlet approximation theorem

Theorem. Dirichlet's approximation theorem says that for every irrational \(\alpha\), there are infinitely many rationals \(p/q\) with \(|\alpha-p/q|<1/q^2\).

Proof

Proof. Apply the pigeonhole principle to fractional parts of \(0,\alpha,2\alpha,ldots,N\alpha\), then convert two close fractional parts into a good rational approximation.

Remark

Remark. Approximation quality separates rational, algebraic, and transcendental behavior. Continued fractions sharpen the one-dimensional theory.
