10. Prime number theorem

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/number-theory/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/number-theory/dirichlet-series-and-euler-products/" next_label="Next page" next_url="/notes/number-theory/dirichlet-characters-and-primes-in-progressions/" %}

Prime number theorem records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the number theory note.

Definition

Definition. The prime number theorem describes the asymptotic density of primes through \(\pi(x)\), the number of primes at most \(x\).

Prime number theorem

Theorem. The prime number theorem states \(\pi(x)\sim x/\log x\). Equivalent forms use Chebyshev functions such as \(\psi(x)\sim x\).

Proof

Proof. Classical proofs study the analytic continuation and zero-free region of the zeta function near \(s=1\), then apply Tauberian arguments to recover asymptotics of prime sums.

Remark

Remark. The theorem shows that primes are sparse but statistically regular at first order. Finer error terms are tied to zeta zeros.
