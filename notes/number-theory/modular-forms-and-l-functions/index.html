20. Modular forms and L-functions

{% include note-topic-nav.html back_label="Back" back_url="/notes/number-theory/" prev_label="Last page" prev_url="/notes/number-theory/elliptic-curves/" %}

Modular forms and L-functions records the objects, main results, proof mechanisms, and boundary cases needed for the number theory note.

Definition

Definition. A modular form is a holomorphic function on the upper half-plane satisfying transformation laws under a modular group and growth conditions at cusps.

Proposition

Proposition. Fourier coefficients of modular forms encode arithmetic data, and their \(L\)-functions often admit analytic continuation and functional equations.

Proof

Proof. Transformation laws impose strong symmetry, while holomorphicity and cusp conditions control Fourier expansions. Hecke operators organize coefficients into multiplicative systems.

Remark

Remark. Modular forms are a meeting point of complex analysis, representation theory, and number theory. They are central in modern reciprocity phenomena.
